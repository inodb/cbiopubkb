## Summary

This paper delineates the spatio-temporal dynamics of breast cancer relapse across 3,240 patients (1,980 with METABRIC molecular data) using a non-homogeneous semi-Markov multistate model that accounts for loco-regional relapse (LR), distant relapse (DR), competing causes of death, and molecular subtype. The central finding is that four integrative subtypes (IntClust 1, 2, 6, 9) together comprise 26% of ER+ tumors and carry median distant relapse risk of 42–55% at 20 years post-diagnosis — far exceeding what IHC or PAM50 subtyping predicts. Each late-recurring subtype is driven by characteristic genomic copy number amplifications. A separate TNBC subtype (IntClust10, Basal-like) largely stops recurring after 5 years, whereas IntClust4ER- remains at chronic risk. The integrative subtypes significantly improve prediction of late distant relapse beyond clinical variables plus IHC (C-index 0.70 vs. 0.63 at 10 years). A web-based recurrence predictor was released.

## Key Findings

### F1: Four late-recurring ER+ integrative subtypes with 42–55% 20-year distant relapse risk

**Finding:** IntClust 1, 2, 6, and 9 collectively account for 26% of ER+ breast cancers and are characterized by markedly elevated risk of distant relapse persisting up to 20 years after surgery — risk that is not captured by IHC or PAM50 subtyping.

**Detail:** Among ER+/HER2− patients disease-free at 5 years, the four late-recurring subtypes had significantly higher probability of distant relapse or cancer death than IntClust3 (best-prognosis ER+) across 10-, 15-, and 20-year horizons. Risk varies over time between subtypes (not proportional hazard), and the pattern is obscured when grouping all ER+ together or using PAM50 Luminal A/B classes. IntClust2 has the worst prognosis among ER+ subtypes, second only to the HER2-enriched IntClust5.

**Genes / cancer types:** IntClust 1 (`RPS6KB1`/S6K1, 17q23), IntClust 2 (`CCND1`, `FGF3`, `EMSY`, `PAK1`, `RSF1`, 11q13), IntClust 6 (`ZNF703`, `FGFR1`, 8p12), IntClust 9 (`MYC`, 8q24) · ER+ breast cancer

**Data availability:** `available`
> METABRIC molecular data including IntClust assignments, copy number, and expression are in `brca_metabric` on cBioPortal. Clinical follow-up (LR, DR, DSD times) are in Supplementary Tables 5–8 of the paper.

**Tool support:** `native`
> OncoPrint and survival (Kaplan–Meier) analyses by IntClust subtype available natively. Multistate transition probabilities require the custom `brcarepred` web tool (https://caldaslab.cruk.cam.ac.uk/brcarepred) or R `mstate` package with provided code (https://github.com/cclab-brca/brcarepred).

### F2: Copy number amplifications define therapeutic targets in each late-recurring subtype

**Finding:** Each of the four late-recurring ER+ subtypes is defined by a characteristic, high-frequency focal copy number amplification in a targetable oncogene.

**Detail:** IntClust2: 96% of cases have `RSF1` amplification on 11q13 (also `CCND1`, `FGF3`, `PAK1`, `EMSY`; 0–22% in other subtypes). IntClust6: 100% have 8p12 amplification of `ZNF703` and `FGFR1` (2–21% in others). IntClust1: 96% have `RPS6KB1` (S6K1) gain/amplification on 17q23 (0–25% in others). IntClust9: 89% have `MYC` amplification at 8q24 (3–42% in others). These amplifications are proposed drivers and several genes are viable drug targets (FGFR1 inhibitors, CDK inhibitors, S6K1 inhibitors).

**Genes / cancer types:** `CCND1`, `FGF3`, `EMSY`, `PAK1`, `RSF1` (11q13); `ZNF703`, `FGFR1` (8p12); `RPS6KB1` (17q23); `MYC` (8q24) · ER+ breast cancer

**Data availability:** `available`
> Copy number data for all genes and GISTIC-level calls are in `brca_metabric`. IntClust assignments are a clinical attribute.

**Tool support:** `native`
> CNA queries by gene and IntClust subtype available via OncoPrint and Cancer Summary tabs.

**Concepts:** #copy-number #targeted-therapy #biomarker #FGFR1 #MYC #CCND1

### F3: TNBC subtypes have opposite long-term relapse trajectories (IntClust10 vs. IntClust4ER-)

**Finding:** Among ER−/HER2− (TNBC) patients, IntClust10 (Basal-like enriched) is essentially relapse-free after 5 years, whereas IntClust4ER- continues to accumulate distant relapses.

**Detail:** Probability of relapse at 5 years: IntClust10 ≈ 0.33, rising to only 0.37 at 20 years — a "plateau" rarely seen in other high-risk subtypes. In contrast, IntClust4ER- shows 0.30 at 5 years rising to 0.49 at 20 years. IHC (ER−/HER2−) and PAM50 (Basal-like) homogenize this risk. Clinical implication: TNBC patients disease-free at 5 years may not need continued intensive surveillance unless they are IntClust4ER-.

**Genes / cancer types:** TNBC; IntClust10 vs IntClust4ER- · Triple-negative breast cancer

**Data availability:** `available`
> IntClust assignments and survival data available in `brca_metabric`.

**Tool support:** `api`
> Survival curves by IntClust stratification can be generated via cBioPortal Kaplan–Meier tool or REST API with patient-level clinical data; multistate probability curves require external tool.

**Concepts:** #TNBC #molecular-subtypes #survival #late-relapse

### F4: Integrative subtypes significantly improve late relapse prediction beyond clinical variables

**Finding:** Including IntClust subtype on top of clinical covariates (age, grade, size, nodes) and IHC subtype significantly improves concordance for predicting late distant relapse in ER+/HER2− patients.

**Detail:** At 10 years, C-index for clinical + IHC model = 0.63 (95% CI 0.58–0.68); adding IntClust improves to 0.70 (95% CI 0.64–0.75; p = 0.00011). Gains persist at 15 years (0.62 → 0.67, p = 0.0016) and 20 years (0.61 → 0.66, p = 0.0017). External validation in an 8-cohort metacohort confirmed the trend. Outcome: IntClust subtyping may help identify ER+ patients who benefit from extended endocrine therapy (>5 years) or novel targeted trials.

**Genes / cancer types:** IntClust 1, 2, 6, 9 vs. IntClust 3 · ER+/HER2− breast cancer

**Data availability:** `available`
> Molecular data in `brca_metabric`. C-index calculations require external R analysis with the paper's published code.

**Tool support:** `custom`
> Quantitative prediction requires R code at https://github.com/cclab-brca/brcarepred or the web tool at https://caldaslab.cruk.cam.ac.uk/brcarepred.

**Concepts:** #survival #biomarker #molecular-subtypes #ER-positive #treatment-resistance

### F5: Site-specific metastasis patterns differ by ER status and subtype; brain mets most lethal

**Finding:** ER+ tumors preferentially metastasize to bone (71% vs. 43% in ER−) while ER− tumors show more visceral spread (brain/meningeal 27% vs. 11%, lung 50% vs. 41%). After distant relapse, brain metastasis is the strongest predictor of cancer-specific death regardless of ER status.

**Detail:** Among ER+ patients the first metastasis is most commonly bone (76%), but bone metastasis prevalence reflects slow accumulation rather than true site tropism — ER− patients die of visceral disease first. Multiple distant relapses are common even in favorable subtypes. ER− disease is characterized by a rapid cascade of relapses after the first event, whereas most ER+ patients experience one early relapse and, if a second occurs, risk of further events increases substantially.

**Genes / cancer types:** Site-of-metastasis analysis (bone, brain/meningeal, lung, liver) · ER+ and ER− breast cancer

**Data availability:** `partial`
> Site-of-relapse data for 618 patients is in Supplementary Tables 5–8; METABRIC molecular data in `brca_metabric`. Full recurrent event data not in the cBioPortal study.

**Tool support:** `custom`
> Cumulative incidence and PWP recurrent-event models require paper's R code. cBioPortal can provide site annotation for overlapping samples if available as clinical attributes.

## Open Questions

1. Do the four late-recurring IntClust subtypes show distinct responses to extended endocrine therapy (aromatase inhibitors beyond 5 years) in randomized trial cohorts?
2. Can `FGFR1` and `RPS6KB1` (S6K1) amplification-driven subtypes be targeted pharmacologically to reduce late relapse?
3. What are the molecular mechanisms maintaining chronic low-level disease in IntClust4ER- TNBC that continue to relapse beyond 5 years?
4. Can the multistate model be trained on contemporary patients treated with CDK4/6 inhibitors or checkpoint immunotherapy — agents unavailable in the 1977–2005 cohort?

## Limitations

- METABRIC cohort diagnosed 1977–2005: systematic use of aromatase inhibitors, HER2-targeted therapy, and immunotherapy post-dates most patients; treatment effects confound natural history estimates.
- IntClust subtyping currently requires microarray gene expression data (iC10 package), limiting application to routinely collected FFPE RNA.
- External metacohort has shorter follow-up (median 8 years) and prohibits 20-year analyses.
- The multistate model uses clock-reset timescales and bootstrap SEs; small numbers of transitions in some subtype-state combinations increase uncertainty.
- Sites-of-relapse dataset (n=618) is a selected subset; not population-representative for metastatic pattern analyses.
