## Summary

Pan-Asia multi-center WES, WGS, RNA-seq, and single-nucleus sequencing (SNS) of 133 combined hepatocellular and intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (cHCC-ICC) cases. cHCC-ICC is a rare primary liver cancer with bilinear HCC and ICC differentiation and poor prognosis. Three morphologic subtypes (separate, combined, mixed) are molecularly distinct: combined type resembles ICC (P1 cluster, biliary markers, KRAS/IDH1 enriched, EMT upregulation) and mixed type resembles HCC (P2 cluster, hepatocyte markers, TP53/AFP enriched). All combined and mixed type cHCC-ICCs are monoclonal origin; separate type shows both mono- and multiclonal origins. High Nestin (NES) expression (81% of cHCC-ICCs positive, vs. lower rates in HCC and ICC) identifies a novel diagnostic and prognostic biomarker. Data available in `hccihch_pku_2019` on cBioPortal.

## Key Findings

### F1: cHCC-ICC has a distinct driver gene landscape — high TP53, absent CTNNB1/KRAS

**Finding:** Significantly mutated genes in cHCC-ICC include TP53, AXIN1, RB1, PTEN, ARID2, and BRD7 (q<0.1). cHCC-ICC shows significantly higher TP53 mutation frequency (49%) than both HCC (31–35%) and ICC (22–38%), suggesting a more prominent TP53 role. Strikingly, CTNNB1 mutation frequency is only 6% vs. 27% in TCGA-HCC (p<0.0001), and KRAS is mutated in 0% vs. 17–19% in ICC (p<0.0001) — defining a unique genomic identity for cHCC-ICC not explained by ethnic/etiologic factors.

**Detail:** A total of 14 driver genes are curated from significantly mutated genes and genes recurrently reported in HCC and ICC. Epigenetic regulators (KMT2D, IDH1, BAP1, EZH2) are mutated in 35.6%; SWI/SNF complex genes (ARID1A, ARID2, PBRM1, BRD7) in 19.5%. ECM/cell adhesion genes (ADGRV1, MUC2, NEB, DST, HMCN1) and nuclear/chromosomal integrity genes (SYNE1/2, SYCP2, FRY) are novel recurrent alterations. AXIN1 mutations are specifically enriched in mixed type vs. combined type cHCC-ICC (20% vs. 2%, p<0.001), the only significant subtype-specific driver difference. TERT promoter mutations (22.9%, all C228T hotspot) are intermediate between HCC (46%) and ICC (0% in ICGC).

**Genes / cancer types:** TP53, AXIN1, RB1, PTEN, ARID2, BRD7, CTNNB1, KRAS, KMT2D, IDH1, BAP1, EZH2, TERT · Combined HCC-ICC

### F2: Four pan-liver-cancer expression clusters separate biliary from hepatic differentiation

**Finding:** Unsupervised expression clustering of 367 PLC samples (HCC n=213, ICC n=49, cHCC-ICC n=105) identifies four clusters (P1–P4). P1 is ICC-enriched with high biliary markers (EPCAM, KRT19, KRAS expression, IDH1 mutations) and predicts poor prognosis. P2 is enriched for Hoshida-S2 HCC, mixed type cHCC-ICC, TP53 mutations, and high serum AFP. P3 (Hoshida-S1/TGFβ) and P4 (Hoshida-S3/CTNNB1) represent better-prognosis HCC clusters with hepatocyte markers (ALB, TBX3, GPC3). Patients in P1/P2 have significantly worse OS than P3/P4 (p<0.0001).

**Detail:** Combined type cHCC-ICC is significantly enriched in P1 (ICC-like cluster; EMT most enriched gene set by GSEA); mixed type cHCC-ICC is significantly enriched in P2 (HCC-like cluster; xenobiotic metabolism and bile acid metabolism most enriched). This molecular distinction has therapeutic implications: combined type may respond to ICC-targeted therapies (including IDH1 inhibitors, FGFR inhibitors), while mixed type may respond to HCC-directed therapies. Immune profiling shows mixed type (P2) is enriched for a "High" immune cluster (with high CTLA4, PD-1, PD-L1 expression), while combined type (P1) is enriched for a "Medium" immune group — suggesting different immunotherapy potential.

**Genes / cancer types:** EPCAM, KRT19, KRAS, IDH1, CTNNB1, ALB, TBX3, GPC3, APOE, TGFB1, TGFB2, PRDM5 · Combined HCC-ICC, HCC, ICC

### F3: Combined and mixed type cHCC-ICC are monoclonal — with distinct evolutionary trajectories

**Finding:** All combined type (H-I component pairs) and mixed type cHCC-ICC cases have monoclonal origin, confirmed by shared mutations (27–95% median 74% shared in combined type; clonal mutations in all mixed type by CCF analysis). Separate type cases show both monoclonal (4/6) and multiclonal (2/6) origins. Phylogenetic analysis of combined type shows branched evolution, with TP53, CTNNB1, and RB1 as truncal driver mutations and branch mutations determining HCC vs. ICC phenotypic divergence.

**Detail:** Two patterns of combined type evolution: (1) early subclonal diversification followed by late phenotypic bifurcation (H-I pairs share many identical subclones); (2) phenotypic bifurcation immediately after subclonal diversification (H-I pairs have no identical subclones). Single-nucleus sequencing of Mix_19 (74 tumor nuclei) validates monoclonal origin with gradual CNA accumulation (diploid to aneuploid), and identifies TP53 and GPR114 as founding clone mutations (100% of cells), with SYNE1 and PTPRT as subclonal (80% of cells). The monoclonal origin of combined and mixed cHCC-ICC refutes the hypothesis of collision tumors and supports a common progenitor cell with bipotent differentiation potential.

**Genes / cancer types:** TP53, CTNNB1, RB1, RASA1, FAT2, SYNE1, PTPRT, GPR114 · Combined HCC-ICC

### F4: Nestin overexpression is a diagnostic and prognostic biomarker for cHCC-ICC

**Finding:** Nestin (NES) expression in cHCC-ICC is significantly higher than in HCC (p<0.01) and comparable to ICC (p=0.07) by RNA-seq. IHC validation in 128 cHCC-ICC, 99 HCC, and 86 ICC cases shows 81.3% of cHCC-ICC are Nestin-positive vs. significantly lower rates in HCC and ICC (p<0.001 for both). Nestin-positive cHCC-ICC patients have significantly worse overall survival than Nestin-negative patients.

**Detail:** Nestin marks bipotent hepatic progenitor (oval) cells and its expression is normally repressed by p53 (which transcriptionally represses NES). High TP53 mutation frequency (49%) in cHCC-ICC may relieve this repression, explaining high Nestin positivity. Nestin positivity in cHCC-ICC is also higher than that of HCC or ICC in an independent external cohort (Tschaharganeh et al.), validating the finding. Among all PLC patients pooled, Nestin-positive status predicts worse OS. These data suggest Nestin IHC could be incorporated into the diagnostic workup of hepatic tumors with ambiguous HCC/ICC morphology.

**Genes / cancer types:** NES (Nestin), TP53 · Combined HCC-ICC vs. HCC vs. ICC

### F5: Aristolochic acid (signature 22) and aflatoxin B1 (signature 24) are major mutational forces in Chinese cHCC-ICC

**Finding:** SigProfiler de novo signature analysis identifies three mutational signatures in cHCC-ICC: A (COSMIC signature 22, aristolochic acid, 63.5% of cases), B (COSMIC signature 5, aging/clock-like), and C (COSMIC signature 24, aflatoxin B1/AFB1, 38.8% of cases). TP53 R249S, a marker of AFB1-induced mutagenesis in HBV+ HCC, accounts for 25.8% of TP53 mutations; all such cases are HBV-positive with signature 24. Most recurrent non-coding mutation: NEAT1 lncRNA (29.7%); most common TERT promoter hotspot: C228T (22.9%). Most recurrent fusion: PTMS-AP1G1 (11.7%).

**Detail:** Signature 22 (aristolochic acid) prevalence in Chinese cHCC-ICC, HCC, and ICC suggests endemic exposure to aristolochic acid as a shared risk factor across PLC subtypes in China. This has implications for geographic prevalence of cHCC-ICC. HBV integration analysis (WGS+WES+RNA-seq) identifies TERT and KMT2B as the two most recurrent integrated genes, with HBV integration into LANCL2, PLG, MDGA2, and PTPRM promoters associated with elevated expression. Focal CNA analysis identifies amplifications in MYC (73%), MET (24%), CCNE1 (24%), CDK6 (20%), and TERT (19%), and deletions in TP53 (46%), CDKN2A (37%), RB1 (26%), AXIN1 (25%).

**Genes / cancer types:** TP53 (R249S), TERT, KMT2B, MYC, MET, CCNE1, CDKN2A, NEAT1 · Combined HCC-ICC (Chinese cohort)

## Open Questions

- Can Nestin IHC be prospectively validated as a diagnostic criterion for cHCC-ICC in routine pathologic workup, potentially replacing more complex molecular testing?
- Do combined type (ICC-like/P1) and mixed type (HCC-like/P2) cHCC-ICC respond differently to available HCC therapies (sorafenib, lenvatinib) or ICC therapies (IDH1i, FGFRi, gemcitabine/cisplatin)?
- What is the bipotent progenitor cell identity that gives rise to the bilinear differentiation in monoclonal cHCC-ICC, and can it be identified by lineage tracing?
- Does the high immune infiltration in mixed type cHCC-ICC (P2, "High" immune cluster) predict benefit from PD-1/L1 checkpoint inhibition?
- How does aristolochic acid exposure geographically shape cHCC-ICC incidence and mutational landscape outside East Asia?

## Limitations

- Predominantly Chinese HBV-positive cohort; findings on etiology (aristolochic acid, AFB1) and mutational signatures may not generalize to Western populations with different HBV/HCV/NASH prevalence
- Only 6 separate type cases limit subtype-specific conclusions for this morphology
- Nestin IHC validation was conducted in the same institution's cohort; independent international validation cohorts are needed
- Single-nucleus sequencing was performed on only one mixed type case (Mix_19); broader conclusions about subclonal architecture require larger datasets
- No paired longitudinal samples (pre/post-treatment) available; treatment response data and association with molecular subtypes are absent
