## Summary
This study analyzed 144 patients with advanced melanoma treated with anti-PD1 immune checkpoint blockade (nivolumab or pembrolizumab), with whole-exome sequencing (WES) and whole-transcriptome sequencing on pre-treatment tumor samples. The study found that tumor mutational burden (TMB) as a predictor of anti-PD1 response is confounded by melanoma subtype (cutaneous/occult vs. acral/mucosal), while MHC-II antigen presentation gene expression and MHC-I amplification were novel biomarkers of response. Critically, previous anti-CTLA4 treatment shaped different predictive features, and parsimonious multivariate models were built for each subgroup: MHC-II expression + LDH + lymph node metastases (AUC 0.90, ipilimumab-experienced) and tumor heterogeneity + purity + ploidy (AUC 0.77, ipilimumab-naive).

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: TMB Confounded by Melanoma Subtype—Not Universally Predictive
- **Detail**: Nonsynonymous TMB was significantly higher in responders (CR+PR) versus progressors overall (MWW, P=0.026), but this association was entirely explained by melanoma subtype. Cutaneous/occult melanomas had ~5× higher median TMB than acral/mucosal (297.5 vs. 58 nonsynonymous mutations; P=1.1×10⁻⁶) and also had higher response rates (~40% vs. ~20%). When stratified by subtype, TMB was not significantly associated with response in any subgroup, and in multivariate regression adjusting for melanoma subtype, TMB was not independently predictive (P=0.24). Notably, mucosal/acral responders had lower TMB than cutaneous/occult progressors.
- **Genes/Alterations**: BRAF (39%), NRAS (30%), NF1 (17%); UV signature dominant (69%); alkylating (3%); aging signature (28%)
- **Data availability**: available — TMB, mutation data, melanoma subtype in mel_iatlas_liu_2019
- **Tool support**: native — TMB distribution, mutation burden, subtype filtering; stratified survival analysis
- **Concepts**: #TMB #ICI-biomarker #melanoma #molecular-subtypes #mutation

### Finding 2: MHC-II HLA Expression Predicts Anti-PD1 Response—Especially Post-Ipilimumab
- **Detail**: Expression of all 13 MHC-II-associated HLA genes was significantly higher in responders (collective binomial test, P=0.0002), with four genes (HLA-DMA, HLA-DMB, HLA-DOB×2) individually significant. A composite ssGSEA score of MHC-II HLA genes was higher in responders than progressors overall (MWW P=0.03), and strongly associated in the post-ipilimumab subgroup (P=0.03). Low MHC-II expression was associated with primary resistance in the overall cohort (OR=2.9, P=0.01) and was the strongest predictor in the ipilimumab-treated subgroup (OR=9.9, P=0.02). MHC-II expression was independent of TMB and formed a separate cluster in feature correlation analysis, suggesting an independent predictive mechanism.
- **Genes/Alterations**: HLA-DMA, HLA-DMB, HLA-DOB, HLA-DRA, HLA-DRB (13 MHC-II genes, collectively significant)
- **Data availability**: available — RNA-seq expression data in mel_iatlas_liu_2019; ssGSEA requires custom computation
- **Tool support**: api — expression data accessible via API; custom ssGSEA scoring required for MHC-II composite
- **Concepts**: #ICI-biomarker #immune #melanoma #RNA-seq #PD1

### Finding 3: TAP2 and MHC-I HLA Amplification Exclusively in Responders
- **Detail**: An unbiased analysis of focal gene amplifications identified TAP2 amplification (6p21, MHC-I antigen loading pathway) exclusively in 6 responders (Fisher's exact test, P=0.008). MHC-I HLA gene amplifications (HLA-A, HLA-B, or HLA-C in a ~1.5 Mb region) were also exclusively in 6 responders (P=0.008). Together, 8 patients had amplification of either TAP2 or HLA-A/B/C, and all 8 were responders (P=0.001). LOH of JAK1 was associated with resistance (OR=0.33, P=0.02). B2M LOH was not significantly associated after controlling for responder/progressor status (P=0.37).
- **Genes/Alterations**: TAP2 (amplification, 6 responders only), HLA-A/B/C (amplification, 6 responders only), JAK1 (LOH, resistance OR=0.33), B2M (LOH 16% responders, 25% progressors, NS)
- **Data availability**: available — CNA data (amplifications) in mel_iatlas_liu_2019; JAK1 LOH queryable
- **Tool support**: native — CNA amplification query; Fisher's exact test for amplification vs. response via study exploration
- **Concepts**: #ICI-biomarker #immune #melanoma #copy-number #PD1

### Finding 4: Prior Ipilimumab Exposure Shapes Predictors of Anti-PD1 Response
- **Detail**: The cohort included 60 ipilimumab-pretreated and 84 ipilimumab-naive patients (similar overall response rates, P=0.44). However, the predictors of response differed markedly. IFN-γ and IFN-α response pathways (GSEA) were significantly enriched in responders only in the ipilimumab-pretreated subgroup (q<0.0001) but not in the naive subgroup (q=0.13, 0.996). Low immune infiltrate was a strong predictor of resistance only in the ipilimumab-pretreated group (OR=12.9, P=0.02) but not in the naive group (P=1.00). B cell markers (CD19, CD20/MS4A1) were higher in responders in the pretreated subgroup. These findings suggest prior CTLA4 blockade restructures the tumor immune microenvironment in ways that determine subsequent PD1 responsiveness.
- **Genes/Alterations**: CXCL9, CXCL10, CXCR3, CD3D, CD19, MS4A1/CD20, CD163, TAP2, TGFB2 (differential by ipilimumab status); CTLA4, CD4, FOXP3 higher in ipilimumab-treated responders
- **Data availability**: available — ipilimumab pretreatment status and RNA-seq data in mel_iatlas_liu_2019
- **Tool support**: api — requires subgroup filtering by prior ipilimumab + RNA-seq expression; custom GSEA not native
- **Concepts**: #ICI-biomarker #immune #melanoma #PD1 #RNA-seq

### Finding 5: Tumor Heterogeneity, Ploidy, and Purity Predict Resistance in Ipilimumab-Naive Patients
- **Detail**: Higher tumor heterogeneity (proportion of subclonal mutations) was associated with progression in the overall cohort (MWW P=0.02) and in the ipilimumab-naive subgroup (P=0.057). Lower tumor ploidy and higher tumor purity were also associated with progressors (P=0.04 each). In the ipilimumab-naive predictive model, heterogeneity, purity, and ploidy were independent predictors of PD (AUC 0.77, cross-validation AUC 0.73, empiric P=0.036). Adding TMB to this model did not improve fit (P=0.63). High model scores were associated with dramatically worse survival (median PFS 3.1 vs. 24.7 months).
- **Genes/Alterations**: No specific gene alterations; tumor-level genomic features: heterogeneity (subclonal fraction), ploidy, purity
- **Data availability**: available — purity/ploidy/heterogeneity estimates in mel_iatlas_liu_2019
- **Tool support**: api — purity/ploidy/heterogeneity data accessible; composite model score requires custom implementation
- **Concepts**: #ICI-biomarker #melanoma #biomarker #survival #molecular-subtypes

### Finding 6: Integrative Predictive Models for Primary Resistance by Treatment History
- **Detail**: Two separate parsimonious logistic regression models were built to predict primary resistance (PD as best response). For ipilimumab-treated patients (n=34): low MHC-II HLA expression, high LDH, and absence of lymph node metastases predicted PD (AUC 0.90, CV AUC 0.83). For ipilimumab-naive patients (n=84): high heterogeneity, high purity, and low ploidy predicted PD (AUC 0.77, CV AUC 0.73). The two models had poor cross-performance (AUC ~0.50) in the opposing subgroup, confirming that prior ipilimumab status stratifies which predictors apply. Patients with high model scores had dramatically shorter PFS and OS in both subgroups.
- **Genes/Alterations**: MHC-II expression (composite ssGSEA), LDH (clinical), lymph node metastases (clinical), tumor heterogeneity, ploidy, purity
- **Data availability**: partial — individual features available; composite model requires custom scoring; LDH is clinical data
- **Tool support**: custom — full predictive model requires external implementation; individual features queryable via API
- **Concepts**: #ICI-biomarker #melanoma #biomarker #survival #immune #PD1

## Open Questions
- Can the MHC-II ssGSEA score be implemented as a custom track in cBioPortal to stratify melanoma patients by response probability?
- Does the ipilimumab-experienced predictive model (MHC-II + LDH + LN mets) validate in larger pooled melanoma immunotherapy cohorts available in cBioPortal or TCGA?
- Can co-mutation analysis of BRAF/NRAS/NF1 subgroups in mel_iatlas_liu_2019 reveal differential immune microenvironment features associated with anti-PD1 response?

## Limitations
- Moderate cohort size (n=144 WES, n=121 RNA-seq) limits power to detect individual gene-level predictors after multiple hypothesis correction
- Validation cohorts available were smaller and lacked complete molecular and clinical data (especially prior ipilimumab status)
- Bulk RNA-seq cannot distinguish tumor-cell-intrinsic MHC-II expression from antigen-presenting cells in the microenvironment
- No single-cell RNA-seq data; immune cell deconvolution is imprecise with bulk methods
- Clinical heterogeneity (two anti-PD1 drugs, mixed treatment lines, mixed sites of disease) may introduce confounding
