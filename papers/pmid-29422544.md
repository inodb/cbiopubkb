## Summary

First genome-wide (WES + CNA) characterization of 15 vulvar squamous cell carcinomas (6 HPV+, 9 HPV−) from Korean patients. The study identifies that HPV(−) tumors carry significantly more somatic mutations and driver mutations than HPV(+) tumors, but CNA loads are similar between subtypes. PIK3CA is the most frequently altered gene overall (60% combining mutations and copy gains), and FAT1 truncating mutations/copy losses are exclusively HPV(−). Mutational signatures match COSMIC Signatures 1 (aging/CpG deamination) and 2 (APOBEC), also seen in cervical SCC—the most similar SCC type by signature. Kataegis (localized hypermutation) at chr16 was found exclusively in 2 HPV(+) cases. CNA patterns diverge by HPV status: 3q gain (83%) predominates in HPV(+); 7p/8q gains and 2q loss in HPV(−). Copy-neutral LOH (uniparental disomy) was found in 4 cases (31%) at loci harboring TP53, PIK3CA, and CASP8. Full data available in `vsc_cuk_2018` on cBioPortal.

## Key Findings

### F1: HPV(−) vulvar SCCs carry markedly higher mutational loads than HPV(+) tumors

**Finding:** HPV(−) SCCs had significantly more total somatic mutations (avg 256 vs 117 per tumor, P=0.03), nonsilent mutations (175 vs 85 per tumor, P=0.03), and putative driver mutations (10.6 vs 2.8 per tumor, P=0.0002). Mutation allele frequency was lower in HPV(−) tumors, consistent with a heterogeneous subclonal architecture.

**Detail:** Mean mutation rate across the cohort was 5.6/Mb, higher than cervical SCC (3.0), HNSC (3.3), and esophageal SCC (2.8), but lower than skin SCC (12.5). The dominant base substitution type was C>T (31% overall). HPV oncoproteins E6/E7 may functionally substitute for somatic driver mutations, rendering HPV(+) cells less dependent on additional genomic lesions for tumor progression.

**Genes / cancer types:** TP53, PIK3CA, HRAS, CDKN2A, APC, FBXW7, BRCA2, RB1, FAT1 · Vulvar squamous cell carcinoma

### F2: PIK3CA is the dominant driver, altered in ~60% of vulvar SCCs via mutation and/or copy gain

**Finding:** PIK3CA hotspot mutations (E545K in 2 cases) combined with focal 3q26.32 copy gains give a combined PIK3CA alteration rate of ~60%. Four HPV(+) cases showed concurrent copy gain at the PIK3CA locus; 2 of these also had concurrent hotspot mutations. Two HPV(−) SCCs also showed PIK3CA gains. The locus at 3q25–q29 was gained in 83% of HPV(+) SCCs, the most recurrent CNA in this subtype, and also harbors SOX2, TP63, BCL6, MECOM, and ETV5.

**Detail:** Where both mutation and copy gain co-occurred at PIK3CA, allele frequencies were elevated (MAF 49% in one case vs. background of 24%), suggesting the mutation and amplification may be on the same allele. Copy-neutral LOH at 3q11.2-q29 was also identified in one case, potentially homozygosing preexisting PIK3CA mutations. APOBEC-mediated cytosine deamination has been specifically linked to PIK3CA helical domain mutations in HPV-infected cells.

**Genes / cancer types:** PIK3CA, SOX2, TP63, BCL6, MECOM · Vulvar squamous cell carcinoma

### F3: FAT1 loss-of-function mutations and copy losses are exclusive to HPV(−) vulvar SCCs

**Finding:** FAT1 alterations (truncating mutations + copy losses) were found in 40% of all tumors but exclusively in HPV(−) cases. Three HPV(−) SCCs had nonsense mutations (p.W4175X, p.S3268X, p.Q1455X) in the cadherin and cytoplasmic domains; three additional HPV(−) SCCs had FAT1 copy losses at 4q35.2. No FAT1 alterations were found in any HPV(+) tumor.

**Detail:** FAT1 inactivation leads to aberrant Wnt pathway activation. Recurrent truncating FAT1 mutations in the same cadherin domain positions have been reported in head/neck SCC (where they associate with better survival in HPV(−) patients), cervical adenocarcinoma, and glioblastoma. FAT1 mutations are also significant in that they frequently co-occur with CASP8 inactivation in other SCC types (synergistic effect demonstrated in OSCC).

**Genes / cancer types:** FAT1, CASP8, SMAD2 · Vulvar squamous cell carcinoma (HPV negative)

### F4: Mutational signatures match aging (S1) and APOBEC (S2); kataegis in 2 HPV(+) cases

**Finding:** NMF decomposition of mutation spectra revealed two COSMIC signatures: Signature 1 (age-related CpG deamination, dominant in 3 cases) and Signature 2 (APOBEC cytidine deaminase, dominant in 3 cases). Signature patterns were not significantly different between HPV(+) and HPV(−) tumors. Cosine similarity analysis showed vulvar SCC signatures most closely resemble cervical SCC among other SCC types. Kataegis (localized hypermutation, inter-mutational distance ≤10 kb) at chr16p12.3-q22.2 was found exclusively in 2 HPV(+) cases.

**Detail:** The kataegis region co-localizes with a copy gain of DHX38, an RNA helicase gene. Kataegis mutations were predominantly TpCpN C>T/C>G transitions, consistent with APOBEC activity. APOBEC enzymes are induced in HPV-infected cells, and APOBEC activity is specifically associated with HPV(+) head/neck cancer. The aging-associated Signature 1 predates oncogenic transformation (clonal), while Signature 2 continues to drive subclonal diversification.

**Genes / cancer types:** APOBEC3A/B (inferred), DHX38 · Vulvar squamous cell carcinoma

### F5: CNA profiles diverge by HPV status; HPV(−) tumors share gains at 7p/8q and loss at 2q

**Finding:** 590 total CNAs (334 gains, 256 losses) were identified in 13 evaluable tumors. CNA load (number and length) did not differ significantly between HPV(+) and HPV(−) subgroups (P=0.53 and P=0.75). The most recurrent CNA in HPV(+) was gain at 3q25.33-q29 (83%), while HPV(−) showed 7p gain (57%), 8q gain (57%), and 2q loss (57%). Novel CNAs included 13q, 18p-q, 20p, 20q gains in HPV(+) and 7p, 10q, 14q, 21p-q gains in HPV(−).

**Detail:** The 3q gain in HPV(+) cases encompasses PIK3CA (3q26.32), SOX2, TP63, and TERT. The 8q gain (also seen in 86% of HPV(−) SCCs in prior studies) encompasses MYC. Cancer-related genes in recurrent gain regions include FOXL2, BCL2, SMAD2, GNAS. Copy-neutral LOH was found at 2q33.1-q37.3, 3q11.2-q29, 7p22.3-p11.2, 7q22.1-q36.3, 9q22.2-q34.3, and 17p13.3-p11.2 in 4 cases, harboring TP53, PIK3CA, NOTCH1, CASP8, EGFR, BRAF, FOXL2, and TP63.

**Genes / cancer types:** PIK3CA, SOX2, TP63, TERT, BCL2, FOXL2, EGFR, NOTCH1, TP53 · Vulvar squamous cell carcinoma

## Open Questions

- Does the near-universal PIK3CA alteration rate (60%) make PI3K/mTOR inhibitors a rational therapeutic strategy in vulvar SCC, particularly for HPV(+) patients who lack other driver mutations?
- Are FAT1 alterations a predictive biomarker of improved survival in HPV(−) vulvar SCC, as suggested for HPV(−) HNSC?
- What is the clinical significance of the recurrent kataegis at the DHX38 locus in HPV(+) cases — is this a driver or passenger phenomenon?
- Does copy-neutral LOH at TP53 and PIK3CA loci represent a clinically important mechanism of biallelic inactivation beyond what is detectable by standard mutation calling?
- Can TERT promoter mutations (a sensitive early marker in other SCC types) serve as a urine or liquid biopsy biomarker for vulvar SCC surveillance?

## Limitations

- Very small cohort (n=15) limits statistical power for all comparisons; no replication cohort available
- Predominantly FFPE material (14/15 cases) may introduce artifact and reduce detection sensitivity for low-frequency variants
- Two samples (VSCC9 and VSCC15) had insufficient CNA quality and were excluded from CNA analysis
- No RNA-seq data; gene expression effects of CNAs and mutations remain unvalidated
- Predominantly Korean patient population; representation of Western vulvar SCC populations is limited
- Lack of survival data or treatment response information precludes prognostic analysis
