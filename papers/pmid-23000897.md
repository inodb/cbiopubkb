## Summary

TCGA multi-platform analysis of 825 primary breast cancers (466 with five-platform data overlap: SNP 6.0 CNA arrays, Illumina Infinium methylation, Agilent mRNA arrays, miRNA-seq, WES; 348 with additional RPPA). The study is the definitive molecular portrait of breast cancer intrinsic subtypes, establishing that Luminal A, Luminal B, HER2-Enriched (HER2E), and Basal-like cancers each have distinct mutation spectra, CNA patterns, methylation profiles, and protein expression signatures. Only three genes (TP53, PIK3CA, GATA3) are mutated at >10% pan-breast frequency; most SMGs are subtype-specific. Basal-like breast cancer is shown to share extensive molecular features with high-grade serous ovarian cancer, suggesting common biology and treatment opportunities. Full data in `brca_tcga_pub` on cBioPortal. Survival analysis was deferred due to short median follow-up (17 months) at time of publication.

## Key Findings

### F1: Four breast cancer subtypes confirmed by multi-platform "Cluster of Clusters" analysis

**Finding:** Integration of CNA, methylation, mRNA, miRNA, and RPPA data via "Cluster of Clusters" (C of C) consensus clustering reproducibly identifies four major subtypes (Luminal A, Luminal B, HER2E, Basal-like), with Basal-like being the most molecularly distinct across all platforms. The four subtypes are driven by different genetic and epigenetic mechanisms, with clinical plasticity occurring largely within subtypes rather than across them.

**Detail:** PAM50 mRNA subtype is highly concordant with unsupervised multi-platform clustering. Basal-like tumors cluster together on every individual platform. The C of C approach remains stable when mRNA data is excluded, or when 10-subtype CNA classifications (Curtis et al.) are substituted, confirming robustness. Two novel RPPA-defined "Reactive" subgroups (Reactive I from Luminal A, Reactive II from mixed subtypes) are identified based on microenvironmental/stromal protein signatures (fibronectin, caveolin 1, collagen VI).

**Genes / cancer types:** ESR1, GATA3, FOXA1, TP53, ERBB2, BRCA1 · Breast cancer (all subtypes)

### F2: Subtype-specific mutation landscapes: PIK3CA in Luminal A, TP53 in Basal-like

**Finding:** Only TP53, PIK3CA, and GATA3 are mutated at >10% pan-breast frequency. SMGs are highly subtype-specific: Luminal A is enriched for PIK3CA (45%), MAP3K1, GATA3, CDH1, MAP2K4; Basal-like for TP53 (80%) with near-absence of other Luminal SMGs (PIK3CA only 9%); HER2E for TP53 (72%) + PIK3CA (39%); Luminal B for TP53 and PIK3CA (~29–32% each).

**Detail:** TP53 mutations in Basal-like are predominantly nonsense/frameshift (loss-of-function), while Luminal A TP53 mutations are missense-enriched. PIK3CA E545K mutation occurs almost exclusively in Luminal A (25/27 cases). GATA3 hot-spot 2bp deletion (intron 4) is Luminal A-specific; exon 5 frameshift mutations occur in Luminal B. CDH1 mutations are enriched in lobular histology (30/36 CDH1-mutant tumors are lobular). Novel SMGs include TBX3, RUNX1, CBFB, PIK3R1, SF3B1, NF1, PTPRD, PTPN22, CCND3. ~10% of apparent sporadic breast cancers harbor germline pathogenic variants in ATM, BRCA1, BRCA2, BRIP1, CHEK2, NBN, PTEN, RAD51C, or TP53.

**Genes / cancer types:** PIK3CA, TP53, GATA3, CDH1, MAP3K1, MAP2K4, ERBB2, TBX3, RUNX1, NF1 · Breast cancer (subtype-specific)

**Data availability:** `available`

### F3: HER2E subtype dissected — two biologically distinct HER2+ populations within clinical HER2+ tumors

**Finding:** Only ~50% of clinically HER2-positive tumors fall into the HER2E mRNA subtype; the remainder are Luminal-mRNA/HER2+. These two groups differ substantially: HER2E/HER2+ tumors show high HER1/p-HER1/HER2/p-HER2 by RPPA, high EGFR, FGFR4, GRB7, and TP53 mutations (72%); Luminal/HER2+ tumors show high ESR1, GATA3, BCL2 expression and TP53 mutation rate similar to other Luminal tumors.

**Detail:** The two HER2+ populations are distinguishable by 302 differentially expressed genes (q=0%). HER2E/HER2+ tumors show the highest overall mutation rate of any subtype and the highest aneuploidy. PIK3CA mutations in HER2E are 39%; PTEN and INPP4B loss also common — suggesting combined HER2 + PI3K inhibition strategies. Clinical response to anti-HER2 therapies (trastuzumab, pertuzumab) may differ between the two HER2+ subgroups and should be tested prospectively. Pertuzumab targeting the HER2-HER3 heterodimer is of particular interest given HER3 mutations also identified.

**Genes / cancer types:** ERBB2 (HER2), ERBB3 (HER3), EGFR (HER1), PIK3CA, PTEN, INPP4B, TP53, GATA3, ESR1 · Breast cancer (HER2+, HER2E subtype)

### F4: Basal-like breast cancer shares extensive molecular features with high-grade serous ovarian cancer

**Finding:** Basal-like breast tumors show striking molecular similarities to high-grade serous ovarian tumors: TP53 mutations in >80%, RB1 pathway inactivation, BRCA1 pathway loss, high genomic instability (MYC amplification 40%, 5q loss, 1q/10p gain), and a proliferation/FOXM1 transcriptional signature. This shared biology suggests potential cross-tumor therapeutic strategies.

**Detail:** PARADIGM pathway analysis identifies hyperactivated FOXM1 as the key proliferation driver in Basal-like; cMYC and HIF1α/ARNT network hubs also hyperactivated. MEMo analysis confirms RB1 loss, BRCA1 loss, and TP53 mutation as co-occurring Basal-like features. PI3K pathway activation in Basal-like occurs primarily through PTEN loss (35%) and INPP4B loss (30%) rather than PIK3CA mutation (7%) — explaining why PIK3CA mutations alone underestimate PI3K pathway activation in this subtype. PARP inhibitors (for germline BRCA1/2) and platinum agents are the main chemotherapy opportunities.

**Genes / cancer types:** TP53, BRCA1, BRCA2, RB1, PTEN, INPP4B, MYC, FOXM1, CDKN2A · Basal-like breast cancer; high-grade serous ovarian cancer (cross-cancer)

### F5: Luminal/ER+ breast cancer: PIK3CA mutations prevalent but PI3K pathway not uniformly activated at protein level

**Finding:** PIK3CA mutations are most frequent in Luminal A (45%), yet pAKT, pS6, and p4EBP1 (canonical PI3K pathway readouts) by RPPA are not elevated in PIK3CA-mutated Luminal A tumors — they are highest in Basal-like and HER2E. PI3K pathway activation in Basal-like and HER2E correlates with INPP4B/PTEN loss and PIK3CA amplification rather than PIK3CA point mutation.

**Detail:** MAP3K1 and MAP2K4 are inactivating-mutated in 12% of Luminal A tumors (two contiguous steps of p38/JNK1 stress kinase pathway); their inactivation is mutually exclusive with each other and largely restricted to Luminal tumors. Cyclin D1 amplification is especially common in Luminal B (58%) vs. Luminal A (29%), consistent with Luminal B's higher proliferation rate. FOXA1 and GATA3 mutations are mutually exclusive in Luminal/ER+ tumors; both regulate the ER transcriptional program. RB1 expression is highest in Luminal A (best prognosis), TP53 pathway is intact in most Luminal A tumors.

**Genes / cancer types:** PIK3CA, MAP3K1, MAP2K4, GATA3, FOXA1, CDH1, CCND1 (Cyclin D1), RB1, TP53 · Luminal A and Luminal B breast cancer

## Open Questions

- Why does PIK3CA mutation in Luminal A not correlate with PI3K pathway activation at the protein level, and does this affect response to PI3K inhibitors (alpelisib)?
- Do the two clinically HER2+ subgroups (HER2E vs. Luminal/HER2+) respond differently to pertuzumab + trastuzumab?
- Can FOXM1 or MYC pathway hyperactivation in Basal-like tumors be exploited therapeutically (e.g., aurora kinase, CDK4/6 inhibitors)?
- What is the clinical significance of novel SMGs TBX3, RUNX1, CBFB, and SF3B1 in breast cancer — are they actionable or prognostic?
- Does the molecular convergence of Basal-like breast and high-grade serous ovarian cancer extend to shared therapeutic vulnerabilities (e.g., PARP inhibitors in BRCA-proficient Basal-like tumors)?

## Limitations

- Median follow-up was only 17 months at time of publication; survival analyses were deferred to subsequent publication
- 466 tumors had complete five-platform data; analyses requiring all platforms are conducted on a subset
- mRNA subtype uses PAM50 (50-gene classifier) rather than unsupervised discovery; Normal-like (n=8) and Claudin-low (n=8) too small for subtype analyses
- Germline pathogenic variant data (BRCA1/2, ATM, CHEK2) from a pre-specified gene list only; genome-wide germline analysis not performed
- RPPA platform (171 antibodies) provides selected proteome coverage; phosphoprotein cross-reactivity between related HER-family members is possible
