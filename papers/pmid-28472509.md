## Summary

A multicenter phase II trial (NCT00588523) evaluates induction TMZ (6 cycles, 200 mg/m²) followed by myeloablative high-dose chemotherapy (thiotepa + busulfan) and autologous stem cell transplant (ASCT) in 41 newly diagnosed anaplastic oligodendroglioma (AO)/oligoastrocytoma (AOA) patients, with the goal of deferring radiotherapy. Among 21 transplanted patients (all 1p/19q codeleted), 2-year PFS is 85.7% and 5-year OS is 100%. In the 33 codeleted patients, 5-year PFS is 50.3% and 5-year OS is 93.4%. NGS via MSK-IMPACT identifies typical oligodendroglioma molecular features (TERT promoter 95%, IDH1 68%, CIC 53%, FUBP1 26%) and glioblastoma-like signatures in non-codeleted tumours. Genomic data are publicly available in cBioPortal as `odg_msk_2017`.

## Key Findings

### F1: TMZ induction followed by myeloablative HDC-ASCT achieves 2-year PFS of 85.7% and 5-year OS of 100% in transplanted 1p/19q codeleted AO patients

**Finding:** Among 21 transplanted patients (all 1p/19q codeleted), 2-year PFS was 85.7% (95% CI 62%–95.2%) and 5-year PFS was 60.4% (95% CI 36%–78%). Median PFS was not reached; no transplanted patient has died (5-year OS 100%) after median follow-up of 65.7 months. The regimen was well tolerated with no toxic deaths.

**Detail:** The myeloablative regimen consisted of thiotepa 250 mg/m²/day × 3 days followed by busulfan 3.2 mg/kg/day × 3 days before ASCT. Median time to neutrophil engraftment was 9 days (range 8–12); platelet engraftment 12 days (range 9–18). Grade ≥3 toxicities included febrile neutropenia (n=5), ALT/AST elevation (n=3), and grade 4 infection/colitis (n=1 each). Among all 1p/19q codeleted patients (N=33, including those who did not proceed to transplant), 5-year PFS was 50.3% and 5-year OS was 93.4%. At 2 years, 82% of codeleted patients were alive without radiotherapy; 52% remained RT-free at 5 years.

**Genes / cancer types:** `IDH1`, `TERT`, `CIC`, `FUBP1` · Anaplastic oligodendroglioma (1p/19q codeleted)

### F2: 1p/19q codeletion is confirmed as the dominant predictor of PFS and OS — non-codeleted tumours display glioblastoma-like molecular features

**Finding:** 1p/19q codeletion (detected in 33/39 assessable patients, 84.6%) is a significant predictor of both PFS (p=0.03) and OS (p<0.001). Non-codeleted tumours (n=6) in the NGS subset displayed glioblastoma-like signatures: wild-type IDH1/2, EGFR amplification and mutation, NF1 mutation, and MDM2 amplification — without 1p/19q loss. One patient initially classified as codeleted by FISH was found to harbour a GBM-like signature (PTEN/CDKN2B/CDKN2A/p14ARF loss, no IDH1/2, no 1p/19q), suggesting FISH false-positive; that patient progressed at the first MRI and died at 13 months.

**Detail:** The molecular divergence between codeleted and non-codeleted oligodendroglial tumours confirms that 1p/19q codeletion is a defining genomic event, not merely a prognostic marker. The identification of a false-positive 1p/19q FISH case underscores the importance of integrating NGS (IDH, CIC, FUBP1 mutation status) with cytogenetic testing. Non-codeleted "oligodendrogliomas" may represent misdiagnosed GBM IDH wild-type and should be managed accordingly.

### F3: Typical 1p/19q codeleted oligodendrogliomas share a canonical mutational landscape — TERT (95%), IDH1 (68%), CIC (53%), FUBP1 (26%)

**Finding:** Among 14 NGS-characterised 1p/19q codeleted patients, TERT promoter mutation is the most frequent abnormality (18/19 overall, 95%), followed by IDH1 (13/19, 68%), CIC (10/19, 53%), NOTCH1 (6/19, 32%), NOTCH2 (5/19, 26%), and FUBP1 (5/19, 26%). None of these mutations were individually predictive of PFS or OS. Wide molecular heterogeneity exists for non-IDH actionable mutations.

**Detail:** MSK-IMPACT (410-gene panel) was applied to 19 patients with available tissue. The high frequency of TERT promoter mutations confirms prior reports; TERT mutations co-occur with IDH1 in the canonical oligodendroglioma pathway. CIC and FUBP1 are paralogues disrupted by mutation in oligodendrogliomas specifically. NOTCH1/2 mutations are identified at high frequency (32% and 26%), suggesting NOTCH pathway dysregulation as a candidate therapeutic target in this disease. OncoKB annotation was applied to identify potentially actionable mutations — aside from IDH1, actionable mutations are rare and variable, reinforcing the molecular heterogeneity.

### F4: TMZ induction outperforms PCV induction for OS in an exploratory comparison — suggesting TMZ as the preferred induction agent for this chemotherapy-transplant strategy

**Finding:** Exploratory unplanned comparison with a prior PCV induction + identical ASCT cohort (N=20) showed that PCV-treated patients had significantly shorter median OS among all patients (median 49.8 months, HR 3.38, p=0.005) and among 1p/19q codeleted patients (median OS not reached but HR 5.68, p=0.018). PFS difference was not statistically significant for codeleted patients (HR 1.54, p=0.34).

**Detail:** Both cohorts used identical myeloablative regimens (thiotepa + busulfan) and ASCT. The OS difference favoring TMZ may reflect lower systemic toxicity of TMZ relative to PCV (procarbazine, CCNU, vincristine), better tolerability, or patient selection differences between the two studies. The data suggest that TMZ is a viable — and possibly superior — induction agent for patients proceeding to HDC-ASCT in newly diagnosed AO. This finding should be interpreted cautiously given the unplanned, non-randomised nature of the comparison.

**Genes / cancer types:** `IDH1`, `TERT`, `CIC` · 1p/19q codeleted anaplastic oligodendroglioma

### F5: Chemotherapy-only approach successfully defers radiotherapy — 82% of codeleted patients radiation-free at 2 years and 52% at 5 years

**Finding:** In patients with 1p/19q codeleted tumours, the median time to radiotherapy was not reached. 82% of codeleted patients (95% CI: 62%–92%) were alive without RT at 2 years; 52% (95% CI: 30%–74%) remained RT-free at 5 years. This demonstrates the feasibility of deferring radiation — and its associated late neurocognitive toxicities — using a chemotherapy-only approach in newly diagnosed codeleted AO.

**Detail:** The motivation for RT deferral is the cumulative neurocognitive toxicity of cranial irradiation, which is especially relevant in young oligodendroglioma patients expected to survive for decades. At the time of progression, patients were referred for RT. The strategy mirrors the approach of EORTC 26951 and RTOG 9402 trials, which showed RT+PCV dramatically improves OS in codeleted AO, but at the cost of RT-related morbidity. A chemotherapy-only approach could achieve durable disease control without RT in a subset, preserving neurocognitive function. The 5-year 50% RT-free rate suggests roughly half of codeleted patients ultimately require RT.

## Open Questions

1. Would TMZ followed by HDC-ASCT compare favourably with the current standard of RT + PCV in a randomised phase III trial for newly diagnosed 1p/19q codeleted AO?
2. Can molecular markers (IDH1 variant, CIC mutation type, NOTCH status) identify the ~50% of codeleted patients who remain RT-free at 5 years vs. those who ultimately require RT?
3. Do NOTCH1/2 mutations in oligodendrogliomas predict sensitivity to emerging NOTCH pathway inhibitors?
4. What is the long-term neurocognitive outcome in transplanted survivors, and does radiation deferral preserve cognitive function compared to upfront RT + PCV cohorts?
5. Can a smaller fraction of patients with molecular high-risk features (e.g., MYC amplification, CDKN2A deletion within codeleted tumours) be identified who would benefit from earlier RT despite codeletion?

## Limitations

- Small sample size (n=41 enrolled, n=21 transplanted, n=19 with NGS) limits statistical power for genomic subgroup analyses; mutation-outcome correlations are underpowered.
- Non-randomised design precludes definitive comparisons with RT+PCV; the TMZ vs. PCV comparison is unplanned and potentially confounded by patient selection.
- MSK-IMPACT (410 genes) does not cover TERT promoter in its original version — TERT frequency may be underestimated depending on panel version used; transcriptome and methylation data are absent.
- Extended follow-up beyond the current 5-year horizon is needed given the very long expected survival (>14 years reported with RT+PCV in EORTC/RTOG trials).
- Single-arm design with no contemporaneous control; comparisons to historical controls from other trials are subject to era effects and patient selection biases.
