## Summary

This single-arm phase II trial (NCT02768701) enrolled 40 patients with heavily pretreated metastatic TNBC to evaluate whether a single low-dose priming cyclophosphamide (Cy, 300 mg/m²) could deplete intratumoral regulatory T cells (Tregs) and sensitize tumors to pembrolizumab. The primary endpoints were progression-free survival (PFS) and reduction in peripheral blood Tregs. Cy did not significantly deplete Tregs (−3.3%, p=0.19), and Tregs rebounded significantly after cycle 1 (+21%, p=0.005). Median PFS was 1.8 months and ORR was 21%. Immune-related adverse events (IRT) correlated with response (p=0.02). Elevated baseline B cell gene expression signatures and BCR repertoire diversity — not Treg depletion or TMB — were the strongest correlates of clinical response, reinforcing the emerging role of B cells in anti-PD-1 immunotherapy.

## Key Findings

### F1: Low-dose cyclophosphamide did not deplete peripheral blood Tregs; Tregs rebounded significantly after pembrolizumab cycle 1

**Finding:** A single 300 mg/m² IV dose of cyclophosphamide administered one day before pembrolizumab failed to significantly reduce peripheral blood CD45+/FoxP3+/CD4+/CD127⁻/ˡᵒ Tregs. Treg numbers increased significantly after initiation of pembrolizumab, peaking by cycle 2.

**Detail:** Peripheral blood Tregs were measured by flow cytometry on C1D1 (pre-Cy), C1D2 (pre-pembrolizumab), and D1 of subsequent cycles. The median change from C1D1 to C1D2 was −3.3% (p=0.19, not significant). From C1D1 to C2D1, Tregs increased 10% (p=0.03); from C1D2 to C2D1, +22% increase (p=0.005). Patients whose Tregs decreased after Cy trended toward improved response vs. those whose Tregs increased (p=0.08, not significant). Changes in Tregs at either timepoint were not associated with overall clinical response (p>0.2). This contrasts with the preclinical premise: intratumoral Treg depletion was observed in murine TNBC models, but peripheral blood Treg levels may not reflect intratumoral dynamics.

**Genes / features:** FOXP3, CD4, CD25, CD127 (Treg markers)

### F2: ORR 21%, median PFS 1.8 months, OS 6.3 months; immune-related toxicity (IRT) significantly associated with response

**Finding:** The combination of Cy priming + pembrolizumab yielded a 21% ORR (1 CR + 7 PR) with a median DoR of 20.4 months among responders. Immune-related toxicity (IRT) correlated with clinical response (p=0.02), while prior lines of therapy and Treg change did not.

**Detail:** 39 patients were evaluable for response (1 non-evaluable). Best responses: CR=1 (2%), PR=7 (18%), SD=3 (8%), PD=28 (72%). Median PFS was 1.8 months (95% CI 1.4–2.3), not reaching the pre-specified alternative hypothesis of 2.9 months nor differing from the null (1.9 months). Median OS was 6.3 months (95% CI 2.8–9.3). At 29 months median follow-up, 88% of patients had died. 10 of 40 patients (25%) experienced any-grade IRT (rash n=6, colitis n=3, hypothyroidism n=3, xerostomia n=3). IRT was significantly associated with response (Fisher's exact, p=0.02); nearly all IRT patients were on therapy >6 months. The CR patient had durable response ≥19 months with high TMB (>20 mut/Mb).

**Genes / features:** `TP53` (54% of WES patients), `BRCA2` (3 patients; all experienced PD)

### F3: Baseline B cell gene expression and BCR diversity — not PD-L1, TMB, or Treg change — correlate with clinical response

**Finding:** Patients with elevated pretreatment B cell metagene signatures in tumor tissue and higher circulating BCR repertoire diversity were significantly more likely to respond to Cy/pembrolizumab and to experience IRT.

**Detail:** RNA-seq on FFPE pretreatment tumor samples identified differential gene expression between responders (CR+PR) and non-responders (SD+PD): ANKRD30A, SYCE1, and TUBB2B were higher in responders; KRT1, NRAP, and KRT2 were higher in non-responders. B cell metagene signatures (derived from immune gene sets) were associated with both response and IRT. Peripheral blood BCR repertoire diversity (pre-treatment and post-therapy) also correlated with response. By contrast: PD-L1 positivity (1% or 10% thresholds) was not significantly associated with response or clinical benefit (Wilcoxon, p>0.37). TMB was not associated with CB or response (Wilcoxon, p>0.288). PAM50 subtype (majority basal-like) was not associated with response (Fisher's exact, p>0.29). GSEA and immune gene signatures did not yield significant associations beyond B cell signatures.

**Genes / features:** `ANKRD30A`, `SYCE1`, `TUBB2B` (higher in responders); `KRT1`, `NRAP`, `KRT2` (higher in non-responders); B cell metagene

### F4: TP53 most frequently mutated (54%); BRCA2 mutations associated with lack of benefit; uncommon mutations in ARID1A, PIK3CA, BAP1 observed

**Finding:** In 26 WES-profiled pretreatment tumors, TP53 was mutated in 54%, consistent with TNBC biology. BRCA2 mutations (3 patients) uniformly showed progressive disease. Mutations in immunosuppressive TME-associated genes (ARID1A, PIK3CA, BAP1) were present but not statistically linked to outcome in this small cohort.

**Detail:** WES was performed on FFPE tumor with matched PBMC normal. Somatic calls required ≥5 supporting reads, ≥40× depth, or ≥10 reads at ≥80× depth for low-MAF events; C>T and G>A artifacts required ≥10% MAF. TP53 was the most frequently mutated cancer gene (14/26, 54%). Three patients with BRCA2 mutations each had PD as best response. The mutation profile included multiple uncommon mutations previously linked to immunosuppressive TME features: ARID1A (dampening interferon responses), PIK3CA (decreased T cell infiltration), and BAP1 (decreased T cell infiltration). No somatic mutations were statistically associated with clinical response in this study. One patient with CR had TMB >20 mut/Mb, but TMB overall was not prognostic.

**Genes / features:** `TP53`, `BRCA2`, `ARID1A`, `PIK3CA`, `BAP1`

### F5: CyTOF immunophenotyping reveals peripheral blood immune differences between responders and non-responders across treatment timepoints

**Finding:** High-dimensional mass cytometry (CyTOF) of peripheral blood from 16 patients (8 responders, 8 non-responders) across early, middle, and late treatment timepoints revealed dynamic immunophenotypic differences associated with response to Cy/pembrolizumab.

**Detail:** 43-marker CyTOF panels were applied to PBMCs collected at 43 total timepoints, grouped as early (cycles 1–2), middle (cycles 3–5), and late (cycle 6+). Specific immunophenotypic differences between responders and non-responders were characterized but not detailed numerically in the main text — this analysis was presented as supporting correlative evidence for immune activation in responders. Together with the BCR/TCR repertoire data, the CyTOF results support a model in which B cell-driven humoral immunity, rather than Treg depletion, is the dominant determinant of response in this setting.

**Genes / features:** Peripheral blood immune cell populations (43-marker CyTOF panel)

**Data availability:** `not-available`

## Open Questions

- If intratumoral Treg depletion (vs. peripheral blood depletion) is essential, can tumor biopsies or imaging agents confirm that 300 mg/m² Cy reaches intratumoral Tregs at biologically effective concentrations in mTNBC?
- Do the B cell metagene signatures associated with response in this study overlap with the "B cell-rich tertiary lymphoid structures" (TLS) gene signatures being evaluated in larger TNBC trials — and could TLS status serve as a prospective patient selection tool?
- BRCA2 mutations (3 patients, all PD) — is this a negative predictive biomarker for anti-PD-1 in TNBC, possibly via homologous recombination deficiency creating a distinct immunosuppressive TME?
- Could higher Cy doses or repeat Cy dosing more durably deplete Tregs, or do tumor-resident Tregs recover through recruitment rather than peripheral expansion?
- The median DoR for responders was 20.4 months — is this patient subset (B cell-rich, IRT-prone) identifiable prospectively to enrich future trials of pembrolizumab monotherapy?

## Limitations

- Small single-arm phase II trial (n=40); lacks a pembrolizumab-alone control arm to assess additive value of Cy
- Peripheral blood Treg depletion is a surrogate for intratumoral Treg depletion — no paired tumor biopsies post-Cy to confirm intratumoral effect
- FFPE tissue availability for correlative analyses was limited (WES: n=26; RNA-seq: subset); selection bias possible for patients with adequate tissue
- PD-L1 assessment performed on archival primary (n=13) or metastatic lesions (n=8), not synchronous with treatment — spatial and temporal heterogeneity not captured
- Study enrolled heavily pretreated patients (median 2 prior metastatic lines; 42% had ≥4 lines); results may not generalize to earlier-line TNBC
- B cell associations are hypothesis-generating; prospective validation in larger cohorts required
