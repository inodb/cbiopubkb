## Summary

The ChangKang (Healthy Bowel) Project performed ultradeep whole-exome sequencing (WES) on 1,015 Chinese CRC patients, generating one of the largest single-centre CRC WES cohorts with rich clinical annotation and long-term follow-up (median 68.8 months). The study identifies 46 high-confidence significantly mutated genes (HC-SMGs), 8 of which are novel (LYST, DAPK1, CR2, KIF16B, NPIPB15, SYTL2, ZNF91, KIAA0586). A four-subtype genomic classification (hypermutated, CIN-high-risk, CIN-low-risk, genome stable) with distinct survival outcomes is established. Oncogenic alterations in cell cycle and TGF-beta pathways are independent prognostic factors, and high mitochondrial DNA copy number (mScore-high, top 10%) independently predicts worse overall survival. The dataset is available in cBioPortal as `crc_sysucc_2022`.

## Key Findings

### F1: 46 HC-SMGs identified, including 8 novel genes not in current cancer gene databases

**Finding:** Multi-tool integrative approach identifies 46 high-confidence significantly mutated genes; 8 (LYST, DAPK1, CR2, KIF16B, NPIPB15, SYTL2, ZNF91, KIAA0586) are unreported in CGC, IntOGen, OncoKB, TCGA, or Vogelstein et al., mutated collectively in 14.9% of patients.

**Detail:** Standard driver genes (TP53, APC, KRAS, FBXW7, CTNNB1) head the list as expected. Five complementary algorithms were combined (dNdScv, MutSigCV, OncodriveCLUSTL, OncodriveFM, e-driver); only genes identified by ≥2 tools were retained. 83.6% of patients harbour ≥1 mutation in HC-SMGs (mean 4.49 mutations/patient). The 8 novel genes are absent from currently deployed clinical panels, suggesting WES or extended panels are needed for comprehensive CRC profiling.

**Genes / cancer types:** `APC`, `TP53`, `KRAS`, `FBXW7`, `CTNNB1`, `SMAD4`, `LYST`, `DAPK1`, `CR2`, `KIF16B`, `NPIPB15`, `SYTL2`, `ZNF91`, `KIAA0586` · Colorectal cancer (COAD + READ)

**Data availability:** `available`
> Mutation data (including all HC-SMGs) present in `crc_sysucc_2022` on cBioPortal. WES coverage supports low-frequency variant detection.

**Tool support:** `native`
> OncoPrint and Mutations tabs display HC-SMG frequencies. Study View allows filtering by gene and stage. Novel genes not in standard OncoKB tiers, so actionability overlays will not highlight them.

### F2: Four-subtype genomic classification with distinct clinical characteristics and survival

**Finding:** Unsupervised clustering defines four CRC genomic subtypes — hypermutated (HM), genome stable (GS), chromosome instability high-risk (CIN-HR), chromosome instability low-risk (CIN-LR) — each with significantly different clinical features and overall survival.

**Detail:** Hypermutated tumours (7.1%, n=72) are predominantly right-sided COAD, earlier stage (65% stage II), younger age, and enriched for POLE/MSI-H. GS tumours show minimal CNV burden. CIN-HR and CIN-LR represent the intermediate-genomic majority but differ in prognosis. CIN-HR is associated with worse OS. The genomic subtyping approach is based on somatic mutations + CNAs from WES; simpler to deploy clinically than transcriptomic CMS subtypes.

**Genes / cancer types:** `POLE`, `POLD1`, `MSH2`, `MLH1`, `BRAF`, `KRAS` (subtype markers) · Colorectal cancer

**Data availability:** `available`
> Subtype labels, TMB, and MSI status available as clinical attributes in `crc_sysucc_2022`. Raw mutation and CNA data available for subtype reproduction.

**Tool support:** `native`
> MSI and TMB filtering in Study View. Survival analysis by subtype group is native via Kaplan-Meier in cBioPortal. CIN subtype labels may require custom attribute query via API.

**Concepts:** #genomic-subtypes #hypermutation #CIN #MSI #survival #subtyping

### F3: Oncogenic cell cycle and TGF-beta pathway alterations are independent prognostic factors

**Finding:** Oncogenic (OncoKB-annotated) alterations in the cell cycle pathway (HR = 2.26; 95% CI 1.53–3.34; P < 0.001) and TGF-beta pathway (HR = 1.56; 95% CI 1.15–2.11; P = 0.004) independently predict shorter overall survival, adjusting for TMB, pathological stage, and grade.

**Detail:** Key cell cycle genes: RB1, CCND1, CCNE1 (mutually exclusive). Key TGF-beta genes: SMAD2, SMAD4 (co-deleted in 18.2% of patients). Mutations classified by OncoKB as oncogenic vs. VUS; only oncogenic events drive the survival associations. Combined MYC + PI3K pathway alterations confer worse prognosis than either alone.

**Genes / cancer types:** `RB1`, `CCND1`, `CCNE1`, `E2F3`, `SMAD2`, `SMAD4`, `ACVR2A`, `MYC`, `PIK3CA` · Colorectal cancer

**Data availability:** `available`
> Pathway alteration data queryable in `crc_sysucc_2022`. OncoKB annotations overlaid in cBioPortal distinguish oncogenic from VUS events.

**Tool support:** `native`
> Pathway view in cBioPortal. Survival analysis by SMAD4/RB1 alteration status is native. Multi-pathway co-alteration analysis requires manual cohort split or API query.

**Concepts:** #cell-cycle #TGF-beta #pathway-alteration #survival #prognosis #OncoKB

### F4: Mitochondrial DNA copy number (mScore) is an independent survival predictor in CRC

**Finding:** CRC patients in the top 10% of tumour mitochondrial DNA copy number (mScore-high) have significantly worse overall survival (HR = 1.84; 95% CI 1.23–2.75; P = 0.003) independent of pathological stage and grade; validated in an independent cohort using qRT-PCR.

**Detail:** mScore represents tumour mtDNA abundance normalised for purity. mScore-high is associated with higher TP53 mutation frequency, higher TGF-beta pathway alteration frequency, and lower DICER1 mutation frequency. Positively correlated with clock-like signature (SBS1), negatively with dMMR signature (SBS6). mScore-qPCR correlates strongly with WES-derived mScore, enabling clinical deployment without WES.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53`, `DICER1`, mitochondrial genes (ND5, CYB, ND4) · Colorectal cancer

**Data availability:** `partial`
> Mutation data in `crc_sysucc_2022`. mtDNA copy number / mScore is a custom metric derived from WES mitochondrial reads; not a standard cBioPortal clinical attribute in the public dataset.

**Tool support:** `custom`
> Requires external computation of mScore from WES BAM files. Association with TP53 and survival is queryable if mScore is uploaded as a custom clinical attribute. qPCR-based mScore is clinically deployable but not in cBioPortal.

**Concepts:** #mitochondria #mtDNA #copy-number #survival #biomarker #mScore

### F5: Hypermutation in Chinese CRC is enriched for POLE/POLD1 and lower MSI-H rate than Western cohorts

**Finding:** Hypermutation rate (TMB ≥10 mut/Mb) is 7.1% in this Chinese cohort, lower than TCGA (15–20%); MSI-H and POLE mutations explain 63.8% and 37.5% of hypermutated cases respectively, with 11 hypermutated cases unexplained by either mechanism.

**Detail:** POLE, POLD1, and PIK3CA mutations are enriched in hypermutated tumours; SMAD4 alterations are depleted. Hypermutated patients are significantly younger (mean 49.6 vs 58.2 years), more often male, right-sided, and stage II. TMB is correlated with immunotherapy eligibility (pembrolizumab FDA-approved for TMB-high); WES-based TMB is the gold standard vs. panel-based estimates.

**Genes / cancer types:** `POLE`, `POLD1`, `PIK3CA`, `SMAD4`, `MSH2`, `MLH1` · Colorectal cancer (predominantly right-sided COAD)

**Data availability:** `available`
> TMB and MSI status available in `crc_sysucc_2022`. POLE mutation status queryable via Mutations tab.

**Tool support:** `native`
> TMB and MSI clinical attributes filterable in Study View. Kaplan-Meier by TMB group is native. POLE-specific filtering requires Mutations tab query.

**Concepts:** #hypermutation #TMB #MSI #POLE #POLD1 #immunotherapy #Asian-cohort

## Open Questions

- Do the 8 novel HC-SMGs (LYST, DAPK1, CR2, KIF16B, NPIPB15, SYTL2, ZNF91, KIAA0586) have functional roles in CRC tumorigenesis, and do they appear in other large CRC WES cohorts (e.g., TCGA, MSKCC)?
- Can the mScore (mtDNA copy number) be validated as a clinical biomarker in prospective CRC cohorts using qPCR?
- What mechanism underlies the 11 hypermutated cases unexplained by MSI-H or POLE mutations?
- Do the CIN-HR vs. CIN-LR subtypes differ in response to adjuvant chemotherapy or immunotherapy?
- Does site-specific APC hotspot distribution (p.R1450 ascending, p.R876 descending, p.R216 rectal) reflect distinct mutational mechanisms or evolutionary pressures?

## Limitations

- Single-centre Chinese cohort; results may not fully generalise to Western CRC populations (lower hypermutation rate, higher rectal fraction)
- Retrospective sample collection from FFPE tissue; potential sequencing artefacts
- Five SMG tools selected to represent major approaches; MutPanning and RF5 not included, potentially missing some drivers
- mScore requires external computation from WES reads; not deployable without raw data
- Limited transcriptomic and proteomic data; pathway annotations rely on OncoKB classification which may lag novel biology
