## Summary

This study uses MSK-IMPACT sequencing of 426 treatment-naïve clinical stage I/II (cN0–cN1) lung adenocarcinoma (LUAD) patients who underwent surgical resection to identify preoperative clinical and tumor genomic features associated with pathologic lymph node (LN) metastasis. Twenty percent of patients had pN-positive disease at surgery. Gene-level analysis identified *STK11*, *SMARCA4*, and *SMAD4* as significantly more altered in pN-positive tumors, while *RBM10* was more altered in pN-negative tumors. Genomic summary metrics — fraction of genome altered (FGA), copy number amplifications, and whole-genome doubling (WGD) — were all elevated in pN-positive tumors, as was enrichment of APOBEC mutational signatures. Multivariable logistic regression combining preoperative clinical (CT morphology, SUVmax, clinical stage) and genomic (*SMARCA4*, *SMAD4* alteration) features provides an integrated preoperative model for predicting nodal disease, which could guide treatment sequencing before surgery.

## Key Findings

### F1: SMARCA4 and SMAD4 alterations independently associated with pathologic LN metastasis on multivariable analysis

**Finding:** Among 27 genes altered in ≥2% of the cohort, *STK11* (22% vs. 12%, p=0.024), *SMARCA4* (8% vs. 1.8%, p=0.006), and *SMAD4* (7% vs. 1.5%, p=0.011) were significantly more altered in pN-positive than pN-negative tumors. In multivariable logistic regression combining clinical and genomic variables, *SMARCA4* (OR 3.67, 95% CI 1.02–13.16, p=0.046) and *SMAD4* (OR 5.01, 95% CI 1.29–19.45, p=0.02) remained independently associated with pathologic LN metastasis.

**Detail:** *SMAD4* mediates TGF-β/BMP signaling and is a known tumor suppressor in pancreatic and colorectal cancer; reduced *SMAD4* in NSCLC is associated with increased DNA damage and sensitivity to topoisomerase inhibitors. *SMARCA4* is the most frequently altered SWI/SNF complex subunit in lung cancer; its inactivation co-occurs with *KRAS*, *STK11*, and *KEAP1* mutations and has been associated with poor outcomes. These alterations are predominantly driven by ever-smoker patients; no significant differences were observed in never-smoker subgroups, likely due to small sample size. *RBM10* was more altered in pN-negative tumors (17% vs. 8%, p=0.044).

### F2: Elevated FGA, copy number amplifications, and WGD mark pN-positive tumors — suggesting chromosomal instability as a driver of nodal spread

**Finding:** Fraction of genome altered (FGA) was significantly higher in pN-positive tumors (median 0.343 vs. 0.269, p=0.037). Copy number amplifications were significantly elevated in pN-positive tumors (median 0.152 vs. 0.085, p=0.001). Whole-genome doubling (WGD) occurred in 22% of pN-positive vs. 13% of pN-negative tumors (p=0.028). Copy number deletions did not differ between groups. Chromosome arm-level gains at 2p and 2q (FDR p=0.122) were enriched in pN-positive tumors.

**Detail:** *CDKN2A* was the most frequently copy-number-altered gene in pN-positive tumors (9% vs. 4%, p=0.039), while *CDK4* was more altered in pN-negative tumors (8% vs. 1.2%, p=0.029). FGA serves as a surrogate for chromosomal instability (CIN), which can promote metastasis via the cGAS-STING pathway. High subclonal copy number alterations have been associated with poorer disease-free survival in resected early-stage NSCLC. WGD was also the highest-level independent predictor of poor long-term survival across cancers.

**Genes / cancer types:** `CDKN2A`, `CDK4` · Lung adenocarcinoma

### F3: APOBEC mutational signatures enriched in pN-positive tumors, consistent with increased tumor heterogeneity

**Finding:** APOBEC signatures SBS2 (27.3% vs. 12.8%, p=0.03) and SBS13 (31.8% vs. 14.9%, p=0.02) were significantly more prevalent in pN-positive than pN-negative tumors. TMB did not differ significantly between groups (median 6.1 vs. 4.9 mut/Mb, p=0.058). The three most frequent signatures overall were the smoking signature (SBS4) and APOBEC signatures (SBS2 and SBS13); signature analysis was performed in samples with ≥13.8 Mut/Mb.

**Detail:** APOBEC-associated signatures have been linked to increased tumor heterogeneity in both primary and metastatic NSCLC. APOBEC signatures may reflect greater clonal diversity and serve as a potential biomarker for immunotherapy response in pN-positive patients. Signature analysis was restricted to 185 samples (44 pN-positive, 141 pN-negative) meeting minimum TMB thresholds.

### F4: Integrated preoperative model combining clinical and genomic features outperforms clinical staging alone

**Finding:** Multivariable logistic regression identified five independent preoperative predictors of pathologic LN metastasis: solid CT morphology (OR 4.25, p=0.001), SUVmax (OR 1.09, p=0.002), clinical stage II vs. I (OR 3.31, p<0.001), *SMARCA4* alteration (OR 3.67, p=0.046), and *SMAD4* alteration (OR 5.01, p=0.02). Clinical staging alone had a 64% false-negative rate for pN-positive disease (15% of cN0 patients were pN-positive at surgery).

**Detail:** Copy number amplification tertiles correlated significantly with preoperative radiographic features (solid CT morphology, SUVmax, tumor size) and adverse pathologic features (micropapillary/solid subtype, visceral pleural invasion, lymphovascular invasion, tumor spread through air spaces). The model incorporates variables available preoperatively: CT morphology, SUVmax, and clinical stage from standard-of-care imaging, plus SMARCA4/SMAD4 alteration status from a preoperative biopsy specimen. The combination of these variables may guide decisions on neoadjuvant therapy sequencing prior to surgery.

## Open Questions

- Can the clinical-genomic model be validated in an external cohort and prospectively used to guide neoadjuvant therapy sequencing?
- Do *SMARCA4*-altered pN-positive LUADs respond better to immunotherapy, as suggested by Schoenfeld et al.?
- Why are APOBEC-enriched tumors more likely to have LN metastasis — do APOBEC-driven heterogeneous clones seed nodal disease?
- Do these associations hold in never-smokers (insufficient power in current study)?
- What is the optimal biopsy site for genomic analysis (primary vs. LN) to capture pN-positive-associated alterations?

## Limitations

- NGS performed on surgical specimen (not preoperative biopsy); single-region sampling may miss intratumoral heterogeneity
- Small sample size in never-smoker subgroup; smoking-driven associations may not generalize
- *SMARCA4* and *SMAD4* alterations are low-frequency (~8% and ~7%), limiting individual predictive power
- Model requires external validation before clinical application
- APOBEC signature analysis restricted to samples with ≥13.8 Mut/Mb (n=185), reducing statistical power
- No long-term survival outcome data presented; model predicts pathologic node status, not recurrence or OS
