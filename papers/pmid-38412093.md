## Summary

The Global Anaplastic Thyroid Cancer Initiative (GATCI) sequenced 329 thyroid cancer regions from 292 patients: 179 primary ATC regions, 1 metastatic ATC, and 34 co-occurring differentiated thyroid cancer (DTC) regions within ATC patients (213 ATC-patient regions total), plus 115 papillary thyroid cancer (PTC) regions. Profiling used whole-exome sequencing (WES), whole-genome sequencing (WGS), SNP microarrays, and RNA-seq; 9 patients additionally underwent multi-region WGS of paired ATC and co-occurring DTC components to enable subclonal reconstruction. ATCs carry elevated SNV and copy-number burdens relative to differentiated thyroid cancers, with 42 recurrently mutated driver genes (top: *TP53*, *NRAS*, *BRAF*, *PIK3CA*) and five distinct CNA subtypes with divergent clinical associations. The multi-region WGS analysis demonstrated that ATC and co-occurring DTC invariably share a common clonal ancestor, with the ATC lineage diverging from a mutagenic field and acquiring characteristic driver mutations (particularly *TP53*) alongside extensive CNA diversification—establishing a genomic model for dedifferentiation and nominating DNA damage repair pathways as actionable therapeutic targets.

## Key Findings

### F1: ATC Has Elevated but Intermediate Mutation Burden with Five CNA Subtypes

**Finding:** ATCs harbor 3.8 ± 1.2 SNVs/Mb and a median of 83.5 copy-number aberrations (CNAs)—significantly more than differentiated thyroid cancers but fewer than most adult cancer types. Unsupervised consensus clustering of CNA profiles reveals five reproducible subtypes (A–E) with distinct genomic architectures and clinical associations.

**Detail:** Subtype A (11% of ATCs) shows wide-scale amplifications; subtype B (29%) shows large-scale deletions with uniparental polysomy reminiscent of oncocytic carcinoma; subtype C (20%) is dominated by chr7/20q gains and 1q gains; subtype D (15.5%) has many focal CNA regions; subtype E (24.5%) has a quiet CNA landscape despite confirmed tumor purity. CNA subtypes are associated with age, *TP53* mutation status, tumor purity, and ploidy. Recurrent arm-level events include loss of chr13 (39%) and gain of chr20q (35%). Focal driver CNAs include *CDKN2A* loss (42%), *BRCA2* deletion (33.6%), and a novel gain of chr7 (26%).

**Genes / cancer types:** `CDKN2A`, `BRCA2`, `RB1`, `NF1`, `CDK7`, `FLT3` · Anaplastic Thyroid Carcinoma

### F2: TP53, BRAF, RAS, and PIK3CA Are Top Recurrent ATC Driver SNVs

**Finding:** SeqSig analysis identified 42 genes with recurrent putative driver SNVs in ATC (FDR < 0.05). The top five by statistical significance are *TP53*, *NRAS*, *BRAF*, *PIK3CA*, and *USH2A*. *BRAF*V600E and *RAS* mutations are mutually exclusive, while *BRAF*V600E and *PIK3CA* co-occur significantly.

**Detail:** *TP53* non-functional or partially functional mutations in primary ATC are associated with elevated *TP53* mRNA (Wilcoxon p = 0.0067, n = 19), consistent with gain-of-function activity. *BRAF*V600E is far more prevalent in PTC (50.9%) than ATC (21.3%), while *TP53* mutation frequency rises dramatically from 0.9% in PTC to 21.4% in co-DTC and 36.8% in ATC. *EIF1AX* mutations (including the thyroid-specific p.A113X splice-site variant) were detected in five tumors, co-occurring with *RAS* mutations. TERT promoter mutations are correlated with *TP53* mutations (FDR < 0.01). Additional novel recurrent SNVs were identified in DNA damage repair (*BRCA1*, *BRCA2*, *ATM*, *ATR*), cell adhesion (*HMCN1*, *HSPG2*), and chromatin remodeling (*SPEN*) genes.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53`, `BRAF`, `NRAS`, `PIK3CA`, `TERT`, `EIF1AX`, `BRCA1`, `BRCA2`, `ATM`, `ATR`, `USH2A` · Anaplastic Thyroid Carcinoma, Papillary Thyroid Carcinoma

### F3: ATC and Co-occurring DTC Share a Common Clonal Origin via Mutagenic Field Effect

**Finding:** Multi-region WGS of 9 patients with paired ATC and co-occurring DTC components demonstrates that both tumor types invariably share a common clonal ancestor, with subsequent parallel divergence. The common ancestor harbors ~95% of CNAs but only ~19% of SNVs, indicating that chromosomal instability precedes ATC-specific point mutation acquisition.

**Detail:** Subclonal reconstruction using PhyloWGS on high-coverage WGS data shows that the ATC and DTC lineages split early in tumor evolution from a common mutagenic field. In several cases (e.g., ATCWGS33, ATCWGS34), spatially distinct tumor regions from both components share identical clonal mutations, providing direct evidence for a prior mutagenic field. The ATC-specific lineage then accumulates characteristic driver SNVs (particularly in *TP53*) and additional CNA diversity. *BRAF*V600E was clonal or early subclonal in ATCs, but half of all *BRAF*V600E variants in matched co-occurring cases were detected only in the DTC component and not in ATC. A cervical ATC metastasis showed nearly undetectable *BRAF*V600E compared with the primary, suggesting allelic loss or metastasis from a low-frequency subclone.

### F4: DNA Damage Repair Gene Alterations Are Recurrent and Therapeutically Actionable

**Finding:** Recurrent alterations in DNA damage repair (DDR) genes—including *BRCA1*, *BRCA2*, and *ATM* at both the SNV and CNA level—are a defining feature of ATC. *BRCA2* deletion occurs in 33.6% of ATCs, is rare in PTC (4.5%), and is paradoxically associated with better overall survival in ATC patients (HR = 0.48, 95% CI 0.29–0.80, p = 0.005).

**Detail:** Integrative analysis of 104 ATC/co-DTC tumors without canonical SNV driver mutations showed that 10/21 such ATCs carried deletions in *CDKN2A*, *RB1*, *CDK7*, or *BRCA2*, suggesting CNA-mediated tumor suppressor loss as an alternative driver mechanism. Germline alterations in DDR genes were also observed: *BRCA2* germline variants in 3 patients and *FANCF* in 3 patients (max recurrence: *RECQL4* in 4 patients, 5% of ATCs). The absence of second somatic hits in these germline-mutated cases limits functional interpretation. The frequency of DDR gene alterations rationalizes ongoing evaluation of PARP inhibitors in ATC, analogous to their success in DDR-deficient cancers of other types.

**Genes / cancer types:** `BRCA1`, `BRCA2`, `ATM`, `ATR`, `CDKN2A`, `RB1`, `CDK7`, `FANCF`, `RECQL4` · Anaplastic Thyroid Carcinoma

### F5: Mutational Signatures Reveal APOBEC Activity and a Novel ATC-Specific Signature

**Finding:** NMF decomposition identified five trinucleotide mutational signatures in ATC: four matching known COSMIC signatures (1, 5, 6, 13) and one novel signature characterized by G[T>G]G mutations found in only three tumors. COSMIC signature 13 (APOBEC/AID activity) and signature 6 (MMR deficiency) are active in ATC but are not associated with sex, age, CNA subtype, TERT promoter status, or overall survival.

**Detail:** COSMIC signatures 1 and 5 (clock-like replication-associated) show a strong correlation with overall SNV density (FDR < 10⁻³). Trinucleotide signatures are not correlated with CNA subtypes, suggesting independent molecular processes drive SNV patterning versus chromosomal instability. In co-occurring DTCs, about half (12/25) showed mutational signatures identical to their paired ATC component, while the remainder showed signature-level divergence, consistent with post-split evolutionary diversification from a common precursor. PTC harbors predominantly COSMIC signatures 2 and 13 (APOBEC) and 6 (MMR deficiency).

**Genes / cancer types:** · Anaplastic Thyroid Carcinoma, Papillary Thyroid Carcinoma

### F6: ATC Tumor Microenvironment Is Distinct with Elevated Fibroblasts and Macrophages

**Finding:** RNA-seq-based immune deconvolution (MCPCounter) reveals that ATC has a tumor microenvironment (TME) markedly distinct from PTC and normal thyroid, characterized by dramatically elevated fibroblasts and macrophages/monocytes and decreased endothelial cells, myeloid dendritic cells, and neutrophils.

**Detail:** A progressive decrease in thyroid differentiation score (TDS) from normal thyroid → PTC → ATC → ATC cell lines reflects stepwise dedifferentiation. ATC cell lines show the lowest TDS, consistent with their representing extreme dedifferentiated forms. BRAF-RAS score (BRS) analysis shows that *RAS*-mutant ATCs appear more BRAF-like, unlike in PTC where BRAF- and RAS-altered tumors are clearly separated. Fusion gene analysis identified 84 fusion events in 24 primary ATCs (median 3 fusions/tumor; max 11), with only 5 recurring in ≥2 patients; none were previously reported in COSMIC. Fusions involving *FN1* and *COL1A1*—genes linked to tumor cell migration and invasion—were associated with elevated transcript abundance of the respective gene (4.3× and 12× higher TPM, respectively).

**Genes / cancer types:** `FN1`, `COL1A1`, `BRAF`, `NRAS` · Anaplastic Thyroid Carcinoma

### F7: Clinicogenomic Associations Identify Prognostic Subgroups in ATC

**Finding:** Among clinical factors, radiotherapy (FDR = 1.7 × 10⁻⁵) and surgery (FDR = 0.0089) are strongly associated with improved survival, while nodal involvement, distal metastasis, leukocytosis, and older age predict worse outcomes. Among genomic features, patients with high mutation rate (>10 SNVs/Mb) have significantly worse survival (HR = 0.51, 95% CI 0.33–0.77, p = 0.002), and *BRCA2* deletion is associated with improved survival.

**Detail:** No individual driver SNV gene (in ≥5% of ATCs) reached significance for overall survival after FDR correction. CNA subtypes A–E were not independently associated with overall survival, but subtype A was enriched for older patients with better survival; subtype D was enriched for male patients with metastatic disease. Tumors with fewer CNAs (lower PGA) paradoxically presented more frequently with distant metastases (FDR = 0.034), suggesting that genomic quietness does not confer indolent behavior. Median survival for ATC patients in this cohort was 126 days (18 weeks) from diagnosis. All patient samples were collected before 2018, predating FDA approval of dabrafenib/trametinib for ATC.

## Open Questions

- Does the mutagenic field model hold for the ~20% of ATCs that arise without a co-occurring or prior DTC? WGS of non-malignant thyroid tissue adjacent to ATC is needed.
- Which of the 42 SeqSig recurrent driver genes are functionally required for ATC dedifferentiation versus being passenger events in a hypermutable background?
- Why does *BRCA2* deletion paradoxically associate with better ATC survival, and does this reflect sensitivity to DNA-damaging therapies or platinum/PARP inhibitors?
- Can the five CNA subtypes (A–E) guide therapeutic stratification, particularly for subtypes with distinct immunological or molecular features?
- What is the clonal and mutational basis of ATC that arises de novo without identifiable prior DTC, and does it follow the same evolutionary trajectory?
- Do the novel fusion genes (*FN1*, *COL1A1* fusions) and cell-adhesion pathway alterations (*HMCN1*, *HSPG2*) functionally drive invasion and metastasis in ATC?

## Limitations

- Samples were collected before 2018 (pre-dabrafenib/trametinib approval), so the cohort does not represent the current treatment era and survival analyses may not reflect modern outcomes.
- Heterogeneity in sequencing platforms (multiple WES capture kits across 8 centers) required harmonization to overlapping exome regions (~26 Mbp), potentially reducing sensitivity for some variants.
- RNA-seq was limited to 24 primary ATC tumors and 13 cell lines—insufficient for robust transcriptomic subtyping or comprehensive immune profiling across the full cohort.
- WGS multi-region subclonal reconstruction was performed on only 9 patients, limiting the statistical power to generalize the evolutionary model to the broader ATC population.
- Low tumor purity in some ATCs (stromal contamination) may have reduced variant detection sensitivity, particularly for subclonal events, despite macro-dissection efforts.
- Germline predisposition analysis (DDR gene germline variants) is underpowered given cohort size; the lack of second somatic hits precludes definitive classification of germline variants as pathogenic in this context.
