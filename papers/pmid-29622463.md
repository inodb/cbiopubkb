## Summary

Pan-cancer analysis of 10,522 TCGA tumor genomes across 33 cancer types using SNP 6.0 arrays and the ABSOLUTE algorithm to define chromosome arm-level aneuploidy scores (0–39 arms altered). Aneuploidy correlates strongly with *TP53* mutation and positively with somatic mutation rate (excluding hypermutated tumors). Within individual cancer types, high aneuploidy is inversely correlated with leukocyte infiltration, mechanistically explained by differences in leukocyte fraction rather than tumor purity. Cancer-type-specific arm-level patterns cluster into biologically coherent groups (squamous, GI, neural lineage). A CRISPR-based model of chromosome 3p arm deletion in lung epithelial cells (AALE) demonstrates that chr_3p loss initially suppresses proliferation and is rescued by chromosome 3 duplication, modeling adaptive karyotype evolution. Data are in the TCGA PanCanAtlas studies on cBioPortal.

## Key Findings

### F1: Aneuploidy score across 33 cancer types — *TP53* as dominant genomic correlate

**Finding:** 88% of 10,522 TCGA tumors harbor at least one arm-level aneuploidy event (mean score 10.0 arms). *TP53* mutation is the strongest positive correlate of aneuploidy in a multivariable pan-cancer model (coefficient +0.13, far exceeding all other genes). Thirty-four other genes associate negatively with aneuploidy (all coefficients <0.027 in magnitude).

**Detail:** Aneuploidy scores range from 0 to 39. Thyroid carcinoma has the lowest mean (0.87); testicular germ cell tumors and uterine carcinosarcomas among the highest (18.7 and 17.2). Whole genome doubling (WGD) tumors show higher aneuploidy overall (Spearman r=0.55), but within each WGD class, higher aneuploidy correlates with lower ploidy, indicating that arm-level events are predominantly losses. Hypermutated tumors (MSI-H/POLE) show low aneuploidy, and excluding them reveals a positive correlation between mutation rate and aneuploidy across most cancer types (r=0.38).

**Genes / cancer types:** TP53, POLE · Pan-cancer (33 TCGA types)

### F2: Aneuploidy anti-correlates with leukocyte infiltration within cancer types

**Finding:** Within individual cancer types, high aneuploidy is negatively correlated with leukocyte fraction (methylation-based estimate). The effect is strongest in pancreatic adenocarcinoma (r=−0.428) and head and neck squamous cell carcinoma (r=−0.312). Controlling for leukocyte fraction in the linear expression model abolishes the immune gene set enrichment signal, establishing leukocyte infiltrate as the mediating factor.

**Detail:** Pan-cancer (across tumor types), aneuploidy shows a slight positive correlation with leukocyte fraction (r=0.057), but this is a compositional artifact of tumor type differences. Within types, the relationship is consistently negative. Adding leukocyte fraction—but not purity or non-leukocyte stroma—to the expression model removes immune pathway enrichment. Pro-proliferative pathways (E2F targets, G2M checkpoint, mitotic spindle) remain positively correlated with aneuploidy regardless of covariates. Non-leukocyte stromal fractions are positively correlated with aneuploidy and may represent fibroblast-rich stroma associated with chromosomally unstable tumors.

**Genes / cancer types:** (Genome-wide; leukocyte fraction from methylation) · Pan-cancer

### F3: Cancer-type-specific arm aneuploidy patterns — squamous cancers defined by 3p loss / 3q gain

**Finding:** Hierarchical clustering of mean arm-level copy number profiles across cancer types and subtypes reveals biologically coherent clusters: squamous cancers (lung SCC, esophageal SCC, cervical SCC, head and neck SCC) segregate together based purely on aneuploidy profiles. The dominant shared feature is chr_3p loss and chr_3q gain, which co-occur significantly more than expected by chance in lung SCC (chi-square p=0.0386), present in ~80% and ~60% of LUSC tumors, respectively.

**Detail:** Additional cross-cancer clusters identified: (1) gastrointestinal tumors (CRC, non-squamous esophageal, gastric, pancreatic) sharing 8q gain, 13q gain, chr_20 gain; (2) gynecological/high-CNA cluster (ovarian, serous endometrial) with chr_7 gain; (3) neural lineage cluster (LGG, GBM, melanoma) with chr_7 gain. IDH-mutant LGG characteristically shows 1p loss and 19q gain. GBM shows chr_7 gain and chr_10 loss. Testicular GCT shows chr_12p gain. These patterns largely recapitulate prior karyotyping and multi-omic clustering studies, validating aneuploidy alone as a major molecular classifier.

**Genes / cancer types:** IDH1/2 (LGG), EGFR (GBM chr7) · Lung SCC, Esophageal SCC, HNSC, GBM, LGG, Ovarian, CRC, Gastric

### F4: Chromosome 3p deletion in lung cells suppresses proliferation — rescued by karyotype evolution

**Finding:** CRISPR-mediated hemizygous deletion of chromosome arm 3p in human lung epithelial cells (AALE) causes G1 cell cycle arrest and reduced proliferation. After additional passaging, cells recover normal proliferation through karyotype evolution: two of three deleted clones develop duplication of the remaining chromosome 3 copy, restoring 3p dosage, demonstrating that compensatory CNA acquisitions can rescue aneuploid-induced fitness defects.

**Detail:** Chr_3p deletion confirmed by qPCR, WGS, and karyotyping. RNA-seq shows 64% of chr_3p genes significantly down-regulated (FDR<0.05), with STAC and ROBO1 most reduced (>15-fold). Up-regulation of interferon response pathways by GSEA (FWER<0.01) at early timepoint, consistent with pan-cancer finding that chr_3p copy number anti-correlates with interferon signaling. At later passage, 3q duplication rescues proliferation and reduces (but does not eliminate) the interferon signal. This models the adaptive karyotype evolution seen in cancer.

**Genes / cancer types:** STAC, ROBO1, UBA7, LMCD1 (chr_3p genes); ISG15 (interferon target) · Lung epithelial (experimental model)

### F5: Chromosome arm alteration frequency — 8p, 17p most commonly deleted; 8q most gained

**Finding:** Across the full pan-cancer cohort, 8p and 17p are the most frequently deleted chromosome arms (33% and 35% of samples, respectively), and 8q is the most commonly gained (33%). Chromosome 2p and 2q are the least frequently altered (<18%). Some arms are predominantly gained (1q, 7p, 8q, 20q) or predominantly lost (3p, 17p), while others show balanced gain and loss (6p, 12q, 17q, 19q).

**Detail:** These frequencies are computed from over 400,000 chromosome arm calls and 175,000 whole-chromosome calls. Arm-length differences do not substantially bias frequency estimates (less than two-fold effect), justifying use of the count-based aneuploidy score. The consistent predominance of 17p loss is consistent with TP53 LOH as a key event; 8p loss and 8q gain co-occur frequently in gastrointestinal and other solid tumors, suggesting coordinate selection for oncogenes on 8q (MYC) and tumor suppressor loss on 8p.

**Genes / cancer types:** TP53 (17p), MYC (8q) · Pan-cancer

## Open Questions

- Does the inverse relationship between aneuploidy and immune infiltration represent active immune exclusion, or is it a consequence of selection for immune-cold clones in chromosomally unstable tumors?
- Can the cancer-type-specific aneuploidy patterns (e.g., 3p/3q in squamous cancers) be leveraged as therapeutic vulnerabilities, for instance by targeting haplo-insufficient tumor suppressors on 3p?
- What is the mechanism by which chr_3p deletion upregulates interferon signaling, and does this create an opportunity for combination with immune checkpoint inhibitors?
- Do the non-leukocyte stromal fractions positively correlated with aneuploidy represent cancer-associated fibroblasts that could be targeted to overcome aneuploidy-associated immune exclusion?
- How does adaptive karyotype evolution (e.g., chromosome 3 duplication rescuing 3p deletion) relate to the acquisition of chromosomal instability and therapy resistance in clinical tumors?

## Limitations

- Aneuploidy scores derived from SNP array data are subject to segmentation artifacts and do not capture chromothripsis or complex rearrangements as discrete arm-level events
- Leukocyte fraction estimates from methylation data are indirect; direct immune cell quantification (e.g., flow cytometry, IHC) was not performed
- The CRISPR chr_3p deletion model used an immortalized lung epithelial line (AALE) with SV40 large T antigen, which may not fully recapitulate primary tumor biology
- Functional studies were limited to chr_3p in a single cell line; other arm deletions prevalent in squamous cancers (e.g., 3q gain, 8p loss) were not modeled
- Aneuploidy score treats all chromosome arms equally; does not account for gene density or the selective value of specific arm alterations
