## Summary

The Cancer Cell Line Encyclopedia (CCLE) — a joint Broad Institute/Novartis resource — comprehensively characterizes 947 human cancer cell lines across 36 tumor types using targeted mutation sequencing (>1,600 genes), SNP6.0 copy-number arrays, and Affymetrix gene expression arrays. Pharmacologic profiling of 24 anticancer drugs (targeted and cytotoxic) across 479 lines with 8-point dose-response curves enables systematic identification of genomic predictors of drug sensitivity via elastic net regression and Bayesian classifiers. Beyond confirming known biomarkers (BRAF V600E → RAF/MEK inhibitors; EGFR/ERBB2 → erlotinib/lapatinib), the study discovers novel associations: AHR expression predicts MEK inhibitor sensitivity in NRAS-mutant lines; SLFN11 expression predicts topoisomerase inhibitor sensitivity across 12/16 lineages; and IGF1/IGF1R expression predicts IGF1R inhibitor sensitivity in multiple myeloma. The CCLE establishes a paradigm for systematic preclinical pharmacogenomic atlases to enable "personalized" drug stratification hypotheses.

## Key Findings

### F1: BRAF V600E predicts sensitivity to RAF and MEK inhibitors; NRAS and KRAS mutations to MEK inhibitors — confirming established biomarkers at scale

**Finding:** Elastic net models for the MEK inhibitor PD-0325901 identified *BRAF* and *NRAS* mutations as top predictors of sensitivity. *BRAF* V600E was also the top predictor for RAF inhibitors PLX4720 and RAF265. Additional expression features — *PTEN*, *PTPN5*, *SPRY2* — were also identified as predictors of MEK inhibitor sensitivity. *EGFR* mutations predicted erlotinib sensitivity; *ERBB2* amplification/overexpression predicted lapatinib sensitivity; *MET* amplification and *HGF* expression predicted MET/ALK inhibitor PF-2341066 sensitivity; *MDM2* overexpression predicted Nutlin-3 sensitivity.

**Detail:** The PLX4720 response profile showed a clear bimodal distribution: sensitive lines were strongly enriched for BRAF V600E, while insensitive lines largely lacked the mutation. KRAS mutations also correlated with MEK inhibitor sensitivity but with lower predictive weight than BRAF and NRAS. *EXT2* variants (heparin sulfate biosynthesis) were significantly correlated with erlotinib sensitivity — consistent with a prior report linking heparin sulfate to erlotinib response. NQO1 expression was the top predictor for the Hsp90 inhibitor 17-AAG, reproducing a known pharmacogenomic relationship. These replications of known biology validate the predictive modeling framework.

**Genes / cancer types:** `BRAF`, `NRAS`, `KRAS`, `EGFR`, `ERBB2`, `MET`, `HGF`, `MDM2`, `PTEN`, `SPRY2`, `EXT2`, `NQO1` · Pan-cancer (multiple lineages)

### F2: AHR expression predicts MEK inhibitor sensitivity in NRAS-mutant cancer cells beyond genotype alone

**Finding:** Within NRAS-mutant cell lines, elastic net analysis identified elevated *AHR* (aryl hydrocarbon receptor) mRNA expression as strongly correlated with MEK inhibitor (PD-0325901) sensitivity. shRNA knockdown of *AHR* inhibited growth of 3 NRAS-mutant cell lines with high AHR expression but had no effect on 2 lines with low expression. MEK inhibitors PD-0325901 and PD-98059 suppressed *CYP1A1* mRNA (an AHR transcriptional target) in NRAS-mutant melanoma lines — suggesting MEK inhibitors may also function as AHR antagonists.

**Detail:** This finding explains why some NRAS-mutant cancers are more sensitive to MEK inhibition than others despite equivalent genotype. Lineage-specific transcriptional programs (MITF, SOX10 in melanoma) also contribute: a melanoma-only MEK inhibitor model outperformed the pan-lineage model, emphasizing that lineage-adjusted predictors are needed for clinical application. The AHR-MEK inhibitor connection introduces a transcription factor dependency as a biomarker independent of classic oncogenic mutation status.

**Genes / cancer types:** `AHR`, `NRAS`, `CYP1A1` · Melanoma, neuroblastoma, other NRAS-mutant cancers

### F3: SLFN11 expression predicts topoisomerase inhibitor sensitivity across 12/16 lineages and is highest in Ewing sarcoma

**Finding:** *SLFN11* expression was the top correlate of sensitivity to the topoisomerase I inhibitor irinotecan in elastic net analysis across 250 cell lines and was independently validated as the top predictor for topotecan. The association was significant in 12/16 lineages. All 3 Ewing sarcoma cell lines showed both high *SLFN11* expression and irinotecan sensitivity. Among 4,103 primary tumor samples across 39 lineages, Ewing sarcoma had the highest SLFN11 expression. SLFN11 knockdown did not affect steady-state growth but sensitized cells to topoisomerase inhibitors.

**Detail:** *SLFN11* encodes a helicase involved in DNA replication stress response. Later studies (post-CCLE) confirmed that SLFN11 blocks replication at stalled forks and that loss confers topoisomerase inhibitor resistance. The Ewing sarcoma connection is clinically meaningful: irinotecan-based combinations are already in trials for this disease. For cervical cancer and other lineages with high SLFN11, topoisomerase inhibitors are already standard; SLFN11 may refine patient selection in lineages with heterogeneous expression (colorectal, ovarian). This finding spurred a decade of SLFN11 biomarker research in clinical oncology.

**Genes / cancer types:** `SLFN11` · Ewing sarcoma, colorectal, ovarian, cervical, and other cancers

### F4: Hematopoietic lineage predicts HDAC inhibitor sensitivity; IGF1/IGF1R expression predicts IGF1R inhibitor response in multiple myeloma

**Finding:** Elastic net analysis of the HDAC inhibitor LBH589 (panobinostat) identified hematologic lineage as the predominant predictor of sensitivity — consistent with clinical reality where panobinostat, vorinostat, and romidepsin show efficacy predominantly in hematological malignancies. For the IGF1 receptor inhibitor AEW541, 12/14 multiple myeloma lines showed enhanced sensitivity, and multiple myeloma had elevated *IGF1* and *IGF1R* mRNA expression. IGF1R expression independently correlated with AEW541 sensitivity.

**Detail:** Panobinostat was approved in 2015 for multiple myeloma (hematologic), consistent with the CCLE lineage prediction. The IGF1/IGF1R finding in multiple myeloma motivated multiple clinical trials of IGF1R inhibitors in this disease, though results were ultimately modest — illustrating both the value and limitations of cell-line-based predictions. The CCLE framework demonstrates that lineage-level transcriptional and epigenetic programs can be as informative as specific mutations for predicting sensitivity to certain drug classes.

### F5: CCLE cell line genomics correlate strongly with primary tumors, validating cell lines as genomic proxies across most lineages

**Finding:** Comparison of CCLE cell lines with primary tumors (from Tumorscape, expO, MILE, COSMIC datasets) showed strong positive correlation in chromosomal copy-number (median r=0.77, range 0.52–0.94) and gene expression (median r=0.60, range 0.29–0.77) patterns for most lineages. Point mutation frequency correlations (median r=0.71, range −0.06–0.97) were also positive across most cancer types, even after removing the dominant TP53 signal (median r=0.64). Exceptions included esophageal, liver, and head/neck cancers where mutation frequencies were partially restored by including TP53.

**Detail:** This validation is foundational for the CCLE drug sensitivity analyses: if cell lines did not recapitulate primary tumor genomics, pharmacogenomic predictions would have limited translational value. The strong correlation across most lineages justifies the use of CCLE as a systematic preclinical model for biomarker discovery. The exceptions (esophageal, liver, head/neck) indicate where cell line predictions should be interpreted with caution.

**Genes / cancer types:** Pan-cancer (36 tumor types)

## Open Questions

- Can SLFN11 expression predict response to topoisomerase inhibitors prospectively in clinical trials (Ewing sarcoma, SCLC, ovarian)?
- Does AHR dependency in NRAS-mutant cancers translate to enhanced clinical activity of MEK inhibitors in AHR-high tumors?
- How do expanded CCLE datasets (pharmacoScan, PRISM, CRISPR screens) integrated into DepMap extend these original pharmacogenomic associations?
- Can cell line-based elastic net models generalize to patient-derived organoids or xenografts with better tumor microenvironment fidelity?
- What accounts for the lineage exceptions (esophageal, liver, head/neck) where cell lines poorly recapitulate primary tumor mutation frequencies?
- How do the 24 CCLE drugs compare with modern targeted agents (KRAS G12C inhibitors, CDK4/6 inhibitors, PD-1/PD-L1) that were not yet available in 2012?

## Limitations

- 2012 platform: targeted sequencing of >1,600 genes (not WES/WGS); TERT promoter and other non-coding drivers not captured
- mRNA expression by Affymetrix microarray (not RNA-seq); lower dynamic range and fusion transcript detection
- Drug panel of 24 compounds limited; does not include many now-approved targeted agents (KRAS G12C, CDK4/6, PD-1, PARP, BTK inhibitors)
- 8-point dose-response curves in 2D culture do not capture 3D architecture, tumor microenvironment, or pharmacokinetic context
- Cell line genomic drift and passage number effects may reduce fidelity to original tumor biology
- Novartis funding introduces potential bias in compound selection toward Novartis clinical pipeline
- AHR-MEK mechanism validated in cell lines only; no clinical cohort validation
- SLFN11 knockdown effect was modest and context-dependent; mechanistic understanding at time of publication was limited
- Multiple testing not fully addressed for >50,000 input features; some associations may be false positives
