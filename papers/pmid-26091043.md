## Summary

This TCGA multi-platform study characterizes 333 primary and/or metastatic cutaneous melanomas from 331 patients using six genomic platforms (WES, SNP arrays, RNA-seq, miRNA-seq, DNA methylation, RPPA). The study establishes a four-subtype genomic classification based on driver mutations: *BRAF*, *RAS*, *NF1*, and Triple-WT. RNA expression analysis defines three transcriptomic subclasses (immune, keratin, MITF-low), with the immune subclass—marked by lymphocytic infiltration and high LCK protein—associated with improved survival in regional metastases. The dataset is available in cBioPortal as `skcm_tcga_pub_2015`.

## Key Findings

### F1: Four genomic subtypes defined by BRAF, RAS, NF1, and Triple-WT mutations

**Finding:** Cutaneous melanomas are classified into four mutually exclusive genomic subtypes based on significantly mutated genes: *BRAF* (52%), *RAS* (28%), *NF1* (14%), and Triple-WT (6%), providing a framework for targeted therapy decisions.

**Detail:** Of 318 WES cases, 166 (52%) harbored *BRAF* mutations — predominantly V600E (n=124), V600K (n=18), V600R (n=3), and K601 (n=5). The RAS subtype included 88 *NRAS* hot-spot mutations plus rare *HRAS* (n=4) and *KRAS* (n=3) mutations. The *NF1* subtype (n=28) showed loss-of-function mutations (nonsense, splice-site, frameshift) and had the highest mutation burden (39 mutations/Mb vs ~16.8/Mb overall). Triple-WT (n=46) lacked canonical driver hotspots and was enriched for *KIT* mutations/amplifications, *GNAQ*/*GNA11* mutations, and complex structural rearrangements. No significant survival difference was seen across genomic subtypes.

**Genes / cancer types:** `BRAF`, `NRAS`, `HRAS`, `KRAS`, `NF1`, `KIT`, `GNAQ`, `GNA11` · Cutaneous melanoma

### F2: Highest mutation burden of any TCGA cancer type — UV signature predominates

**Finding:** Cutaneous melanoma has a mean mutation rate of 16.8 mutations/Mb, the highest of any cancer type analyzed by TCGA at the time, with UV-induced C>T transitions at dipyrimidines as the dominant mutational signature.

**Detail:** Of 318 WES samples, 76% of primaries and 84% of metastases carried a UV signature (>60% C>T at dipyrimidines or >5% CC>TT). The NF1 subtype had the highest per-sample burden (39 mutations/Mb). In total 228,987 mutations were identified across the cohort. *TERT* promoter mutations (C228T and C250T) were confirmed in 23.5% and 40.9% of 115 assayed samples, respectively; TERT promoter mutations were present in 75–85% of *BRAF*, *RAS*, and *NF1* subtypes but in only 6.7% of Triple-WT, where *TERT* amplification was the alternative mechanism.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TERT`, `TP53` · Cutaneous melanoma

### F3: 13 significantly mutated genes identified including novel candidates RAC1, DDX3X, and NF1

**Finding:** Integrative analysis with MutSig and InVEx identified 13 significantly mutated genes (SMGs), including established drivers (*BRAF*, *NRAS*, *CDKN2A*, *TP53*, *PTEN*), recently described genes (*RAC1*, *MAP2K1*, *PPP6C*, *ARID2*), newly confirmed SMGs (*NF1*, *IDH1*, *RB1*), and a novel candidate (*DDX3X*).

**Detail:** *RAC1* P29S hotspot (UV-induced) was mutated in 6.9% of samples and has been linked to BRAF inhibitor resistance. *IDH1* R132 was mutated in ~6.2% and enriched in the CIMP subgroup. *RPS27* 5′UTR mutations occurred in ~9% and may affect mTOR-regulated translation. *ARID2* mutations (~15% across subtypes) co-occurred with *NF1* mutations and are targets of synthetic lethal strategies. *PTEN* mutations/deletions were more frequent in the *BRAF* subtype (~20%), while AKT3 amplification and overexpression were enriched in *RAS*, *NF1*, and Triple-WT.

**Genes / cancer types:** `RAC1`, `NF1`, `IDH1`, `ARID2`, `PTEN`, `MAP2K1`, `PPP6C`, `DDX3X`, `RPS27`, `CDKN2A`, `TP53` · Cutaneous melanoma

### F4: Triple-WT subtype is driven by structural rearrangements and RTK amplifications rather than point mutations

**Finding:** Triple-WT melanomas (6% of cases) are characterized by KIT/PDGFRA/KDR focal amplifications, complex structural rearrangements, low UV signature frequency, and TERT activation through amplification rather than promoter mutation — implicating alternative mutational processes.

**Detail:** *KIT* focal 4q12 amplifications were significantly enriched in Triple-WT (Fisher's p<0.01, FDR<0.05), often co-amplified with *PDGFRA* and *KDR* (*VEGFR2*). Complex structural rearrangements were found in 38% of all samples (45/117 with WGS) but enriched in Triple-WT (11/16; Fisher's p=0.00098), particularly those without a UV signature (7/7). TERT promoter mutations occurred in only 6.7% of Triple-WT vs. 75–85% in other subtypes. BCL-2 upregulation and *MDM2* amplification (~15%) were also enriched in Triple-WT. 224 candidate fusion drivers were identified; Triple-WT was significantly enriched for fusions (p=2e-04).

**Genes / cancer types:** `KIT`, `PDGFRA`, `KDR`, `MDM2`, `CDK4`, `CCND1`, `TERT`, `BCL2` · Cutaneous melanoma (Triple-WT subtype)

### F5: Three RNA expression subclasses — immune, keratin, and MITF-low — with distinct survival outcomes

**Finding:** Unsupervised clustering of the top 1,500 most variable genes in 329 samples identifies three stable transcriptomic subclasses: "immune" (51%), "keratin" (31%), and "MITF-low" (18%), with significantly different post-accession survival among regionally metastatic patients (log-rank p=0.001).

**Detail:** The immune subclass showed high expression of T cell, B cell, and NK cell markers, immune checkpoint proteins (PD-1, PD-L1), cytokines and chemokines; 74% of immune cluster samples came from regional lymph nodes but immune gene expression did not differ between lymph node and non-lymph node sites. The keratin subclass had high expression of keratins, kallikreins, and epidermal genes; 74% of primary melanomas clustered here. The MITF-low subclass was enriched for neuronal/developmental genes and low pigmentation markers. Regional metastatic patients in the immune subclass had the best survival (log-rank p=0.003 vs. other subclasses). MITF-low had the highest proportion of BRAF hot-spot mutations (66%).

**Genes / cancer types:** `MITF`, `LCK`, `SYK`, `CD274` (PD-L1), `PDCD1` (PD-1) · Cutaneous melanoma

### F6: Immune infiltration and LCK protein expression are independent prognostic markers in regional metastases

**Finding:** A combination of pathologist-assessed lymphocyte score (LScore), RNA immune subclass, and high LCK protein expression (RPPA) independently predicts improved post-accession survival in regionally metastatic melanoma (combined log-rank p=8.0e-6; bivariate LScore + LCK: log-rank p=7.9e-5, HR=5.5).

**Detail:** Melanomas from regional or distant lymph nodes had significantly higher LScores (Wilcoxon p=5.6e-8). High LScore correlated strongly with immune subclass assignment (Fisher's p<1e-12). High LCK protein (T-cell marker) but not SYK (B-cell marker) was associated with improved survival, suggesting T-cell signaling is the relevant effector. Multivariable Cox regression confirmed LScore and LCK as independent prognostic factors. PD-1 and PD-L1 expression were significantly higher in the immune subclass (~50% of all cases), suggesting overlap with the immunotherapy-responsive population, though the study did not directly test this.

**Genes / cancer types:** `LCK`, `SYK`, `CD274` (PD-L1), `PDCD1` (PD-1) · Cutaneous melanoma (regional metastases)

### F7: Subtype-specific pathway activation revealed by RPPA protein profiling

**Finding:** Protein-level analysis of 181 cancer-related proteins/phosphoproteins in 200 samples shows differential MAPK, PI(3)K, and apoptotic pathway activation across the four genomic subtypes.

**Detail:** Phospho-MEK1/2 (S217/S221) was elevated in both *BRAF* and *RAS* hot-spot subtypes. Phospho-ERK1/2 (T202/Y204) was highest in the *RAS* subtype. KIT protein abundance was highest in Triple-WT (consistent with amplification data). CRAF expression was highest in the *NF1* subtype, reflecting differential MAPK activation. BCL-2 protein was highest in Triple-WT; IGFBP2 was highest in the *BRAF* subtype. AKT3 overexpression was enriched in *RAS*, *NF1*, and Triple-WT compared to *BRAF* (p<0.05). *PTEN* alterations were more frequent in *BRAF* mutant tumors (~20%).

**Genes / cancer types:** `BRAF`, `MAP2K1`, `MAPK1`, `KIT`, `RAF1`, `BCL2`, `IGFBP2`, `AKT3`, `PTEN` · Cutaneous melanoma

## Open Questions

- Does genomic subtype (BRAF, RAS, NF1, Triple-WT) predict differential response to immune checkpoint inhibitors (PD-1/PD-L1 blockade), beyond what is captured by the immune transcriptomic subclass?
- Can the LScore + LCK bivariate prognostic model be validated prospectively and incorporated into AJCC staging for stage III melanoma?
- What mutational processes drive the structural rearrangements and complex copy number alterations in Triple-WT melanomas that lack a UV signature?
- Do *NF1* loss-of-function mutations predict sensitivity to MEK and/or ERK inhibitors in clinical settings, as suggested by cell line data?
- What is the functional significance of the recurrent 5′UTR mutations in *RPS27* and *MRPS31*, and do they represent bona fide driver events?

## Limitations

- 80% of samples are metastatic; findings on primary melanoma biology may not be generalizable, and matched primary-metastatic pairs were available for only two patients.
- No direct treatment response data; therapeutic implications (e.g., MEK inhibitor sensitivity for NF1 subtype) are inferred from cell line studies and not validated clinically in this cohort.
- TERT promoter mutation analysis was performed on only a subset (n=115), limiting subtype-specific conclusions.
- The immune subclass/LCK prognostic analysis is restricted to regionally metastatic tumors; its value for primary or distantly metastatic disease is not established.
- Triple-WT is a heterogeneous catch-all subgroup; deeper sequencing and functional studies are needed to further stratify this minority (~6%) population.
