## Summary

This Cancer Discovery paper provides the first detailed characterization of "atypical SCLC" (aSCLC)—a rare SCLC subset (3% of 600 consecutive SCLC) lacking *RB1* and *TP53* co-inactivation, arising exclusively in never/light smokers. Comprehensive genomic profiling (MSK-IMPACT, WGS, RNA-seq) demonstrates that 84% of aSCLC is driven by extensive chromothripsis with extrachromosomal DNA (ecDNA) amplification of *CCND1* (chr11), or *CCND2/CDK4/MDM2* (chr12), functionally mimicking RB1/TP53 loss. aSCLC exhibits histogenetic and genomic links to pulmonary carcinoids in 55% of cases, is uniformly ASCL1-positive, and has distinct therapeutic vulnerabilities including high DLL3/SEZ6 expression and potential sensitivity to immune checkpoint inhibitors and temozolomide.

## Key Findings

### F1: Atypical SCLC (RB1+/TP53+) comprises 3% of SCLC and arises exclusively in never/light smokers

**Finding:** Among 600 consecutive de novo SCLC patients undergoing MSK-IMPACT, 20 (3%) had dual RB1/TP53 proficiency by integrated genomic and IHC analysis; all were never or light smokers (≤10 pack-years), in contrast to the rest of the cohort (88% RB1−/TP53−, predominantly smokers).

**Detail:** RB1 proficiency required absence of genomic RB1 alterations plus retained pRb expression by IHC plus D-type cyclin–high/p16-low profile. TP53 proficiency required wild-type p53 IHC and absence of genomic TP53 alterations. Mean age 53 years vs. 67 years for conventional SCLC (p<0.0001). Mean Ki67 69% vs. 87% (p<0.0001). All presented with metastatic disease (distant in 16, regional nodes in 4). TMB was remarkably low (<2 mutations/Mb) in most cases; mutational signatures lacked tobacco signature.

**Genes / cancer types:** RB1, TP53 · Atypical SCLC, smoking-associated SCLC

### F2: Chromothripsis is the hallmark genomic mechanism of aSCLC (84% of non-MSI cases)

**Finding:** WGS confirmed extensive chromothripsis—massive localized chromosome shattering—in 10/11 WGS cases (all except one MSI-H case). Oscillating CNA patterns and average 565 SVs per case (up to >1,900) were characteristic. Using tNGS-based oscillating CNA detection, chromothripsis was identified in 16/19 (84%) non-MSI aSCLC overall.

**Detail:** Chromothripsis involved an average of 2 chromosomes per case (range 1–4). Chromosome 11 was most commonly affected (35%), invariably co-occurring with chromothripsis on chromosome 3. Chromosome 12 was second (20%), and chromosome 1 third (15%). For comparison, chromothripsis prevalence and extent in LUAD and RB1−/TP53− SCLC WGS cohorts were significantly lower. A tNGS detection approach based on oscillating CNAs showed 100% specificity and 77% sensitivity vs. WGS gold standard.

**Genes / cancer types:** CCND1, CCND2, CDK4, MDM2, MYCL1, MEN1, ARID1A, ATM, TGFBR2 · Atypical SCLC

### F3: Chromothripsis drives ecDNA amplification of cell-cycle oncogenes mimicking RB1/TP53 loss

**Finding:** Chromothripsis on chromosome 11 produced CCND1 amplification (high CN, up to 125 copies) in 30% of aSCLC; chromosome 12 chromothripsis produced co-amplification of CCND2/CDK4/MDM2 in 15%; chromosome 1 chromothripsis produced MYCL1 amplification in 10%. These amplifications were mutually exclusive and confirmed as extrachromosomal DNA (ecDNA/"double-minutes") by FISH in all tested cases.

**Detail:** Amplifications were confirmed by protein overexpression (IHC) and mRNA overexpression (RNA-seq). CDK4 amplification was absent in conventional SCLC (0%; p=0.0006) and rare in carcinoids (1%; p=0.01). MDM2 co-amplification alongside CDK4/CCND2 on chromosome 12 phenocopies TP53 inactivation. Cases with chromosome 12 chromothripsis showed paradoxical p16 overexpression (p16-high/pRb-wild-type), hypothesized to reflect extreme CDK4-mediated pRb functional inhibition. ATM mutations were enriched in aSCLC vs. carcinoids (30% vs. 8%; p=0.008) and SCLC (30% vs. 3%; p=0.0003), potentially facilitating DNA break permissiveness.

**Genes / cancer types:** CCND1, CCND2, CDK4, MDM2, MYCL1, ATM · Atypical SCLC

### F4: aSCLC has histogenetic links to pulmonary carcinoids (55% of cases)

**Finding:** 55% of aSCLC exhibited evidence of histogenetic relationship with pulmonary carcinoids, including: (1) co-occurring carcinoid histotype in primary/intrathoracic samples in 5 patients (SCLC in metastases, carcinoid in primary); (2) OTP expression (carcinoid-specific marker, absent in conventional SCLC) by IHC/RNA-seq; and/or (3) carcinoid-characteristic genomic alterations (MEN1, EIF1AX, ARID1A).

**Detail:** All evaluated aSCLC were exclusively ASCL1-positive (a marker of ASCL1-expressing carcinoid subset), and YAP1 expression was entirely absent—contrasting with nsSCLC (RB1−/TP53−), which showed YAP1 enrichment (marker of NSCLC-to-SCLC plasticity). Matching chromothripsis in carcinoid primary and SCLC metastatic samples in 2 patients suggests chromothripsis precedes but does not cause the histotype transition, which may require additional epigenetic reprogramming.

**Genes / cancer types:** MEN1, EIF1AX, ARID1A, ASCL1 (HASH1), OTP · Atypical SCLC, Pulmonary carcinoid

### F5: Two distinct SCLC pathogenesis pathways in never-smokers identified

**Finding:** Never-smoker SCLC includes two molecularly distinct subsets: (1) aSCLC (RB1+/TP53+): carcinoid-to-SCLC pathway, driven by chromothripsis, ASCL1-positive, YAP1-negative, no APOBEC signature; (2) nsSCLC (RB1−/TP53−, n=18): NSCLC-to-SCLC pathway, EGFR-mutant (39%) or KRAS-mutant (6%), adenocarcinoma histologic components (56%), APOBEC signature enriched, YAP1+.

**Detail:** These two pathways are genomically and clinically non-overlapping. APOBEC mutagenesis signature was enriched in nsSCLC but absent in aSCLC. Chromothripsis-based oscillating CNA was absent in nsSCLC. nsSCLC had survival outcomes similar to smoking-related SCLC, whereas aSCLC had intermediate survival between conventional SCLC and atypical carcinoids.

**Genes / cancer types:** EGFR, KRAS, RB1, TP53, YAP1 · Never-smoker SCLC (both subtypes)

### F6: aSCLC has distinct therapeutic profile—lower platinum response, potential TMZ and ICI sensitivity

**Finding:** Only 33% of aSCLC patients achieved complete or partial response to platinum-based chemotherapy (13% CR, 20% PR), well below the expected ~70% for conventional SCLC. Subset analysis suggested sensitivity to immune checkpoint inhibitors (3/5 treated patients on ICI for 2–>5 years) and temozolomide (4/6 treated for >10 months). All evaluated aSCLC samples expressed high DLL3 (mean H-score 278) and SEZ6 (mean H-score 240), consistent with ASCL1-positive subtype.

**Detail:** Low TMB in most aSCLC (below ICI threshold) but ICI responses were observed, possibly reflecting tumor microenvironment features. MGMT (O6-methylguanine-DNA methyltransferase) was inversely correlated with TMZ treatment duration in the two longest responders (A01, A07). High DLL3 expression makes aSCLC potential candidates for DLL3-targeted ADCs (e.g., rovalpituzumab tesirine, Rova-T) and bispecifics (e.g., tarlatamab).

**Genes / cancer types:** DLL3, SEZ6, MGMT, ASCL1 · Atypical SCLC

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Does chromothripsis in pulmonary carcinoids predate the SCLC phenotype and can it serve as an early marker of carcinoid malignant progression risk?
- What epigenetic events (beyond chromothripsis) trigger the carcinoid-to-SCLC phenotypic transition in aSCLC?
- Can oscillating CNA pattern in clinical NGS panels become a standardized clinical test for chromothripsis detection in aSCLC and other cancer types?
- Do the ecDNA amplifications in aSCLC lead to gene dosage heterogeneity that drives drug resistance (given ecDNA's ability to rapidly shed drug-resistance genes)?
- Are DLL3-targeting therapies (tarlatamab, rovalpituzumab) clinically active in aSCLC given consistently high DLL3 expression?
- Does the ATM enrichment in aSCLC provide a therapeutic window for ATM inhibitors or PARP inhibitors in combination strategies?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Small cohort (20 patients) due to rarity of aSCLC; insufficient for robust survival or treatment response correlations.
- WGS was feasible for only 11 patients; tNGS-based chromothripsis detection has 77% sensitivity vs. WGS gold standard.
- RNA-seq available for only 7 patients, limiting transcriptomic characterization.
- Germline data evaluable for only 17 of 20 patients, leaving predisposing germline alterations partially uncharacterized.
- The mechanism of carcinoid-to-SCLC transition (what epigenetic triggers superimpose on chromothripsis) remains undefined.
- Treatment response data is retrospective and from heterogeneous treatment regimens with small numbers per regimen.
