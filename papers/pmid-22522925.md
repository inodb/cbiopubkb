## Summary

The METABRIC study performed an integrated copy-number and gene-expression analysis of 997 discovery and 995 validation primary breast tumours with long-term clinical follow-up, representing the largest such integrative cohort at the time. Somatic copy number aberrations (CNAs) dominated the expression landscape in both *cis* and *trans*, affecting ~40% of genes genome-wide. Unsupervised joint clustering of CNA and expression profiles revealed 10 integrative clusters (IntClust 1–10) that cut across PAM50 intrinsic subtypes and carried distinct disease-specific survival trajectories reproducible in the independent validation cohort. Notable findings include a high-risk ER-positive 11q13/14 *cis*-acting subgroup (IntClust 2) and a genomically quiescent CNA-devoid subgroup (IntClust 4) driven by a TCR-deletion-mediated adaptive immune response.

## Key Findings

### F1: CNAs Dominate the Breast Cancer Expression Landscape in Cis and Trans

**Finding:** Somatic CNAs, germline CNVs, and SNPs were associated with ~40% (11,198/28,609) of expression probes genome-wide. CNAs were associated with the greatest number of expression profiles, with 5,401 genes significantly associated in *cis* and 5,462 in *trans* (Šidák-adjusted P < 0.0001). Whereas *cis*-associations were stronger per gene, *trans*-acting loci modulated a larger total number of mRNAs.

**Detail:** Cis loci were defined as variants within a 3-Mb window around the gene of interest; trans loci were outside that window. CNAs explained the largest share of genome-wide expression variation, while SNPs explained a greater proportion per gene. Off-diagonal CNA–expression association patterns were detected on chromosomes 1q, 7p, 8, 11q, 14q, 16, 17q, and 20q. Trans-acting aberration hotspots (CNAs associated with >30 mRNAs) could be grouped into pathway modules highlighting both known drivers (ERBB2, MYC) and novel loci. The T-cell-receptor loci on chromosomes 7 (TRG) and 14 (TRA) were the largest *trans* hotspots, each modulating hundreds of immune-related mRNAs.

**Genes / cancer types:** `ERBB2`, `MYC`, `TRG`, `TRA` · Breast cancer

### F2: Ten Integrative Clusters (IntClust 1–10) Define Novel Breast Cancer Subgroups

**Finding:** Joint latent-variable clustering of 1,000 top *cis*-associated genes across copy number and expression data in the discovery cohort (n=997) identified 10 integrative clusters (IntClust 1–10) selected by Dunn's index. Each cluster was typified by well-defined, recurrent CNA signatures and split many PAM50 intrinsic subtypes, revealing finer biological stratification.

**Detail:** A nearest-shrunken-centroids classifier (754 features) trained on the discovery set was applied to the independent validation cohort (n=995), assigning comparable proportions to all 10 subgroups with nearly identical copy number profiles (validated by in-group proportion, IGP). Kaplan–Meier plots and Cox proportional hazard models demonstrated subgroup-specific disease-specific survival outcomes reproducible in both cohorts. Subgroups span the full spectrum from excellent prognosis (IntClust 3, IntClust 4) to very poor prognosis (IntClust 2, IntClust 5). IntClust 3 (n=156, luminal A enriched, low genomic instability) and IntClust 4 (n=167, CNA-devoid) showed the best outcomes; IntClust 2 and IntClust 5 the worst.

**Genes / cancer types:** `CCND1`, `ERBB2`, `MYC`, `ZNF703` · Breast cancer

### F3: High-Risk 11q13/14 Cis-Acting ER-Positive Subgroup (IntClust 2)

**Finding:** IntClust 2 (n=45 discovery, ~4.5%) is a distinctly high-risk ER-positive luminal subgroup defined by amplification of the 11q13/14 locus. It exhibited the steepest early mortality trajectory among ER-positive clusters, with hazard ratios of 3.62 (95% CI 1.905–6.878) in discovery and 3.353 (95% CI 1.381–8.141) in validation versus IntClust 3.

**Detail:** All 45 discovery members harboured amplifications in the 11q13/14 region; high frequencies of CCND1 amplification (n=39) and EMSY amplification (n=34) were observed. Two separate amplicons were delineated: one centred on CCND1 (11q13.3) and a second at 11q13.5–11q14.1 spanning UVRAG–GAB2 and centred on PAK1, RSF1, C11orf67, and INTS4. The subgroup showed enrichment for cell-cycle-related alterations involving the G1/S transition (BTG family proteins, CCND1, PPP2R1B, E2F2). The aggressive pathophysiology is likely driven by a cassette of co-amplified genes rather than a single oncogene.

### F4: CNA-Devoid Subgroup (IntClust 4) with Adaptive Immune Response Signature

**Finding:** IntClust 4 (n=167, ~17% of discovery cohort) had an essentially flat genome-wide copy number landscape ('CNA-devoid') composed of both ER-positive and ER-negative cases and was associated with favourable prognosis. A significant proportion exhibited extensive lymphocytic infiltration, and the subgroup displayed a strong immune/inflammation gene expression signature.

**Detail:** Trans-acting deletion hotspots at T-cell receptor loci TRG (chr7) and TRA (chr14) were present at nearly twice the frequency (~20% of cases) in IntClust 4 compared to other subtypes. TCR locus deletions were significantly associated with severe lymphocytic infiltration (χ² P < 10⁻⁹ and P < 10⁻⁸, respectively). Trans-associated mRNAs from these hotspots were significantly enriched for T-cell activation, proliferation, dendritic cell presentation, leukocyte activation, OX40 signalling, and cytotoxic T-lymphocyte-mediated apoptosis. These findings indicate that somatic TCR locus deletions (reflecting mature, rearranged T-lymphocytes) drive a coordinated adaptive immune response that characterises this genomically quiescent subgroup.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TRG`, `TRA` · Breast cancer (ER-positive and ER-negative mixed)

### F5: Novel Deletion-Driven Tumor Suppressors: PPP2R2A, MTAP, MAP2K4

**Finding:** Beyond known deletion targets (PTEN), the expression-outlier CNA landscape identified three recurrently deleted loci harboring putative novel breast cancer tumor suppressors: PPP2R2A (8p21), MTAP (9p21), and MAP2K4 (17p11). Each exhibits heterozygous and homozygous deletions that drive loss of transcript expression.

**Detail:** PPP2R2A encodes a B-regulatory subunit of the PP2A mitotic exit holoenzyme and is specifically lost in mitotic ER-positive (luminal B) cancers, with its dysregulation adding pathophysiology to this subtype. MTAP at 9p21 is frequently co-deleted with tumor suppressors CDKN2A and CDKN2B in multiple cancer types; this co-deletion was confirmed here. MAP2K4 (MKK4, 17p11) is a p38/JNK dual-specificity kinase previously proposed as a recessive cancer gene: this study provides the first evidence of recurrent MAP2K4 deletion with concordant outlying expression in predominantly ER-positive primary breast tumors, including confirmed homozygous deletions, strengthening its candidacy as a tumor suppressor.

**Genes / cancer types:** `PPP2R2A`, `MTAP`, `MAP2K4`, `CDKN2A`, `CDKN2B` · Breast cancer

### F6: ERBB2-Amplified IntClust 5 Shows Worst Overall Disease-Specific Survival

**Finding:** IntClust 5 (n=94) encompasses ERBB2-amplified cancers including both HER2-enriched ER-negative and ER-positive luminal cases, refining the classical HER2-enriched intrinsic subtype. This subgroup had the worst disease-specific survival at both 5 and 15 years and the highest hazard ratios of any cluster (discovery HR 3.899, 95% CI 2.234–6.804; validation HR 4.447, 95% CI 2.284–8.661 vs. IntClust 3).

**Detail:** Patients in METABRIC were enrolled before widespread trastuzumab use, providing an unconfounded natural history. The integrative clustering grouped luminal ER-positive ERBB2-amplified patients together with classical HER2-enriched cases, potentially identifying a broader population that might benefit from HER2-targeted therapy. The cluster demonstrates that copy-number-driven HER2 amplification, irrespective of PAM50 HER2-enriched classification, marks the poorest prognosis subgroup in this population-level cohort.

### F7: Basal-Like IntClust 10 and Chromosome 5q Trans-Acting Mitotic Network

**Finding:** The majority of basal-like tumours formed IntClust 10 (n=96), a high-genomic-instability subgroup with characteristic CNA features (5q loss, 8q gain, 10p gain, 12p gain) and relatively good long-term outcomes after 5 years. A chromosome 5q deletion restricted to this subgroup drives a broad *trans*-acting mitotic gene network.

**Detail:** The 5q deletion signal was detected via continuous copy number correlation (not discrete eQTL framework), modulating coordinate transcriptional control of genomic/chromosomal instability and cell cycle regulation. Trans-regulated genes include AURKB, BCL2, BUB1, CDC20, CDC45, CHEK1, FOXM1, IGF1R, KIF2C, RAD51AP1, TTK (MPS1), and UBE2C — consistent with the high mitotic index of basal-like tumours. TTK/MPS1, a dual-specificity kinase assisting AURKB in chromosome alignment, was upregulated. This subtype-specific trans module contrasts with the predominantly cis-acting 11q13/14 subgroup, illustrating that different genomic mechanisms underlie distinct molecular subtypes.

**Genes / cancer types:** `AURKB`, `FOXM1`, `CHEK1`, `TTK`, `BCL2` · Breast cancer (basal-like)

## Open Questions

- Can the 10 IntClust subgroups be used prospectively in clinical trial design to enrich for treatment-responsive populations, particularly IntClust 2 and IntClust 5?
- What are the somatic single-nucleotide mutation profiles within each IntClust, especially in the CNA-devoid IntClust 4 population (~17% of patients)?
- Do TCR locus deletions in IntClust 4 reflect truly tumor-infiltrating mature T-lymphocytes, and can checkpoint immunotherapy exploit this immune microenvironment?
- Are the novel tumor suppressors PPP2R2A, MTAP, and MAP2K4 therapeutically actionable (e.g., synthetic lethality approaches)?
- How do the IntClust subgroups map onto response to specific chemotherapeutic regimens or targeted therapies beyond trastuzumab?
- Can IntClust classification be implemented using lower-cost assays (e.g., shallow whole-genome sequencing or targeted panels) for clinical deployment?

## Limitations

- No whole-exome or whole-genome sequencing; SNV landscape within IntClust subgroups is inferred from Sanger TP53 sequencing only.
- None of the HER2+ patients received trastuzumab, limiting applicability of IntClust 5 survival data to the modern treatment era.
- Microarray-based gene expression (Illumina HT-12 v3) lacks the dynamic range and isoform resolution of RNA-seq.
- The integrative clustering used only the top 1,000 *cis*-associated genes, potentially missing subgroup-defining features driven by *trans* effects or rare events.
- Cohort is predominantly of European ancestry (UK and Canada), limiting generalizability to diverse populations.
- The CNA-devoid subgroup (~17%) and rare amplification events (<1% each) are likely underpowered for subgroup-specific mechanistic conclusions.
