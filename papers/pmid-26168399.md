## Summary

The first comprehensive whole-genome sequencing study of SCLC, profiling 110 human tumors with matched normals plus SNP array data in 142 cases and transcriptome data in 81. Near-universal bi-allelic inactivation of TP53 and RB1 is the obligatory founding event. Novel findings include: oncogenic TP73 rearrangements generating N-terminally truncated p73 isoforms (TP73Δex2/3) in 7% of cases; NOTCH family gene inactivation in 25% (validated as a tumor suppressor and master regulator of neuroendocrine differentiation in mouse models); CREBBP/EP300 mutations; and MYC-family (MYCL1, MYCN, MYC) and FGFR1 amplifications. Rare kinase mutations (BRAF, KIT, PIK3CA) suggest individual patients may benefit from targeted agents. Two tumors with wild-type RB1 showed chromothripsis driving CCND1 overexpression as an alternative Rb inactivation mechanism. The study was performed at University of Cologne (non-TCGA) and the companion cBioPortal study is sclc_ucologne_2015.

## Key Findings

### F1: Obligatory bi-allelic inactivation of TP53 and RB1 in virtually all SCLCs

**Finding:** Both TP53 and RB1 are bi-allelically inactivated in 100% and 93% of tumors respectively (among the 108 without chromothripsis). Inactivation involves diverse mechanisms: point mutations, splice-site mutations, translocations, homozygous deletions, hemizygous loss, and copy-neutral LOH — many requiring WGS to detect.

**Detail:** Previous exome studies had reported TP53 (up to 90%) and RB1 (up to 65%) alteration rates; WGS revealed that essentially all remaining cases had inactivating events missed by exome sequencing (e.g., complex RB1 translocations, intronic splice mutations confirmed by RNA-seq). Two exceptional cases with wild-type RB1 showed chromothripsis generating CCND1 overexpression, providing cyclin D1 as an alternative mechanism of Rb-pathway inactivation. TP53 and RB1 follow a classical Knudson two-hit paradigm in SCLC.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53`, `RB1`, `CCND1` · SCLC

**Data availability:** `available`

**Tool support:** `native`

### F2: NOTCH family inactivation (25% of SCLC) — a tumor suppressor and neuroendocrine differentiation regulator

**Finding:** NOTCH1, NOTCH2, NOTCH3, and NOTCH4 are inactivated (predominantly damaging mutations in extracellular domains) in 25% of human SCLC cases. Functional mouse studies demonstrate that Notch activation dramatically reduces tumor number and extends survival, and abrogates neuroendocrine gene expression.

**Detail:** Notch mutations were mutually exclusive with CREBBP, EP300, TP73, and RBL1/RBL2 mutations, suggesting parallel tumor-suppressive mechanisms. In Trp53;Rb1;Rbl2 triple-knockout mice, conditional Notch2-ICD expression reduced tumor number (p<0.001) and extended median survival (210→274 days). N1ICD expression in human and mouse SCLC cell lines inhibited growth and induced cell cycle arrest. Notch activation suppressed neuroendocrine markers (ASCL1, DLK1) and upregulated Hes1/Hey targets. Two SCLC transcriptional clusters were identified: majority (~77%) with high neuroendocrine markers and low Notch activity; minority (~23%) with intermediate neuroendocrine phenotype.

**Genes / cancer types:** `NOTCH1`, `NOTCH2`, `NOTCH3`, `NOTCH4`, `ASCL1`, `DLK1` · SCLC

### F3: Oncogenic TP73 rearrangements generating dominant-negative p73Δex2/3 isoforms in 13% of SCLC

**Finding:** Somatic genomic rearrangements at the TP73 locus (chromosome 1) create intragenic fusions that produce N-terminally truncated p73 variants (p73Δex2 and p73Δex2/3) in 7% of cases, with additional point mutations yielding 13% overall. These dominant-negative variants can suppress both wild-type p73 and p53.

**Detail:** Breakpoints cluster in introns 1, 2, and 3 of TP73. RNA-seq confirmed production of p73Δex2 and p73Δex2/3 transcripts only in cases with genomic rearrangements. p73Δex2/3 lacks the transactivation domain and acts as a confirmed oncogene in vivo. A further variant, p73Δex10, produces a C-terminal truncation with dominant-negative activity. This is the first report of precise genomic rearrangements generating these specific TP73 variants in human cancer.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP73` · SCLC

**Data availability:** `partial`
> TP73 point mutations present in `sclc_ucologne_2015`; structural rearrangements (intragenic fusions) not captured in standard alteration tables — require raw sequencing data.

**Tool support:** `custom`
> Standard cBioPortal Mutations tab shows TP73 point mutations only. Detection of the oncogenic rearrangements requires WGS data access and custom analysis.

**Concepts:** #TP73 #structural-variants #dominant-negative #oncogene

### F4: CREBBP and EP300 mutations — recurrent histone acetyltransferase inactivation (mutually exclusive with each other and with NOTCH)

**Finding:** CREBBP and EP300 (histone acetyltransferases) carry significantly clustered inactivating mutations and recurrent translocations in SCLC, are mutually exclusive with each other and with NOTCH mutations, suggesting they represent an alternative but parallel tumor-suppressive pathway.

**Detail:** CREBBP/EP300 mutations were confirmed in previous exome sequencing studies; WGS revealed additional inactivating translocations. Their mutual exclusivity with NOTCH family mutations across the cohort (and in mouse SCLC models) implies functionally equivalent contributions to SCLC pathogenesis. CREBBP mutations were also confirmed in Trp53/Rb1/Rbl2 mouse tumors.

**Genes / cancer types:** `CREBBP`, `EP300` · SCLC

### F5: MYC-family amplification, FGFR1 amplification, and rare kinase mutations — actionability in small subsets

**Finding:** Recurrent amplifications of MYCL1, MYCN, and MYC, as well as FGFR1 and IRS2, occur in subsets of SCLC. Rare kinase mutations (BRAF, KIT, PIK3CA) present in individual tumors may offer patient-specific targeted therapy opportunities.

**Detail:** MYC-family amplification is a hallmark of SCLC (previously reported). FGFR1 amplification (focal) occurs in a subset (2% focal IRS2 amplification also noted). Among 110 tumors, 4 carried mutations in known oncogenic drivers: BRAF (associated with vemurafenib response), KIT (imatinib), PIK3CA (PI3K inhibitors). CDKN2A homozygous deletion occurs in cases with both bi-allelic TP53/RB1 loss and hemizygous RB1 loss, adding another cell-cycle layer. Subclonal diversity in SCLC is ~3-fold lower than in lung adenocarcinoma, suggesting pronounced differences in tumor evolution.

## Open Questions

- Can NOTCH pathway activators or Notch-based interventions be therapeutically exploited in NOTCH-inactivated SCLC?
- Do TP73Δex2/3-expressing SCLCs respond differently to p53-targeting approaches or MDM2 inhibitors?
- Why is subclonal diversity so low in SCLC compared to adenocarcinoma? Does this reflect a more deterministic evolutionary path?
- What distinguishes the two transcriptional subtypes (high vs. moderate neuroendocrine) at the clinical and therapeutic level?
- Are CREBBP/EP300-mutated SCLCs sensitive to HDAC inhibitors or BET bromodomain inhibitors?

## Limitations

- Non-TCGA cohort (University of Cologne + partners); sourced from multiple institutions with potentially heterogeneous tissue handling.
- Only 5 relapse/treated samples; resistance mechanisms not captured.
- SCLC is rarely resected; surgical specimens enriched for early-stage tumors, underrepresenting advanced/metastatic disease biology.
- Transcriptome data available for only 81/110 WGS cases; expression-based findings less powered.
- Structural variant detection (TP73 rearrangements, RB1 translocations) requires WGS; not reproducible from exome data alone.
