## Summary

The first large-scale functional precision medicine landscape of sarcoma, using patient-derived tumor organoids (PDTOs) from 194 specimens collected from 126 patients spanning 24 distinct sarcoma subtypes at UCLA Health. A high-throughput organoid drug screening pipeline with results available within one week from surgery tests >400 compounds per sample (single agents and combinations). Drug sensitivity correlates with sarcoma subtype, treatment history, age, and disease stage. Subtype-specific and patient-specific drug vulnerabilities are identified. At least one FDA-approved or NCCN-recommended effective regimen is identified for 59% of specimens, demonstrating actionability superior to genomic-only precision medicine (~30%). PDTO responses correlate significantly with clinical outcomes (RÂ˛=0.921 for time to next treatment; n=5 matched cases).

## Key Findings

### F1: PDTOs faithfully replicate sarcoma histology and molecular features; 93% take rate across 24 subtypes

**Finding:** Patient-derived tumor organoids were successfully established from diverse bone and soft tissue sarcomas including subtypes traditionally resistant to in vitro culture (chordoma, chondrosarcoma). Organoids replicated histopathological features (small round blue cells in Ewing, vacuolated cells in chordoma, spindle cells in rhabdomyosarcoma/MPNST) and maintained transcriptomic concordance with parent tumors (Spearman r >0.8 for all 32 paired RNA-seq samples, median 0.87). Key oncogenic drivers including MDM2 amplification (in well-differentiated liposarcoma) were preserved.

**Detail:** 194 specimens were processed; 124 reached the screening stage. Of these, 10 showed no growth, yielding a 93% take rate. Cryopreservation of 40 samples (including COVID-era collections) was successfully implemented, enabling cross-institutional extension. Machine learning-based brightfield segmentation (U-net) was used for quantitative growth tracking â€” chordoma and LMS grew minimally over 5 days while myxofibrosarcoma, RMS, epithelioid sarcoma, and CIC-rearranged sarcoma showed exponential growth. This heterogeneous ex vivo growth dynamics mirrors known clinical proliferative behavior across sarcoma subtypes.

### F2: 80% of screened sarcomas have at least one significant drug response; distinct subtype-specific and patient-specific patterns

**Finding:** Of 92 specimens screened against â‰Ą10 compounds, 80.4% (74/92) ranked in the top 5% of viability scores for at least one tested regimen. Only 19.6% showed no significant response (typically tested on smaller panels). Osteosarcoma PDTOs were significantly more sensitive than pan-sarcoma average to ceralasertib (ATR inhibitor), topotecan, cabozantinib, and everolimus. Chordoma PDTOs were significantly more resistant to everolimus, alvocidib, apitolisib, and bortezomib, but more sensitive to TAK-285 (EGFR/HER2/Aurora B/MEK), BI-D1870 (RSK inhibitor), and A-769662 (AMPK activator).

**Detail:** Sarcoma organoids generally did not cluster by histology in pathway-level drug response analysis â€” patient identity (intra-patient clustering) was stronger than histological subtype clustering for samples from the same patient. Known response patterns were recapitulated ex vivo: leiomyosarcoma PDTOs were significantly less sensitive to topotecan (consistent with clinical trial evidence of topotecan inefficacy in LMS); RMS PDTOs were more sensitive to pazopanib and trametinib; osteosarcoma PDTOs showed expected sensitivity to MAP (methotrexate, doxorubicin, cisplatin) components. Novel findings include chordoma sensitivity to RSK/AMPK axis (BI-D1870, A-769662), supporting investigation of this pathway in this treatment-refractory cancer.

### F3: Functional screening identifies actionable regimens for 59% of specimens, outperforming genomic-only approaches

**Finding:** Cross-referencing the top-5 most effective regimens per sample (within the top 10% of responders across all samples for each drug) with FDA approval status and NCCN guidelines identifies at least one actionable regimen for 59% of sarcoma specimens (57/97 screened) and 58% of patients (43/74). Only ~30% of sarcoma patients benefit from genomic precision medicine approaches â€” PDTOs provide a complementary, higher-yield strategy.

**Detail:** Of 203 unique drug-diagnosis pairs identified: 4% are FDA-approved for the same sarcoma type found sensitive ex vivo; 31% are approved for other cancers; 1% are in clinical trials for the specific indication; 21% are in trials for other cancers; 43% are preclinical or terminated. Osteosarcoma has the most established NCCN guidelines; MAP (first-line) sensitivity was validated in osteosarcoma PDTOs. Gemcitabine + docetaxel was among top 5 effective regimens for ~15% (3/20) of osteosarcoma PDTOs. Most cSCC-subtypes (DSRCT, CIC-rearranged sarcoma) have no NCCN guidelines â€” PDTOs represent the only avenue for evidence generation in ultra-rare subtypes.

### F4: PDTO responses correlate with clinical outcomes â€” necrosis, PFS, and time to next treatment

**Finding:** PDTO drug sensitivity predicts clinical response in two independent validation analyses: (1) MAP sensitivity in treatment-naive osteosarcoma biopsies correlates with % necrosis at resection post-neoadjuvant MAP (SARC0041: 99% necrosis, SARC0064: 95% necrosis vs. SARC0135: 60% necrosis, concordant with lower/higher residual viability ex vivo), and with long-term outcomes (SARC0041 and SARC0064 disease-free at 1400â€“1500 days; SARC0135 relapsed within 200 days). (2) Normalized organoid viability vs. time to next treatment (TTNT) for 5 matched cases yields RÂ˛=0.921 (p=0.009).

**Detail:** The TTNT correlation, while limited to n=5 cases, spans a range of histotypes (osteosarcoma, rhabdomyosarcoma) and regimens (bortezomib+panobinostat, gemcitabine+docetaxel, mocetinostat+vinorelbine, irinotecan+temozolomide). TTNT was normalized to the immediately preceding treatment to control for disease trajectory. A clinical trial (NCT06064682, PREMOST) has been initiated to prospectively validate MAP response prediction in osteosarcoma (Arm 1) and identify active drugs in advanced osteosarcoma (Arm 2). PDX correlation for 3 overlapping cases showed good-to-moderate concordance, with PDTOs offering the advantage of capturing intra-patient heterogeneity across multiple tumor sites simultaneously.

### F5: Intra-patient tumor heterogeneity in drug response captured by multi-lesion PDTO profiling

**Finding:** For 21% of patients with multiple lesions, PDTOs from the same patient show both concordant and discordant drug responses. Tumor-to-tumor variation within a single patient (measured by Pearson correlation of drug responses across overlapping compounds) ranges from 0.64â€“0.87, with some lesions from the same surgery showing markedly distinct sensitivity profiles. This mirrors clinically observed inter-lesion heterogeneity and confirms that single-biopsy precision medicine approaches may systematically miss spatial heterogeneity.

**Detail:** SARC0075 (epithelioid sarcoma, 3 lesions from same surgery): lesions 1 and 4 respond to ~23% of drugs (Pearson 0.84), while lesion 2 responds to only 7.7% and shows strong everolimus resistance. Complex CNV heterogeneity (whole-genome duplications, chromosomal arm gains/losses) between lesions from SARC0075 was confirmed genomically. SARC0139 (LMS, 2 metastases from single procedure): high Pearson correlation (0.80) consistent with transcriptomic similarity. Genomic profiling of SARC0075 revealed few shared pathogenic SNVs across lesions (ARID2, CRTC1, MAP3K1), with largely non-overlapping complex CNV profiles, suggesting late-stage divergent evolution.

## Open Questions

- Can the PREMOST trial (NCT06064682) prospectively validate MAP response prediction in osteosarcoma and support regulatory approval of organoid-guided treatment selection?
- What genomic features (beyond PIK3CA mutations) predict PDTO drug sensitivity, enabling biomarker-driven patient selection without functional screening?
- Do RSK/AMPK pathway inhibitors (BI-D1870, A-769662) show clinical activity in chordoma, a tumor type with no approved systemic therapy?
- How can intra-patient heterogeneity in drug response (multiple lesion discordance) be managed in a clinical decision framework â€” which lesion should guide treatment selection for disseminated disease?
- Can cryopreserved PDTO biobanks enable cross-institutional studies of ultra-rare sarcoma subtypes (DSRCT, CIC-rearranged) where any single center sees <5 patients/year?
- Does PDTO-based screening predict response better than genomic precision medicine in a head-to-head prospective trial across all sarcoma subtypes?
- What is the false-negative rate of PDTO screening â€” how often does an effective drug fail to show ex vivo activity, and what are the underlying mechanisms?

## Limitations

- Clinical outcome correlation is limited to n=5 matched cases for TTNT and n=3 for neoadjuvant MAP response â€” underpowered for definitive conclusions; prospective trial needed
- No normalization for proliferation rate â€” highly proliferative sarcomas may show artifactually higher sensitivity to broadly cytotoxic agents (especially in rapidly growing subtypes)
- RECIST-based outcome measures not available; TTNT used as surrogate which may not capture all disease progression signals (FDG-PET, functional MRI not used)
- Single-institution (UCLA) study; patient population (age, subtype distribution, referral patterns) may not reflect population-level sarcoma epidemiology
- Drug library focused on FDA-approved agents and known targeted therapies; novel mechanisms not systematically covered
- Average of 117 drugs per sample but with high variance (6â€“423); samples with small panels may have missed effective regimens
- 10 samples (of 124 attempted) showed no ex vivo growth â€” these cannot be screened, potentially biasing against indolent tumors
- Comparison with PDX models limited to 3 cases; PDX concordance is expected to be higher for samples with sufficient engraftment biology
