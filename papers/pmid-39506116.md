## Summary

This paper describes MSK-CHORD (Clinicogenomic Harmonized Oncologic Real-world Dataset), a publicly available clinicogenomic resource integrating NLP-derived annotations from 705,241 radiology reports with MSK-IMPACT tumor genomics, treatment records, tumor registry, and demographics for 24,950 patients across 5 cancer types (NSCLC, breast, colorectal, prostate, pancreatic). NLP models achieved AUC >0.9 and outperformed billing codes for metastatic site annotation. Multimodal random survival forest models outperform stage-alone and genomics-alone for OS prediction. Key novel findings include: (1) NLP-derived tumor sites are the single most predictive modality for stage IV survival, (2) SETD2 mutation predicts immunotherapy benefit in LUAD even at low TMB, (3) RB1 alteration predicts CNS and liver metastases across cancers. MSK-CHORD is available at cBioPortal study `msk_chord_2024`.

## Key Findings

### F1: NLP models annotate free-text oncology notes with AUC >0.9, outperforming billing codes

**Finding:** Transformer-based NLP models trained on GENIE BPC-curated labels achieve AUC >0.9 and precision/recall >0.78 for annotating radiology, histopathology, and clinical notes; these models generalize across held-out cancer types and outperform billing codes for metastatic site identification (precision/recall improvement 0.03–0.32).

**Detail:** Models annotate: cancer progression, sites of tumors, prior outside treatment (from radiology impressions and clinical notes), hormone receptor/HER2 status (from histopathology/notes), smoking status, Gleason score, PDL1 and MMR status (rule-based). A random sample of discrepant cases reviewed by clinicians found that many original BPC curation labels were incorrect—the NLP model was right. Model performance was training-sample-size dependent; reproductive organ tumor sites had worse performance due to fewer positive examples.

**Genes / cancer types:** N/A (NLP annotation infrastructure), pan-cancer (NSCLC, breast, CRC, prostate, pancreatic)

**Tool support:** `api`

### F2: Multimodal models including NLP-derived tumor sites outperform single-modality (stage, genomics) OS prediction

**Finding:** Random survival forest models integrating all data modalities (stage, genomics, NLP-derived sites, tumor markers, demographics, treatment) outperform stage-alone and genomics-alone models across all 5 cancer types; c-indices range from 0.58 (stage IV pancreatic) to 0.83 (stage I–III breast).

**Detail:** For stage IV cancers, NLP-derived tumor sites alone were the single most prognostic data modality, outperforming genomic drivers in all cancer types. In NSCLC and pancreatic cancer, risk stratification by the multimodal model identified high-risk stage I–III patients with worse predicted survival than low-risk stage IV patients—demonstrating that stage alone is insufficient. Models were validated both by 5-fold internal cross-validation and externally in the non-MSK BPC cohort, with generally consistent performance.

**Genes / cancer types:** N/A (outcome models), NSCLC, breast, CRC, prostate, pancreatic cancer

**Data availability:** `available`
> Tumor site annotations, stage, genomic data, OS outcomes available in `msk_chord_2024`.

**Tool support:** `api`
> Full multimodal modeling requires downloading data via cBioPortal API and running custom RSF pipelines; individual feature–survival correlations are native.

**Concepts:** #real-world-data #survival #pan-cancer-map #molecular-subtypes

### F3: SETD2 mutation predicts immunotherapy benefit in LUAD independent of TMB and histologic subtype

**Finding:** SETD2 driver mutations (3% of LUAD, n=204/5,957) were associated with longer time to next treatment or death following immune checkpoint blockade but not cytotoxic chemotherapy or targeted therapy; this association held in patients with low TMB (<10 mut/Mb) and was validated in two independent cohorts.

**Detail:** SETD2 mutation was also associated with lower rates of CNS metastasis in LUAD (hazard ratio <1) and better OS. SETD2 alterations were positively associated with BRAF and ARID1A alterations, and negatively with EGFR, MDM2, and mucinous subtype. TMB was statistically higher in SETD2-mutant LUAD (Mann-Whitney p=2×10⁻⁹), but the immunotherapy benefit was independent of TMB (persisted at TMB <10 mut/Mb). This makes SETD2 a candidate biomarker for immunotherapy benefit beyond standard TMB/PD-L1 testing.

**Genes / cancer types:** `SETD2`, `BRAF`, `ARID1A`, `EGFR`, `MDM2` · Lung adenocarcinoma (LUAD)

**Data availability:** `available`
> SETD2 alteration data, treatment annotations (NLP-derived), and OS in `msk_chord_2024`.

**Tool support:** `api`
> SETD2 mutation filtering and survival stratification by treatment modality requires API data download and time-to-next-treatment analysis; basic mutation frequency is native.

**Concepts:** #ICI-biomarker #biomarker #KRAS #tumor-microenvironment #real-world-data

### F4: RB1 alteration predicts CNS and liver metastases across multiple cancer types

**Finding:** Oncogenic RB1 alterations were significantly associated with higher rates of CNS and liver metastases (but not bone and lung) in LUAD, hormone-receptor-positive breast cancer, and prostate cancer; RB1 alterations were also enriched in metastatic samples sequenced from brain and liver sites.

**Detail:** Analysis of time-to-specific organ metastasis in 705,241 longitudinally annotated radiology reports showed consistent RB1–metastasis site associations across cancer types (Benjamini-Hochberg q<0.01 after adjustment for prior treatment, stage, and histologic subtype). TP53 and CDKN2A in LUAD were associated with metastases to all sites (pan-metastatic). RTK–RAS pathway alterations in prostate cancer were associated with bone but lower liver metastasis rates.

**Genes / cancer types:** `RB1`, `TP53`, `CDKN2A`, RTK-RAS pathway · LUAD, hormone receptor-positive breast, prostate, CRC (MSS), pancreatic

**Data availability:** `available`
> RB1 alteration data and NLP-derived metastatic site annotations in `msk_chord_2024`.

**Tool support:** `api`
> Time-to-metastasis analysis requires downloading longitudinal radiology NLP annotations; co-occurrence and alteration frequency by cancer type is available natively.

**Concepts:** #TP53 #pan-cancer-map #tumor-microenvironment #real-world-data #biomarker

### F5: MSK-CHORD reveals post-treatment genomic enrichments (ESR1, MET amplification, TP53, clonal hematopoiesis) at scale

**Finding:** MSK-CHORD's size enables detection of known post-treatment genomic enrichments that were statistically underpowered in the BPC alone: ESR1 and CCND1 in hormone-treated breast cancer, EGFR T790M and MET amplification in EGFR-mutant NSCLC, AR and TP53 in prostate cancer, and CHEK2/PPM1D/TP53 CH mutations in systemically treated patients.

**Detail:** NLP-derived prior treatment annotations enabled these analyses in patients treated at multiple institutions or prior to MSK care. For prostate cancer, a dose-dependent association was found between NLP-annotated Gleason grade and TP53, PTEN, and BRCA2 alterations (q<0.05 after multiple hypothesis correction). Discordant MMR/MSI cases (dMMR + MSS or pMMR + MSI, n=10 stage IV CRC patients) had intermediate immunotherapy benefit between clear MSI and clear MSS patients.

**Genes / cancer types:** `ESR1`, `CCND1`, `NF1` (breast); `EGFR T790M`, `MET` (NSCLC); `AR`, `TP53`, `PTEN`, `BRCA2` (prostate); `CHEK2`, `PPM1D`, `TP53` (clonal hematopoiesis); `MLH1/MSH2/MSH6` (CRC MSI/MMR)

**Data availability:** `available`
> All alteration and NLP-derived treatment data in `msk_chord_2024`; clonal hematopoiesis data in previously published MSK-CH subset.

**Tool support:** `api`
> Post-treatment enrichment analysis requires combining NLP treatment annotation with genomic data via API; basic co-mutation queries are native.

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Does the SETD2 immunotherapy benefit extend beyond LUAD to other solid tumors with SETD2 mutations (e.g., RCC, urothelial, CRC), as suggested by its epigenetic mechanism?
- Can NLP-derived metastatic site annotations (rather than just genomics) be incorporated into clinical decision-making tools for surveillance imaging frequency?
- What is the mechanism by which RB1 alteration predicts CNS/liver tropism—is it driven by cell-intrinsic lineage plasticity, TGF-β signaling, or other pathways?
- Can comorbidities and ECOG performance status (not captured in MSK-CHORD) close the remaining performance gap in pancreatic cancer OS models?
- Will an extended version of MSK-CHORD with liquid biopsy (ctDNA) and lab values further improve OS prediction?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Cohort predominantly from a single academic cancer center (MSK) in New York/New Jersey; socioeconomic and demographic diversity may affect generalizability
- Cohort entry (genomic sequencing date) is not random and is enriched for advanced/progressive disease; immortality bias partially addressed by left truncation but may persist
- Comorbidities and performance status—critical clinical variables—not captured in either BPC or MSK-CHORD
- NLP models trained on BPC (academic centers); performance may degrade in community hospital notes with different documentation styles
- Tumor marker models (CA19-9, PSA, etc.) showed worse external validation in non-MSK BPC cohort due to sparser testing—caution for marker-based prognostication
- Whole-genome, RNA-seq, and single-cell data not included; mechanistic insights into metastatic tropism limited
