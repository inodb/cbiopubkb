## Summary

This TCGA multi-platform study characterizes 206 adult soft tissue sarcomas across 6 major types: leiomyosarcoma (LMS, n=80, including 53 STLMS and 27 ULMS), dedifferentiated liposarcoma (DDLPS, n=50), undifferentiated pleomorphic sarcoma (UPS, n=44), myxofibrosarcoma (MFS, n=17), synovial sarcoma (SS, n=10), and malignant peripheral nerve sheath tumor (MPNST, n=5). Three overarching findings emerge: (1) unlike epithelial cancers, sarcomas are driven predominantly by somatic copy-number alterations (SCNAs) with low mutational loads and only 3 significantly mutated genes pan-sarcoma (TP53, ATRX, RB1); (2) integrated molecular clustering defines prognostically distinct subtypes within sarcoma types; and (3) the immune microenvironment — inferred from methylation and mRNA profiles — strongly associates with outcome and suggests biomarkers for checkpoint inhibitor trials. Data are publicly available on cBioPortal (sarc_tcga_pub) and provide the most comprehensive open molecular resource for adult STS research.

## Key Findings

### F1: Adult STS are predominantly copy-number-driven with only 3 pan-sarcoma significantly mutated genes (TP53, ATRX, RB1)

**Finding:** In stark contrast to most epithelial malignancies, adult soft tissue sarcomas (excluding SS) are characterized by complex somatic copy-number alterations (SCNAs) with very low point-mutation burdens (mean 1.06/Mb). MuSiC analysis identifies only 3 significantly mutated genes pan-sarcoma: *TP53*, *ATRX*, and *RB1*. MDM2-p53 and p16-CDK4-RB1 pathway SCNAs are common across types.

**Detail:** DDLPS showed the highest SCNA frequency of any TCGA tumor type (driven by recurrent 12q13-15 amplifications). SS had the fewest SCNAs and mutations. *TP53* mutations most prevalent in LMS (40/80 = 50%). *RB1* deep deletions in 14% LMS, 16% UPS, 24% MFS; *CDKN2A* deep deletions in 8% LMS, 20% UPS, 18% MFS. MDM2 amplification in all DDLPS by definition (100%). Two hypermutated samples carried *MSH6* frameshift or low *MSH2* expression (mismatch repair deficiency). Age-correlated clock-like signatures (COSMIC1 and COSMIC5) dominate in several sarcoma types, suggesting most point mutations are passengers.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53`, `ATRX`, `RB1`, `MDM2`, `CDK4`, `CDKN2A`, `NF1`, `NF2`, `PRKDC` · Adult soft tissue sarcoma (pan-sarcoma)

### F2: DDLPS defined by 12q13-15 amplicon; JUN amplification and DNA hypermethylation associate with worse survival

**Finding:** All 50 DDLPS are defined by *MDM2* amplification (100%), with co-amplification of *CDK4* (92%), *HMGA2* (76%), *FRS2* (96%), and *NAV3* (60%). Integrating SCNA clusters (JUN-amplified K1, TERT-amplified/unstable K2, 6q25.1-amplified K3) and DNA methylation clusters (hypomethylated Meth1 vs. hypermethylated Meth2) defines 3 prognostic groups: K3 (best disease-specific survival), hypomethylated K1+K2, hypermethylated K1+K2 (worst DSS, HR=4.4 for Meth2).

**Detail:** *JUN* amplification (42%) inhibits adipocyte differentiation and increases migration/invasion; it may be a therapeutic target. *ATRX* deletion (30% of DDLPS) is a potential CDK4-inhibitor resistance biomarker (ATRX required for response to CDK4 inhibition in preclinical models). Hypermethylated DDLPS had lower leukocyte fraction and higher Th2 content. Integrating SCNA and methylation status creates a 3-tier prognostic model applicable for clinical stratification and trial design.

**Genes / cancer types:** `MDM2`, `CDK4`, `HMGA2`, `FRS2`, `JUN`, `ATRX`, `TERT`, `CDKN2A` · Dedifferentiated liposarcoma (DDLPS)

### F3: LMS molecular subtypes — ULMS and STLMS are molecularly distinct; PI3K-AKT-MTOR pathway broadly activated

**Finding:** Despite overall genomic similarity, ULMS and STLMS have significantly different DNA methylation and mRNA expression signatures. STLMS iCluster analysis defines two subgroups (C1 and C2) with markedly different DSS (C1 worse, median 6.7 years; C2 not reached; p=0.008). PI3K-AKT-MTOR pathway is broadly activated in LMS: 84% of ULMS + STLMS-C1 have AKT pathway alterations vs. 44% STLMS-C2. miR-181b-5p is an independent predictor of RFS (univariate HR=8.03, adjusted P<0.0001).

**Detail:** LMS-specific features: *TP53* mutation 50%, *RB1* deletion 78% shallow + 14% deep, *PTEN* deletion/mutation in ~18%, recurrent IGF1R amplification, AKT/RICTOR/MTOR amplification/upregulation. STLMS-C1 (worse prognosis) shows hypermethylation, higher IGF1R expression, *MYOCD* amplification (17p11.2-p12, 40%), RB1 mutation enrichment. ULMS has higher DNA damage response score and ESR1 target gene hypomethylation. High miR-181b-5p (>571 RPM) predicts poor RFS independently of subtype and size. MTOR inhibitors (everolimus, temsirolimus) have shown partial efficacy in LMS; dual TORC1/2 and PI3K/MTOR inhibitors may be more effective.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53`, `RB1`, `PTEN`, `AKT`, `MTOR`, `IGF1R`, `RICTOR`, `MYOCD` · Leiomyosarcoma (ULMS and STLMS)

### F4: UPS and MFS represent a molecular continuum; Hippo pathway (VGLL3/YAP1) defines a subset with therapeutic implications

**Finding:** Contrary to their recent classification as distinct clinical entities, UPS and MFS are largely indistinguishable across all molecular platforms. A myxoid stroma gene expression signature better discriminates them than histology. A subset of UPS/MFS shows Hippo pathway activation via *VGLL3* amplification (11%), *YAP1* amplification (3%), and *CCNE1* amplification (10%); a VGLL3/YAP1 target gene signature is strongly expressed in UPS/MFS (p=1×10⁻²⁴). miR-22 downregulation is the strongest miRNA predictor of poor disease-specific survival.

**Detail:** VGLL3 and YAP1 are TEAD transcriptional cofactors in the Hippo pathway, which regulates proliferation and organ size. Hippo pathway inhibitors (e.g., TEAD inhibitors) are in development and represent a rational therapeutic strategy for this subset. The molecular continuum of UPS/MFS supports common treatment approaches for both histologies. In the SARC028 pembrolizumab trial, 40% of UPS cases responded — our study suggests MFS should be specifically explored in PD-1 inhibitor trials given similar molecular profiles. *RB1* deletion and *VGLL3* amplification are the most recurrent SCNAs in UPS/MFS.

**Genes / cancer types:** `VGLL3`, `YAP1`, `CCNE1`, `RB1` · Undifferentiated pleomorphic sarcoma (UPS), Myxofibrosarcoma (MFS)

### F5: Immune microenvironment composition differentially associates with survival across sarcoma types; supports checkpoint inhibitor biomarker development

**Finding:** Immune cell infiltration scores (derived from mRNA expression signatures) are commonly detected in genomically complex DDLPS, LMS, UPS, and MFS, and are strongly associated with clinical outcome in a sarcoma-type-specific manner. NK cells are the only immune cell type to correlate significantly with better DSS across multiple sarcoma types. PD-L1 expression correlates with CD274 copy number (r=0.42, p=4×10⁻¹⁰) and is highest in STLMS. B7-H3, TGFB1, and TIM3 are differentially expressed by sarcoma type and may serve as alternative checkpoint targets.

**Detail:** DDLPS has highest median macrophage and CD8 scores; STLMS has highest median PD-L1 score (significantly higher than ULMS). High Th2 signature in DDLPS associates with worse DSS. High DC/iDC scores in UPS/MFS associate with improved DSS. These data provide rationale for PD-1 blockade specifically in UPS/MFS (with DC/NK signatures), and suggest caution in DDLPS where Th2 dominance may indicate an immunosuppressive microenvironment. Immune signatures validated in an independent 113-sarcoma RNA-seq cohort (Lesluyes 2016).

**Genes / cancer types:** `CD274` (PD-L1), `CD8A`, `TGFB1`, `CD276` (B7-H3), `HAVCR2` (TIM3), `NK cell signature` · Soft tissue sarcoma (pan-sarcoma)

## Open Questions

- Does ATRX status (deletion or mutation in ~30% of DDLPS) predict resistance to CDK4 inhibitors (palbociclib, ribociclib) in clinical trials?
- Can Hippo pathway inhibitors (TEAD inhibitors, now in clinical trials) specifically benefit the VGLL3-amplified/YAP1-activated UPS/MFS subset?
- Do the molecular distinctions between ULMS and STLMS support different systemic therapies, or do combined PI3K/MTOR inhibitors benefit both equally?
- Does PD-L1 expression driven by CD274 copy-number gain (rather than IFN-γ signaling) represent a mechanistically different, potentially less immunotherapy-responsive state?
- Can miR-181b-5p or a multi-miRNA signature be validated prospectively as a prognostic biomarker in LMS?
- What is the clinical relevance of TRIO-TERT fusions (n=3, associated with highest TERT expression) in DDLPS — do they associate with worse outcome?
- Can immune cell type composition (particularly NK cell and DC infiltration) predict response to pembrolizumab in UPS/MFS beyond the SARC028 data?

## Limitations

- Small numbers for some subtypes (SS n=10, MPNST n=5) limit subtype-specific conclusions; SS analysis is descriptive only
- Complex karyotype sarcomas may have undergone additional selection/evolution during tissue collection; frozen tissue quality may have biased molecular data
- MFS samples were enriched for high-grade/epithelioid areas, potentially explaining molecular similarity to UPS
- UPS is a diagnosis of exclusion — some cases may represent misclassified tumors; expert pathology review helps but is not infallible
- MPNST too small (n=5) for robust molecular characterization
- No RNA-fusion detection in SCLC or DDLPS (RNA-seq not performed on all samples for fusion calling)
- Survival analyses for molecular subtypes should be considered hypothesis-generating; prospective validation required
- Immune microenvironment scores are inferred computationally from bulk mRNA/methylation — not validated by IHC or flow cytometry at scale
- No targeted therapy outcome data; actionability conclusions are mechanistic, not clinically validated in this study
