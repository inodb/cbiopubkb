## Summary

This integrative multi-omic study of 292 prostate cancer (PC) genomes вЂ” 203 early-onset (EOPC, age в‰¤55) and 85 late-onset вЂ” using WGS, methylome arrays (450k), and mRNA-seq identifies age-associated genomic features, a novel oncogene (ESRP1), and a methylation-based epigenetic risk index (PEPCI). Clock-like APOBEC3B (A3B) mutagenesis is identified as an endogenous age-associated mutational process contributing to early PC mutations including SV formation at driver gene loci. An integrative analysis of co-expression clusters (CCs) and PEPCI defines four molecular subgroups with distinct prognosis. The PRESCIENT model reconstructs temporal mutation order from single-biopsy data. Data are available in cBioPortal as `prostate_dkfz_2018`.

## Key Findings

### F1: ESRP1 recurrent genomic duplication at 8q22 identifies a novel oncogene independently associated with biochemical recurrence

**Finding:** Recurrent genomic duplications at 8q22 centered on *ESRP1* (RNA-binding protein, epithelial-to-mesenchymal transition regulator) were identified in 17% of EOPC. Tumors with ESRP1 gain showed >1.5-fold increased ESRP1 mRNA. In 11,954 TMA samples, high ESRP1 protein was an independent prognostic marker for shorter time to biochemical recurrence (BCR) in multivariate analysis including Gleason score, pT, lymph node status, and PSA.

**Detail:** ESRP1 duplications were the most significantly Gleason ScoreвЂ“associated genomic event in the cohort (P<1Г—10вЃ»В№В№). ESRP1 gain was strongly associated with PEPCI-high risk group (OR=15.7, FDR P<1Г—10вЃ»вЃµ). High ESRP1 protein correlated with higher proliferation rate (Ki67 index) irrespective of GS. The minimal overlap peak at 8q22 is 33 Mb from MYC вЂ” only ESRP1 (not MYC) showed significant mRNA elevation in affected samples, confirming ESRP1 as the target.

**Genes / cancer types:** `ESRP1` В· Early-onset prostate cancer; validated in unselected PC (TMA, n=11,954)

> ESRP1 copy-number and mRNA data available in `prostate_dkfz_2018`. Protein-level TMA data not in cBioPortal.

**Tool support:** `native`
> ESRP1 CNA (gain) and mRNA expression queryable in cBioPortal. Correlation between CNA and expression native via Plots tab. Survival by ESRP1 expression or CNA status available through Kaplan-Meier.

### F2: APOBEC3B (A3B) is an endogenous age-associated mutational process driving earliest mutations in prostate cancer

**Finding:** APOBEC-associated signatures 2 and 13 show a clock-like accumulation with patient age in PC (Spearman ПЃ=0.17, P=5.2Г—10вЃ»Ві). Age-associated C/G cluster (kataegis) mutations are primarily attributable to A3B-like mutagenesis. Germline APOBEC3B deletion carriers had significantly fewer APOBEC signature mutations and a shift from A3B-like to A3A-like mutagenesis вЂ” confirming endogenous A3B activity in prostate cells.

**Detail:** Six mutational signatures were identified: clock-like (1, 5), APOBEC (2, 13), homologous recombination defect (3), and mismatch repair defect (6). APOBEC C/G clusters co-localize with SV breakpoints (1вЂ“10 kb), and several such breakpoints result in driver gene alterations including TMPRSS2-ERG fusion and PTEN, FOXP1, BRCA2 disruption. Germline DDR mutations (BRCA1, BRCA2, PALB2, ATM, CHEK2) associate with increased APOBEC (signature 2) and homologous recombination repairвЂ“defect (signature 3) exposure.

**Genes / cancer types:** `APOBEC3B`, `TMPRSS2`, `ERG`, `PTEN`, `FOXP1`, `BRCA2`, `BRCA1`, `ATM`, `CHEK2` В· Prostate cancer

**Data availability:** `available`

### F3: PEPCI вЂ” a methylation-based epigenetic risk index вЂ” independently predicts Gleason score and BCR

**Finding:** The Purity-adjusted Epigenetic Prostate Cancer Index (PEPCI), derived from cell-type composition of tumor methylation profiles (T-luminal, immune, basal, luminal fractions), is strongly associated with high pT (P<1Г—10вЃ»вЃ·), high GS (P<1Г—10вЃ»В№вЃ·), and BCR (log-rank P<0.0001). PEPCI stratifies intermediate-risk (GS7) patients вЂ” validated in the TCGA cohort of primarily LOPC samples. AUC for BCR prediction: 0.702.

**Detail:** PEPCI integrates FACS-sorted cell typeвЂ“specific methylation references for basal, luminal, T-luminal (tumor-specific luminal), and immune cells. High GS tumors show a shift from normal basal/luminal to T-luminal and immune cells. PEPCI adds prognostic information beyond GS alone in multivariable analysis (P=0.003 in the study cohort; P=0.01 in TCGA). ESRP1 gain is the RGA most strongly associated with PEPCI-high risk (OR=15.7).

**Genes / cancer types:** Methylome-based (genome-wide); `ESRP1`, `PTEN` (associated RGAs) В· Prostate cancer

**Data availability:** `partial`

**Tool support:** `custom`

### F4: Four integrative molecular subgroups вЂ” defined by CC signatures and PEPCI вЂ” stratify EOPC prognosis beyond Gleason score

**Finding:** Integration of seven co-expression clusters (CCs) with PEPCI defined four PC subgroups. Subgroup 1 (PEPCI-high, CC7-high reactive stroma, CC2-low basal/luminal loss) and Subgroup 2 (PEPCI-high, CC1-high immune, CC5-high stroma) associate with high GS and shorter BCR-free survival. Subgroup 4 (PEPCI-low, CC2/CC4-high normal-like) is the most favorable.

**Detail:** CC2-low (loss of normal luminal/basal epithelial signature) and CC7-high (reactive stroma/myofibroblast signature) are independently associated with BCR in multivariable analysis (P=0.003 in study cohort; P=0.01 in TCGA; n=462). The subgrouping improves BCR prediction beyond GS alone in GS7 cases (P=0.015 in TCGA). PTEN loss was strongly associated with CC2-low (P<0.0001) and CC7-high (P<0.0001). TMPRSS2-ERG enriches in CC3 but does not align strongly with any of the four clinical subgroups.

**Genes / cancer types:** `PTEN`, `ESRP1`, `TMPRSS2-ERG`, `ASPN` В· Early-onset prostate cancer (validated in TCGA)

**Data availability:** `available`
> mRNA-seq co-expression data and PTEN CNA/mutation data in `prostate_dkfz_2018`. CC cluster assignments available in supplementary data.

**Tool support:** `native`
> Gene expression (mRNA-seq) and CNA data for PTEN, ESRP1, TMPRSS2-ERG queryable. Survival by expression subgroup requires local analysis of exported data.

### F5: PRESCIENT temporal model reconstructs PC mutation order and predicts clinical trajectory from single-biopsy data

**Finding:** PRESCIENT (PREdiction of Sequential Changes In EvoNascent Tumors), a conditional probability network model, uses the probability of two RGAs co-occurring as exclusive events (Exclusion score) to infer temporal mutation order. ERG has the highest exclusion score вЂ” most often the initiating event вЂ” followed by FOXP1. Pathway-level trajectories: ETS fusion в†’ AR signaling/cell cycle в†’ downstream events.

**Detail:** Multi-regional WGS of 7 EOPC patients (40 local metastasis pairs) validated PRESCIENT by cross-validation. For divergent clones, the branch with shorter predicted event-free survival was the more dominant clone by relative tumor mass. Single-area biopsy predictions converge on the same clinical trajectory in most cases (as shown for PCA035). The model enables both next-mutation prediction and probability of event-free survival (PEFS) from a single molecular profile.

**Genes / cancer types:** `ERG`, `FOXP1`, `PTEN`, `TP53`, `RB1`, `BRCA1`, `KLF5`, `ESRP1` В· Early-onset prostate cancer

**Data availability:** `available`
> RGA data and clonal architecture in `prostate_dkfz_2018`. PRESCIENT model not a standard cBioPortal tool.

**Tool support:** `custom`
> PRESCIENT model requires local implementation. RGA/SV data exportable from cBioPortal for input. Clonality estimates from SV/SNV data exportable via API for downstream PRESCIENT analysis.

## Open Questions

- Can ESRP1 protein staining from diagnostic biopsy serve as a clinically practical biomarker to guide active surveillance vs. treatment decisions in GS7 disease?
- Does PEPCI-based risk stratification improve over commercially available genomic classifiers (Oncotype DX, Decipher, Prolaris)?
- Can PRESCIENT be applied prospectively to serial biopsies or liquid biopsy ctDNA to track tumor evolution in real time?
- Do germline BRCA1/BRCA2 PTVs directly promote A3B-associated kataegis events at driver gene loci, and does this explain their association with TMPRSS2-ERG fusions?
- Are EOPC tumors more sensitive to PARP inhibitors given their clonal (monoclonal) architecture and higher frequency of germline DDR mutations?

## Limitations

- EOPC cohort is European (German); age-of-onset and molecular associations may differ across ancestry groups
- Methylation 450k arrays (not EPIC) limit CpG coverage for PEPCI; newer arrays may improve precision
- PEPCI requires FACS-validated cell-type references not yet commercially available
- mRNA-seq available for only 96 of 203 EOPC patients; CC classification not globally applied
- PRESCIENT validation uses cross-validation and reconstructed tumor trees вЂ” prospective validation in independent cohorts not yet reported
- No systematic immune infiltration or microenvironment data; clinical outcome data are limited to BCR (no metastatic or cancer-specific survival endpoints for most patients)
