## Summary
Dual inhibition of MCL1 (S63845) and BCL2 (venetoclax) induces durable remissions in DLBCL xenograft models after only 9 treatments, but causes significant hematologic toxicity and weight loss at efficacious doses. Encapsulating either drug in fucoidan-coated, P-selectin-targeted nanoparticles achieves 5–8× tumor enrichment, enables 3.5- to 6.5-fold dose reductions, and eliminates toxicity while maintaining antitumor efficacy — directly relevant to cBioPortal users exploring BCL2/MCL1 co-dependency across lymphoma subtypes.

## Dataset
- **Cohort**: 14 human lymphoma cell lines spanning ABC-DLBCL, GCB-DLBCL, double-hit lymphoma (DHL), and mantle cell lymphoma (MCL); SU-DHL-6 and U-2973 used in mouse xenograft models
- **Assay**: HemePACT targeted deep sequencing (585 cancer genes) at MSK Integrated Genomic Operation Core
- **N**: 14 cell lines
- **cBioPortal study ID(s)**: lymphoma_cellline_msk_2020

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: Heterogeneous single-agent sensitivity of MCL1 inhibitor S63845 across lymphoma subtypes
- **Detail**: In 14 lymphoma cell lines, S63845 IC₅₀ was <0.1 µM (sensitive) in 4 lines, 0.1–1 µM (moderately sensitive) in 5 lines, and >1 µM (resistant) in 5 lines. Only 4 lines (29%) were sensitive to both MCL1 and BCL2 inhibitors. Sensitive lines expressed lower levels of MCL1, BCL2, BCL-xL, and BIM. Sensitivity was independent of TP53 and other BCL2 family genetic alterations.
- **Genes**: MCL1, BCL2, BCL2L1, BCL2L11, TP53
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: mutation, copy_number, expression

### Finding 2: Synergistic BCL2/MCL1 dual inhibition overcomes single-agent resistance
- **Detail**: Venetoclax enhanced S63845 efficacy in all tested lines, including those highly resistant to each agent alone. Synergy (log-odds < 0) was confirmed in the 3 lines most resistant to S63845 (U-2932, HBL-1, Ri-1) and the 3 most resistant to venetoclax (Jeko-1, TMD8, Z-138). 57% of lines responded differentially to the two drugs, making the combination broadly applicable.
- **Genes**: MCL1, BCL2
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: expression, pathway

### Finding 3: Durable complete remission in DLBCL xenograft with BCL2/MCL1 combination
- **Detail**: In SU-DHL-6 xenograft mice, S63845 (25 mg/kg IV 5×/week) + venetoclax (100 mg/kg oral 5×/week) achieved complete remission after 9 treatments, sustained for up to 216 days with increased survival. Monotherapy with either agent alone reduced tumor volume only ~36–41%. Combination treatment induced cleavage of caspase-3 and PARP, confirming apoptosis.
- **Genes**: MCL1, BCL2, CASP3, PARP1
- **Data availability**: not-available
- **Tool support**: custom
- **Concepts**: survival, pathway

### Finding 4: Efficacious systemic dual inhibition causes significant hematologic toxicity
- **Detail**: Mice receiving the full-dose combination (S63845 25 mg/kg + venetoclax 100 mg/kg 5×/week) suffered ~15% body weight loss and significant reductions in peripheral blood WBCs, RBCs, hemoglobin, and platelets — not seen with either drug alone. Lower doses that reduced toxicity also lost durable remission (tumor regrowth ~day 75), confirming a toxicity-efficacy trade-off for free drug combinations.
- **Genes**: MCL1, BCL2
- **Data availability**: not-available
- **Tool support**: custom
- **Concepts**: clinical

### Finding 5: P-selectin-targeted S63845 nanoparticles achieve 5–8× tumor enrichment and 3.5-fold dose reduction
- **Detail**: Fucoidan-coated S63845 nanoparticles (S63845-NP; 268 ± 33 nm, −55 mV) preferentially accumulated in DLBCL tumors at 5–8× higher fluorescence than normal organs by in vivo/ex vivo IVIS imaging. S63845-NP at 12.5 mg/kg 3×/week equaled efficacy of free S63845 at 25 mg/kg 5×/week (3.5-fold total dose reduction). Combined with venetoclax, S63845-NP achieved sustained remission (0/4 mice with measurable tumors on day 75) vs. tumor regrowth with equivalent free drug dosing.
- **Genes**: MCL1, SELP
- **Data availability**: not-available
- **Tool support**: custom
- **Concepts**: pathway, tumor-microenvironment

### Finding 6: Venetoclax nanoparticles enable 6.5-fold dose reduction with superior tumor pharmacokinetics
- **Detail**: Venetoclax-NP (68 ± 7 nm, −33 mV, stable over 7 days) at 25 mg/kg 3×/week matched efficacy of free venetoclax at 100 mg/kg 5×/week in the SU-DHL-6 model (6.5-fold total dose reduction). Mass spectrometry confirmed 27% increase in tumor drug concentration, 43% decrease in plasma levels, and decreases in other organs. S63845 (12.5 mg/kg 3×/week) + venetoclax-NP (25 mg/kg 3×/week) achieved complete tumor regression (0/7 mice with measurable tumors on day 20); free drug at the same dose left 7/7 mice with tumors.
- **Genes**: BCL2, SELP
- **Data availability**: not-available
- **Tool support**: custom
- **Concepts**: pathway, clinical

### Finding 7: Nanoparticle encapsulation of either drug eliminates combination toxicity
- **Detail**: S63845-NP (12.5 mg/kg 3×/week) + venetoclax caused no weight loss and no significant deviation in WBC or RBC counts from control animals. Similarly, venetoclax-NP (25 mg/kg 3×/week) + S63845 produced no significant weight loss or hematologic changes. In contrast, free drug combinations at lower doses reduced toxicity but also lost efficacy — NPs uniquely decoupled toxicity from efficacy.
- **Genes**: MCL1, BCL2
- **Data availability**: not-available
- **Tool support**: custom
- **Concepts**: clinical, survival

## Open Questions
- Which DLBCL molecular subtypes (e.g., MYC/BCL2 double-hit, ABC vs. GCB) from `lymphoma_cellline_msk_2020` show the highest co-dependency on MCL1 and BCL2, and do BCL2 amplification or MYC translocation correlate with synergy scores?
- Can BCL2/MCL1 co-expression levels in primary DLBCL patient tumors (from MSK-IMPACT datasets) predict sensitivity to this combination?
- Do mutations in TP53, MYD88, or CD79B (common in ABC-DLBCL) modulate response to BCL2/MCL1 dual inhibition in the cell line data?

## Limitations
- Study uses cell line xenografts (not PDX or primary tumor models) that may not capture the immune microenvironment relevant to in vivo drug response.
- S63845 has 6× higher affinity for human MCL1 vs. mouse MCL1, so toxicity in mouse models likely underestimates human toxicity; humanized MCL1 models were not used.
- Nanoparticle batch-to-batch variability (especially S63845-NP at 268 ± 33 nm vs. venetoclax-NP at 68 ± 7 nm) may pose challenges for clinical translation.
- Only two DLBCL xenograft cell lines (SU-DHL-6, U-2973) were used in vivo; broader genomic context (e.g., double-hit status, BCL2 amplification) was not systematically tested in animal models.
- No patient-derived clinical data; translational relevance to human pharmacokinetics requires validation.
