## Summary

This correlative molecular study analyzed pretreatment tumor biopsies from 33 ER+/HER2-negative metastatic breast cancer patients enrolled in a phase Ib trial of buparlisib (pan-PI3K inhibitor) plus letrozole, with additional validation in an alpelisib (PI3Kα-selective) plus letrozole cohort. MSK-IMPACT targeted sequencing identified that activating *PIK3CA* mutations and inactivating *MAP3K1* mutations were individually associated with clinical benefit (≥6 months stable disease or response), and patients harboring both alterations had the highest clinical benefit rate (71%). However, mechanistic studies in cell lines showed that MAP3K1 knockdown does not directly sensitize cells to PI3K inhibition. Instead, *MAP3K1* alterations were found to be a strong surrogate for luminal A breast cancer subtype — and luminal A PAM50 status was itself associated with clinical benefit across both trial cohorts. These findings suggest that luminal A subtype, rather than MAP3K1 per se, is the key predictive biomarker for PI3K inhibitor efficacy combined with endocrine therapy.

## Key Findings

### F1: PIK3CA and MAP3K1 alterations are individually and jointly associated with clinical benefit from PI3K inhibition

**Finding:** *PIK3CA* alterations were moderately enriched in patients with CR/PR/SD (p=0.09). *MAP3K1* alterations were significantly enriched in patients with CR/PR/SD vs. PD (p=0.01). Patients with both *PIK3CA* and *MAP3K1* alterations showed the highest clinical benefit rate: 71% (5/7) vs. 40% for *PIK3CA*-only, 50% for *MAP3K1*-only, and 11% for neither. PFS was significantly improved in patients with MAP3K1 alterations (p=0.03) and PIK3CA alterations (p=0.009) by log-rank test.

**Detail:** *MAP3K1* alterations in this cohort were predominantly truncating or frameshift mutations (loss-of-function), not missense variants. The *PIK3CA* mutations were hotspot mutations (kinase and helical domain). The co-occurrence of both alteration types in the highest-benefit group suggests convergent pathway effects — PIK3CA activation driving PI3K dependency, while MAP3K1 loss eliminating a potential JNK-mediated adaptive escape mechanism. However, mechanistic in vitro studies could not confirm MAP3K1 loss directly sensitizes cells to buparlisib, suggesting MAP3K1 status acts as a surrogate marker rather than a causal sensitizer.

### F2: MAP3K1 loss does not directly sensitize breast cancer cells to PI3K inhibition despite suppressing JNK/cJUN activation

**Finding:** PI3K inhibition with buparlisib activates the JNK/cJUN pathway in *PIK3CA*-mutant T47D cells in a MAP3K1-dependent manner (confirmed by shRNA knockdown), representing a potential escape mechanism. However, MAP3K1 knockdown does not alter buparlisib sensitivity in proliferation or colony assays under full serum or estrogen-deprived conditions.

**Detail:** T47D cells treated with buparlisib for 5 days show sustained AKT suppression (p-S473) concurrent with strong reciprocal activation of p-cJUN (p-S73). MAP3K1 shRNA knockdown abolishes this cJUN activation without affecting AKT inhibition. Despite the mechanistic plausibility (eliminating an adaptive stress response), MAP3K1 knockdown showed no change in the therapeutic window of buparlisib in T47D, MCF7, or BT483 cells. The finding reveals that cJUN activation is not a sufficient escape mechanism from PI3K inhibition in standard cell culture conditions, and that MAP3K1's role in clinical benefit is likely through a non-cell-autonomous or tumor microenvironment mechanism.

### F3: Dual PIK3CA/MAP3K1 alterations are a strong surrogate for luminal A subtype in TCGA breast cancer

**Finding:** In 959 TCGA breast cancers, tumors with co-alterations in *MAP3K1* and *PIK3CA* showed significantly higher correlation to luminal A centroids than luminal B centroids (p<0.0001 by two-sample t-test). Luminal A status was significantly enriched in the combined PI3K inhibitor trial cohort among patients achieving clinical benefit (Fisher's exact p=0.07 trend across 26 patients with PAM50 data).

**Detail:** PAM50 analysis was performed using a custom NanoString RNA panel on available tissue from 14 buparlisib and 12 alpelisib trial patients; all predicted as either luminal A or B. In the combined analysis, luminal A classification trended toward enrichment in clinical benefit patients. This suggests that the prognostic/predictive value of the MAP3K1/PIK3CA co-mutation signature derives from its co-segregation with the luminal A gene expression program, which may itself confer PI3K pathway dependency and lower intrinsic resistance through its low proliferative, hormone-sensitive biology.

### F4: PTEN deletions associated with primary progression despite PI3K pathway membership

**Finding:** *PTEN* deletions or mutations were associated with progressive disease at the first scan in the buparlisib cohort, contrasting with *PIK3CA* mutations which were associated with benefit. Paradoxically, a deep *PTEN* deletion was also detected in the only patient to achieve a partial response — indicating PTEN loss does not universally confer resistance to pan-PI3K inhibition.

**Detail:** This apparent paradox reflects the complexity of PI3K pathway alterations in predicting response to PI3K inhibitors. PTEN loss activates PI3K signaling through a mechanism distinct from PIK3CA mutation (loss of negative regulation vs. gain of activating mutation), and may activate the pathway in a manner that is less dependent on PI3Kα or more dependent on feedback re-activation. Additionally, PTEN loss may co-occur with other pathway alterations that confer resistance. These findings highlight that simple PI3K pathway membership is insufficient as a predictive biomarker — the specific alteration type and co-occurring molecular context matter.

### F5: MAP3K1 alterations in ER+ breast cancer are predominantly loss-of-function and enriched in luminal A subtype in large-scale genomic datasets

**Finding:** MAP3K1 alterations identified in clinical trial patients were primarily truncating and frameshift mutations consistent with loss-of-function. Analysis of AACR GENIE data and TCGA confirmed that MAP3K1 loss-of-function is recurrently observed in breast cancer and that this alteration pattern is most common in luminal A tumors, consistent with prior large-scale genomic studies.

**Detail:** *MAP3K1* simultaneously encodes both serine/threonine kinase activity and E3-ubiquitin ligase activity (degrading c-Jun and ERK1/2). Loss of MAP3K1 is therefore expected to both impair JNK activation and reduce ERK1/2 degradation. The predominance of truncating/frameshift mutations (vs. missense) supports loss-of-function as the primary mechanism. The luminal A enrichment of MAP3K1 mutations in population-level data supports its role as a marker of tumor biology rather than a causal driver of PI3K inhibitor sensitivity.

## Open Questions

- Is luminal A status — independent of PIK3CA or MAP3K1 mutation — a predictive biomarker for PI3K inhibitor benefit when combined with endocrine therapy in larger randomized cohorts?
- Does MAP3K1 loss cooperate with PI3K activation to enhance tumor dependency on the PI3K/AKT/mTOR axis through IRS1-mediated feedback, and can this be exploited therapeutically?
- Would alpelisib (PI3Kα-selective, FDA-approved for PIK3CA-mutant ER+ breast cancer) show an even stronger enrichment for clinical benefit in MAP3K1/PIK3CA co-mutant tumors compared to pan-PI3K buparlisib?
- Can the MAP3K1/PIK3CA co-mutation signature be used prospectively to select patients for PI3K inhibitor trials with higher predicted benefit?
- What fraction of MAP3K1-altered ER+ breast cancers also carry ESR1 mutations acquired during prior endocrine therapy, and does ESR1 mutation status modify the PI3K inhibitor response?

## Limitations

- Small clinical cohort (n=33 for primary analysis; n=14/12 for PAM50 subsets) limits statistical power; key associations may be underpowered
- Non-randomized phase Ib study with no control arm; clinical benefit rate cannot be directly compared to endocrine therapy alone
- PAM50 analysis performed only in a small subset with available tissue; luminal A association is a trend (p=0.07) rather than a significant finding
- Mechanistic studies (MAP3K1 knockdown) performed only in T47D cells; findings may not generalize to all ER+ breast cancer cell lines or in vivo
- No paired biopsy data for mechanisms of acquired resistance in patients who initially benefited then progressed
- MAP3K1 protein-level analysis hampered by lack of reliable antibodies; knockdown confirmed only by qRT-PCR
- The phase Ib dose-escalation design means different patients received different drug doses, potentially confounding dose-response relationships
