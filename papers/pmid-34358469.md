## Summary

CPTAC proteogenomic profiling of 108 treatment-naïve lung squamous cell carcinoma (LSCC) tumors and 99 paired normal adjacent tissues across nine data types (WGS/WES, RNA-seq, proteome, phosphoproteome, acetylome, ubiquitylome, methylome) identifies five multi-omic subtypes, nominates NSD3 (not FGFR1) as the key driver in 8p11.23-amplified tumors, and links low-ΔNp63 expression to survivin overexpression as a therapeutic vulnerability. Loss of CDK4/6 pathway inhibitors (CDKN2A or RB1) is identified as a universal LSCC feature, with phospho-Rb levels offering a better CDK4/6 inhibitor biomarker than CDKN2A mutation status alone.

## Key Findings

### F1: Five multi-omic NMF subtypes stratify LSCC biology and survival

**Finding:** NMF-based multi-omic clustering identifies five LSCC subtypes—Basal-Inclusive, EMT-Enriched, Classical, Inflamed-Secretory, and Proliferative-Primitive—with distinct pathway, immune, and histological profiles.

**Detail:** Five subtypes derived from CNA, RNA, protein, phosphoprotein, and acetylprotein data (n=108). Classical subtype: KEAP1/CUL3/NFE2L2 mutations, SOX2/TP63 amplification, CIMP-high, upregulated OxPhos. EMT-E: upregulated EMT, angiogenesis, myogenesis, fibroblast infiltration, myxoid features. Inflamed-Secretory: strong immune pathway upregulation. Mixed subtype membership (tumor heterogeneity marker) significantly associated with worse survival. SOX2 amplification enriched in mixed subgroup (p=0.0038).

**Genes / cancer types:** `SOX2` · `TP63` · `KEAP1` · `CUL3` · `NFE2L2` · Lung squamous cell carcinoma

**Tool support:** native
> Mutation and CNA data for these genes queryable in lusc_cptac_2021; survival analysis possible in Survival tab.

### F2: NSD3, not FGFR1, is the likely driver oncogene in the 8p11.23 amplicon

**Finding:** Proteomics data show that NSD3 (WHSC1L1), co-amplified with FGFR1 at 8p11.23, is the functionally elevated gene product, nominating it as an alternative therapeutic target in FGFR1-amplified LSCC.

**Detail:** CNA-proteome cis-effects identified 138 cancer-associated genes. FGFR1 high-level amplification (GISTIC=2) did not show proportional FGFR1 protein elevation, but NSD3 protein levels were elevated. After this paper was submitted, NSD3 was independently demonstrated as a key regulator of LSCC tumorigenesis via oncogenic chromatin changes. FGFR1-targeted therapies have repeatedly failed in LSCC clinical trials.

**Genes / cancer types:** `NSD3` · `FGFR1` · `CCND1` · `SOX2` · Lung squamous cell carcinoma

**Data availability:** partial
> CNA data in lusc_cptac_2021; protein-level NSD3 data requires CPTAC proteomics portal.

**Tool support:** api
> CNA and mRNA for NSD3/FGFR1 queryable in cBioPortal; protein correlation requires external CPTAC data download.

### F3: Low-ΔNp63 tumors overexpress survivin — a targetable vulnerability

**Finding:** A subset (~10%) of LSCC tumors with low ΔNp63α expression overexpresses survivin (BIRC5), and TP63-low LSCC cell lines show selective vulnerability to the survivin inhibitor YM-155.

**Detail:** 10 "Np63-low" tumors histologically confirmed as LSCC but lacking TP63 RNA/protein elevation. BIRC5 (survivin) is the most overexpressed protein in this group. TP63-low cell lines were significantly more sensitive to YM-155 than TP63-high lines. Survivin promotes cell proliferation and G2M checkpoints (elevated in Np63-low samples). ΔNp63α amplification associated with improved survival in TCGA dataset.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP63` · `BIRC5` · Lung squamous cell carcinoma

**Data availability:** partial
> TP63 expression and CNA data in lusc_cptac_2021; protein-level survivin data from CPTAC only.

**Tool support:** api
> TP63 mRNA and CNA queryable in lusc_cptac_2021; functional vulnerability data not in cBioPortal.

### F4: Loss of CDK4/6 pathway is universal in LSCC; phospho-Rb outperforms CDKN2A mutation as biomarker

**Finding:** Every LSCC tumor loses either CDKN2A/p16 or RB1 (mutually exclusive), but CDK4/6 inhibitor response is better predicted by phospho-Rb levels than by CDKN2A mutation status.

**Detail:** CDKN2A homozygous deletion (most frequent), hypermethylation, or missense mutation in majority of LSCC; 28 WT tumors show p16 promoter hypermethylation. Mutual exclusivity between CDKN2A/p16 and RB1 loss confirmed. CCND1 amplification raises mean Rb phosphoprotein but with overlapping distributions. In LSCC cell lines, phospho-Rb (not CDKN2A status) correlates with CDK4/6 inhibitor response. Outlier responders to abemaciclib/palbociclib in trials suggest a biomarker-selected population exists.

**Genes / cancer types:** `CDKN2A` · `RB1` · `CDK4` · `CDK6` · `CCND1` · Lung squamous cell carcinoma

**Data availability:** available
> CDKN2A/RB1 mutation and CNA data in lusc_cptac_2021; phospho-Rb data from CPTAC only.

**Tool support:** native

### F5: EMT-Enriched subtype driven by PDGFRB and ROR2 phospho-signaling — targetable RTKs

**Finding:** The EMT-E subtype shows elevated PDGFRB and ROR2 phosphorylation scores (not mutation-driven), activating non-canonical Wnt and EMT programs, with TGFβ inhibitor signatures reversing this phenotype.

**Detail:** 9 RTKs show high CBPE (correlation-based phosphosite enrichment) scores in LSCC; 7 significantly associate with NMF subtypes. PDGFRB and ROR2 markedly elevated in EMT-E (Wilcoxon p=1.5×10⁻⁶ and 2.7×10⁻⁷). Strong correlation with loss of epithelial junction markers and upregulation of mesenchymal proteins (EMT, ECM remodeling). LINCS query identifies TGFβ inhibitors as reversing EMT-E signature.

**Genes / cancer types:** `PDGFRB` · `ROR2` · Lung squamous cell carcinoma

**Data availability:** partial

**Tool support:** custom

### F6: NRF2 pathway activated beyond its mutated cases — Classical subtype enrichment via CDK5

**Finding:** NRF2 pathway activation extends beyond tumors with KEAP1/CUL3/NFE2L2 mutations, with the Classical NMF subtype showing CDK5-mediated NRF2 phosphorylation.

**Detail:** NRF2 pathway mutated in 33/108 tumors (one-hit n=28, two-hit n=5). ssGSEA-based NRF2 pathway score elevated in Classical subtype even without pathway mutations. CDK5 protein and PTM-SEA-derived activity significantly upregulated in Classical subtype (FDR<0.05). CDK5 phosphorylates NFE2L2 S433, mediating NRF2 activation. Unlike LUAD, KEAP1 mutations do not reduce KEAP1 protein in LSCC.

**Genes / cancer types:** `NFE2L2` · `KEAP1` · `CUL3` · `CDK5` · Lung squamous cell carcinoma

**Data availability:** partial
> KEAP1/NFE2L2/CUL3 mutations in lusc_cptac_2021; CDK5 protein/phospho data from CPTAC only.

**Tool support:** api
> NRF2 pathway mutations queryable in lusc_cptac_2021; NRF2 score computation requires custom scripting with RNA data.

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Can phospho-Rb levels from tumor biopsies prospectively select LSCC patients for CDK4/6 inhibitor trials?
- Is NSD3 inhibition (e.g., BRD4 or SMARCA4 targeting at the 8p11.23 locus) effective in FGFR1-amplified LSCC cell lines with low FGFR1 protein?
- Does the EMT-E subtype predict resistance to immunotherapy given its low immune signaling?
- Can survivin (BIRC5) inhibitors be combined with radiation or chemotherapy in TP63-low LSCC?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Small cohort (n=108); some subtype comparisons underpowered
- Proteomics performed on bulk tumor; tumor heterogeneity and immune cell contamination can confound protein measurements
- Cross-sectional design; no treatment response or longitudinal data collected
- Some PTM data types (ubiquitylome) not available for all 108 samples, excluded from multi-omic NMF
- Phosphosite-based RTK activity inference is correlational; functional validation needed for PDGFRB/ROR2 in EMT-E
