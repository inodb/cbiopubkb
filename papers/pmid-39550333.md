## Summary

This integrated multi-omic study of 100 UTUC nephroureterectomy specimens (MSK100 cohort) using whole transcriptome RNA-seq and MSK-IMPACT targeted DNA sequencing identifies five molecular clusters (C1–C5) with distinct clinical outcomes and immune phenotypes. Favorable clusters (C1/C2) are luminal-like with immunologically cold (depleted) TME. C3 is characterized by FGFR3 alterations, highest TMB, and all MSI-H tumors. Poor-prognosis clusters (C4/C5) harbor an immunologically hot TME predicting response to immune checkpoint blockade (ICB). Findings validated in an independent Japanese cohort of 158 UTUC patients. Data are in cBioPortal as `bladder_msk_2024`.

## Key Findings

### F1: Five UTUC molecular clusters with distinct survival outcomes identified by RNA-seq cNMF clustering

**Finding:** Unsupervised consensus NMF clustering of the top 10% most differentially expressed genes (n=2,260) divided 100 UTUC tumors into five clusters. C5 had the highest risk of metastatic recurrence followed by C4; C1 had the best 10-year OS. C4 and C5 were enriched for high-stage tumors (pT3/pT4: 58.8% and 72.2%), while C1 was enriched for noninvasive disease (<pT2: 83.3%). Transcriptional subtype C5 was associated with inferior DFS (P<0.001) in univariate analysis.

**Detail:** Prior MIBC consensus classification labeled 89% of MSK100 UTUC tumors as luminal papillary (LumP) — a classification that failed to capture meaningful prognostic differences. cNMF-based clustering revealed previously unappreciated heterogeneity. Basal/squamous, stromal-rich, and neuroendocrine-like subtypes were enriched in the recurrent group (14.3% vs. 4.17%, P=0.066). In multivariable Cox regression, pT stage was the only factor independently associated with DFS — transcriptional subtype did not independently add prognostic value after adjusting for stage.

**Genes / cancer types:** Panel-wide transcriptome (2,260 genes) · Upper tract urothelial carcinoma

### F2: C3 cluster contains all MSI-H tumors, FGFR3 alterations in 93%, and the highest TMB — defines a distinct molecular entity

**Finding:** All five MSI-H tumors (four Lynch syndrome-associated, one somatic MSH2) were in C3. FGFR3 alterations were present in 93% of C3 tumors (28/30), including the FGFR3 R248C hotspot enriched in Lynch syndrome–associated UTUC. Mean TMB was highest in C3 of all five clusters. FGFR3-mutant tumors had favorable CSS (P<0.05) and TP53-mutant tumors had worse CSS (P<0.01) and OS (P<0.01).

**Detail:** The co-occurrence of FGFR3 alterations, high TMB, and MSI-H in C3 supports a specific carcinogenesis pathway in Lynch syndrome UTUC. FGFR3 mutational status alone is insufficient to predict ICB response — FGFR3 mutant tumors are a heterogeneous group and MSI-H (not FGFR3 per se) likely drives ICB sensitivity in responsive FGFR3-mutant cases. TERT (37.4%), KDM6A (23.2%), KMT2D (30.3%), TP53 (23.2%), and FGFR3 (48.5%) were the most commonly mutated genes overall.

**Genes / cancer types:** `FGFR3`, `TP53`, `TERT`, `KDM6A`, `KMT2D`, `MSH2` · Upper tract urothelial carcinoma, Lynch syndrome–associated

### F3: High-risk clusters C4 and C5 have immunologically hot TME — the paradox of worst prognosis but best ICI response

**Finding:** Immune deconvolution, GSEA, and WGCNA consistently showed C4 and C5 as immunologically hot: elevated IFN-γ, ImmuneCheckpoint, CD8+ T-cell, NK cell, and cytolytic activity (CYT) signatures. C1 and C2 had immune-desert phenotypes. No objective ICB responses occurred in C1/C2 patients; complete responses were observed only in C3 and C5. ICB objective response rate was highest in C3 (highest TMB/MSI) and C5 (immune-inflamed).

**Detail:** CYT score (GZMA + PRF1 mRNA expression) was highest in C4 (median 4.47) and C5 (median 5.24) and predicted ICB response. Antigen processing machinery (APM1) signature enrichment in C4 was significantly associated with objective ICB response (P=0.04). PD-L1 expression was higher among ICB responders (not statistically significant). Adenosine signaling (AdenoSig), associated with NK/CD8 suppression, was also enriched in C4 and C5 — suggesting a complex immunosuppressive overlay.

**Genes / cancer types:** `GZMA`, `PRF1`, `CD274` (PD-L1), `CTLA4`, `LAG3`, `PD1` (PDCD1), `TIM3` (HAVCR2), `TIGIT` · Upper tract urothelial carcinoma

### F4: Independent validation of molecular clusters and immune phenotypes in 158-patient Japanese cohort

**Finding:** A random-forest predictor trained on MSK100 and applied to the Fujii158 cohort (158 Japanese UTUC patients) reproduced the PFS pattern and T-stage distribution (48% MIBC in both cohorts). TME deconvolution in Fujii158 showed similar immune features — higher infiltration in C4 and C5 vs. C1 and C2. CD8+ T-cell signals were similarly high in C3 and C5 in both cohorts.

**Detail:** Somatic mutation-based subtypes from Fujii et al. (hypermutated, TP53/MDM2, RAS, FGFR3, triple-negative) showed only moderate correlation with expression-based clusters (phi coefficients 0.2–0.52). This confirms that RNA-based clustering captures complementary biological information beyond DNA-level somatic alterations — similar to findings in bladder cancer where transcriptomics add clinical utility beyond somatic mutations alone.

**Genes / cancer types:** Transcriptome-wide · UTUC (cross-institutional, cross-ethnicity validation)

**Data availability:** `partial`
> MSK100 data in `bladder_msk_2024`. Fujii158 external cohort data are not integrated into cBioPortal but are published with the Fujii et al. paper.

**Tool support:** `api`
> Cross-cohort comparison requires combining MSK100 data with external Fujii158 data. REST API enables export of MSK100 expression/clinical data for combined analysis locally.

### F5: EMT, claudin-low, and myofibroblast signatures in C5 associate with worst outcomes and anti-angiogenic biology

**Finding:** C5 is characterized by mesenchymal features: high myofibroblast, claudin-low, EMT, and p53-like gene signatures. Cell cycle/proliferation, immune infiltration, and angiogenesis/TGF-β gene expression modules (WGCNA) all correlated with inferior DFS. C5 had the highest NK cell and angiogenesis signature expression, a unique feature distinguishing it from C4. Squamous differentiation (P=0.006) and EMT (P=0.001) signatures associated with inferior DFS across all clusters.

**Detail:** WGCNA identified 8 co-expressed gene modules. Genes involved in organic hydroxyl and hydrogen peroxide compound processing were associated with improved DFS — suggesting metabolic vulnerability in more differentiated, favorable-prognosis tumors. C5 closely resembles the TCGA "luminal infiltrated" MIBC subtype, which has been reported to respond to atezolizumab (anti-PD-L1). The combination of high angiogenesis and immune infiltration in C5 suggests potential benefit from anti-VEGF + ICI combinations.

**Genes / cancer types:** EMT-related, claudin-low, myofibroblast gene sets · Upper tract urothelial carcinoma

## Open Questions

- Can the five-cluster transcriptional classification be applied prospectively using small pre-surgical biopsy specimens (ureteroscopy) rather than nephroureterectomy tissue?
- Should patients with C4/C5 UTUC receive neoadjuvant or adjuvant ICB given their high recurrence risk and inflamed TME?
- Does CYT score from RNA-seq add predictive value beyond TMB and PD-L1 IHC in the perioperative ICB setting?
- Do antibody-drug conjugates (enfortumab vedotin, sacituzumab govitecan) show differential activity across molecular clusters, particularly in C1/C2 where ICB fails?
- Is the immunologically hot phenotype of C4/C5 driven primarily by tumor-intrinsic features (e.g., high neoantigen burden, EMT) or by extrinsic stromal signals?

## Limitations

- RNA-seq performed on bulk frozen tissue from primary site — cannot distinguish tumor, stromal, and immune compartments; spatial or single-cell resolution needed
- Small ICB-treated cohort (n=31) limits statistical power for response analysis across clusters
- Response data extrapolated from primary site TME to metastatic disease site treated with ICB
- Clinical heterogeneity: cohort includes low- and high-grade tumors and neoadjuvant chemotherapy–exposed patients
- cNMF clustering optimal cluster number selection (k=5) based on within-cohort metrics; different k may apply in other datasets
- Temporal evolution of transcriptional subtype (primary vs. metastatic vs. post-therapy) not addressed
