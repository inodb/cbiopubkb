## Summary

This study uses a series of 10 small cell lung cancer (SCLC) patient-derived xenograft (PDX) models to model acquired chemoresistance to cisplatin/etoposide in vivo. Whole-exome sequencing revealed no recurrent genetic mutations driving resistance; instead, RNA-seq implicated epigenetic silencing of SLFN11 via EZH2-mediated H3K27me3 deposition as the dominant mechanism. EZH2 inhibition (EPZ011989) restores SLFN11 expression, resensitizes tumors to chemotherapy, and prevents emergence of acquired resistance in both chemosensitive and chemoresistant SCLC models.

## Key Findings

### F1: Acquired chemoresistance in SCLC PDX is not driven by recurrent somatic mutations

**Finding:** Whole-exome sequencing of 10 paired chemonaive/chemoresistant SCLC PDX models found no recurrent acquired mutations or focal copy number alterations in the chemoresistant state; private mutations were rare passenger events.

**Detail:** >90% of mutations were shared between chemonaive and chemoresistant tumors. Both models with matched normal DNA showed tobacco-signature C>A transversions with mutational stability. A minor component of Signature 3 (homologous recombination deficiency) was acquired in resistant models. Copy number alterations were concordant between states with no evidence of treatment-induced genome doubling.

**Genes / cancer types:** TP53, RB1 (maintained from primary SCLC) · Small cell lung carcinoma

### F2: SLFN11 silencing is a recurrent epigenetic event in acquired chemoresistance

**Finding:** SLFN11 was among the most significantly downregulated genes across 7 of 10 chemoresistant PDX models by RNA-seq, and was confirmed at the protein level by IHC in both PDX and SCLC cell lines derived from previously treated patients (p=0.003 vs. chemonaive PDX; p=0.031 in treated vs. untreated cell lines).

**Detail:** SLFN11 expression is bimodal across cancer cell lines (CCLE). SCLC cell lines from treated patients showed significantly lower SLFN11. TWIST1 upregulation was a mutually exclusive alternative resistance mechanism (present in 3 of the remaining models). Combined, SLFN11 suppression and TWIST1 induction accounted for 7 of 10 resistant models.

**Genes / cancer types:** SLFN11, TWIST1 · Small cell lung carcinoma

### F3: EZH2-mediated H3K27me3 silences SLFN11 in the chemoresistant state

**Finding:** ChIP-seq from in vivo SCLC PDX models showed elevated global H3K27me3 and reduced H3K27Ac in the chemoresistant state; specific deposition of H3K27me3 in the SLFN11 gene body was causally linked to SLFN11 silencing and abolished by EZH2 inhibitor EPZ011989.

**Detail:** ChIP-seq was performed on EZH2, H3K27me3, and H3K27Ac from flash-frozen chemonaive or chemoresistant SCRX-Lu149 PDX tumors treated with vehicle or EPZ (250 mg/kg PO bid, 21 days). Global H3K27me3 was significantly elevated in resistant tumors and reversed by EPZ. H3K27Ac was globally decreased in resistant tumors and rescued by EPZ. Super-enhancer landscape was altered globally but no specific super-enhancer gains/losses were discrete.

### F4: EZH2 inhibition restores SLFN11 and chemosensitivity in vitro and in vivo

**Finding:** EZH2 inhibitor EPZ011989 (but not 5-azacitidine) restored SLFN11 expression in chemoresistant SCLC PDX ex vivo cultures and SCLC cell lines with low baseline SLFN11, and resensitized cells to topoisomerase inhibitors (etoposide, topotecan) with Pearson r=0.916 correlation between SLFN11 re-expression and topotecan sensitivity.

**Detail:** In NCI-H82 (treated patient-derived, SLFN11-low), SLFN11 protein re-expression was detectable after 7–10 days of 1 µM EPZ, with sustained expression through 10-day washout. SLFN11 re-expression by EPZ was suppressible with shRNA targeting SLFN11. Doxycycline-inducible SLFN11 in NCI-H82 and NCI-H446 sensitized to topotecan (~1 log shift). In vivo EPZ showed modest single-agent tumor growth inhibition in 3 of 4 models.

**Genes / cancer types:** EZH2, SLFN11 · Small cell lung carcinoma

### F5: SLFN11 expression correlates with chemotherapy response in clinical SCLC samples

**Finding:** In SCLC tumor microarrays, SLFN11 H-score was significantly higher in patients who responded to therapy vs. non-responders (p=0.0192 across all treated patients); 83% of untreated patients with H-score >75 responded, and 100% of previously-treated patients with H-score >75 responded.

**Detail:** TMAs from Vanderbilt Medical Center (VMC, n=215 cores, untreated) and Case Western Reserve University (CWRU, n=22 cores, previously treated) analyzed by SLFN11 IHC (pathologist-blinded). SLFN11 was higher in limited- vs. extensive-stage SCLC (p=0.0397). High SLFN11 did not confer OS benefit, suggesting chemosensitivity does not translate to long-term survival in this dataset.

### F6: EZH2 inhibition combined with chemotherapy prevents emergence of acquired resistance

**Finding:** Combining EZH2 inhibitor EPZ with cisplatin/etoposide (C/E) in vivo prevented the emergence of acquired resistance in chemonaive SCLC PDX models and augmented efficacy in chemoresistant models, establishing a preclinical rationale for EZH2 inhibitor + chemotherapy combinations in SCLC.

**Detail:** C/E + EPZ co-treatment schedules tested across multiple PDX models showed superior tumor control and delayed/prevented resistance compared to C/E alone. The combination was well-tolerated by animal weight measurements.

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Does baseline SLFN11 expression predict benefit from platinum-based chemotherapy in prospective SCLC trials?
- Does EZH2 inhibitor + immunotherapy combination have activity in SCLC, given the chromatin remodeling effects?
- What is the upstream signal that increases EZH2 activity in chemoresistant SCLC—is it intrinsic lineage plasticity or treatment-induced?
- Is TWIST1 upregulation in resistant SCLC associated with a specific transcriptional subtype (ASCL1/NEUROD1/POU2F3/YAP1)?
- Can SLFN11 IHC be prospectively validated as a companion diagnostic for topoisomerase inhibitor therapy in SCLC?

## Limitations (from paper)

- PDX models are derived predominantly from chemonaive patients; may not fully recapitulate clinical acquired resistance kinetics.
- Small number of models (10 PDX) limits statistical power for identifying less frequent resistance mechanisms.
- EZH2 inhibitor single-agent in vivo efficacy was modest; optimal dosing schedule for epigenome remodeling may require longer treatment than feasible in mouse models.
- SLFN11 IHC clinical cohorts are small and retrospective; OS benefit not demonstrated.
- TWIST1's role in resistance is associative, not mechanistically established in this study.
