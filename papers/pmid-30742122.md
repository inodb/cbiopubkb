## Summary

First randomized clinical trial demonstrating a survival benefit for neoadjuvant (pre-surgical) versus adjuvant-only PD-1 blockade in recurrent, surgically resectable glioblastoma. 35 patients across 7 institutions were enrolled; neoadjuvant pembrolizumab (200 mg IV, 14±5 days pre-surgery) followed by adjuvant pembrolizumab was compared to adjuvant pembrolizumab alone. Neoadjuvant arm achieved median OS of 417 days (13.7 months) vs. 228 days (7.5 months) adjuvant-only (HR 0.39, P=0.04 log-rank). Comprehensive translational analyses — TCR sequencing, gene expression profiling (NanoString + RNA-seq), multiplex immunofluorescence, and mass cytometry — demonstrated that neoadjuvant timing uniquely activates interferon-γ signaling, suppresses tumor cell cycle programs, induces focal PD-L1 upregulation, and drives coordinated local and systemic T cell responses. The cell cycle GSVA score explained 57% of OS variance (R²=0.57). Data available in `gbm_iatlas_prins_2019` on cBioPortal.

## Key Findings

### F1: Neoadjuvant pembrolizumab confers significant OS and PFS benefit in recurrent GBM

**Finding:** Patients receiving neoadjuvant + adjuvant pembrolizumab had a median OS of 417 days (13.7 months) vs. 228 days (7.5 months) for adjuvant-only (HR 0.39, 95% CI 0.17–0.94, P=0.04, log-rank). Median PFS was 99.5 vs. 72.5 days (HR 0.43, 95% CI 0.20–0.90, P=0.03). The survival benefit held in the 15-per-arm per-protocol analysis of histologically confirmed GBM (OS HR 0.35, P=0.03). Baseline patient characteristics (age, sex, KPS, IDH status, MGMT, tumor volume, extent of resection) were balanced between arms.

**Detail:** As of July 2, 2018 cutoff, median follow-up was 476 days (15.6 months). 31/32 patients had discontinued pembrolizumab by cutoff (27 due to progression). 24 of 27 with progression received bevacizumab. The PFS endpoint may be confounded by pseudoprogression, a known phenomenon with GBM immunotherapy. Elastic net Cox regression selected neoadjuvant treatment, IDH mutation status, lower baseline peripheral T cell receptor clonality, and higher TIL density as survival-associated covariates.

**Genes / cancer types:** IDH1, MGMT · Glioblastoma (recurrent)

### F2: Neoadjuvant PD-1 blockade induces intratumoral interferon-γ and suppresses cell cycle programs

**Finding:** Tumors from neoadjuvant patients showed significant transcriptional upregulation of interferon-γ-related gene expression (Mann-Whitney P=0.03). RNA-seq GSVA analysis identified interferon pathway and T cell pathway induction in 9/14 neoadjuvant tumors vs. 5/15 adjuvant-only tumors. Cell cycle/proliferation gene set enrichment was positive in only 3/14 neoadjuvant tumors vs. 11/15 adjuvant-only (P=0.01, Fisher exact test). These two programs were largely mutually exclusive: only 1 neoadjuvant tumor showed both interferon and cell cycle activation.

**Detail:** The cell cycle GSVA enrichment score (FARMER_BREAST_CANCER_CLUSTER_2) explained 57% of OS variance (R²=0.57) and improved the Cox model R² from 0.36 to 0.62 when added to clinical covariates. Proportions of interferon/cell cycle signatures were comparable to TCGA GBM (73/166, P=0.7) and an independent recurrent GBM dataset (GSE79671; 11/20, P=0.8), validating baseline rates. This interferon-mediated cell cycle suppression mechanism is consistent with known effects of interferon-regulated gene products on cell cycle arrest and anti-proliferation.

**Genes / cancer types:** IFN-γ responsive genes, cell cycle genes · Glioblastoma

### F3: Neoadjuvant timing induces focal PD-L1 upregulation with CD8+ T cell infiltration

**Finding:** Multiplex immunofluorescence (CD8, PD-1, PD-L1, CD45, GFAP) classified tumors as constitutive, focal, or negative PD-L1 expressors. Seven of 15 neoadjuvant patients (vs. 3/15 adjuvant-only) showed focal PD-L1 expression with high CD8+ T cell infiltration. Focal PD-L1 in the adjuvant cases was qualitatively less intense, suggesting a less robust endogenous T cell response. The 3 adjuvant-only patients with focal PD-L1 tended toward below-median OS.

**Detail:** Focal (rather than constitutive) PD-L1 upregulation is mechanistically explained by interferon-γ-producing TILs inducing PD-L1 locally in regions of high T cell infiltration, consistent with the adaptive immune resistance model. Constitutive PD-L1 expression in GBM may reflect alternative (non-interferon) regulatory mechanisms. PD-L1 induction was not associated with steroid dose at registration, suggesting corticosteroid confounding is minimal at the doses used.

**Genes / cancer types:** CD274 (PD-L1), CD8A, PDCD1 (PD-1) · Glioblastoma

### F4: Neoadjuvant PD-1 blockade initiates coordinated local–systemic T cell response via TCR modulation

**Finding:** TCRβ sequencing on peripheral blood (baseline, surgery, cycle 2) and tumor tissue revealed that in neoadjuvant patients, the fraction of expanded T cell clones was correlated with tumor-blood TCR overlap across all three time points (Spearman rs=0.86–0.89, all P<0.001). These correlations were absent in adjuvant-only patients, demonstrating that pre-surgical checkpoint release uniquely drives systemic tumor-specific T cell priming. The amount of TCR overlap at surgery was also significantly correlated with TIL density in neoadjuvant patients only (rs=0.83, P=0.0005).

**Detail:** Absolute numbers of expanded clones and TCR diversity did not significantly differ between groups at any time point. The absence of TCR diversification with PD-1 blockade is consistent with the known biology that PD-1 does not mediate T cell repertoire expansion — rather it restores functional T cell effector activity. Baseline peripheral TCR clonality trended toward association with worse OS (HR 1.48 per SD, P=0.12), suggesting greater initial T cell diversity may predict better checkpoint inhibitor responsiveness.

**Genes / cancer types:** PDCD1 (PD-1), T cell receptor loci · Glioblastoma

### F5: Systemic immune changes — monocyte depletion and PD-1 reduction on CD4+ T cells — are neoadjuvant-specific

**Finding:** CyTOF (mass cytometry) on 84 concatenated peripheral blood samples identified a statistically significant decrease in an intermediate monocyte population (CD11b+CD11c−CD14+CD16+HLA-DRhi) in neoadjuvant patients between baseline and post-adjuvant cycle (mean 1.35% → 0.56%, Benjamini-Hochberg corrected P=0.007). Additionally, CD4+ T cells in neoadjuvant patients showed significantly decreased PD-1 expression (corrected P=0.025) and increased CTLA-4 (CD152, corrected P=0.0015) after the first pembrolizumab dose — changes not observed in adjuvant-only patients.

**Detail:** Unsupervised FlowSOM + ConsensusClusterPlus clustering identified 100 SOM vectors grouped into optimal clusters. No specific cell population consistently predicted survival, differing from previous findings in other cancers. The decrease in intermediate monocytes may reflect a shift in myeloid-mediated immune suppression. Increased CTLA-4 on CD4+ T cells post-PD-1 blockade is consistent with adaptive resistance and suggests rationale for combination PD-1 + CTLA-4 blockade in future trials.

**Genes / cancer types:** CTLA4, PDCD1, CD14, CD16, HLA-DR · Glioblastoma

## Open Questions

- Would neoadjuvant combination PD-1 + CTLA-4 blockade (nivolumab + ipilimumab) generate a superior systemic T cell response, given the observed CTLA-4 upregulation on CD4+ T cells post-pembrolizumab?
- Can baseline peripheral TCR clonality serve as a prospective patient selection biomarker for neoadjuvant checkpoint blockade in GBM (trend toward HR 1.48 per SD)?
- Does MGMT promoter methylation status modulate the neoadjuvant pembrolizumab benefit, given the imbalance in MGMT methylation between arms (38% vs. 69%) and its known role in TMZ response?
- What is the mechanism by which interferon-γ signaling suppresses tumor cell cycle gene expression — direct STAT1-mediated transcriptional repression or indirect immunoediting?
- Can this neoadjuvant approach be combined with standard bevacizumab post-progression therapy without ablating the immunogenic benefit?

## Limitations

- Small sample size (n=35 enrolled, n=32 per protocol); study was powered for tissue analyses, not formal efficacy endpoints
- MGMT methylation imbalance between arms (38% neoadjuvant vs. 69% adjuvant) may confound survival analysis despite randomization
- Most patients received bevacizumab after progression, confounding post-progression survival attribution
- Dexamethasone use was heterogeneous; immunosuppressive effects may differentially affect immune response, though no significant correlation was found at study doses
- Multi-center design limited tissue availability for comprehensive translational analyses
- Pseudoprogression may have confounded PFS endpoint interpretation
- Genomic/mutational data for tumors (e.g., TMB, specific driver mutations) not central to the published analysis
