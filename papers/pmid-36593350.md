## Summary

This retrospective study of 103 GIST patients with matched tumor-germline sequencing (MSK-IMPACT) at MSKCC demonstrates that expanded germline testing, irrespective of syndromic features, identifies clinically important hereditary cancer predisposition in a substantial fraction of patients. Among KIT/PDGFRA-wildtype GISTs, 69% harbored a germline pathogenic/likely pathogenic (P/LP) variant — predominantly in SDH-complex genes (SDHA/B/C: 46%) or NF1 (20%) — and 63% of those had no personal or family history of syndromic features. A parallel tumor-only analysis of 499 paired tumor-normal cases showed that P/LP variants in certain genes (BRCA1/2, SDHB) detected in tumor-only sequencing are almost exclusively germline in origin, providing guidance for triaging germline testing from somatic data. Data are available in cBioPortal as `gist_msk_2022`.

## Key Findings

### F1: 69% of KIT/PDGFRA-wildtype GISTs carry a germline P/LP variant in a GIST-associated gene

**Finding:** Among 35 patients with KIT/PDGFRA-wildtype GISTs, 69% (24/35) harbored a germline P/LP variant in a GIST-associated gene (SDHA/B/C or NF1 or KIT). In the overall cohort of 103 GIST patients, 23% had a germline P/LP variant in a GIST-associated gene.

**Detail:** The 24 germline P/LP carriers in KIT/PDGFRA-wildtype GISTs included: 16 in SDH-complex genes (seven SDHA, seven SDHB, two SDHC; 46%), seven in NF1 (20%), and one in KIT (3%). Seven additional patients had somatic driver mutations (NF1 biallelic, SDHA biallelic, BRAF). Three inconclusive monoallelic SDHB/D cases were identified. Patients with germline variants had younger median age of onset (39.5 vs. 52 years, p=0.01), higher rates of metastatic disease (50% vs. 20%, p=0.01), and more multifocal lesions (50% vs. 18%, p=0.01) than patients without variants.

**Genes / cancer types:** `SDHA`, `SDHB`, `SDHC`, `NF1`, `KIT` · GIST (KIT/PDGFRA-wildtype)

### F2: 63% of GIST patients with germline GIST-associated variants have no syndromic features — challenging historical testing criteria

**Finding:** Of the 24 patients with germline P/LP variants in GIST-associated genes, 63% (15/24) had no personal or family history of syndromic features at time of testing. This refutes the assumption that germline alterations in GIST predominantly present with syndromic (paraganglioma, NF1) features.

**Detail:** Of 16 patients with germline SDH-complex defects, 88% (14/16) had no personal or family history of syndromic features. SDHB immunohistochemistry showed absent expression in 13/13 tested cases. For NF1, 86% (6/7) had NF1 features, but one patient's germline NF1 variant led to recognition of previously overlooked café-au-lait spots and freckling. One patient had a germline KIT p.Lys509Ile pathogenic variant. These findings directly challenge current guidelines that restrict germline testing to patients with syndromic presentations.

### F3: Tumor-only sequencing can flag likely germline variants in GIST — gene-specific VAF patterns distinguish germline from somatic

**Finding:** In a de-identified cohort of 499 GISTs with paired tumor-normal sequencing analyzed in a simulated tumor-only manner, P/LP variants in BRCA1/2 (4/4) and SDHB (10/11) were almost exclusively germline in origin, while KIT variants (430/431) and RB1 (9/9) were primarily somatic.

**Detail:** ESMO-recommended VAF thresholds (>30% for SNVs, >20% for indels) correctly distinguished all true germline from somatic P/LP variants as confirmed by matched normal blood data. This enables clinicians to identify patients who should be referred for germline testing based on tumor-only sequencing results — particularly when germline sequencing was not performed or consented. Of 69 KIT/PDGFRA-wildtype GISTs in the 499-patient cohort, 48% had germline P/LP variants in GIST-associated genes, consistent with the germline cohort findings.

### F4: SDH-deficient and NF1-related GISTs are resistant to imatinib — germline testing informs targeted therapy selection

**Finding:** Correct identification of germline SDH-complex or NF1 alterations in GIST patients has direct therapeutic implications: SDH-deficient and NF1-mutant GISTs respond poorly to traditional imatinib therapy, requiring alternative treatment strategies.

**Detail:** KIT/PDGFRA-wildtype GISTs driven by SDH-complex or NF1 alterations do not respond to imatinib (which targets KIT/PDGFRA kinase activity), making accurate molecular diagnosis essential for first-line therapy selection. Additionally, germline SDH-complex gene mutations confer risk for paragangliomas and pheochromocytomas, while germline NF1 mutations confer risk for nerve sheath tumors, breast cancer, and gliomas — requiring broader cancer surveillance and family cascade testing.

**Genes / cancer types:** `SDHA`, `SDHB`, `SDHC`, `NF1` · GIST

**Data availability:** `available`

### F5: Germline incidental findings in non-GIST cancer predisposition genes detected in 8% of KIT/PDGFRA-mutant GIST patients

**Finding:** Eight patients (all with KIT/PDGFRA-mutant GISTs) had germline P/LP variants in cancer predisposition genes not known to be associated with GIST: TP53, MLH1, BRCA2, RECQL (one each), APC p.Ile1307Lys (two patients), and monoallelic MUTYH (two patients).

**Detail:** For the MLH1 carrier, the tumor was microsatellite-stable; for the BRCA2 carrier, no homologous recombination deficiency signature was detected — suggesting incidental findings rather than causal roles. LOH was not observed in these genes in corresponding tumors. These findings support offering broad cancer predisposition gene panels (not GIST-focused genes only) to all GIST patients, as 8% carry clinically actionable germline variants in other cancer predisposition genes.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53`, `MLH1`, `BRCA2`, `RECQL`, `APC`, `MUTYH` · GIST (KIT/PDGFRA-mutant)

## Open Questions

- Do germline SDH-deficient GIST patients have better or worse outcomes on alternative therapies (sunitinib, regorafenib, ripretinib) compared to sporadic SDH-deficient cases?
- Should all GIST patients — regardless of KIT/PDGFRA mutation status — be offered germline testing for both GIST-associated and broader cancer predisposition genes?
- Can SDHB immunohistochemistry alone reliably substitute for germline SDH gene sequencing as a screening tool?
- What is the optimal VAF threshold for flagging likely germline variants in tumor-only GIST sequencing across different sequencing platforms and tumor purities?
- How does germline NF1 mutation status impact treatment decisions beyond imatinib resistance in GISTs?

## Limitations

- Germline testing cohort limited to 103 patients who consented; referral at physician discretion introduces selection bias
- Structural variants and low-level mosaicism are not reliably detected by MSK-IMPACT, potentially underestimating germline variant prevalence
- Detailed clinical phenotyping (syndromic features) available only for the 103-patient consented cohort, not the full 499-patient cohort
- Relatively short follow-up limits survival outcome analyses stratified by germline status
- Functional significance of some germline NF1 and SDHB monoallelic variants cannot be fully assessed without additional tumor LOH data
