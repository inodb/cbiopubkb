## Summary

This landmark Nature study uses prospectively collected matched trios (normal colon, primary CRC, and synchronous metastasis) from 31 MSS/pMMR colorectal cancer (CRC) patients to map cancer cell-state transitions during metastatic progression using scRNA-seq, multiplexed immunofluorescence, and patient-derived organoids. Primary CRC tumors are enriched in LGR5⁺ intestinal stem cell (ISC)-like states, while metastases progressively lose intestinal identity. A conserved fetal progenitor intermediate state bridges canonical intestinal programs to divergent non-canonical differentiation into squamous-like and neuroendocrine-like lineages. These non-canonical states, enriched in metastases and exacerbated by chemotherapy, are associated with poor patient survival in two independent cohorts (TCGA COAD and MSK LARC). *PROX1* is identified as a key transcriptional repressor of non-intestinal lineage; its downregulation licenses non-canonical reprogramming. Metastatic cells demonstrate greater intrinsic multilineage plasticity than primary tumor cells in organoid assays. This study fundamentally reframes CRC metastasis as a process of progressive phenotypic plasticity rather than simple genetic selection.

## Key Findings

### F1: Metastases progressively lose LGR5+ ISC identity and gain non-canonical squamous-like and neuroendocrine-like programs, enriched relative to primary tumors

**Finding:** Using Hotspot gene co-expression module discovery, 10 gene modules were identified: four canonical intestinal (ISC-like, absorptive, secretory, injury-repair) and six non-canonical (EMT, endodermal development, squamous-like, neuroendocrine-like, osteoblast-like, and an L1CAM+/EMP1+ metastasis-initiating module). Metastases were significantly enriched for cells expressing non-canonical modules (p=0.001, rank-sum test), while primary tumors were enriched for canonical intestinal modules. This pattern was replicated in an independent 5-patient cohort.

**Detail:** In contrast to mouse CRC metastasis models (which show ISC dependency), human metastases express ISC programs at *lower* levels than primary tumors, particularly after chemotherapy. Squamous-like (KRT5, ELF5) and neuroendocrine-like (NEUROD1, CHGB) modules are expressed in untreated patients — preceding chemotherapy — indicating that therapy is not a prerequisite for non-canonical state acquisition. Multiplex IF confirmed CHGA (neuroendocrine) and CK5 (squamous) protein expression in metastatic tumor sections. CK20 (differentiated intestinal) and OLFM4 (ISC) were downregulated in metastases, while TROP2 (injury-repair state) was upregulated.

**Genes / cancer types:** `LGR5`, `ASCL2`, `L1CAM`, `EMP1`, `KRT5`, `ELF5`, `NEUROD1`, `CHGB`, `OLFM4`, `TACSTD2` (TROP2), `CK20` · Colorectal cancer (MSS/pMMR)

### F2: A conserved fetal progenitor intermediate state bridges canonical ISC programs to non-canonical metastatic differentiation

**Finding:** Cancer cell-state trajectories across patients reveal a consistent stepwise progression: differentiated intestinal states → ISC-like state → fetal progenitor state → divergent non-canonical (squamous or neuroendocrine) states. The fetal progenitor state — defined by 113 genes differentially expressed in first-trimester colonic progenitors vs. second-trimester colonocytes in the Human Cell Atlas — is significantly enriched in metastases (14-gene conserved core, p=0.0004). High fetal progenitor signature expression in pretreatment primary tumors is associated with decreased disease-free survival in TCGA COAD (p=0.0016) and MSK LARC (p=0.0350) cohorts.

**Detail:** The fetal progenitor state is identified across all four patients with sufficient non-canonical cells for trajectory analysis (KG146, KG182, KG150, KG183) and independently in a published dataset. A core set of 14 WNT-associated genes (including *TCF7*, *PTK7*) defines this state across patients. The fetal signature deviates substantially from published mouse dedifferentiation signatures (low Jaccard overlap), emphasizing the importance of human-specific characterization. This state represents a plastic regenerative intermediate distinct from homeostatic ISCs, consistent with observations of fetal reversion in tissue repair biology.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TCF7`, `PTK7`, `LGR5`, `EPHB2`, `ASCL2`, `BMP7`, `SOX4` · Colorectal cancer

### F3: Non-canonical gene expression in primary tumors predicts poor survival across two independent cohorts (TCGA, MSK LARC)

**Finding:** ssGSEA enrichment of non-canonical modules in pretreatment primary tumor bulk RNA-seq data from TCGA COAD (n=445) and MSK LARC (n=108) is associated with tumor relapse and overall survival <10 years. Canonical ISC/absorptive modules correlate with improved survival. In TCGA COAD multivariable regression correcting for clinical confounders, absorptive intestinal module expression associated with improved disease-free and overall survival; endoderm module associated with worse outcomes. The absorptive, secretory, endoderm, and squamous modules all show significant survival associations (log-rank p=0.003, 0.003, 0.001, 0.026, respectively).

**Detail:** Non-canonical module expression corresponds to CRC consensus molecular subtype CMS4 (associated with poor outcomes, EMT features), while canonical modules correspond to CMS2 or CMS3. This links the plasticity program to an established CRC classification system with clinical relevance. The finding that non-canonical states in pretreatment primary tumors — before metastasis has become clinically apparent — associate with poor outcomes suggests these states are acquired early and could serve as prognostic biomarkers at diagnosis.

**Genes / cancer types:** Module-level signatures (LGR5, KRT5, NEUROD1, CDX2, etc.) · Colorectal cancer (stage I–IV)

### F4: PROX1 represses non-intestinal lineage in the fetal progenitor state; its downregulation licenses non-canonical reprogramming

**Finding:** *PROX1*, a transcriptional repressor, is coordinately upregulated with the fetal progenitor state across multiple patients. Downregulation of *PROX1* is identified as a key molecular event that licenses differentiation into non-canonical (squamous, neuroendocrine) lineages. Patient-derived organoids from matched trios demonstrate that metastatic cells exhibit greater cell-autonomous multilineage differentiation potential in response to microenvironment cues compared with intestinal lineage-restricted primary tumor counterparts.

**Detail:** The PROX1 finding provides a molecular mechanism for the lineage restriction observed in primary CRC vs. the plasticity seen in metastases. In organoid assays, primary tumor organoids retain intestinal lineage restriction (absorptive/secretory), while matched metastatic organoids differentiate into squamous-like or neuroendocrine-like states in response to microenvironmental signals — recapitulating the in vivo observations. This cell-autonomous plasticity of metastatic cells is consistent with epigenetic (rather than purely genetic) reprogramming, as mutations are largely conserved between primary and metastatic tumors from the same patient.

### F5: Chemotherapy exacerbates non-canonical plasticity; injury-repair and metastasis-initiating states are enriched in treated patients

**Finding:** Treated patients (5-FU-based chemotherapy pre-operatively, n=22) show significantly greater depletion of ISC programs (p<0.001 vs. untreated, Wilcoxon rank-sum) and greater enrichment of non-canonical module-expressing cells in both primary tumors and metastases. The injury-repair module (*L1CAM*, *EMP1*, *TACSTD2*/TROP2) is upregulated in metastases across both treated and untreated patients, but is particularly elevated after treatment.

**Detail:** The L1CAM+/EMP1+/TROP2+ injury-repair module marks metastasis-initiating cells at the invasion front, functionally linking transcriptionally defined cell states to metastatic biology. *TACSTD2* (TROP2) is the target of sacituzumab govitecan, an approved ADC in urothelial and triple-negative breast cancer. The enrichment of TROP2-expressing cells in CRC metastases — particularly post-chemotherapy — raises the hypothesis that CRC patients with chemotherapy-treated metastatic disease may benefit from TROP2-directed therapy. Importantly, non-canonical states were already present in untreated patients, indicating that chemotherapy amplifies but does not initiate this plasticity program.

**Genes / cancer types:** `L1CAM`, `EMP1`, `TACSTD2` (TROP2), `CD70`, `OSMR` · Colorectal cancer (MSS/pMMR)

## Open Questions

- Does PROX1 downregulation occur via epigenetic mechanisms (DNA methylation, histone modification), and can it be reversed to restore lineage restriction?
- Can non-canonical module expression in liquid biopsies (ctDNA, circulating tumor cells) serve as a real-time biomarker of metastatic plasticity?
- Do CRC patients with high non-canonical module expression (fetal progenitor or squamous/neuroendocrine states) in primary tumors benefit from different therapeutic strategies (e.g., TROP2-directed ADCs, platinum-based chemotherapy)?
- Are similar plasticity trajectories (fetal progenitor → non-canonical) observed in other gastrointestinal malignancies (pancreatic, gastric)?
- Do the L1CAM+/TROP2+ metastasis-initiating cells represent a discrete clonal subpopulation with distinct driver mutations, or is their state epigenetically plastic?
- Is osteoblast-like CRC module expression responsible for bone tropism in a subset of metastases?

## Limitations

- Small cohort (n=31 patients); MSS/pMMR only — MSI-high CRC excluded, limiting generalizability
- scRNA-seq captures transcriptional state; matched whole-genome sequencing not performed for all patients (only MSK-IMPACT for subset)
- Single time point per metastasis site (except 6 patients with metachronous samples); temporal dynamics of plasticity not fully captured
- Liver metastasis predominant (29/31 patients); plasticity in other metastatic sites (lung, bone, peritoneum) underrepresented
- Organoid and xenograft experiments in subset of patients (n=15); not all cell states successfully established in culture
- Bulk RNA-seq validation cohorts (TCGA COAD, MSK LARC) use primary tumor tissue — not metastasis — which may underestimate the prognostic signal of non-canonical states
- PROX1 mechanism shown by correlation and organoid experiments; direct causal in vivo validation in patient-derived models limited
