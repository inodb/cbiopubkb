## Summary
This study prospectively sequenced 127 patients with advanced HCC using MSK-IMPACT (341+ gene panel) and correlated genomic profiles with outcomes on sorafenib and immune checkpoint inhibitors. PI3K-mTOR pathway activation was associated with poor sorafenib outcomes, while WNT/β-catenin activation predicted innate resistance to immune checkpoint blockade. Twenty-four percent of patients harbored potentially actionable alterations, and 5.7% were matched to genome-directed targeted therapies.

## Dataset
- **Cohort**: 127 unique HCC patients (134 samples) prospectively enrolled at MSKCC, August 2014–August 2017; enriched for advanced/unresectable disease (61% not eligible for curative resection)
- **Platform**: MSK-IMPACT hybridization capture-based NGS (341, 410, or 468 gene panels); matched tumor/normal; mean coverage 690×
- **Data types**: Somatic mutations (SNVs, indels), copy number alterations, structural rearrangements, tumor mutation burden (TMB), MSI score; clinical treatment and outcome data
- **cBioPortal study**: hcc_mskimpact_2018

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: Mutually exclusive WNT/β-catenin and TP53 genomic subsets define HCC
- **Detail**: WNT/β-catenin pathway alterations (CTNNB1, AXIN1, APC) occurred in ~45% of tumors and TP53 mutations in 33%; these two subsets were mutually exclusive (P=0.003), defining the major molecular subgroups of HCC. Virally mediated HCC was enriched for TP53 mutations (45% vs. 21%, P=0.001).
- **Genes**: TERT, CTNNB1, TP53, AXIN1, APC, ARID1A
- **Data availability**: available — mutation and CNA data in hcc_mskimpact_2018
- **Tool support**: native — oncoprint and mutation frequency views available in cBioPortal UI
- **Concepts**: mutation frequency, oncoprint, mutual exclusivity, molecular subtypes

### Finding 2: PI3K-mTOR pathway activation predicts poor sorafenib outcomes
- **Detail**: In 81 sorafenib-treated patients, PI3K-mTOR pathway alterations were associated with markedly lower disease control rate (DCR 8.3% vs. 40.2%, P=0.05), shorter median PFS (1.9 vs. 5.3 months, HR 3.8, P<0.0001), and shorter median OS (10.4 vs. 17.9 months, HR 2.5, P=0.01).
- **Genes**: PIK3CA, TSC1, TSC2, PTEN
- **Data availability**: available — genomic alterations available; sorafenib treatment outcome data may be partial
- **Tool support**: api — survival analysis with genomic stratification requires API or custom code for treatment-specific outcomes
- **Concepts**: survival analysis, mutation frequency, actionability, targeted therapy

### Finding 3: WNT/β-catenin activation confers innate resistance to immune checkpoint inhibitors
- **Detail**: Among 31 ICI-treated patients, none of the 10 with WNT pathway-activated tumors achieved clinical benefit (0% DCR), vs. 53% DCR in non-WNT tumors (P=0.009); WNT activation also correlated with shorter PFS (2.0 vs. 7.4 months, HR 9.2, P<0.0001).
- **Genes**: CTNNB1, AXIN1
- **Data availability**: available — genomic alterations available; ICI treatment-specific outcomes partial
- **Tool support**: api — ICI response stratification by pathway requires custom query
- **Concepts**: survival analysis, mutation frequency, molecular subtypes, actionability

### Finding 4: TMB is uniformly low in HCC; MSI-H not observed
- **Detail**: Median TMB in HCC was 4.08 mutations/Mb with low variance (SD=2.71); no MSI-H tumors were identified in this cohort. TMB did not differentiate ICI responders from non-responders (median TMB 3.8 vs. 3.9, P=0.98), suggesting TMB and MSI are unlikely to be major predictors of ICI response in HCC.
- **Genes**: N/A
- **Data availability**: available — TMB data available in hcc_mskimpact_2018
- **Tool support**: native — TMB plots and comparison across studies available in cBioPortal
- **Concepts**: tumor mutation burden, MSI, mutation frequency

### Finding 5: Actionable alterations in 24% of patients; rare but targetable
- **Detail**: OncoKB annotation identified potentially actionable alterations in 24% of patients, including TSC1/2 inactivating mutations (8.5%), FGF19 amplification (6.3%), MET amplification (1.5%), and oncogenic PIK3CA/HRAS/NRAS/IDH1 mutations (<1%); no Level 1/2A alterations were found, highlighting the investigational nature of these targets.
- **Genes**: TSC1, TSC2, FGF19, MET, PIK3CA, HRAS, NRAS, IDH1, PTEN
- **Data availability**: available — all alterations available in cBioPortal; OncoKB actionability levels queryable via API
- **Tool support**: api — OncoKB-annotated actionability requires API access
- **Concepts**: actionability, targeted therapy, mutation frequency

### Finding 6: Prospective NGS enabled patient matching to targeted therapies
- **Detail**: 5.7% of patients receiving systemic therapy were matched to targeted treatments based on NGS: 4 of 10 TSC1/2-mutant patients received everolimus (mTOR inhibitor) and 1 MET-amplified patient was enrolled in a MET/VEGFR2 trial; 4 of 5 matched patients achieved disease stabilization after rapid sorafenib failure.
- **Genes**: TSC1, TSC2, MET, VEGFA
- **Data availability**: partial — genomic data available; trial enrollment and treatment matching data not in cBioPortal
- **Tool support**: custom — clinical trial matching and treatment response tracking requires custom analysis
- **Concepts**: actionability, targeted therapy

### Finding 7: Genomic landscape of advanced HCC mirrors retrospective resection cohorts
- **Detail**: Despite enrichment for advanced disease, mutation frequencies in the MSK prospective cohort did not differ significantly from the TCGA resection cohort, validating MSK-IMPACT as a clinically deployable profiling tool across disease stages.
- **Genes**: TERT, CTNNB1, TP53, ARID1A, TSC2, CCND1
- **Data availability**: available — full genomic data in hcc_mskimpact_2018; TCGA HCC data also in cBioPortal
- **Tool support**: native — cross-study comparison available in cBioPortal UI
- **Concepts**: mutation frequency, oncoprint, molecular subtypes

## Open Questions
- Can WNT/β-catenin activation as a predictive biomarker of ICI resistance be validated in larger, prospective HCC cohorts?
- Do combination strategies (e.g., WNT inhibitors + ICI) overcome immune exclusion in CTNNB1-mutant HCC?
- Does PI3K-mTOR inhibition restore sorafenib sensitivity in TSC1/2-mutant or other PI3K-mTOR-activated HCCs?

## Limitations
- Small sample sizes for ICI (N=31) and sorafenib (N=81) outcome analyses limit statistical power and preclude multivariable adjustment for confounders such as etiology, AFP, and extent of disease.
- Single-site tumor biopsies cannot capture intratumoral heterogeneity; ctDNA or multi-region sampling were not performed.
- Targeted panel sequencing excludes epigenetic alterations, viral HBV DNA integration, and genes outside the panel; structural rearrangements are incompletely detected.
