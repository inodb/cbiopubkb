## Summary

MSK-IMPACT study of 604 patients with completely resected stage I–III lung adenocarcinoma (LUAD), correlating predominant histologic subtype — lepidic (LEP, 15%), acinar/papillary (ACI/PAP, 68%), micropapillary/solid (MIP/SOL, 17%) — with tumor genomics, mutational signatures, oncogenic pathway alterations, chromosomal instability (CIN), and post-resection recurrence. MIP/SOL tumors harbor the highest genomic complexity (TMB, FGA, WGD, pathway count). APOBEC mutational signatures (SBS2 and SBS13) predict recurrence across all subtypes. PI3K pathway alterations predict recurrence in ACI/PAP and MIP/SOL tumors. MIP/SOL tumors are enriched for BRAF-V600E, representing a targetable opportunity. This is the first systematic genomic characterization linked to LUAD histologic grade in a surgical cohort.

## Key Findings

### F1: MIP/SOL tumors exhibit markedly higher genomic complexity — TMB, FGA, WGD, and pathway burden all increase with histologic grade

**Finding:** Genomic complexity increases stepwise with LUAD histologic invasiveness. Median TMB: LEP 3.9, ACI/PAP 4.9, MIP/SOL 7.9 mut/Mb (p<0.001). Median FGA: LEP 0.174, ACI/PAP 0.222, MIP/SOL 0.304 (p=0.001). WGD rate: LEP 4.5%, ACI/PAP 10%, MIP/SOL 18% (p=0.008). Mean oncogenic pathways altered: LEP 1.65, ACI/PAP 2.00, MIP/SOL 2.34 (p<0.001). Copy number amplifications were highest in MIP/SOL (p=0.021).

**Detail:** The correlation between TMB and FGA also strengthened with grade (LEP: ρ=−0.03, p=0.770; ACI/PAP: ρ=0.11, p=0.029; MIP/SOL: ρ=0.28, p=0.004), suggesting that highly invasive subtypes accumulate both mutational and CNA-driven genomic damage in a coordinated fashion. Arm-level CNA differences across subtypes were significant at 13 chromosomal arms including 17p (TP53 locus, p=0.008) and 17q (p<0.001). MIP/SOL tumors had the highest copy number amplifications consistent with oncogene selection. These data suggest that genomic complexity metrics (TMB, FGA, WGD) could serve as continuous surrogates for histologic grade when pathology is unavailable.

### F2: TP53, SETD2, MGA, and SMARCA4 are significantly enriched in high-grade MIP/SOL LUAD; EGFR and RBM10 enriched in lepidic

**Finding:** Four genes are significantly enriched in MIP/SOL vs. LEP and ACI/PAP tumors: TP53 (62% vs. 33% vs. 16%; p<0.001), SETD2 (8% vs. 3% vs. 1.1%; p=0.048), MGA (11% vs. 2% vs. 2.3%; p<0.001), and SMARCA4 (6% vs. 1.5% vs. 2.3%; p=0.035). Conversely, EGFR (42% vs. 31% vs. 19%; p=0.001), RBM10 (26% vs. 12% vs. 10%; p=0.001), and TERT (14% vs. 5% vs. 10%; p=0.012) are enriched in LEP tumors.

**Detail:** TP53 enrichment in MIP/SOL confirms that p53 loss drives high-grade LUAD biology. SETD2 (H3K36 methyltransferase, chromatin remodeling), MGA (MAX dimerization protein, Myc network), and SMARCA4 (SWI/SNF chromatin remodeler) represent novel epigenetic/transcriptional regulators enriched in aggressive LUAD, identifying new mechanistic drivers of histologic progression. EGFR and RBM10 enrichment in LEP aligns with prior reports of EGFR-mutant LUAD being predominantly low-grade. The RTK/RAS pathway as a whole was more commonly altered in LEP tumors (92% vs. 91% vs. 81%; p=0.010), dominated by EGFR and KRAS.

### F3: APOBEC mutational signatures (SBS2 and SBS13) independently predict post-resection recurrence across all LUAD subtypes

**Finding:** Across all LUAD subtypes, the presence of APOBEC-attributed mutational signatures SBS2 (subhazard ratio [SHR] 2.07, 95% CI 1.11–3.84; p=0.021) and SBS13 (SHR 2.27, 95% CI 1.29–4.02; p=0.005) is significantly associated with increased risk of post-resection recurrence after competing-risks regression. This association was replicated in the ACI/PAP subset (SBS2: SHR 2.56, p=0.02; SBS13: SHR 2.85, p=0.007). SBS3 (defective HR repair) was enriched in LEP.

**Detail:** Mutational signatures were computed only in tumors with ≥13.8 mut/Mb (n=268; 44% of cohort). APOBEC3A/3B cytidine deaminase activity (SBS2/13) increases with histologic grade and is associated with higher recurrence risk independent of stage and resection type. This raises the possibility that APOBEC mutagenesis drives clonal evolution and metastatic potential in LUAD. SBS3 enrichment in LEP may reflect a distinct HR-defective subtype of low-grade LUAD not previously characterized. No signature was significantly associated with recurrence in MIP/SOL tumors, possibly due to the dominant role of structural chromosomal alterations in that group.

### F4: Three oncogenic pathways — p53, Wnt, and Myc — are significantly enriched in high-grade MIP/SOL LUAD; PI3K alterations predict recurrence

**Finding:** MIP/SOL tumors are significantly enriched for p53 pathway (67% vs. 43% vs. 28%; p<0.001), Wnt pathway (7.6% vs. 5% vs. 0%; p=0.042), and Myc pathway (13% vs. 5.6% vs. 9.1%; p=0.022) alterations. Among ACI/PAP tumors, cell cycle pathway (SHR 2.74; p<0.001) and PI3K pathway (SHR 2.11; p=0.002) alterations were independently associated with recurrence; among MIP/SOL tumors, PI3K pathway alterations remained significant (SHR 1.97; p=0.049).

**Detail:** The p53–cell cycle co-occurrence was the strongest inter-pathway pair across all subtypes (OR 4.47; p<0.001). PI3K pathway alterations are a pan-subtype recurrence predictor, suggesting a role for PI3K pathway activation in LUAD dissemination regardless of grade. Wnt and Myc enrichment in MIP/SOL identifies candidate therapeutic targets beyond MAPK pathway inhibition in high-grade LUAD. The mean number of pathways altered ranged from 1.65 (LEP) to 2.34 (MIP/SOL; p<0.001), and ≥4 pathways altered was most frequent in MIP/SOL (16%).

### F5: BRAF-V600E mutations are enriched in MIP/SOL LUAD, identifying a targetable subpopulation within high-grade tumors

**Finding:** BRAF-V600E is significantly more frequent in MIP/SOL tumors compared with ACI/PAP and LEP tumors (p=0.046). This enrichment within the highest-grade, highest-recurrence-risk subtype identifies BRAF-V600E as a therapeutic target in aggressive LUAD with a prognostically unfavorable histology.

**Detail:** BRAF-V600E occurs in approximately 1–3% of all LUAD; its enrichment in MIP/SOL, though based on small absolute numbers, has therapeutic implications given FDA-approved BRAF+MEK inhibitor combinations (dabrafenib+trametinib) for BRAF-V600E NSCLC. This finding connects histologic grade with precision oncology, supporting integrated histologic-genomic assessment at diagnosis. The overall actionable alteration rate across all LUAD subtypes was not reported as a single figure but reflects MSK-IMPACT panel breadth for KRAS, EGFR, ALK, RET, ROS1, MET, and BRAF targets.

## Open Questions

- Does integration of APOBEC signature status with histologic grade improve recurrence prediction beyond grade alone in a prospective surgical setting?
- Can WGD rate or FGA threshold serve as a continuous surrogate for histologic grade in samples where pathology is ambiguous (e.g., core biopsies, cytology)?
- Does PI3K pathway inhibition reduce recurrence in LUAD patients with PI3K-altered ACI/PAP or MIP/SOL tumors in an adjuvant setting?
- What molecular mechanisms underlie SETD2, MGA, and SMARCA4 enrichment in MIP/SOL tumors — do these represent independent drivers of histologic progression or co-selected events with TP53 loss?
- Is SBS3 (defective HR repair) enrichment in LEP tumors associated with BRCA1/2 germline variants or somatic HR gene mutations, and does it predict PARP inhibitor sensitivity?
- Do patients with BRAF-V600E MIP/SOL LUAD benefit from adjuvant BRAF/MEK inhibitors compared with chemotherapy alone?

## Limitations

- Retrospective single-institution study; selection biases from referral patterns, surgical eligibility, and IMPACT eligibility
- Median follow-up of only 2.51 years; long-term (5-year) recurrence events not captured for most patients
- Mutational signature analysis restricted to 44% of the cohort (those with ≥13.8 mut/Mb), limiting generalizability of signature findings to low-TMB tumors
- LEP subtype lacks recurrence events sufficient for independent multivariable analysis
- No RNA-seq, proteomics, or spatial transcriptomics data to mechanistically link histologic and genomic features
- Histologic subtype classification based on predominant pattern only; mixed-subtype heterogeneity within tumors not captured
- STAS, LVI, VPI are prognostic pathologic features associated with subtype but not incorporated into genomic analyses
- Small numbers of MIP and SOL tumors combined (n=105) may obscure biologically distinct features of each micropattern
