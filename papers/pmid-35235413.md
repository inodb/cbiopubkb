## Summary

This study systematically characterizes 47 treatment-naive colorectal cancer (CRC) patients harboring dual MAPK pathway driver mutations (CRC-DD) — specifically co-occurring RAS hotspot and/or BRAF V600E mutations — identified from a cohort of 4,561 CRC patients profiled by MSK-IMPACT. Dual-driver CRC occurs in ~1% of cases and challenges the canonical model of MAPK pathway mutual exclusivity. The authors develop a single-cell genotyping (SCG) method with 96.3% mutation detection rate and demonstrate that both driver mutations co-occur within the same tumor cells in 14–95% of cells depending on the case. CRC-DD tumors are enriched for microsatellite instability-high (MSI-H) status (23% vs. 11.4%), show lower rates of allelic imbalance compared to single-driver CRC, and retain both drivers across multiple tumor sites in most cases. These findings have critical implications for EGFR and BRAF inhibitor eligibility and interpretation of clinical genomic testing results.

## Key Findings

### F1: Dual RAS/BRAF driver mutations occur in ~1% of CRC and are confirmed within the same tumor cells by single-cell genotyping

**Finding:** Of 4,561 CRC patients profiled by MSK-IMPACT, 47 (1.03%) harbor dual MAPK pathway hotspot mutations. Single-cell genotyping (SCG) of 4 representative cases confirms that both driver mutations co-exist within the same individual tumor cells, with co-occurrence rates of 95%, 48%, 44%, and 14% per case, respectively.

**Detail:** CRC-DD mutation combinations included: KRAS/KRAS (n=32), KRAS/NRAS (n=11), KRAS/BRAF V600E (n=2), NRAS/BRAF V600E (n=1), NRAS/NRAS (n=1). The SCG method achieved 96.3% mutation detection rate and 92.1% genotype prediction accuracy, validated by Sanger sequencing in independent single cells. The case with highest co-occurrence (NRAS G13D/KRAS G12C, 95% of cells) showed spatial homogeneity across four tumor regions, with tumor evolution inference placing NRAS G13D as an early event followed by KRAS G12C acquisition. The case with BRAF V600E/KRAS G12D (44% co-occurrence) was MSI-H, consistent with the hypermutation-driven acquisition of secondary MAPK events.

### F2: MSI-H status is enriched in dual-driver CRC and explains a subset of dual-driver cases via hypermutation

**Finding:** MSI-H status is significantly enriched in CRC-DD compared to CRC-SD (23% vs. 11.4%; P=0.028). Both CRC-DD cases with discordant multiregional sequencing (i.e., dual drivers not present across all tumor sites) were MSI-H, consistent with hypermutation-driven, subclonal acquisition of secondary MAPK events.

**Detail:** MSI-H tumors accumulate thousands of somatic mutations due to mismatch repair deficiency; the co-occurrence of two MAPK pathway hotspot mutations in MSI-H CRC likely reflects hypermutation rather than a specific selective advantage. In contrast, MSS CRC-DD cases may reflect genuine co-selection of dual MAPK drivers — a biologically distinct mechanism. The enrichment of MSI-H in CRC-DD is clinically important: MSS BRAF V600E CRC did not acquire a second driver MAPK mutation before therapy, supporting the interpretation that BRAF V600E + RAS in MSS CRC is truly co-selected rather than a sequencing artifact.

### F3: Dual-driver CRC shows lower allelic imbalance and higher subclonal mutation rates than single-driver CRC

**Finding:** Allelic imbalance (AI) favoring the oncogenic allele is significantly less frequent in CRC-DD (31.5%, 24/76 mutations) vs. single-driver CRC (51.3%, 119/232 mutations; P=0.013). CRC-DD also shows higher rates of subclonal RAS hotspot mutations compared to CRC-SD.

**Detail:** In single-driver MAPK-mutant CRC, AI (including copy-neutral LOH or amplification of the mutant allele) is a common mechanism to increase oncogenic signaling dose. The lower AI in CRC-DD suggests that having two MAPK drivers may reduce the need for allelic dosage increase of either individual driver — consistent with a dose-dependent signaling model where combined MAPK input from two drivers substitutes for allelic imbalance of a single driver. The four clonality groups identified by FACETS: clonal/clonal (21%), clonal/subclonal (40%), subclonal/subclonal (19%), and indeterminate (21%).

### F4: Dual driver mutations are preserved across multiple tumor sites in most cases; liquid biopsy captures subclonal events

**Finding:** Of 7 CRC-DD cases with multiregional sequencing, 5 retained both driver mutations at all sequenced tumor sites. Both discordant cases (drivers not present at all sites) were MSI-H. The study highlights that subclonal mutations may be lost in metastases, and liquid biopsy may be needed to fully characterize the dual-driver landscape.

**Detail:** Spatial persistence of both dual drivers across primary and metastatic sites in MSS cases supports a clonal origin model where both mutations arise early and are propagated through metastatic spread. In MSI-H cases, the subclonal nature of at least one driver is consistent with a stochastic late-arising mutation. This has implications for cfDNA-based monitoring: liquid biopsy sampling from multiple timepoints may capture subclonal drivers missed by a single tissue biopsy. The clinical relevance is high — BRAF inhibitor eligibility requires absence of RAS mutation, and missing a subclonal second driver could lead to inappropriate treatment selection.

### F5: CRC-DD overall survival is not significantly worse than single-driver CRC, with MSS BRAF V600E CRC lacking a second driver at baseline

**Finding:** Overall survival did not differ significantly between CRC-DD and CRC-SD (P=0.07; median CRC-DD 63.5 months vs. CRC-SD 42.9 months). Multivariate analysis identified single-mutant status as associated with worse OS (HR=1.74; 95% CI 1.34–2.30; P<0.001). Critically, no MSS BRAF V600E CRC in the dataset had an acquired second MAPK driver before EGFR/BRAF inhibitor therapy.

**Detail:** The numerically longer survival in CRC-DD (vs. CRC-SD) may reflect the higher MSI-H proportion in CRC-DD (which carries a better prognosis and checkpoint inhibitor sensitivity). The absence of a pre-existing second driver in MSS BRAF V600E CRC is clinically important: it validates that BRAF inhibitor combinations are appropriate in MSS BRAF V600E CRC without co-existing RAS mutation. Secondary RAS mutations that emerge after BRAF inhibitor treatment in BRAF V600E CRC represent an acquired resistance mechanism, not a pre-existing dual driver.

## Open Questions

- Do dual-driver MSS CRC cases respond to EGFR antibodies (cetuximab/panitumumab)? Are both drivers equally predictive of resistance, or is one sufficient to confer resistance?
- What is the mechanism of co-selection of two MAPK pathway drivers in the same cell in MSS CRC — are there specific genomic contexts (e.g., mismatch repair proficiency with high mutation burden from tobacco/age) that predispose?
- Can circulating tumor DNA reliably detect both subclonal and clonal dual drivers for therapy selection?
- Would checkpoint inhibitor therapy (PD-1/L1) be particularly effective in MSI-H CRC-DD?
- What is the prevalence of dual MAPK drivers in other cancer types with high MAPK mutation rates (e.g., pancreatic, lung, melanoma)?
- Does the dose-dependent signaling model (lower AI + dual drivers) predict differential sensitivity to MEK inhibitors vs. RAS-specific inhibitors?

## Limitations

- CRC-DD cohort small (n=47); power is limited for multivariate survival analysis and subgroup comparisons
- SCG performed on only 4 cases; co-occurrence rates vary dramatically (14–95%) and may not be representative of the full CRC-DD population
- SCG requires fresh-frozen tissue (not FFPE), limiting applicability to routine clinical samples
- Comparator CRC-SD cohort (n=271) is from a historical curated publication, not a contemporaneous matched cohort from the same institutional dataset
- Allelic imbalance analysis excludes samples with low tumor purity (4 excluded), potentially biasing estimates
- Tumor evolution inference with infSCITE assumes an infinite sites model, which may not hold in MSI-H tumors with hypermutation
- Liquid biopsy data limited to case descriptions; systematic ctDNA sensitivity analysis not presented
