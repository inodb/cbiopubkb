## Summary
This study performed whole exome sequencing of 24 adenoid cystic carcinoma (ACC) tumors, revealing a low somatic mutation rate (mean 13/exome), chromatin remodeling gene disruption in 50% of cases, and activating *FGFR2* mutations in a subset — pointing to a potential therapeutic target. A novel cancer gene, *SPEN* (a NOTCH signaling repressor), was identified with truncating mutations enriched in the poor-prognosis solid histology subtype, making this dataset foundational for ACC genomics in cBioPortal.

## Dataset
- **Cohort**: 24 pre-treatment ACC tumors (23 primary salivary gland, 1 locoregional lymph node metastasis) with matched normal salivary gland parenchyma; 19/24 scored MYB-activation positive; mixture of cribriform and solid histological subtypes
- **Assay**: Whole exome sequencing (solution-phase capture + next-generation sequencing); Affymetrix SNP6.0 arrays for copy number; expression arrays for transcriptomics; FGFR2 and SPEN validated by Sanger sequencing in additional cases (42 and 25 cases respectively)
- **N**: 24 (exome discovery); 42 additional for SPEN validation; 25 additional for FGFR2 validation
- **cBioPortal study ID(s)**: acyc_sanger_2013

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: Low somatic mutation rate compared to other solid tumors
- **Detail**: 312 somatic mutations identified across 24 exomes; mean of 13 mutations per exome (range 2–35). No statistically significant difference in mutation count by histological subtype (P=0.34) or MYB status (P=0.28). Mutation types: 182 missense, 59 silent, 14 nonsense, 45 frameshift, 6 in-frame indels, 6 splice-site.
- **Genes**: (genome-wide; no single driver)
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: mutation

### Finding 2: MYB-NFIB fusion as the dominant oncogenic driver
- **Detail**: 19/24 cases (79%) scored positive for MYB activation via fusion transcript sequencing, qRT-PCR, FISH, and 3′ RACE. The recurrent t(6;9)(q22-23;p23-24) translocation fuses *MYB* to *NFIB*. SNP6.0 arrays confirmed breakpoints and copy number changes at *MYB* and *NFIB* loci in most MYB-positive cases.
- **Genes**: MYB, NFIB
- **Data availability**: partial
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: fusion, copy_number

### Finding 3: Chromatin remodeling gene mutations in 50% of cases
- **Detail**: 12/24 cases (50%) harbored mutations in genes directly involved in chromatin biology. Affected genes included SWI/SNF complex members (ARID1A, ARID1B, SMARCA2), histone acetyltransferases (CREBBP, EP300), histone methylases/demethylases (MLL3, KDM6A, KDM5A), a chromodomain helicase (CHD2), a bromodomain protein (BRD2), and an ARID-domain member (ARID5B). CREBBP p.R1446C (KAT11 domain) recurs at a conserved residue consistent with oncogenic function.
- **Genes**: ARID1A, ARID1B, SMARCA2, CREBBP, EP300, MLL3, KDM6A, KDM5A, CHD2, BRD2, ARID5B
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: mutation, pathway

### Finding 4: SPEN as a novel ACC cancer gene enriched in solid histology
- **Detail**: 6 truncating mutations in *SPEN* (chromosome 1p36 NOTCH signaling repressor) identified in 5/24 exome cases, all of solid histology (poor prognosis subtype). Sanger sequencing in 42 additional cases found 2 more truncating mutations (p.R1403*, p.Q3355*), both solid histology. LOH detected in 3/5 discovery cases. SPEN encodes an RBP-J co-repressor; truncating mutations all occur upstream of the SPOC domain required for transcriptional repression.
- **Genes**: SPEN
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: mutation, clinical, survival

### Finding 5: NOTCH1/2 mutations implicate NOTCH pathway deregulation
- **Detail**: *NOTCH1* mutated in 2 cases (p.F1702S missense; p.Y550fs*81 frameshift); *NOTCH2* had 2 truncating mutations (p.Q2308fs*5, p.E2420*) in 1 case. Functional direction (activating vs. tumor-suppressive) is ambiguous and context-dependent. Combined with SPEN mutations, NOTCH pathway alterations affect a substantial subset of ACC.
- **Genes**: NOTCH1, NOTCH2, SPEN
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: mutation, pathway

### Finding 6: Activating FGFR2 mutations as a therapeutic target
- **Detail**: 3 likely activating *FGFR2* mutations identified: p.Y376C (identical to Beare-Stevenson syndrome activating germline mutation; reported somatically in endometrial/ovarian cancer), p.I389_V393>M (transmembrane domain in-frame deletion), and p.K642R (Pfeiffer syndrome kinase domain mutation, found in 1/25 validation cases). p.Y376C causes constitutive receptor dimerization via unpaired cysteine. FGFR inhibitor Dovitinib was under clinical trial in ACC at time of publication.
- **Genes**: FGFR2
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: mutation, pathway

### Finding 7: Recurrent copy number losses and known cancer gene mutations
- **Detail**: Recurrent losses of 1p36, 6q, 9p, and 12q identified by SNP6.0 arrays. *CDKN2A* truncating frameshift in 1 case plus LOH at the *CDKN2A* locus in 3 additional cases (not attributable to *NFIB* fusion). Additional mutations: *PIK3CA* p.H1047L (canonical activating), *ATM* p.R337C (likely oncogenic — recurrent across CRC and CLL), *SF3B1* (splicing factor), *SUFU*, *TSC1*, *CYLD* (truncating, tumor suppressor syndromes).
- **Genes**: CDKN2A, PIK3CA, ATM, SF3B1, SUFU, TSC1, CYLD
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: mutation, copy_number

## Open Questions
- Do *FGFR2*-mutant ACC patients respond to FGFR inhibitors (e.g., Dovitinib), and can cBioPortal data cross-reference mutation status with clinical outcomes?
- Are chromatin remodeling gene mutations (ARID1A/ARID1B/CREBBP/SMARCA2) mutually exclusive or co-occurring, and do they define a molecular subtype with distinct survival?
- Does *SPEN* mutation status (enriched in solid histology) correlate with *MYB* fusion status or NOTCH pathway co-alteration in expanded ACC cohorts?
- What is the overlap between ACC genomic alterations and those in breast cancer, where MYB-NFIB fusions also occur?

## Limitations
- Small discovery cohort (N=24); while SPEN and FGFR2 were validated in additional cases, statistical power for rare mutations is limited
- Predominantly primary salivary gland tumors; generalizability to ACC at other anatomical sites (trachea, lacrimal gland, breast) is uncertain
- Functional direction (oncogenic vs. tumor-suppressive) of NOTCH1/2 mutations in ACC could not be determined from mutational pattern alone
- No matched longitudinal or metastatic samples beyond one lymph node metastasis, limiting understanding of clonal evolution
- Expression array and qRT-PCR data deposited separately (ArrayExpress); may not be directly linked in cBioPortal study
