## Summary

This paper examines 1,360 consecutive PDAC patients undergoing surgical resection at MSK (2004–2019) and 397 with MSK-IMPACT genomic profiling to characterize allele-specific differences between KRAS mutants. The central finding is that KRAS G12R mutations are enriched in early-stage (stage I) PDAC due to increased node-negativity rather than smaller tumor size — and associate with decreased distant recurrence and improved overall survival compared to KRAS G12D. Complementary bulk RNA-seq (n=100, COMPASS cohort), spatial molecular imaging (n=20 patients, CosMx), and isogenic mouse PDAC organoids (Kras^G12R; p53^KO vs. Kras^G12D; p53^KO) demonstrate that KRAS G12D features enhanced oncogenic signaling, EMT, and proliferative programs, while KRAS G12R shows increased NF-κB/TNF signaling and reduced migratory potential. These findings establish mutation-specific PDAC biology with direct prognostic and therapeutic implications. Full MSK dataset is publicly available in `pancreas_msk_2024`.

## Key Findings

### F1: KRAS G12R is enriched in early-stage PDAC through increased node-negativity, not smaller tumor size

**Finding:** KRAS G12R mutations are enriched in stage I PDAC (23% of G12R patients vs. 11% of G12D patients, p=0.022). This enrichment is driven by increased node-negativity (47% N0 in G12R vs. 26% in G12D, p=0.019) rather than smaller tumor size — T-stage and tumor diameter did not significantly differ between G12R and G12D.

**Detail:** 1,360 resected patients; 397 with IMPACT sequencing (103 early-stage, 294 late-stage). KRAS G12R enrichment in early AJCC stage persists when stratifying by upfront resection vs. neoadjuvant chemotherapy cohorts (trends, not always significant in subgroups). KRAS G12R patients also more frequently received neoadjuvant chemotherapy (32.7% vs. 17.9% for G12D, p=0.094), and among patients receiving NAC, G12R enrichment suggests higher response rate (not enriched in overall NAC population). KRAS G12R patients more frequently report family history of pancreatic cancer (16.4% vs. 4.2%, p=0.003). KRAS G12D and G12V not enriched in early or late stage.

### F2: KRAS G12R associates with decreased distant recurrence and improved OS; KRAS G12D with worst outcomes across multiple cohorts

**Finding:** KRAS G12R patients have lower 2-year cumulative incidence of distant recurrence (24.2% vs. 43.2% for G12D, p=0.017) and improved OS (median 39 vs. 30 months). However, local recurrence is increased in G12R (2-year CIF 20.6% vs. 10.6% for G12D, p=0.045). In external validation (n=855, pooled), KRAS G12D has worst median OS (15.6 months vs. G12R 20.8, G12V 22.4, WT 27 months, p=0.006). G12R (HR 0.77, p=0.038) and G12V (HR 0.71, p=0.02) are independent predictors of improved OS in multivariate analysis.

**Detail:** TP53 mutations associated with worse OS (median 33.7 vs. 43.6 months, p=0.014). Number of co-mutated tumor suppressors inversely correlates with OS: 0 suppressors → 28.2 months; 1 → 19.6; 2–3 → 17 months (p=0.013). KRAS G12R tumors have higher CDKN2A mutation frequency (40% vs. 22.1% for G12D, p=0.046) and more frequently harbor ≥2 tumor suppressor co-mutations (51% vs. 30%, p=0.029). KRAS G12V improves OS without the early-stage enrichment of G12R — distinct biological mechanism. Rare KRAS variants ('other') have best OS (47 months, internal; external validated).

### F3: KRAS G12D tumors show enhanced EMT and oncogenic KRAS signaling; KRAS G12R shows NF-κB/TNF enrichment and reduced mesenchymal features

**Finding:** Bulk RNA-seq GSEA (n=100, COMPASS cohort) shows KRAS G12D enrichment for EMT, mTORC1 signaling, E2F, and G2M hallmarks; KRAS G12R enrichment for 'KRAS signaling down' and 'TNFα signaling via NF-κB'. Spatial protein profiling confirms: G12D enriched for vimentin, fibronectin (EMT/mesenchymal); G12R enriched for cytokeratin, NF-κB (p65), CD31 (epithelial, inflammatory).

**Detail:** Spatial molecular imaging with CosMx on 14 PDAC TMAs (7 G12R, 7 G12D) plus 6 normal pancreata (~400 FOVs). Cell typing revealed similar overall cell-type composition (stroma, immune infiltrates) between G12D and G12R, but niche analysis (neighborhood composition) showed niche 3 (T-cell-rich, tumor-absent) and niche 8 more abundant in G12D; niche 2 (T-cells + tumor co-present) more abundant in G12R. G12R depleted for CD45, CD3, CD8, CD15 immune markers in spatial analysis despite higher TIL-tumor co-localization in niche 2. Transcriptionally, G12D enriched in PDAC-specific MYC/KRAS signatures vs. G12R; no difference in KRAS copy number or molecular subtype (Collisson, Moffitt, Bailey classifications) between alleles.

### F4: Mouse KRAS G12R; p53 KO PDAC organoids recapitulate reduced migration and improved orthotopic survival relative to G12D

**Finding:** Isogenic Kras^G12R; Trp53^KO and Kras^G12D; Trp53^KO PDAC organoids (derived from LSL-Kras knock-in mice) show identical growth rates and proliferation but robust reduction in migration for G12R in wound-healing assays. Orthotopic transplantation into immunocompetent B6 mice: Kras^G12R median OS 90 days vs. 44 days for G12D (p<0.001).

**Detail:** Organoids were previously validated for comparable RAS-GTP levels and downstream phospho-Erk and phospho-Akt signaling. Despite equivalent signaling levels, Kras^G12R organoids show ~50% reduced migration. RNA-seq of organoids confirms same hallmark enrichment pattern as human tumors: G12D enriched for mutant KRAS PDAC signatures and MYC activation; G12R depleted. Orthotopic survival difference (90 vs. 44 days) closely mirrors human OS difference (39 vs. 30 months for G12R vs. G12D). Importantly, Kras^G12R is known to be deficient in PI3K-p110α engagement and macropinocytosis relative to G12D — mechanistic defect validated here at functional level.

### F5: Tumor suppressor co-mutation burden modulates PDAC outcomes independent of KRAS allele; KRAS status warrants routine clinical profiling

**Finding:** The number of co-mutated tumor suppressors (TP53, CDKN2A, SMAD4) is an independent predictor of OS: 0 co-mutations → best survival; each additional suppressor associated with worse outcome. KRAS G12R shows excess tumor suppressor co-mutations (51% ≥2 suppressors vs. 30% for G12D, p=0.029), yet still has improved OS — suggesting G12R's intrinsic attenuated oncogenicity requires additional suppressor loss to drive malignancy, but even with those losses, the tumor is less aggressive than G12D.

**Detail:** In multivariate analysis stratified by data source (external pooled cohorts), KRAS G12R (HR 0.77, p=0.038), KRAS G12V (HR 0.71, p=0.02), and absence of tumor suppressor co-mutation (HR 0.74, p=0.02) were all independent OS predictors. No interaction between KRAS allele and neoadjuvant chemotherapy on OS (p=0.439). Within early-stage patients, those receiving NAC had worse RFS than those with upfront resection (HR 1.49, p<0.05), consistent with NAC being used in borderline-resectable disease. Authors advocate for routine KRAS mutational profiling in all PDAC patients to guide prognosis and potentially treatment selection.

## Open Questions

- Why does KRAS G12V improve OS without early-stage enrichment or reduced distant recurrence — is there a distinct biological mechanism (e.g., differential effector engagement or metabolic dependency) compared to G12R?
- Does KRAS G12R's reduced PI3K-p110α engagement make G12R PDAC patients better responders to PI3K inhibitors, or more dependent on alternative survival pathways?
- Are the enhanced local recurrence rates in G12R patients (due to inadequate distant dissemination) clinically addressable through intensified local treatment (extended resection, IORT)?
- Can KRAS allele-specific PDAC biology inform selection of novel KRAS inhibitors (e.g., G12D-specific MRTX1133, G12R-specific vs. pan-KRAS inhibitors) in upcoming trials?
- Does the family history enrichment in KRAS G12R PDAC patients suggest a germline predisposition context warranting enhanced genetic counseling?

## Limitations

- Cohort restricted to surgically resected (stage I–III) PDAC; findings may not generalize to unresectable or metastatic stage IV disease.
- Spatial profiling (CosMx) performed on small patient numbers (7 G12R, 7 G12D); one G12R patient excluded, reducing power for spatial niche comparisons.
- RNA-seq cohort (n=100, COMPASS) includes only resected patients from a specific trial; transcriptomic findings may not represent the full KRAS G12R biology spectrum.
- KRAS G12R organoid lines limited; mechanistic conclusions (PI3K engagement, migration) are models of the human disease rather than definitive proof.
- Co-mutation burden analysis (TP53, CDKN2A, SMAD4) based on targeted sequencing; copy-number deletion of CDKN2A underrepresented on IMPACT panel.
- KRAS allele × treatment interaction analysis was underpowered; whether KRAS G12R patients differentially benefit from specific chemotherapy regimens (FOLFIRINOX vs. gemcitabine/nab-paclitaxel) was not addressed.
