## Summary

This study establishes the first comprehensive multiplatform molecular landscape of schwannomatosis-related schwannomas (SWNTS-SWNs) in 165 tumors from 72 patients, integrating DNA methylation (EPIC array), whole-exome sequencing (WES, n=29), whole-genome sequencing (WGS, n=22), RNA-seq (n=24), and structural variant analysis. Compared to histologically identical non-syndromic sporadic schwannomas (NS-SWNs), SWNTS-SWNs show global hypomethylation, four distinct anatomically-associated DNA methylation subgroups, elevated 22q deletions, higher NF2 somatic mutation rates, and a recurrent *SH3PXD2A-HTRA1* gene fusion. Upregulated transcriptional programs (PIGF, VEGF, MEK, ERBB2, SHH) and RAS/MAPK pathway activation in *LZTR1*-mutant tumors suggest therapeutic targets. Painful tumors are associated with distinct immune and transcriptional profiles.

## Key Findings

### F1: Four distinct DNA methylation subgroups of SWNTS-SWNs defined by anatomic location and transcriptional programs

**Finding:** Consensus clustering of SWNTS-SWNs identifies four stable DNA methylation subgroups strongly associated with tumor anatomic location (extremity vs. spinal vs. truncal vs. head/neck) and linked to distinct oncogenic transcriptional programs.

**Detail:** tSNE and unsupervised hierarchical clustering of top 10,000 variably methylated CpGs showed SWNTS-SWNs from extremities segregate from other sites. Consensus clustering (top 2,000 most variable CpGs) yielded four clusters associated with upregulation of cAMP, KRAS, MEK/PIGF, and PIGF/VEGF pathways, respectively. Multiple tumors from the same individual distributed across clusters, indicating that distinct Schwann cell-of-origin populations from different body regions drive methylation subtype. SWNTS-SWNs did not separate from NS-SWNs in unsupervised clustering, suggesting a shared cell of origin; however, 75% of differentially methylated CpGs (supervised analysis) were hypomethylated in SWNTS-SWNs relative to NS-SWNs, predominantly at CpG islands and promoters.

**Genes / features:** `TET1`, `TET2`, `DNMT1`, `DNMT3A`, `L1TD1` (LINE-1); methylation clusters linked to `KRAS`, `VEGF`, `PIGF`, `MEK` pathways

**Data availability:** `partial`
> DNA methylation data deposited with the `schw_ctf_synodos_2025` study; cBioPortal does not natively host EPIC array methylation beta values, so direct methylation subgroup analysis requires external tools. Copy number segments derived from methylation data are available.

**Tool support:** `custom`
> Methylation-based subgroup assignment requires R/minfi or similar bisulfite array analysis pipelines. Cluster annotations, if deposited as clinical attributes, could be queried natively via Study View.

### F2: SWNTS-SWNs are globally hypomethylated relative to NS-SWNs, with LINE-1 and promoter hypomethylation

**Finding:** Global DNA hypomethylation, evidenced by LINE-1 repetitive element hypomethylation and upregulated TET demethylation enzymes, distinguishes SWNTS-SWNs from sporadic NS-SWNs.

**Detail:** LINE-1 elements were significantly hypomethylated in SWNTS-SWNs vs NS-SWNs (Student's t-test, p=0.0005), concordant with higher transcription of L1TD1. TET1 and TET2 mRNA expression was elevated in SWNTS-SWNs. Supervised analysis identified 75% of differentially methylated CpGs as hypomethylated in SWNTS-SWNs, enriched at CpG islands and promoters. MSH6 and MLH1 were hypomethylated in SWNTS-SWNs, potentially explaining higher MMR gene expression. LZTR1-mutant tumors showed additional local hypomethylation but not global LINE-1 hypomethylation.

**Genes / features:** `TET1`, `TET2`, `MSH6`, `MLH1`, LINE-1

**Data availability:** `partial`
> Gene expression (RNA-seq) for TET1/TET2 and MMR genes available in schw_ctf_synodos_2025 if RNA data is deposited. Methylation beta values themselves not in cBioPortal natively.

**Tool support:** `api`
> Expression comparisons between SWNTS and NS-SWN samples (if loaded with clinical group labels) can be queried via the cBioPortal REST API. Native expression distribution plots and violin plots available if RNA data is in study.

**Concepts:** #methylation #epigenetics #LINE-1 #TET #hypomethylation

### F3: NF2 is the only recurrent somatic driver; significantly more frequent in SWNTS-SWNs (83%) than NS-SWNs (58%)

**Finding:** Somatic *NF2* mutations or copy number loss were detected in 83% of SWNTS-SWNs versus 58% of NS-SWNs. All *LZTR1* germline-mutant SWNTS-SWNs harbored NF2 alterations. The tumor mutation burden (TMB) is low in both tumor types (~0.17–0.23 Mb⁻¹ by WES).

**Detail:** WES (n=29) and WGS (n=22) confirmed NF2 as the sole recurrent somatic driver across all SWN subtypes. Chi-square test showed significantly higher NF2 alteration rate in SWNTS-SWNs (83% vs 58%, p<0.0001). Deep deletions encompassing NF2, LZTR1, and SMARCB1 on 22q were detected in 76% of SWNTS-SWNs vs 35% of NS-SWNs (p<0.01). TMB in SWNTS-SWNs: WES=0.17/Mb (5.92 mutations/sample), WGS=0.23/Mb. Dominant COSMIC mutation signatures: Signature 1A (MUTYH, 26/29 samples), signatures 6 and 15 (MMR-related, 7/29 and 4/29), Signature 2 (APOBEC, 3/29 and absent in NS-SWNs). No microsatellite instability at 22q microsatellites.

**Genes / features:** `NF2`, `LZTR1`, `SMARCB1`; COSMIC Signatures 1A, 6, 15, 2

**Data availability:** `available`
> NF2 somatic mutations and 22q copy number alterations available in `schw_ctf_synodos_2025`. Germline LZTR1/SMARCB1 status may be captured as clinical attributes.

**Tool support:** `native`
> OncoPrint and Mutations tab in cBioPortal display NF2 alteration frequency and type. Copy number visualization available natively. Germline vs. somatic distinction requires clinical metadata filtering.

**Concepts:** #NF2 #SMARCB1 #LZTR1 #22q #driver-context #hotspots #TMB

### F4: SH3PXD2A-HTRA1 gene fusion detected in 14% of SWNTS-SWNs, enriched in LZTR1-mutant tumors

**Finding:** The *SH3PXD2A-HTRA1* gene fusion, previously characterized in NS-SWNs (~10%), is also present in 14% of SWNTS-SWNs and strongly associated with germline *LZTR1* mutations (68% of fusion-positive cases, Chi-square p=0.025).

**Detail:** RNA-seq fusions were detected using FusionCatcher and validated by RT-PCR and Sanger sequencing. The fusion showed male predominance (14/22 = 63%). This fusion activates MEK signaling and has direct therapeutic implications: MEK inhibitors have been proposed based on preclinical work in NS-SWNs with this fusion. Additional novel fusions enriched in SWNTS-SWNs include NAIP1-OCLN on chromosome 5 (67% vs 29% in NS-SWNs, p<0.01). KANSARL fusion (KANSL1-ARL17A on 17q21.31) was found at similar rates in both tumor types.

**Genes / features:** `SH3PXD2A`, `HTRA1`, `LZTR1`, `NAIP1`, `OCLN`, `KANSL1`, `ARL17A`

**Data availability:** `partial`
> Fusion detection requires RNA-seq data; if RNA-seq is deposited in `schw_ctf_synodos_2025`, fusion calls may be queryable. SV calls from WGS may partially capture the fusion at DNA level.

**Tool support:** `api`
> Fusion/structural variant data, if deposited, accessible via cBioPortal Fusions tab or REST API. Full characterization of fusion prevalence by germline status requires clinical metadata join.

**Concepts:** #fusion-oncogenes #MEK #LZTR1 #nerve-sheath #targetable-fusion

### F5: SWNTS-SWNs overexpress PIGF, VEGF, MEK, ERBB2, and SHH pathways; LZTR1-mutant tumors specifically activate RAS/MAPK

**Finding:** Transcriptome profiling shows SWNTS-SWNs have a distinct gene expression profile from NS-SWNs, with upregulation of PIGF, VEGF, MEK, ERBB2, and SHH oncogenic pathways, and downregulation of RB. In LZTR1-mutant tumors, RAS/MAPK is specifically elevated, supporting MEK inhibitor sensitivity.

**Detail:** Consensus clustering of top 2,500 DEGs (SWNTS n=18, NS n=41) placed SWNTS-SWNs in one cluster while NS-SWNs formed two. GSEA C6 analysis identified PIGF, VEGF, MEK, ERBB2, SHH as top upregulated pathways (p<0.0001). RB was the top downregulated pathway. In LZTR1-mutant vs. LZTR1-wild-type SWNTS-SWNs, RAS/MAPK was elevated, consistent with LZTR1's role in RAS ubiquitination/degradation. NF2-mutant SWNTS-SWNs also showed MAPK pathway upregulation via the Hippo pathway. LZTR1 and SMARCB1 mRNA were significantly lower in SWNTS-SWNs, consistent with 22q deletion (q<0.001).

**Genes / features:** `LZTR1`, `NF2`, `SMARCB1`, `VEGF`, `PIGF`, `MEK` pathway, `ERBB2`, `SHH`, `RB1`

**Data availability:** `partial`
> RNA-seq expression data for pathway component genes should be in `schw_ctf_synodos_2025` if RNA data is deposited. GSEA analysis itself requires external tools.

**Tool support:** `api`
> Per-gene expression comparisons by LZTR1 status and NF2 mutation status available via API if clinical attributes are annotated. Pathway-level GSEA requires external analysis of downloaded expression matrix.

### F6: Immune microenvironment of SWNTS-SWNs differs from NS-SWNs; NF2-mutant tumors have more M2 macrophages; mast cells elevated in painful tumors

**Finding:** SWNTS-SWNs have a distinct immune infiltrate with higher naïve B cells, plasma cells, and activated NK cells but lower total macrophages and CD8+ T cells vs NS-SWNs. NF2-mutant SWNTS-SWNs have more M2 macrophages. Painful tumors show elevated mast cells. HLA-A/B/C and HLA-DPB1 expression is decreased in SWNTS-SWNs.

**Detail:** CIBERSORT deconvolution of RNA-seq data (SWNTS n=24, NS n=41) identified statistically significant differences (Student's t-test, p<0.05). NF2-mutant SWNTS-SWNs had significantly more M2 macrophages vs NF2-wild-type (p<0.01), consistent with NF2 models showing M2-polarized macrophages as pro-tumorigenic in schwannomas. Painful SWNTS-SWNs showed elevated mast cell proportions vs non-painful tumors. HLA class I (A, B, C) and HLA-DPB1 expression was decreased, while T cell stimulatory genes CD28 and CD80 were upregulated.

**Genes / features:** `NF2`, `HLA-A`, `HLA-B`, `HLA-C`, `HLA-DPB1`, `CD28`, `CD80`

**Data availability:** `not-available`
> Immune cell deconvolution results are derived from RNA-seq using CIBERSORT and are not directly stored as a cBioPortal data type. HLA expression data from bulk RNA-seq is a proxy; single-cell resolution unavailable in this study.

**Tool support:** `custom`
> Immune deconvolution requires downloading RNA-seq data and running CIBERSORT/CIBERSORTx externally. Gene-level HLA expression could be queried via the cBioPortal API if expression data is deposited.

## Open Questions

- Do the four DNA methylation subgroups of SWNTS-SWNs correspond to distinct Schwann cell developmental subtypes (sensory vs. motor vs. autonomic), or purely to regional anatomic differences?
- Can MEK inhibitors achieve therapeutic benefit in LZTR1-mutant SWNTS-SWNs with confirmed RAS/MAPK pathway upregulation and SH3PXD2A-HTRA1 fusions, similar to efficacy signals in NF2-related tumors?
- What drives pathogenesis in the 9 (31%) SWNTS-SWNs with only two hits (22q deletion, no NF2 mutation)? Are there other 22q tumor suppressor genes (MAPK1, EWSR1, PATZ1, ZNRF3, MYH9) that substitute for NF2?
- Is the increased M2 macrophage polarization in NF2-mutant SWNTS-SWNs therapeutically targetable (e.g., CSF1R inhibitors)?
- What is the molecular basis of pain in schwannomatosis — can the mast cell and PIGF/VEGF transcriptional signature serve as a biomarker for pain severity or response to anti-VEGF therapy?

## Limitations

- Cohort from multiple institutions but relatively small n for WES (29) and WGS (22); RNA-seq on subset only (n=24)
- Limited longitudinal data; cannot assess temporal evolution of methylation subgroups or clonal dynamics
- Germline characterization limited to NF2, SMARCB1, and LZTR1; other potential germline drivers not systematically screened
- CIBERSORT-based immune deconvolution from bulk RNA-seq cannot resolve spatial or single-cell immune heterogeneity
- Functional validation of novel somatic alterations (MYPN, CAV3, SMOX) and recurrent SVs (ZNF708-ZNF138) not performed
- Pain assessment was patient-reported; pain could not be precisely linked to individual tumor molecular features in patients with multiple tumors
