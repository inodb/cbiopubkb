## Summary

The TCGA Pan-Cancer Clinical Data Resource (TCGA-CDR) is a curated, standardized clinical annotation dataset for 11,160 TCGA patients across 33 cancer types. The authors processed 33 enrollment files and 97 follow-up files to derive four harmonized clinical outcome endpoints — Overall Survival (OS), Progression-Free Interval (PFI), Disease-Free Interval (DFI), and Disease-Specific Survival (DSS) — and provide per-cancer-type recommendations for their appropriate use in genomic survival analyses.

## Key Findings

### F1: Four harmonized clinical endpoints defined for TCGA Pan-Cancer
**Finding:** The TCGA-CDR defines four clinical outcome endpoints — OS, PFI, DFI, and DSS — derived from integrated enrollment and follow-up clinical records for 11,160 patients across 33 cancer types.
**Detail:** OS is the most accurately derived endpoint; however, it is appropriate only for aggressive cancers (e.g., GBM, where median OS event time was 12.6 months and 82.4% of cases reached the endpoint). For indolent cancers such as PRAD, OS and DSS are not recommended; PFI and DFI are preferred.
**Genes / cancer types:** Pan-cancer; GBM, OV, BRCA, PRAD highlighted.
**Data availability:** available > All TCGA Pan-Cancer Atlas 2018 studies in cBioPortal contain these clinical annotations.
**Tool support:** native > Study View survival plots in each `*_tcga_pan_can_atlas_2018` study; Kaplan-Meier curves for OS/PFI/DFI/DSS.
**Concepts:** #pan-cancer-clinical #survival #pan-cancer-map

### F2: Cancer-type-specific endpoint recommendations
**Finding:** Endpoint suitability varies by cancer aggressiveness and follow-up duration. OS is appropriate for GBM, OV, MESO; PFI and DFI are preferred for BRCA, UCEC, and other slower-progressing cancers; OS and DSS are not recommended for PRAD.
**Detail:** For UCEC and OV, all four endpoints are recommended. For BRCA, PFI and DFI are recommended but OS depends on subtype (appropriate for basal-like, not Luminal A). The TCGA-CDR table provides explicit recommendations for each of the 33 cancer types.
**Genes / cancer types:** BRCA (ER+/Luminal A vs. basal-like), PRAD, GBM, UCEC, OV.
**Data availability:** available > Endpoint recommendation table (Table S3) is published; clinical data are in cBioPortal per-cancer studies.
**Tool support:** native > Survival tab in Study View for each TCGA Pan-Cancer Atlas study.
**Concepts:** #pan-cancer-clinical #survival #molecular-subtypes #biomarker

### F3: Consistency with independent cancer genomics datasets
**Finding:** TCGA-CDR survival outcome patterns are consistent with results from independent cancer genomics cohorts, validating that the curated endpoints can serve as reliable ground truth for genomic biomarker studies.
**Detail:** Survival curve shapes and cancer-type prognosis from TCGA-CDR agree with established benchmark survival rates (e.g., 5-year rates for OV ~40%, GBM ~5%), demonstrating translational validity.
**Genes / cancer types:** Pan-cancer.
**Data availability:** available > TCGA Pan-Cancer Atlas studies in cBioPortal.
**Tool support:** api > Pull clinical and genomic data via cBioPortal REST API for cross-cohort comparison.
**Concepts:** #pan-cancer-clinical #survival #biomarker

### F4: Short follow-up is a key limitation of the original TCGA clinical data
**Finding:** The original TCGA program had neither comprehensive clinical outcome collection as a primary objective nor sufficient follow-up time for indolent cancer types, leading to low event rates in some cohorts and unreliable OS estimates for slow-growing tumors.
**Detail:** For PRAD, few deaths from prostate cancer were recorded during follow-up, making OS unreliable. For BRCA Luminal A, which may take >10 years to recur, the relatively short TCGA follow-up is insufficient for OS analysis.
**Genes / cancer types:** PRAD, BRCA.
**Data availability:** partial > Clinical annotations are available in cBioPortal but short follow-up limits survival analyses for indolent tumors.
**Tool support:** api > Retrieve clinical data with endpoint flags from cBioPortal API; filter by recommended endpoints per cancer type.
**Concepts:** #pan-cancer-clinical #survival #pan-cancer-map

### F5: TCGA-CDR enables curated genomic-clinical correlations at unprecedented scale
**Finding:** By integrating 10 clinical data elements from enrollment and follow-up files and deriving standardized endpoints, the TCGA-CDR enables high-quality genomic-survival correlation studies across 33 cancer types in a single framework.
**Detail:** The resource includes 11 additional clinical features (age, stage, grade, race, etc.) and case redaction flags. It is publicly available to support pan-cancer survival analyses linking molecular subtypes, mutations, and copy-number events to outcomes.
**Genes / cancer types:** Pan-cancer (33 types).
**Data availability:** available > All TCGA Pan-Cancer Atlas 2018 studies in cBioPortal include standardized clinical annotations from TCGA-CDR.
**Tool support:** native > OncoPrint + Survival in cBioPortal; api > cBioPortal clinical data endpoint; custom > Kaplan-Meier analyses using TCGA-CDR supplementary table.
**Concepts:** #pan-cancer-clinical #survival #pan-cancer-map #biomarker #molecular-subtypes

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Can updated TCGA follow-up data (via GDC) substantially extend survival analyses for indolent cancer types like PRAD and BRCA Luminal A?
- Do the recommended endpoints remain optimal when integrating treatment data (e.g., neoadjuvant therapy, adjuvant immunotherapy)?
- Can machine-learning models trained on TCGA-CDR endpoints improve cross-cancer-type prognostic models?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Short follow-up time for many TCGA cohorts limits event rates, especially for indolent cancers.
- Treatment data were incompletely collected, precluding treatment-adjusted survival analyses.
- DSS is provided as an approximation only (not adjudicated cause of death).
- Primary tumors were predominantly collected (not metastatic), except SKCM.
- Clinical data were collected under time and logistical constraints that limited completeness.
