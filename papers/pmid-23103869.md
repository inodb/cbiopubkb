## Summary

A prospective clinical cohort study performing exome sequencing and copy-number analysis on 142 patients with early-stage (stages I-II) pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC), conducted by the Australian Pancreatic Cancer Genome Initiative (APGI) as part of the International Cancer Genome Consortium (ICGC). Analysis of 99 informative tumors (>20% cellularity or ≥10 validated somatic mutations) identified 2,016 non-silent mutations and 1,628 copy-number variations. The study reaffirms core PDAC drivers (*KRAS*, *TP53*, *CDKN2A*, *SMAD4*) in a large prospective clinical cohort, discovers novel significantly mutated genes in chromatin modification (*EPC1*, *ARID2*) and DNA damage repair (*ATM*), and makes the landmark finding that axon guidance pathway genes — especially SLIT/ROBO signaling — are frequently aberrant in PDAC, implicating a novel developmental signaling axis in pancreatic carcinogenesis.

## Key Findings

### F1: 16 significantly mutated genes define the PDAC landscape; four core drivers (KRAS, TP53, CDKN2A, SMAD4) are reaffirmed with population-level frequencies in a prospective clinical cohort

**Finding:** MutSigCV analysis identifies 16 significantly mutated genes across the 99-sample informative cohort, confirming the four canonical PDAC drivers and revealing 12 additional significantly or recurrently mutated genes. This is the first large-scale prospective early-stage PDAC cohort with exome sequencing, providing population-representative mutation frequencies unbiased by cell line or xenograft selection.

**Detail:** 2,016 non-silent somatic mutations (mean 26 per patient, range 1–116), consistent with expected sensitivity given tumor cellularity. Core drivers: *KRAS* (>90%), *TP53*, *CDKN2A*, *SMAD4*, *MLL3*, *TGFBR2*, *ARID1A*, *SF3B1* (all previously known). Novel significantly mutated genes: chromatin modification — *EPC1* (Enhancer of Polycomb 1, histone acetylation) and *ARID2* (SWI/SNF complex); DNA damage repair — *ATM* (8% of cohort: mutated 5%, LOH/loss 5%); others — *ZIM2*, *MAP2K4*, *NALCN*, *SLC16A4*, *MAGEA6*. 48% overlap with previously reported mutated genes (Biankin reference), with 186/998 previously reported mutated genes confirmed. *KRAS* mutation detected in 93% of all 142 samples at 1,000× deep sequencing.

**Genes / cancer types:** `KRAS`, `TP53`, `CDKN2A`, `SMAD4`, `MLL3`, `TGFBR2`, `ARID1A`, `SF3B1`, `EPC1`, `ARID2`, `ATM`, `MAP2K4` · Pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC)

**Data availability:** `available`

### F2: ATM is mutated in 8% of PDAC — identifying sporadic PDAC as a DNA damage repair–deficient cancer beyond BRCA-pathway association

**Finding:** *ATM* alterations (mutation in 5%, LOH/loss in 5%, with overlap) affect 8% of the prospective PDAC cohort, establishing ATM as a significant novel driver in sporadic PDAC. The pattern of mutation + LOH mirrors biallelic inactivation previously described only in familial PDAC (germline ATM mutation + somatic LOH). Functional validation via shRNA screen in 102 cancer cell lines confirmed ATM knockdown affects cell survival.

**Detail:** ATM mutations identified in 5% of the 99 informative tumors; LOH or focal loss in 5%; two patients with both mutation and LOH/loss (biallelic confirmation). The finding extends ATM's known role in familial pancreatic cancer susceptibility to common sporadic disease. ATM loss activates DNA damage response deficiency, suggesting sensitivity to platinum-based chemotherapy and potentially to PARP inhibitors — a therapeutic hypothesis with direct clinical implications given the poor prognosis of PDAC. ATM confirmed as significant insertion site in SB mutagenesis screens (*P* < 0.001 combined).

**Genes / cancer types:** `ATM` · Pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma

### F3: Axon guidance pathway genes are recurrently aberrant in PDAC — SLIT/ROBO signaling affected in ~23% of patients, with prognostic and functional significance

**Finding:** Pathway enrichment analysis of recurrently mutated genes identifies axon guidance as the most significantly enriched pathway (SLIT/ROBO: *P* = 4.67×10⁻⁸ when all mutated genes used). *SLIT2* and *ROBO2* mutations are present in 5% of patients; focal copy-number loss of *ROBO1* and *SLIT2* by GISTIC2.0 affects an additional ~15-18% — totaling ~23% with SLIT/ROBO pathway aberrations. ROBO2 low expression correlates with poor survival (*P* = 0.04); ROBO3 (inhibitor of ROBO2) high expression correlates inversely with poor survival (*P* < 0.006). Independent Sleeping Beauty transposon mutagenesis in Kras-driven mouse models identifies axon guidance genes as significantly enriched insertion sites (*P* = 1.6×10⁻³).

**Detail:** SLIT/ROBO signaling normally enhances β-catenin complex formation with E-cadherin and suppresses WNT signaling. Loss of ROBO1/2 signaling promotes β-catenin nuclear translocation (WNT activation) and augments MET signaling (relevant because SLIT/ROBO can downregulate MET downstream). This provides alternative mechanisms for WNT and MET pathway deregulation in PDAC beyond direct mutation of WNT or MET pathway components. SB transposon screens (two independent Kras-transgenic models): 14 axon guidance genes detected as significant insertion sites. Additional targeted PCR sequencing of 30 additional PDAC cases confirmed *ROBO1*, *SLIT2*, *ROBO2* mutations. Dysregulation of SLIT/ROBO and semaphorin genes is also evident in murine models of early pancreatic carcinogenesis (acinar-to-ductal metaplasia), suggesting involvement in tumor initiation.

**Genes / cancer types:** `SLIT2`, `ROBO1`, `ROBO2`, `ROBO3` · Pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma

### F4: Class 3 semaphorin signaling (SEMA3A/PLXNA1) is amplified and prognostically significant, representing a second major axon guidance pathway aberration in PDAC

**Finding:** *SEMA3A* amplification is detected in 18% of PDAC patients by GISTIC2.0, with additional 3% harboring *SEMA3A* mutations. High *SEMA3A* amplification correlates with high mRNA expression (*P* = 0.03). Both high *SEMA3A* mRNA and high *PLXNA1* (semaphorin receptor) expression are independent poor prognostic factors on multivariate analysis, with PDAC cell line data showing *SEMA3A* promotes invasiveness.

**Detail:** Class 3 semaphorins are the only secreted semaphorins in vertebrates; they signal through neuropilin and plexin receptors to regulate cell growth, invasiveness, and angiogenesis. SEMA3A and PLXNA1 expression association with poor survival was independently validated on multivariate analysis with clinicopathological variables. Cell line data show SEMA3A promotes invasiveness of PDAC. Therapeutics targeting axon guidance molecules have been developed for neurological contexts; this study provides rationale for testing in PDAC. Reciprocal regulation: SLIT/ROBO loss (pro-tumorigenic) vs. SEMA3A gain (pro-invasive) represent parallel but distinct axon guidance pathway disruptions in PDAC.

**Genes / cancer types:** `SEMA3A`, `PLXNA1`, `SEMA3E` · Pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma

### F5: Chromatin remodeling genes (EPC1, ARID2, ARID1A) are novel significantly mutated genes in PDAC, expanding the epigenetic driver landscape beyond MLL3

**Finding:** In addition to the previously known *ARID1A* (SWI/SNF complex), two additional chromatin modification genes — *EPC1* (Enhancer of Polycomb, histone acetylation) and *ARID2* (PBAF SWI/SNF complex) — are newly identified as significantly mutated in PDAC. Together with *MLL3* (histone H3K4 methylation), these findings establish chromatin remodeling disruption as a major recurrent oncogenic mechanism in PDAC alongside the canonical KRAS/TP53/CDKN2A/SMAD4 drivers. Both EPC1 and ARID2 are validated in SB mutagenesis screens.

**Detail:** ARID1A: SWI/SNF ATP-dependent chromatin remodeling; previously reported in gastric, ovarian, and other cancers; now confirmed in PDAC. ARID2: PBAF variant of SWI/SNF; novel in PDAC. EPC1: component of NuA4 histone acetyltransferase complex; novel in PDAC. MLL3: H3K4 histone methyltransferase; mixed lineage leukemia family. Together, four chromatin-modifying genes are significantly mutated, suggesting that epigenetic dysregulation via chromatin remodeling is as important as direct signaling pathway disruption in PDAC. These mutations may create synthetic lethal vulnerabilities with EZH2 inhibitors or other epigenetic therapies.

**Genes / cancer types:** `ARID1A`, `ARID2`, `EPC1`, `MLL3` · Pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma

## Open Questions

1. Are SLIT/ROBO and semaphorin pathway alterations actionable targets, given that therapeutics targeting axon guidance molecules exist in neurological contexts?
2. Does ATM loss in sporadic PDAC confer sensitivity to platinum-based chemotherapy or PARP inhibitors, analogous to BRCA1/2-deficient pancreatic cancer?
3. What is the relationship between SLIT/ROBO loss, WNT/MET pathway activation, and therapeutic resistance to MET inhibitors in PDAC?
4. Are chromatin remodeling gene (ARID1A/ARID2/EPC1/MLL3) alterations synthetic lethal with EZH2 or other epigenetic therapies in PDAC?
5. Can the axon guidance pathway findings from exome sequencing be extended to the non-coding genome (e.g., regulatory variants affecting ROBO/SLIT expression) with WGS data?

## Limitations

- Low mean tumor cellularity (38%) reduces mutation detection sensitivity (~45% estimated); many mutations are missed in stromal-contaminated PDAC specimens.
- Exome sequencing does not capture structural variants, gene fusions, or non-coding drivers — a significant limitation for a cancer type with extensive genomic rearrangements.
- mRNA expression associations (ROBO2, SEMA3A survival data) are from microarray platforms with limited resolution and no single-cell context.
- No treatment response data; study population underwent curative-intent resection for stage I-II disease, not representative of the majority of PDAC patients who present with metastatic disease.
- SB mutagenesis and shRNA screens provide only indirect functional validation; PDAC-specific in vivo models with SLIT/ROBO knockouts are needed.
