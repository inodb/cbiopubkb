## Summary

Comprehensive genomic analysis of 1,913 B-progenitor ALL cases identifies a new subtype — comprising up to 7.6% of B-ALL — defined by universal *DUX4* rearrangement to the IGH enhancer and consequent transcriptional deregulation of *ERG*. DUX4 overexpression drives expression of a novel dominant-negative ERG isoform (ERGalt) from a non-canonical first exon in *ERG* intron 6, and promotes RAG-mediated focal *ERG* deletions in the majority of cases. ERGalt lacks the N-terminal pointed domain but retains DNA-binding and transactivation domains, inhibits wild-type ERG, and directly promotes lymphoid leukemogenesis in mouse models. This subtype has a distinct gene expression profile, intermediate-to-high risk age distribution, and — importantly — a favorable prognosis despite co-occurring alterations (e.g., IKZF1 deletion) otherwise associated with poor outcome. The data are available in cBioPortal as `all_stjude_2016`.

## Key Findings

### F1: DUX4 rearrangement to IGH is a universal, early clonal event in this ALL subtype

**Finding:** All 141 cases with the characteristic gene expression profile harbor rearrangement of *DUX4* (chromosome 4q35 macrosatellite repeat) adjacent to the IGH enhancer, placing DUX4 under IGH control and causing marked overexpression. This is the defining initiating event.

**Detail:** DUX4-IGH rearrangements are structurally complex: DUX4 C-termini are variably truncated and fused to IGH-derived sequences. The insertions are clonal, confirmed in all 6 cases tested by RT-PCR/genomic PCR. DUX4 protein overexpression confirmed by immunoblotting. DUX4 rearrangement is not visible by karyotype and is challenging to detect by standard WGS/RNA-seq due to the highly repetitive DUX4 locus and IGH insertion site. Detection requires dedicated transcriptome or genome sequencing with appropriate handling of repetitive regions.

**Genes / cancer types:** `DUX4`, `IGH` · B-progenitor ALL

### F2: DUX4 directly binds ERG intron 6 and induces expression of a dominant-negative isoform (ERGalt)

**Finding:** DUX4 binds two motifs within a 372-nt region in *ERG* intron 6 (the non-canonical first exon of ERGalt) and directly induces transcription of ERGalt — confirmed by ChIP-seq in NALM-6 and Reh cell lines and by DUX4 transduction in cord blood CD34+ cells and B-ALL lines.

**Detail:** ERGalt is a truncated ERG protein (~28 kDa, C-terminal; confirmed by immunoblot) that retains the ETS DNA-binding domain and transactivation domain, but lacks the N-terminal pointed domain and central regulatory domains. ERGalt acts as a competitive inhibitor of wild-type ERG in transcriptional reporter assays (gpIX target). ERGalt expressed from the non-deleted ERG allele in cases with clonal ERG deletion. Two coding ERGalt isoforms identified (ERGalt a, ERGalt b). High-level ERGalt expression is restricted to DUX4-rearranged ALL.

**Genes / cancer types:** `DUX4`, `ERG` (ERGalt isoform) · B-progenitor ALL

### F3: ERG deletions affect 55% of DUX4-rearranged cases and are secondary RAG-mediated events

**Finding:** 85/141 (55.6%) of DUX4/ERG ALL cases have focal deletions of *ERG* at 21q22.3, most commonly involving exons 3–7 (n=27) or exons 3–9 (n=22). Conserved heptamer recombinase signal sequences at breakpoints indicate RAG-mediated aberrant recombination.

**Detail:** ERG deletions are secondary events: DUX4 binding to intron 6 opens chromatin (ATAC-seq evidence), creating a susceptible locus for RAG activity. Subclonal ERG deletions were detected in cases lacking clonal deletions, and clonal deletions emerged at relapse in xenograft experiments. ERG deletions remove intron 6 (the ERGalt first exon locus) so ERGalt must be expressed from the remaining non-deleted allele. ERG deletions are not observed in other B-ALL or T-ALL subtypes.

### F4: The DUX4/ERG ALL mutational landscape — IKZF1 deletions not prognostically adverse in this context

**Finding:** DUX4/ERG ALL has a distinct mutational landscape: mean 17.5 non-silent mutations/case, frequent epigenetic modifier mutations (56.3%, including KMT2D, SETD2, ARID2, NCOR1), RAS pathway (35.2%), IKZF1 deletion (36.7%), and PAX5 mutation (11.3%). Critically, IKZF1 deletions — a powerful adverse prognostic marker in other B-ALL — are not associated with poor outcome in DUX4/ERG ALL.

**Detail:** Cell cycle alterations in 22.5% of cases. Transcription factor/regulator mutations (21%) including MYC, MYCBP2, MGA, ZEB2 — uncommon in other B-ALL subtypes. RAS activating mutations (35.2%) also frequent. Structural alterations are otherwise sparse. IKZF1 deletion rates (36.7%) resemble BCR-ABL1-like ALL, but the prognostic impact is entirely different.

### F5: ERGalt is transforming in mouse models and shifts lineage from erythromegakaryoblastic to lymphoid

**Finding:** Transplantation of Arf−/− mouse bone marrow transduced with ERGalt (vs. wild-type ERG) produces lymphoid precursor, biphenotypic, or pre-B cell leukemias with longer latency, in contrast to the rapid lethal erythromegakaryoblastic leukemia induced by wild-type ERG overexpression.

**Detail:** ERGalt + NRAS G12D promoted serial replating of lymphoid cells in vitro. ERGalt-induced mouse leukemias co-expressed B220 and CD19, confirming a B-lymphoid immunophenotype. This lineage shift (myeloid/erythroid → lymphoid) mirrors the human DUX4/ERG ALL phenotype. Wild-type ERG expression was not tolerated in virus producer cells (full-length), limiting mechanistic studies.

**Genes / cancer types:** `ERG`, `NRAS`, `ARF` (Cdkn2a) · B-progenitor ALL (model)

## Open Questions

- Can ERGalt expression be reliably detected in clinical RNA-seq panels as a diagnostic marker, given the repetitive nature of the DUX4 locus?
- Do DUX4/ERG ALL patients benefit from the same treatment as standard B-ALL, or does the favorable prognosis justify therapy de-escalation?
- What is the order and timing of additional co-mutations (e.g., IKZF1, RAS) relative to DUX4 rearrangement during leukemogenesis?
- Are there other DUX4 target genes (beyond ERG) that contribute to leukemogenesis?
- Does IKZF1 deletion in DUX4/ERG ALL have any biological effect even if not prognostically adverse?

## Limitations

- WGS/WES/RNA-seq performed on only a subset (~76 of 141 DUX4/ERG cases); full landscape may be incomplete
- Mouse ERG exon 6 alt is incompletely conserved, limiting direct modeling of human ERGalt induction by DUX4
- Clinical outcome data is reported for subgroups but detailed survival analysis across age groups is limited in this paper
- The repetitive nature of DUX4 and its macrosatellite repeat makes standard FISH and clinical sequencing-based identification challenging
- External validation of the ERGalt immunoblot as a clinical diagnostic tool not yet established
