## Summary

Comprehensive integrative genomic study of 1001 newly diagnosed DLBCL patients using whole-exome sequencing (400 tumor-normal pairs + 601 unpaired) and RNA sequencing (n=775). Identified 150 genetic driver genes including 27 novel to DLBCL (SPEN, KLHL14, MGA). A genome-wide CRISPR screen across six DLBCL cell lines pinpointed 35 functional oncogenes, nine with subtype-specific essentiality in ABC vs. GCB DLBCL. A multivariate genomic risk model combining DNA alterations with cell-of-origin (COO) and MYC/BCL2 expression outperformed IPI, COO alone, and double-expressor status. All data are accessible in `dlbcl_duke_2017` on cBioPortal.

## Key Findings

### F1: 150 genetic drivers catalogued; 27 novel to DLBCL

**Finding:** Whole-exome analysis of 1001 DLBCLs identified 150 putative driver genes with a mean of 7.75 mutations per tumor, including 27 previously unimplicated genes: SPEN, KLHL14, MGA, CDKN2A (novel point mutations beyond known CNA), and RB1.

**Detail:** The discovery set (n=502 tumor-normal pairs) provided ~100% power for genes mutated at ≥10% and >90% power for ≥5% frequency. Three complementary approaches—MutsigCV, HotNet2, and a custom variant-enrichment model—were combined to maximize sensitivity and specificity. Alterations spanned missense mutations (oncogene pattern, e.g., BCL2, CARD11, IRF4) and truncating mutations/deletions (tumor suppressor pattern, e.g., SPEN, CDKN2A, TNFAIP3). Sanger-based validation of 1130 variants across 61 genes yielded 90% concordance.

**Genes / cancer types:** BCL2, MYC, CARD11, IRF4, SPEN, CDKN2A, TNFAIP3, EZH2, MYD88, KLHL14, MGA, TP53 · DLBCL

### F2: ABC vs. GCB cell-of-origin differentially enriched for 20 driver genes

**Finding:** RNAseq-based COO classification of 775 DLBCLs (313 ABC, 331 GCB, rest unclassified) revealed 20 genes mutated at significantly different frequencies by subtype. GCB-enriched: EZH2, SGK1, GNA13, SOCS1, STAT6, TNFRSF14. ABC-enriched: ETV6, MYD88, PIM1, TBL1XR1.

**Detail:** RNAseq COO classifier showed excellent agreement with NanoString (R²=0.87) and Hans IHC algorithm (p<10⁻⁶), confirming reliability. Pairwise mutual exclusivity and co-occurrence network across 150 driver genes identified 61 genes with significant relationships (p<0.01), including MLL2 mutual exclusivity with MYC, and TP53 mutual exclusivity with KLHL6. ABC DLBCLs had significantly worse OS than GCB DLBCLs (p=0.002). The ABC/GCB distinction was confirmed prognostic but insufficient alone to resolve prognosis within double-expressor patients.

**Genes / cancer types:** EZH2, SGK1, GNA13, MYD88, ETV6, PIM1, SOCS1, STAT6, TNFRSF14 · DLBCL (ABC, GCB)

**Data availability:** `available`

### F3: CRISPR screen defines 35 functional oncogenes; 9 are subtype-specific

**Finding:** A genome-wide GeCKO v2 CRISPR dropout screen across six DLBCL cell lines (3 ABC: LY3, TMD8, HBL1; 2 GCB: SUDHL4, Pfeiffer; 1 Burkitt: BJAB) identified 35 driver genes whose knockout reduced cell viability, confirming oncogene function. Nine showed subtype-specific lethality.

**Detail:** ABC-specific lethal knockouts: EBF1, IRF4, CARD11, MYD88, IKBKB. GCB-specific lethal knockouts: ZBTB7A, XPO1, TGFBR2, PTPN6. 36% of DLBCL patients have alterations in one of the nine drug-targetable CRISPR hits, and nine of the 35 oncogenes are direct targets of drugs already in clinical use or trials. Importantly, dropout of NOTCH2, PIK3CD, and JAK2—commonly nominated from mutational data—had no significant growth impact, underscoring the need for functional validation before therapeutic nomination.

**Genes / cancer types:** MYC, RHOA, SF3B1, MTOR, BCL2, EBF1, IRF4, CARD11, MYD88, XPO1, BTK, PIK3R1 · DLBCL

**Data availability:** `available`

### F4: Genomic risk model outperforms IPI, COO, and double-expressor status

**Finding:** A multivariate Cox proportional hazards model integrating 313 combinatorial features (up to 3 genetic/expression markers at a time) generated a genomic risk score that significantly outperforms IPI, COO, and MYC+BCL2 double-expressor status in predicting overall survival.

**Detail:** The genomic model was validated in an independent 20% hold-out test set (logrank p=8×10⁻⁵) and by 5-fold cross-validation repeated 100 times (median p=8×10⁻⁶). The integrated DNA+RNA model surpassed DNA-only and RNA-only models, and models using the same features without interaction terms (confirming that combinatorial feature sets capture key interactions). IPI was highly prognostic for early mortality but waned over time; the genomic model maintained significance for both early and late mortality. Crucially, the genomic model stratified survival within each COO class, double-expressor stratum, and IPI risk group, adding independent prognostic value.

**Genes / cancer types:** MYC, BCL2 (expression + mutation), + 150 driver genes, COO · DLBCL

### F5: MYC mutation + high MYC expression defines worst-prognosis subgroup

**Finding:** Among combinatorial genomic features in the multivariate model, MYC genetic alterations combined with high MYC expression defined the single subgroup with the worst overall survival. MYC translocations associate with high MYC expression, while BCL2 translocations associate with BCL2 amplification/mutation.

**Detail:** Double-expressors (MYC-high + BCL2-high by RNAseq) were significantly associated with worse survival (Figure S4). ABC/GCB COO did not further stratify survival within double-expressors. FISH analysis confirmed that MYC translocations co-occur with MYC mutations and high expression. BTG1, PAX5, and CDKN2A alterations were associated with poorer survival in ABC DLBCL. CREBBP alterations were associated with favorable survival in ABC DLBCL. In GCB, NFKBIA and NCOR1 predicted poor prognosis; EZH2, MYD88, and ARID5B mutations predicted better prognosis.

**Genes / cancer types:** MYC, BCL2, BTG1, PAX5, CDKN2A, CREBBP, NFKBIA, NCOR1, EZH2 · DLBCL

**Data availability:** `available`
> MYC and BCL2 expression and mutation data in `dlbcl_duke_2017`. FISH translocation data not available in cBioPortal.

**Tool support:** `native`
> Survival analysis by mutation and expression group. OncoPrint can display co-mutation patterns of MYC alterations.

## Open Questions

- Can the genomic risk model be prospectively validated in randomized clinical trials to guide treatment intensification?
- Which of the 27 novel DLBCL drivers have targetable functional roles that can be confirmed in primary patient material beyond cell lines?
- Do the CRISPR screen results translate to primary tumor sensitivity—particularly for MYD88 and IKBKB in ABC DLBCL?
- How does clonal evolution under rituximab-based therapy alter the frequencies of the 150 driver genes in relapsed/refractory disease?

## Limitations

- The majority of cases were FFPE, which may undercount low-VAF variants; however, matched FFPE/frozen analysis across 34 pairs showed equivalent mutation recovery (Jaccard similarity preserved, p=0.49).
- No matched germline for 601 tumor-only cases; however, mutation frequencies in paired vs. unpaired sets were nearly identical (R²=0.94).
- CRISPR screen performed in only six cell lines and under steady-state growth conditions; in vivo functional dependencies may differ.
- Neoantigen and immunogenomic analyses not performed; tumor microenvironment contributions to prognosis not captured.
- Validation of the genomic risk model in an independent prospective cohort has not yet been reported in this study.
