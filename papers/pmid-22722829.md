## Summary

Whole-genome sequencing of 37 medulloblastomas (discovery) plus targeted validation in 56 additional cases (total n=93) identifies 41 novel recurrently mutated genes in four molecular subgroups (WNT, SHH, subgroup-3, subgroup-4). Key findings: KDM6A inactivation and EZH2 overexpression cooperatively maintain H3K27me3 in subgroup-3 and 4 tumors; SMARCA4, CREBBP, TRRAP, and MED13 mutations in WNT-subgroup target CTNNB1-associated chromatin remodelers; DDX3X missense mutations in WNT-subgroup provide a proliferative advantage to lower rhombic lip progenitors (LRLPs); CDH1 mutations activate WNT signaling as an alternative to CTNNB1 stabilization; PIK3CA mutations accelerate but do not initiate WNT-subgroup tumorigenesis. Subgroup-specific mutation patterns underscore the need for subgroup-stratified clinical trial design. Data linked to `mbl_pcgp` in cBioPortal.

## Key Findings

### F1: KDM6A inactivation and EZH2 overexpression define a stem-like chromatin state in subgroup-3 and 4 medulloblastoma

**Finding:** Subgroup-3 and 4 medulloblastomas show aberrant preservation of H3K27me3 via two mechanisms: EZH2 overexpression (driven by chr7q35 gain; writing H3K27me3) and KDM6A biallelic inactivation (loss of the H3K27me3 eraser). KDM6A was mutated in 6 subgroup-4, 1 subgroup-3, and 1 unclassified tumor. KDM6A mutations were absent from WNT and SHH subgroups.

**Detail:** Chr7q gain significantly enriched in subgroup-3 and 4 (P<0.005); EZH2 expression directly correlated with Chr7q dosage. Males with KDM6A inactivation frequently also deleted chromosome Y (harboring the paralog UTY), confirming a two-hit KDM6A–UTY tumor suppressor model (57% of KDM6A-mutant males deleted ChrY vs. 6% of KDM6A-WT males, P<0.005). Mutations in 6 additional KDM family members (KDM1A, KDM3A, KDM4C, KDM5A, KDM5B, KDM7A) were exclusively in subgroup-3 and 4, implicating broad disruption of lysine demethylation. CHD7 (frameshift/nonsense, 4 tumors) and ZMYM3 (Xq13.1 frameshift/nonsense/missense, 3 male subgroup-4 tumors; all co-mutating KDM6A or KDM1A) were also selectively altered in subgroup-3/4, disrupting H3K4me3-associated transcription.

**Genes / cancer types:** KDM6A, EZH2, CHD7, ZMYM3, KDM1A, KDM3A, KDM4C, KDM5A, KDM5B, UTY · Medulloblastoma (subgroup-3, subgroup-4)

### F2: WNT-subgroup medulloblastomas harbor mutations in CTNNB1-associated chromatin remodelers: SMARCA4, CREBBP, TRRAP, MED13

**Finding:** Six of 11 WNT-subgroup medulloblastomas (55%) mutated chromatin modifiers recruited to TCF/LEF WNT-responsive genes by CTNNB1: SMARCA4 (4 tumors, heterozygous helicase domain mutations; P<0.002), CREBBP (2 tumors; P<0.02), TRRAP (1 tumor), MED13 (1 tumor). These mutations co-occur with CTNNB1 stabilizing mutations or CDH1 mutations.

**Detail:** >70% (8/11) of WNT-subgroup tumors had CTNNB1 stabilizing mutations (confirmed by nuclear CTNNB1 IHC). SMARCA4 mutations were WNT-specific and targeted the ATPase/helicase domain, likely acting in a dominant-negative fashion. CREBBP nonsense mutations occurred in two WNT tumors (one also carrying SMARCA4 mutation). TRRAP and MED13 mutations each occurred in single WNT tumors. These chromatin remodelers are part of the CTNNB1 co-activator complex at WNT-responsive genes, suggesting that disruption of epigenetic regulation at WNT target genes contributes to WNT-subgroup pathogenesis beyond CTNNB1 stabilization alone.

**Genes / cancer types:** CTNNB1, SMARCA4, CREBBP, TRRAP, MED13, APC · Medulloblastoma (WNT subgroup)

### F3: DDX3X missense mutations in WNT-subgroup medulloblastoma promote LRLP proliferation rather than perturbing migration

**Finding:** DDX3X (DEAD-box RNA helicase, Xp11.3) was recurrently mutated in WNT-subgroup medulloblastomas (P<0.0001), with 7/8 mutations clustering in the DEAD-box domain (predicted to alter nucleic acid binding rather than abolish ATPase activity). In utero electroporation of mutant Ddx3x (T275M, G325E) into mouse LRLPs increased labeled cell numbers by ~50% without impairing migration, while Ddx3x knockdown ablated LRLP survival — establishing DDX3X mutations as gain-of-function proliferative drivers.

**Detail:** DDX3X escapes X inactivation; 2/3 DDX3X-mutant female medulloblastomas retained the wild-type allele, consistent with dominant gain-of-function mutation. Structural modeling predicted the DEAD-box mutations alter RNA substrate binding specificity/affinity. DDX3X is essential for LRLP maintenance (Ddx3x shRNAs: <0.5% of transduced cells survived to P1), while mutant Ddx3x provides a proliferative advantage. This establishes DDX3X as an oncogene in WNT-medulloblastoma arising from LRLPs, and distinguishes it from KDM6A and ZMYM3 mutations (selective for non-WNT subgroups in functional validation).

**Genes / cancer types:** DDX3X · Medulloblastoma (WNT subgroup)

### F4: PIK3CA mutations accelerate but do not initiate WNT-subgroup medulloblastoma; CDH1 mutations substitute for CTNNB1 stabilization

**Finding:** PIK3CA activating mutations (Q546K, H1047R, N345K) were detected in one case each of WNT, SHH, and subgroup-4 medulloblastoma. Mouse modeling showed Blbp-Cre; Pik3ca-E545K alone did not produce tumors, but 100% of Blbp-Cre; Ctnnb1+/lox(Ex3); Tp53+/flx; Pik3ca-E545K mice developed WNT-medulloblastoma by 3 months (vs. 4% of Blbp-Cre; Ctnnb1+/lox(Ex3); Tp53+/flx mice at 11 months), establishing PIK3CA as a potent cooperating mutation. CDH1 missense mutations (R63G, V329F) were found exclusively in 2 CTNNB1-wild-type WNT tumors; CDH1 knockdown in mouse LRLPs doubled self-renewal, upregulated Tcf/Lef transcription, and impeded LRLP migration — functionally substituting for mutant CTNNB1.

**Detail:** CDH1 sequesters CTNNB1 at the cell membrane; CDH1 mutations predicted to disrupt this interaction promote WNT signaling. CDH1 mutations were mutually exclusive with CTNNB1 mutations. The combination of CDH1 mutation + CTNNB1 activation (via loss of E-cadherin sequestration) converges on the same WNT signaling output as direct CTNNB1 stabilization. PIK3CA mutations are likely acquired later in WNT-subgroup tumorigenesis, activating the AKT pathway (confirmed by elevated pS6, p4EBP1 in Pik3ca-mutant mouse tumors) as a co-operative oncogenic event.

**Genes / cancer types:** PIK3CA, CDH1, CTNNB1, APC, TP53 · Medulloblastoma (WNT subgroup)

### F5: Novel mutations in SHH-subgroup beyond SUFU/PTCH1 include GABRG1; germline mutations are rare

**Finding:** Only 4/13 SHH-subgroup tumors had expected biallelic SUFU or PTCH1 inactivation. Two SHH tumors (#11 and #12) harbored identical novel T48M missense mutations in GABRG1 (GABA-A receptor gamma-1 subunit), predicted to be deleterious; GABA-A disruption can enhance neural stem cell proliferation. Previously reported MLL2, TP53, and PTEN mutations were also identified but are not SHH-specific.

**Detail:** Across all 93 medulloblastomas, only 23 germline mutations previously associated with cancer were detected (including 1 confirmed Turcot syndrome with germline APC Y935* and somatic APC deletion, WNT-subgroup #11). Known germline medulloblastoma predisposition genes (TP53, PTCH1, APC, CREBBP) were present but rare. Fusion proteins were uncommon (<2%, 6/314 SVs excluding two highly rearranged tumors); none affected the same gene or pathway, making fusion drivers unlikely as a general mechanism. TERT promoter and coding fusions were not the focus of this study (predating widespread TERT sequencing in medulloblastoma).

## Open Questions

- Do EZH2 inhibitors (e.g., tazemetostat) show selective activity in KDM6A-mutant or EZH2-overexpressing subgroup-3 and 4 medulloblastomas, and can H3K27me3 IHC serve as a pharmacodynamic biomarker?
- Can DDX3X mutation status guide clinical enrollment into WNT-subgroup-specific trials targeting the PI3K or DDX3X pathway, and do DDX3X mutations define a subset with particularly favorable or unfavorable prognosis?
- What are the transcriptional targets of MYBL1 (ZMYM3) in subgroup-4 medulloblastoma, and do ZMYM3–KDM6A co-mutations define a distinct therapeutic vulnerability?
- Does GABRG1 mutation represent a druggable target in the subset of SHH medulloblastomas lacking SUFU/PTCH1 inactivation?
- Given the subgroup-specificity of mutations, should clinical trials stratify enrollment by molecular subgroup — particularly for SMARCA4-directed BRD9 degraders (WNT) and EZH2 inhibitors (subgroup-3/4)?

## Limitations

- Total cohort of 93 tumors (37 WGS + 56 validation) limits statistical power for rare subgroup-specific mutations, especially in WNT and SHH subgroups (n=11 and 13 respectively)
- 12 'unclassified' cases in the validation cohort (no expression profiles available) reduce subgroup-specific enrichment analysis power
- Validation of coding mutations used Sanger sequencing on 25 discovery cases and custom capture on 12 — less comprehensive than WGS re-sequencing
- Expression profiling used Affymetrix U133v2 arrays (not RNA-seq); fusion transcripts and splice variants not fully characterized
- Mouse models used LRLP-specific Blbp-Cre for WNT-subgroup functional studies; GNP-specific and subgroup-3 progenitor models may differ in their response to the same mutations
- Data deposited in dbGaP (restricted access); may require application for raw sequencing data access
