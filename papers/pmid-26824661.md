## Summary

TCGA integrated genomic, transcriptomic, and epigenomic profiling of 1,122 adult diffuse gliomas (grade II–IV), unifying the LGG and GBM datasets for the first time. The central finding is that IDH mutation status—not histological grade—is the primary molecular determinant of glioma biology. Three major molecular subgroups emerge: IDH-wildtype (enriched for classic GBM alterations, poor prognosis), IDH-mutant with 1p/19q co-deletion (Codel, best prognosis), and IDH-mutant non-codel (intermediate). Within IDH-mutant non-codel tumors, a novel G-CIMP-low subtype shows DNA demethylation, CDK4/CDKN2A cell cycle alterations, and GBM-like survival despite lower histological grade. A separate IDH-wildtype subgroup (PA-like) shows molecular resemblance to pilocytic astrocytoma and favorable outcomes. ATRX—not TERT promoter—mutations correlate with the Alternative Lengthening of Telomeres (ALT) mechanism and increased telomere length. The DNA methylation–based classification provides independent prognostic value beyond age and grade, achieving a C-Index of 0.836.

## Key Findings

### F1: IDH mutation status is the primary molecular stratifier of diffuse glioma, superseding histology

**Finding:** IDH1/IDH2 mutation status drives the entire molecular landscape of diffuse glioma — DNA methylation, gene expression, copy number, and survival — more than histological grade or tumor type. IDH-mutant tumors show genome-wide hypermethylation (G-CIMP), longer survival, and distinct driver profiles compared to IDH-wildtype tumors.

**Detail:** 99% of IDH-mutant samples cluster into the LGm1/LGm2/LGm3 methylation macro-group versus 99% of IDH-wildtype samples in LGm4/LGm5/LGm6. MGMT promoter methylation co-occurs with IDH mutation in 85.5% of IDH-mutant non-codel and 98.8% of Codel cases. IDH-wildtype LGG behaves clinically like GBM (median OS 14.0 months). EGFR amplification, PTEN loss, and chr 7 gain/chr 10 loss are hallmarks of IDH-wildtype. IDH1 R132H is the dominant hotspot (~80% of IDH mutations). The IDH mutation and 1p/19q co-deletion classification was later codified in the 2016 WHO CNS tumor classification.

**Genes / cancer types:** `IDH1`, `IDH2`, `EGFR`, `PTEN`, `TP53`, `ATRX` · Diffuse glioma (LGG, GBM)

### F2: ATRX mutation (not TERT promoter) drives the Alternative Lengthening of Telomeres mechanism

**Finding:** ATRX mutations and TERT promoter (TERTp) mutations are nearly mutually exclusive (85% of diffuse gliomas carry one or the other). ATRX-mutant gliomas exhibit significantly longer telomeres than TERTp-mutant tumors (p<0.0001), consistent with ALT as the telomere maintenance mechanism. TERT expression is a highly sensitive (91%) and specific (95%) surrogate for TERTp mutation status.

**Detail:** TERTp mutations occur in 80% of IDH-wildtype GBM and ~50% of IDH-mutant Codel tumors but are rare in IDH-mutant non-codel (2.8%). ATRX mutations predominate in IDH-mutant non-codel (estimated ~60%). TERTp mutations appear to precede chromosome 7 gain/10 loss events in IDH-wildtype glioma, suggesting an early initiating role. TRIM28 and CACNG6 promoter mutations also correlate with expression changes, expanding the noncoding regulatory mutation landscape of glioma.

**Genes / cancer types:** `ATRX`, `TERT`, `TRIM28` · Diffuse glioma

### F3: G-CIMP-low is a novel IDH-mutant subtype with poor outcome driven by cell cycle gene activation

**Finding:** Within IDH-mutant non-codel gliomas, a G-CIMP-low subtype (~10% of IDH-mutant) shows focal DNA demethylation at open-sea intergenic regions, enriched SOX2/OLIG2 transcription factor motifs, activation of CDK4 and CDKN2A cell cycle genes, and significantly worse survival than G-CIMP-high or Codel tumors (HR ~5.6 vs. Codel reference).

**Detail:** G-CIMP-low comprises both LGG (grade II-III) and GBM samples with IDH mutations. 15/18 G-CIMP-low cases have CDK4/CDKN2A abnormalities vs. 36/241 G-CIMP-high. The demethylation occurs predominantly in open-sea CpG regions (69%), with depletion in CpG islands. Serial tumor samples confirm that G-CIMP-high transitions to G-CIMP-low at recurrence in a subset of cases (4/10 clear, 6/10 partial), establishing a disease progression trajectory. G-CIMP-low clinical behavior mimics IDH-wildtype GBM despite molecular IDH-mutant status.

### F4: A PA-like IDH-wildtype LGG subgroup shows favorable survival and molecular similarity to pilocytic astrocytoma

**Finding:** Among IDH-wildtype gliomas, a "PA-like" methylation cluster (LGm6-like) contains grade II-III LGGs with dramatically better survival than other IDH-wildtype tumors (log-rank p=3.6×10⁻⁵), younger age at diagnosis (mean 37.6 vs. 50.8 years), and enrichment for MAPK pathway alterations (BRAF, NF1, NTRK1/2, FGFR1/2 — 52% in PA-like vs. 11–13% in Classic/Mesenchymal-like IDH-wt).

**Detail:** Pilocytic astrocytomas (PA, grade I) classified by random-forest using the pan-glioma methylation probes unanimously fall in this cluster, validating the molecular similarity. Only 2/25 PA-like LGGs show TERT expression vs. 60/65 Classic-like (92%) — emphasizing TERT as a hallmark of aggressive IDH-wildtype glioma. The PA-like subgroup extends beyond BRAF-mutated grade II oligodendroglioma and represents a biologically distinct, clinically favorable form of IDH-wildtype diffuse glioma that warrants separate clinical management.

### F5: 75 significantly mutated genes identified; chromatin modification pathway altered in 54% of IDH-mutant tumors

**Finding:** Comprehensive pan-glioma SMG analysis identified 75 significantly mutated genes, including 45 not previously associated with glioma (mutation frequencies 0.5–2.6%). The chromatin modification pathway is altered in 54% of all tumors (423/793 with overlapping data), predominantly in IDH-mutant non-codel cases (87%). The cohesin complex (NIPBL, STAG2) is disrupted in 16% of gliomas, nominating it as a major gliomagenesis pathway.

**Detail:** Newly predicted drivers include chromatin organization genes SETD2, ARID2, DNMT3A, and oncogenes KRAS/NRAS (25 and 5 cases). 1,144 gene fusions were detected, with 37 in-frame RTK fusions in RNA-seq data. Ras-Raf-MEK-ERK pathway altered in 73% of all samples, predominantly IDH-wildtype (92%). PARADIGM pathway analysis confirmed convergence of drivers onto cell cycle, angiogenesis, and neuronal differentiation pathways distinguishing GBM from LGG progression. HOXA gene cluster activation in IDH-wildtype tumors (chr7 amplification region) suggests stem cell maintenance pathway relevance.

**Genes / cancer types:** `SETD2`, `ARID2`, `DNMT3A`, `KRAS`, `NRAS`, `NIPBL`, `STAG2`, `CIC`, `FUBP1`, `TP53` · Diffuse glioma

### F6: DNA methylation–based epigenetic classification provides independent prognostic value beyond age and grade

**Finding:** A multivariable survival model including age, WHO grade, and DNA methylation epigenetic subtype achieves a C-Index of 0.836 in the discovery cohort and 0.746 in external validation (n=183), outperforming models using histology alone. Mesenchymal-like IDH-wildtype subtype carries the worst prognosis (HR 8.71 vs. Codel reference), while G-CIMP-high approaches the favorable outcome of Codel (HR 1.92, not significantly worse).

**Detail:** The epigenetic subtypes recapitulate previously published GBM subtypes (Classical, Mesenchymal) within the IDH-wildtype cluster and add new granularity within IDH-mutant cases. Tumor Map (UCSC) integrating gene expression and methylation profiles confirms cluster topology. RPPA analysis shows GBM-like cluster enrichment for IGFBP2, EGFR, phosphoAKT, and CyclinB1, while LGG-like shows PTEN and BRAF activity — demonstrating multi-omic convergence on the same biological groupings. TERT expression and TERTp mutations were not independently prognostic after adjusting for age and grade.

**Genes / cancer types:** `EGFR`, `PTEN`, `BRAF` · Diffuse glioma

## Open Questions

- What drives the G-CIMP-low transition from G-CIMP-high — is it epigenetic drift, subclonal selection, or treatment-induced? Can it be predicted prospectively?
- Do PA-like IDH-wildtype LGGs benefit from MAPK-pathway targeted therapies (BRAF/MEK inhibitors) that are effective in classic pilocytic astrocytoma?
- Why does TERTp mutation appear to precede chr7/chr10 events in IDH-wildtype glioma — is TERT activation a true initiating event or a bystander in a field of cells already genomically unstable?
- How does the cohesin complex disruption (NIPBL, STAG2) mechanistically drive gliomagenesis, and does it confer PARP inhibitor sensitivity as suggested in some cell line models?
- Can the 6-group epigenetic classification be reduced to a clinically deployable PCR/sequencing assay for routine diagnostic use, replacing the full 450k methylation array?

## Limitations

- Methylation platforms differ (450k for LGG, 27k for most GBM), requiring a merged probe set that may lose resolution; batch effects were corrected but not fully eliminated.
- TERTp mutations were available for only a subset (~287 targeted + ~42 WGS); the majority lacked TERTp status, limiting subgroup analyses.
- No prospective treatment or survival data linked to molecular subtypes; all survival associations are retrospective and may reflect selection biases in TCGA sample acquisition.
- The G-CIMP-low subgroup is small (n=25 in discovery) and may be underpowered to fully characterize its biological and clinical spectrum.
- Primarily North American patient population; generalizability to other ancestries and healthcare settings is uncertain.
