## Summary

Machine-learning study integrating CT radiomics, PD-L1 IHC texture features, and MSK-IMPACT genomics to predict response to PD-(L)1 blockade in 247 NSCLC patients at MSK (2014–2019). The multimodal deep attention-based multiple-instance learning (DyAM) model achieved AUC=0.80 (95% CI 0.74–0.86), substantially outperforming single-modality predictors: TMB (AUC=0.61), PD-L1 TPS (AUC=0.73), and CT features alone (AUC=0.65). EGFR mutations (8.9%), STK11 mutations (17.8%), and high TMB were independently predictive of PFS outcome. The study demonstrates that routinely collected clinical data—CT scans, PD-L1 IHC slides, and MSK-IMPACT sequencing—can be integrated into a multimodal biomarker superior to any single modality. Genomic data underlying this cohort is available in `lung_msk_mind_2020` on cBioPortal.

## Key Findings

### F1: Multimodal DyAM model (AUC=0.80) substantially outperforms all unimodal predictors

**Finding:** Integrating CT radiomics, PD-L1 IHC features (TPS + texture), and genomic features via the DyAM framework achieved AUC=0.80 (95% CI 0.74–0.86) for predicting immunotherapy response in NSCLC — significantly better than TMB alone (0.61), PD-L1 TPS alone (0.73), or CT features alone (0.65). Layering complementary modalities consistently improved performance.

**Detail:** DyAM (dynamic deep attention-based multiple-instance learning with masking) outputs modality-specific partial risk scores, attention weights, and an overall score. Adding site-specific CT features to the DyAM base improved AUC from 0.65 to 0.70. Bimodal combinations: CT+genomic features (AUC=0.76), PD-L1+genomic (AUC=0.72), CT+PD-L1 (AUC=0.68). Best fully automated approach (CT+IHC texture+genomic) achieved AUC=0.78. Best overall (CT+PD-L1 TPS+genomic) was AUC=0.80. Kaplan-Meier stratification by multimodal DyAM score separated high- and low-risk patients more effectively than PD-L1 TPS or TMB alone.

**Genes / cancer types:** (All MSK-IMPACT genes) · Non-small cell lung cancer

**Data availability:** `available`
> MSK-IMPACT genomic data (mutations, CNA, TMB) for 247 NSCLC patients with immunotherapy outcomes in `lung_msk_mind_2020`. Clinical attributes include PD-L1 TPS, treatment type, response (CR/PR/SD/PD), PFS, OS.

**Tool support:** `native`

### F2: CT radiomic features from lung parenchymal lesions modestly predict immunotherapy response (AUC=0.64)

**Finding:** CT-based radiomic features from lung parenchymal lesions provided modest but consistent predictive value (AUC=0.64, 95% CI 0.54–0.74), validated in an independent radiology cohort (AUC=0.66). Predictions from pleural lesions were uninformative (AUC=0.28). Lesion-type-specific feature extraction outperformed pooled multiple-instance learning (AUC=0.61).

**Detail:** 187 of 247 patients had clearly delineated lesions segmented by three board-certified thoracic radiologists. Lesion categories: lung parenchymal (163 patients, 87%), pleural (21, 11%), pathologically enlarged lymph nodes (67, 36%). Superpixel-based perturbation augmented segmentations for robust feature extraction. L1-regularized logistic regression selected mean 35 (lung), 10 (pleural), 25 (nodal) features. PCA showed lesion-type clusters with distinct texture profiles. Lymph node models showed >20% failure rate due to convergence issues.

**Genes / cancer types:** (Imaging features only, no gene-level findings) · NSCLC

**Data availability:** `not-available`
> CT radiomic features are not deposited in cBioPortal. CT imaging data is proprietary to MSK clinical systems. Only genomic data is available in `lung_msk_mind_2020`.

**Tool support:** `custom`
> Radiomic feature extraction pipeline described in paper (Python-based). Integration into cBioPortal not supported natively.

### F3: PD-L1 IHC texture features from digitized slides approximate pathologist assessment

**Finding:** Automated GLCM-based texture features extracted from digitized PD-L1 IHC slides significantly correlated with pathologist-assessed TPS (inversely, r²=-0.38) and progression-free survival (r²=0.17). The most predictive texture feature (GLCM autocorrelation skewness) achieved AUC=0.68 for response prediction, comparable to lesion-averaged CT features.

**Detail:** 201 of 247 patients had quality-controlled digitized PD-L1 IHC; 105 (52%) showed positive staining (TPS≥1%). IHC-A model (18 features: autocorrelation matrix + pixel intensity statistics) AUC=0.62 (95% CI 0.51–0.73). Full 150-feature GLCM model (IHC-G) achieved AUC=0.63 multimodal, 0.74 in pathology validation cohort. Automated texture was inferior to pathologist-scored TPS (AUC=0.73) but complementary when combined with genomic features. GLCM autocorrelation measures texture coarseness reflecting spatial distribution of PD-L1 expression.

**Genes / cancer types:** (PD-L1 / CD274 protein expression) · NSCLC

**Data availability:** `partial`

**Tool support:** `native`
> PD-L1 TPS queryable as clinical attribute in Study View. IHC texture features require custom computational pipeline.

### F4: EGFR mutations (aHR=2.14) and STK11 mutations (aHR=2.53) are independent negative predictors of PFS on immunotherapy

**Finding:** In multivariable Cox proportional hazard analysis of PFS, EGFR alterations (n=22, 8.9%) and STK11 alterations (n=44, 17.8%) were significant independent negative predictors of immunotherapy benefit (EGFR aHR=2.14, P=0.03; STK11 aHR=2.53, P<0.005). TMB (aHR=0.14, P=0.04) was a positive predictor. EGFR and STK11 were uncorrelated with each other and with TMB.

**Detail:** LR classification using TMB alone achieved AUC=0.61; adding genomic alterations (EGFR, ALK, ROS1, RET, MET, ERBB2, BRAF, STK11, ARID1A) improved to AUC=0.65. These genomic predictors were consistent with published literature and served as validated inputs for DyAM multimodal integration. EGFR-mutated tumors were 8.9% of the cohort; STK11-mutated tumors were 17.8% — both well-represented for signal detection.

### F5: DyAM attention-based framework enables adaptive modality weighting and missing-data masking

**Finding:** The DyAM model assigns adaptive attention weights to each modality for each patient, allowing contributions from CT, IHC, and genomics to vary dynamically based on data availability. The masking mechanism handles missing modalities (e.g., PD-L1 negative tumors or non-segmentable CT) without imputation, preserving model applicability in real-world settings.

**Detail:** Attention weight analysis showed radiology contributing most when CT lesions were clearly delineated; genomic features dominated when high-TMB or driver mutations were present. The masking capability addresses a key limitation of existing multimodal models: 46% of patients had PD-L1 TPS=0 (IHC non-informative), and 24% had limited CT segmentable disease. All multimodal DyAM results significantly exceeded null-hypothesis AUCs from permutation testing. Model was validated across 10-fold cross-validation to reduce overfitting risk.

**Genes / cancer types:** (Model architecture, no specific gene findings) · NSCLC

**Data availability:** `partial`
> Genomic inputs to DyAM (MSK-IMPACT alteration data, TMB) available in `lung_msk_mind_2020`. DyAM model weights, CT radiomics features, and IHC texture features not deposited publicly.

**Tool support:** `custom`
> DyAM model architecture published; implementation requires access to imaging data and custom ML pipeline. Clinical genomic inputs to the model are queryable in cBioPortal.

## Open Questions

- Can the multimodal DyAM approach generalize to non-MSK cohorts and community oncology settings where CT, PD-L1 IHC, and genomic data quality vary?
- Would adding circulating biomarkers (cfDNA, ctDNA, blood TMB) further improve the multimodal model beyond AUC=0.80?
- Does the relative contribution of each modality (CT vs IHC vs genomics) change across histologic subtypes (adenocarcinoma vs squamous) or treatment lines?
- Can automated PD-L1 IHC texture features replace pathologist-scored TPS in resource-limited settings while maintaining predictive accuracy?
- How does the model perform for combination immunotherapy (PD-1+CTLA-4) vs monotherapy, given only 12 patients received combination therapy?

## Limitations

- Retrospective single-institution (MSK) cohort; external validation in independent datasets is needed
- Only 25% response rate (class-balanced in training); may not reflect first-line pembrolizumab-chemotherapy combination settings now standard of care
- CT imaging limited to chest with contrast; extrathoracic disease not captured
- PD-L1 IHC scored on biopsy specimens (potential intra-tumor heterogeneity); digitized IHC from a single representative slide
- MSK-IMPACT is a targeted panel (not WES/WGS); genome-wide mutational signatures and non-panel gene alterations not captured
- Temporal gap (2014–2019) means patients may have received now-obsolete treatment regimens; modern first-line pembrolizumab-chemo combinations are not represented
- 76 of 247 patients had incomplete multimodal data (missing imaging or IHC), requiring modality masking
