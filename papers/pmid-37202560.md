## Summary

The AC-ICAM (Sidra-LUMC Atlas and Compass of Immune–Cancer–Microbiome) study profiled 348 treatment-naive primary colon cancers using RNA-seq, whole-exome sequencing, TCR-β sequencing, and 16S rRNA microbiome profiling on matched tumor–normal pairs with curated long-term follow-up. The Immunologic Constant of Rejection (ICR), a 20-gene immune signature capturing Th1/cytotoxic inflammation, outperformed CMS subtypes and MSI as a prognostic classifier. A Ruminococcus bromii–driven microbiome signature independently associated with favorable outcomes. Combining ICR and microbiome features yielded the mICRoScore, identifying patients with excellent survival. Genetic immunoediting (fewer neoantigens than expected) further refined prognostic stratification.

## Key Findings

### F1: ICR 20-gene immune signature outperforms CMS and MSI as a prognostic classifier
**Finding:** The Immunologic Constant of Rejection (ICR), a 20-gene Th1/cytotoxic signature, stratified colon cancer patients into ICR-high ("hot"), ICR-medium, and ICR-low ("cold") clusters with significantly different overall survival (OS) and progression-free survival (PFS), outperforming CMS subtypes and MSI status.
**Detail:** ICR-high tumors showed the best outcomes; ICR remained prognostic within individual CMS subtypes including CMS4 (mesenchymal), which showed high immune heterogeneity. The ICR associated with clonally expanded, tumor-enriched T cell clones identified by TCR-β sequencing.
**Genes / cancer types:** Colon adenocarcinoma; ICR genes include CXCR3/CCR5 ligands, IFN-γ pathway, cytotoxicity markers.
**Data availability:** available > coad_silu_2022 in cBioPortal includes RNA expression and clinical data.
**Tool support:** api > ICR score can be computed from cBioPortal RNA-seq data via API; custom > Survival analysis by ICR cluster using downloaded expression matrix.
**Concepts:** #tumor-microenvironment #immune-infiltrate #survival #ICI-biomarker #molecular-subtypes #MSI

### F2: Genetic immunoediting refines ICR prognostic value
**Finding:** Quantification of genetic immunoediting — defined as fewer observed neoantigens than expected from mutational load — refined the prognostic value of the ICR beyond the 20-gene signature alone.
**Detail:** Tumors with low neoantigen presentation relative to mutation burden (evidence of immune escape) had worse outcomes even within ICR-high groups, suggesting active clonal immune evasion. WES was used to compute neoantigen burdens.
**Genes / cancer types:** Colon adenocarcinoma; neoantigen load from WES.
**Data availability:** partial > WES data available in coad_silu_2022 but neoantigen calling requires HLA typing and external tools (pVACtools or similar).
**Tool support:** custom > Neoantigen prediction from WES data; TMB proxy available natively in cBioPortal.
**Concepts:** #tumor-microenvironment #immune-infiltrate #ICI-biomarker #clonality #biomarker

### F3: Ruminococcus bromii microbiome signature associated with favorable prognosis
**Finding:** A tumor microbiome signature driven by Ruminococcus bromii was identified as independently associated with favorable clinical outcomes in colon cancer, representing the first large multi-platform validated microbiome prognostic signature in this disease.
**Detail:** Microbiome profiling was performed by 16S rRNA gene sequencing on 246 matched tumor-normal pairs and validated. The microbiome signature was derived from the difference between tumor and normal tissue microbial composition.
**Genes / cancer types:** Colon adenocarcinoma.
**Data availability:** not-available > Microbiome/16S data are not available in cBioPortal (requires dedicated microbiome data portal or GEO/ENA access).
**Tool support:** custom > 16S rRNA analysis pipelines (QIIME2/DADA2) on raw sequencing data.
**Concepts:** #microbiome #tumor-microenvironment #survival #biomarker

### F4: mICRoScore — composite immune + microbiome prognostic score
**Finding:** Combining the ICR immune signature with the microbiome signature produced the mICRoScore, a composite index that identifies a group of colon cancer patients with excellent survival probability, outperforming either feature alone.
**Detail:** The mICRoScore was developed and validated within the AC-ICAM cohort (n=348), demonstrating superior stratification of OS and PFS compared to CMS, MSI, or ICR alone. It integrates orthogonal biological signals (adaptive immunity and gut microbiome).
**Genes / cancer types:** Colon adenocarcinoma.
**Data availability:** partial > ICR component computable from coad_silu_2022 expression data; microbiome component requires raw 16S data not in cBioPortal.
**Tool support:** api > ICR component via cBioPortal expression API; custom > Full mICRoScore requires microbiome data integration.
**Concepts:** #microbiome #immune-infiltrate #survival #biomarker #molecular-subtypes

### F5: CMS4 immune heterogeneity revealed by TCR repertoire analysis
**Finding:** Within the CMS4 (mesenchymal) subtype, the ICR signature identified biologically distinct subgroups with significantly different outcomes, demonstrating that transcriptomic classification alone is insufficient for prognosis within CMS4.
**Detail:** tSNE of expression data showed poor segregation of CMS4 from other subtypes. TCR-β sequencing confirmed that ICR-high CMS4 tumors had clonally expanded T cell infiltrates associated with improved OS.
**Genes / cancer types:** Colon adenocarcinoma; CMS4 subtype.
**Data availability:** partial > CMS classification and RNA expression in coad_silu_2022; TCR repertoire data requires access to raw immunoSEQ data.
**Tool support:** api > CMS and expression data available via cBioPortal; custom > TCR clonality from raw immunoSEQ data.
**Concepts:** #molecular-subtypes #immune-infiltrate #tumor-microenvironment #clonality #MSI

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Can the mICRoScore be prospectively validated in independent cohorts or clinical trials?
- Does the Ruminococcus bromii association with favorable outcomes reflect causality (immunostimulation) or correlation with host immune status?
- Can the ICR or mICRoScore predict benefit from adjuvant chemotherapy or immunotherapy in stage II/III colon cancer?
- How does CMS4 heterogeneity affect response to anti-VEGF or checkpoint inhibition?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Single-institution biobank (Leiden University Medical Center) limits generalizability.
- Retrospective design with prospective validation on retrospectively collected samples.
- Fresh-frozen requirement limits applicability to FFPE-based clinical cohorts.
- Microbiome composition may be affected by technical variables (16S vs. WGS) and sample handling.
- Follow-up time (median 4.6 years) may be insufficient to capture late recurrences in stage I/II patients.
- TCGA-COAD was shown to have insufficient follow-up for immune-survival associations, motivating this study but limiting external replication.
