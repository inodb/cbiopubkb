## Summary

Using MSK-IMPACT targeted DNA sequencing, this study performs the first large-scale comparative genomic analysis of the four major radiation-associated (RT) sarcoma histotypes: angiosarcoma (RT-AS, n=44), malignant peripheral nerve sheath tumor (RT-MPNST, n=12), undifferentiated pleomorphic sarcoma (RT-UPS, n=14), and osteosarcoma (RT-OS, n=12). Each is then compared against histology-matched sporadic counterparts. RT-AS—predominantly arising in the breast/chest wall after breast cancer irradiation—has a distinct genomic profile dominated by *MYC* amplification (75%) and co-occurring *FLT4*, *CRKL*, *HRAS*, and *KMT2D* alterations, with very low fraction of genome altered (FGA) and virtually no *CDKN2A/B* or *TP53* events. In contrast, RT-MPNST harbors the highest FGA and is enriched for *CDKN2A/B* deletion, *NF1* loss, and *TP53* mutations—more so than sporadic MPNST. RT-UPS and RT-OS share similar genomic profiles with their sporadic counterparts. Mutational signatures across all histotypes reflect DNA repair and replication errors rather than an ionizing radiation-specific signature. The study supports histology-based rather than RT-status-based treatment stratification.

## Key Findings

### F1: RT-AS is genomically distinct from sporadic AS — dominated by MYC amplification and MAPK/VEGF pathway genes

**Finding:** RT-AS is defined by *MYC* amplification (75% vs 13% in sporadic AS) that co-occurs with *FLT4* (16%), *CRKL* (14%), *HRAS* (14%), and *KMT2D* (11%) alterations. All *MYC*-amplified RT-AS cases arose in the breast/chest wall.

**Detail:** *HRAS* mutations are all hotspot Ras GTPase domain mutations (A59T, Q61R/L, G60S65_dup). *CRKL*, *FLT4*, *HRAS*, and *MYC* all participate in the stress-activated MAP kinase cascade. *HRAS*, *FLT4*, and *KDR* form part of the VEGF/VEGFR signaling network. In sporadic AS, *KDR* amplification (24% vs 11%), *ATM* (10% vs 2%), and *ATRX* (7% vs 2%) are enriched. Alterations exclusive to sporadic AS include *KIT*, *PDGFRA*, *ARID1A*, *POT1*, and *TERT*. *MYC*-amplified RT-AS cases have a shorter latency from radiation to diagnosis (log-rank p=0.0083).

**Genes / cancer types:** `MYC`, `FLT4`, `CRKL`, `HRAS`, `KMT2D`, `KDR`, `ATM`, `ATRX` · Angiosarcoma (RT vs sporadic)

### F2: RT-sarcoma histotypes show histology-specific genomic landscapes — MPNST vs UPS/OS vs AS are each distinct

**Finding:** Each RT-sarcoma histotype harbors a distinct mutational and copy-number profile. *CDKN2A/B* deletions are present in 92% of RT-MPNST and 33% of RT-OS, but only 2% of RT-AS. *TP53* alterations affect 42–50% of RT-UPS and RT-OS vs 9% of RT-AS.

**Detail:** RT-MPNST most frequently harbors *CDKN2A/B* (92%), *NF1* (67%), *TEK* (42%), *SUZ12* (33%), *TP53* (25%), *PTPRD* (25%). RT-UPS: *TP53* (50%), *CDKN2A/B* (29%), *KMT2D* (21%), *RB1* (14%), *NOTCH3* (14%). RT-OS: *TP53* (42%), *CDKN2A/B* (33%), *MYC* (17%), *FGFR1* (17%). The genomic landscapes of RT-MPNST, RT-UPS, and RT-OS are broadly similar to their sporadic counterparts, whereas RT-AS is markedly different from sporadic AS.

### F3: RT-AS has the lowest genomic instability (FGA) among RT-sarcomas; RT-MPNST has the highest

**Finding:** Fraction of genome altered (FGA) is significantly different across RT-histotypes: RT-AS (9%) < RT-UPS (25%) < RT-OS (32%) < RT-MPNST (51%) (ANOVA p<0.0001). RT-MPNST FGA is significantly higher than sporadic MPNST (51% vs 31%, p=0.014).

**Detail:** Despite low FGA in RT-AS, this does not translate to better survival; RT-AS, RT-MPNST, RT-UPS, and RT-OS showed median OS of 5.1, 2.1, 4.8, and 2.8 years respectively (not significant, small cohort). RT-AS also has the lowest insertion:SNV and deletion:SNV ratios, while RT-UPS has the highest. Structural variant types also differ: large deletions are most frequent in RT-UPS and absent in RT-OS; translocations are more frequent in RT-MPNST and RT-OS and absent in RT-AS.

### F4: Predominant mutational signatures reflect DNA repair defects, not ionizing radiation

**Finding:** Across all four RT-sarcoma histotypes, the dominant mutational signatures are associated with defective DNA mismatch repair and DNA replication slippage (COSMIC SBS mismatch repair signatures), rather than an ionizing radiation-specific signature.

**Detail:** This observation is consistent with the low overall mutation burden in RT-sarcomas compared to carcinogen-associated cancers (tobacco, UV), and explains the relatively low absolute risk of secondary malignancy after radiotherapy (0.03–0.2%). Radiation likely contributes structural genomic damage (balanced inversions, small deletions per prior WGS studies) detectable at the genome level but not prominently in SNV signatures. Additional somatic driver mutations are likely required for RT-sarcoma development.

### F5: RT-AS from breast/chest wall is a clinically and genomically coherent entity driven by MYC; MYC amplification associates with shorter latency

**Finding:** 87% of RT-AS from breast/chest wall (38/44 cases) harbor *MYC* amplification; no *MYC* amplifications were found in RT-AS from other sites. Cases with *MYC* amplification have a significantly shorter latency from radiation to diagnosis (median 8 years overall; shorter in MYC+ subgroup; log-rank p=0.0083).

**Detail:** The strong anatomic and clinical specificity of RT-AS (98% female, mean age 70, 89% chest wall site, 86% prior breast carcinoma) and *MYC* amplification co-occurrence with *FLT4*, *CRKL*, *HRAS*, *KMT2D* implicates a specific molecular oncogenic program. RT-AS from non-breast sites lack *MYC* amplification and show no recurrent non-*MYC* alterations. The *MYC*-high FGA paradox (high frequency of co-occurring alterations despite low overall FGA) suggests focal, targeted copy number events over widespread chromosomal instability.

## Open Questions

- Does *MYC* amplification alone drive RT-AS, or do co-occurring *FLT4*/*HRAS*/*KMT2D* alterations represent independent or cooperative hits?
- Are RT-MPNST cases with higher FGA than sporadic MPNST associated with germline *NF1* mutations or de novo NF1 alterations?
- Can the MAPK/VEGF pathway enrichment in RT-AS be therapeutically exploited with existing inhibitors (anti-VEGF, MEK inhibitors)?
- Why does RT setting specifically predispose to angiosarcoma in the breast field, but not other histotypes at the same site?
- Would whole-genome sequencing reveal RT-specific structural signatures (balanced inversions, small deletions) that distinguish RT-AS from sporadic AS more clearly than the current panel data?

## Limitations

- Small cohort per histotype (n=12–44 per RT group); limits statistical power for rare alterations
- Targeted panel (MSK-IMPACT) cannot detect non-coding mutations, regulatory alterations, or full spectrum of structural variants
- Allele-specific *TP53* inactivation status not assessed
- Differences in prior therapies between patients may confound comparison
- No functional validation of identified targets
- Survival analysis underpowered; stage at diagnosis and treatment not controlled for
