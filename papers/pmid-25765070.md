## Summary

This landmark paper demonstrates that higher nonsynonymous tumor mutation burden (TMB) in NSCLC strongly predicts clinical benefit from pembrolizumab (anti-PD-1) in two independent cohorts totaling 34 patients. Molecular smoking signature (transversion-high), neoantigen burden, and DNA repair gene mutations each correlate with improved response and progression-free survival. In one exceptional responder, neoantigen-specific CD8+ T cell reactivity (to HERC1 P3278S) was directly induced by anti-PD-1 therapy and tracked tumor regression.

## Key Findings

### F1: High nonsynonymous TMB predicts durable clinical benefit from pembrolizumab

**Finding:** Patients with high nonsynonymous mutation burden derive significantly greater and more durable benefit from anti-PD-1 therapy than those with low burden.

**Detail:** In the discovery cohort, median nonsynonymous mutations were 302 (DCB) vs. 148 (NDB; Mann-Whitney P=0.02). ORR 63% vs. 0% (P=0.03); median PFS 14.5 vs. 3.7 months (HR 0.19, P=0.01). Validated in the independent cohort (244 vs. 125; P=0.04; PFS HR 0.15, P=0.006). Across both cohorts, TMB ≥178 nonsynonymous mutations had 75–86% sensitivity and 67–75% specificity for DCB. AUC=0.87 in the discovery cohort.

**Genes / cancer types:** `POLD1`, `POLE`, `MSH2`, `PRKDC`, `RAD17`, `KRAS` · NSCLC

### F2: Molecular smoking signature (transversion-high) outperforms self-reported smoking history as predictor of response

**Finding:** Tumors classified as transversion-high (TH; molecular smoking signature) respond far better to pembrolizumab than transversion-low (TL) tumors, whereas self-reported smoking status does not significantly discriminate responders.

**Detail:** ORR 56% (TH) vs. 17% (TL; P=0.03); DCB 77% vs. 22% (P=0.004); PFS HR 0.15 (95% CI 0.06–0.39; P=0.0001). Self-reported ever-smokers vs. never-smokers: DCB P=0.66, PFS P=0.29 (not significant). C-to-A transversions enriched in DCB tumors; C-to-T transitions enriched in NDB.

**Genes / cancer types:** Mutational signature (tobacco) · NSCLC

**Data availability:** `partial`

### F3: Neoantigen burden independently associates with PFS and correlates tightly with TMB

**Finding:** Higher candidate neoantigen burden (peptides with ≤500 nM HLA binding affinity) is independently associated with durable clinical benefit and improved PFS.

**Detail:** Median 112 neoantigens per tumor (range 8–610); Spearman ρ=0.91 with mutation burden (P<0.0001). DCB patients had significantly higher neoantigen burden (median 203 vs. 83; P=0.001). High neoantigen burden: median PFS 14.5 vs. 3.5 months (HR 0.23, 95% CI 0.09–0.58; P=0.002). Absolute neoantigen burden—not neoantigen frequency per mutation—correlated with response.

**Genes / cancer types:** Neoantigen load (HLA class I binding) · NSCLC

**Data availability:** `not-available`
> Neoantigen predictions require patient HLA alleles and mutation data; HLA typing not available in cBioPortal. Mutation data available; HLA-based neoantigen pipeline must be run externally.

**Tool support:** `custom`

### F4: DNA repair gene mutations (POLD1, POLE, MSH2) drive extreme TMB and exceptional responses

**Finding:** Deleterious mutations in DNA repair/replication genes explain ultra-high mutation burdens even in never-smokers and are enriched among exceptional pembrolizumab responders.

**Detail:** Three of the highest-burden responders carried deleterious mutations in POLD1 (E374K, C284Y — exonuclease proofreading domain), POLE, and MSH2. The POLD1 E374K never-smoker tumor had the highest TMB among all never-smokers (n=507 nonsynonymous mutations) with a C-to-T dominant signature distinct from tobacco mutagenesis. Additional DDR mutations (PRKDC nonsense, RAD17 nonsense) identified in responders.

**Genes / cancer types:** `POLD1`, `POLE`, `MSH2`, `PRKDC`, `RAD17` · NSCLC

### F5: KRAS mutation enriched among DCB patients, likely reflecting smoking-associated mutagenesis

**Finding:** KRAS mutations were significantly more common in DCB patients, likely as a surrogate of smoking-related high TMB rather than a direct immunotherapy biomarker.

**Detail:** KRAS mutated in 7/14 DCB vs. 1/17 NDB patients (Fisher exact, nominally significant). Association explained by strong link between tobacco carcinogenesis, KRAS mutation, and high nonsynonymous burden in NSCLC. No mutations in antigen-presentation pathway genes or PD-L1 (CD274) correlated with response or resistance.

**Genes / cancer types:** `KRAS`, `CD274` · NSCLC

**Data availability:** `available`

**Tool support:** `native`

### F6: Neoantigen-specific CD8+ T cell reactivity induced by pembrolizumab tracks tumor regression

**Finding:** Anti-PD-1 therapy can rapidly induce or expand neoantigen-specific CD8+ T cells detectable in peripheral blood, with kinetics that parallel tumor regression.

**Detail:** HERC1 P3278S neoantigen (ASNA**S**SAAK) reactive CD8+ T cells undetectable pre-treatment (limit 0.005%), rose to 0.040% by Day 21, maintained at Day 44, then returned to near-background as tumor regression plateaued. MHC-multimer+ cells displayed activated effector phenotype (CD45RA-CCR7-HLA-DR+LAG-3+). Intracellular cytokine staining confirmed polyfunctionality (IFNγ, TNFα, CD107a, CCL4) specific to mutant vs. wild-type peptide.

**Genes / cancer types:** `HERC1` · NSCLC

**Data availability:** `not-available`
> Requires longitudinal peripheral blood lymphocyte and T cell immunophenotyping data; not in cBioPortal.

**Tool support:** `custom`
> MHC-multimer screening and intracellular cytokine staining; external immunology lab assays required.

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Can TMB thresholds be validated prospectively as companion diagnostics across NSCLC subtypes and other tumor types?
- Does combined PD-L1 expression + TMB improve predictive power beyond either marker alone? (Data here limited by cohort size and PD-L1 enrichment in trial eligibility.)
- Can peripheral blood neoantigen-reactive T cell monitoring serve as a non-invasive response biomarker for anti-PD-1 therapy?
- Do other DNA repair deficiency subtypes (MSH6, MLH1, PMS2) confer similar TMB-driven ICI sensitivity in NSCLC?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Small cohorts (n=16 discovery, n=18 validation); underpowered for subgroup analyses.
- Trial largely enrolled PD-L1-expressing tumors (80%), limiting power to assess TMB/PD-L1 interaction.
- Self-reported smoking history incomplete and less discriminating than molecular classifier.
- Neoantigen immunogenicity demonstrated in a single exceptional responder; not generalizable across cohort.
- WES-based TMB not directly translatable to panel-based clinical TMB assays without harmonization.
