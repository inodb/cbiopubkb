## Summary

This data descriptor paper reports the creation of a clinically annotated biospecimen repository for NF1-associated nerve sheath tumors at Johns Hopkins Hospital, comprising 55 tumor samples from 23 patients with neurofibromatosis type 1 (NF1). The repository includes primary tumor tissue, patient-derived cell lines, and patient-derived xenografts (PDX), with paired exome sequencing and RNA-seq data publicly released through the NF Data Portal (Synapse). The resource is specifically designed to address the critical shortage of primary NF1 patient tissue for biological research and preclinical drug development.

## Key Findings

### F1: Comprehensive multi-omic biorepository for NF1-associated tumors

**Finding:** The Johns Hopkins NF1 biorepository provides paired exome and RNA-seq data for 55 NF1 tumor samples across the full spectrum of nerve sheath tumor types.

**Detail:** The cohort includes cNF, pNF, ANNUBP, and MPNST tumors from 23 unique NF1 patients. Exome sequencing used Agilent SureSelectXT V6 capture with HiSeq2500 125 bp paired-end sequencing. RNA-seq alignment was performed with Salmon v0.11.3 against GENCODE v29. Blood-derived germline DNA was available for most patients. All data are publicly released on Synapse (synapse.org/jhubiobank) and harmonized with existing pNF cell culture sequencing data.

**Genes / cancer types:** `NF1` · NF1-associated nerve sheath tumors (cNF, pNF, ANNUBP, MPNST)

**Data availability:** available
> Genomic and transcriptomic data are deposited in the NF Data Portal (Synapse) and linked to the cBioPortal study nst_nfosi_ntap.

**Tool support:** native
> Mutation profiles, copy number alterations, and gene expression can be explored in cBioPortal Study View and OncoPrint for the nst_nfosi_ntap study.

### F2: NF1 alterations and MPNST-specific somatic drivers confirmed across samples

**Finding:** NF1 copy-number loss at the NF1 locus on chromosome 17 is present in MPNST tumors, and MPNST-specific somatic drivers (CDKN2A, TP53, EED/SUZ12, ATRX) are identified.

**Detail:** Genomic profiling of the full cohort showed NF1 germline alterations in all patients (consistent with NF1 diagnosis). In MPNST samples, somatic copy-number reduction at the NF1 locus (chromosome 17) was confirmed, with a copy ratio reduction from 1.0 (blood) to 0.5 (tumor), preserved in derived cell lines and PDX models. Key MPNST somatic drivers detected include *CDKN2A*, *TP53*, *EED*/*SUZ12* (PRC2 complex), and *ATRX*. PRC2 complex gene mutations were notably absent in non-MPNST samples, consistent with their published role as MPNST-specific events.

**Genes / cancer types:** `NF1`, `CDKN2A`, `TP53`, `EED`, `SUZ12`, `ATRX` · MPNST, Plexiform neurofibroma

**Data availability:** available
> Somatic mutation and copy number data are available in nst_nfosi_ntap on cBioPortal.

**Tool support:** native
> Gene mutations and copy number can be visualized in cBioPortal OncoPrint and Mutations tab.

**Concepts:** #CDKN2A #TP53 #copy-number #epigenetics #chromatin #germline #NF1 #MAPK

### F3: PDX models successfully generated for MPNST with 50% engraftment rate

**Finding:** Patient-derived xenograft (PDX) models were successfully established from MPNST tumors at a 50% engraftment rate, with faithful recapitulation of the original tumor's genomic profile.

**Detail:** Two to ten NOD scid gamma mice were implanted per tumor for known pNF and MPNST cases. The overall PDX success rate was 50% for MPNST. Genomic fidelity was confirmed by showing that SNVs and copy-number profiles at the NF1 locus in the original tumor were preserved in derived cell lines and PDX models (Fig. 3c/3d in the paper). Cell lines were also established and validated for consistent growth rates.

**Genes / cancer types:** `NF1` · MPNST

**Data availability:** not-available
> PDX engraftment and preclinical pharmacological data are not available in cBioPortal; in vivo data requires the Synapse repository and direct collaboration.

**Tool support:** custom
> PDX model data require external resources (Synapse) and preclinical experimental pipelines.

### F4: RNA-seq enables transcriptomic comparison across nerve sheath tumor subtypes

**Finding:** RNA-seq data from 28 tumor samples (after QC removal of one high-duplication sample) provide a transcriptomic resource for comparing gene expression across NF1-associated tumor types.

**Detail:** RNA-seq was performed on flash-frozen tumor specimens aligned with Salmon v0.11.3 (GENCODE v29). One sample (2-025 neurofibroma) was excluded due to >90% duplication rate, leaving 28 samples for transcriptomic analysis. Principal component analysis of normalized counts separated samples by tumor type (cell line, xenograft, tumor tissue), supporting sample quality. Raw counts were assembled via tximport and are available on Synapse, harmonized with previously published pNF cell culture RNA-seq data.

**Genes / cancer types:** — · cNF, pNF, ANNUBP, MPNST

**Data availability:** partial
> RNA-seq data are on Synapse (not directly in cBioPortal); harmonization with cBioPortal gene expression data possible but requires external download.

**Tool support:** custom
> Full RNA-seq expression analysis requires downloading from Synapse and running external pipelines; cBioPortal may have gene expression from this cohort in nst_nfosi_ntap.

### F5: Clinical annotation captures full NF1 phenotypic spectrum

**Finding:** The repository includes detailed clinical annotation across the full NF1 phenotypic spectrum, including dermal neurofibromas, plexiform neurofibromas, optic glioma, and MPNST status.

**Detail:** Clinical data include NF1 diagnosis age, dermal neurofibroma burden (absent/scattered/dense), plexiform neurofibroma presence, optic glioma status, MPNST diagnosis, and family history of NF1. Data are stored in a de-identified, password-protected database and shared via Synapse with appropriate data use agreements. Of 23 patients, 7 had MPNST (30%), 21 had plexiform neurofibromas. The repository accrues ~20 patients per year from the Johns Hopkins Comprehensive Neurofibromatosis Center.

**Genes / cancer types:** `NF1` · NF1-associated tumors

**Data availability:** available
> Clinical metadata are available on Synapse and potentially in the cBioPortal study nst_nfosi_ntap.

**Tool support:** native
> Clinical attributes (tumor type, NF1 phenotype) can be filtered in cBioPortal Study View.

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Can cBioPortal be used to compare mutation frequencies across NF1 tumor subtypes (cNF vs. pNF vs. MPNST) in the nst_nfosi_ntap study?
- Do PRC2 (EED/SUZ12) mutations in MPNST co-occur with CDKN2A loss, and can this co-mutation pattern predict prognosis?
- Are there transcriptomic signatures distinguishing pNF from ANNUBP that predict malignant transformation risk?
- Can ATRX mutations serve as a biomarker for MPNST diagnosis in the context of NF1?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Small cohort (n=23 patients, 55 samples); sample size limits statistical power for genomic comparisons between tumor subtypes.
- PDX success rate for MPNST was only 50%, and pNF PDX engraftment was not established.
- One RNA-seq sample excluded due to high duplication (2-025), reducing transcriptomic cohort to 28 samples.
- No germline sequencing was available for all patients (blood insufficient in rare cases; adjacent normal tissue used instead).
- Repository data require Synapse access and institutional data use agreements for full utilization.
- Single-institution cohort at Johns Hopkins; may not capture full NF1 phenotypic diversity.
