## Summary

Landmark TCGA multi-platform genomic characterization of 489 stage II–IV high-grade serous ovarian adenocarcinomas (HGS-OvCa), with WES of 316 tumors. Key findings: TP53 is mutated in 96% of tumors; homologous recombination (HR) defects affect ~50% (including germline/somatic BRCA1/2 mutations in 22%, BRCA1 promoter methylation in 11.5%, and alterations in other HR genes); four transcriptional subtypes are defined (Immunoreactive, Differentiated, Proliferative, Mesenchymal); a 193-gene survival signature is validated across independent datasets; FOXM1 transcriptional network is activated in 87% of cases; and RB1 and PI3K/RAS pathways are deregulated in 67% and 45% respectively. The Notch pathway is altered in 23%. Data available in `ov_tcga_pub` on cBioPortal.

## Key Findings

### F1: TP53 mutated in 96% of HGS-OvCa — near-universal driver with simple mutational spectrum overall

**Finding:** TP53 was mutated in 303/316 (96%) tumors sequenced by WES, with only 9 additional significantly mutated genes (BRCA1, BRCA2, NF1, RB1, CDK12, CSMD3, FAT3, GABRA6) identified at statistically recurrent rates — each in only 2–6% of tumors. The overall mutational spectrum was remarkably simple compared to other TCGA cancer types.

**Detail:** Two independent algorithms identified significantly mutated genes above background mutation rates. CSMD3, FAT3, and GABRA6 appear to lack expression in HGS-OvCa or fallopian tube tissue, reducing confidence in their functional relevance. CDK12 (involved in RNA splicing regulation, implicated in lung/intestine tumors) had 9 mutations, 5 of which were nonsense or indel (loss-of-function), with 4 missense mutations clustered in the kinase domain. Comparison with COSMIC and OMIM databases identified rare but important driver mutations: BRAF (N581S), PIK3CA (E545K, H1047R), KRAS (G12D), NRAS (Q61R) — each in <1% of tumors. In striking contrast to the simple mutation landscape, HGS-OvCa showed a remarkably high degree of genomic disarray: 8 recurrent chromosome arm gains, 22 losses, 63 focal amplification regions, and 50 focal deletions.

**Genes / cancer types:** TP53, BRCA1, BRCA2, NF1, RB1, CDK12, PIK3CA, KRAS, BRAF · High-grade serous ovarian carcinoma

### F2: Homologous recombination deficiency in ~50% of HGS-OvCa — rationale for PARP inhibitors

**Finding:** Approximately 50% of HGS-OvCa have defective homologous recombination (HR): germline BRCA1 mutations in 9%, germline BRCA2 mutations in 8%; somatic BRCA1/2 mutations in an additional 3% each; BRCA1 promoter hypermethylation in 11.5%; and HR gene alterations including EMSY amplification (8%), PTEN deletion/mutation (7%), RAD51C methylation (3%), ATM/ATR mutation (2%), and Fanconi anemia gene mutations (5%). BRCA1/2 mutations confer better OS than BRCA-WT; epigenetically silenced BRCA1 does not.

**Detail:** BRCA1 promoter hypermethylation is mutually exclusive with BRCA1/2 mutations (P=4.4×10⁻⁴, Fisher's exact test). Epigenetically silenced BRCA1 cases had survival similar to BRCA1/2-WT (median OS 41.5 vs. 41.9 months, P=0.69), while BRCA1/2-mutated cases showed better survival — suggesting that the mechanism of BRCA1 inactivation determines prognosis and potentially PARP inhibitor benefit. CCNE1 amplification (present in >20% of tumors) was inversely associated with BRCA inactivation events (8% vs. 26% in BRCA-WT, FDR-adjusted P=0.0048). CCNE1 amplification showed no survival disadvantage after accounting for BRCA-mutated cases' better survival.

**Genes / cancer types:** BRCA1, BRCA2, EMSY, PTEN, RAD51C, ATM, ATR, FANCA, FANCC, CCNE1 · High-grade serous ovarian carcinoma

### F3: Four transcriptional subtypes — Immunoreactive, Differentiated, Proliferative, Mesenchymal — with distinct pathway enrichments

**Finding:** NMF consensus clustering of 11,864 integrated mRNA expression measurements identified four robust transcriptional subtypes: Immunoreactive (CXCL10, CXCL11, CXCR3), Differentiated (MUC16, MUC1, SLPI), Proliferative (MCM2, PCNA, HMGA2, SOX11), and Mesenchymal (HOX genes, FAP, ANGPTL1/2). The same four subtypes were independently recapitulated in the Tothill et al. external dataset.

**Detail:** The Immunoreactive subtype, characterized by T-cell chemokine ligands, showed the highest immune infiltration signal and later emerged as the most prognostically favorable. The Mesenchymal subtype, with high stromal gene expression (myofibroblast and pericyte markers), has consistently been associated with shorter survival in follow-up analyses. Survival duration did not differ significantly across transcriptional subtypes in the original TCGA dataset alone (insufficient follow-up), but the 193-gene prognostic signature derived from this same dataset validated across four external cohorts with significant OS association. MYC amplification rate was lower in the Proliferative group; the Immunoreactive subtype had increased 3q26.2 (MECOM) amplification. Three miRNA subtypes partially overlapped mRNA subtypes: miRNA subtype 1 (Proliferative-overlap) had the longest survival.

**Genes / cancer types:** CXCL10, CXCL11, CXCR3, MUC16, MUC1, SLPI, MCM2, PCNA, HMGA2, SOX11, MECOM, MYC · High-grade serous ovarian carcinoma

### F4: FOXM1 transcriptional network activated in 87% — RB1 and PI3K/RAS pathways deregulated in majority

**Finding:** PARADIGM pathway analysis identified the FOXM1 transcription factor network as significantly activated in 87% of HGS-OvCa. FOXM1 target genes (AURB, CCNB1, BIRC5, CDC25, PLK1) are consistently overexpressed but not amplified — consistent with transcriptional regulation via TP53 mutation (which normally represses FOXM1 following DNA damage). RB1 pathway deregulation occurs in 67% and PI3K/RAS pathway deregulation in 45% of cases. Notch pathway alteration (HotNet analysis) in 23%.

**Detail:** FOXM1 overexpression is driven by TP53 loss of its repressive function — directly connecting the near-universal TP53 mutation to a transcriptional proliferation program. The RB1 pathway deregulation encompasses RB1 deletion/mutation, CCNE1 amplification, and CDK12 mutation. PI3K/RAS alterations span PIK3CA mutations, PIK3R1 mutations, PTEN deletion, KRAS and BRAF mutations, and IGF pathway components. The Notch pathway (altered in 23% by HotNet network analysis of protein-protein interactions) represents a drug target with emerging inhibitors. GISTIC analysis identified 22 targetable amplified genes including MECOM, MAPK1, CCNE1, and KRAS (each amplified in ≥10% of cases), and 113 total significant focal CNA regions across the genome.

**Genes / cancer types:** FOXM1, AURB, CCNB1, BIRC5, PLK1, CDC25, RB1, CCNE1, CDK12, PIK3CA, PIK3R1, PTEN, KRAS, BRAF, MECOM, MAPK1 · High-grade serous ovarian carcinoma

### F5: Survival-predictive 193-gene expression signature validated across four independent cohorts

**Finding:** A 193-gene transcriptional signature (108 genes correlating with poor survival, 85 with good survival by univariate Cox regression) defined from 215 TCGA training samples significantly predicted OS in an independent 255-sample TCGA test set and in three additional external HGS-OvCa expression datasets. Kaplan-Meier analysis showed statistically significant separation in all four validation cohorts.

**Detail:** The signature score reflects the balance of proliferative and immune gene expression patterns. Patients with BRCA1/2 mutations consistently scored favorably within the signature framework. DNA methylation consensus clustering also identified four subtypes (modest but significant overlap with mRNA subtypes, P<2.2×10⁻¹⁶, Chi-square), showing differential association with patient age, BRCA inactivation events, and survival. Key epigenetically silenced genes include AMT, CCL21, SPARCL1 (hypermethylated in >80% of tumors), and BRCA1 (11.5%). RAB25 — previously reported as amplified and overexpressed — also appeared epigenetically silenced in a subset of tumors (hypermethylation).

**Genes / cancer types:** (193-gene signature; key members: BRCA1, BRCA2, CCNE1, immune genes) · High-grade serous ovarian carcinoma

## Open Questions

- Does the mechanism of BRCA1 inactivation (germline mutation vs. somatic mutation vs. promoter methylation) predict differential PARP inhibitor response, and if so, should clinical trial eligibility be stratified accordingly?
- Can the FOXM1 transcriptional program be directly targeted, or does PLK1/AURB/BIRC5 inhibition represent viable indirect strategies in HGS-OvCa?
- What explains the mutual exclusivity of CCNE1 amplification and BRCA inactivation, and does CCNE1-amplified HGS-OvCa represent a distinct therapeutic vulnerability (e.g., CDK2 inhibition)?
- Are the Immunoreactive and Mesenchymal transcriptional subtypes predictive of differential response to platinum-based chemotherapy, anti-angiogenic therapy, or immune checkpoint inhibitors?
- Can circulating tumor DNA or plasma biomarkers recapitulate the key genomic features (BRCA status, HR deficiency, CCNE1 amplification) identified in this tissue-based analysis?

## Limitations

- Clinical follow-up was short (median 30 months) at the time of analysis; survival analyses are preliminary and likely underpowered for subtype-specific OS differences
- DNA methylation profiling used Illumina HumanMethylation27 arrays covering ~27K CpG sites — a fraction of the genome subsequently covered by 450K/850K arrays
- WES performed on only 316 of 489 tumors; the remaining 173 lack mutation data for integrative analyses
- Survival signature (193 genes) was trained on 215 TCGA samples and validated on the remaining 255 TCGA samples — partially overlapping cohort; external validation is reassuring but not fully independent
- As a pure somatic analysis, germline HR deficiency beyond BRCA1/2 (e.g., RAD51C, PALB2) may be underestimated given the WES approach and germline calling methods available at the time
- Tissue collection from multiple centers introduces potential batch effects in expression and methylation data, though integrative analysis mitigated platform-specific effects
