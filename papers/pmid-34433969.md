## Summary

This study integrated DNA copy number, somatic point mutations, DNA methylation, and mRNA expression from 121 meningioma discovery samples (plus 80 validation samples) to define four novel consensus molecular groups (MG1–4): immunogenic, NF2-wildtype, hypermetabolic, and proliferative. The MG classification predicted recurrence-free survival significantly better than WHO grade alone or any single molecular platform. Each group showed distinctive biology with potential therapeutic implications, including selective sensitivity of proliferative (MG4) meningiomas to the HDAC inhibitor vorinostat validated in patient-derived cell lines and intracranial xenografts.

## Key Findings

### F1: Four integrative molecular groups supersede WHO grade for predicting recurrence

**Finding:** Multiplatform integration (COCA) of copy number, methylation, and RNA-seq data identifies four molecular groups (MG1–4) that predict recurrence-free survival better than WHO grade or any single molecular platform.

**Detail:** COCA combining six copy number, six methylation, and six RNA clusters converged to four stable molecular groups. MG3 and MG4 had significantly shorter recurrence-free survival than MG1 and MG2 (log-rank P=5×10⁻¹⁵). MG classification was independently associated with recurrence even after adjusting for WHO grade, extent of resection, and adjuvant radiotherapy (multivariable Cox regression). Brier prediction curve confirmed MG classification was superior to WHO grade and DKFZ methylation classification for predicting time to recurrence.

### F2: Distinct somatic mutation patterns define MG groups

**Finding:** NF2 mutations are nearly universal in MG1 but rare in MG2, while MG2 is exclusively defined by non-NF2 mutations (TRAF7, AKT1, KLF4, POLR2A); MG3/4 harbor novel chromatin/epigenetic driver mutations.

**Detail:** *NF2* mutations present in 88% of MG1 vs. 9% of MG2 (Fisher's exact P=5.9×10⁻⁸). Non-NF2 mutations *TRAF7* (25%), *AKT1* (13%), *KLF4* (13%), and *POLR2A* (6%) were exclusive to MG2 (P=1.20×10⁻⁸). Novel recurrent driver mutations were identified in *KDM6A* and *CHD2* (chromatin remodeling), *PTEN* (tumor suppressor), and subthreshold hits in *CREBBP*, *FBXW7*, and *RB1*. These novel mutations were enriched in MG3/4 vs. MG1/2 (Fisher's exact P=0.002). MG4 had significantly higher mutational burden than MG1–3 (Kruskal-Wallis P=1.6×10⁻³).

### F3: MG3 and MG4 show high chromosomal instability; MG4 has unique gains and losses

**Finding:** Aggressive meningioma molecular groups (MG3, MG4) are characterized by high aneuploidy and genomic disruption, with MG4 showing distinct chromosome 1q gain and chromosome 10 loss.

**Detail:** MG1 tumors were relatively diploid except for uniform 22q loss paired with NF2 point mutations (biallelic NF2 inactivation). MG3 and MG4 showed losses at 22q (93%/86%), 1p (77%/89%), 6q (30%/38%), and chromosomes 14 and 18. MG4-specific events: 1q gain (34% vs. 2% in MG3, P=2.9×10⁻⁴) and chromosome 10 loss (38% vs. 14% in MG3, P=0.025). Median percent genome altered was 3.5% (MG1), 9.6% (MG2), 16.9% (MG3), 19.5% (MG4) (Kruskal-Wallis P=5.2×10⁻⁶). Non-recurrent interchromosomal fusion events were more frequent in MG3/4.

### F4: MG1 (immunogenic) is enriched for immune infiltrates; MG4 (proliferative) shows immune exclusion

**Finding:** Molecular group biology translates to distinct tumor microenvironment compositions, with MG1 enriched for immune cell infiltration and MG4 showing immune exclusion and proliferative pathway enrichment.

**Detail:** MG1 (immunogenic) tumors showed enrichment in immune regulation and signaling pathways and higher ESTIMATE immune scores. MG4 (proliferative) tumors showed downregulation of immune signatures and enrichment for cell-cycle regulation pathways (MYC, FOXM1, E2F transcription factor networks), mTORC1, CDKs, and kinesins. MG3 (hypermetabolic) was enriched for macromolecular metabolism pathways. MG2 (NF2-wildtype benign) showed vascular/angiogenic pathway enrichment. Single-nucleus RNA-seq confirmed enrichment of cycling neoplastic cells in MG3/4 (P<0.022) and immune cells in MG1 (P=0.018).

### F5: Vorinostat selectively reduces viability of MG4 (proliferative) meningiomas in vitro and in vivo

**Finding:** The HDAC inhibitor vorinostat selectively kills MG4 patient-derived meningioma cell lines and significantly attenuates tumor growth and improves survival in intracranial MG4 xenografts.

**Detail:** FDA-approved drug mapping to MG4-upregulated pathways identified vorinostat (HDAC inhibitor) as a candidate. Vorinostat reduced cell viability >50% in MG4-aligned patient-derived cell lines, but not in MG1 or MG3 cell lines (P<0.05, Student's t-test). The DNA methyltransferase inhibitor 5-azacytidine had no effect on any MG cell lines. In intracranial MG4 xenografts, vorinostat significantly reduced tumor volume (MRI-measured) and improved overall survival vs. control mice (log-rank P<0.05).

### F6: Proteogenomic markers validate molecular group biology and enable IHC-based classification

**Finding:** Proteomic markers S100B (MG1), SCGN (MG2), ACADL (MG3), and MCM2 (MG4) were identified and validated by immunohistochemistry, enabling molecular group identification in standard neuropathology labs.

**Detail:** Whole-cell proteomics quantified 6,568 unique protein groups in 96 tumors. Protein-based pathway enrichment matched mRNA-based enrichment with high concordance (Pearson ρ=0.49, OR for concordance vs. discordance = 213.17 for survival-associated genes, P<2.2×10⁻¹⁶). Group-specific abundant proteins were: S100B (MG1, immunogenic), SCGN (MG2, NF2-wildtype), ACADL (MG3, hypermetabolic), MCM2 (MG4, proliferative). IHC with unbiased digital quantification confirmed strong concordance with proteomic data and discrimination between MGs (AUC >0.8 for each marker).

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Can the MG4 (proliferative) phenotype be identified by somatic genomic features alone (high TMB, RB1/PTEN loss, 1q gain) in cBioPortal without methylation/proteomics data?
- Do patients with MG2 (TRAF7/AKT1/KLF4 mutations) respond to PI3K/AKT inhibitors? This could be explored by co-occurrence analysis in mng_utoronto_2021.
- Is NF2 mutation status a reliable surrogate for MG1 classification in a cBioPortal dataset?
- Can survival differences between MG3 and MG4 be recapitulated in larger external meningioma datasets?
- Are there actionable immune checkpoint targets specifically expressed in MG1 (immunogenic) tumors?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Single-institution discovery cohort (n=121); validation cohort (n=80) uses mRNA signatures only (not full multiplatform integration).
- Enrichment for rare higher-grade (WHO grade 2–3) tumors; may overrepresent aggressive phenotypes relative to community practice.
- Multi-platform integration (COCA) is computationally complex and not yet clinically deployable without IHC surrogates.
- Proteomics performed in only 96/121 discovery samples; single-cell data limited to 8 tumors.
- Methylation-based classification (DKFZ) was used for comparison but not fully independent from the EPIC array data used in COCA.
- IHC marker validation requires further independent prospective testing before clinical adoption.
