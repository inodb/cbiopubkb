## Summary

Primary CNS lymphoma (PCNSL) is an aggressive non-Hodgkin lymphoma confined to the CNS; its genomic landscape relative to systemic DLBCL was poorly characterised. This study combined aCGH, whole exome sequencing, FISH, and targeted sequencing in 19 immunocompetent PCNSL patients, revealing a highly complex karyotype (median 21 CNA/patient) and novel recurrent biallelic inactivation of *TOX* and *PRKCD*—alterations absent in nodal DLBCL. BCR/TLR/NF-κB pathways were collectively altered in >93% of cases, and deletion of 6q21 (*PRDM1*) was associated with significantly shorter overall survival, establishing a distinct and therapeutically actionable genomic signature for PCNSL.

## Key Findings

### F1: Complex karyotype with a high burden of copy-number alterations
**Finding:** PCNSL displays a genomically complex karyotype with a median of 21 copy-number alterations (CNA) per patient (range 10–47), including 16 chromosomal abnormalities present in >25% of cases.

**Detail:** Of 18 cases analysed by aCGH, 10 recurrent deletions and 6 recurrent gains were identified. The most prevalent losses were del 9p21.3 (*CDKN2A/CDKN2B*, 83%), del 6q21 (*PRDM1*, 55%), del 6q23 (*TNFAIP3*, 55%), del 6p21 (*HLA* loci, 50%), and del 6q14.1 (*TMEM30A*, 44%). Recurrent gains included 3p14 (*FOXP1*), 3q27 (*BCL6*), 7q21–q31 (39%), 11q (28%), 12q (50%), and 19q13 (33%). Gain of 12q was mutually exclusive with del 10p15.3-p14 and gains of 3p14 and 11q (*P* = 0.0004), suggesting two distinct chromosomal evolution paths.

**Genes / cancer types:** `CDKN2A` · `CDKN2B` · `PRDM1` · `TNFAIP3` · `TMEM30A` · `FOXP1` · `BCL6` · Primary CNS Lymphoma

**Data availability:** available — CNA data deposited in GEO (GSE28952) and represented in the pcnsl_mayo_2015 cBioPortal study.

### F2: Homozygous deletion of CDKN2A is the single most frequent alteration
**Finding:** Homozygous deletion (HD) of *CDKN2A* and *CDKN2B* at 9p21.3 was identified in 56% of cases, with an additional 21% showing monoallelic loss, making it the most common high-impact focal event in PCNSL.

**Detail:** Overall, 83% of PCNSL cases harboured some form of deletion at 9p21.3 (HD in 10/18 cases; monoallelic in another ~4 cases). This frequency substantially exceeds rates seen in nodal DLBCL subtypes and underscores the importance of cell-cycle checkpoint loss in CNS-confined lymphomagenesis. The HD was resolved to a minimal deleted region of ~80 kb by high-density aCGH.

**Genes / cancer types:** `CDKN2A` · `CDKN2B` · Primary CNS Lymphoma

**Data availability:** available — CDKN2A deletions are present in the pcnsl_mayo_2015 study.

**Tool support:** native — visible via cBioPortal CNA tab and OncoPrint.

### F3: Novel PCNSL-specific biallelic inactivation of TOX and PRKCD
**Finding:** Recurrent biallelic inactivation of *TOX* (thymocyte selection-associated HMG-box) and *PRKCD* (protein kinase C delta) was identified exclusively in PCNSL, establishing them as CNS-lymphoma-specific tumour suppressors.

**Detail:** For *TOX*: 11% of cases showed HD and 17% showed focal monoallelic deletion at 8q12.1; no HD of TOX was found across 754 cancer cell lines (including 127 haematopoietic lines) in public datasets. For *PRKCD*: splice-donor mutations were identified in 2/10 WES cases (one also carrying a p.W608S missense), each with concurrent monoallelic deletion, confirming biallelic loss; focal monoallelic deletions at 3p21.31 were also found in 3 additional aCGH cases. PRKCD mutations were absent from nodal DLBCL and found in only 1/1058 haematological malignancies in COSMIC (0.01%). Both findings were confirmed by Sanger sequencing and FISH.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TOX` · `PRKCD` · Primary CNS Lymphoma

**Data availability:** available — mutations and CNA for these genes are in the pcnsl_mayo_2015 study.

**Tool support:** native — viewable per-gene in cBioPortal mutation/CNA views.

### F4: High prevalence of MYD88 L265P mutations — nearly double the rate in ABC-DLBCL
**Finding:** Activating *MYD88* mutations were identified in 79% of PCNSL (11/14 cases), predominantly the L265P hotspot, a rate approximately twice that reported in nodal ABC-DLBCL.

**Detail:** By WES, 70% of 10 cases were mutated in *MYD88*. An additional 4 cases were screened by IonTorrent targeted deep sequencing (average 2,005× coverage), yielding an overall prevalence of 79% (11/14). The dominant variant was L265P; two cases carried rarer variants at residues 217 (V217F) and 232 (M232T). Three of four *CD79B*-mutated cases also harboured *MYD88* mutations, suggesting cooperative BCR/TLR pathway activation. Prior PCNSL studies reported rates of 38–75%, and nodal ABC-DLBCL rates are typically ~30–40%.

**Genes / cancer types:** `MYD88` · `CD79B` · Primary CNS Lymphoma

**Data availability:** available — MYD88 and CD79B mutation data are in pcnsl_mayo_2015.

**Tool support:** native — mutation lollipop plots and OncoPrint accessible in cBioPortal.

### F5: BCR/TLR/NF-κB pathway convergence in >93% of PCNSL
**Finding:** Integrated copy-number and sequencing data showed that components of the B-cell receptor, Toll-like receptor, and NF-κB signalling pathways are collectively altered in >93% of PCNSL cases.

**Detail:** Activating mutations in *MYD88* (79%), *CD79B* (40%), *CARD11* (30%), and deletions of *TNFAIP3* (55% monoallelic loss; HD in individual cases) and *TBL1XR1* were identified as the main contributors. *PIM1* was mutated in 40% and *BTG2* in 30% of WES cases. *GNA13* and *BRAF* were mutated in 20% and 10%, respectively. The near-universal disruption of these interconnected pathways highlights their central role in PCNSL pathogenesis and their potential as therapeutic targets (e.g., BTK inhibitors, TLR pathway inhibitors).

**Genes / cancer types:** `MYD88` · `CD79B` · `CARD11` · `TNFAIP3` · `TBL1XR1` · `PIM1` · `BTG2` · `GNA13` · `BRAF` · Primary CNS Lymphoma

**Data availability:** available — mutation data for these pathway genes are in pcnsl_mayo_2015.

**Tool support:** native — pathway enrichment and OncoPrint views in cBioPortal; api for programmatic pathway queries.

### F6: Deletion of 6q21 (PRDM1) associated with significantly shorter overall survival
**Finding:** Monoallelic or biallelic loss of 6q21 (*PRDM1*) was associated with significantly shorter overall survival in univariate analysis (*P* = 0.001).

**Detail:** 6q21 (*PRDM1*) was lost in 55% of cases (10/18 by aCGH); HD was detected in 2 cases. Kaplan-Meier analysis showed a highly significant survival difference between patients with and without this deletion. The result must be interpreted cautiously given the small cohort size (N=19) and heterogeneous treatment regimens received. Loss of PRDM1, a transcriptional repressor involved in terminal B-cell differentiation, may impair apoptotic checkpoints. Additional recurrent chromosome 6 losses (6p21 HLA loss in 50%, 6q23 TNFAIP3 in 55%, 6q14.1 TMEM30A in 44%) contribute to widespread 6q haploinsufficiency in PCNSL.

**Genes / cancer types:** `PRDM1` · `TNFAIP3` · `TMEM30A` · Primary CNS Lymphoma

**Data availability:** available — CNA data for chromosome 6 regions are in pcnsl_mayo_2015; survival data may be partial given small cohort.

**Tool support:** native — CNA and survival plots in cBioPortal; survival analysis requires clinical data linkage.

**Concepts:** #survival #prognosis #copy-number #CNS-lymphoma

### F7: IgH-BCL6 translocations and BCL6/FOXP1 gains link PCNSL to ABC-DLBCL biology
**Finding:** IGH-BCL6 translocations were identified in 2/11 PCNSL cases (18%), and gains of 3q27 (*BCL6*) and 3p14 (*FOXP1*) were present in a substantial subset, connecting PCNSL to post-germinal-centre ABC-DLBCL oncogenic mechanisms.

**Detail:** FISH confirmed IGH-BCL6 fusions in 2 cases; breakpoints in aCGH data for BCL6 (3q27 gain) and FOXP1 (3p14 gain) were consistent with unbalanced chromosomal translocations placing these genes under immunoglobulin regulatory elements. Gain of 3p14 (*FOXP1*) and 11q were found exclusively in cases lacking gain of 12q (*P* = 0.0004), defining a genomically distinct subgroup. High-copy amplifications were rare overall; focal amplifications of *BCL10* (1p31), *CARD11* (7p22), *TCF4* (18q21), and *TCF3* (19p13) were each found in single cases.

**Genes / cancer types:** `BCL6` · `FOXP1` · `BCL10` · `CARD11` · `TCF4` · Primary CNS Lymphoma

**Data availability:** available — CNA data in pcnsl_mayo_2015; FISH results are not directly accessible in cBioPortal.

**Tool support:** native — CNA gains visible in cBioPortal; api for querying specific loci.

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Does biallelic loss of *TOX* directly impair immune surveillance in the CNS microenvironment, and could restoring TOX expression (or downstream signalling) sensitise PCNSL to immunotherapy?
- Given that *PRKCD* inactivation is essentially unique to PCNSL among haematological malignancies, does the CNS immune-privileged microenvironment create a selective pressure for loss of this pro-apoptotic kinase?
- Can the two genomically distinct subgroups (12q gain vs. FOXP1/11q gain) be validated in larger cohorts, and do they predict differential response to BTK inhibitors or high-dose methotrexate–based regimens?
- What is the functional consequence of *MYD88* L265P co-occurring with *CD79B* mutations (present in 3/4 CD79B-mutated cases)—does this cooperation explain the extreme NF-κB dependency seen in PCNSL compared with nodal ABC-DLBCL?
- Does deletion of 6q21 (*PRDM1*) remain an independent prognostic factor after adjusting for treatment, age, and performance status in a larger, uniformly treated cohort?
- Could the near-universal BCR/TLR/NF-κB pathway alteration make ibrutinib (BTK inhibitor) or TLR pathway antagonists effective in PCNSL, and which co-alterations predict resistance?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Small cohort (N=19 patients), limiting statistical power for survival analyses and subgroup comparisons; the OS association with 6q21 deletion must be interpreted cautiously.
- Heterogeneous treatment regimens across patients preclude robust outcome analysis; no uniform treatment protocol was applied.
- The majority of samples were FFPE, imposing constraints on DNA yield and quality; whole genome amplification was required for 3 frozen-tissue cases, potentially introducing amplification bias.
- WES was performed on only 10 of 19 patients, so mutation frequencies for rarer events are based on a small denomintor and may not be representative.
- Absence of matched normal tissue in most cases (tumour-free tissue available for only 9/19) required reliance on computational germline filtering, which may introduce false-positive or false-negative somatic calls.
- No functional validation of *TOX* or *PRKCD* loss-of-function was performed in this study; causal roles in PCNSL pathogenesis remain to be demonstrated experimentally.
