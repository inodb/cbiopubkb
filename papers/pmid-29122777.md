## Summary

This study reports the first large prospective application of MSK-IMPACT targeted sequencing (n=295 patients, all stage IV) in esophagogastric adenocarcinoma to identify predictive biomarkers of response to platinum-based chemotherapy, trastuzumab, and immune checkpoint inhibitors. Key findings include: MSI-H tumors (3% of metastatic cases) are intrinsically chemotherapy-resistant but respond durably to immunotherapy; *ERBB2* amplification level by sequencing outperforms IHC/FISH as a trastuzumab biomarker; co-alterations in RTK/RAS/PI3K pathways confer intrinsic trastuzumab resistance; and clonal selection for *ERBB2*-negative subpopulations drives acquired resistance. EBV positivity was associated with a dramatic durable complete response to immunotherapy. All genomic and clinical data are publicly available in cBioPortal.

## Key Findings

### F1: MSI-H esophagogastric cancers are chemotherapy-resistant but immunotherapy-sensitive

**Finding:** MSI-H tumors (3% of metastatic esophagogastric cancers) have significantly shorter PFS on platinum-based first-line chemotherapy but achieve durable responses to immune checkpoint inhibitors, supporting early redirection to immunotherapy.

**Detail:** MSI-H patients (n=9, 3%) had significantly shorter PFS on first-line platinum/fluoropyrimidine (median 4.8 vs. 6.9 months for non-MSI-H; HR=0.4; P=0.027, log-rank). Of 5 MSI-H patients receiving immunotherapy, durable responses (>12 months) were observed in 4 (80%). MSI-H frequency was significantly lower in metastatic (3%) than in the TCGA early-stage cohort (16%; P=8e-10), as MSI-H is a favorable prognostic marker in early-stage disease. MSI status was robustly detected by MSIsensor from NGS data (score ≥10 = MSI-H).

**Genes / cancer types:** MSI-H (mismatch repair deficiency) · Esophagogastric adenocarcinoma

### F2: ERBB2 amplification level by sequencing is a stronger predictor of trastuzumab benefit than IHC/FISH

**Finding:** The quantitative level of *ERBB2* amplification by NGS—not merely its presence or absence—predicts PFS on trastuzumab, with the highest amplification quartile achieving median PFS of 24.3 months. Co-alterations in RTK/RAS/PI3K pathways confer intrinsic resistance.

**Detail:** Among 50 HER2+ patients (by IHC/FISH) receiving first-line trastuzumab, patients with the highest quartile of *ERBB2* amplification by NGS had median PFS of 24.3 months. Those with co-alterations in RTK/RAS/PI3K pathways had significantly shorter PFS (median 8.4 months; multivariate analysis P<0.05). Overall concordance between IHC/FISH and NGS was 93.7% (PPV 90%, NPV 96.9%). The 4 patients with discordant NGS-negative results had significantly shorter PFS on trastuzumab (median 5.8 vs. 14.0 months; P=1e-6). NGS-based *ERBB2* quantification thus captures tumor heterogeneity and dose effect better than binary IHC/FISH.

### F3: Clonal selection for ERBB2-negative subclones and acquired co-mutations drive trastuzumab resistance

**Finding:** Acquired trastuzumab resistance is driven by selection of pre-existing *ERBB2*-negative clones, acquisition of an *ERBB2* exon 16 deletion (delta-16 HER2, constitutively active), and emergence of *KRAS* and *PIK3CA* co-mutations.

**Detail:** Analysis of 23 matched pre/post-trastuzumab tumor pairs identified: (1) Loss of *ERBB2* amplification in 7/44 (16%) post-progression samples confirmed by IHC; (2) *ERBB2* exon 16 deletion in one post-progression tumor (delta-16 HER2, constitutively hyperphosphorylated and resistant to anti-HER2 antibodies); (3) *KRAS* mutations enriched post-treatment (1/50, 2% pre-treatment vs. 3/23, 13% post-treatment); (4) *PIK3CA* mutations: 1/50 (2%) pre-treatment vs. 2/23 (8.6%) post-treatment. Concordance of shared alterations between pre/post samples was high; discordances predominantly reflected acquired alterations.

### F4: Tumor mutational burden and EBV positivity predict durable immunotherapy responses

**Finding:** Higher TMB (top quartile >9.7 mut/Mb) was associated with better OS on immunotherapy; EBV positivity was associated with a complete, durable response to anti-PD-1 therapy; B2M loss-of-function mutations were identified as a mechanism of acquired immunotherapy resistance.

**Detail:** Among 40 immunotherapy-treated patients, top-quartile TMB (>9.7 mut/Mb) was associated with significantly better OS (median OS 16.8 vs. 6.62 months; 2-year OS 44% vs. 14%; HR=0.40; log-rank P=0.058). Five patients with durable responses (>12 months) included 2 MSI-H and 1 EBV+ tumor. The EBV+ patient (only 1 of 26 tested) achieved a complete response lasting >30 months. A patient with acquired anti-PD-1 resistance showed acquired *B2M* exon 1 loss-of-function mutation confirmed by IHC; 44% (4/9) of MSI-H tumors in the cohort harbored *B2M* alterations.

**Genes / cancer types:** `B2M`, MSI status, TMB, EBV status · Esophagogastric adenocarcinoma

### F5: CIN subtype dominates metastatic cohort; 53% harbor actionable alterations

**Finding:** Chromosomal instability (CIN, 63%) dominates the metastatic esophagogastric cancer landscape; *TP53* was the most frequently mutated gene (73%); 53% of patients have at least one OncoKB-actionable alteration.

**Detail:** Molecular subtype distribution in the MSK stage IV cohort (CIN 63%, GS 34%, MSI-H 3%) differs substantially from TCGA early-stage distribution (MSI-H 16%), reflecting stage-specific biology. Most frequent altered genes: *TP53* (73%), *ARID1A* (15%), *CDKN2A* (12%). CIN tumors had frequent RTK amplifications: *ERBB2* (25%), *KRAS* (16%), *EGFR* (8%), *ERBB3* (7%), *PIK3CA* (7%), *FGFR2* (5%), *MET* (5%). GS tumors were enriched for diffuse histology (32% vs. 9%, P=3e-5) and *CDH1* mutations (20% vs. 7%, P=0.01). 53% of patients had ≥1 OncoKB Level 1–4 actionable alteration.

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Can prospective MSI testing and TMB measurement replace empiric chemotherapy with first-line immunotherapy in metastatic MSI-H esophagogastric cancer?
- Does routine EBV status assessment (by EBER-ISH or NGS-based detection) identify additional immunotherapy-sensitive patients beyond MSI-H?
- Can B2M mutation burden before immunotherapy predict non-response, or should B2M testing be reserved for acquired resistance scenarios?
- Do patients with high ERBB2 amplification (top quartile by NGS) and no RTK/RAS/PI3K co-mutations warrant more aggressive ERBB2-directed therapy (e.g., T-DXd, bispecific antibodies) as initial treatment?
- Can circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) capture ERBB2 amplification heterogeneity and monitor clonal evolution on trastuzumab to enable earlier detection of resistance?

## Limitations

- Predominantly single-site biopsy per patient; tumor heterogeneity in multi-site metastatic disease cannot be fully captured.
- EBV status determined by EBER-ISH in only 26 of 295 patients; EBV detection from targeted NGS was not implemented.
- MSI-H patients (n=9) and immunotherapy-treated patients (n=40) are small subsets, limiting statistical power for biomarker analyses in these groups.
- HRD (homologous recombination deficiency) inference from LST score was not predictive of platinum response; true allele-level HRD analysis not performed.
- Targeted panel-based sequencing cannot detect promoter methylation, fusion transcripts not covered by the panel, or non-coding driver alterations.
- Favorable OS compared to published literature may reflect MSK patient population characteristics (ECOG 0–1: 90%), limiting generalizability to community settings.
