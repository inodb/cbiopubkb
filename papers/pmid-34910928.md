## Summary

This paper presents a multi-omic single-cell atlas of the two most common human colorectal precursor lesions — conventional adenomas (ADs) and serrated polyps (SERs) — and their resulting CRC counterparts. Integrating scRNA-seq, WES, and multiplex imaging from 128 datasets across 62 participants (COLON MAP study), the authors define two distinct cellular origins and molecular programs driving CRC tumorigenesis. Adenomas arise via WNT-driven stem cell expansion (APC-mutant, bottom-up), while sessile serrated lesions (SSLs) originate from a gastric metaplastic process in differentiated cells (BRAF-mutant, top-down). These distinct origins persist into malignancy: MSS CRCs retain the stem/WNT signature of ADs, while MSI-H CRCs retain metaplastic features of SSLs but acquire WNT reactivation. The immune microenvironment is shaped by cellular origin before hypermutation: SERs harbor a cytotoxic immune environment linked to antigen-presentation differences. The atlas serves as a framework for precision surveillance and prevention.

## Key Findings

### F1: Adenomas and serrated polyps have mutually exclusive driver mutations defining two tumorigenesis pathways

**Finding:** APC mutations dominate adenomas (85% of tubular ADs), while BRAF V600E is near-universal in sessile serrated lesions (89%); neither mutation type crosses subtypes. No SSLs harbored APC or KRAS mutations; no ADs harbored BRAF mutations.

**Detail:** WES on FFPE polyps and targeted sequencing of 281 additional polyps confirmed distinct mutational profiles. KRAS mutations increase from 5% (TAs) to 42% (TVAs), consistent with progression. BRAF enrichment in SSLs (67%) vs. TAs (1%) and TVAs (4%). No biallelic MMR loss in any polyp; MLH1 expression in SSLs comparable to ADs. Phylogenetic reconstruction (DENDRO on scRNA-seq) confirmed ASCs (AD-specific cells) genetically relate more to crypt-base stem cells, while SSCs (serrated-specific cells) cluster with differentiated colonocytes.

**Genes / cancer types:** `APC`, `KRAS`, `BRAF`, `RNF43`, `TP53` · Colorectal (pre-malignant)

### F2: Adenoma-specific cells (ASCs) arise from WNT-driven stem cell expansion; serrated-specific cells (SSCs) from gastric metaplasia of differentiated cells

**Finding:** scRNA-seq with SCENIC regulon analysis identifies two distinct neoplastic cell populations. ASCs express LGR5, OLFM4, ASCL2, AXIN2 (WNT targets), have high CytoTRACE stem potential scores, and derive from the crypt-base stem cell compartment. SSCs lack WNT activation, express gastric markers (MUC5AC, TFF2, AQP5, MSLN), downregulate CDX2, and derive from absorptive/differentiated lineage.

**Detail:** CytoTRACE score distributions skewed toward high stem potential in ASCs and low in SSCs. RNA velocity trajectories reversed in SERs vs. normal (vectors originate from differentiated cells). Multiplex imaging confirmed MUC5AC-positive neoplastic cells at the luminal surface of crypts in SSLs (not at crypt base), while stem markers (OLFM4, SOX9) were abundant in ADs but reduced in SSLs. SSC-specific regulon network featured RELB/IRF1/IRF7 (NF-κB, interferon response) reflecting a damage/inflammation-triggered metaplastic mechanism.

**Genes / cancer types:** `LGR5`, `OLFM4`, `ASCL2`, `AXIN2`, `RNF43`, `EPHB2`, `CDX2`, `MUC5AC`, `TFF2`, `MSLN`, `MDK` · Colorectal pre-malignant

### F3: MSS CRCs retain adenoma stem signatures; MSI-H CRCs retain serrated metaplastic signatures but acquire WNT reactivation

**Finding:** CRC cells preserve molecular features of their precursor lesions. MSS CRC cells score highly for WNT-driven stem cell programs (CMS2) mirroring ASCs. MSI-H CRCs retain metaplastic/fetal signatures (CMS1/CMS3) mirroring SSCs, but additionally acquire non-APC WNT pathway mutations and elevated stem potential (CytoTRACE), indicating acquisition of a hybrid stem/metaplastic identity.

**Detail:** MSI-H CRCs accumulated non-APC WNT pathway mutations: RNF43 (60%), TCF7L2 (53%), ZNRF3 (33%), APC2 (27%), AXIN2 (20%), FAT1/2/4 (33–47%). MUC5AC spatial staining positive in 13/14 MSI-H CRCs vs. 0/17 MSS CRCs. Intratumoral heterogeneity in MSI-H: regions of MUC5AC+ metaplastic cells coexist with OLFM4+ stem-like cells (cytotoxic immune depletion correlated spatially with stem-like regions). CMS classification validated on TCGA bulk RNA-seq (n=63).

**Genes / cancer types:** `RNF43`, `TCF7L2`, `ZNRF3`, `APC2`, `FAT1`, `MUC5AC`, `LGR5`, `OLFM4` · Colorectal cancer (MSS and MSI-H)

### F4: Serrated polyps have a cytotoxic immune microenvironment prior to hypermutation, driven by antigen-presentation differences related to differentiation status

**Finding:** CD8+ T cells, NK cells, and γδT cells (cytotoxic cells) are significantly enriched in SERs vs. ADs, despite SSLs lacking hypermutation. This cytotoxic enrichment parallels MSI-H CRCs vs. MSS CRCs, establishing a pre-mutation immune phenotype linked to tumor cell differentiation status.

**Detail:** CD4+ T cells were increased similarly in both polyp subtypes vs. normal. Cytotoxic cell enrichment in SERs was confirmed by MxIF quantification (CD8 intraepithelial counts). Within MSI-H CRCs, spatial analysis showed CD8/CD3-positive cells depleted in OLFM4+ stem-like non-metaplastic regions, with higher cytotoxic infiltration in MUC5AC+ metaplastic regions. This suggests antigen-presentation by differentiated/metaplastic (vs. stem-like) tumor cells drives cytotoxic recruitment. NF-κB/IRF inflammatory regulons in SSCs may underlie immune recruitment before neoantigens accumulate.

**Genes / cancer types:** `CD8A`, `MSLN`, `MUC5AC`, `CDX2`, `RELB`, `IRF1` · Colorectal (pre-malignant and CRC)

### F5: Subtype-specific molecular signatures classify polyps and enable pre-cancer–cancer continuum analysis

**Finding:** Gene signatures derived from ASCs and SSCs accurately classify polyp subtypes and predict CRC CMS assignments in bulk RNA-seq. WNT-driven programs (CMS2) associate with ADs and MSS CRCs; metaplastic/fetal programs (CMS1/3) associate with SSLs and MSI-H CRCs. Targeted sequencing of 281 polyps validated mutational trends at scale.

**Detail:** Bulk RNA-seq on 58 ADs and 8 SSLs confirmed transcriptomic separation. CMS scoring (single-sample predictor) on scRNA-seq data showed ASCs/MSS CRCs high for CMS2; SSCs/MSI-H CRCs high for CMS1/3, low for CMS2. None enriched for CMS4 (mesenchymal). GSEA confirmed enrichment of WNT, Hippo, DNA repair pathways in respective subtypes. The study provides a pre-cancer-to-cancer gene set resource for future biomarker and therapeutic target development.

**Genes / cancer types:** `WNT` pathway, `BRAF`, `KRAS`, `APC`, `MLH1` · Colorectal pre-malignant and CRC

## Open Questions

- Do the distinct immune microenvironments of ADs vs. SERs persist in the context of hereditary CRC syndromes (Lynch, FAP)?
- What drives MUC5AC-positive metaplastic patches at the luminal colonic surface in normal individuals and ulcerative colitis — and do these represent reversible or obligate pre-neoplastic events?
- Can WNT reactivation in MSI-H CRCs (via RNF43, TCF7L2 mutations) be therapeutically targeted with porcupine inhibitors or RNF43-directed agents?
- Does the cytotoxic microenvironment of SSLs translate into better responses to immune checkpoint inhibitors in MSS patients with serrated precursors?
- Could scRNA-seq-derived ASC/SSC gene signatures be converted to clinical IHC panels for polyp risk stratification at colonoscopy?

## Limitations

- Polyp sizes were small (median ≤5 mm), limiting FFPE WES quality; ~50% of specimens generated sufficient sequence data.
- Cross-sectional design precludes direct observation of polyp-to-cancer transition in matched individuals.
- Serrated polyp numbers (8 SSLs) in bulk RNA-seq validation cohort are small.
- scRNA-seq does not capture copy-number alterations or epigenetic changes (CIMP, MLH1 methylation) that drive serrated tumorigenesis.
- ICB response data not available; immune findings are observational.
- Single-institution COLON MAP cohort; demographic diversity included but CRC enrichment may not reflect population-level polyp distribution.
