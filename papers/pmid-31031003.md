## Summary
This CPTAC study performed comprehensive proteogenomic profiling of 110 prospectively collected colon cancer tumors and matched normal adjacent tissues using WXS, RNA-Seq, and TMT-based global and phosphoproteomics. Integrating these data layers revealed three unified molecular subtypes (MSI, CIN, Mesenchymal), identified Rb phosphorylation as an oncogenic driver targetable via CDK2 inhibition, and uncovered glycolysis-mediated immune suppression in MSI tumors as a mechanism of checkpoint inhibitor resistance.

## Dataset
- **Cohort**: 110 colon cancer patients with matched tumor and normal adjacent tissues (NATs); 24 MSI-H, 82 MSS
- **Platform**: Whole-exome sequencing (WXS), Illumina SNP array, RNA-Seq, miRNA-Seq, label-free shotgun proteomics, TMT isobaric labeling-based global and phosphoproteomics, selected reaction monitoring (SRM)
- **Data types**: Somatic mutations, copy number alterations, mRNA expression, miRNA expression, global proteomics, phosphoproteomics
- **cBioPortal study**: coad_cptac_2019

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: Three Unified Multi-Omics Subtypes (MSI, CIN, Mesenchymal)
- **Detail**: Integration of genomic (MSI status), transcriptomic (CMS), and proteomic subtype classifiers defined three unified multi-omics subtypes (UMS): MSI (enriched for cytotoxic CD8 T cells), CIN (chromosome instability, RB1 amplification), and Mesenchymal (EMT features, suppressor immune cells including MDSCs and Tregs).
- **Genes**: RB1, STAT1, STAT3, MIR200
- **Data availability**: partial — genomic and transcriptomic subtype data are in cBioPortal; phosphoproteomic subtype markers are not
- **Tool support**: native — MSI status, CNA, and mRNA-based CMS classification are accessible via cBioPortal UI; full UMS integration requires custom analysis
- **Concepts**: molecular subtypes, oncoprint, mutation frequency, copy number alteration

### Finding 2: Rb Phosphorylation as an Oncogenic Driver and CDK2 Inhibition Target
- **Detail**: RB1 is recurrently amplified (not deleted) in colon cancer, with overexpressed and hyperphosphorylated Rb protein driving both proliferation (via E2F1 activation) and suppression of apoptosis; phospho-Rb is most elevated in the CIN subtype, suggesting CDK2 inhibition as a subtype-specific therapeutic strategy.
- **Genes**: RB1, CDK2, E2F1, CDK4
- **Data availability**: partial — RB1 copy number and mutation data are in cBioPortal; phosphoproteomic Rb phosphorylation data are not
- **Tool support**: native — copy number and expression queries available in cBioPortal; phosphosite data require custom analysis
- **Concepts**: copy number alteration, protein expression, targeted therapy, actionability

### Finding 3: SOX9 Protein Overexpression Reclassifies It as an Oncogene
- **Detail**: SOX9 carries frequent truncating mutations in colon cancer suggesting tumor suppressor status, but proteomics shows significant protein overexpression in tumors (including truncation-bearing tumors); mutations cluster downstream of the HMG-box but upstream of ubiquitination site K398, likely stabilizing SOX9 protein and conferring oncogenic gain-of-function.
- **Genes**: SOX9
- **Data availability**: partial — SOX9 mutation data are in cBioPortal; protein abundance data are not
- **Tool support**: native — mutation lollipop plots and frequency available; protein-level interpretation requires external data
- **Concepts**: mutation frequency, oncoprint, protein expression

### Finding 4: Glycolysis Upregulation Drives Immune Evasion in MSI Tumors
- **Detail**: MSI subtype tumors show broad upregulation of glycolytic enzymes (including SLC2A3 and PKM2) at the protein but not always mRNA level, and glycolytic activity is strongly and negatively correlated with CD8 T cell infiltration specifically in MSI tumors; this supports combined glycolysis and checkpoint inhibitor blockade as a therapeutic strategy.
- **Genes**: SLC2A3, PKM2, LDHA, HK2
- **Data availability**: not-available — glycolytic enzyme protein abundance and CD8 infiltration estimates from proteomics are not in cBioPortal
- **Tool support**: custom — requires proteomics data from CPTAC portal combined with immune deconvolution results
- **Concepts**: immune infiltration, molecular subtypes, targeted therapy

### Finding 5: Cancer-Associated Kinases as Therapeutic Targets
- **Detail**: Phosphoproteomics identified seven cancer-associated kinases with elevated activity in tumors versus NATs, including CDK1, CDK2, CDK4, CDK7, MELK, PFKFB3, and PI4KB; CDK4 is targeted by FDA-approved drugs and the others by clinical trial agents.
- **Genes**: CDK1, CDK2, CDK4, CDK7, MELK, PFKFB3, PI4KB
- **Data availability**: not-available — kinase activity inferred from phosphosite enrichment is not available in cBioPortal
- **Tool support**: custom — requires phosphoproteomics data and kinase-substrate databases
- **Concepts**: targeted therapy, actionability

### Finding 6: Proteomics-Supported Tumor Antigens for Immunotherapy
- **Detail**: Proteomics-validated neoantigens were identified for 38% of patients (enriched in MSI-H); three cancer/testis antigens (IGF2BP3, SPAG1, ATAD2) were recurrently overexpressed in tumors; combined, 78% of tumors carried at least one proteomics-supported tumor antigen, with CT antigens most relevant for MSS tumors ineligible for checkpoint inhibition.
- **Genes**: IGF2BP3, SPAG1, ATAD2
- **Data availability**: partial — somatic mutation data for neoantigen prediction are in cBioPortal; HLA binding and CT antigen protein expression data are not
- **Tool support**: custom — neoantigen prediction and CT antigen protein expression require external tools and CPTAC proteomics data
- **Concepts**: mutation frequency, actionability, molecular subtypes

### Finding 7: Somatic Mutations Have Unexpected Proteomic Consequences
- **Detail**: Integration of mutations with phosphoproteomics uncovered non-intuitive effects: TP53-R273 hotspot mutations were associated with markedly increased TP53-S315 phosphorylation; APC and TGFBR2 frameshift/stop-gain mutations reduced downstream phosphosite abundance; these findings could not be inferred from genomic data alone.
- **Genes**: TP53, APC, TGFBR2
- **Data availability**: partial — mutation data are in cBioPortal; phosphosite abundance data are not
- **Tool support**: native — mutation data and lollipop plots available; phosphoproteomic correlation requires custom analysis
- **Concepts**: mutation frequency, oncoprint, protein expression

## Open Questions
- Does CDK2 inhibition show preferential efficacy in the CIN subtype of colon cancer with high Rb phosphorylation, and can Rb phosphorylation serve as a predictive biomarker?
- Can glycolysis inhibition (e.g., targeting SLC2A3 or PKM2) overcome checkpoint inhibitor resistance in MSI-H tumors that do not respond to PD-1/PD-L1 blockade?
- Do CT antigens like IGF2BP3 elicit durable anti-tumor immune responses in MSS colon cancer patients in clinical settings?

## Limitations
- Cohort includes only colon cancers (not rectal), limiting generalizability to all colorectal cancers.
- Phosphoproteomics and proteomics data are not yet integrated into cBioPortal, restricting interactive exploration of the most novel findings.
- Therapeutic hypotheses (CDK2 inhibition, glycolysis blockade, CT antigen vaccination) are based on associative data and lack in vivo or clinical validation in this study.
