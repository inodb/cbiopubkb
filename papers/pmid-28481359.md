## Summary

This paper describes the first large-scale deployment of MSK-IMPACT clinical sequencing, profiling 10,945 matched tumor-normal samples from 10,336 patients with advanced cancer across >300 detailed tumor types. It establishes the mutational landscape of metastatic cancer, characterizes TERT promoter mutations, kinase fusions, and mutation signatures at scale, and demonstrates actionability: 37% of patients harbor a clinically relevant alteration and 11% were enrolled on a genomically matched clinical trial. The complete dataset is publicly available on cBioPortal (`msk_impact_2017`). This is the foundational paper for the MSK-IMPACT cohort, later expanded 5× in [[pmid-41895280]].

## Key Findings

### F1: Pan-cancer somatic landscape of advanced/metastatic cancer, enriched vs. primary (TCGA)

**Finding:** MSK-IMPACT findings are broadly concordant with TCGA primary tumor data, but metastatic/treated tumors show significant enrichment of specific driver genes reflecting disease progression and treatment resistance.

**Detail:** 78,066 nonsynonymous mutations detected. TP53 most frequently altered (41% of patients), KRAS second (15%). KRAS G12 is the single most common mutant codon (80% of KRAS mutations, 12% of all patients). Key metastasis/resistance-associated enrichments vs. TCGA: TP53 in prostate (29% vs 7%), AR L702H/H875Y in prostate (18% vs 1%), ESR1 in breast (11% vs 4%, almost exclusively post-hormone therapy metastases).

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53`, `KRAS`, `AR`, `ESR1`, `PIK3CA`, `BRAF` · Pan-cancer; prostate, breast highlighted

**Data availability:** `available`
> Full mutation, CNA, and fusion data in `msk_impact_2017`. Clinical metadata (tumor type, metastatic status) available.

**Tool support:** `native`
> Cancer Summary, OncoPrint, and Mutations tabs reproduce frequency and distribution natively. TCGA comparison requires loading both studies and using cross-study query.

**Concepts:** #pan-cancer-map #metastatic-enrichment #treatment-resistance #driver-alterations

---

### F2: TERT promoter mutations: largest cross-cancer analysis, associated with worse survival

**Finding:** TERT promoter mutations are highly prevalent in 43 principal tumor types; most common in bladder (70%), glioma (67%), thyroid (60%), and cutaneous melanoma (49%), and consistently associated with shorter overall survival.

**Detail:** G>A substitutions at −124 and −146 bp account for 96.3% of TERT mutations. 10 additional recurrent sites identified (novel), all within 100 bp of the transcription start site. TERT promoter is absent from WES, making targeted panels uniquely powered for this analysis.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TERT` (promoter) · Bladder, glioma, thyroid, cutaneous melanoma; 43 tumor types total

**Data availability:** `available`
> TERT promoter mutations loaded in `msk_impact_2017`. Overall survival data available as clinical attribute (subset of patients).

**Tool support:** `native`
> Mutations tab for TERT (filter to promoter region). KM survival by TERT promoter status available natively via the Survival tab in Results View after cohort selection.

**Concepts:** #TERT #promoter-mutations #survival #pan-cancer-map

---

### F3: Kinase fusions occur across many more tumor types than previously known

**Finding:** 35% of all detected fusions involve kinase genes. ALK, RET, and ROS1 fusions — previously known mainly in lung cancer — were found in 11 additional tumor types. 51 novel kinase fusion partner genes identified.

**Detail:** BRAF fusions span 11 tumor types and 18 partner genes (10 novel). Novel CDK5RAP2-BRAF found in 2 independent melanomas without other drivers. 7 samples with BRAF intragenic multiexon deletions confer RAS-independent dimerization; 4 arose de novo.

**Genes / cancer types:** `ALK`, `RET`, `ROS1`, `BRAF`, `NTRK1/2/3` · Pan-cancer; lung, melanoma, sarcoma highlighted

**Data availability:** `available`
> Fusion/SV data loaded in `msk_impact_2017`.

**Tool support:** `native`
> Structural Variants tab in ResultsView shows partner genes and tumor type distribution. Cross-cancer fusion comparison possible with multi-study queries.

**Concepts:** #fusion-oncogenes #BRAF #kinase-fusions #de-novo-resistance #tumor-agnostic

### F4: Mutation signatures inferred from panel; MMR/MSI detection identifies immunotherapy candidates

**Finding:** Mutation signatures can be reliably inferred from targeted panel data (R²=0.76 vs. matched WES). 102 patients across 11 tumor types had MSI by combined signature + MSIsensor; 45% were not previously tested.

**Detail:** Signatures expected by cancer type: UV in melanoma, temozolomide in glioma, smoking in lung, POLE/MMR in CRC/endometrial. Prostate case study: MSK-IMPACT revealed unanticipated MMR signature → anti-PD-L1 trial with marked response.

**Genes / cancer types:** `POLE`, `MLH1`, `MSH2`, `MSH6`, `PMS2` · CRC, endometrial, melanoma, glioma, lung; prostate (unexpected MSI case)

**Data availability:** `available`
> TMB_NONSYNONYMOUS and MSI status available as clinical attributes. Mutation data for full signature analysis available via API.

**Tool support:** `api`
> TMB and MSI filters available natively in Study View. Full signature decomposition (SigProfiler, deconstructSigs) requires pulling SNV data via API and running externally.

**Concepts:** #mutation-signatures #MSI #TMB #ICI-biomarker #POLE #immunotherapy

### F5: 37% of patients have clinically actionable alterations; 11% enrolled on genomically matched trial

**Finding:** Using OncoKB tiered annotation, 36.7% of patients harbored at least one actionable somatic alteration. 11% of the first 5,009 patients were enrolled on a genomically matched clinical trial.

**Detail:** Highest actionability rates: GIST (76%), thyroid (61%), breast (61%), melanoma (58%). BRAF V600 in non-melanoma histologies (n=211): 36% received BRAF-targeted therapy; clinical benefit rate identical to melanoma (71%).

**Genes / cancer types:** `BRAF`, `PIK3CA`, `KRAS`, `ERBB2`, `ALK`, `RET`, `EGFR` · Pan-cancer; GIST, thyroid, breast, melanoma highest

**Data availability:** `available`
> OncoKB annotations available via cBioPortal overlay. Clinical trial enrollment data not in cBioPortal but alteration data is.

**Tool support:** `native`
> OncoKB actionability levels overlay available natively in Mutations and OncoPrint views. Trial enrollment outcomes require external clinical data.

**Concepts:** #actionability #OncoKB #tumor-agnostic #precision-oncology #BRAF #clinical-utility

---

### F6: Matched normal sequencing enables germline pathogenic variant detection

**Finding:** Matched tumor-normal sequencing allows identification of pathogenic germline variants as a byproduct of somatic profiling, with an IRB-approved return-of-results process.

**Detail:** 69% of somatic mutations detected would be difficult to distinguish from germline without a matched normal. Germline findings inform PARP inhibitor eligibility and hereditary cancer risk for family members.

**Genes / cancer types:** `BRCA1`, `BRCA2`, `ATM`, `PALB2`, `MLH1`, `MSH2` · Pan-cancer

**Data availability:** `partial`
> Somatic calls in `msk_impact_2017`. Germline pathogenic variants not in public release (separate consent/privacy restrictions).

**Tool support:** `custom`
> Germline variant analysis requires matched normal BAM access (not publicly available) and germline classification pipelines.

**Concepts:** #germline #hereditary-cancer #BRCA #matched-normal #clinical-pipeline

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Do the AR and ESR1 resistance mutations detected at higher rates in metastatic tumors show further enrichment in the 2026 MSK-50K cohort, consistent with increasing hormone therapy use?
- The 71% BRAF-inhibitor clinical benefit rate was identical in melanoma and non-melanoma histologies — does this hold in the 2026 cohort with 5× more non-melanoma BRAF patients?
- Can the ~55% "actionable but not enrolled" gap be explained by co-mutation context (canonical vs. non-canonical drivers per the 2026 paper)?
- Which of the 51 novel kinase fusion partner genes identified here now have additional clinical evidence in later cBioPortal studies?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Single major referral center; skewed toward advanced disease and heavily pre-treated patients
- Mixture of 341- and 410-gene panels; some genes not consistently profiled across all patients
- Mutation signature analysis restricted to 9% with elevated TMB; may underestimate MSI prevalence
- No RNA-seq, methylation, or WGS; limited to panel-targeted regions
- Clinical utility assessment (11% trial enrollment) reflects one institution's trial portfolio at one point in time
