## Summary

The largest uniform whole-exome sequencing meta-analysis of prostate cancer (1,013 tumor-normal pairs; 680 primary, 333 metastatic) identifies 97 significantly mutated genes (SMGs) — 70 not previously implicated in prostate cancer and 9 novel across all cancer types. SMGs follow a long-tail distribution with most genes mutated in <3% of cases, discoverable only at this sample size. The study establishes ETS-fusion-negative tumors with epigenetic regulator mutations as a distinct molecular class (20% of cases), implicates the spliceosome pathway (4%; *SF3B1*, *U2AF1*) as a new prostate cancer driver pathway, identifies *CUL3* as a new *SPOP*-related ubiquitin ligase gene, and defines *SPEN* as a novel androgen-signaling driver enriched in metastatic disease. A metastasis-enriched genomic signature is defined across PI3K, DNA repair, cell cycle, WNT, and epigenetic pathways.

## Key Findings

### F1: 97 significantly mutated genes in a long-tail distribution; 70 newly implicated in prostate cancer

**Finding:** Mutational significance analysis of 1,013 prostate cancers identifies 97 SMGs — far exceeding the ~25 previously established. The majority are mutated in <5% of cases and were only discoverable at this sample size (power analyses confirm >900 samples required). Known prostate cancer genes (*AR*, *SPOP*, *FOXA1*, *TP53*, *PTEN*) are confirmed; 70 genes known in other cancers are newly implicated in prostate cancer; 9 SMGs are novel across all cancer types.

**Detail:** The SMG distribution illustrates that low-frequency drivers collectively impact a major fraction of patients: *SPOP* (11%), *FOXA1* (9%), *TP53* (19%), *PTEN* (16% homozygous loss), *AR* (2% primary, higher in metastatic), with many genes at 1–2% frequency. *CUL3* (1.3%), *SPEN* (2.4%), *PIK3R2* (1%), *MRE11A*, *PALB2*, and spliceosome genes (*SF3B1* 1.1%, *U2AF1* 0.5%) represent newly defined, individually rare but collectively important drivers. Validation in two independent cohorts (Foundation Medicine n=204; MSK-IMPACT n=706) confirms key novel findings including *CUL3* p.Met299Arg (1.3% in MSK-IMPACT).

### F2: ETS-fusion-negative tumors with epigenetic regulator mutations define a new molecular class (20% of cases)

**Finding:** In tumors lacking ETS fusions (and lacking *SPOP*, *IDH1*, *CUL3*, or *FOXA1* drivers), mutations in epigenetic regulators and chromatin remodelers are significantly enriched (p=1×10⁻⁴ vs. ETS-fusion-positive tumors). Overall, 20% of all prostate cancers harbor mutations in epigenetic modifier/chromatin remodeling genes, including SWI/SNF members (*ARID1A* 1.6%, *ARID4A* 1%, *ARID2* 1.3%, *SMARCA1* 1.1%). These mutations are predominantly truncating and are associated with higher Gleason score (10% in Gleason 3+4 vs. 22% in Gleason 8–10; p=0.001).

**Detail:** The ETS-fusion-negative class with epigenetic regulator mutations represents a genomically distinct subtype that lacks an established oncogenic driver (ETS, SPOP, IDH1, FOXA1) and may have distinct biology and therapeutic vulnerabilities. SWI/SNF disruption in prostate cancer parallels SWI/SNF loss in bladder, renal, gastric, and ovarian cancers. *KMT2C* and *KMT2D* mutations are enriched in metastatic disease (p<0.05), suggesting progressive epigenetic rewiring during metastatic evolution. The epigenetic regulator class is enriched in primary tumors with high Gleason score, supporting early-stage biological relevance.

**Genes / cancer types:** `ARID1A`, `ARID2`, `ARID4A`, `SMARCA1`, `KMT2C`, `KMT2D`, `SPOP`, `CUL3`, `IDH1`, `FOXA1` · Prostate cancer

### F3: CUL3 identified as new SPOP-related ubiquitin ligase prostate cancer gene; spliceosome pathway implicated in 4%

**Finding:** *CUL3* (encodes the cullin scaffold of the BTB-CUL3-RBX1 E3 ubiquitin ligase complex with SPOP) is mutated in 1.3% of prostate cancers with a recurrent hotspot at p.Met299Arg, shows mutual exclusivity with *SPOP* mutations, and shares a copy-number profile (losses at 5q, 6q, 13) with *SPOP*-mutant tumors — supporting functional convergence. The spliceosome pathway is altered in 4% of tumors via *SF3B1* (1.1%, hotspot mutations in HEAT repeat C-terminus) and *U2AF1* (0.5%), extending the spliceosome-driver paradigm from MDS/CLL to prostate cancer.

**Detail:** CUL3's SPOP-associated ubiquitin ligase complex degrades prostate cancer regulators including AR, SRC-3, and TRIM24; CUL3 mutations may dysregulate these substrates similarly to SPOP mutations. Validation in MSK-IMPACT (706 samples) confirms 1.3% CUL3 mutation rate and three p.Met299Arg recurrences. *USP28* (1.4%) and *USP7* (1.2%) — other ubiquitin pathway members — are also SMGs, making the ubiquitin pathway collectively altered in 12% of prostate cancers. For spliceosome pathway: SF3B1 mutations cluster in HEAT repeats (same pattern as in MDS, CLL, breast, pancreatic cancers) and are predicted to disrupt 3′ splice site recognition, potentially affecting RNA processing of androgen-regulated transcripts.

**Genes / cancer types:** `CUL3`, `SPOP`, `USP28`, `USP7`, `SF3B1`, `U2AF1` · Prostate cancer

### F4: SPEN identified as novel androgen-signaling driver enriched in metastatic prostate cancer

**Finding:** *SPEN* (hormone-inducible transcription repressor, 2.4% mutation rate) is a newly identified SMG in prostate cancer, predominantly mutated by truncating variants in clonal metastatic disease (enriched in metastatic samples, q=0.008). SPEN represses estrogen receptor via NCOR2 recruitment of histone deacetylases, and its mutations may dysregulate hormone receptor signaling cross-talk in advanced disease.

**Detail:** SPEN protein recruits HDAC and SRA (RNA co-activator) to repress estrogen receptor signaling via NCOR2; it is activated by estrogen and potentially other sex hormones. SPEN overexpression is associated with tamoxifen sensitivity in breast cancer. In prostate cancer, truncating SPEN mutations are clonal in metastatic samples, suggesting selection during treatment (androgen deprivation therapy may alter estrogen/androgen receptor cross-talk, creating selective pressure for SPEN inactivation). Other AR-pathway SMGs include *SPOP* (AR protein stability), *FOXA1* (AR chromatin binding), and the *WNT/CTNNB1* pathway (10% of cases, including *APC* and *CTNNB1* hotspot mutations at the AXIN-interaction domain).

### F5: Metastatic prostate cancer enriched for TP53, AR, PTEN, RB1, BRCA2, APC, CDK12, and epigenetic regulators versus primary disease

**Finding:** Systematic comparison of 680 primary and 333 metastatic tumors identifies a metastasis-associated genomic signature: *TP53*, *AR*, *PTEN*, *RB1*, *FOXA1*, *APC*, *BRCA2*, *KMT2C*, *KMT2D* are all significantly enriched in metastatic samples. Conversely, *SPOP*, *IDH1*, and *ZMYM3* mutations are enriched in primary tumors. At the pathway level, PI3K, DNA repair, cell cycle, WNT/CTNNB1, and epigenetic regulators are all significantly more frequently altered in metastatic disease (p<0.0001 Fisher's exact test).

**Detail:** This enrichment pattern suggests two prostate cancer evolutionary trajectories: (1) SPOP/epigenetic-driver-dominated primary tumors with favorable biology; (2) TP53/RB1/PTEN/DNA repair-dominated tumors with high metastatic potential. CDK12 is mutated primarily by truncating mutations (consistent with biallelic inactivation — 15/31 CDK12-mutant tumors have ≥2 mutations), with missense variants clustering in the kinase domain (p<0.001), suggesting both TSG and dominant-negative mechanisms. PALB2 and MRE11A are newly identified DNA repair SMGs with potential PARP inhibitor relevance. The metastasis-associated genomic markers define a risk stratification signature potentially applicable to localized disease prognosis.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53`, `RB1`, `PTEN`, `AR`, `BRCA2`, `CDK12`, `PALB2`, `MRE11A`, `APC`, `KMT2C`, `KMT2D`, `FOXA1` · Prostate cancer (primary vs. metastatic)

## Open Questions

- Do ETS-fusion-negative/epigenetic-regulator-mutant prostate cancers represent a clinically distinct subset with different responses to androgen deprivation therapy, taxanes, or PARP inhibitors?
- Can *CUL3* p.Met299Arg hotspot mutations be targeted therapeutically (e.g., by preventing SPOP/CUL3 substrate stabilization), analogous to targeting SPOP-mutant prostate cancer with BET inhibitors?
- Do spliceosome mutations (*SF3B1*, *U2AF1*) create neoantigens or alter splicing of androgen-regulated transcripts in ways that could be exploited therapeutically?
- Is SPEN inactivation a mechanism of resistance to second-generation androgen receptor antagonists (enzalutamide, apalutamide), and does SPEN status predict response to AR-targeting therapy?
- Does the metastasis-associated genomic signature (TP53/PTEN/RB1/BRCA2/CDK12) predict benefit from platinum chemotherapy, PARP inhibitors, or anti-PD-1 therapy in metastatic castration-resistant prostate cancer?
- For the 9 SMGs novel across all cancers, what are the functional mechanisms and potential therapeutic dependencies?
- How does the long-tail distribution of prostate cancer drivers inform the design of clinical trials — is biomarker-agnostic functional sequencing or pathway-level basket trials needed?

## Limitations

- Meta-analysis of cohorts with different capture kits: intersected BED file restricts analysis to common covered regions, reducing power to detect mutations in kit-specific regions
- All metastatic samples are castration-resistant — biology may differ from hormone-sensitive metastatic disease; direct comparison to primary disease may reflect treatment selection as well as biology
- ETS fusion status available for only 765/1,013 samples, limiting power for ETS-stratified analyses
- CUL3 and SPOP mutual exclusivity not statistically significant at this cohort size — trend only
- Clonality analysis (FACETS) assumes diploid or aneuploid models; complex rearrangements may yield inaccurate CCF estimates
- No RNA-seq: fusion transcripts (ETS fusions for some samples inferred from expression outlier analysis), splice variant effects of SF3B1/U2AF1 not directly assessed
- The 9 SMGs novel across all cancer types lack functional validation and may include false positives arising from residual analytical confounders
- Clinical outcome data restricted to disease status (primary/metastatic) and Gleason score; treatment response, OS, and PFS not available for most samples
