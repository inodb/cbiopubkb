## Summary

Comprehensive whole-genome sequencing (WGS) study of 230 Burkitt lymphomas (BL; 100 adult, 181 pediatric; with some overlap) and 295 DLBCL tumors, with matched RNA-seq where available. Identified 57 significantly mutated genes (SMGs) in BL, including novel ones (SIN3A, USP7, HIST1H1E, CHD8, RFX7). Tumor EBV status was more strongly associated with genetic and molecular features than patient age. Three BL-predominant genetic subgroups were defined by NMF clustering: DGG-BL (DDX3X, GNA13, GNAI2), IC-BL (ID3, CCND3), and Q53-BL (quiet TP53). A random forest classifier achieved 93.3% accuracy in distinguishing BL from DLBCL. Subgroup-specific survival associations differed between adult and pediatric BL cohorts. Data are accessible in `mbn_sfu_2023` on cBioPortal.

## Key Findings

### F1: EBV status shapes BL biology more strongly than patient age

**Finding:** Stratification by tumor EBV positivity revealed significantly more distinct genetic and molecular profiles within BL than stratification by patient age (adult vs. pediatric). EBV-positive BLs showed lower rates of driver mutations in apoptotic pathway genes, higher aberrant somatic hypermutation (aSHM) rates, higher AICDA expression, and distinct MYC translocation breakpoint mechanisms relative to EBV-negative BLs.

**Detail:** EBV-positive BLs had enriched breakpoints upstream of MYC (SHM-mediated, associated with AID activity), whereas EBV-negative BLs had significantly more CSR-mediated breakpoints within MYC (p<0.001, Fisher test). AICDA expression was significantly higher in EBV-positive cases in both adult and pediatric cohorts. Genes mutated at significantly higher frequency in EBV-negative BL: GNAI2, CCND3, TP53, CDKN2A, SMARCA4, PTEN, FBXO11, STAT6, BCR, PHF6 (all p<FDR-corrected threshold). EBV-positive BL had more FOXO1 and BCR mutations. These findings extend the pBL EBV-stratification framework to aBL, confirming that EBV influences BL pathogenesis universally across age groups.

**Genes / cancer types:** FOXO1, TP53, CCND3, GNAI2, SMARCA4, CDKN2A, PTEN · Burkitt lymphoma (adult + pediatric)

### F2: Three BL genetic subgroups — DGG-BL, IC-BL, Q53-BL — with distinct biology

**Finding:** NMF consensus clustering of WGS features (SSMs, CNVs, SVs, aSHM patterns, hotspot mutations) across BL and DLBCL tumors yielded six subgroups, three predominantly BL: DGG-BL (characterized by DDX3X, GNA13, GNAI2 mutations; n=100 tumors, 85 BL), IC-BL (ID3, CCND3; n=112, 103 BL), and Q53-BL (quiet TP53, enriched for TP53 mutations; n=27, 20 BL).

**Detail:** DGG-BL was the most EBV-positive enriched subgroup (71% EBV+) and had the highest frequency of HNRNPU mutations (11%) and FOXO1 hotspot mutations. IC-BL had the highest rates of ID3 and CCND3 mutations and the lowest overall mutation burden (lower than DGG-BL, p=0.004). Q53-BL was enriched for TP53 mutations and notable for a paucity of other driver mutations or CNVs beyond MYC translocations; nonsynonymous MYC mutations were also the least prevalent (11%). Q53-BL differed from TP53-deficient DLBCL, which typically shows aneuploidy. More adult BLs were in Q53-BL; more pediatric BLs were in IC-BL and DGG-BL. A random forest classifier trained on exome-accessible features achieved 93.3% accuracy (94.1% sensitivity, 92.7% specificity) in BL vs. DLBCL classification.

**Genes / cancer types:** DDX3X, GNA13, GNAI2, ID3, CCND3, TP53, FOXO1, HNRNPU · Burkitt lymphoma

### F3: IC-BL and DGG-BL have distinct gene expression programs resembling ABC vs. GCB DLBCL

**Finding:** Differential gene expression analysis between IC-BL and DGG-BL identified 71 differentially expressed genes (p<0.05), with IRF4, TNFRSF13B, and SERPINA9 showing the strongest differences. IC-BL had substantially higher expression of IRF4 and TNFRSF13B — bimodal distributions mirroring the ABC vs. GCB DLBCL distinction. Gene set enrichment identified 17 differentially expressed pathways, including IRF4 induction in ABC DLBCL and memory B-cell pathways enriched in IC-BL. NF-κB pathway activity, which distinguishes ABC from GCB DLBCL, did not differ between IC-BL and DGG-BL.

**Detail:** IRF4, TNFRSF13B, and SERPINA9 are components of the DLBCL cell-of-origin (COO) and DHITsig classifiers and have probes on the DLBCL90 NanoString assay. The elevated IRF4 in IC-BL (relative to DGG-BL) may reflect fundamentally different B-cell differentiation states at the origin of each subgroup, with IC-BL BLs arising from a more IRF4-active, post-germinal center precursor. Despite these expression differences, IC-BL and DGG-BL are both robustly classified as BL by genetics and distinct from DLBCL in the NMF clustering.

**Genes / cancer types:** IRF4, TNFRSF13B, SERPINA9 · Burkitt lymphoma (IC-BL vs. DGG-BL)

### F4: 57 SMGs identified in BL; 18 shared with DLBCL; novel genes include SIN3A, USP7, CHD8

**Finding:** Integrated analysis of SSMs from 252 BL genomes alongside 252 DLBCL genomes identified 57 significantly mutated genes in BL (≥2% prevalence, ≥4 patients). 18 (31%) were also recurrently mutated in DLBCL. Novel BL-associated SMGs confirmed: SIN3A, USP7, HIST1H1E, CHD8, RFX7. Additional newly identified low-frequency SMGs include TET2, HNRNPU, BRAF, SYNCRIP, and EZH2 (some at higher rates in DLBCL than BL).

**Detail:** ARID1A was mutated at significantly higher frequency in pBL (46% vs. 26% in aBL, FDR-corrected), and TET2 was more common in aBL (10% vs. 1.5% in pBL). These are among the few genes with significant age-stratified differences, further supporting that EBV rather than age is the primary determinant of BL biology. HNRNPU truncating mutations were enriched in EBV-positive BLs and associated with lower HNRNPU mRNA expression, suggesting loss-of-function. Dominant mutational signatures: SBS1 (aging/clock), SBS5 (clock), SBS9 (AID/aSHM); SBS9 significantly increased in EBV-positive BL. aBLs showed higher SBS1 and SBS5, lower SBS9 relative to pBLs.

**Genes / cancer types:** ID3, CCND3, DDX3X, GNA13, GNAI2, TP53, MYC, ARID1A, TET2, HNRNPU, SIN3A, USP7, CHD8, RFX7 · Burkitt lymphoma

### F5: BL genetic subgroups have distinct survival associations that differ by patient age

**Finding:** Overall survival (OS) differences between BL genetic subgroups were not significant across the full cohort (excluding Uganda and Brazil due to batch effects/missing data). However, within adult BL, the strongest survival associations were for individual genes that define subgroups: ID3 mutations (IC-BL proxy) and TP53 mutations (Q53-BL proxy). Within pediatric BL, DGG-BL subgroup membership was associated with the most inferior outcomes.

**Detail:** The discordant survival associations between aBL and pBL underscore that genetic subgroups interact differently with host biology, treatment, and disease context across age groups. For aBL, IC-BL (ID3-mutant) was associated with better outcomes, while Q53-BL (TP53-mutant) was associated with worse outcomes. In pBL, DGG-BL (EBV-positive enriched, DDX3X/GNA13-driven) had the worst prognosis. These findings support the need for age-stratified analyses in BL clinical trials and for subgroup-specific prognostic models. Missing treatment data in 55% of patients limited full survival modeling.

**Genes / cancer types:** ID3, TP53, DDX3X, GNA13 · Burkitt lymphoma (adult vs. pediatric)

### F6: Random forest classifier distinguishes BL from DLBCL with 93.3% accuracy using exome-accessible features

**Finding:** A random forest model trained on mutations detectable in exomes (not requiring WGS) achieved 93.3% overall accuracy (94.1% sensitivity, 92.7% specificity) in distinguishing BL from DLBCL. Applied to three published cohorts, it correctly classified 92.3% of DLBCLs from Schmitz et al. (434/470); 9% of DLBCLs classified as BL-like had significantly shorter PFS (p=0.006, log-rank).

**Detail:** The classifier was applied to cohorts from Zhou et al. (BL: 58.9% IC-BL, 30.1% DGG-BL, 9.6% DLBCL) and Panea et al. (unexpectedly, 37.6% classified as DLBCL, corroborated by enrichment for BCL6, KMT2D, CREBBP, EZH2 hotspot mutations atypical for BL — suggesting misclassified or histological borderline cases). Of 8 non-BL cases that failed central pathology review, 6 (75%) were assigned to a BL cluster (IC-BL n=3, DGG-BL n=2, Q53-BL n=1), validating the classifier for resolving diagnostically ambiguous BL/DLBCL cases. DLBCLs molecularly assigned to BL subgroups were enriched for aggressive disease features.

**Genes / cancer types:** DDX3X, GNA13, ID3, CCND3, TP53 (classifier features) · Burkitt lymphoma / DLBCL

## Open Questions

- Should BL clinical trial enrollment and treatment stratification incorporate genetic subgroup (IC-BL, DGG-BL, Q53-BL) in addition to EBV status?
- What is the mechanistic basis for the better prognosis of ID3-mutant (IC-BL) adult BL and the worse prognosis of DGG-BL pediatric BL?
- Do the BL-like DLBCLs (9% of DLBCL assigned to BL clusters) represent a true biologically distinct DLBCL entity requiring different therapy?
- How does HIV co-infection alter the genetic subgroup distribution and prognosis in adult BL?

## Limitations

- Survival analyses excluded patients from Uganda and Brazil due to batch effects and missing clinical data, limiting applicability to endemic BL cohorts.
- Treatment data were missing for 55% of patients, precluding comprehensive treatment-adjusted survival analyses.
- Some BL cases lacked matched germline DNA, potentially introducing germline variants into somatic mutation calls.
- The random forest classifier was trained on WGS-derived features and adapted for exome use by restricting to coding mutations; application to targeted panel data requires further validation.
- pBLs were somewhat overrepresented relative to aBLs, which could affect the generalizability of subgroup frequency estimates to adult BL populations.
