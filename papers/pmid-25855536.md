## Summary

Whole-exome sequencing of 109 microdissected pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDA) cases with matched clinical outcome data reveals that PDA carries a substantially higher mutational burden than previously reported (~67 non-synonymous mutations per case vs. ~26 from prior studies), attributable to the needle-dissection enrichment approach that improved tumor cellularity. MutSigCV identifies 24 significantly mutated genes, including novel prognostic markers (RBM10 favorable, ARID1A adverse). The study defines KRAS allele-specific biology — Q61 mutations associate with better survival and lower ERK activation than codon 12 alleles — and demonstrates that BRAF V600E mutant PDA (3% of cases), exclusive of KRAS mutations, is sensitive to vemurafenib in patient-derived models. MYC amplification (14%) is uniquely associated with poor prognosis and adenosquamous histology. High-frequency alterations define targetable pathways including SWI/SNF (42%), DNA repair (>35%), and Wnt/β-catenin, each with associated therapeutic strategies.

## Key Findings

### F1: MYC amplification at 8q24 is the single copy-number alteration uniquely associated with poor survival and adenosquamous histology in PDA

**Finding:** Among all significantly amplified and deleted genomic regions identified by GISTIC analysis, only the 8q24 locus harboring MYC was associated with overall survival in a multivariate analysis. MYC amplification was confirmed by FISH and was significantly over-represented in adenosquamous PDA subtype — the most aggressive histological variant with the worst prognosis. MYC-amplified PDA cases were also enriched in PanIN precursor lesions, suggesting a role in initiation, not just progression.

**Detail:** MYC amplification frequency was ~14% of cohort. In a MYC-driven mouse model (Pdx1-Cre/CAG-βgeo-tTA/TetO-Myc), the tumors exhibited adenosquamous histology and p63 positivity, providing mechanistic in vivo support for the clinical association. High levels of chromosomal instability (clusters 5 and 6 from APC clustering) were enriched for DNA break repair gene mutations and trended toward poor outcome, but only MYC amplification uniquely predicted survival as a single-copy-number event. CDK9 inhibition (PHA767491) and BET bromodomain inhibition (JQ1) were proposed as MYC-directed therapeutic strategies.

**Genes / cancer types:** `MYC`, `CDK9` · Pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma

### F2: KRAS codon allele context determines ERK pathway activation intensity and survival — Q61 alleles associate with paradoxically better prognosis

**Finding:** KRAS mutations were identified in 92% of PDAs. While all codon-12 alleles (G12D, G12V, etc.) showed equivalent survival, KRAS Q61 mutations (codons 61, ~rare) were associated with significantly longer overall survival despite exhibiting high-grade pathological features (pT3 stage, lymph node metastasis). Immunohistochemistry showed that Q61-mutant tumors had lower pERK staining than codon-12 mutants, suggesting weaker downstream RAS pathway activation.

**Detail:** This finding challenges the assumption that all oncogenic KRAS alleles are equivalent drivers. Different KRAS alleles have intrinsically different biochemical properties — Q61 mutations hydrolyze GTP more slowly than some codon-12 alleles, which can paradoxically reduce the amplitude of RAS signaling. The paradoxical histological aggressiveness with better survival in Q61 mutants may reflect different stromal biology or immune infiltration rather than differences in cancer cell-intrinsic proliferative capacity. This finding supports an allele-context view of KRAS driver oncogenesis.

### F3: BRAF V600E (3% of PDAs) is mutually exclusive with KRAS and confers sensitivity to FDA-approved BRAF inhibitor PLX-4032 (vemurafenib)

**Finding:** BRAF V600E mutations were identified in 3% of cases and were strictly mutually exclusive with KRAS mutations, defining a genomic subtype of KRAS wild-type PDA. A patient-derived cell line established from a BRAF V600E-mutant PDA was equally sensitive to PLX-4032 (vemurafenib) as BRAF V600E melanoma cells, while KRAS-driven PDA cells were resistant — validating BRAF V600E as an actionable driver in this rare PDA subset.

**Detail:** In KRAS wild-type PDAs, alternative oncogenic drivers were identified: BRAF V600E (3%), PIK3CA activating mutations (1%), and in remaining wild-type cases, alterations in STK11, GNAS, CHEK2, and RB1. This hierarchy recapitulates the pan-cancer view where RAS pathway activation is nearly universal in PDA, but the mechanism varies. The experimental BRAF V600E PDA cell line (PDA_014) demonstrated ERK inhibition and cell-cycle arrest on PLX-4032, providing a direct functional basis for clinical testing of BRAF inhibitors in this molecular subtype.

### F4: RBM10 mutations associate with paradoxically better survival despite high-grade histology; ARID1A loss predicts poor outcome

**Finding:** Two chromatin/splicing genes show opposing prognostic associations. RBM10, a regulator of alternative splicing, was significantly mutated in PDA (4/109) and associated with significantly longer survival despite all RBM10-mutant cases being high-grade pT3 with lymph node metastasis in 3/4 cases. ARID1A (SWI/SNF complex) mutations trended toward poor survival in the sequenced cohort and ARID1A protein deficiency (by IHC in an independent cohort of 296 PDAs) was significantly associated with poor outcome, confirming ARID1A as a prognostic marker.

**Detail:** ARID1A-deficient PDA cell lines showed vulnerability to ARID1B depletion (synthetic lethality), suggesting a therapeutic strategy analogous to what has been described in ovarian cancer. The paradoxical RBM10 association — histological aggressiveness but clinical benefit — echoes the KRAS Q61 paradox and may reflect distinct tumor-stromal biology or immune phenotypes in splicing-altered tumors. ARID1A loss was independently validated by IHC in 296 additional cases, making it one of the most robustly validated prognostic markers identified in this study.

### F5: High-frequency alterations in SWI/SNF (42%), DNA repair (>35%), and Wnt/Notch pathways define potentially actionable genomic landscape beyond KRAS

**Finding:** Beyond the canonical KRAS/TP53/SMAD4/CDKN2A landscape, pathway-level analysis reveals: SWI/SNF chromatin remodeling genes mutated/deleted in 42% (ARID1A, ARID2, SMARCA4); DNA repair pathway altered in >35% (BRCA1/2, ATM, CHEK2, Fanconi anemia gene family, BCLAF1); Wnt/β-catenin pathway in ~18% (RNF43 loss, AXIN1/2 or APC loss); Notch amplification in 31%. Each pathway has associated therapeutic vulnerabilities including PARP inhibitors (BRCA/Fanconi), porcupine/tankyrase inhibitors (Wnt), gamma-secretase inhibitors (Notch), and CDK4/6 inhibitors (RB pathway, with CDKN2A loss in 41%).

**Detail:** Chromosomal instability clusters (high CNA burden) were enriched for DNA repair gene mutations, suggesting a mutator phenotype driven by defective break repair. Fanconi anemia gene family members (14%) and BRCA1/2 (3%) are actionable with cross-linking agents and PARP inhibitors — an observation validated in patient-derived xenografts. The RNF43/AXIN1 Wnt pathway alteration rate is noteworthy given that RNF43-mutant tumors show sensitivity to Wnt ligand inhibitors (porcupine inhibitors). These alterations occur even in the presence of KRAS mutations, providing opportunities for combination targeting.

### F6: Smoking-associated mutational signature (C>A transversion enrichment) in PDA and associated worse survival

**Finding:** Smokers in the cohort exhibited enrichment of C>A transversions in their tumor mutation spectra, mirroring the smoking-associated mutagenic signature seen in lung adenocarcinoma. Smoking status was associated with significantly worse overall survival (median survival approximately half that of non-smokers). Additionally, hypermutated cases (top quartile of mutation burden) were enriched for mismatch repair gene mutations and exhibited MMR-characteristic mutation spectra (T>C transitions at CTG trinucleotides).

**Detail:** The smoking-associated signature in PDA is consistent with inhalation of carcinogens causing direct DNA damage in pancreatic ductal cells, providing an etiological basis for the epidemiological association between smoking and PDA risk. The MMR-deficient hypermutator phenotype (~5% of cases) defines a potential immunotherapy-responsive subgroup. The identification of distinct mutational spectra subtypes by hierarchical clustering suggests that PDA is not a homogeneous entity at the level of mutagenic etiology.

## Open Questions

- Can KRAS allele status (Q61 vs. codon 12) serve as a prospective stratification factor in PDA clinical trials, and what underlies the paradoxical survival benefit?
- What is the objective response rate of BRAF V600E-mutant PDA (3% of cases) to BRAF ± MEK inhibitor combinations in prospective trials?
- Do patients with ARID1A-deficient PDA benefit specifically from ARID1B synthetic lethality strategies or from other SWI/SNF-directed therapies?
- Does RBM10 mutation alter splicing in a way that affects immunogenicity or drug sensitivity in PDA?
- Which Fanconi anemia gene-mutant PDA patients respond to PARP inhibitors or DNA crosslinking agents (mitomycin C), and does KRAS mutation status modify response?
- What proportion of the hypermutated MMR-deficient PDA cases respond to PD-1/PD-L1 checkpoint blockade?

## Limitations

- ~50× average sequencing depth is relatively low; 21 deeply sequenced cases (120×) showed additional low-allele-frequency variants not detectable at standard depth
- Non-TCGA, single-center study limits direct comparison to other PDA cohorts without batch correction
- Tumor purity based on average cellularity estimate (>50%), not clonal deconvolution; intratumoral heterogeneity not modeled
- ARID1A prognostic finding validated only by IHC in an independent cohort (296 cases); orthogonal genomic validation needed
- CNA analysis derived from exome coverage ratios — lower resolution than SNP array or WGS approaches; structural rearrangements not captured
- Stage distribution skewed toward resected (largely Stage I-II) disease; findings may not represent Stage IV PDA biology
- No transcriptomic data; fusion transcripts, isoform switching, and RNA-based subtypes (e.g., classical vs. basal-like) not characterized
