## Summary

This prospective-retrospective study of 240 advanced NSCLC patients treated with anti-PD-(L)1 therapy and profiled by MSK-IMPACT demonstrates that tumor mutation burden (TMB) assessed by targeted NGS accurately estimates TMB by whole-exome sequencing (WES; ρ=0.86) and independently predicts durable clinical benefit (DCB). TMB and PD-L1 expression are independent biomarkers with similar predictive capacity; a composite of high TMB + PD-L1 ≥1% enriches for DCB (50% rate). STK11 and EGFR mutations associate with lack of ICI benefit. The fraction of copy number–altered genome (FGA) is inversely associated with response. Data are available as `nsclc_pd1_msk_2018` in cBioPortal.

## Key Findings

### F1: Targeted NGS accurately estimates TMB and elevated TMB predicts durable ICI benefit in NSCLC

**Finding:** TMB measured by MSK-IMPACT targeted NGS is highly correlated with WES TMB (Spearman ρ=0.86, P<.001). Higher TMB associates with DCB vs. no durable benefit (NDB; median 8.5 vs. 6.6 SNVs/Mb, P=.006) and with improved PFS (HR 1.38 at ≥50th TMB percentile, P=.024). DCB rate was 38.6% above vs. 25.1% below the median TMB.

**Detail:** Median TMB was 7.4 SNVs/Mb (range 0.8–91.8). TMB was normalized by panel coding region size (0.98–1.22 Mb across panels). The correlation between targeted NGS and WES holds down to panels with ≥0.5 Mb coverage; smaller panels may be unreliable. In non-ICI NSCLC patients, TMB showed an inverse relationship with OS, confirming that the effect of TMB is predictive (ICI-specific) rather than prognostic.

**Genes / cancer types:** Panel-wide TMB · Advanced NSCLC

### F2: FGA (copy number burden) inversely associates with ICI benefit — aneuploidy may mediate immune evasion

**Finding:** Fraction of copy number–altered genome (FGA) was significantly higher in NDB vs. non-ICI NSCLC patients (median 0.16 vs. 0.11, P=.007). Despite a modest positive association between FGA and TMB (ρ=0.31), FGA negatively associates with ICI response — suggesting aneuploidy independently promotes immune evasion.

**Detail:** FGA was defined as fraction of genome with log2 copy number gain >0.2 or loss <−0.2. Even though FGA and TMB are positively correlated (aneuploidy drives mutation accumulation), high aneuploidy tumors derive less ICI benefit — consistent with reports in melanoma linking aneuploidy to reduced cytotoxic immune infiltrates. Mechanistic explanations include suppression of antigen presentation by CNA-driven disruption of MHC/B2M loci.

### F3: EGFR mutations associate with lack of ICI benefit; STK11 mutations enrich in NDB

**Finding:** EGFR-mutant tumors rarely experienced DCB (7%), and EGFR was significantly underrepresented in the DCB group vs. the non-ICI NSCLC comparison group (FDR-adjusted P=.013). STK11 was significantly enriched in the NDB group vs. non-ICI NSCLC (FDR-adjusted P=.007).

**Detail:** The lack of EGFR benefit is likely confounded by low TMB in never-smokers. KRAS mutations were common (n=83, 35%) with DCB rate (36%) similar to the overall cohort — KRAS alone does not enrich for ICI benefit. STK11 loss reduces tumor inflammation in murine models and human tumors; LKB1/STK11-deficient NSCLC tumors have low immune infiltrate and are cold immunologically. Other driver mutations (ALK, ROS1, MET, RET) were too rare in this cohort for definitive conclusions.

### F4: TMB and PD-L1 expression are independent, complementary predictors; composite maximizes enrichment

**Finding:** TMB and PD-L1 expression were not correlated (Spearman ρ=0.19, P=.08) and had similar individual predictive capacity for DCB (TMB AUC 0.601; PD-L1 AUC 0.646). Patients with both high TMB (above median) and PD-L1 ≥1% had a 50% DCB rate — the highest of any subgroup — vs. 18% in those negative for both.

**Detail:** PD-L1 IHC was available for 84 patients using 22C3, 28-8, or E1L3N antibodies (largely concordant). PD-L1 ≥1% was associated with improved PFS (HR 0.526, P=.011). The independence of these biomarkers implies different underlying biology: TMB reflects neoantigen load driving T-cell priming, while PD-L1 reflects an existing inflamed microenvironment. Their combined use in multivariable models may substantially improve patient selection for ICI.

**Genes / cancer types:** PD-L1 (CD274) protein expression; TMB (panel-wide) · Advanced NSCLC

### F5: Acquired resistance mechanisms — B2M and JAK2 loss-of-function — confirmed in a primary resistance setting

**Finding:** Likely deleterious B2M mutations were rare (n=1) but notable: a patient with two trans B2M mutations and confirmed loss of B2M expression by IHC achieved an ongoing PR at 48 SNVs/Mb, suggesting TMB may overcome antigen presentation defects. One patient with homozygous JAK2 loss (splice mutation + LOH) had progressive disease, consistent with IFN-γ signaling defects mediating primary ICI resistance.

**Detail:** These cases (B2M, JAK2) were originally described as acquired resistance mechanisms in melanoma PD-1 blockade. Their identification in NSCLC at primary resistance suggests shared mechanisms of ICI failure across tumor types. MDM2/MDM4 amplifications (n=8) were not associated with hyperprogression in this cohort (HR 1.4, P=.44), contrasting with a prior report.

## Open Questions

- What TMB threshold is clinically actionable for NSCLC and how does this vary across NGS assays and panel sizes?
- Can a composite score integrating TMB, PD-L1, FGA, and STK11 status prospectively identify ICI non-responders for alternative therapy?
- Do KRAS G12C inhibitor combinations with PD-(L)1 blockade overcome the immune-cold phenotype in STK11-comutated tumors?
- Does B2M loss genuinely confer primary resistance even in high-TMB tumors, or is TMB high enough to partially rescue antigen presentation?
- Can serial ctDNA TMB assessment track adaptive immune escape during ICI therapy?

## Limitations

- Moderate sample size (n=240) limits power for subgroup analyses and multiple gene-level comparisons
- Retrospective response adjudication for patients outside clinical trials; real-world RECIST may differ from trial standards
- Single institution with MSK-IMPACT panel — TMB cut points are assay-specific and not directly generalizable
- PD-L1 IHC available for only 35% of patients (n=84); multiple antibody clones used
- Study does not distinguish primary vs. acquired resistance; most pre-treatment samples preclude analysis of adaptive resistance
- CTLA-4 combination therapy included (~minority); differential effects not fully stratified
