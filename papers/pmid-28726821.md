## Summary

Northcott et al. performed whole-genome sequencing (WGS) of 491 medulloblastomas (MBs) spanning all four molecular subgroups (WNT, SHH, Group 3, Group 4), supplemented by methylation profiling of 1,256 cases and RNA-seq on 164 tumours. Integrative analysis confidently assigned candidate driver events to 76–100% of cases per subgroup, more than doubling the proportion of explained Group 3 and Group 4 tumours compared with earlier studies. Novel discoveries include hotspot in-frame insertions in *KBTBD4*, enhancer hijacking of *PRDM6* via *SNCAIP* structural variants, eight methylation-defined subtypes within Group 3/4, and a subgroup-enriched mutational-signature landscape, providing a rich resource for targeted therapy development.

## Key Findings

### F1: WNT Subgroup — Universal Driver Assignment with CTNNB1 and SWI/SNF Mutations

**Finding:** All 36 WNT MBs were explained by at least one driver event; *CTNNB1* mutations were present in 86% and monosomy 6 in 83%, with the remaining cases harbouring germline *APC* variants (Turcot syndrome).

**Detail:** Beyond the canonical WNT pathway, *CSNK2B* mutations occurred in 14%, *EPHA7* in 8%, and SWI/SNF complex subunits (*SMARCA4*, *ARID1A*, *ARID2*) were collectively mutated in 33% of WNT patients. SWI/SNF loss creates an epigenetic dependency on PRC2, supporting the use of EZH2 inhibitors (NCT02601937) as a rational targeted therapy. Neither *CTNNB1* mutation nor monosomy 6 is universally present, meaning that clinical assignment based solely on these markers will miss ~10–15% of bona fide WNT cases.

**Genes / cancer types:** `CTNNB1`, `APC`, `CSNK2B`, `EPHA7`, `SMARCA4`, `ARID1A`, `ARID2` · WNT medulloblastoma

**Data availability:** `available`
> SNVs/indels and copy-number calls (including monosomy 6) for WNT cases are accessible in the mbl_dkfz_2017 cBioPortal study. SWI/SNF mutation status is queryable via the oncoprint.

**Tool support:** `native`

### F2: SHH Subgroup — Age-Stratified Driver Landscape with TERT and TP53 Dichotomy

**Finding:** Driver events were assigned to >95% of SHH MBs; the canonical SHH pathway genes (*PTCH1* 43%, *SMO* 9%, *SUFU* 9%) dominate, but *TERT* promoter mutations mark adult SHH whereas *TP53* mutations define a poor-prognosis paediatric SHH subset.

**Detail:** *TERT* promoter hotspot mutations were identified as the only confident recurrently mutated noncoding region across the entire MB genome (genome-wide significance threshold P < 10⁻²⁵) and are highly enriched in adult SHH. *TP53* mutations in child SHH co-occur with chromothripsis, linking catastrophic genomic rearrangements to inferior outcomes. Six *IDH1* R132C mutations (five SHH) were also discovered and confirmed to induce a CpG island methylator phenotype (CIMP+). Histone acetyltransferase complex genes (*CREBBP*, *KAT6B*, *EP300*, *BRPF1*, *KANSL1*) were mutated in 19% of SHH cases, significantly enriched versus other subgroups (q = 2.2×10⁻³).

**Genes / cancer types:** `PTCH1`, `SMO`, `SUFU`, `TERT`, `TP53`, `IDH1`, `CREBBP`, `EP300`, `KAT6B` · SHH medulloblastoma

**Data availability:** `available`
> SNV/indel data for SHH cases including PTCH1, SMO, SUFU, TERT promoter, and TP53 mutations are present in mbl_dkfz_2017. CIMP/methylation phenotype is not directly viewable in cBioPortal.

**Tool support:** `native`

### F3: Group 3 — MYC Amplification Drives Poor Prognosis; Driver Assignment Doubled

**Finding:** Candidate driver events were assigned to 76% of Group 3 MBs — more than doubling the proportion explained by prior NGS studies (32%) — with *MYC* amplification in 17% representing the most clinically adverse alteration.

**Detail:** *MYC* amplifications were restricted exclusively to Group 3 (17%), while *MYCN* amplifications were found at lower frequency in both Group 3 (5%) and Group 4 (6%). *SMARCA4* mutations cluster in Group 3 alongside Notch and TGFβ pathway alterations, the first genomic evidence for Notch pathway mutations in MB. Structural variants activating *GFI1* or *GFI1B* through enhancer hijacking are distributed across both Group 3 and Group 4 and are mutually exclusive with *MYC/MYCN* amplifications. Most driver events were mutually exclusive, consistent with strong single-oncogene dependency in individual tumours.

**Genes / cancer types:** `MYC`, `MYCN`, `SMARCA4`, `GFI1`, `GFI1B` · Group 3 medulloblastoma

**Data availability:** `available`

**Tool support:** `native`
> Use the Copy Number tab or OncoPrint in mbl_dkfz_2017, filtering to Group 3, to identify MYC-amplified cases and co-occurring alterations.

### F4: Group 4 — KDM6A Mutations and PRDM6 Enhancer Hijacking Define Subtypes

**Finding:** Driver events were assigned to 82% of Group 4 MBs; *KDM6A* and *ZMYM3* mutations cluster in methylation subtype VIII, while *PRDM6* — activated by *SNCAIP* locus structural variants — emerged as the most prevalent driver alteration in Group 4.

**Detail:** CESAM (cis expression structural alteration mapping) systematically identified *PRDM6* as the top-ranked enhancer hijacking candidate in Group 4. Group 4 cases with *SNCAIP*-associated structural variants showed ≥20-fold *PRDM6* upregulation relative to cases without these variants, driven by rearrangements that juxtapose *PRDM6* to a Group 4-specific *SNCAIP* super-enhancer across a disrupted TAD boundary. *KDM6A* (a histone H3K27 demethylase) mutations were significantly enriched in subtype VIII alongside *ZMYM3*. *MYCN* and *CDK6* amplifications also occur in Group 4 (~6% each), representing potential therapeutic targets.

**Genes / cancer types:** `KDM6A`, `ZMYM3`, `PRDM6`, `SNCAIP`, `MYCN`, `CDK6` · Group 4 medulloblastoma

**Data availability:** `partial`

### F5: Methylation-Based Subtyping Resolves Eight Subtypes Within Group 3/4

**Finding:** t-SNE analysis of 740 Group 3/4 methylation profiles (from 1,256 total MBs) resolved up to eight molecularly distinct subtypes, each differentially enriched for specific driver events and copy-number profiles.

**Detail:** Iterative downsampling confirmed that ≥500 samples are required for stable subtype resolution, underscoring the necessity of large cohorts. Specific driver genes showed striking subtype specificity: *GFI1B* (subtype I), *MYC* (subtype II), *KDM6A* and *ZMYM3* (subtype VIII). *KBTBD4* hotspot insertions clustered in subtypes II (21%) and VII (14%), identifying *KBTBD4* as the dominant driver in these previously unexplained subtypes. Copy-number profiles and gene expression signatures varied substantially across subtypes, confirming that Group 3 and Group 4 represent collections of biologically distinct diseases rather than homogeneous entities.

**Genes / cancer types:** `GFI1B`, `MYC`, `KDM6A`, `ZMYM3`, `KBTBD4`, `PRDM6` · Group 3, Group 4 medulloblastoma

**Data availability:** `partial`
> Methylation subtype annotations for the 1,256-sample cohort are not fully represented in mbl_dkfz_2017. The WGS/WES cohort mutations and CNVs are available; subtype labels may be available as clinical attributes.

**Tool support:** `custom`

### F6: Novel KBTBD4 Hotspot In-Frame Insertions in Group 3/4

**Finding:** Recurrent in-frame insertions in *KBTBD4* — not detected in prior MB studies — were found in 20 cases, with 18/20 (90%) clustering across six amino acids at the Kelch domain substrate-binding interface.

**Detail:** The predominant Group 3 insertion (R313>PRR: proline-arginine at Arg313) differed from the Group 4 insertion (P311>PP: proline at Pro311), suggesting subgroup-specific oncogenic mechanisms. Homology modelling confirmed that the insertions converge on the known substrate-binding interface of the Kelch domain without disrupting overall domain structure, consistent with gain-of-function alteration of ubiquitin ligase substrate specificity. *KBTBD4* mutations were absent from other known driver-event categories in affected patients, supporting their role as primary oncogenic events in subtypes II and VII.

**Genes / cancer types:** `KBTBD4` · Group 3, Group 4 medulloblastoma

**Data availability:** `available`
> KBTBD4 in-frame insertion calls are included in the mbl_dkfz_2017 mutation data. Indel-level detail (specific insertion sequence) can be reviewed in the Mutations tab.

**Tool support:** `native`

### F7: Mutational Signatures Reveal Unexpected BRCA-Associated Signature 3 in Group 3/4

**Finding:** Analysis of 440,459 somatic mutations across 385 MB genomes detected 24 active signatures; signature 3 (linked to *BRCA1/2* deficiency) was unexpectedly observed in most Group 3 and Group 4 patients and a subset of SHH patients.

**Detail:** Signature 1 (age-associated) was the most prevalent across all subgroups. Signature 3, previously undetected in MB and associated with homologous recombination deficiency in breast, prostate, and pancreatic cancers, was pervasive in Group 3/4, raising the hypothesis that HR deficiency may contribute to MB pathogenesis and could be exploitable with PARP inhibitors. Subgroup-enriched signatures included signature 18 in Group 3 (P = 4.7×10⁻⁵) and signature 5 in Group 4 (P = 1.0×10⁻¹¹); the latter correlated positively with patient age. One hypermutator (>25,000 mutations) was identified with a somatic *POLE* mutation, and one case exhibited mismatch repair deficiency due to somatic *MLH1* mutation.

**Genes / cancer types:** `BRCA1`, `BRCA2`, `POLE`, `MLH1` · Group 3, Group 4, SHH medulloblastoma

**Data availability:** `not-available`
> Mutational signature exposures per sample are not directly displayed in cBioPortal for mbl_dkfz_2017. Raw mutation data (SNVs) are available and can be used to recompute signatures externally.

**Tool support:** `custom`

## Open Questions

- What are the normal cellular functions of KBTBD4 and PRDM6, and how do their somatic alterations specifically drive MB pathogenesis?
- Do the eight methylation-defined Group 3/4 subtypes correspond to distinct cells of origin, and can they be faithfully modelled in subtype-relevant in vitro/in vivo systems?
- Does pervasive signature 3 (HR deficiency) in Group 3/4 confer sensitivity to PARP inhibitors or platinum-based chemotherapy, and can this be prospectively exploited?
- Which WNT MB patients with SWI/SNF mutations (33%) would benefit most from PRC2/EZH2 inhibitor therapy, and how should this be tested clinically?
- Can TERT promoter mutation status reliably stratify adult SHH patients for de-escalation or targeted (SMO inhibitor) therapy?
- What germline predisposition landscape underlies the ~10–15% of WNT MBs lacking both CTNNB1 mutation and monosomy 6?

## Limitations

- WES cases (n=101) lack structural variant and noncoding mutation data, limiting comprehensive driver assignment relative to the WGS cohort.
- Methylation subtypes were defined in a cross-sectional cohort; longitudinal (primary vs. relapse) methylation dynamics are not characterised here.
- Functional validation of KBTBD4 and PRDM6 as bona fide oncogenes is absent; driver status is inferred from statistical and structural modelling evidence alone.
- CESAM (enhancer hijacking detection) requires matched RNA-seq and WGS; only 164 samples had both, limiting the power to detect rare hijacking events.
- Germline predisposition analysis is described as being detailed in a companion study; full integration of somatic and germline drivers is not presented in this paper.
- ChIP-seq and Hi-C data were generated on small subsets, restricting the breadth of enhancer and TAD boundary analyses.
