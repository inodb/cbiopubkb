## Summary

The FUTURE trial (NCT03805399) is a phase Ib/II umbrella trial that combines IHC-based molecular subtyping (LAR, IM, BLIS, MES) with targeted NGS to assign 69 heavily pretreated refractory metastatic TNBC patients to one of seven treatment arms. Overall ORR was 29.0% (vs. ~5–10% for historical monotherapy). The immunomodulatory (IM) subtype arm (anti-PD-1 + nab-paclitaxel, arm C) achieved the highest ORR of 52.6%, demonstrating proof-of-concept that subtype-guided precision therapy outperforms histology-only enrollment. Genomic landscape of refractory TNBC is described (TP53 72%, PIK3CA 18%) with TOP2A mutation and CD8 IHC score as potential immunotherapy response predictors. Data are available in cBioPortal as `brca_fuscc_2020`.

## Key Findings

### F1: Subtype-guided umbrella trial design improves ORR over histology-only approaches in refractory metastatic TNBC

**Finding:** Overall ORR was 29.0% (95% CI 18.7%–41.2%) in 69 ITT patients (40.0% in 50 per-protocol patients) with median treatment duration of 3.5 months vs. 2.4 months for the prior line (P=0.02). This substantially exceeds the ~5–10% ORR seen with unselected ICI monotherapy in comparable refractory populations.

**Detail:** Patients were heavily pretreated: 99% taxanes, 86% anthracyclines, 88% platinums, 81% vinorelbine. TNBC subtypes were assigned by three IHC markers (AR for LAR, CD8 for IM, FOXC1 for BLIS/MES), replacing the original mRNA classifier with a clinically feasible surrogate. IHC-to-mRNA concordance underpinned arm assignment. Disease control rate (CR+PR+SD) was 42.0% in ITT.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53`, `PIK3CA`, `PTEN`, `KMT2D`, `TSC2` · Triple-negative breast cancer

### F2: Anti-PD-1 plus nab-paclitaxel achieves 52.6% ORR in immunomodulatory (IM) TNBC — the highest efficacy arm

**Finding:** Arm C (camrelizumab, anti-PD-1 + nab-paclitaxel) in IM-subtype TNBC enrolled 19 patients and achieved ORR 52.6% (ITT) and 62.5% (per-protocol), with median duration of response 3.1 months. This dramatically exceeds ORR from unselected ICI±chemotherapy trials in ≥2nd-line TNBC.

**Detail:** The IM subtype is characterized by elevated immune-cell infiltration, high TIL content, and overexpression of immune-checkpoint genes (PD-1, PD-L1, CTLA-4). Prior mRNA profiling (465 Chinese TNBCs, FUSCC cohort) predicted ICI benefit in IM tumors. Six of ten responding patients had PR at the first post-baseline evaluation. Ongoing responses at cutoff (three patients, 2.6–6.5 months). Historical comparison: IMpassion130 first-line PD-L1+ ORR was 58.9%; FUTURE arm C achieves >50% in ≥3rd-line IM-selected patients.

**Genes / cancer types:** `CD274` (PD-L1), `PDCD1` (PD-1), `CTLA4` · Immunomodulatory TNBC

### F3: TOP2A mutation and CD8 IHC score are candidate predictors of immunotherapy response in IM TNBC

**Finding:** TOP2A mutations were found exclusively in 2 of 6 non-PR patients in arm C (immunotherapy arm), while absent in all 10 PR patients. Median CD8 IHC score was higher in non-progressive patients (30 vs. 22.5, P=0.25, trend). PD-L1 IHC positivity trended higher in responding patients.

**Detail:** TOP2A encodes DNA topoisomerase II-α; copy number variation and mRNA overexpression of TOP2A correlate with chemotherapy resistance. The two identified TOP2A mutations occurred in the DNA-binding domain and at the inter-domain junction, potentially disrupting DNA binding. These mutations may impair DNA damage sensing, reducing immunogenic cell death and ICI synergy. CD8 IHC staining was used prospectively to identify the IM subtype, reinforcing its value as a practical immune biomarker for ICI selection.

### F4: Anti-VEGFR therapy (arm E) shows 26.1% ORR in BRCA1/2 wild-type BLIS TNBC but with significant toxicity

**Finding:** Arm E (apatinib ± VP-16, anti-VEGFR) in BRCA1/2 wild-type BLIS subtype enrolled 23 patients with ORR 26.1% (ITT; 35.3% per-protocol) and median duration of response 4.1 months. One CR (supraclavicular lymph node, 6.6 months). However, substantial grade ≥3 toxicity (hypertension 22%, proteinuria 17%, hand-foot syndrome 17%) led to dose reduction in 40% of apatinib patients and protocol revision to lower dose.

**Detail:** The BLIS (basal-like immune-suppressed) subtype lacks immune infiltration and has enriched angiogenic signaling, providing the biologic rationale for VEGFR inhibition. Prior unselected apatinib trials showed 10.7% ORR in heavily pretreated TNBC. Subtype selection improved efficacy but heterogeneity of response and toxicity burden of apatinib 500 mg prompted revision to apatinib 250 mg or famitinib 20 mg. PTEN, RB1, and NOTCH3 mutations trended higher in progressors across arms.

### F5: Refractory metastatic TNBC genomic landscape shows TP53 dominance and metastasis-enriched mutations in PTPRD, TSC2, PLCG1

**Finding:** Most frequently mutated genes in refractory TNBC: TP53 (72%), PIK3CA (18%), PTEN (10%), KMT2D (9%), TSC2 (9%). Comparing metastatic vs. primary TNBC, several genes including PTPRD, TSC2, PLCG1, ARID1B, CREBBP, and FAM47C are enriched in metastatic samples. TP53 mutation frequency was higher in the US MSKCC cohort vs. the Chinese FUSCC cohort.

**Detail:** The metastasis-enriched genes suggest candidate drivers of progression and potential therapeutic targets. PTPRD acts as a negative regulator of breast cancer metastasis (IL-6/STAT3/E2F axis). TSC2 mutations in the PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway may mediate drug resistance. CREBBP participation in chromatin remodeling is linked to TNBC drug resistance via FOXM1. Ethnic differences in TP53 frequency (higher in American cohort) were not accompanied by other significant inter-racial genomic differences, suggesting convergent mutational outcomes.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53`, `PIK3CA`, `PTEN`, `KMT2D`, `TSC2`, `PTPRD`, `PLCG1`, `ARID1B`, `CREBBP` · Metastatic TNBC

## Open Questions

1. Can TOP2A mutation status and CD8 IHC score be prospectively validated as ICI response predictors in larger IM-TNBC trials?
2. What are the optimal VEGFR inhibitor dosing strategies and companion biomarkers for the BLIS subtype beyond BRCA1/2 wild-type status?
3. Why did the CDK4/6 inhibitor arm (arm B, LAR subtype) fail despite prior molecular rationale, and what alternative targets exist for LAR-TNBC?
4. Are PARP inhibitor failures in arm D (BLIS + germline BRCA1/2) attributable to platinum pre-exposure, small sample size, or a biologically distinct BRCA-mutant BLIS context?
5. Does the FUTURE subtyping approach remain valid after prior treatment alters IHC biomarker expression in the metastatic setting?

## Limitations

- Small sample sizes per arm (2–23 patients) preclude definitive efficacy conclusions; the trial is a proof-of-concept study.
- Median follow-up of 9.2 months is short for durability assessment and long-term OS analysis.
- IHC subtyping as a surrogate for mRNA subtyping introduces classification uncertainty; concordance was not formally validated in this specific cohort.
- TOP2A and CD8 findings are exploratory and require validation in independent cohorts.
- The trial enrolled only Chinese patients at a single institution, limiting generalizability across ethnicities and healthcare settings.
