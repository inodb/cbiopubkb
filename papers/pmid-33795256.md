## Summary

Large single-institution MSK-IMPACT cohort study of 487 lower esophageal and esophagogastric junction adenocarcinomas (EAC), prospectively sequenced between 2014–2019 with high-quality clinical annotation. Patients were stratified by treatment intent: curative (CIT, n=230, predominantly stages I–III) and palliative (PIT, n=257, predominantly stage IV). The study identifies 16 recurrent oncogenic driver alterations present in ≥5% of patients — 10/16 are amplifications — reflecting EAC's chromosomal instability–dominated biology. Higher genomic instability (more CNAs, whole-genome doubling, oncogenic drivers) is associated with more advanced disease. In multivariable Cox models, CDKN2A alterations, SMAD4 alterations, and KRAS amplifications independently predict worse OS; ERBB2 amplification predicts better OS (attributable in part to trastuzumab therapy). Cell Cycle and TGFβ pathway enrichment are independently prognostic. All data freely available in `egc_mskcc_2020` on cBioPortal.

## Key Findings

### F1: 16 recurrent oncogenic driver alterations; 10/16 are amplifications, reflecting chromosomal instability

**Finding:** 16 genes with ≥5% prevalence of oncogenic driver alterations were identified using OncoKB and Cancer Hotspots validation: ERBB2, KRAS, CCNE1, MYC, CCND1, MDM2, VEGFA, EGFR, CDK6, and CCND3 (all amplifications), plus TP53, CDKN2A, SMAD4, ARID1A mutations/deletions, and two others. TP53 mutation/deletion was the most prevalent (80% of patients in each cohort). MDM2 amplification (24% of TP53 wild-type tumors) and TP53 mutation were mutually exclusive (P<0.001, q<0.05).

**Detail:** Median TMB was 4.5 mutations/Mb overall, with no significant difference between CIT and PIT cohorts. Only 3.1% were MSI-high. TMB was negatively correlated with fraction of genome altered (Spearman −0.113, P=0.017), confirming the expected distinction between hypermutated MSI-high and CIN-dominant subtypes. Whole-genome doubling was present in 29% of CIT and 46% of PIT tumors (P<0.001). Median fraction of genome altered: 0.40 (CIT) vs. 0.50 (PIT).

**Genes / cancer types:** TP53, ERBB2, KRAS, CDKN2A, SMAD4, CCNE1, MYC, MDM2, ARID1A · Esophageal adenocarcinoma

### F2: CDKN2A, SMAD4, and KRAS amplification are independently prognostic of worse OS

**Finding:** In multivariable Cox models adjusting for age, clinical stage, tumor grade, and curative vs. palliative treatment intent, three genomic alterations were independently associated with worse OS: KRAS amplification (adjusted HR 1.83, 95% CI 1.31–2.55, P<0.001), SMAD4 alteration (adjusted HR 1.61, 95% CI 1.14–2.28, P=0.007), and CDKN2A alteration (adjusted HR 1.50, 95% CI 1.14–1.97, P=0.004). Higher number of oncogenic drivers also independently predicted worse OS (HR 1.11 per driver, P=0.030).

**Detail:** SMAD4 loss has been previously linked to poor prognosis and disease recurrence in EAC. KRAS amplification has been reported with dramatically increased mRNA expression and worse OS in Japanese EAC cohorts. CDKN2A alterations include both deletions and mutations; neither alone was prognostic in univariable analysis, suggesting the combination provides additional prognostic information. Adding genomics to clinicopathologic variables improved the model (Harrell's C-index 0.71 vs. 0.68 with clinicopathologic variables alone).

**Genes / cancer types:** KRAS, SMAD4, CDKN2A · Esophageal adenocarcinoma

### F3: ERBB2 amplification is independently associated with better OS — likely due to trastuzumab

**Finding:** ERBB2 amplification (22% overall; 13% CIT, 30% PIT, P<0.001) was independently associated with better OS in multivariable analysis (adjusted HR 0.65, 95% CI 0.48–0.90, P=0.009). Of 108 ERBB2-amplified tumors, 88 (81%) had HER2 overexpression by IHC or FISH, and 86 received trastuzumab (64 first-line, 22 second-line). ERBB2 amplification was also associated with better tumor differentiation (38% vs. 11% well/moderate, P<0.001) and trended toward mutual exclusivity with MDM2 (P<0.05, q<0.05).

**Detail:** 79/86 patients receiving trastuzumab had stage IV disease (FDA-approved indication). The survival benefit of ERBB2 amplification is likely multifactorial: trastuzumab efficacy, better differentiation (associated with chemosensitivity), and mutual exclusivity with resistance-conferring RTK-RAS/PI3K pathway alterations. Only 22 ERBB2-amplified patients did not receive trastuzumab, making it impossible to deconvolve drug benefit from tumor biology.

**Genes / cancer types:** ERBB2, MDM2, PIK3CA · Esophageal adenocarcinoma

### F4: Cell Cycle and TGFβ pathway enrichment are independently prognostic of worse OS

**Finding:** At the pathway level, Cell Cycle pathway alteration (HR 1.30, 95% CI 1.02–1.66, P=0.036) and TGFβ pathway alteration (HR 1.46, 95% CI 1.05–2.02, P=0.024) were independently associated with worse OS in multivariable models including clinicopathologic variables. Both pathways were significantly more prevalent in PIT (56% and enriched vs. CIT, respectively). Cell Cycle pathway enrichment was driven primarily by CDKN2A/B, CCNE1, and CCND1 alterations; TGFβ pathway enrichment by SMAD4 alterations.

**Detail:** 11 canonical TCGA PanCancer pathways were evaluated. The Cell Cycle pathway (56% vs. 40%, PIT vs. CIT, q<0.05) and RTK-RAS pathway (65% vs. 46%, q<0.05) were the two significantly enriched in advanced disease. TP53 pathway alteration was nearly universal (~80%) and was not associated with OS — consistent with a universal driver event not differentiating outcome. Neither Myc, Notch, Hippo, NRF2, PI3K, Wnt, nor DDR pathway alterations were independently prognostic.

### F5: Higher genomic instability distinguishes advanced (palliative-intent) from localized EAC

**Finding:** PIT patients had significantly higher fractions of genome altered (median 0.50 vs. 0.40, P<0.001), rate of whole-genome doubling (46% vs. 29%, P<0.001), number of altered pathways (median 3 vs. 2, P<0.001), and number of oncogenic drivers (median 3 vs. 2, P=0.001). These differences remained significant after adjusting for tumor purity and site of tissue sampling. Amplification load differences were particularly pronounced in PIT vs. CIT patients.

**Detail:** These findings suggest a model in which progressive chromosomal instability drives EAC from localized to metastatic disease. Recent clonal evolution studies (Noorani et al.) of EAC Barrett's progression similarly demonstrate increasing structural variant rates with disease progression. The correlation of genomic complexity with dismal metastatic prognosis mirrors observations in other CIN-dominant cancers (NSCLC, CRC). Importantly, TMB did not differ between CIT and PIT (median 4.4 vs. 4.9, P=0.59), confirming that chromosome-level instability rather than point mutation burden drives progression.

## Open Questions

- Can the CDKN2A/SMAD4/KRAS amplification prognostic model be validated in independent EAC cohorts (ICGC, TCGA) and used prospectively to guide clinical trial stratification?
- Does the survival benefit of ERBB2 amplification reflect intrinsically better biology (differentiation, fewer co-drivers) or exclusively trastuzumab therapy — and can ERBB2-amplified non-stage-IV patients benefit from neoadjuvant trastuzumab?
- What is the impact of KRAS amplification on response to emerging KRAS G12C inhibitors in EAC patients where KRAS is amplified rather than point-mutated?
- Do SMAD4-altered tumors show differential responses to TGFβ inhibitors currently under investigation in GI cancers?
- Can panel-based sequencing of neoadjuvant biopsies provide prognostic information that rivals or supplements pathologic staging (which is unavailable pre-surgery in neoadjuvant settings)?

## Limitations

- Single-institution (MSK) cohort may reflect referral bias toward more complex or refractory cases
- Panel-based (MSK-IMPACT) rather than whole-genome sequencing; structural variants and non-coding alterations not captured
- Three panel versions used (v1–v3); while all 16 driver genes are present across all panels, variant detection sensitivity may differ
- 191 patients had received some prior treatment before sequencing; though no significant effect on driver counts was found, treatment artifacts cannot be fully excluded
- SMAD4 and CDKN2A deletion detection may be limited by panel sensitivity at lower tumor purity
- Trastuzumab confounds the ERBB2 survival association; a drug-naïve cohort would be needed to isolate tumor-intrinsic effects
- No external validation cohort of comparable size with both genomics and clinical outcome data was available at time of publication
