## Summary

Comprehensive multi-omic genomic characterization of 261 patient-derived xenograft (PDX) models from the Pediatric Preclinical Testing Consortium (PPTC), spanning 37 unique molecular subtypes of childhood cancer. Assays performed: WES (n=240), whole-transcriptome RNA-seq (n=244), and SNP array CNA profiling (n=252). STR profiling confirmed identity for all 261 models. Competitive human-mouse mapping validated human content. PDX models faithfully recapitulate primary and relapsed pediatric cancer genomics — including histology-specific hallmark alterations, driver fusions, and copy number landscapes. TMB is significantly higher in relapse-derived vs. diagnosis-derived models. Machine learning classifiers based on expression data accurately infer *TP53* (AUROC=0.89) and *NF1* (AUROC=0.77) pathway inactivation. Dataset is a public resource for preclinical agent prioritization and rational pediatric trial design. Data available in `pptc_2019` on cBioPortal / PedcBioPortal.

## Key Findings

### F1: PDX models faithfully recapitulate histology-specific driver alterations across 37 pediatric cancer subtypes

**Finding:** PDX models faithfully maintained hallmark genomic alterations across all major pediatric cancer histologies: *EWSR1-FLI1* fusions in all sequenced Ewing sarcomas, *PAX3-FOXO1* fusions in all alveolar RMS, *BCR-ABL1* in all Ph+ ALL, *KMT2A* fusions in all MLL-ALL, *SMARCB1/SMARCA4* inactivation in all ATRTs, *MYCN* amplification in 66% of neuroblastomas, and *TP53* alteration in 82% of osteosarcomas. Oncogenic pathways (RAS, JAK-STAT, PI3K, p53, MYC) confirmed by GSEA.

**Detail:** The competitive human-mouse mapping approach (human WES reads aligned to hybrid hg19-mm10 reference) was validated biochemically by qPCR showing Pearson correlation R=0.943 between percent human reads and percent human DNA (p<2.2e-16, n=35). STR profiling confirmed identity for all 261 models; 26 models were excluded following contamination/misidentification QC. Expression profiles clustered strongly by tissue of origin and molecular subtype (TumorMap visualization). Histology-specific pathway enrichment was confirmed by GSEA across 15 histology groups. This fidelity to primary tumor biology enables PDX models to serve as valid surrogates for clinical genomic context.

**Genes / cancer types:** EWSR1, FLI1, PAX3, FOXO1, BCR, ABL1, KMT2A, SMARCB1, SMARCA4, MYCN, TP53 · All 37 pediatric cancer subtypes

### F2: *CDKN2A/B* focal deletions are a pan-pediatric hallmark, especially in leukemias and Ewing sarcoma

**Finding:** *CDKN2A/B* focal deletions were the most recurrent single alteration across multiple pediatric tumor types: 45–48% of BCP-ALL models (mostly homozygous), 72–76% of ETP/T-ALL models, 60% of Ewing sarcoma models (homozygous, mutually exclusive with *STAG2* mutations), and recurrent in medulloblastoma. In BCP-ALL, *CDKN2A/B* deletions were present alongside enriched RAS pathway (*KRAS* 30%, *NRAS* 18%) and JAK-STAT pathway (*JAK2/3* 15%) alterations.

**Detail:** The mutual exclusivity of *CDKN2A/B* deletion with *STAG2* mutations in Ewing sarcoma (as previously reported in primary tumors) was recapitulated in PDX models, validating the biological integrity of the models. In BCP-ALL, *ETV6-RUNX1* fusions were detected in 27% of models (88% pairing ETV6 with RUNX1), *PAX5* rearrangements in 18%, and *CRLF2* rearrangements in 6%. Ph-like ALL models showed *CRLF2* fusions in 42% (predominantly P2RY8-CRLF2) and *JAK2* rearrangements in 55%. The breadth of *CDKN2A/B* deletion across childhood cancers supports CDK4/6 inhibitor trials in pediatric oncology basket designs.

**Genes / cancer types:** CDKN2A, CDKN2B, STAG2, ETV6, RUNX1, CRLF2, JAK2 · BCP-ALL, T-ALL, ETP-ALL, Ph-like ALL, Ewing sarcoma

**Data availability:** `available`

**Tool support:** `native`
> OncoPrint with homozygous deletion filter for CDKN2A/B in `pptc_2019`. Co-occurrence/mutual exclusivity tool for CDKN2A vs. STAG2 in Ewing sarcoma. Fusion browser in Mutations tab.

### F3: Relapse-derived PDX models show significantly higher TMB and enrichment of hallmark gene alterations vs. diagnosis-derived models

**Finding:** Relapse-derived PDX models have significantly higher TMB than diagnosis-derived models across all histologies (median 3.08 vs. 2.57 Mut/Mb; Wilcoxon p=2.2e-5). In paired diagnosis-relapse models, TMB was significantly higher at relapse (median 98.0 vs. 27.5 mutations; Wilcoxon p=0.0083). Histology-specific increases in key driver alteration frequencies were observed in relapsed vs. diagnostic oncoprints across BCP-ALL, T-ALL, neuroblastoma, and osteosarcoma.

**Detail:** The elevated TMB at relapse is consistent with known clonal evolution under treatment pressure: post-therapy tumors accumulate additional somatic mutations through mutagenic therapy (alkylating agents, topoisomerase inhibitors) and/or selection of pre-existing resistant subclones. The osteosarcoma subgroup was notable in showing no significant TMB difference between diagnosis and relapse (Wilcoxon p=0.42), consistent with the globally rearranged osteosarcoma genome that is established early and does not accumulate many additional SNVs with therapy. The relapse-enriched nature of the PDX cohort (27% relapse-derived) better reflects the clinical context of early-phase pediatric trials, where patients are heavily pretreated.

**Genes / cancer types:** TP53, CDKN2A, ALK, KRAS, NRAS, MYCN · BCP-ALL, T-ALL, neuroblastoma, osteosarcoma

### F4: ML expression classifiers accurately identify *TP53* and *NF1* pathway inactivation beyond simple mutation calls

**Finding:** Machine learning classifiers trained on TCGA PanCanAtlas gene expression data accurately predict *TP53* pathway inactivation (AUROC=0.89) and *NF1* inactivation (AUROC=0.77) in pediatric PDX transcriptomes. Models with *TP53* pathway gene alterations had significantly higher classifier scores (mean 0.790 vs. 0.419 in WT; Wilcoxon p<2.2e-16). All 32 osteosarcoma models with RNA-seq were predicted as *TP53*-pathway-inactive, consistent with the near-universal *TP53* alteration rate in osteosarcoma.

**Detail:** Many PDX models annotated as *TP53*-WT had high *TP53* inactivation scores, revealing pathway inactivation through *MDM2* amplification, *RB1* loss, or other *TP53*-pathway interactors that phenocopy direct *TP53* mutation. Overall copy number burden (number of SNP array breakpoints) correlated significantly with *TP53* classifier score (R=0.51, p=1.8e-17, n=239), mechanistically linking chromosomal instability to p53 pathway dysfunction. The Ras pathway classifier performed poorly (AUROC=0.55), likely reflecting fundamental differences between pediatric and adult Ras signaling transcriptional programs. These classifiers enable rational drug prioritization: targeting *TP53*- or *NF1*-pathway-inactivated models for appropriate agents rather than relying on mutation status alone.

**Genes / cancer types:** TP53, MDM2, RB1, NF1, KRAS, NRAS, HRAS · All pediatric cancer types with RNA-seq

**Data availability:** `available`

### F5: Histology-specific driver fusions and chromothripsis patterns faithfully recapitulated in PDX models

**Finding:** High-confidence fusion annotation (925 unique high-confidence fusions; 92 known oncogenic driver fusions) confirmed fidelity of PDX fusion landscape. Osteosarcoma models harbored *TP53* intron-1 fusions as a mechanism of TP53 inactivation. Neuroblastoma models showed 17% with high breakpoint density (HBD) on chromosomes 2, 5, 16, 17, 19 — consistent with chromothripsis at *MYCN*, *TERT*, and 17q loci. 97% of osteosarcomas had HBD; 70% had HBD on ≥5 chromosomes, reflecting globally rearranged genomes. DIPG models confirmed high H3F3A/B expression; ETMR models showed C19MC amplification.

**Detail:** A total of 25% (64/252) of SNP-profiled models showed HBD on ≥1 chromosome. Medulloblastoma HBDs were recurrent on chromosomes 2, 8, 14, and 17, consistent with published chromothripsis reports. In five diagnosis-relapse pairs, four driver fusions (PAX5-RP11-465M18.1, IGH-MYC, CIC-DUX4, TP53-TNR) present at diagnosis were absent at relapse, suggesting alternative initiating events arose at relapse. Astrocytoma model NCH-MN-1, initially suspected as ATRT, was reclassified by molecular profiling as a high-grade glioma (BRAF V600E + CDKN2A/B deletion; no SMARCB1 inactivation), demonstrating the diagnostic utility of the dataset.

**Genes / cancer types:** TP53, MYCN, H3F3A, H3F3B, EWSR1, FLI1, PAX3, FOXO1, CIC, DUX4, BRAF · All pediatric cancer types; osteosarcoma, neuroblastoma, medulloblastoma, DIPG, ATRT, Ewing sarcoma

## Open Questions

- Can the *TP53* pathway inactivation classifier score predict sensitivity to drugs that exploit p53 loss (e.g., MDM2 inhibitors, Wee1 inhibitors) across pediatric cancer types in vivo?
- Do the PDX models with elevated *CDKN2A/B* deletion rates benefit from CDK4/6 inhibitors preclinically, and can these data support a pediatric basket trial?
- Why does osteosarcoma not show significantly increased TMB at relapse despite its dramatically rearranged genome — is structural genomic evolution (chromothripsis) the dominant mechanism of progression?
- For the four driver fusions absent at relapse in paired PDX models, what are the alternative initiating events at relapse, and can they be identified by whole-genome sequencing?
- Can the pan-pediatric PDX RNA-seq dataset be used to identify expression-based subgroups that predict drug sensitivity (e.g., for antibody-drug conjugates, T-cell engagers) beyond current mutation-based biomarkers?

## Limitations

- No paired normal DNA for most models; TMB values are inflated by unfiltered rare germline and private variants; mutation rate trends across histologies are informative but absolute values are overestimates
- WES without paired germline likely inflates somatic TMB; whole-genome sequencing needed to detect all structural variants (e.g., non-coding drivers, complex rearrangements, DIPG histone mutations not detectable by WES)
- PDX cohort biased toward high-risk, refractory pediatric cancers; does not represent the full spectrum of newly diagnosed or low-risk pediatric tumors
- Missing histologies: AML, JMML, lymphomas, retinoblastoma, melanoma, thyroid malignancies, histone-mutant midline gliomas (including H3K27M DIPG)
- Ras pathway ML classifier performed poorly in pediatric context (AUROC=0.55), limiting utility for preclinical drug selection targeting RAS-driven pediatric tumors
