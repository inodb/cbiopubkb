## Summary

The PRINCE trial is a randomized phase 2 study evaluating nivolumab (anti-PD-1) and/or sotigalimab (CD40 agonist antibody) with gemcitabine/nab-paclitaxel chemotherapy in 105 patients with first-line metastatic pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC). Only the nivolumab/chemotherapy arm met its primary endpoint of 1-year overall survival (57.7% vs. historical 35%, p=0.006; median OS 16.7 months). Sotigalimab combinations did not reach statistical significance. Comprehensive multi-omic biomarker analyses of serial blood and tumor biopsies uncovered treatment-specific immune correlates of benefit: nivo/chemo responders had baseline activated effector-memory CD8 T cells and T follicular helper cells; sotiga/chemo responders had elevated cross-presenting dendritic cells and CD4 T cell infiltration. The study provides a hypothesis-generating immune atlas for PDAC chemoimmunotherapy and defines distinct CD40 vs. PD-1 mechanism-of-action signatures.

## Key Findings

### F1: Nivolumab/chemotherapy met the primary 1-year OS endpoint; sotigalimab combinations did not

**Finding:** Nivo/chemo achieved a 1-year OS rate of 57.7% (one-sided p=0.006 vs. 35% historical; median OS 16.7 months, ORR 50%), meeting the primary endpoint. Sotiga/chemo (1-yr OS 48.1%, p=0.062, median OS 11.4m) and sotiga/nivo/chemo (1-yr OS 41.3%, p=0.223, median OS 10.1m) did not meet significance thresholds. The study was not powered for inter-arm comparisons.

**Detail:** Efficacy population included phase 2 randomized patients plus 12 DLT-evaluable phase 1b patients at the recommended phase 2 dose of sotiga. PFS was similar across arms (6.4, 7.3, 6.7 months respectively). DCR was 74%, 78%, and 69%. Safety was comparable across arms; cytokine release syndrome observed only in sotiga-containing arms (24–34% incidence). The single MSI-H patient (nivo/chemo arm) had OS of 8.1 months — not driving the arm's result. KRAS/TP53/SMAD4 mutation frequencies were balanced across arms and not associated with OS differences.

### F2: Nivo/chemo benefit associated with baseline activated effector-memory CD8 T cells and T follicular helper cells

**Finding:** In the nivo/chemo arm, longer OS associated with higher pre-treatment frequencies of activated (CD38+) effector-memory CD8 T cells (co-expressing PD-1, 2B4, Eomes, Tbet) and T follicular helper (Tfh; CD4+PD-1+CXCR5+) cells — Tfh had the highest predictive value in multivariable modeling. These pre-treatment immune features suggest a less immunosuppressed baseline tumor microenvironment is required for anti-PD-1 benefit in PDAC.

**Detail:** CD38+ EM CD8 T cells: higher pre-treatment frequency in >1-year survivors by KM analysis (log-rank test). Tfh cells: CCR7+, TCF-1+, ICOS+ co-expression; most differentiating at late on-treatment timepoint (C4D1). Antigen-experienced (PD-1+CD39+) EM1 and CM CD4 T cells also associated with longer survival in nivo/chemo. Tumor gene expression: patients with longer survival had lower pre-treatment TNF-α/NFκB, TGF-β, IL6/JAK-STAT3 signatures and higher oxidative phosphorylation/fatty acid metabolism signatures. Lower intratumoral iNOS+ macrophages at baseline also associated with longer OS in nivo/chemo arm (not sotiga arms).

### F3: Sotiga/chemo benefit linked to baseline cross-presenting dendritic cells and intratumoral CD4 T cells

**Finding:** Distinct from nivo/chemo, survival after sotiga/chemo was associated with higher pre-treatment frequencies of cross-presenting DCs (CD1c+CD141+ in circulation), greater on-treatment differentiated CD141+ DCs (C1D15), and higher intratumoral non-proliferating (Ki-67−) CD4 T cells (both conventional and Foxp3+ regulatory). Higher circulating HLA-DR+CCR7+ B cells and type-1 (Tbet+Eomes+) CD4 T cells pre-treatment also associated with longer OS.

**Detail:** CD141+ cross-presenting DCs had the highest predictive value in the sotiga/chemo multivariable model. On-treatment soluble CD83 and soluble ICOSL (DC maturation markers) associated with longer OS. Tumor expression: Th1 and IFN-γ response signatures associated with longer survival (unique to sotiga/chemo arm); E2F signaling signature associated with shorter survival. The CD40 agonist mechanism is distinct: it activates APCs (DCs/macrophages), and benefit correlated with APC compartment competence rather than T cell effectors — consistent with preclinical PDAC models showing CD40 reprograms tumor-associated macrophages.

### F4: Sotiga/nivo/chemo triple combination showed no additive benefit; distinct resistance-associated immune phenotypes identified

**Finding:** The triple combination (sotiga/nivo/chemo) did not outperform either doublet and showed no additive benefit identifiable by biomarker analysis. Uniquely, higher pre-treatment frequencies of chronically activated (CD38+) non-naive CD4 and CD8 T cells were associated with shorter OS in this arm — the opposite direction from nivo/chemo, suggesting that combined PD-1 + CD40 agonism may paradoxically be counterproductive in certain immune contexts.

**Detail:** No biomarkers predictive in the other two arms were predictive in the sotiga/nivo/chemo arm. Lower on-treatment (C1D15) CCR7+CD11b+CD27− B cells associated with longer OS in triple arm only. Chronically activated circulating T cells (expressing CCR7, TCF-1, CTLA-4, PD-1, ICOS in CD4 compartment; CCR7, PD-1, Tbet, Eomes, TCF-1, 2B4 in CD8 compartment) at baseline associated with shorter survival. The mechanistic explanation may involve competitive or interfering pharmacodynamics between CD40 agonism and PD-1 blockade at the level of DC-T cell interactions.

### F5: Gemcitabine/nab-paclitaxel alone has limited immunogenic activity; IFN-γ increases observed with all regimens

**Finding:** Longitudinal blood profiling showed that all three treatment arms induced significant increases in IFN-γ and proliferating (Ki-67+) non-naive CD8 and CD4 T cells on-treatment. However, T cell activation (HLA-DR+, CD38+) and chemokine induction (CXCL9, CXCL10) occurred earlier and more strongly in nivo-containing arms, suggesting nivolumab drives a more rapid and potent T cell response on top of the chemotherapy backbone.

**Detail:** IFN-γ increases: all arms (Wilcoxon signed-rank, on-treatment vs. baseline). CXCL9/CXCL10 increases earlier in nivo-containing arms (C1D15 and C2D1 vs. later in sotiga/chemo). Soluble PD-1 (sPD-1) increased on-treatment in nivo-containing arms but not sotiga/chemo. Integrated DIABLO analysis showed CXCL10, CXCL9, sPD-1 correlated with activated proliferating T cells and were associated with nivo-containing arms specifically. Pharmacodynamic effects confirm biological activity of both drugs but do not predict which patients benefit from sotigalimab specifically.

## Open Questions

- Can the pre-treatment Tfh and activated EM CD8 T cell signatures be prospectively validated to select PDAC patients for anti-PD-1-based chemoimmunotherapy?
- Why does sotiga/nivo/chemo not outperform either doublet — is there mechanistic interference between CD40 agonism and PD-1 blockade in PDAC specifically?
- Do KRAS mutation subtypes (G12D, G12V, G12R) or co-occurring SMAD4/TP53 status modulate immune microenvironment in ways that predict ICI or CD40 agonist benefit?
- Would patients with TGF-β-high tumor microenvironments benefit from TGF-β inhibition to sensitize to anti-PD-1?
- Are there genomic biomarkers (TMB, HRD, KRAS allele) in the WES data that correlate with the immune biomarkers of benefit?
- Can the iNOS+ macrophage or cross-presenting DC signatures be assessed from standard IHC to enable enrollment enrichment in future trials?

## Limitations

- Phase 2; not powered for inter-arm comparisons; multiple testing not corrected in exploratory biomarker analyses
- WES available for only 60% of patients; genomic subgroup analyses underpowered
- Only one MSI-H patient — cannot assess whether rare PDAC patients with high TMB drive the nivo/chemo signal
- Tumor biopsy tissue limited in quantity; mIF analyses performed on small paired subsets (n=3–6 per arm)
- Tumor expression analyses restricted to liver metastases (64% of biopsies) to control for tissue-of-origin effects; results may not generalize to primary or other metastatic sites
- Historical control OS of 35% at 1 year (gemcitabine/nab-paclitaxel) may underestimate current standard-of-care outcomes in well-selected patients
- Sotigalimab development was subsequently discontinued; clinical relevance of CD40 agonist biomarkers depends on successor agents
