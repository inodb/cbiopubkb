## Summary

Analysis of 3,880 MSK-IMPACT breast cancer samples from 3,117 patients shows that APOBEC3 mutational signatures (driven by A3A and A3B) are the most prevalent dominant mutational process in metastatic breast cancer and are enriched in post-treatment hormone receptor-positive (HR+) tumors. APOBEC3-dominant HR+ metastatic breast cancer (MBC) is independently associated with shorter progression-free survival on CDK4/6 inhibitor plus endocrine therapy (HR 1.5, P = 2.4×10⁻⁴). Mechanistically, APOBEC3 activity causally promotes therapy resistance by inducing characteristic alterations in resistance-associated genes including site-specific *ESR1*, *PIK3CA*, and *RB1* alterations, highlighting APOBEC3 signature as a predictive biomarker and therapeutic target.

## Key Findings

### F1: APOBEC3 is the dominant mutational process in metastatic breast cancer, especially HR+ subtypes

**Finding:** APOBEC3 is the most prevalent dominant mutational process in the MSK-IMPACT breast cancer cohort, with significantly higher prevalence in metastatic vs. primary HR+ and TNBC samples.

**Detail:** APOBEC3-dominant signature was present in 28.7% of metastatic HR+/HER2− samples vs. 15.7% of primary, and 16.9% of metastatic TNBC vs. 8.4% of primary. APOBEC3-dominant HR+/HER2− MBC had significantly higher FGA than primary tumors. APOBEC3-dominant HR+/HER2− tumors had lower median FGA than non-APOBEC3 tumors, but significantly higher TMB. Among TMB-high (≥10 mut/Mb) MBC eligible for tumor-agnostic anti-PD-1 therapy, APOBEC3 was the dominant process in most HR+ subtypes. Invasive lobular breast cancer was associated more frequently with APOBEC3 signature regardless of sample type.

**Genes / cancer types:** `APOBEC3A` · `APOBEC3B` · HR+ breast cancer · TNBC

### F2: APOBEC3-dominant MBC has shorter PFS on CDK4/6i plus endocrine therapy

**Finding:** APOBEC3-dominant HR+/HER2− MBC treated with first-line CDK4/6 inhibitor plus ET has independently shorter median PFS compared with tumors with other dominant signatures (HR 1.5; 95% CI 1.2–1.8; P = 2.4×10⁻⁴; n=549).

**Detail:** HRD-dominant MBC also had shorter PFS on CDK4/6i + ET (HR 1.8; 95% CI 1.4–2.2; P = 6.4×10⁻⁷). For first-line ET monotherapy (n=111), APOBEC3-dominant tumors had numerically shorter median PFS (8.6 vs 15.6 months, HR 1.4; 95% CI 0.9–2.2; P = 0.12, non-significant). No significant effect was seen with everolimus-based combinations or chemotherapy in smaller cohorts.

**Genes / cancer types:** HR+/HER2− breast cancer · MBC

### F3: APOBEC3 activity causally promotes resistance to endocrine and HER2-targeted therapies

**Finding:** Cell line experiments with doxycycline-inducible A3A and A3B overexpression demonstrate that APOBEC3 enzymatic activity causally accelerates acquisition of resistance to fulvestrant, abemaciclib, palbociclib, lapatinib, neratinib, AKT inhibitor MK2206, and T-DXd.

**Detail:** A3A-WT cells acquired fulvestrant resistance significantly faster than A3A-E72Q (catalytically inactive) cells; 2/3 A3B-WT replicates acquired resistance vs. 0/3 A3B-E255Q replicates. APOBEC3-positive BT-474 cells acquired lapatinib resistance faster than A3A-KO cells. Resistance was associated with continued accumulation of APOBEC3 signature mutations during drug pressure by WGS. APOBEC3-positive MDA-MB-453 cells also showed selective resistance to T-DXd.

**Genes / cancer types:** `APOBEC3A` · `APOBEC3B` · HR+ breast cancer · HER2+ breast cancer

### F4: APOBEC3 induces site-specific resistance alterations in ESR1, PIK3CA, and RB1

**Finding:** APOBEC3-context mutations in *ESR1* E380Q and *PIK3CA* helical domain mutations (E545K, E542K) are specifically enriched in APOBEC3-dominant, post-treatment HR+/HER2− samples; *RB1* loss is also enriched.

**Detail:** ESR1 E380Q enrichment: q = 7.8×10⁻¹² in APOBEC3-dominant post-treatment HR+/HER2− samples. PIK3CA E545K: P = 2×10⁻¹⁰; E542K: P = 4.5×10⁻⁶. In contrast, highly activating ESR1 mutations in the helix 11–12 loop (L536X, Y537X, D538X)—not APOBEC3-context substitutions—were rare (8%) in APOBEC3-dominant samples, suggesting ESR1 reactivation is not the dominant resistance mechanism in APOBEC3-driven tumors. 51.9% of APOBEC3-context mutations were exclusive to later-stage samples vs. 32.3% shared with earlier or treatment-naive samples (Fisher's exact P significant).

**Genes / cancer types:** `ESR1` · `PIK3CA` · `RB1` · HR+/HER2− breast cancer · MBC

### F5: APOBEC3 activity is detectable pre-treatment and may serve as a predictive biomarker

**Finding:** Evidence of APOBEC3 mutagenesis in pre-treatment samples establishes its pervasive role in breast cancer evolution independent of treatment exposure and supports its utility as a predictive biomarker.

**Detail:** APOBEC3-dominant signature was detectable in primary treatment-naive samples (15.7% of primary HR+/HER2−). An HR+ tumor progressively evolved to 100% APOBEC3 exposure over >20 years of therapeutic pressure (WGS of paired primary-metastatic). APOBEC3 activity was associated with kataegis, chromothripsis, and structural variants in both cell lines and paired patient samples.

**Genes / cancer types:** `APOBEC3A` · `APOBEC3B` · Breast cancer

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Can APOBEC3 signature score from a pre-treatment biopsy prospectively stratify patients for CDK4/6i + ET vs. alternative regimens?
- Does combining APOBEC3 inhibition (e.g., targeting A3A/A3B directly) with CDK4/6i improve durability of response?
- Is APOBEC3-mediated *RB1* loss the dominant CDK4/6i resistance mechanism in APOBEC3-dominant tumors?
- Do APOBEC3-dominant tumors with high TMB respond better to immune checkpoint inhibitors given their mutational burden?
- Does the enrichment of APOBEC3 signature in invasive lobular breast cancer reflect lineage-specific mutagenic vulnerability?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Mutational signature calling from targeted panel (MSK-IMPACT) requires ≥5 SNVs; small tumors may not qualify.
- Survival analyses are retrospective and not randomized; confounding by indication is possible.
- Cell line overexpression models may not fully recapitulate endogenous APOBEC3 episodic activity.
- Only 5 paired primary-metastatic WGS pairs profiled; limited power to detect all resistance mechanisms.
- APOBEC3-context enrichment analysis uses permutation testing to address hypermutator bias but is not fully resolved.
