## Summary

Updated analysis of a Phase II trial (NCT02954536) of pembrolizumab + trastuzumab + chemotherapy (PTC) in HER2+ metastatic esophagogastric cancer (EGC, n=37), combined with a retrospective MSK institutional cohort (n=226 trastuzumab-treated). Median PFS 13 months, median OS 27 months, ORR 89% in the phase II cohort at 44-month median follow-up. Key findings: (1) homogeneous HER2 IHC 3+ expression predicts improved PFS; (2) ⁸⁹Zr-trastuzumab PET avidity correlates with lesion-level response; (3) early ctDNA clearance by 9 weeks predicts durable PFS; (4) scRNA-seq identifies *MT1H/MT1E/MT2A/MSMB* expression as a trastuzumab resistance program; (5) *ERBB2* amplification is a positive, and *MYC*/*CDKN2A/B* alterations are negative, PFS prognostic features. Data are publicly available at `egc_trap_ccr_msk_2023` on cBioPortal.

## Key Findings

### F1: Homogeneous HER2 IHC 3+ overexpression predicts superior PFS from trastuzumab-based therapy

**Finding:** Among 193 trastuzumab-treated HER2+ EGC patients, uniform IHC 3+ expression was associated with significantly longer median PFS (15 vs. 8.5 months; CI 0.43–0.86) compared to patients with IHC 2+ or heterogeneous HER2 expression.

**Detail:** 67% (129/193) of patients had uniformly high HER2 IHC 3+. ERBB2 amplification was present in 88% of IHC 3+ tumors vs. 36% of IHC 2+/heterogeneous (P=1.8×10⁻¹²), explaining much of the IHC association. Intrapatient HER2 heterogeneity — HER2+ primary and HER2− metastatic site — was identified in a subset and was associated with poorer outcomes and resistance. More than half of progressing patients (52%) became HER2-negative upon progression.

### F2: ctDNA clearance by 9 weeks predicts durable progression-free survival

**Finding:** Patients who cleared all tumor-matched ctDNA alterations by 9 weeks had substantially longer PFS than those who did not (HR 0.18; 95% CI 0.06–0.53); 76% (13/17) who cleared ctDNA achieved ≥6-month PFS vs. 17% (1/6) who did not.

**Detail:** ctDNA decline in maxVAF by week 3 (after a single dose of pembrolizumab + trastuzumab) predicted 6-month PFS: 9/12 (75%) with declining maxVAF achieved ≥6-month PFS vs. 0/4 (0%) with rising maxVAF. ctDNA dynamics were compared with lesion-level CT: patients lacking ERBB2-amplified ctDNA had no responding lesions by 3-week CT, while 56% of patients with tissue-only ERBB2 amplification did respond, implicating intrapatient HER2 heterogeneity. Uniform CT response (all lesions improving) at 9 weeks also predicted improved PFS (HR 0.38; 95% CI 0.17–0.81).

### F3: ⁸⁹Zr-trastuzumab PET intensity correlates with lesion-level response to trastuzumab

**Finding:** All 15 intensely ⁸⁹Zr-trastuzumab-avid lesions (SUV ≥10) decreased in size after a single dose of pembrolizumab + trastuzumab, vs. only 9/32 (8%) non-intensely avid lesions (P=2.4×10⁻⁶). All 4 patients with ≥1 intensely avid lesion achieved ≥6-month PFS.

**Detail:** 8 of 25 on-protocol patients underwent ⁸⁹Zr-trastuzumab PET; 7/8 had ≥1 PET-avid lesion. Significant intrapatient variability in PET avidity (66% of 47 lesions PET-avid; 48% of those were intensely avid). PET non-avid lesions were enriched in patients with HER2 heterogeneity or lack of ERBB2 amplification. This noninvasive imaging modality can reveal intrapatient HER2 expression heterogeneity not captured by single-site biopsy.

### F4: scRNA-seq identifies MT1H, MT1E, MT2A, and MSMB as trastuzumab resistance program

**Finding:** GeneVector analysis of paired pre- and on-treatment scRNA-seq data identified *MT1H*, *MT1E*, *MT2A* (metallothionein isoforms), and *MSMB* (microseminoprotein-beta) expression as associated with clonal expansion despite trastuzumab + chemotherapy, representing a putative transcriptional resistance program.

**Detail:** scRNA-seq of 1,116 viable cells from an on-treatment biopsy showed extensive ERBB2 expression heterogeneity and co-occurring high FGFR3 expression in a subpopulation. A pretreatment subclonal FGFR3-TACC3 fusion (not detected in tissue but identified by ctDNA) expanded over time. Clones expressing the MT/MSMB program persisted and expanded relative to HER2-expressing clones that were rapidly cleared. MT2A has been previously associated with chemotherapy resistance; MSMB-mediated MAPK activation has been linked to VEGF inhibitor resistance.

### F5: ERBB2 amplification, MYC, and CDKN2A/B alterations independently predict PFS in multivariable analysis

**Finding:** In a multivariable Cox regression model of 217 trastuzumab-treated MSK patients (with or without PD-1 inhibitor), *ERBB2* amplification was a positive prognostic factor for PFS, while oncogenic alterations in *MYC* and *CDKN2A/B* were negative prognostic factors.

**Detail:** Univariate analysis also identified KRAS, MYC, CCND1, and CDKN2A/B alterations as associated with inferior PFS, and fewer disease sites and ERBB2 amplification as associated with superior PFS. TP53 alterations were excluded from multivariable model due to high correlation with ERBB2 amplification status. The prognostic impact of PD-1 inhibitor addition was not separable from trastuzumab+chemotherapy in multivariable analysis in this institutional cohort, despite impressive trial results.

## Open Questions

- Can ctDNA clearance at 9 weeks be validated prospectively as a decision point for adaptive therapy escalation/de-escalation in HER2+ EGC?
- Does MT1H/MT1E/MT2A/MSMB expression define a clinically targetable resistance subtype, and what drugs overcome this program?
- Is ⁸⁹Zr-trastuzumab PET practical/scalable as a pre-treatment screening tool, or can PET avidity be replaced by a simpler biomarker?
- Why do MYC and CDKN2A/B co-occur with HER2 amplification and independently worsen prognosis — is this via CDK4/6 pathway co-activation?
- How does adding pembrolizumab mechanistically augment trastuzumab efficacy (T-cell and dendritic cell trafficking changes) and which patients benefit most from the immunotherapy component?

## Limitations

- Phase II cohort is small (n=37), single-arm, single-institution; no randomized comparison arm
- ⁸⁹Zr-trastuzumab PET sub-study is very small (n=8) and exploratory; findings require prospective validation
- scRNA-seq resistance program (MT1H/MT1E/MT2A/MSMB) identified in a limited number of patients from an external dataset; requires orthogonal validation
- Multivariable PFS analysis does not definitively separate PD-1 inhibitor contribution from chemotherapy + trastuzumab backbone
- Loss of HER2 expression at progression assessed in only ~50% of progressing patients; full resistance mechanism picture is incomplete
- ctDNA analysis limited by the requirement for matched tumor-tissue sequencing to define "tumor-matched" ctDNA alterations
