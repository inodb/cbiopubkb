## Summary

This multi-cohort retrospective study characterizes the genomic landscape and immune microenvironment of solitary fibrous tumors (SFTs) — rare mesenchymal tumors defined by *NAB2-STAT6* fusions — in 408 patients across 4 Chinese institutions (discovery: SYSUCC n=131; validation: FAHSYSU n=115, CHCAMS1 n=101, CHCAMS2 n=61). Using a 1021-gene targeted NGS panel plus comprehensive IHC, the authors identify *IDH1* p.R132S as an actionable mutation in 6.9% of SFTs, characterize high PD-L1 expression (24.4%) and macrophage-dominant immune infiltration as therapeutic targets, and develop an integrated 4-variable risk model (mitotic count, Ki-67+ density, CD163+ density, *MTOR* mutation) that outperforms the WHO classification, Demicco model, and G-score for predicting disease progression. The model is validated in both non-CNS and CNS SFTs.

## Key Findings

### F1: IDH1 p.R132S is an actionable mutation enriched in high-grade SFTs

**Finding:** *IDH1* p.R132S — the same mutation targeted by FDA-approved ivosidenib in AML and cholangiocarcinoma — occurs in 6.9% (9/131) of SFTs and is enriched in malignant, high-cellularity, and high-nuclear-pleomorphism cases.

**Detail:** This is the first systematic report of *IDH1* mutations in SFTs, likely missed in prior studies due to small sample sizes and panel limitations. The mutation co-occurred with high PD-L1 expression in 2 cases, suggesting potential for combination IDH1 inhibitor + immune checkpoint therapy. *IDH1/2* inhibitors (ivosidenib, enasidenib) are approved in other IDH1-mutated cancers; this finding opens a therapeutic avenue for refractory high-grade SFT.

**Genes / cancer types:** `IDH1` (p.R132S) · Solitary fibrous tumor

**Data availability:** `partial`

**Tool support:** `native`

### F2: Macrophage-dominant immune microenvironment and PD-L1 expression suggest immunotherapy combination strategies

**Finding:** The predominant tumor-infiltrating immune cell type in SFTs is macrophages (CD163+ tumor-associated macrophages, TAMs). High macrophage infiltration significantly associates with shorter PFS. PD-L1 is highly expressed in tumor or immune cells in 24.4% (32/131) of cases; high CD20+ B-cell density co-occurs with PD-L1+ tumors and associates with long PFS.

**Detail:** High CD163+ cell density was an independent prognostic factor and included in the final risk model. PD-L1 expression (≥20% of tumor cells or ≥25% of immune cells) supports exploring checkpoint inhibition in a biomarker-selected subset. CSF1R inhibition (targeting TAMs) combined with PD-1/PD-L1 blockade is a rational therapeutic hypothesis. Pazopanib already shows ~50% ORR and is ESMO-recommended first-line; angiogenesis + PD-1 blockade combinations are also a strategy. Multiplex immunofluorescence confirmed macrophage identity and PD-L1-expressing cell types spatially.

**Genes / cancer types:** `CD163`, `CD274` (PD-L1), `CD20`, `CD8A` · Solitary fibrous tumor

**Data availability:** `partial`
> IHC-derived immune cell density data for the sft_sysucc_2023 cohort is in cBioPortal as clinical attributes. Raw multiplex IF data not typically available in cBioPortal.

**Tool support:** `api`
> PD-L1 status and immune infiltrate levels are clinical attributes; cohort filtering and KM survival queries by PD-L1 status available via cBioPortal API or Study View. Detailed spatial immune analyses require external tools.

### F3: MTOR mutation is a novel molecular risk factor incorporated into the integrated risk model

**Finding:** *MTOR* mutations are recurrently detected in SFTs and associate with worse PFS. They contribute independently to risk stratification and are incorporated as the sole molecular variable in the integrated risk model alongside histopathologic features.

**Detail:** *MTOR* is targetable (mTOR inhibitors are approved; e.g., everolimus), and mutation-based selection could identify SFT patients most likely to benefit. The integrated risk model (mitotic count, Ki-67+ density, CD163+ density, *MTOR* mutation) outperforms WHO classification, Demicco score, and G-score by C-index and AUC across all 4 cohorts and in CNS SFTs — a setting not covered by any prior model.

**Genes / cancer types:** `MTOR` · Solitary fibrous tumor (non-CNS and CNS)

**Data availability:** `available`

**Tool support:** `native`

### F4: NAB2-STAT6 fusion confirmed as universal defining alteration; STAT6 IHC is robust surrogate

**Finding:** The *NAB2-STAT6* gene fusion is the defining event in SFTs. STAT6 IHC positivity was ≥91% across all 4 cohorts (overall 95.59%), confirming it as a robust diagnostic surrogate. RT-PCR identified additional NAB2-STAT6 fusions in STAT6 IHC-negative cases due to antibody limitations in aged tissue.

**Detail:** STAT6 IHC cannot detect the fusion at DNA level due to the small inverted segment between closely positioned *NAB2* and *STAT6* loci. This study confirms IHC sensitivity is 91-98% in fresh tissue, with loss in old FFPE. Combined STAT6 IHC + RT-PCR achieved 95.42% diagnostic confirmation. NGS panels without RNA-based fusion detection may miss some NAB2-STAT6 variants.

**Genes / cancer types:** `NAB2`, `STAT6` · Solitary fibrous tumor

**Data availability:** `partial`
> NAB2-STAT6 fusion data may not be fully available in sft_sysucc_2023 as it was detected by RT-PCR/IHC rather than NGS RNA fusion calling in most cases.

**Tool support:** `custom`
> Fusion data from RNA-seq would be needed for complete NAB2-STAT6 characterization; current study relied on IHC/RT-PCR. External fusion analysis tools required.

**Concepts:** #NAB2-STAT6 #fusion #diagnosis #sarcoma #SFT

### F5: Integrated risk model outperforms existing models for primary SFTs with negative margins

**Finding:** A 4-variable integrated risk model (mitotic count ≥4/10HPF, Ki-67+ cell density, CD163+ cell density, *MTOR* mutation) stratifies primary non-CNS SFTs with negative tumor margins into 3 risk groups with significantly different PFS. It outperforms the mDemicco model, G-score, and WHO grade in all 4 cohorts and also performs well in primary CNS SFTs — the only model validated for both anatomical locations.

**Detail:** C-index and AUC were consistently higher for the integrated model. Applicability to CNS SFTs (WHO grade 1/2/3) is clinically important, as existing models exclude CNS tumors. However, the model requires NGS for MTOR mutation status and digital pathology for cell density quantification, adding complexity and cost to clinical adoption. Long-term follow-up is still insufficient for some cohorts.

**Genes / cancer types:** `MTOR`, `Ki-67 (MKI67)`, `CD163` · Solitary fibrous tumor (non-CNS and CNS)

**Data availability:** `available`
> Model variables (mitotic count, MTOR mutation, Ki-67 density, CD163 density) are available as clinical data in sft_sysucc_2023.

**Tool support:** `api`
> Each individual variable is queryable. Composite model score computation and full KM stratification into 3 risk tiers requires scripting via API or custom analysis against exported clinical data.

## Open Questions

- Can IDH1 p.R132S SFT patients respond to ivosidenib, and is response rate similar to cholangiocarcinoma?
- What is the significance of the two cases with concurrent IDH1 p.R132S and PD-L1 expression — does dual targeting improve outcomes?
- Do any of the 8 cases with no common NAB2-STAT6 fusion by RT-PCR harbor novel/rare fusions detectable by RNA-seq?
- Does the MTOR mutation associate with mTOR pathway activation by IHC/RPPA (pS6, p4EBP1)?
- Can the integrated risk model be prospectively validated and simplified for clinical use without digital pathology quantification?
- What is the spatial relationship between CD163+ macrophages, CD8+ T cells, and PD-L1+ cells and how does this relate to response to combination immunotherapy?

## Limitations

- No matched germline DNA; somatic vs. germline variant distinction may be imperfect
- RNA-based NGS absent; some gene fusions (including NAB2-STAT6 variants) may be missed
- Retrospective design with variable follow-up (median 29–72 months depending on cohort)
- Model relies on digital pathology quantification (HALO) not universally available
- Relapsed SFTs cohort (n=31) showed no significant associations — model does not apply to recurrent disease
- All cohorts are from China; generalizability to other populations requires validation
