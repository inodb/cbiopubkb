## Summary

Using a humanized mouse model of recurrent Shh medulloblastoma (microsurgical resection + fractionated craniospinal irradiation) and whole-genome sequencing of 33 matched human diagnostic/post-therapy medulloblastoma pairs, this study demonstrates that recurrent medulloblastoma is highly genetically divergent from the therapy-naïve primary tumor. In both mice and humans, the dominant clone at recurrence arises from clonal selection of a pre-existing minor clone (present at <5% frequency at diagnosis), rather than from the dominant primary clone. Only ~11.8% of human somatic SNVs and indels are shared between matched diagnostic and recurrent samples, and mutation burden increases ~5-fold at recurrence. TP53 pathway alterations are the most common recurrence-specific events in Shh medulloblastoma. These findings explain the widespread failure of targeted therapy trials in recurrent medulloblastoma: targeted therapies are designed against the primary tumor genome but are unlikely to encounter their targets in the recurrent disease.

## Key Findings

### F1: Recurrent medulloblastoma shares fewer than 12% of somatic events with the matched primary tumor — extreme genetic divergence

**Finding:** On average, only 11.8% of somatic SNVs and indels are shared between therapy-naïve diagnostic and patient-matched post-therapy recurrent medulloblastomas across all subgroups. In 13/15 patients with germline controls, somatic mutational burden increased by an average of 5-fold at recurrence (p=2.7×10⁻⁴).

**Detail:** Structural aberrations are also at higher frequency at recurrence, with a subset of aberrations at diagnosis absent at recurrence. For example, MB-REC-14's *TERT* amplification present at diagnosis is absent at recurrence, making TERT a less desirable target at that time point. MB-REC-09 shows chromothripsis involving the *MYC* locus only at recurrence. The proportion of clonal mutations increases 1.9-fold post-therapy (p=8.7×10⁻³), consistent with therapy-induced selection eliminating low-frequency lineages. 60.5% of damaging clonal mutations present in primary tumors decrease in abundance post-therapy: 25.9% become subclonal and 34.6% disappear completely.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53`, `MYC`, `TERT`, `PTCH1`, `CDKN2A`, `CDKN2B`, `DYNC1H1` · Medulloblastoma (all subgroups)

### F2: Recurrent medulloblastoma arises from clonal selection of a pre-existing rare subclone — present at <5% frequency at diagnosis

**Finding:** Ultra-deep sequencing of 192 patient-specific SNVs in 20 patients confirms that in 16/20 patients, clones initially present at <5% frequency in the therapy-naïve primary expand to clonal dominance at recurrence (p demonstrated by detection of subclonal events at frequencies as low as 2/10,000).

**Detail:** EXPANDS clonal modeling shows: in 8/14 patients, all clones in the recurrent tumor arise from a single lineage in the primary tumor (single common ancestor). Three patterns are observed: (1) subclonal lineages expanding to clonal dominance at relapse; (2) clonal lineages becoming subclonal at relapse; (3) lineages maintaining the same cellular prevalence. In every tumor, novel mutations restricted to the dominant recurrent clone are observed. MB-REC-12 provides a compelling example: homozygous *PTCH1* driver (heterozygous mutation + chr9q LOH) is completely eradicated by therapy, and the tumor reconstitutes from an ancestral clone with wild-type chr9q driven by *CDKN2A/B* homozygous loss—a complete switch in trunk driver mutations.

**Genes / cancer types:** `PTCH1`, `CDKN2A`, `CDKN2B`, `CREBBP`, `TP53` · Medulloblastoma (Shh subgroup primarily)

**Data availability:** `available`

### F3: TP53 pathway alterations are the dominant recurrence-specific event in Shh medulloblastoma — occurring in 52% of recurrences

**Finding:** *TP53* gene mutations occur in 6/23 (26%) and broader TP53 pathway gene alterations in 12/23 (52%) of human recurrences, predominantly in Shh subgroup medulloblastoma. Mouse model confirms *Trp53* loss as a key driver of recurrence: Trp53 insertions are subclonal at diagnosis but become clonally dominant at recurrence.

**Detail:** In the mouse model, *Trp53* insertions are highly clonal in recurrences and either absent or present in subclones of matched primaries. Animals with clonally selected *Trp53* loss at recurrence have worse prognosis. Drosophila brain tumor model validates radiation sensitivity: wild-type p53 tumors show widespread apoptosis at 40 Gy, while dominant-negative p53 (p53^R159N) abrogates radiation-induced cell death. Recurrent damaging *DYNC1H1* mutations are found in 16% of Shh recurrences and are mutually exclusive with *TP53* mutations. Therapy-naïve Shh medulloblastoma with chr14q deletion (which encompasses *DYNC1H1*) has significantly worse prognosis (log-rank p=0.0109 in n=578 independent cohort), replicated (p=0.000995, n=35).

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53`, `DYNC1H1`, `PTCH1` · Medulloblastoma (Shh subgroup)

### F4: Drug targets identified in the primary tumor are not retained at recurrence — failure mode for targeted therapy trials

**Finding:** Of 15 patients with actionable targets in therapy-naïve tumors (defined as having drug-gene interactions), only 4/15 (44%) retain all actionable targets post-therapy. In two patients, actionable targets completely switch between diagnosis and recurrence; three patients gain new actionable targets only at recurrence.

**Detail:** The current standard practice of testing novel agents in recurrent medulloblastoma using tumor models derived from pre-therapy samples or primary tumors rests on the unvalidated assumption of genetic similarity. This study directly falsifies that assumption. A number of targetable events are present as subclonal mutations in the recurrent tumor, indicating combination therapy may be needed to minimize evolution of resistance. Convergent biological evolution is also observed: one patient shows different *SMO* mutations in the pre- and post-therapy sample, indicating convergent pathway re-activation despite genetic divergence.

**Genes / cancer types:** `SMO`, `PTCH1`, `TERT`, `MYC`, `CDKN2A`, `CDKN2B` · Medulloblastoma (Shh, Group 4)

### F5: Mouse model of humanized therapy reproduces the clonal divergence seen in human patients — Shh pathway drives primary, TP53 pathway drives recurrence

**Finding:** In the mouse model, Shh pathway genes (gCISs in Shh signaling) are enriched driver events at diagnosis, while TP53 pathway and DNA damage response genes dominate at recurrence—with only *CREBBP* as a shared gCIS across all compartments.

**Detail:** 23 gCISs identified in 11 primary tumors; 40 gCISs in recurrences. Only *Trp53*, *Arid1b*, and *Tcf4* found in both recurrent compartments (local and metastatic). *CREBBP* is the only gCIS found across all compartments. Pathway analysis confirms the shift: initiating driver insertions enriched in Shh signaling at diagnosis, but DNA damage response genes (including TP53 pathway) over-represented at recurrence. Surgery + CSI improves tumor-free survival vs controls (median 118 days vs 5 days; p=0.0001), but 61% of treated mice still develop local and/or metastatic relapse, providing a model for therapeutic testing in the post-treatment state.

**Genes / cancer types:** `CREBBP`, `TP53`, `ARID1B`, `TCF4` · Medulloblastoma Shh subgroup (mouse model)

**Data availability:** `not-available`

## Open Questions

- Can liquid biopsy (ctDNA) capture the evolutionary divergence between diagnosis and relapse in medulloblastoma, enabling prospective tracking of recurrence-specific clones without re-biopsy?
- Do Group 3 and Group 4 medulloblastomas show the same degree of clonal divergence as Shh tumors at recurrence, or are there subgroup-specific patterns of clonal selection?
- Is the pre-existing minor clone that gives rise to recurrence detectable at diagnosis by ultra-sensitive sequencing (error-corrected ctDNA or single-cell WGS)?
- Can targeted treatment delivered at diagnosis—before clonal selection—prevent the selection of TP53-deficient clones that drive Shh recurrence?
- Do DYNC1H1 mutations at recurrence provide any therapeutic vulnerability (e.g., through synthetic lethality with microtubule-targeting agents)?

## Limitations

- Small human cohort (33 matched pairs for WGS) with limited statistical power to detect subgroup-specific recurrence patterns
- Recurrence biopsy is technically challenging and ethically complex in pediatric patients; cohort biased toward patients who underwent surgery at recurrence
- Ultra-deep sequencing to detect rare (<5%) subclones was only feasible for a subset (20 patients)
- Mouse model is Shh-specific (Ptch+/− / Math1-SB11); may not recapitulate Group 3/4 medulloblastoma recurrence biology
- Transposon insertional mutagenesis in mice creates a different mutational landscape than human tumors
- Clonal evolution analysis assumes adequate tumor purity; mixed biopsies may obscure subclonal detection
- No functional validation of recurrence-specific targets in patient-derived models
