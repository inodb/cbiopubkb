## Summary
This study performed an integrated multi-platform oncogenomic analysis of 218 prostate tumors (181 primaries, 37 metastases) and 12 cell lines/xenografts using aCGH, mRNA/microRNA transcriptome profiling, and focused exon resequencing of 138 cancer-related genes. Key discoveries include identification of *NCOA2* as a novel androgen receptor coactivator oncogene on 8q13, a prostate-specific 3p14 deletion co-occurring with *TMPRSS2-ERG* fusion implicating *FOXP1*, *RYBP*, and *SHQ1* as cooperative tumor suppressors, and demonstration that copy number alterations—more than transcriptome data—robustly stratify biochemical relapse risk beyond Gleason score.

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: NCOA2 Identified as Novel Oncogene on 8q13.3 Amplicon
- **Detail**: GISTIC analysis of 194 tumors identified a focal peak of copy-number gain on 8q13.3 spanning *NCOA2* (nuclear receptor coactivator 2, also known as SRC2/TIF2/GRIP1), distinct from and statistically more significant than the known 8q24 *MYC* amplicon. High-level *NCOA2* focal amplification occurred in 6.2% of tumors (1.9% primaries, 24.3% metastases), with broader 8q gains in 17% overall. NCOA2 amplification correlated with elevated NCOA2 transcript levels and increased androgen-responsive AR transcriptional output. Functional assays showed that increasing NCOA2 protein levels shifted the DHT dose-response curve, both lowering the androgen threshold for AR activation and boosting maximal AR output. Non-castrate patients with NCOA2 gain had significantly higher biochemical recurrence rates.
- **Genes/Alterations**: NCOA2 (broad gain 17%, high-level amplification ~6%), with associated mutations in 2 primary tumors; also NCOR2 (3 tumors), EP300, NRIP1, TNK2
- **Data availability**: available — CNA and expression data in prad_mskcc
- **Tool support**: native — CNA query, expression correlation, survival analysis by alteration all native
- **Concepts**: #AR #driver-genes #copy-number #actionability #prostate

### Finding 2: TMPRSS2-ERG Fusion Co-Occurs with Prostate-Specific 3p14 Deletion
- **Detail**: *TMPRSS2-ERG* fusion was detected in 52% of tumors (consistent with prior literature). Analysis of 194 tumors for CNAs associated with fusion status revealed three significant co-deletion regions: *PTEN* (10q23), *TP53* (17p13), and a novel, prostate-specific deletion at 3p14.1-p13. This 3p14 deletion—spanning only 8 genes—was the most statistically significant TMPRSS2-ERG-associated CNA and was not observed in 13 other tumor types. Integrative analysis of CNA, expression, and exon resequencing implicated three candidate cooperative tumor suppressors: *FOXP1* (forkhead box transcription factor), *RYBP* (polycomb group repressor, MDM2 inhibitor), and *SHQ1* (H/ACA ribonucleoprotein assembly factor). A confirmed somatic P22S mutation in the SHQ1 CS domain was identified.
- **Genes/Alterations**: TMPRSS2-ERG (fusion 52%), FOXP1/RYBP/SHQ1 (3p14 deletion, co-deletion with ERG fusion), PTEN (loss ~21%), TP53 (loss ~24%)
- **Data availability**: available — fusion status, CNA, and expression in prad_mskcc; 3p14 deletion queryable via CNA
- **Tool support**: native — fusion query, CNA/expression co-alteration analysis; api for complex co-deletion queries
- **Concepts**: #fusion #copy-number #driver-genes #prostate #PTEN

### Finding 3: PI3K, RAS/RAF, and RB Pathways Commonly Altered—Especially in Metastases
- **Detail**: Integrated pathway analysis incorporating CNA, expression outliers, and mutations showed frequent alteration of three canonical pathways. PI3K pathway was altered in ~43% of primaries and ~100% of metastases, driven primarily by *PTEN* loss (deletion ~21%) plus alterations in *INPP4B*, *PHLPP*, *PIK3CA*, and PIK3R subunits. RB pathway was altered in ~34% of primaries and ~74% of metastases. RAS/RAF was similarly altered. Critically, common point mutations in *PIK3CA*, *KRAS*, and *BRAF* were rare (2 tumors each), highlighting that pathway disruption in prostate cancer occurs predominantly through copy-number changes and expression outliers rather than point mutations.
- **Genes/Alterations**: PTEN (loss ~21%), INPP4B, PIK3CA (mutation rare, expression outlier common), RB1 (loss), CDKN1B, BRAF, KRAS (mutation rare)
- **Data availability**: available — all pathway genes queryable in prad_mskcc
- **Tool support**: native — pathway view, multi-gene OncoPrint, co-alteration queries; api for comprehensive pathway alteration scores
- **Concepts**: #PTEN #copy-number #driver-genes #actionability #prostate

### Finding 4: AR Pathway Broadly Altered in Primaries and Universally in Metastases
- **Detail**: While AR gene amplification/mutation was restricted to metastatic disease (58%), broader AR pathway analysis (11 coactivators/corepressors) revealed alteration in 56% of primary tumors and 100% of metastases. The most notable finding was *NCOA2* gain as an early oncogenic event enhancing AR transcriptional output before castration resistance. AR pathway alterations in primaries include coactivators (*NCOA2*, *EP300*) and corepressors (*NCOR2*, *NRIP1*). Only 4 samples—all metastases—had AR point mutations, consistent with treatment resistance rather than driver events in primary disease.
- **Genes/Alterations**: AR (amplification/mutation restricted to mets, 58%), NCOA2 (amplification/gain ~17%), NCOR2 (mutation 3 tumors), EP300, NRIP1, TNK2
- **Data availability**: available — AR pathway genes in prad_mskcc; clinical stage data for stratification
- **Tool support**: native — primary vs metastasis comparison via study filtering; expression comparison native
- **Concepts**: #AR #prostate #driver-genes #copy-number #actionability

### Finding 5: CNA-Based Clustering Defines Biochemical Relapse Risk Beyond Gleason Score
- **Detail**: Unsupervised hierarchical clustering of discretized CNA data from 194 tumors defined 6 genomic groups with significant differences in time to biochemical (PSA) relapse. Two broad categories emerged: minimal CNA (clusters 1–4) and substantial CNA (clusters 5–6, including most metastatic samples). Cluster 2 (largely diploid, minimal CNA) had extremely favorable prognosis; cluster 5 (genome-wide alterations) had the worst prognosis. Importantly, CNA clustering was not redundant with Gleason score—poor Gleason scores were distributed across clusters 2–6 and favorable Gleason scores were not enriched in clusters 1–4. Specific prognostic associations included combined 13q+18q loss, 5p amplification, and 5q21.1 deletion.
- **Genes/Alterations**: Combined 13q+18q loss, 5p13/5p15 amplification, 5q21.1 deletion (prognostic); NKX3.1 (8p loss), MYC (8q gain)
- **Data availability**: available — CNA data and clinical outcome (PSA recurrence) linked in prad_mskcc
- **Tool support**: native — survival analysis by CNA native; clustering not natively reproducible; api for custom group analysis
- **Concepts**: #copy-number #survival #biomarker #molecular-subtypes #prostate

### Finding 6: Low Somatic Mutation Rate in Prostate Cancer
- **Detail**: Exon resequencing of 138 genes across 80 tumors identified 84 confirmed somatic mutations in 57 genes, with no single gene mutated in more than 4 samples (AR in metastases). The estimated background mutation rate was ~0.31 mutations/Mb—among the lowest reported for solid tumors. *TP53* and *PTEN* were commonly altered but primarily through copy-number loss, not point mutation. Common oncogene mutations (*PIK3CA*, *KRAS*, *BRAF*, *IDH1/2*) were rare (1–2 tumors). One tumor with an MSH6 mutation had 11 somatic mutations vs. average of 2.
- **Genes/Alterations**: AR (4 mutations, all metastases), TP53 (2 mutations, predominantly CNA loss), PTEN (0 mutations, predominantly CNA), MSH6 (1 mutation with hypermutator phenotype)
- **Data availability**: available — mutation data in prad_mskcc for these genes
- **Tool support**: native — mutation frequency, lollipop plots, TMB calculation all native in cBioPortal
- **Concepts**: #mutation #prostate #driver-genes #MSI

## Open Questions
- Can the 6 CNA-based prognostic clusters be reproduced in the larger SU2C/PCF prostate cancer datasets in cBioPortal to validate clinical utility?
- Does NCOA2 amplification predict response to novel AR pathway inhibitors (enzalutamide, abiraterone) in metastatic disease, and can this be queried in clinical outcome data in cBioPortal?
- Are the FOXP1/RYBP/SHQ1 deletions at 3p14 prognostically significant independent of TMPRSS2-ERG fusion status?

## Limitations
- Enriched for larger, more aggressive prostate cancers (required >70% tumor content); may not represent indolent disease spectrum
- Exon resequencing limited to 138 pre-selected cancer genes; comprehensive somatic mutation landscape not available
- Limited sample size for metastatic cohort (n=37); pathway alteration frequencies in metastases may be imprecise
- No proteomics data; expression-based pathway alterations are surrogate measures
- CNA-based prognostic model derived from a single-institution cohort; validation in independent large datasets is needed
