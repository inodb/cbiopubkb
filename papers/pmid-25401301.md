## Summary

The first comprehensive multi-platform genomic characterization of non-clear cell renal cell carcinoma (nccRCC) subtypes — papillary RCC (pRCC, n=67), chromophobe RCC (chRCC, n=49), renal oncocytoma (n=35), translocation RCC (tRCC, n=6), and unclassified RCC (n=8) — using exome sequencing, RNA-seq, and SNP copy number analysis of 167 primary tumors. Key findings include: MET mutations in 15% of pRCC (higher than previously reported, including novel activating kinase domain mutations); TP53 and PTEN as the dominant significantly mutated genes in chRCC; a novel transforming ACTG1-MITF gene fusion in tRCC; a five-gene RNA expression classifier that distinguishes chRCC, oncocytoma, and pRCC subtypes; and upregulation of anti-apoptotic BIRC7 in MiTF-high RCCs as a potential therapeutic target. The study also reveals that pRCC has higher mutation burden than chRCC/oncocytoma, and identifies PRKAG2 (AMPK γ-subunit) and PDHB (metabolic enzymes) as novel significantly mutated genes in chRCC.

## Key Findings

### F1: MET mutations in 15% of pRCC, including novel activating kinase domain mutations

**Finding:** MET is mutated in 15% (10/65) of pRCC samples analyzed — significantly higher than the previously reported 8% (9/121) — with novel recurrent activating kinase domain mutations identified. All but one mutation map to the kinase domain. Functional validation confirms increased constitutive MET phosphorylation and anchorage-independent growth promotion for all novel MET mutants.

**Detail:** Ten pRCC MET mutations: 9 kinase domain, 1 extracellular domain (ECD). Novel activating mutations: p.Val1088Ala, p.Ile1095Thr, recurrent p.Phe1218Ile (kinase domain); p.Asp153Tyr (ECD). Crystal structure mapping (PDB 1R0P, 1SHY) positions these near the active site or substrate binding interface. NIH3T3 transformation assays: all MET mutants produced significantly more colonies than WT MET in soft agar. Elevated phospho-MET confirmed by Western blot. MET mutations were mutually exclusive with NF2, PTEN, TSC1, MTOR mutations (trend). Chromosome 7 amplification (including MET locus) found in ~70% of pRCCs; a subset had focal MET amplification. MET mutations define actionable subtype for MET inhibitor therapy.

**Genes / cancer types:** `MET`, `NF2`, `SLC5A3`, `PNKD`, `CPQ` · Papillary renal cell carcinoma

### F2: TP53 and PTEN are the dominant driver mutations in chromophobe RCC; novel metabolic gene mutations identified

**Finding:** TP53 is significantly mutated in chRCC (enriched in classic subtype, p=2.3×10−5) alongside PTEN. Additionally, novel significantly mutated genes include PDHB (pyruvate dehydrogenase E1β subunit — metabolic), PRKAG2 (AMPK γ-subunit — energy sensing), and FAAH2, suggesting that metabolic deregulation is a defining feature of chRCC pathogenesis distinct from pRCC.

**Detail:** chRCC significantly mutated genes (q-score analysis, FDR≤10%): TP53, PTEN, FAAH2, PDHB, PDXDC1, ZNF765. PDHB mutations: p.Phe222fs*35 (truncating) and p.Arg105Leu at a conserved residue (previously linked to lactic acidosis); disrupts pyruvate-to-acetyl-CoA conversion, potentially favoring glycolysis. PRKAG2 mutations in CBS domain regions: p.Ile388Val (pseudosubstrate sequence — constitutively activating) and p.Arg531Gln (AMP-binding site CBS4 — previously associated with glycogen storage cardiomyopathy). PRKAG2 upregulated 3.36 log2 fold in chRCC vs. pRCC. Additional recurrent but below-threshold mutations in ARID1A, TSC1/TSC2/MTOR (mTOR pathway). Copy number: classic chRCC shows multi-chromosomal losses (chr 1, 2, 6, 8, 10, 13, 17, 21); eosinophilic chRCC is nearly diploid; oncocytoma shows chr 1 deletion most frequently.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53`, `PTEN`, `PDHB`, `PRKAG2`, `FAAH2`, `TSC1`, `TSC2`, `MTOR`, `ARID1A` · Chromophobe renal cell carcinoma / Renal oncocytoma

### F3: Novel ACTG1-MITF gene fusion in translocation RCC is transforming

**Finding:** RNA-seq identified a novel ACTG1-MITF gene fusion in translocation RCC. Ectopic expression of the ACTG1-MITF fusion protein in cell lines induced cellular transformation and upregulated downstream MITF target genes, establishing this fusion as a cancer driver.

**Detail:** tRCC is defined by translocations involving MiTF family transcription factors (TFE3, TFEB, and MITF). ACTG1-MITF is a novel fusion partner: ACTG1 (gamma-actin) fused to MITF drives constitutive MITF transcriptional activity independent of the normal regulatory context. In vitro validation: ectopic ACTG1-MITF expression in NIH3T3 cells induced focus formation and colony growth, with upregulation of canonical MITF targets. A pRCC sample showed TFEB focal amplification confirmed by FISH — indicating amplification (not just translocation) as an alternative MiTF activation mechanism. MiTF-family activation appears to be a convergent oncogenic mechanism across multiple nccRCC subtypes (TFE3 translocation, TFEB translocation, TFEB amplification, MITF fusion).

**Genes / cancer types:** `MITF`, `TFE3`, `TFEB`, `ACTG1`, `BIRC7` · Translocation renal cell carcinoma / MiTF-family RCC

### F4: Five-gene RNA expression classifier robustly distinguishes pRCC, chRCC, and renal oncocytoma

**Finding:** Unsupervised clustering of 400 most variable genes by RNA-seq separates nccRCC into histological subgroups. A minimal five-gene set — ASB1, GLYAT, PDZK1IP1, PLCG2, SDCBP2 — is sufficient to classify chRCC, oncocytoma, and pRCC subtypes with 95.3% accuracy (41/43 in leave-one-out cross-validation), validated in two independent cohorts.

**Detail:** The five-gene classifier was derived by pairwise differential expression analysis and minimum gene selection from the 25 top differentially expressed genes per subtype pair. SDCBP2, ADAP1, HOOK2, BAIAP3, SPINT1 are most highly expressed in chRCC (2.5–5.3 log2 fold vs. oncocytoma). Leave-one-out k-nearest-neighbors classifier: 95.3% accuracy in training cohort; confirmed in external validation (4 oncocytoma, 4 chRCC, 19 pRCC). Clinical relevance: distinguishing chRCC (malignant) from renal oncocytoma (benign) by histology alone is challenging; this RNA-based test provides an objective molecular tool. tRCC and most unclassified RCC samples cluster with pRCC, suggesting shared signaling pathway activation.

**Genes / cancer types:** `ASB1`, `GLYAT`, `PDZK1IP1`, `PLCG2`, `SDCBP2`, `ADAP1`, `HOOK2` · Chromophobe RCC / Papillary RCC / Renal oncocytoma

### F5: BIRC7 upregulation in MiTF-high RCC defines a potential therapeutic vulnerability

**Finding:** Anti-apoptotic gene BIRC7 (Livin/ML-IAP) is consistently upregulated in tumors with high MiTF-family transcription factor activity (TFE3-translocation, TFEB-amplification, and ACTG1-MITF fusion RCCs), suggesting that MiTF-driven cancers may be dependent on BIRC7-mediated apoptosis suppression and sensitive to BIRC7 inhibitors.

**Detail:** BIRC7 is a direct transcriptional target of MITF. In MiTF-high RCC tumors, BIRC7 mRNA expression is substantially elevated compared to other RCC subtypes. BIRC7 (also known as ML-IAP or Livin) belongs to the inhibitor of apoptosis protein (IAP) family and has been proposed as a therapeutic target due to its cancer-restricted expression. The finding that MiTF pathway activation in RCC converges on BIRC7 upregulation parallels its known role in melanoma — where MITF transcriptionally drives BIRC7 expression as a survival factor. This observation extends the therapeutic rationale for BIRC7/IAP antagonists (smac mimetics) from melanoma to tRCC/MiTF-high RCC.

## Open Questions

- Do the novel MET kinase domain mutations (V1088A, I1095T, F1218I, D153Y) in pRCC predict response to MET inhibitors (cabozantinib, crizotinib, tepotinib) in clinical trials?
- Can the five-gene expression classifier be adapted to a qRT-PCR or NanoString assay for routine clinical use in distinguishing chRCC from oncocytoma in diagnostic biopsies?
- How do TSC1/TSC2/MTOR mutations in chRCC influence response to mTOR inhibitors (everolimus, temsirolimus), which have shown modest activity in nccRCC?
- Is PRKAG2 mutation a functional driver in chRCC, and does it confer sensitivity to AMPK inhibitors or metabolic targeting strategies?
- Do BIRC7/IAP inhibitors (smac mimetics) show activity in MiTF-high RCC tumors as in melanoma models?
- What drives the nearly diploid genome of eosinophilic chRCC compared to the dramatic multi-chromosomal losses in classic chRCC — are these biologically distinct diseases?

## Limitations

- Study pre-dates TCGA nccRCC papers (TCGA pRCC 2016, TCGA chRCC 2019); some significantly mutated gene findings may differ in larger cohorts
- Translocation RCC cohort is very small (n=6); ACTG1-MITF fusion conclusions are based on limited samples
- No clinical outcome (survival) data reported; cannot assess whether genomic subtype or specific mutations predict outcomes
- Renal oncocytoma low mutation burden limits power to identify significantly mutated genes — ERCC2 finding in oncocytoma requires larger validation
- Morphological classification as gold standard has known diagnostic ambiguity (mixed chRCC/oncocytoma tumors), potentially contaminating subtype-specific genomic findings
- No immunotherapy or targeted therapy outcome data — study predates routine use of PD-1/PD-L1 inhibitors in RCC
- BIRC7 hypothesis is observational; no functional ablation experiments in RCC models presented
