## Summary

MiMSI is a deep multiple-instance learning classifier that detects microsatellite instability (MSI-H) from targeted NGS data (MSK-IMPACT) using vectorized reads at microsatellite loci. It outperforms the established MSISensor algorithm, especially in low tumor-purity samples that frequently yield false negatives in clinical practice. Tested across 45,112 pan-cancer samples and validated against orthogonal IHC/PCR, MiMSI also reduces the indeterminate call rate from 3.8% to 0.47%.

## Key Findings

### F1: MiMSI outperforms MSISensor in challenging test cohort

**Finding:** MiMSI achieves higher sensitivity and auROC than MSISensor on a curated test set enriched for difficult low-purity cases.

**Detail:** Test cohort n=317 (training n=741). MiMSI (200X, attention): sensitivity 0.895, auROC 0.971, accuracy 0.942. MSISensor: sensitivity 0.67, auROC 0.907, accuracy 0.835. MiMSI classified 91% (226/247) of MSISensor-indeterminate cases correctly.

**Genes / cancer types:** `MMR` · Pan-cancer

**Data availability:** available
> Data linked to pancan_mimsi_msk_2024 cBioPortal study; MSI calls are part of the public MSK-IMPACT cohort.

**Tool support:** api
> MSI status is a queryable field in cBioPortal Study View; comparison of MSI rates by cancer type feasible via API.

**Concepts:** #MSI #ICI-biomarker #tumor-agnostic #biomarker

### F2: MiMSI maintains superiority in low tumor-purity samples prospectively

**Finding:** In a prospective cohort of 5,037 samples, MiMSI sensitivity exceeds MSISensor, with the greatest advantage in low-purity specimens.

**Detail:** Overall sensitivity: MiMSI 91.6% (95% CI 89.5–93.4%) vs MSISensor 86.1% (95% CI 83.3–88.6%). For tumor purity <30%: MiMSI 85.1% vs MSISensor 72.8% (McNemar's p=8.244×10⁻⁷). MSH2 loss associated with highest TMB (median 46.5) vs MLH1 loss (median 37.7).

**Genes / cancer types:** `MLH1` · `MSH2` · `MSH6` · `PMS2` · Pan-cancer

**Data availability:** available
> Large-scale MSK-IMPACT cohort with IHC data is referenced; MSI calls available through cBioPortal.

**Tool support:** native
> Study View in cBioPortal can filter by MSI status; cancer-type breakdown available natively.

**Concepts:** #MSI #TMB #ICI-biomarker #biomarker #pan-cancer-map

### F3: MiMSI dramatically reduces indeterminate MSI calls at scale

**Finding:** Across 45,112 MSK-IMPACT samples, MiMSI reduces the fraction of indeterminate calls 8-fold compared to MSISensor.

**Detail:** MSISensor indeterminate rate 3.8% (n=1,724); MiMSI indeterminate rate 0.47% (n=210). Overall concordance between the two callers for clear MSS/MSI-H calls: 96%.

**Genes / cancer types:** Pan-cancer

**Data availability:** available
> Pan-cancer MSI data from MSK-IMPACT cohort is accessible.

**Tool support:** native
> MSI status filterable in Study View; OncoPrint can overlay MSI calls with mutation data.

**Concepts:** #MSI #pan-cancer-map #tumor-agnostic

### F4: MMR protein-specific molecular fingerprints revealed in large cohort

**Finding:** Loss of different MMR proteins produces distinct TMB and indel-to-SNV ratio profiles, with MSH2 loss driving highest TMB and MSH6 loss producing lowest indel burden.

**Detail:** Median TMB: MMR-D 39 vs MMR-P 5.27 (p<2.2×10⁻¹⁶). MSH2 loss TMB 46.5 > MLH1 loss 37.7 (p=0.0013). Indel/SNV ratio: MLH1 loss highest (0.57), MSH6 loss lowest (0.09). MMR signature contribution: MLH1 loss highest (0.67), MSH6 lowest (0.42).

**Genes / cancer types:** `MLH1` · `MSH2` · `MSH6` · `PMS2` · Pan-cancer

**Data availability:** available
> Part of the same MSK cohort linked to pancan_mimsi_msk_2024.

**Tool support:** api
> TMB and MSI status jointly queryable via cBioPortal API; custom scripting needed for indel/SNV decomposition.

**Concepts:** #MSI #TMB #mutation-signatures #biomarker

### F5: MiMSI generalizes to whole-exome sequencing without retraining

**Finding:** MiMSI trained on targeted-panel data classifies WES data with 98.6% concordance, enabling cross-platform use out-of-the-box.

**Detail:** 582 samples re-captured with WES probes; 5 discordant calls observed vs MSK-IMPACT classifications. Caution advised: validation against a local truth set is recommended for new datasets.

**Genes / cancer types:** Pan-cancer

**Data availability:** partial
> WES data not directly in cBioPortal; MSK-IMPACT counterparts are available.

**Tool support:** custom
> MiMSI is an open-source tool applicable to any NGS BAM/SAM data; not a native cBioPortal feature.

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Does MiMSI's improved sensitivity in low-purity tumors translate into more immunotherapy-eligible patients identified per cancer type?
- Can the attention mechanism be used to rank informative microsatellite loci for biological insight into MMR deficiency mechanisms?
- What is the clinical concordance between MiMSI-only calls and IHC/PCR in MSH6-loss tumors, given their low indel/SNV ratio?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Trained at a single institution (MSK); potential institutional bias in model performance
- Tumor-only mode (without matched normal) yields higher false-positive rates; pooled normal mitigates but does not eliminate this
- POLE-deficient tumors with concurrent MMR deficiency remain a classification challenge
- Low number of loci (<100 sites) increases indeterminate rate substantially
