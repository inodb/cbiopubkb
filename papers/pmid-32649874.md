## Summary
This CPTAC study performed comprehensive proteogenomic characterization of 110 LUAD tumors and 101 matched normal adjacent tissues (NATs), integrating WES, WGS, RNA-seq, DNA methylation, deep-scale proteomics, phosphoproteomics, and acetylproteomics. Multi-omics NMF clustering revealed four molecular subtypes defined by driver mutations (KRAS, EGFR, STK11, TP53), country of origin, and gender. Phosphoproteomic analyses uncovered driver-specific therapeutic vulnerabilities — including PTPN11/Shp2 inhibition in EGFR/ALK-driven tumors and SOS1 inhibition in KRAS mutants — and implicated neutrophil degranulation as an immunosuppressive mechanism in STK11-mutant immune-cold tumors.

## Dataset
- **Cohort**: 110 treatment-naïve LUAD tumors and 101 matched normal adjacent tissues (NATs); demographically diverse including balanced smokers and never-smokers, and patients from USA, Vietnam, and China
- **Platform**: WES (150× nominal), WGS (15× nominal), RNA-seq, miRNA-seq, Illumina DNA methylation arrays, TMT-based isobaric labeling mass spectrometry for proteomics/phosphoproteomics/acetylproteomics
- **Data types**: Somatic mutations, copy number alterations, gene fusions, RNA expression, DNA methylation, global proteome, phosphoproteome, acetylproteome
- **cBioPortal study**: luad_cptac_2020

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: Four multi-omics molecular subtypes defined by driver mutations and demographics
- **Detail**: NMF clustering of RNA, protein, phosphosite, and acetylsite data identified four stable clusters (C1–C4) corresponding to proximal-inflammatory (TP53 mutant, CIMP-high), two proximal-proliferative subtypes (one enriched for Vietnamese patients and STK11 mutations; one for Western patients), and terminal respiratory unit (EGFR-mutant, female, Chinese). Each cluster had distinct pathway enrichment and clinical correlates.
- **Genes**: TP53, EGFR, KRAS, STK11, EML4-ALK
- **Data availability**: partial — genomic/RNA data in cBioPortal; proteomics/phosphoproteomics not in cBioPortal
- **Tool support**: native — mutation profiles and oncoprint for genomic subtypes; proteomics clustering requires custom analysis
- **Concepts**: molecular subtypes, oncoprint, mutation frequency, copy number alterations

### Finding 2: ALK fusion-specific phosphorylation at Y1507 as a therapeutic biomarker
- **Detail**: All ALK fusion-positive tumors showed outlier phosphorylation of ALK Y1507 — more dramatically than total ALK protein or mRNA — validated by IHC in all available ALK fusion cases and absent in ROS1/RET fusion or NAT samples. Novel 5' fusion partners (HMBOX1, ANKRD36B) and downstream phosphoproteomic targets (SND1, HDLBP, ARHGEF5) of oncogenic ALK were identified.
- **Genes**: ALK, EML4, HMBOX1, ANKRD36B, SND1, HDLBP, ARHGEF5
- **Data availability**: partial — ALK fusion status available in cBioPortal; phosphosite-level data not available
- **Tool support**: native — fusion events visible via cBioPortal; phosphosite analysis requires custom code
- **Concepts**: gene fusions, mutation frequency, oncoprint

### Finding 3: Driver-specific kinase outliers nominate druggable therapeutic targets
- **Detail**: Systematic hyperphosphorylation analysis of kinases identified driver-specific outliers: PRKCD in KRAS mutants, BRAF in TP53 mutants, WEE1 in EML4-ALK fusions, and EGFR in EGFR mutants. Most outliers lacked CNA/RNA/protein-level changes, underscoring the unique value of phosphoproteomics. Many outlier kinases had known FDA-approved or investigational inhibitors, supported by DepMap essentiality data.
- **Genes**: PRKCD, BRAF, WEE1, EGFR, KRAS, TP53, ALK
- **Data availability**: partial — mutation/CNA context available in cBioPortal; phosphosite outlier data not available
- **Tool support**: api — mutation and CNA data accessible via API; kinase outlier analysis requires custom code
- **Concepts**: mutation frequency, actionability, targeted-therapy

### Finding 4: PTPN11/Shp2 and SOS1 as phosphoproteomic therapeutic vulnerabilities in EGFR/ALK and KRAS tumors
- **Detail**: EGFR-mutant tumors showed consistent elevated phosphorylation of PTPN11/Shp2 at Y62 (stabilizing active conformation) without RNA or protein-level changes, and ALK fusion tumors showed phosphorylation at Y546/Y584. KRAS-mutant tumors showed elevated SOS1 phosphorylation at S1161, which may relieve GRB2 interaction and promote membrane recruitment for KRAS activation. Both PTPN11 inhibitors (in clinical trials) and SOS1 inhibitors represent actionable vulnerabilities.
- **Genes**: PTPN11, SOS1, KRAS, EGFR, ALK, GRB2
- **Data availability**: partial — EGFR/KRAS/ALK mutation status in cBioPortal; phosphosite data not available
- **Tool support**: native — mutation and co-occurrence data in cBioPortal; phosphoproteomic analysis requires custom code
- **Concepts**: mutation frequency, actionability, targeted-therapy, oncoprint

### Finding 5: STK11 mutation drives immune-cold phenotype via neutrophil degranulation
- **Detail**: STK11-mutant tumors showed dramatic reduction in dendritic cell, NK T-cell, and macrophage infiltration (immune-cold) despite moderate mutation burden. Neutrophil degranulation was the most significantly enriched pathway in STK11 mutants, with 16 proteins (CAMP, LTF, BPI, MMP8, MMP9, MPO, LCN2, ELANE, ARG1, others) coherently overexpressed only at the protein level — invisible at the RNA level. A deep-learning histopathology model predicted STK11 status with 94% accuracy based on immune-cold morphology.
- **Genes**: STK11, CAMP, LTF, BPI, MMP8, MMP9, MPO, LCN2, ELANE, ARG1
- **Data availability**: partial — STK11 mutation status in cBioPortal; proteomics-level neutrophil degranulation signal not available
- **Tool support**: native — STK11 mutation browsable in cBioPortal; immune cell deconvolution requires custom code
- **Concepts**: mutation frequency, survival analysis, molecular subtypes, oncoprint

### Finding 6: Proteomics-based tumor-NAT comparison identifies biomarker candidates and cancer testis antigens
- **Detail**: Comparing 110 tumors to 101 matched NATs identified 289 proteins consistently upregulated in tumors (log2 FC >2, FDR <0.01, ≥90% of pairs), including GREM1, OCIAD2, DHFR, and HYOU1 as poor-prognosis markers. Nine cancer testis antigens were recurrently overexpressed (≥10% of samples), including KIF2C (63% of samples), with novel CT antigens MORC1 and NUF2. Additionally, 2481 mRNA-validated and 49 peptide-validated somatic mutation neoantigens were identified across 104 patients.
- **Genes**: GREM1, OCIAD2, KIF2C, MORC1, NUF2, AKAP4, IGF2BP3, DHFR, HYOU1
- **Data availability**: not-available — proteomic biomarker and CT antigen data not in cBioPortal
- **Tool support**: custom — requires proteomics data from CPTAC portal and custom analysis
- **Concepts**: survival analysis, actionability

## Open Questions
- Can PTPN11/Shp2 inhibitors show clinical benefit specifically in EGFR-mutant or ALK-fusion LUAD patients identified by phosphoproteomic outlier status?
- Does neutrophil degranulation mechanistically drive immune exclusion in STK11-mutant tumors, and can targeting degranulation proteins (e.g., MPO, ELANE) restore immunotherapy sensitivity?
- Do the four multi-omics molecular subtypes predict differential responses to existing targeted therapies or immunotherapy regimens in prospective clinical cohorts?

## Limitations
- Interdependence of mutational status, ethnicity/geography, gender, and smoking status limits ability to isolate individual variable effects in a cohort of 110 samples.
- Bulk tissue analyses lack spatial and single-cell resolution, preventing disambiguation of tumor epithelium versus microenvironment contributions.
- Most findings are hypothesis-generating associations; causal mechanistic and clinical validation studies are needed before clinical translation.
