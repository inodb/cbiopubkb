## Summary
This CPTAC study applied proteogenomics (proteomics, phosphoproteomics, acetylproteomics, WES, RNA-seq) to 122 prospectively collected, treatment-naive primary breast cancers. Non-negative matrix factorization (NMF)-based multi-omics clustering identified four molecular subtypes that refined PAM50 classification, revealing that a subset of PAM50 LumA tumors have intermediate-to-poor prognosis driven by LumB-like biology. Key translational findings include proteogenomic refinement of ERBB2 status, identification of immune-active luminal tumors as immunotherapy candidates, and Rb protein abundance as a superior predictor of CDK4/6 inhibitor response compared with RB1 genomic status alone.

## Dataset
- **Cohort**: 122 treatment-naive primary breast cancer patients (prospectively collected; stages IIA–IIIC); 134 enrolled, 122 fully analyzed after QC
- **Platform**: TMT-based mass spectrometry (proteomics, phosphoproteomics, acetylproteomics); Illumina WES and RNA-seq; IHC for ERBB2/CD3/hormone receptors
- **Data types**: Somatic mutations (WES), somatic copy number alterations, RNA-seq (gene expression), global proteome (~10,107 proteins), phosphoproteome (~38,968 sites), acetylproteome (~9,869 sites)
- **cBioPortal study**: brca_cptac_2020

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: NMF Multi-omics Subtypes Refine PAM50 Classification
- **Detail**: Integrative NMF clustering across all omics layers identified four proteogenomic subtypes (LumA-I, LumB-I, HER2-I, Basal-I). A subset of PAM50 LumA tumors were reclassified into the higher-risk NMF LumB-I cluster and showed intermediate survival outcomes in the METABRIC validation cohort, demonstrating that PAM50 mRNA-based classification underestimates biological heterogeneity in luminal disease.
- **Genes**: ESR1, PGR, CCND1, PIK3CA, TP53
- **Data availability**: partial — mRNA, mutation, and CNA data available in cBioPortal; proteomics/phosphoproteomics subtype assignments not directly queryable
- **Tool support**: native — PAM50 subtype filtering and mutation co-occurrence available natively; NMF subtype labels require custom data loading
- **Concepts**: molecular subtypes, oncoprint, mutation frequency, survival analysis

### Finding 2: Proteogenomic ERBB2 Status Identifies Pseudo-ERBB2+ Cases
- **Detail**: Approximately 2–3 of every 17 ERBB2-amplified tumors showed protein expression within the range of non-amplified cases ("pseudo-ERBB2+"), suggesting these patients may not benefit from anti-HER2 therapy. In some pseudo-ERBB2+ cases, TOP2A amplification with overexpression—not ERBB2—drove the chromosome 17q amplicon. PAM50 HER2E and ERBB2 PG+ status were discordant in nearly half of cases, with HER2E/ERBB2 PG− tumors showing elevated phosphorylation of other ERBB family members and MAPK signaling instead.
- **Genes**: ERBB2, TOP2A, STARD3, GRB7, EGFR, ERBB3
- **Data availability**: partial — copy number, mutation, and mRNA data available; ERBB2 protein abundance and phosphopeptide data not available in cBioPortal
- **Tool support**: native — CNA and expression comparison by ERBB2 amplification status; protein-level discrimination requires custom analysis
- **Concepts**: copy number alteration, oncoprint, protein expression, actionability

### Finding 3: Immune Microenvironment Is Active Across All PAM50 Subtypes, Including Luminal
- **Detail**: PD-L1 and PD1 expression at RNA and phosphoprotein levels, along with CIBERSORT immune deconvolution and CD3 IHC, revealed active immune tumor microenvironments (I-TME) in LumB and even subsets of LumA tumors—not only in Basal/TNBC. APOBEC-enriched luminal tumors had higher mutation burden and elevated APOBEC3B/G protein with upregulated PD-L1, suggesting that APOBEC-driven mutagenesis is a key contributor to immunogenicity in ER+ breast cancer. Loss of single-strand break repair (SSBR) proteins (NER, BER, MMR) was anti-correlated with immune scores specifically in luminal tumors.
- **Genes**: CD274, PDCD1, CTLA4, APOBEC3B, APOBEC3G, STAT1
- **Data availability**: partial — mutation burden and mRNA data (PD-L1/PD1/CTLA4) in cBioPortal; APOBEC protein levels, immune signatures, and SSBR protein scores are not available
- **Tool support**: native — tumor mutational burden, mRNA expression, mutation type filtering; immune deconvolution scores require custom tools
- **Concepts**: tumor mutational burden, mutation frequency, survival analysis, actionability

### Finding 4: Rb Protein Abundance Predicts CDK4/6 Inhibitor Sensitivity Better Than RB1 Genomic Status
- **Detail**: In HR+/ERBB2− tumors, CDK4/6 activity and Rb phosphorylation strongly correlated with proliferation (MGPS), whereas in TNBC, Rb protein loss—not RB1 mutation—was the dominant driver of high proliferation. RB1 wild-type TNBC cell lines with low Rb protein were less sensitive to palbociclib, suggesting that proteomics-based Rb quantification could identify a CDK4/6-inhibitor-responsive TNBC subset missed by genomic analysis alone.
- **Genes**: RB1, CDK4, CDK6, CCND1, CCNE1, CDK2
- **Data availability**: partial — RB1 mutation and copy number data available; Rb protein abundance and phosphoproteomics not available in cBioPortal
- **Tool support**: native — RB1 mutation/CNA overlap with subtype; Rb protein-level analysis requires custom tools
- **Concepts**: mutation frequency, copy number alteration, actionability, targeted-therapy, molecular subtypes

### Finding 5: Subtype-Specific Activated Kinases Define Therapeutic Vulnerabilities
- **Detail**: Phosphoproteomic outlier analysis (BlackSheep) identified subtype-enriched hyperphosphorylated kinases as putative therapeutic targets: PRKDC, MAP4K4, and SPEG in NMF Basal-I; ERBB2 and CDK12 in NMF HER2-I; and DCLK1 in NMF LumA-I. Mutational context further refined targets—TNIK phosphorylation was elevated in ARID1A mutant tumors, and RIPK3 in MAP3K1 mutant tumors—linking tumor suppressor loss to targetable kinase signaling.
- **Genes**: PRKDC, MAP4K4, SPEG, ERBB2, CDK12, DCLK1, TNIK, RIPK3, MAP3K1, ARID1A, SLK, LATS1
- **Data availability**: not-available — kinase phosphorylation outlier scores are not in cBioPortal; underlying mutation data (ARID1A, MAP3K1) are available
- **Tool support**: custom — phosphoproteomic outlier scoring (BlackSheep) requires external tools; mutation co-occurrence queryable natively
- **Concepts**: mutation frequency, actionability, targeted-therapy, oncoprint

### Finding 6: TNBC Subtype Heterogeneity Informs Therapeutic Strategy
- **Detail**: Within TNBC, basal-like 1 (BL1) tumors had high proliferation with Rb loss and TP53 mutation, while luminal androgen receptor (LAR) tumors had lower proliferation, intact Rb, high AR protein, and PIK3CA mutations—suggesting different therapeutic strategies (CDK4/6i for Rb-intact TNBC; AR inhibition or PI3K inhibition for LAR subtype). mTOR activity was elevated in Rb-phosphoprotein-high TNBC, indicating PIK3CA-AKT-mTOR pathway activity independent of PIK3CA mutation.
- **Genes**: TP53, PIK3CA, RB1, AR, CDK4, CDK6, AKT1, MTOR
- **Data availability**: partial — TP53, PIK3CA, RB1, AR mutation/CNA data available; Rb protein and mTOR activity scores are not available in cBioPortal
- **Tool support**: native — mutation/CNA filtering by TNBC subtype; protein-level Rb and mTOR activity require custom tools
- **Concepts**: molecular subtypes, mutation frequency, actionability, oncoprint

### Finding 7: Metabolic Reprogramming and Acetylproteomics Reveal Subtype-Specific Vulnerabilities
- **Detail**: NMF Basal-I tumors showed upregulated glycolysis proteins (HK3, PFKP, GAPDH, LDHA) and serine synthesis enzymes (PHGDH, PSAT1) with simultaneous hypoacetylation of glycolytic enzymes—suggesting post-translational activation. Mitochondrial TCA cycle and β-oxidation proteins were hyperacetylated in Basal-I, linked to depleted SIRT3 protein, potentially suppressing mitochondrial function. The NMF HER2-I cluster showed upregulated cholesterol biosynthesis and lipid metabolism, independent of ERBB2 amplification.
- **Genes**: PHGDH, PSAT1, HK3, PFKP, GAPDH, LDHA, SIRT3, ERBB2
- **Data availability**: not-available — metabolic protein expression and acetylation data are not available in cBioPortal
- **Tool support**: custom — acetylproteomics and metabolic pathway scoring require external analysis
- **Concepts**: molecular subtypes, actionability

## Open Questions
- Can proteomics-based Rb quantification be implemented in clinical core needle biopsy workflows to prospectively select TNBC patients for CDK4/6 inhibitor trials?
- Do luminal breast cancers with APOBEC-driven mutagenesis or SSBR deficiency show durable response to immune checkpoint inhibitors, and what is the optimal combination with endocrine therapy?
- Do pseudo-ERBB2+ tumors (ERBB2-amplified but protein-low) respond differently to antibody-drug conjugates targeting HER2 (e.g., trastuzumab deruxtecan) compared with those with high ERBB2 protein expression?

## Limitations
- Sample size of 122 limits power for rare subgroup analyses (e.g., HER2-enriched, n=14; APOBEC-enriched, n=6) and demographic representation.
- Bulk cryopulverized tissue precludes single-cell or spatial resolution of tumor-immune interactions captured by imaging mass cytometry or spatial transcriptomics.
- Associations between proteogenomic features and clinical outcomes (drug response, survival) are largely hypothesis-generating from correlative analyses; prospective validation in therapeutic trials is required.
