## Summary

Genome-wide loss of heterozygosity (LOH), measured by MSK-IMPACT targeted sequencing with FACETS, is the strongest molecular predictor of aggressive, treatment-refractory behavior in pituitary neuroendocrine tumors (PitNETs). A recurrent pattern of chromosomal LOH spanning 12 specific chromosomes (rcLOH) was found in 11 of 14 treatment-refractory corticotroph tumors versus only 1 of 14 benign corticotrophs (p = 1.7×10⁻⁴), consistent with a hypodiploid genomic state. A random forest classifier using fraction of LOH achieved an accuracy of 0.88 (95% CI: 0.70–0.96) on the test set, outperforming TP53 mutation status alone (AUC 0.87 vs. 0.75), establishing LOH as a novel, actionable biomarker for this rare and deadly PitNET subtype.

## Key Findings

### F1: Aggressive PitNETs have elevated tumor mutational burden

**Finding:** Treatment-refractory PitNETs carry significantly more oncogenic somatic mutations than benign tumors.

**Detail:** Using MSK-IMPACT, tumor mutational burden (TMB) was significantly higher in the 23 treatment-refractory tumors compared to the 69 benign tumors (one-sided Wilcoxon, p = 1.3×10⁻¹⁰). Only 61% (15/23) of treatment-refractory cases harbored known high-risk mutations (TP53, ATRX, DAXX, or SF3B1), motivating the investigation of chromosomal-level genomic instability. The most common gene-level alteration was TP53 mutation, found in 12/23 treatment-refractory versus 1/69 benign tumors (Fisher's exact, p = 4.2×10⁻⁸); 11 of the 12 TP53 mutations were clonal.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53` · `ATRX` · `DAXX` · `SF3B1` · Pituitary neuroendocrine tumor

**Data availability:** available — somatic mutation data are deposited in cBioPortal study ptad_msk_2024.

**Tool support:** native — mutation frequencies and oncoplots are viewable directly in the cBioPortal UI.

### F2: Fraction of LOH is significantly elevated in treatment-refractory PitNETs

**Finding:** Treatment-refractory PitNETs have a higher fraction of loss of heterozygosity (LOH) than benign tumors, reflecting widespread chromosomal aneuploidy.

**Detail:** Allele-specific copy-number analysis with FACETS revealed significantly higher fraction of genome altered (FGA) and fraction of LOH in treatment-refractory tumors (one-sided Wilcoxon: p = 7.3×10⁻⁶ and p = 8.5×10⁻⁹, respectively). Cohort medians were FGA = 0.22 and fraction of LOH = 0.03; tumors above these thresholds were classified as FGA-High or LOH-High. LOH was further elevated in tumors harboring TP53 mutations (p = 3.3×10⁻⁸), consistent with TP53-driven genomic instability. LOH was stable across sequential resections and was observed in radiation-naïve samples, indicating it is an early and treatment-independent event.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53` · Pituitary neuroendocrine tumor

**Data availability:** available — copy-number and LOH data (derived from MSK-IMPACT + FACETS) are in ptad_msk_2024.

**Tool support:** native — fraction of genome altered is accessible via the cBioPortal UI; detailed per-chromosome LOH fractions may require api or custom FACETS queries.

**Concepts:** #LOH #copy-number #chromosomal-instability #pituitary #neuroendocrine

### F3: Recurrent chromosomal LOH pattern (rcLOH) is a hallmark of treatment-refractory corticotroph tumors

**Finding:** Treatment-refractory corticotroph PitNETs display a striking recurrent pattern of LOH across 12 specific chromosomes (rcLOH), consistent with hypodiploidy driven by multiple monosomies.

**Detail:** rcLOH (LOH covering ≥75% of each chromosome) was found in 11/14 (78%) treatment-refractory corticotroph tumors versus 1/14 (7%) benign corticotroph tumors (one-sided Fisher's exact, p = 1.7×10⁻⁴). The recurrently lost chromosomes were 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 10, 11, 15, 17, 18, 21, and 22. In 11 of 12 tumors with rcLOH, loss of ≥9 chromosomes occurred in the same cellular fraction, suggesting a single catastrophic event or early clonal sweep. FISH validation in 5 patients confirmed heterozygous loss (monosomy) rather than copy-neutral LOH for the affected chromosomes.

**Genes / cancer types:** `CDKN2A` · `CDKN2B` · `CCND3` · Corticotroph PitNET

**Data availability:** available — chromosomal-level LOH calls are in ptad_msk_2024; per-chromosome detail may require api or custom FACETS outputs.

**Tool support:** api — per-chromosome LOH fractions accessible via cBioPortal REST API; FISH data not available in cBioPortal.

**Concepts:** #LOH #chromosomal-instability #pituitary #neuroendocrine #molecular-subtypes #classification #treatment-refractory

### F4: Random forest classifier identifies LOH as top predictive feature for treatment-refractoriness

**Finding:** A random forest model trained on clinical and genomic features identified fraction of LOH as the single most predictive variable for aggressive, treatment-refractory behavior, outperforming all gene-level alterations including TP53.

**Detail:** The RF model was trained on 64 samples (17 treatment-refractory, 47 benign) with 22 non-zero-variance features and achieved an OOB error of 14.06%. On the 26-sample test set, the fraction of LOH binary classifier (threshold = 0.11) achieved accuracy = 0.88 (95% CI: 0.70–0.96), sensitivity = 0.83, specificity = 0.90, negative predictive value = 0.95, and AUC = 0.87—versus AUC = 0.75 for TP53 mutation status alone. Five of six treatment-refractory tumors (83%) in the test set were correctly classified. Feature importance ranking placed fraction of LOH first by mean minimal depth, mean decrease in accuracy, and Gini index.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53` · Pituitary neuroendocrine tumor

**Data availability:** partial — fraction of LOH values from FACETS are deposited in ptad_msk_2024; the RF model itself and its feature importance outputs are not directly available in cBioPortal.

**Tool support:** custom — reproducing the random forest classifier requires the randomForest R package and the facets-suite utility; LOH threshold query accessible via api.

**Concepts:** #LOH #prognosis #classification #treatment-refractory #pituitary #neuroendocrine

### F5: MMR deficiency identifies a subset of treatment-refractory corticotroph tumors potentially responsive to immunotherapy

**Finding:** Mismatch repair (MMR) deficiency was identified in 5 treatment-refractory corticotroph PitNETs, including 4 cases with MMR alterations in the pre-treatment diagnostic resection, conferring MSI-High status and high mutational burden.

**Detail:** Oncogenic or likely oncogenic alterations in MSH2, MSH6, or MLH1 were found in 5 treatment-refractory corticotroph tumors. Four of the 5 had MMR alterations in the treatment-naïve resection (TMB range 8.78–12.29 mut/Mb; MSI-High or MSI-indeterminate by MiMSI). A fifth patient developed MMR deficiency after temozolomide exposure, with a hypermutated profile (TMB = 93 mut/Mb, 76% of mutations attributed to temozolomide signature). Loss of MMR protein expression on IHC confirmed functional significance. Dramatic responses to PD-1 inhibitor pembrolizumab have been reported for MMR-deficient PitNETs.

**Genes / cancer types:** `MSH2` · `MSH6` · `MLH1` · Corticotroph PitNET

**Data availability:** available — MMR gene mutation data available in ptad_msk_2024; IHC and MiMSI scores not available in cBioPortal.

**Tool support:** native — MSI/MMR mutation status accessible in the cBioPortal UI; TMB and MSI scores accessible via api.

**Concepts:** #pituitary #neuroendocrine #molecular-subtypes #treatment-refractory

### F6: ATRX, DAXX, and TERT mutations mark alternative lengthening of telomeres in treatment-refractory corticotrophs

**Finding:** Mutations in ATRX, DAXX, and TERT—drivers of alternative lengthening of telomeres (ALT)—were identified exclusively in treatment-refractory corticotroph PitNETs and not in benign tumors.

**Detail:** ALT-associated mutations in ATRX, DAXX, and TERT were detected in aggressive, treatment-refractory corticotroph PitNETs and were absent from all 69 benign tumors in the cohort. TSC2 mutations were also found exclusively in treatment-refractory tumors. These findings are consistent with prior reports of ALT in recurrent PitNETs and suggest ALT is a late event or selected during progression. These mutations co-occurred with rcLOH and TP53 mutations in some patients, reflecting multi-hit genomic instability in the most aggressive tumors.

**Genes / cancer types:** `ATRX` · `DAXX` · `TERT` · `TSC2` · Corticotroph PitNET

**Data availability:** available — somatic mutation data for ATRX, DAXX, TERT, and TSC2 available in ptad_msk_2024.

**Tool support:** native — gene mutation data queryable directly in the cBioPortal UI.

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Can the rcLOH classifier (fraction LOH > 0.11) be prospectively validated in a larger, multi-institutional PitNET cohort to confirm its utility as a clinical biomarker before or shortly after first surgery?
- Do specific genes within the 12 recurrently lost chromosomes (1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 10, 11, 15, 17, 18, 21, 22) act as haploinsufficient tumor suppressors driving the treatment-refractory phenotype, or is the fitness advantage from monosomy itself?
- Could early identification of rcLOH at initial resection justify more aggressive adjuvant treatment (e.g., earlier radiotherapy or temozolomide) and improve outcomes in corticotroph PitNETs?
- Why do the two "benign" cases misclassified as treatment-refractory (high LOH gonadotroph and male lactotroph) have elevated LOH—do they represent tumors with latent aggressiveness that will manifest with longer follow-up?
- Does the rcLOH pattern in corticotroph PitNETs share functional pathway consequences (haploinsufficiency, gene dosage effects) with the analogous pattern seen in aggressive pancreatic neuroendocrine tumors?
- Given dramatic immunotherapy responses in MMR-deficient PitNETs, should routine MMR IHC or MSI testing be performed at initial diagnosis of all treatment-refractory or high-risk PitNETs?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Small total sample size (n = 92; only 23 treatment-refractory), which limits statistical power, may not capture the full genomic diversity of PitNETs, and necessitates validation in a larger prospective cohort.
- Retrospective design of the aggressive cohort introduces selection bias: these patients were enriched for the most extreme treatment-refractory phenotype, potentially overstating the predictive performance of the LOH classifier in a more typical clinical population.
- Median clinical follow-up in the prospective (benign) cohort was not long enough to exclude late recurrences; two "benign" cases with high LOH may represent tumors that have not yet declared themselves clinically.
- USP8 is not on the MSK-IMPACT panel and required separate whole-exome sequencing for detection; other potentially relevant genes absent from the IMPACT panel may be missed.
- Pathological tissue was obtained from FFPE specimens of varying age (particularly in the retrospective cohort), which may affect DNA quality and sequencing sensitivity for detecting low-allele-frequency events.
- The study does not directly test whether early intervention guided by LOH status improves patient outcomes; prospective clinical trials are needed to translate this biomarker into a treatment decision tool.
