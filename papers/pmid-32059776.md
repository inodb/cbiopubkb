## Summary
This CPTAC study performed comprehensive proteogenomic profiling of 95 prospectively collected endometrial carcinomas (83 endometrioid, 12 serous) using WES, WGS, RNA-seq, methylation arrays, and mass-spectrometry-based proteomics, phosphoproteomics, and acetylomics. Tumors were classified into the four TCGA genomic subtypes (POLE, MSI, CNV-low, CNV-high) and protein/PTM-level differences across subtypes revealed new mechanistic insights into Wnt/β-catenin, p53, and DNA damage response pathways. The study identified novel therapeutic targets in CNV-high tumors and showed that antigen processing and presentation efficiency—independently of TMB—shapes the immune microenvironment and immunotherapy eligibility.

## Dataset
- **Cohort**: 95 prospectively collected endometrial carcinomas (83 endometrioid, 12 serous) plus 49 matched normal tissue samples
- **Platform**: WES, WGS, total RNA-seq, miRNA-seq, Illumina Infinium MethylationEPIC array, LC-MS/MS (TMT isobaric labeling) for proteome/phosphoproteome/acetylome
- **Data types**: Somatic mutations, copy number, mRNA expression, miRNA, DNA methylation, proteomics, phosphoproteomics, acetylomics
- **cBioPortal study**: ucec_cptac_2020

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: Four genomic subtypes with distinct proteomic signatures
- **Detail**: Tumors were classified as POLE (n=7), MSI (n=25), CNV-low (n=43), and CNV-high (n=20). CNV-high tumors (all serous and some endometrioid) showed elevated ribosome biogenesis and ATM signaling phosphorylation, while CNV-low had downregulated cell cycle proteins. MSI tumors showed MLH1 promoter methylation silencing and high rates of PTEN (92%), ARID1A (76%), and JAK1 (44%) mutations.
- **Genes**: POLE, PTEN, ARID1A, MLH1, JAK1, RPL22
- **Data availability**: available — mutation, CNA, mRNA, and subtype classification are in cBioPortal for ucec_cptac_2020
- **Tool support**: native — genomic subtype, oncoprint, and mutation frequency views available in cBioPortal UI
- **Concepts**: mutation frequency, copy number alteration, oncoprint, molecular subtypes

### Finding 2: TP53 mutation type determines distinct downstream proteomic consequences
- **Detail**: All 12 serous tumors harbored TP53 mutations. Hotspot missense mutations in the DNA-binding domain elevated p53 protein and activated AURKA (supporting AURKA inhibitor use), while truncating mutations increased PLK1-T210 phosphorylation and mitotic entry. A third set of proteins (CDK1, XPO1, TPX2) was dysregulated by any TP53 mutation type.
- **Genes**: TP53, AURKA, CDK1, XPO1, PLK1, TPX2, XRN2
- **Data availability**: partial — TP53 mutation data and mRNA are available; phosphoproteomics and protein-level effects are not in cBioPortal
- **Tool support**: api — mutation and expression data queryable via API; phosphoproteomic data requires external access (CPTAC Data Portal)
- **Concepts**: mutation frequency, oncoprint, survival analysis

### Finding 3: CTNNB1 mutations activate Wnt signaling and worsen low-grade endometrioid prognosis
- **Detail**: CTNNB1 exon 3 hotspot mutations were found in 23 tumors (mostly CNV-low/MSI, all grade ≤2), elevating β-catenin, APC, and AXIN1 protein levels and increasing overall Wnt pathway activity. APC-mutated tumors showed a complementary but weaker increase in β-catenin, confirming two mutually exclusive mechanisms of Wnt activation associated with recurrence risk in otherwise favorable tumors.
- **Genes**: CTNNB1, APC, AXIN1
- **Data availability**: available — CTNNB1 and APC mutation data are in cBioPortal; Wnt protein-level effects not available
- **Tool support**: native — mutation frequency, co-occurrence, and oncoprint available in cBioPortal UI
- **Concepts**: mutation frequency, oncoprint, pathway analysis

### Finding 4: PLK1 overexpression and DDR activation define CNV-high / serous tumors with actionable targets
- **Detail**: Serous tumors showed markedly elevated PLK1 protein and phosphorylation of TP53BP1-S1763 and CHEK2-S163, driving G2M checkpoint bypass. DDR-high tumors were enriched in CNV-high samples, and hyperphosphorylated proteins in CNV-high endometrioid and serous tumors include CDK12 and SMARCA4—targeted by FDA-approved drugs—and DNMT1 (inhibited by azacitidine/decitabine). CDK12 hyperphosphorylation also suggests potential for PARP inhibitor sensitization and enhanced response to immune checkpoint blockade.
- **Genes**: PLK1, CDK12, SMARCA4, DNMT1, CHEK2, TP53BP1
- **Data availability**: partial — mRNA and CNA for these genes are in cBioPortal; phosphoproteomic data and DDR scores are not
- **Tool support**: api — expression and copy number data accessible via API; drug-target analysis requires custom code
- **Concepts**: copy number alteration, actionability, targeted-therapy, survival analysis

### Finding 5: 1q amplification suppresses p53 pathway activity via histone modifiers
- **Detail**: Chromosome 1q amplification—observed in both MSI and MSS tumors—was anti-correlated with p53 pathway activity. SETDB1 (1q histone methyltransferase) showed the strongest anticorrelation with p53 target proteins CDKN1A and TNFRSF10B, suggesting that SCNA-driven overexpression of 1q histone modifiers (SETDB1, PARP1, GATAD2B) can suppress p53 activity even in tumors without TP53 mutations.
- **Genes**: SETDB1, MDM4, PARP1, GATAD2B, SDE2, CDKN1A
- **Data availability**: partial — copy number and mRNA for 1q genes are in cBioPortal; protein-level p53 pathway activity scores are not
- **Tool support**: api — CNA and expression queryable via API; correlation analysis requires custom code
- **Concepts**: copy number alteration, pathway analysis, molecular subtypes

### Finding 6: Immune landscape defined by TMB and antigen processing efficiency
- **Detail**: Tumors were classified into four immune groups based on tumor mutation burden (TMB) and antigen processing/presentation machinery (APM) efficiency. JAK1 frameshift mutations (from microsatellite indels, present in 44% of MSI tumors) are a major immune evasion mechanism and correlate with TMB-high/APM-low status and lower IFNγ/IFNα activity. Reduced TAP1, TAP2, and HLA protein levels provided alternative APM suppression mechanisms. 78% of tumors had evidence of neoantigens or highly expressed cancer/testis antigens (e.g., IGF2BP3, ATAD2, PBK), supporting immunotherapy candidacy.
- **Genes**: JAK1, STAT1, TAP1, TAP2, IGF2BP3, ATAD2, PBK
- **Data availability**: partial — mutation and mRNA data are in cBioPortal; APM scores, HLA protein levels, and neoantigen data require external tools
- **Tool support**: custom — TMB/APM subtyping and immune infiltration scoring require custom analysis pipelines
- **Concepts**: mutation frequency, molecular subtypes, survival analysis

### Finding 7: circRNA–QKI–ESRP2 axis as a novel EMT regulatory mechanism
- **Detail**: 234 recurrent circRNAs were identified; their levels were globally co-regulated but uncorrelated with host gene expression. QKI protein levels positively correlated with circRNA abundance and EMT scores, while ESRP2 (an epithelial splicing regulator) was negatively correlated with QKI and 20 circRNAs. This suggests a positive feedback loop in which QKI promotes circRNA biogenesis, represses ESRP2, and drives EMT—a potentially actionable axis in aggressive EC.
- **Genes**: QKI, ESRP2, FBXW7, ZEB1, ZEB2
- **Data availability**: not-available — circRNA quantification and EMT scores from proteogenomics are not in cBioPortal
- **Tool support**: custom — circRNA analysis and protein-level EMT scoring require external tools
- **Concepts**: molecular subtypes, pathway analysis

## Open Questions
- Can AURKA inhibitors selectively benefit serous EC patients with hotspot TP53 missense mutations, as suggested by the proteomic data?
- Does JAK1 frameshift status, measured at the DNA level, reliably predict APM deficiency and resistance to immune checkpoint inhibitors in clinical practice?
- Do the SETDB1/1q amplification-driven p53 suppression mechanisms translate into worse outcomes compared to TP53-mutated tumors, and are they targetable?

## Limitations
- Small serous tumor cohort (n=12) limits power to detect serous-specific findings; proteogenomic comparisons between histotypes are underpowered.
- Clinical outcome data were immature at time of publication (prospective cohort), so survival associations are largely inferred from known biology rather than directly demonstrated.
- Proteomics and phosphoproteomics data are not available in cBioPortal, limiting reproducibility of protein-level findings without access to the CPTAC Data Portal or LinkedOmics.
