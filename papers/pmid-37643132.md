## Summary

This prospective study characterized the genomic landscape of 177 cervical cancer patients across multiple histologic subtypes (squamous, endocervical adenocarcinoma, gastric-type, adenosquamous, and other) sequenced with MSK-IMPACT at Memorial Sloan Kettering. PIK3CA (25%) and ERBB2 (12%) were the most common actionable alterations. Thirteen percent of patients had high TMB (>10 mut/Mb), and 37% had at least one OncoKB level 3B actionable alteration. Genomic profiling directly impacted clinical management: 18% of patients enrolled in therapeutic clinical trials with 10% matched based on their genomic results. All clinical and genomic data were made publicly available in cBioPortal and AACR GENIE.

## Key Findings

### F1: PIK3CA and ERBB2 are the most prevalent actionable alterations in cervical cancer
**Finding:** PIK3CA somatic mutations or amplifications were detected in 25% of cervical cancer patients, and ERBB2 alterations in 12%, making these the top actionable targets. KMT2C (10%) and KMT2D (9%) were also frequently altered but are not directly actionable.
**Detail:** Alterations were detected across histologic subtypes. The TCGA cervical cancer study had focused on early-stage SCC (n=144 SCC, few adenocarcinomas), leaving the genomic landscape of gastric-type and adenosquamous subtypes poorly characterized prior to this study.
**Genes / cancer types:** PIK3CA, ERBB2, KMT2C, KMT2D; cervical cancer (all histologies).
**Data availability:** available > cervix_msk_2023 in cBioPortal includes all MSK-IMPACT alteration data.
**Tool support:** native > OncoPrint, Mutations tab, and Study View in cervix_msk_2023; filter by histologic subtype.
**Concepts:** #PIK3CA #actionability #biomarker #PI3K #molecular-subtypes

### F2: 13% of patients had high TMB, 37% had at least one OncoKB level 3B actionable alteration
**Finding:** Thirteen percent of cervical cancer patients had TMB >10 mut/Mb (high TMB), of whom 3 were also MSI-H. Thirty-seven percent of patients had at least one OncoKB level 3B actionable alteration, representing a large fraction with potential therapeutic targeting options.
**Detail:** OncoKB level 3B alterations include mutations with clinical evidence in different tumor types or compelling biological evidence. No NTRK fusions, RET fusions, or BRAF V600E mutations were identified in this cohort, excluding tumor-agnostic kinase inhibitor indications. Thirteen percent had level 1 (FDA-authorized) actionable alterations — all MSI-H or TMB-H.
**Genes / cancer types:** Cervical cancer; TMB-H, MSI-H subsets.
**Data availability:** available > cervix_msk_2023 in cBioPortal; TMB and MSI status included.
**Tool support:** native > TMB and MSI filters in Study View; api > Retrieve OncoKB annotation programmatically.
**Concepts:** #TMB #MSI #actionability #ICI-biomarker #biomarker #immunotherapy

### F3: Genomic profiling drove clinical trial enrollment for 10% of patients
**Finding:** Of 177 patients, 30 (17%) enrolled in therapeutic clinical trials. Of these, 18 (10%) were matched to a trial specifically based on their MSK-IMPACT sequencing results, demonstrating the direct clinical utility of prospective tumor genomic profiling in cervical cancer.
**Detail:** Twenty patients (11%) participated in immune checkpoint inhibition trials; 2 remain progression-free at >5 years. This durable ICI response occurred in TMB-low/MSS tumors, raising questions about TMB as the sole biomarker for ICI benefit in cervical cancer.
**Genes / cancer types:** Cervical cancer; TMB-H, MSI-H, PIK3CA.
**Data availability:** available > cervix_msk_2023 in cBioPortal; clinical trial enrollment status in clinical data.
**Tool support:** native > Patient-level clinical data in Study View.
**Concepts:** #actionability #immunotherapy #ICI-biomarker #TMB #biomarker

### F4: Gastric-type and rare histologic subtypes have distinct and undercharacterized genomic landscapes
**Finding:** This study provides the first systematic prospective genomic characterization of gastric-type endocervical adenocarcinoma (GEA, n=22) and adenosquamous (n=21) cervical carcinoma using a comprehensive NGS panel, filling a major gap left by TCGA, which predominantly included SCC.
**Detail:** Prior large sequencing studies (TCGA: 144 SCC, 31 adenocarcinoma, 3 adenosquamous) left rare histologies underrepresented. No significant difference in OS was observed across histologic subtypes in this MSK cohort (though sample sizes were small), in contrast to prior SEER data showing worse survival for GEA.
**Genes / cancer types:** PIK3CA, KMT2C, ERBB2; cervical cancer — gastric-type, adenosquamous subtypes.
**Data availability:** available > cervix_msk_2023 in cBioPortal; filter by histology in Study View.
**Tool support:** native > Histology filter in Study View; OncoPrint by subtype.
**Concepts:** #molecular-subtypes #actionability #biomarker #PIK3CA

### F5: Durable ICI responses occurred in TMB-low/MSS tumors, questioning TMB as sole immunotherapy biomarker
**Finding:** Two patients with exceptional responses (>5 years progression-free) to immune checkpoint inhibition had TMB-low/MSS tumors, challenging the assumption that TMB-H or MSI-H are necessary for durable ICI benefit in cervical cancer.
**Detail:** The modest overall response rate (~10–14%) to single-agent anti-PD-1 in cervical cancer is consistent with the MSK experience. The presence of exceptional responders with TMB-L/MSS tumors suggests that additional biomarkers (PD-L1, tumor-infiltrating lymphocytes, HPV integration patterns) merit investigation.
**Genes / cancer types:** Cervical cancer.
**Data availability:** partial > Clinical outcomes data for ICI trials available in cervix_msk_2023; biomarker details require clinical records beyond cBioPortal.
**Tool support:** native > Survival by TMB/MSI in cBioPortal; api > Clinical and genomic data download.
**Concepts:** #ICI-biomarker #TMB #MSI #immunotherapy #biomarker

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- What is the biomarker basis for durable ICI response in TMB-low/MSS cervical cancers — PD-L1, TIL density, HPV type, or other?
- Can dual checkpoint blockade (anti-PD-1 + anti-CTLA-4) substantially improve response rates over single-agent PD-1 blockade in PIK3CA-mutant or ERBB2-amplified cervical cancers?
- Do gastric-type endocervical adenocarcinomas (HPV-negative) have a distinct alteration landscape (e.g., ARID1A, KRAS) that predicts response to targeted therapy?
- Can ERBB2 amplification predict HER2-directed therapy response in cervical cancer (analogous to gastric/breast cancer)?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Single-institution study (MSK) with potential referral and selection bias toward advanced/recurrent cases.
- Small sample sizes within histologic subtypes limit subgroup analyses.
- Survival analysis limited by short median OS (59 months) and heterogeneous treatment histories.
- No NTRK/RET/BRAF V600E alterations identified, limiting generalizability of tumor-agnostic biomarker findings.
- OncoKB level 3B actionability classification may change as new evidence accumulates.
- ICI response biomarkers beyond TMB/MSI-H were not systematically evaluated.
