## Summary

This study applied a supervised machine learning algorithm (the hidden-genome classifier) to classify intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (IHC) based on genetic similarity to either extrahepatic biliary tumors (EHC/GBC) or hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), using MSK-IMPACT data from 1,370 patients with biliary tract cancers and HCC. The classifier identified IHC subclasses with distinct genomic drivers and significantly different overall survival outcomes, outperforming histologic subtyping for prognosis. IHC tumors genetically most similar to EHC/GBC (biliary-class, 23%) had the worst survival, independent of *FGFR2* and *IDH1* alterations.

## Key Findings

### F1: IHC genetics form a continuous spectrum from biliary to hepatocellular

**Finding:** The hidden-genome classifier reveals that IHC tumors span a genetic continuum between extrahepatic biliary tumors and HCC, with 78% biliary-aligned and 22% HCC-aligned.

**Detail:** Of 527 IHC tumors with at least one MSK-IMPACT alteration, 410 (78%) had >50% genetic similarity to EHC/GBC (biliary-aligned), while 117 (22%) had >50% similarity to HCC (HCC-aligned). The two extreme subclasses — biliary-class (biliary score >0.9, n=122, 23%) and HCC-class (biliary score <0.1, n=30, 5.7%) — showed distinct clinical and genomic features. The hidden-genome model achieved AUC of 99% (EHC/GBC) and 98% (HCC) in cross-validation.

### F2: Biliary-class IHC is characterized by KRAS, SMAD4, and CDKN2A alterations

**Finding:** The biliary-class IHC tumors are enriched for genetic drivers typical of extrahepatic biliary cancers.

**Detail:** Biliary-class IHC was enriched for *KRAS* mutation (33%, p<0.001), *CDKN2A* loss (30%, p<0.001), and *SMAD4* mutation (7.4%, p<0.001). The trinucleotide mutational context C>T T.A was also significantly enriched (23%, p<0.001). These alterations are the primary genetic predictors of EHC/GBC in the hidden-genome model (KRAS OR=1.48, CDKN2A loss OR=1.28).

**Genes / cancer types:** `KRAS`, `SMAD4`, `CDKN2A` · Intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (biliary-class)

### F3: HCC-class IHC is characterized by TERT alterations and HCC-like copy-number changes

**Finding:** HCC-class IHC tumors are enriched for TERT promoter mutations and HCC-associated copy-number alterations.

**Detail:** HCC-class IHC was enriched for *TERT* mutation (43%, p<0.001), specifically the *TERT* promoter G>A mutation at Chr5:1295228 (OR=2.20 in the model). Additional HCC-like features included cytoband 8p21.3 deletion and 1q31.1 amplification, associated with hepatocyte differentiation. HCC-class was predominantly small-duct IHC (SD-IHC, 64%) and was associated with higher BMI and diabetes — risk factors more typical of HCC.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TERT`, `CTNNB1` · Intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (HCC-class), Hepatocellular carcinoma

**Data availability:** available

### F4: Biliary-class IHC has significantly worse survival than non-biliary-class IHC

**Finding:** Biliary-class IHC has substantially worse overall survival compared to non-biliary-class IHC in both resectable and unresectable patients.

**Detail:** In unresectable patients, median OS was 1.0 year (95% CI: 0.77–1.5) for biliary-class vs. 1.8 years (95% CI: 1.6–2.0) for non-biliary-class IHC. In resected patients, median OS was 2.4 years (95% CI: 2.1–NR) vs. 5.1 years (95% CI: 4.4–6.9). The biliary-class survival was comparable to that of EHC/GBC, while non-biliary-class IHC survival approached that of HCC.

### F5: Genomic classifier predicts OS independently of FGFR2 and IDH1 alterations

**Finding:** The hidden-genome classifier predicts overall survival independently of the clinically actionable IHC-specific alterations *FGFR2* fusion and *IDH1* mutation.

**Detail:** Neither *IDH1* mutation nor *FGFR2* fusion correlated with OS in either unresected or resected patients (all p>0.3). Excluding patients with *IDH1* or *FGFR2* alterations did not attenuate the prognostic power of the genomic classifier: median OS was 0.98 vs. 1.6 years (unresected) and 2.1 vs. 5.1 years (resected) for biliary- vs. non-biliary-class IHC. *FGFR2* fusion was notably more common in HCC-aligned (26%) than biliary-aligned (7.1%) IHC (p<0.001).

**Genes / cancer types:** `FGFR2`, `IDH1` · Intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma

**Data availability:** available
> FGFR2 fusions and IDH1 mutations are in hcc_msk_2024; survival data included.

**Tool support:** api
> Interaction testing between classifier class and FGFR2/IDH1 requires scripting; individual gene-survival associations can be explored natively.

### F6: Histologic subtype is inferior to genomic classification for prognosis

**Finding:** Histologic classification into large-duct (LD-IHC) and small-duct (SD-IHC) subtypes does not predict overall survival, whereas the genomic classifier does.

**Detail:** Among unresected patients, OS did not differ significantly across LD-IHC (1.4 yr), SD-IHC (1.3 yr), and indeterminate (0.8 yr) histologic subtypes (p=0.68). By contrast, biliary-class vs. HCC-class IHC showed significantly different OS (1.0 yr vs. 2.0 yr, p=0.031). LD-IHC was more common in biliary-class (33% vs. 3.3%) and HCC-class tumors were predominantly SD-IHC (67% vs. 40%; p=0.001).

**Genes / cancer types:** — · Intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma

**Data availability:** partial

**Tool support:** custom
> Histologic subtype data requires external annotation; genomic classifier requires external ML pipeline.

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Can the hidden-genome biliary score be approximated by simple genomic proxies (e.g., KRAS/CDKN2A positive vs. FGFR2/IDH1 positive) queryable natively in cBioPortal?
- Does biliary-class IHC respond differently to FOLFOX/GEMCIS chemotherapy compared to non-biliary-class?
- Are there survival differences within HCC-class IHC by TERT promoter mutation vs. other TERT alterations?
- Could the hidden-genome approach stratify patients in ongoing FGFR2/IDH1-targeted therapy trials?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Retrospective single-center design at MSK; treatment patterns may not reflect community practice.
- Hidden-genome classifier trained and validated at the same institution; external validation cohorts used different sequencing panels.
- Histologic subtype classification limited to a subset of 151 tumors (biliary-class n=121, HCC-class n=30); some tumors classified as indeterminate.
- Small sample size in HCC-class (n=30) limits power for survival analyses in this subgroup.
- Overall survival, not disease-specific survival, used as the endpoint.
