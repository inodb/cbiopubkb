## Summary
This study performed integrated whole-exome and RNA sequencing of 37 high-grade UTUC tumors from three academic centers (WCM, BCM, MDACC), revealing that UTUC is predominantly luminal-papillary (84%) and T-cell depleted (88%), in stark contrast to bladder cancer. FGFR3 is a putative driver of this immune-cold phenotype by suppressing interferon-gamma signaling, providing a rationale for combining FGFR3 inhibitors with immune checkpoint blockade as a UTUC-specific strategy.

## Dataset
- **Cohort**: High-grade UTUC patients from Weill Cornell Medicine (WCM), Baylor College of Medicine, and MD Anderson Cancer Center (BCM–MDACC); TCGA UCB cohort (n=128) used as comparator
- **Assay**: Whole-exome sequencing (WES, Illumina HiSeq 2500, ~85× coverage) and RNA sequencing (RNAseq); IHC for MMR protein expression
- **N**: 37 UTUC tumor–normal WES pairs; 32 UTUC tumors for RNAseq (WCM + BCM-MDA)
- **cBioPortal study ID(s)**: utuc_cornell_baylor_mdacc_2019

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: FGFR3 is mutated at higher frequency in UTUC than bladder cancer
- **Detail**: FGFR3 mutations found in 11/37 (29.7%) UTUC vs. 17/124 (13.7%) TCGA UCB (Wilcoxon P=0.04). FGFR3 mRNA expression outliers detected in 14/32 (43.7%) UTUC tumors. Cell lines with activating FGFR3 fusions (FGFR3-TACC3, FGFR3-BAIAP2L1) show IFNG response gene upregulation upon erdafitinib treatment.
- **Genes**: FGFR3
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: mutation, expression, fusion

### Finding 2: UTUC is predominantly luminal-papillary
- **Detail**: 27/32 (84.3%) UTUC tumors classified as luminal by BASE47 classifier vs. 59/128 (46.1%) UCB. By TCGA classifier, 20/32 (62.5%) UTUC were luminal-papillary vs. 27.3% of UCB. Result confirmed by three independent classifiers (BASE47, MDACC, TCGA) and unsupervised NMF.
- **Genes**: FGFR3, KRT20, UPK1A, UPK2, SNX31
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: api
- **Concepts**: expression, molecular-subtypes, clinical

### Finding 3: UTUC has a T-cell depleted immune contexture
- **Detail**: 28/32 (87.5%) UTUC tumors were T-cell depleted by a 170-immune-gene classifier, compared to nearly equal split in UCB (55.5% T-cell depleted, 44.5% T-cell inflamed; Fisher's exact P=0.0009). Key downregulated genes include CD8A, CCL2, CCL3, CCL4, CXCL9, CXCL10, and IFNG signaling genes.
- **Genes**: CD8A, CCL2, CCL3, CCL4, CXCL9, CXCL10, IFNG
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: api
- **Concepts**: expression, pathway

### Finding 4: FGFR3 suppresses interferon-gamma signaling and drives immune exclusion
- **Detail**: FGFR3 expression is significantly higher in T-cell-depleted vs. T-cell-inflamed clusters (Wilcoxon P=1.3×10⁻⁶). shRNA knockdown and pharmacologic inhibition with erdafitinib (1–5 nM) in RT-112, RT-4, SW780 cell lines upregulate IFNG-response genes BST2, MX2, IRF9, GBP2. GSEA shows enrichment of IFNG-response gene sets after FGFR3 blockade (FDR=0.062).
- **Genes**: FGFR3, BST2, MX2, IRF9, GBP2, STAT1, PPARG
- **Data availability**: partial
- **Tool support**: custom
- **Concepts**: expression, pathway

### Finding 5: Sporadic UTUC has lower tumor mutational burden than bladder cancer
- **Detail**: Mean TMB 2.91 mut/MB in UTUC vs. 5.46 mut/MB in TCGA UCB (Mann–Whitney P=1.9×10⁻⁵). Despite lower TMB, dominant mutational signatures are APOBEC (COSMIC signatures 2 and 13), MMR-associated C>T at CpG (signature 6), and NER/ERCC2-related (signature 5). MSI sensor scores below threshold of 3.5 in both cohorts.
- **Genes**: ERCC2
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: mutation, clinical

### Finding 6: MMR protein expression is reduced in UTUC without causing microsatellite instability
- **Detail**: MLH1, PMS2, MSH2, and MSH6 mRNA and protein levels (by IHC, H-score) are significantly lower in UTUC vs. stage-matched UCB (n=16 UTUC, n=14 UCB; t-test P<0.05). No germline MMR mutations detected; MSI sensor scores remain below MSI-H threshold (3.5). Partial/incomplete MMR protein loss in sporadic UTUC is insufficient to produce MSI or elevated TMB.
- **Genes**: MLH1, PMS2, MSH2, MSH6, EXO1, RFC3
- **Data availability**: partial
- **Tool support**: api
- **Concepts**: expression, mutation, clinical

### Finding 7: FGFR3 inhibition as a therapeutic strategy in UTUC
- **Detail**: Erdafitinib received FDA accelerated approval for FGFR-altered metastatic bladder cancer (32.2% response rate in 87 patients). Authors propose FGFR3 inhibitor + PD-1/PD-L1 inhibitor combinations as UTUC-specific strategy based on FGFR3's role in immune exclusion. Luminal-papillary/T-cell-depleted phenotype associated with lowest response rates to atezolizumab in prior phase 2 trials.
- **Genes**: FGFR3
- **Data availability**: not-available
- **Tool support**: custom
- **Concepts**: clinical, pathway

## Open Questions
- Can FGFR3 mutation or expression outlier status in `utuc_cornell_baylor_mdacc_2019` predict survival outcomes in UTUC patients?
- Do FGFR3 fusion subtypes (FGFR3-TACC3 vs. FGFR3-BAIAP2L1) have different immune contextures or clinical outcomes in the cBioPortal cohort?
- Does the T-cell-depleted immune signature in UTUC correlate with stage, grade, or anatomic location (renal pelvis vs. ureter)?
- Can co-mutation patterns (e.g., FGFR3 + chromatin modifier mutations) be interrogated in the cBioPortal study to define biological subgroups?

## Limitations
- Small sample size (n=37 WES, n=32 RNAseq) limits statistical power and generalizability; UTUC is a rare tumor type
- All samples from three academic institutions; potential selection bias toward high-grade disease
- No matched primary–metastatic pairs; molecular profiles across disease stages not characterized
- Functional FGFR3–IFNG link established in bladder cancer cell lines, not primary UTUC cultures
- No survival analysis performed within the UTUC cohort; clinical outcome data limited
- Lynch syndrome patients not included; findings apply specifically to sporadic UTUC
