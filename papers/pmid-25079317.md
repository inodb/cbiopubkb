## Summary

TCGA multi-platform analysis of 295 primary gastric adenocarcinomas using WES, somatic copy-number arrays, mRNA-seq, miRNA-seq, DNA methylation arrays, and RPPA. The central contribution is a four-subtype molecular classification—EBV-positive (9%), microsatellite unstable/MSI (22%), genomically stable/GS (20%), and chromosomally unstable/CIN (50%)—that cuts across the traditional Lauren histological categories and provides a framework for patient stratification. Each subtype carries distinct actionable targets: PIK3CA/JAK2/PD-L1 in EBV; ERBB2/ERBB3 hotspots in MSI; RHOA/CLDN18–ARHGAP fusions in GS; RTK amplifications in CIN. This taxonomy is directly queryable in the `stad_tcga_pub` cBioPortal study.

## Key Findings

### F1: Four-subtype molecular classification of gastric cancer

**Finding:** Integrative clustering across six molecular platforms resolves gastric cancer into four biologically distinct subtypes: EBV+ (9%), MSI (22%), genomically stable/GS (20%), and chromosomally unstable/CIN (50%), with distinct clinical, histological, and therapeutic correlates.

**Detail:** A decision-tree classifier first assigns EBV positivity, then MSI status, then degree of aneuploidy (CIN vs. GS). Diffuse histology is enriched in GS (73%, P=7.5×10⁻¹⁷). CIN tumors cluster at the gastroesophageal junction/cardia (65%). MSI tumors occur at older ages (median 72 yr) and are female-enriched; EBV tumors are male-enriched (81%). No survival differences were detected at the time of publication, but the taxonomy informs targeted therapy trial design.

**Genes / cancer types:** EBV, MSI/MLH1, RHOA, TP53, CDH1 · Gastric adenocarcinoma

### F2: EBV-positive subtype: extreme DNA hypermethylation and PIK3CA mutations

**Finding:** EBV-positive tumors display the highest degree of CpG island hypermethylation of any cancer type reported by TCGA (EBV-CIMP), near-universal CDKN2A silencing, and PIK3CA mutations in 80% of cases (vs. 3–42% in other subtypes), with frequent amplification of JAK2, PD-L1 (CD274), and PD-L2 (PDCD1LG2) at 9p24.1.

**Detail:** All EBV-positive tumors show CDKN2A promoter hypermethylation; none show MLH1 hypermethylation (distinguishing EBV-CIMP from MSI-CIMP). PIK3CA mutations are dispersed across the gene in EBV tumors (vs. kinase-domain enriched in EBV-negative). The 9p24.1 JAK2/PD-L1/PD-L2 amplification occurs in ~15% of EBV tumors, with elevated expression of PD-L1/2 in the broader EBV group, providing rationale for immune checkpoint inhibitors and JAK2 inhibitors in this subtype.

**Genes / cancer types:** PIK3CA, JAK2, CD274 (PD-L1), PDCD1LG2 (PD-L2), CDKN2A, ARID1A, BCOR · Gastric adenocarcinoma (EBV+ subtype)

### F3: RHOA mutations and CLDN18–ARHGAP fusions define the genomically stable subtype

**Finding:** Genomically stable tumors, enriched for diffuse histology, harbor recurrent RHOA mutations (15% of GS cases) and CLDN18–ARHGAP26/6 gene fusions (13 total cases), both enriched in GS and mutually exclusive with each other. Together they affect ~30% of GS tumors, implicating dysregulated RHO GTPase signaling in the invasive diffuse phenotype.

**Detail:** RHOA mutations cluster in the amino-terminal region at the RHOA-ROCK1 interface (Y42 and D59 hotspots), predicted to modulate—not abolish—downstream signaling. CLDN18–ARHGAP26 fusions produce an in-frame protein retaining CLDN18 transmembrane domains and the ARHGAP GAP domain, potentially altering RHOA regulation and cellular adhesion. CDH1 somatic mutations are also enriched in GS (37%), paralleling germline CDH1 mutations in hereditary diffuse gastric cancer.

**Genes / cancer types:** RHOA, CLDN18, ARHGAP26, ARHGAP6, CDH1 · Gastric adenocarcinoma (GS subtype)

### F4: CIN subtype: RTK amplifications and TP53 mutations as therapeutic opportunities

**Finding:** Chromosomally unstable (CIN) tumors, comprising 50% of gastric adenocarcinomas, harbor TP53 mutations in 71%, marked aneuploidy, and focal amplifications of multiple actionable RTKs: ERBB2, EGFR, MET, KRAS, VEGFA, CCNE1, CCND1, CDK6, GATA4, GATA6, and CD44.

**Detail:** Phospho-EGFR (pY1068) is elevated in CIN tumors by RPPA, consistent with EGFR amplification. VEGFA amplification is notable given clinical activity of ramucirumab (VEGFR2 antibody) in gastric cancer. CCNE1, CCND1, and CDK6 amplifications suggest CDK inhibitor testing. ERBB2 amplification and point mutations (including S310F, a drug-sensitizing hotspot) are actionable with trastuzumab and pertuzumab.

**Genes / cancer types:** ERBB2, EGFR, MET, KRAS, VEGFA, CCNE1, CCND1, CDK6, TP53 · Gastric adenocarcinoma (CIN subtype)

### F5: MSI subtype: elevated mutation rate with hotspot mutations in targetable oncogenes

**Finding:** MSI gastric tumors (22%) show elevated mutation rates driven by MLH1 promoter hypermethylation and MLH1-CIMP, with significantly mutated genes including ERBB2, ERBB3, EGFR, PIK3CA, KRAS, ARID1A, RNF43, B2M, and HLA-B; notably, BRAF V600E (common in MSI colorectal cancer) is absent.

**Detail:** Within MSI tumors, MutSigCV identifies 10 significantly mutated genes in the base-substitution analysis, expanding to 37 with indels. B2M and HLA-B mutations (MHC class I) suggest immune evasion mechanisms distinct from those in EBV tumors. ERBB3 mutations affect 16/63 hypermutated tumors with 13 at hotspot/COSMIC sites. The absence of BRAF V600E distinguishes gastric MSI from colorectal MSI, suggesting different therapeutic contexts.

**Genes / cancer types:** ERBB3, ERBB2, PIK3CA, KRAS, ARID1A, RNF43, B2M, HLA-B, TP53, PTEN · Gastric adenocarcinoma (MSI subtype)

## Open Questions

- Does the four-subtype classification maintain prognostic power with longer follow-up and across non-TCGA cohorts?
- Can RHOA mutations and CLDN18–ARHGAP fusions serve as companion biomarkers for diffuse gastric cancer trials (e.g., claudiximab)?
- What is the clinical benefit of PD-1/PD-L1 checkpoint blockade specifically in the EBV-positive subtype with JAK2/PD-L1 amplification?
- Are the MSI-specific ERBB2/ERBB3 hotspot mutations functionally activating and responsive to HER2-targeted agents?
- Why is BRAF V600E absent in gastric MSI tumors when it is common in colorectal MSI?

## Limitations

- No prior chemotherapy or radiation treatment in included patients — limits applicability to treated/advanced disease
- Survival data were immature at time of publication (outcome analyses deferred)
- 9p24.1 amplification frequency (15% of EBV tumors) may be underestimated with array-based CNA
- Whole-genome sequencing performed on only a subset (n=107), limiting structural variant discovery to that subset
- RHOA mutation functional studies performed externally; mechanistic understanding of GS biology remains incomplete
