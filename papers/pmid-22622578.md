## Summary

First high-resolution whole-genome sequencing of 25 metastatic melanoma/germline pairs, plus capillary sequencing of PREX2 in 107 additional tumors. Average mutation rate was 30 mutations/Mb but ranged nearly 100-fold, from 3/Mb in acral melanoma to 111/Mb in a chronic-sun-exposure case. Dominant signatures: C→T transitions at dipyrimidines (UV damage) and transcription-coupled repair strand bias. Confirmed BRAF V600E (64%) and NRAS (36%) as the top drivers, and discovered PREX2 — a PTEN-interacting Rho-GEF — as the fourth most significantly mutated gene (14% in the extension cohort), with truncating and point mutations that are gain-of-function oncogenic in xenograft models. Structural rearrangement analysis found 106 recurrently rearranged loci including PTEN, MAGI2, and ETV1. Dataset in `skcm_broad_dfarber` on cBioPortal.

## Key Findings

### F1: UV mutagenesis produces a nearly 100-fold range in mutation rate; acral melanoma has a distinct mutational profile

**Finding:** Average somatic mutation rate of 30/Mb across 25 genomes, but ranging from 3/Mb (acral, non-UV-exposed extremity skin) to 111/Mb (sample ME009 with chronic sun exposure history). C→T transitions at CpC/TpC dinucleotides dominate in sun-exposed tumors; acral melanoma (ME015) shows a CpG-enriched pattern typical of non-UV tumor types.

**Detail:** 93% of substitutions in the hypermutated ME009 were C→T transitions vs. only 36% in acral ME015. Transcription-coupled repair strand bias was present in most tumors, reflecting differential DNA damage repair on transcribed vs. non-transcribed strands. Acral melanoma ME032 had one of the lowest base mutation rates (21st of 25) but the second-highest structural rearrangement count (314 validated rearrangements), illustrating that different tumors may preferentially use point mutations vs. structural alterations. This dataset was one of the first to demonstrate chromothripsis-like rearrangement patterns in melanoma (ME020, ME035).

### F2: PREX2 is the fourth most significantly mutated gene in melanoma — a novel oncogene with gain-of-function mutations

**Finding:** PREX2 (PTEN-interacting phosphatidylinositol 3,4,5-trisphosphate RAC exchange factor 2) was the fourth most significant gene in the 25-sample discovery cohort (q=8×10⁻⁵), mutated in 11/25 tumors with 13 non-synonymous mutations including 4 nonsense truncations. In the 107-sample extension cohort, 14% had non-synonymous PREX2 mutations. 13 of 14 mutations (in the discovery cohort) were non-synonymous, suggestive of strong positive selection.

**Detail:** Mutations were distributed throughout the full length of PREX2, spanning DH, PH, DEP, PDZ, and inositol phosphatase-homology domains. At least 2 mutations appeared homozygous by allele frequency analysis. In ME032, a truncating mutation (E824*) occurred on the non-amplified allele alongside PREX2 amplification and 9 somatic rearrangements. Xenograft experiments in PMEL-NRAS* immortalized human melanocytes showed that 3 truncated PREX2 variants and one point mutant (G844D) significantly accelerated tumor formation vs. GFP or wild-type PREX2 controls. This suggests a gain-of-function mechanism — truncated/variant PREX2 may act as a dominant negative toward PTEN or acquire new activities — rather than simple loss-of-function.

### F3: BRAF V600E (64%) and NRAS (36%) remain the dominant drivers; mutual exclusivity confirmed except in hypermutated tumors

**Finding:** BRAF V600E was present in 16/25 tumors (64%) including one acral melanoma (ME015). NRAS was mutated in 9/25 (36%) in a mutually exclusive pattern with BRAF, with a single exception (NRAS T50I, non-canonical, in hypermutated ME009). KIT exon 11 in-frame deletion was found in acral ME032.

**Detail:** The mutual exclusivity of BRAF and NRAS mutations is nearly absolute outside of hypermutated backgrounds, consistent with both activating the MAPK pathway. The acral KIT exon 11 deletion is clinically significant: KIT mutations occur in ~15% of acral/mucosal melanomas and exon 11 variants predict imatinib sensitivity. BRAF V600E mutations were confirmed as actionable targets for selective RAF inhibitors (vemurafenib), with resistance mechanisms involving other genomic events identified in this and companion studies.

### F4: Recurrent structural rearrangements implicate PTEN, MAGI2, ETV1, and A2BP1 in melanoma pathogenesis

**Finding:** Average of 97 structural rearrangements per genome (range 6–420). 106 genes were recurrently rearranged in ≥2 samples. Tumor suppressor PTEN was disrupted by rearrangement in 4 tumors; MAGI2 (which encodes a PTEN-stabilizing scaffold) in 3. ETV1 (melanoma lineage gene) had 6 validated rearrangements in ME032. A2BP1 rearrangements (4 tumors) may disrupt RNA splicing regulation.

**Detail:** PTEN disruption by structural rearrangement extends beyond the known point mutation and deletion mechanisms in melanoma and reinforces the PI3K pathway as central. MAGI2 rearrangement was also found in prostate cancer WGS, highlighting pan-cancer relevance. The chromothripsis-like pattern in ME020 and ME035 (90+ interchromosomal rearrangements concentrated between 2 chromosomes) represents catastrophic genomic instability associated with unique genomic histories. Recurrently rearranged genes at fragile sites (FHIT, MACROD2, CSMD1) require careful interpretation as they may be passenger events.

**Genes / cancer types:** PTEN, MAGI2, ETV1, A2BP1, FHIT · Metastatic melanoma

### F5: PREX2 gains oncogenic function through mutation — a new category distinct from classical oncogenes and tumor suppressors

**Finding:** PREX2 mutations span the entire protein, include both truncating and missense events, and generate proteins with gain-of-function tumorigenicity in vivo. This pattern — distributed mutations generating dominant-active or dominant-negative variants from a gene that interacts with a tumor suppressor (PTEN) — may define a new class of cancer genes beyond classical oncogenes and tumor suppressors.

**Detail:** In the extension cohort, 100% of mutations in tumor samples (n=4) were non-synonymous vs. 55% in short-term cultures, suggesting that in vitro growth relaxes selection for PREX2 mutations. At least one tumor (ME018) has 3 missense mutations, 2 co-occurring on a single allele (I534M, G1581R). ME032 showed amplification, rearrangement, AND a truncating mutation on separate alleles, suggesting multiple independent mechanisms of PREX2 dysregulation in a single tumor. PREX2 functions as a Rho-GEF and interacts with PTEN to modulate PI3K/AKT signaling; mutant PREX2 may constitutively activate this pathway.

## Open Questions

1. What is the mechanism by which truncated PREX2 gains oncogenic activity — dominant negative toward PTEN, constitutive Rac1-GEF activity, or PI3K pathway de-repression?
2. Are PREX2-mutant melanomas more sensitive to PI3K/AKT inhibitors, given the predicted disruption of PTEN regulation?
3. Does PREX2 mutation status predict resistance to BRAF/MEK inhibitors via parallel PI3K pathway activation?
4. How does chromothripsis arise in melanoma (ME020, ME035), and does it correlate with specific clinical features or UV exposure history?
5. Can the UV mutation signature be exploited to predict immunotherapy (anti-PD-1) response in melanoma beyond current TMB-based approaches?

## Limitations

- Discovery cohort of 25 tumors has limited power to detect genes mutated in <10% of melanomas.
- All 25 tumors are metastatic; the genomic landscape of primary melanomas may differ.
- Extension cohort used capillary sequencing of PREX2 only; other novel genes from the discovery cohort were not validated in the larger series.
- Short-term melanoma cell cultures in the extension cohort may not fully represent the in vivo tumor biology (evidenced by altered non-synonymous:synonymous ratios).
- Functional studies focused on NRAS-mutant melanocytes; PREX2 effects in BRAF V600E backgrounds not directly tested.
