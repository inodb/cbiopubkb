## Summary

This phase I/II trial evaluated ibrutinib monotherapy (840 mg/day) in 46 patients with relapsed/refractory primary (PCNSL, n=31) or secondary (SCNSL, n=15) CNS lymphoma with a median follow-up exceeding 4–5 years. Ibrutinib achieved tumor responses in 74% of PCNSL and 60% of SCNSL, with 3-year PFS of 10.1% and 20.7% respectively, confirming durable long-term responses in a subset. Exploratory biomarker analysis identified *TBL1XR1* mutations as a candidate predictor of long-term ibrutinib response (p=0.0075), and CSF ctDNA clearance as a correlate of complete and durable response.

## Key Findings

### F1: Ibrutinib achieves high response rate in relapsed/refractory CNS lymphoma

**Finding:** Single-agent ibrutinib produced tumor responses in 74% of PCNSL and 60% of SCNSL patients in the relapsed/refractory setting.

**Detail:** Among 29 evaluable PCNSL patients, 23 (74%) responded: 12 complete responses (CR) and 11 partial responses (PR). Among 13 evaluable SCNSL patients, 9 (60%) responded, including 7 CRs. Median PFS for PCNSL was 4.5 months (95% CI: 2.8–9.2); median OS was 25.4 months (95% CI: 13.2–NR). For SCNSL, median PFS was 5.3 months (95% CI: 1.3–14.5) with median OS not reached. 3-year PFS was 10.1% (PCNSL) and 20.7% (SCNSL).

### F2: TBL1XR1 mutations associate with durable ibrutinib response in PCNSL

**Finding:** PCNSL tumors harboring *TBL1XR1* mutations had significantly longer progression-free survival on ibrutinib than *TBL1XR1*-wild-type tumors.

**Detail:** *TBL1XR1* mutations were identified in 9/25 (36%) archival PCNSL tumor biopsies. Patients with *TBL1XR1* mutations had a median PFS of 16.5 months (95% CI: 2.5–40.0) vs. 3.1 months (95% CI: 2.5–4.5) for *TBL1XR1*-wild-type patients (log-rank p=0.0075; HR=0.29, 95% CI: 0.11–0.76). Crucially, *TBL1XR1* mutations were not associated with improved OS in a reference cohort of 177 PCNSL patients treated with standard-of-care regimens, suggesting a predictive rather than prognostic role. All mutations mapped to WD40 domains, consistent with loss-of-function.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TBL1XR1` · Primary CNS lymphoma (DLBCL)

### F3: CD79B and MYD88 mutations are common and MYD88 associates with longer PFS

**Finding:** BCR/TLR pathway mutations are frequent in this PCNSL cohort, and *MYD88* mutation associates with longer ibrutinib PFS.

**Detail:** Mutation frequencies in archival PCNSL biopsies: *MYD88* 72%, *CD79B* 48%, *CARD11* 24%, *TBL1XR1* 36%. These rates are comparable to a larger MSK PCNSL reference cohort (n=177), suggesting representativeness. *MYD88* mutation was associated with longer median PFS (9.2 months vs. 2.9 months; log-rank p=0.027, HR=0.39). Conversely, *CARD11* mutations (present in 6 patients) were associated with shorter PFS (2.2 vs. 5.5 months), consistent with ibrutinib resistance in systemic DLBCL. 84% of PCNSLs in the trial were non-germinal center (ABC) subtype by Hans classification.

**Genes / cancer types:** `MYD88`, `CD79B`, `CARD11` · Primary CNS lymphoma (DLBCL)

**Data availability:** available

**Tool support:** native

### F4: CSF ctDNA clearance correlates with complete response and durable remission

**Finding:** Clearance of circulating tumor DNA from CSF within 4 weeks of ibrutinib initiation is associated with imaging complete response and longer progression-free survival.

**Detail:** Paired pre- and on-treatment CSF samples were collected from 14 patients. Complete disappearance of CSF ctDNA within 4 weeks occurred in 8/14 (57%) patients. All patients with a complete response on imaging had undetectable CSF ctDNA after treatment. In contrast, 67% of non-complete responders still had detectable ctDNA. In patients with PFS ≥6 months, the median reduction in ctDNA alterations between CSF1 and CSF2 was 100% (range 95.1–100) vs. 30.8% (range 0–100) in those with PFS <6 months (p=0.018).

**Genes / cancer types:** — · Primary CNS lymphoma, Secondary CNS lymphoma

### F5: Ibrutinib is detectable in CSF and CNS penetration is dose-dependent

**Finding:** Ibrutinib achieves CNS penetration with higher CSF concentrations at the 840 mg dose versus 560 mg.

**Detail:** CSF ibrutinib concentrations 2 hours post-dose were 1.386 ng/mL (3 nM) at 560 mg (n=3) and 2.695 ng/mL (6 nM) at 840 mg (n=27). Higher plasma concentrations correlated with higher CSF concentrations. No significant difference in ibrutinib CSF concentration was observed between PCNSL and SCNSL patients, and CSF drug levels did not significantly differ between long-term responders and non-responders.

**Genes / cancer types:** — · Primary CNS lymphoma, Secondary CNS lymphoma

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Can TBL1XR1 mutation status be used prospectively to select PCNSL patients for ibrutinib monotherapy versus combination regimens?
- Does the DLBCL "cluster 5" molecular subtype (enriched for TBL1XR1 + MYD88 mutations) overlap with ibrutinib long-term responders in the pcnsl_msk_2024 dataset?
- Could CSF ctDNA quantification replace MRI for early response assessment in PCNSL clinical trials?
- Can CARD11 mutation predict ibrutinib resistance prospectively and guide early switch to alternative BTK inhibitors?
- How do second-generation BTK inhibitors (e.g., zanubrutinib) compare to ibrutinib in TBL1XR1-mutant PCNSL?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Small, single-center trial (n=46) with limited statistical power for biomarker analyses; TBL1XR1 findings are exploratory and require prospective validation.
- Non-randomized design; no comparator arm for ibrutinib vs. standard of care.
- Archival tumor tissue available in only 25/31 PCNSL patients, limiting biomarker analysis.
- CSF ctDNA analysis limited to 14 patients with paired samples.
- Long follow-up (median >4 years) introduces temporal heterogeneity in treatment landscape.
- Fungal infection risk in heavily pretreated patients on corticosteroids warrants prophylaxis consideration.
