## Summary

Comprehensive WES plus structural variant (SV) analysis of 304 primary DLBCLs identifies 98 candidate cancer genes and discovers five robust genetic clusters (C1–C5) via NMF consensus clustering of mutations, SCNAs, and SVs. Three of the five clusters were previously undescribed. The clusters capture distinct pathogenic mechanisms — two ABC-DLBCL subtypes with different NF-κB activation routes (C1: extrafollicular/MZ-like; C5: GC-experienced), two GCB subtypes (C3: BCL2/EZH2-driven; C4: histone/BRAF-driven), and an ABC/GCB-independent group with biallelic TP53 loss and genomic instability (C2). Cluster membership predicts PFS/OS independently of IPI. Data are available in cBioPortal as `dlbcl_dfci_2018`.

## Key Findings

### F1: NMF clustering of integrated genetic drivers identifies five biologically and clinically distinct DLBCL subtypes

**Finding:** Non-negative matrix factorization applied to 158 recurrent genetic driver alterations (mutations, SCNAs, SVs) partitions 304 DLBCLs into five clusters (C1–C5, 51–72 tumors each) plus a small driver-negative group (C0, n=12). Cluster membership predicts PFS independently of the International Prognostic Index (IPI) in 254 R-CHOP-treated patients.

**Detail:** C3 and C5 have inferior PFS; C1, C4, and C0 have favorable PFS. Combining unfavorable (C3+C5), intermediate (C2), and favorable (C0+C1+C4) groups produces highly significant survival separation (log-rank). Multivariate analysis shows clusters retain significance after IPI adjustment. MYC SVs and 18q21.33/BCL2 gain are additional cross-cluster adverse prognostic features.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53`, `MYD88`, `CD79B`, `BCL2`, `BCL6`, `MYC`, `CREBBP`, `KMT2D`, `EZH2`, `RHOA`, `BRAF`, `NOTCH2`, `PTEN`, `CDKN2A` · Diffuse large B-cell lymphoma

### F2: C5 ABC-DLBCLs originate from GC-experienced B cells and are driven by MYD88-L265P/CD79B co-mutations with 18q/BCL2 gain

**Finding:** C5 (n=64) represents high-risk ABC-DLBCLs with near-uniform 18q gain (BCL2/MALT1), MYD88-L265P (50%), and CD79B mutations (48%), high aberrant somatic hypermutation (cAID signature), and extranodal tropism — 8/9 testicular DLBCL cases fall in C5.

**Detail:** MYD88-L265P and CD79B co-mutations (present in ~35% of C5) strongly activate the BCR-NF-κB signaling axis and are previously described in primary CNS and testicular lymphoma. High cAID activity confirms a GC-passaged origin. Rational treatment strategy: proximal BCR/TLR signaling inhibition (ibrutinib-class) plus BCL2 antagonism.

### F3: C1 ABC-DLBCLs arise from an extrafollicular/marginal-zone precursor with NOTCH2 and BCL6 alterations — a favorable, previously unrecognised subtype

**Finding:** C1 (n=56) DLBCLs harbor BCL6 SVs plus NOTCH2 activating mutations (PEST domain), SPEN loss, BCL10/TNFAIP3 mutations, and FAS inactivation — features shared with marginal-zone lymphoma (MZL). These tumors have absent cAID activity (extrafollicular origin) and favorable PFS despite ABC COO classification.

**Detail:** C1 and C5 are both ABC-type by COO but are pathogenetically distinct: C1 MYD88 mutations are predominantly non-L265P (unable to activate IRAK1/IRAK4 complex), and cAID is absent. Multiple immune-escape mechanisms (B2M, CD70, FAS, PD-L1/PD-L2 SVs) suggest immunotherapy vulnerability. Treatment rationale: NOTCH/BCL6 inhibition plus immune-checkpoint blockade.

**Genes / cancer types:** `BCL6`, `NOTCH2`, `SPEN`, `BCL10`, `TNFAIP3`, `FAS`, `B2M`, `CD70`, `MYD88` · ABC-DLBCL (extrafollicular)

### F4: C3 GCB-DLBCLs are driven by BCL2 rearrangement plus chromatin-modifier mutations (EZH2, CREBBP, KMT2D) and have high rates of double-hit lymphoma

**Finding:** C3 (n=55) DLBCLs are uniformly GCB-type with BCL2 IgH SVs, BCL2 mutations, chromatin modifier mutations (KMT2D, CREBBP, EZH2), PTEN loss/mutation (two independent mechanisms), and MEF2B/GNA13 alterations. Tumors with co-occurring BCL2 and MYC SVs ("double-hit") are significantly enriched in C3 (P=0.003).

**Detail:** PTEN inactivation occurs via focal 10q23.31 loss or predominantly truncating mutations — the two mechanisms differ in their effects on the N-terminal (PI3K antagonism) and C-terminal (genomic stability) PTEN domains. Rational therapy: BCL2 inhibition (venetoclax), PI3K inhibitors, EZH2 inhibitors (in EZH2-mutant cases), CREBBP-targeted strategies.

**Genes / cancer types:** `BCL2`, `EZH2`, `CREBBP`, `KMT2D`, `PTEN`, `MEF2B`, `GNA13`, `MYC`, `IRF8` · GCB-DLBCL

### F5: C2 DLBCLs are ABC/GCB-independent and defined by biallelic TP53 loss with CDKN2A/RB1 co-deletion and genomic instability

**Finding:** C2 (n=64) DLBCLs exhibit biallelic TP53 inactivation (TP53 mutations + 17p loss), CDKN2A deletion (9p21.13), RB1 loss (13q14.2), genome doubling, and the highest SCNA burden of all clusters. These tumors span ABC and GCB COO and have inferior outcomes.

**Detail:** Transcriptional profiling shows decreased TP53 targets and increased E2F targets (consistent with RB1 loss). Prognostically significant alterations (13q31.3/miR-17-92 gain, 1q42.12 loss) are enriched in C2. The absence of a clear COO affiliation and the predominance of structural alterations over point mutations define a biology driven by genomic catastrophe rather than lineage-specific signaling.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53`, `CDKN2A`, `RB1`, `MCL1` · DLBCL (ABC and GCB)

### F6: Three mutational processes shape DLBCL genomes — aging (CpG deamination), canonical AID (cAID), and a secondary AID signature (AID2)

**Finding:** SignatureAnalyzer applied to 303 DLBCLs identifies three mutational signatures: aging (C>T at CpG; 80% of mutations), cAID (RCY motif; enriched at BCL2, SGK1, PIM1, IGLL5), and AID2 (WA motif; non-canonical AID). cAID activity is highest in C5 and lowest in C1, reflecting GC transit history.

**Detail:** AID-driven mutations cluster within 2 kb of transcription start sites and show low non-synonymous:synonymous ratios, hallmarks of aberrant somatic hypermutation. In contrast, genes like MYD88, KMT2D, TP53 are predominantly mutated by the aging signature. The three-signature model provides evidence for distinct oncogenic mechanisms across clusters. SignatureAnalyzer tool developed in this study.

**Genes / cancer types:** `MYD88`, `KMT2D`, `TP53`, `BCL2`, `PIM1`, `SGK1`, `IGLL5` · DLBCL

**Data availability:** `available`

## Open Questions

1. Do the five genetic clusters remain stable across independent large cohorts, and do they map onto recently described LymphGen/cluster classifications from other groups?
2. Can cluster assignment be achieved from limited tissue (e.g., liquid biopsy ctDNA or targeted panel) for routine clinical use?
3. What are the optimal targeted therapies for C1 (NOTCH2-driven) and C4 (BRAF/histone-driven) clusters, for which no approved agents exist?
4. Do C1 low-risk ABC-DLBCLs benefit less from R-CHOP intensification and more from novel combinations?
5. How do microenvironmental features (TIL density, PD-L1 expression) interact with genetic clusters to influence outcome?

## Limitations

- 55% of samples lacked patient-matched normals, requiring novel tumor-only filtering — residual germline variants may increase background noise despite robust validation.
- WES excludes non-coding driver alterations outside the bait-set; promoter mutations (e.g., TERT) not captured.
- COO classification performed on available subset; cluster-COO relationships may be influenced by classification algorithm choice.
- Functional validation of the extrafollicular/MZ cell-of-origin for C1 is inferential (genetic similarity to MZL) rather than demonstrated experimentally.
- Outcome data restricted to R-CHOP-treated patients (n=259); applicability to other frontline regimens unknown.
