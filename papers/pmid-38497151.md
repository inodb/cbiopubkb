## Summary
This study validated the LymphGen DLBCL classifier — originally developed for WGS/WES — on MSK-IMPACT HEME, a 400-gene clinical NGS panel, achieving 92% overall accuracy (86% sensitivity, 98% specificity) against the NCI WGS ground truth. Applied to 396 MSK DLBCL patients, 55% were successfully classified into one of six molecular subtypes. The work demonstrates that research-grade genomic DLBCL classification can be routinely implemented in clinical care using well-designed targeted panels.

## Dataset
- **Cohort**: (1) NCI DLBCL cohort (filtered to IMPACT genes for validation); (2) MSK DLBCL clinical cohort
- **Assay**: MSK-IMPACT HEME (hybridization capture, 400-gene panel); SNV, INDEL, CNA; FISH for BCL2/BCL6
- **N**: 396 MSK DLBCL cases (320 with BCL2/BCL6 cytogenetics); NCI cohort used for accuracy benchmarking
- **cBioPortal study ID(s)**: mbn_msk_2024

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: LymphGen achieves high accuracy on a 400-gene clinical panel
- **Detail**: Filtering NCI cohort data to the 400 IMPACT genes yielded 92% overall classification accuracy, 86% sensitivity, 98% specificity vs. comprehensive WGS/WES ground truth. High-confidence "Core" calls (>90% probability) reached 96% accuracy.
- **Genes**: MYD88, BCL6, EZH2, TP53, BCL2, NOTCH2
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: api
- **Concepts**: mutation, copy_number, clinical

### Finding 2: 55% classification rate in the MSK clinical cohort
- **Detail**: 55% of 396 MSK DLBCL cases were assigned to a subtype (vs. 58% on NCI data filtered to IMPACT, vs. 63% with comprehensive methods in original NCI report). Subtype breakdown: EZB 22%, BN2 10%, MCD 10%, ST2 7%, N1 3%, A53 2%, composite 2%. ~37% of all cases fell in the high-confidence "Core" group (>90% probability).
- **Genes**: EZH2, NOTCH2, MYD88, TP53, BCL2
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: mutation, copy_number, clinical

### Finding 3: BCL2 and BCL6 translocations are robust subtype classifiers
- **Detail**: BCL2 translocations enriched in EZB (81%; 54/67 cases); BCL6 translocations enriched in BN2 (77%; 24/31 cases). Only 2 BCL2-translocation cases fell in the unclassified group, confirming BCL2 translocation as a robust EZB marker. MYC translocations co-occurred with BCL6 in 15.3% of BN2 cases.
- **Genes**: BCL2, BCL6, MYC
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: mutation, fusion, copy_number, clinical

### Finding 4: CNA data is essential for A53 subtype and overall accuracy
- **Detail**: Running LymphGen with only SNV/INDEL + FISH (no CNA) dropped balanced accuracy to 81% (sensitivity 83%, specificity 89%) and rendered the A53 subtype unclassifiable. 4.7% of cases were misclassified to "Other" when CNA was omitted.
- **Genes**: TP53
- **Data availability**: partial
- **Tool support**: api
- **Concepts**: copy_number, clinical

### Finding 5: Immunophenotype aligns with genetic subtypes
- **Detail**: CD10, CD23, LMO2 expression enriched in EZB and ST2 (GCB phenotype); MUM1 enriched in BN2, MCD, N1, A53 (non-GCB phenotype). 50% of N1 cases expressed CD5 and LEF1, suggesting a subset may represent Richter's transformation of CLL.
- **Genes**: CD5, LEF1
- **Data availability**: partial
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: clinical, mutation

### Finding 6: Survival trends are consistent but not statistically significant
- **Detail**: Survival analyses in the MSK cohort showed trends consistent with prior studies (e.g., worse outcomes in MCD/A53), but did not reach statistical significance, attributed to cohort heterogeneity and relatively short follow-up.
- **Genes**: MYD88, TP53
- **Data availability**: partial
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: survival, clinical

### Finding 7: >99% sequencing success rate in clinical practice
- **Detail**: Over 99% of DLBCL cases at MSK were successfully sequenced with IMPACT HEME due to rigorous pre-analytical quality checks; the authors note that success rates may differ at other institutions.
- **Genes**: (none specific)
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: clinical

## Open Questions
- Do LymphGen subtype-specific survival differences reach significance with longer follow-up or a larger merged cohort (e.g., combining mbn_msk_2024 with other DLBCL studies in cBioPortal)?
- Can co-mutation patterns within each LymphGen subtype (e.g., secondary hits in EZB or MCD) be identified using cBioPortal mutual exclusivity and co-occurrence tools to refine prognostic grouping?
- What is the treatment response and outcome by LymphGen subtype in patients treated with novel agents (CAR-T, BTK inhibitors) present in this cohort?

## Limitations
- Short follow-up duration in the MSK cohort limits statistical power for survival analyses by subtype.
- A53 subtype classification requires CNA; poor sample quality or low tumor content in clinical specimens can compromise CNA calling accuracy.
- Classification rate (55%) leaves 45% of patients unclassified ("Other"), reducing clinical utility for nearly half the cohort.
- Study is a single-center experience; generalizability to other IMPACT workflows or different targeted panels is not guaranteed.
- RNA-seq data (used in the original NCI LymphGen) is not available in this clinical pipeline, potentially reducing sensitivity for certain subtype features.
