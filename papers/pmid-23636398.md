## Summary

Landmark TCGA multi-platform analysis of 373 endometrial carcinomas (307 endometrioid, 66 serous/mixed) using WES (n=248), low-pass WGS (n=106), SNP 6.0 CNA arrays (n=363), RNA-seq, miRNA, RPPA, and DNA methylation. Key finding: endometrial cancers are reclassified into four molecular subtypes—POLE ultramutated, microsatellite instability (MSI) hypermutated, copy-number low, and copy-number high—that cut across traditional histological categories and have distinct prognoses. POLE-mutant tumors carry the highest mutation burdens observed in any cancer and show improved progression-free survival. ~25% of grade 3 endometrioid tumors cluster with serous carcinomas by molecular features, suggesting misclassification by histology alone. Uterine serous carcinomas share molecular features with basal-like breast cancer and high-grade serous ovarian cancer. PI3K/AKT pathway alterations affect 93% of endometrioid tumors. Full data available in `ucec_tcga_pub` on cBioPortal.

## Key Findings

### F1: Four molecular subtypes replace histological classification — POLE, MSI, CN-low, CN-high

**Finding:** Integrated multi-platform analysis defines four endometrial cancer subtypes: (1) POLE ultramutated (7%); (2) MSI hypermutated (28%); (3) copy-number low, MSS endometrioid (39%); (4) copy-number high, serous-like (26%). These subtypes differ in mutation rate, copy-number burden, MSI status, hormonal receptor levels, and progression-free survival, making them more clinically informative than histological grade or type alone.

**Detail:** POLE-mutant tumors have the highest mutation rates ever observed (232 × 10⁻⁶/Mb), driven by C>A transversions in the exonuclease domain; hotspot mutations at P286R and V411L account for 76% of POLE-mutant cases. MSI tumors have ~18 × 10⁻⁶/Mb (10× above MSS endometrioid), predominantly via MLH1 promoter methylation. CN-low tumors have 2.9 × 10⁻⁶/Mb; CN-high tumors have 2.3 × 10⁻⁶/Mb but extensive SCNAs. POLE tumors and CN-low tumors have the best prognosis; CN-high has the worst (log-rank P=0.003). ~25% of grade 3 endometrioid tumors cluster with CN-high serous, suggesting these patients may benefit from chemotherapy rather than adjuvant radiotherapy.

**Genes / cancer types:** POLE, MLH1, TP53, PTEN · Endometrial carcinoma

### F2: PI3K/AKT pathway alterations in 93% of endometrioid tumors — highest of any TCGA cancer type

**Finding:** PTEN (83%), PIK3CA (53%), PIK3R1 (33%), and AKT1/2 mutations co-occur at extraordinary rates in endometrioid tumors, making endometrial cancer the cancer type with the highest frequency of PI3K/AKT pathway mutations among all TCGA studies at the time. PTEN and PIK3CA/PIK3R1 mutations strongly co-occur in MSI and CN-low groups but are mutually exclusive in CN-high tumors.

**Detail:** PTEN mutations (84% of non-serous tumors, 11% of serous-like) are inactivating and correlate with reduced PTEN protein expression by RPPA. PIK3CA and PIK3R1 mutations show mutual exclusivity with each other but co-occur with PTEN alterations unlike other tumor types, suggesting distinct regulatory logic. ERBB2 is focally amplified with protein overexpression in 25% of serous/serous-like tumors. A small trial of trastuzumab in unselected endometrial tumors showed no activity, but few HER2 FISH-amplified serous cases were enrolled—this finding supports HER2-directed therapy in molecularly selected serous endometrial cancer.

**Genes / cancer types:** PTEN, PIK3CA, PIK3R1, AKT1, ERBB2 · Endometrial carcinoma

### F3: Uterine serous carcinomas are genomically similar to basal-like breast and HGSOC

**Finding:** Uterine serous carcinomas share key molecular features with high-grade serous ovarian carcinoma (HGSOC) and basal-like breast cancer: near-universal TP53 mutations (91% uterine serous vs. 96% HGSOC vs. 84% basal-like breast), extensive SCNAs, low PTEN mutation rates, and similar DNA methylation and transcriptomic profiles. These shared features suggest overlapping treatment paradigms.

**Detail:** MYC (8q24.12), ERBB2 (17q12), and CCNE1 (19q12) amplifications are recurrently shared across the three tumor types. CN-high endometrial tumors have higher frequencies of FBXW7 (22%), PPP2R1A (22%), and PIK3CA (42%) mutations than HGSOCs or basal-like breast—differences that may guide distinct therapeutic approaches. The MC3 DNA methylation subtype (minimal methylation changes) is common across all three tumor types. Transcriptome analysis identifies a "mitotic" expression cluster (85% of CN-high tumors) driven by CCNE1, PIK3CA, MYC overexpression and CDKN2A upregulation.

### F4: WNT/β-catenin pathway driven by mutually exclusive CTNNB1, KRAS, and SOX17 in CN-low tumors

**Finding:** In copy-number low (MSS endometrioid) tumors, CTNNB1 activating mutations (52%) are the most frequent alteration. CTNNB1, KRAS (mutually exclusive at 26%), and SOX17 (novel, 8%) show strong mutual exclusivity, suggesting they activate WNT signaling through distinct mechanisms converging on β-catenin stabilization. This is molecularly distinct from colorectal WNT activation (dominated by APC), despite similar clinical features.

**Detail:** SOX17 mutations at recurrent positions (A96G, S403I) are novel findings in endometrial cancer; SOX17 mediates proteasomal degradation of β-catenin. KRAS activation can increase β-catenin stability via GSK-3β inhibition, representing an APC-independent mechanism. FBXW7 and FGFR2 are also in the mutually exclusive WNT module. MSI endometrioid tumors have few CTNNB1 mutations despite sharing other molecular features with CN-low MSS tumors. In contrast to colorectal cancer, KRAS and CTNNB1 are mutually exclusive in endometrial cancer.

**Genes / cancer types:** CTNNB1, KRAS, SOX17, FBXW7, FGFR2, APC · Endometrial carcinoma

### F5: Recurrent BCL-family translocations and novel focal SCNAs identify therapeutic opportunities

**Finding:** Low-pass WGS on 106 tumors identifies recurrent translocations in BCL family members (BCL2, BCL7A, BCL9, BCL2L11) as the most frequent structural events (5/106 cases), predicted to result in activation or increased expression of anti-apoptotic factors. Novel focal SCNAs include FGFR3 (4p16.3), SOX17 (8q11.23), and IGF1R (15q26.2) amplifications; LRP1B (resistance to liposomal doxorubicin) and SMAD4 deletions.

**Detail:** RPPA supervised clustering shows TP53 mutation associated with increased p53 protein (likely stabilizing missense mutations), whereas PTEN and ARID1A mutations correlate with decreased protein expression (inactivating). Increased RAD50 expression in CN-low tumors suggests differential DNA repair activity. ASNS and CCNB1 are overexpressed in POLE tumors; phospho-AKT is elevated in MSI tumors. The ARID5B gene (same ARID family as ARID1A) is more frequently mutated in MSI (23%) than in MSS (5.6%) or serous tumors (0%)—a novel finding. RPL22 frameshift indels near Lys15 (36.9%) are almost exclusive to MSI tumors.

**Genes / cancer types:** BCL2, ARID1A, ARID5B, RPL22, FGFR3, IGF1R, LRP1B · Endometrial carcinoma

## Open Questions

- Does the POLE ultramutated subtype predict response to immune checkpoint inhibitors (PD-1/PD-L1) given its extremely high TMB, and can POLE mutation status replace MSI as a predictive biomarker?
- Will treating CN-high endometrioid tumors (molecularly serous-like) with platinum-based chemotherapy rather than radiotherapy improve outcomes in prospective trials?
- What is the therapeutic utility of ERBB2 amplification (~25% of serous tumors) — can molecularly selected trastuzumab trials demonstrate benefit in endometrial serous cancer?
- Do BCL-family translocations confer sensitivity to BCL2 inhibitors (e.g., venetoclax) in the subset of endometrial tumors carrying these events?
- How does the POLE mutational spectrum differ between endometrial and colorectal POLE ultramutated tumors, and are the downstream immune microenvironments similarly activated?

## Limitations

- Median follow-up of 32 months (range 1–195) limits survival analysis; only 21% of patients had recurred and 11% had died at time of analysis
- Low-pass WGS (6× depth) on a subset (n=106) gives incomplete structural variant detection; BCL translocation breakpoints confirmed in only 4 of 5 cases
- mRNA and miRNA profiling used multiple platforms across samples; integration of array and RNA-seq data introduces technical heterogeneity
- RPPA covers only ~200 antibodies; proteome coverage is limited compared to LC-MS/MS-based approaches
- Histological subtype classification of mixed/borderline tumors (13 cases) may introduce misclassification; the reclassification of one serous case as grade 3 endometrioid illustrates this challenge
- POLE hotspot focus (P286R, V411L) may undercount broader exonuclease domain mutations as drivers
