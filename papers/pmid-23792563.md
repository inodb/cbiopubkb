## Summary

TCGA characterized 446 clear cell renal cell carcinoma (ccRCC) samples using WES (417), SNP copy number arrays, RNA-seq, miRNA-seq, DNA methylation arrays, and reverse-phase protein arrays (RPPA). Nineteen significantly mutated genes were identified, with VHL, PBRM1, SETD2, KDM5C, PTEN, BAP1, MTOR, and TP53 among the most extreme. Chromosome 3p loss (91% of tumors) encompasses all four major mutated genes. Key findings: (1) chromatin-modifier mutations (PBRM1, BAP1, SETD2) dominate — beyond VHL, ccRCC is fundamentally an epigenetic disease; (2) SETD2 loss drives genome-wide DNA hypomethylation at non-promoter regions; (3) PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway is mutually exclusively altered in 28%; (4) four mRNA and miRNA expression subtypes with significant survival differences; (5) poor prognosis correlates with a metabolic shift toward the Warburg effect (pentose phosphate up, Krebs cycle down, fatty acid synthesis up). BAP1 mutation is the only single-gene predictor of poor survival. mTOR pathway inhibitors (everolimus, temsirolimus) already had FDA approval at the time, supporting therapeutic relevance.

## Key Findings

### F1: Chromosome 3p loss and four co-localized chromatin-modifier mutations define ccRCC biology

**Finding:** Arm-level loss of chromosome 3p occurs in 91% of ccRCC tumors and encompasses all four most commonly mutated genes: VHL, PBRM1, BAP1, and SETD2. A second hit (point mutation, small indel) on the remaining allele leads to bi-allelic inactivation. ccRCC has significantly fewer focal SCNAs than most cancers; when CNAs occur, they tend to be arm-level or chromosome-wide.

**Detail:** The 3p loss creates a haploinsufficiency background in which a single somatic event on the remaining allele can inactivate four major tumor suppressors (each on 3p). VHL epigenetic silencing (methylation) occurs in an additional ~7% of cases, mutually exclusive with VHL mutation. Arm-level gains of 5q (67%) and losses of 14q (45%, associated with HIF1A loss and more aggressive disease) are the other major arm events. Focal amplifications implicate PRKCI (3p26), MECOM (3p26), MDM4 (1q32), MYC (8q24), and JAK2 (9p24).

**Genes / cancer types:** `VHL`, `PBRM1`, `BAP1`, `SETD2`, `HIF1A`, `MYC`, `MDM4`, `JAK2` · ccRCC

### F2: SETD2 mutation drives genome-wide DNA hypomethylation at non-promoter regions via H3K36me3 loss

**Finding:** SETD2-mutant tumors show significantly increased loss of DNA methylation at non-promoter regions (H3K36-marked heterochromatin), consistent with the emerging model that H3K36me3 recruits DNMT3A to maintain DNA methylation. This links a histone methyltransferase mutation directly to a DNA methylation phenotype.

**Detail:** SETD2 is a non-redundant H3K36 trimethyltransferase. Its loss depletes H3K36me3, which normally recruits DNMT3A; the result is broad regional loss of DNA methylation (hypomethylation) genome-wide but particularly at non-promoter, H3K36me3-enriched regions. This is mechanistically distinct from the promoter hypermethylation seen in other cancers. Promoter hypermethylation frequency correlated positively with higher tumor stage and grade. An additional 289 genes beyond VHL showed epigenetic silencing in ≥5% of tumors; UQCRH was most commonly silenced (36%), suggesting a novel tumor-suppressor role.

**Genes / cancer types:** `SETD2`, `VHL`, `DNMT3A` (mechanistic) · ccRCC

### F3: PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway mutually exclusively altered in 28% of tumors — the key therapeutic target

**Finding:** MEMo analysis identified mutually exclusive alteration modules in the PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway in 28% of ccRCC, including mutations in PTEN, PIK3CA, AKT1, MTOR, TSC1, TSC2 and amplification of genes from the 5q35.3 amplicon (GNB2L1/RACK1 and SQSTM1/p62) that activate PI3K signaling.

**Detail:** MTOR mutation was among the eight most significantly mutated genes (q<0.00001). mTOR pathway inhibitors (everolimus, temsirolimus) were already clinically approved for ccRCC, validating the pathway as a therapeutic target. The MEMo-identified mutual exclusivity module includes overexpression of EGFR (correlating with receptor phosphorylation and lapatinib response), adding to the actionable set. The HotNet subnetwork analysis additionally identified VHL-interacting partners (most frequent subnetwork) and the PBRM1-ARID1A-SMARCA4 SWI/SNF subnetwork as the second most frequently mutated.

**Genes / cancer types:** `MTOR`, `PIK3CA`, `PTEN`, `AKT1`, `TSC1`, `TSC2`, `EGFR` · ccRCC

### F4: Four mRNA and miRNA expression subtypes with distinct survival, genomic, and metabolic correlates

**Finding:** Unsupervised clustering identified four mRNA clusters (m1–m4) and four miRNA clusters (mi1–mi4) with significant survival differences. m1 (corresponding to good-prognosis ccA) is enriched for PBRM1 mutations and chromatin remodeling gene sets. m3 has most CDKN2A deletion and PTEN mutations. m4 has highest BAP1 mutation rate and mTOR mutations. BAP1 mutation is the single gene most strongly associated with poor survival.

**Detail:** m1 vs. m3 survival difference is large and significant. miRNA-based clusters (mi1–mi4) show strong concordance with mRNA clusters. miR-21 (associated with worse outcome, high glucose-inducible, down-regulates PTEN) and GRB10 (associated with better outcome, PI3K negative regulator) are key miRNA/methylation correlates of survival. Platform-specific survival signatures (mRNA, miRNA, methylation, protein) each independently predict outcome in validation subsets. BAP1 mutation, alone among the 19 SMGs, significantly correlates with poor survival.

**Genes / cancer types:** `BAP1`, `PBRM1`, `CDKN2A`, `PTEN`, `MTOR` · ccRCC

### F5: Metabolic shift toward Warburg phenotype correlates with aggressive ccRCC and poor prognosis

**Finding:** Aggressive (poor-prognosis) ccRCC shows coordinated down-regulation of Krebs cycle and AMPK complex genes and up-regulation of pentose phosphate pathway genes (G6PD, PGLS, TALDO, TKT) and fatty acid synthesis genes (FASN, ACC). This metabolic shift mirrors the Warburg effect and implicates glutamine-dependent lipogenesis in ccRCC progression.

**Detail:** Reduced AMPK protein (RPPA) and elevated ACC (acetyl-CoA carboxylase) contribute to fatty acid synthesis promotion. Promoter methylation of miR-21 (lower methylation → higher expression → PTEN suppression → worse outcome) and GRB10 (negative PI3K regulator) are epigenetic drivers of the PI3K/metabolic pathway activity. The overall signature parallels the metabolic phenotype of type 2 papillary RCC (FH mutation) and VHL-null ccRCC cell lines showing reductive glutamine metabolism for lipid biosynthesis. These findings make metabolic enzymes and PI3K pathway targets with dual metabolic/signaling roles (AMPK, FASN) candidates for therapeutic intervention.

**Genes / cancer types:** `VHL`, `HIF1A`, `G6PD`, `FASN`, `AMPK`, `PTEN`, `GRB10`, `miR-21` · ccRCC

## Open Questions

- How do the three chromatin-modifier mutations (PBRM1, BAP1, SETD2) each differentially remodel the transcriptome — and are any targetable?
- Do MTOR-mutant ccRCCs have superior responses to mTOR inhibitors (everolimus/temsirolimus) vs. VHL-only mutant tumors?
- What are the mechanisms by which the metabolic shift toward the Warburg phenotype is initiated — is VHL/HIF sufficient or do chromatin modifier mutations contribute?
- Does tumor heterogeneity (high regional variation reported in ccRCC) confound therapeutic targeting of subclonal PBRM1/BAP1/SETD2 mutations?
- Can the multi-platform survival signatures (mRNA, miRNA, protein, methylation) be clinically implemented for treatment stratification?

## Limitations

- Single-site biopsy study in a disease with known high intratumoral heterogeneity (subclonal PBRM1/BAP1/SETD2 mutations reported).
- Computational tumor purity estimates (median 54%) lower than pathological assessment (85%), suggesting stromal/endothelial contamination or tumor heterogeneity.
- Only 22 WGS cases; structural variants and rearrangements not characterized at scale.
- Primary tumor specimens only; no metastatic or treatment-resistant samples.
- Metabolic findings are transcriptomic/proteomic correlates — no direct metabolite measurements in tumors.
