## Summary

This multinational study performed targeted DNA sequencing of 943 localized clear-cell renal cell carcinoma (ccRCC) tumor-normal pairs, divided into Discovery (n=469) and Validation (n=474) cohorts, to evaluate whether genomic data can stratify recurrence risk beyond TNM stage and grade. Using a 12-gene classifier based on co-occurring driver mutations alongside VHL status, the study identified four genomically defined patient groups with markedly divergent 5-year disease-free survival (DFS) rates. The genomic classifier stratifies ~80% of ccRCC patients (those with VHL mutations) into groups that could guide adjuvant therapy selection — sparing low-risk patients from treatment while prioritizing high-risk patients.

## Key Findings

### F1: VHL mutation burden defines four genomic risk groups with divergent DFS
**Finding:** Patients were classified into four groups based on VHL mutation status plus the number of co-occurring driver mutations: VHL+0, VHL+1, VHL+2, and VHL+≥3. These groups had 5-year DFS rates of 90.8%, 80.1%, 68.2%, and 50.7%, respectively, in the Discovery cohort, replicated in the Validation cohort.
**Detail:** VHL mutations occur in ~80% of sporadic ccRCC. The prognostic stratification is driven by co-mutations in PBRM1 (~40%), SETD2 (~20%), BAP1 (~15%), and other chromatin-remodeling genes. Each additional co-driver beyond VHL substantially worsens outcome.
**Genes / cancer types:** VHL, PBRM1, SETD2, BAP1; clear-cell RCC.
**Data availability:** available > ccrcc_sjuh_2023 in cBioPortal includes mutation data and clinical outcomes.
**Tool support:** native > OncoPrint and Survival tabs in ccrcc_sjuh_2023; co-mutation analysis in Study View.
**Concepts:** #survival #biomarker #molecular-subtypes #classification #copy-number

### F2: VHL+0 patients (29%) may be spared adjuvant therapy
**Finding:** Patients with VHL mutation alone (VHL+0), comprising 29% of the VHL-mutant cohort (eligible for adjuvant therapy), had a 5-year DFS of 79.3% and represent a low-risk group that could potentially be spared adjuvant immunotherapy.
**Detail:** Given the 68% placebo-arm DFS at 2 years in KEYNOTE-564 and the high cost and toxicity of ICI agents, the VHL+0 group defines patients for whom active surveillance may be appropriate. This reduces unnecessary treatment in nearly one-third of adjuvant-eligible patients.
**Genes / cancer types:** VHL; clear-cell RCC.
**Data availability:** available > ccrcc_sjuh_2023 genomic and clinical data in cBioPortal.
**Tool support:** native > Filter by VHL mutation status in Study View; Survival analysis by alteration.
**Concepts:** #survival #actionability #ICI-biomarker #targeted-therapy #biomarker

### F3: VHL+2 and VHL+≥3 patients (32%) are highest-risk and should be prioritized for adjuvant therapy
**Finding:** Patients with VHL plus ≥2 co-driver mutations (VHL+2 and VHL+≥3) together constitute 32% of adjuvant-eligible patients and have 5-year DFS rates of 45.6% and 35.3%, respectively — comparable in poor outlook — and should be prioritized for adjuvant treatment.
**Detail:** These high-risk genomic groups show recurrence rates exceeding 50–65% at 5 years, far above the overall ~20–30% relapse rate, justifying aggressive post-surgical management and adjuvant ICI consideration regardless of TNM stage.
**Genes / cancer types:** VHL, PBRM1, SETD2, BAP1; clear-cell RCC.
**Data availability:** available > ccrcc_sjuh_2023 in cBioPortal.
**Tool support:** native > OncoPrint filtering and Survival plots by co-mutation groups.
**Concepts:** #survival #biomarker #actionability #ICI-biomarker #targeted-therapy

### F4: Genomic risk groups are independent of tumor mutational burden and TNM stage/grade
**Finding:** The VHL+0/+1/+2/+≥3 classification provides prognostic information independent of TNM stage and grade (pT stage, Fuhrman grade) and independent of tumor mutational burden (TMB), establishing the 12-gene co-driver classifier as an orthogonal prognostic tool.
**Detail:** Multivariate analyses confirmed that the genomic group assignment remained statistically significant after adjusting for pT stage, grade, and patient age. TMB was not significantly associated with DFS in this localized ccRCC cohort, contrasting with its role in ICI response prediction in other cancers.
**Genes / cancer types:** VHL, PBRM1, SETD2, BAP1; clear-cell RCC.
**Data availability:** available > ccrcc_sjuh_2023 in cBioPortal; clinical data for stage/grade available.
**Tool support:** api > Multivariate analysis using cBioPortal clinical + genomic data; custom > Cox regression with genomic covariates.
**Concepts:** #survival #biomarker #classification #TMB #molecular-subtypes

### F5: The 12-gene classifier captures ~80% of ccRCC via VHL-mutation-based grouping
**Finding:** Because ~80% of sporadic ccRCC harbor VHL mutations, the classifier applies to the vast majority of patients; only VHL-wildtype cases (~20%) fall outside this stratification framework.
**Detail:** VHL wildtype cases were not further stratified by this classifier and represent a distinct biological subset (potentially TCEB1- or BAP1-driven) requiring separate genomic characterization for risk prediction.
**Genes / cancer types:** VHL, TCEB1, BAP1; clear-cell RCC.
**Data availability:** available > ccrcc_sjuh_2023 in cBioPortal.
**Tool support:** native > Filter by VHL WT vs. mutant in Study View; OncoPrint shows co-alteration landscape.

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Can the VHL+n classifier predict differential benefit from adjuvant pembrolizumab vs. placebo in KEYNOTE-564–type trials?
- How does the 12-gene classifier interact with DNA methylation-based RCC subtypes or transcriptomic classifications?
- Can multi-region sequencing clarify whether the additional co-drivers are clonal vs. subclonal, and how this affects prognosis?
- What is the optimal strategy for VHL-wildtype ccRCC (20% of cases) — a different genomic panel, transcriptomics, or immune markers?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Targeted sequencing (12-gene panel) may miss relevant co-drivers outside the panel.
- Cohort composed of patients from specific European sites, potentially limiting generalizability across ethnicities.
- Some patients received prior cytokine-based adjuvant therapies (pre-ICI era), which could confound DFS estimates.
- VHL-wildtype cases (~20%) are not addressed by this classifier.
- Prospective validation in a clinical trial setting is lacking; DFS used as surrogate for overall survival.
