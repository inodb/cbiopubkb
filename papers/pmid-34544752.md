## Summary

Mechanistic and translational study identifying INK4 tumor suppressor proteins (p15INK4B, p18INK4C) as drivers of CDK4/6 inhibitor (CDK4/6i) resistance in breast cancer. Surveying 1,366 metastatic tumors from 1,115 HR+/HER2− breast cancer patients by MSK-IMPACT, the study found that 17% harbored genetic alterations (FAT1 loss, PTEN loss, ARID1A loss) that converge on CDK6 upregulation. Mechanistically, high CDK6 induces INK4 protein expression, and the resulting CDK6–INK4 complex is shown by structural modeling, biochemical assays, and MST experiments to occlude CDK4/6i binding (−87.65% volume for abemaciclib) while weakly suppressing ATP binding, conferring a drug-resistant but kinase-active state. Bifunctional PROTAC degraders (BSJ-05-017, BSJ-03-096) that degrade both CDK4 and CDK6 overcome this resistance mechanism in vitro and in vivo.

## Key Findings

### F1: CDK6 upregulation via FAT1, PTEN, or ARID1A loss is a recurrent CDK4/6i resistance mechanism in 17% of metastatic HR+ breast cancers

**Finding:** Genomic survey of 1,366 metastatic HR+/HER2− breast cancer tumors found that 190 of 1,115 patients (17%) harbored at least one alteration in FAT1, PTEN, or ARID1A that promotes CDK6 upregulation and CDK4/6i resistance.

**Detail:** FAT1 loss activates YAP1, which upregulates CDK6 transcription via Hippo pathway suppression. PTEN loss activates AKT, which also drives CDK6 expression (suppressible by AKT inhibitor MK-2206). ARID1A loss de-represses YAP1, similarly upregulating CDK6. In PDX models, 7 of 8 high-CDK6 models were resistant to CDK4/6i, while low-CDK6 models were sensitive. These findings extend previous work identifying CDK6 upregulation as a convergent resistance mechanism, now attributable to multiple upstream genetic alterations. The PRISM cell line screen also identified CDKN2A (INK4A) overexpression and RB1 loss as top genomic correlates of palbociclib resistance.

**Genes / cancer types:** CDK6, FAT1, PTEN, ARID1A, YAP1, CDKN2A, RB1 · HR+/HER2− breast cancer

### F2: INK4 proteins bind CDK6 in resistant cells and occlude the CDK4/6i binding pocket while preserving ATP binding

**Finding:** In CDK4/6i-resistant, CDK6-high cells, INK4 proteins (p15INK4B, p18INK4C) specifically associate with CDK6 but not CDK4; this complex remains kinase-active (phosphorylates RB) but is profoundly resistant to ATP-competitive CDK4/6i.

**Detail:** Structural modeling showed that INK4 binding to CDK6 distorts the N-lobe, decreasing the effective binding pocket volume for abemaciclib by −87.65% and palbociclib by −85.03%, while minimally affecting the ATP analogue AMP-PNP binding volume (+0.54%). MST assays confirmed a fourfold increase in the Kd of abemaciclib for CDK6 in the presence of p18INK4C. The CDK6–cyclin D3–p18 ternary complex retained partial kinase activity in biochemical assays despite near-complete drug insensitivity. Disruption of the CDK6–INK4 interface using CDK6 mutants V16D or R31C (which preserve kinase activity but abolish INK4 binding) restored abemaciclib sensitivity, confirming that the CDK6–INK4 interaction is mechanistically necessary for resistance.

### F3: Knocking out INK4 or disrupting the CDK6–INK4 interface restores CDK4/6i sensitivity in resistant cells

**Finding:** Genetic knockout of CDKN2B (p15) and CDKN2C (p18) in CDK6-high, CDK4/6i-resistant cells partially (p18 alone) or near-completely (double knockout) restored RB phosphorylation suppression by abemaciclib and reversed long-term growth resistance.

**Detail:** In CDK6-high MCF7-FAT1CR cells, double knockout of p15INK4B and p18INK4C restored abemaciclib sensitivity to levels comparable to parental (CDK6-low) cells in both short-term RB phosphorylation assays and 14/21-day growth assays. Conversely, overexpression of p16INK4A in T47D cells reduced the potency of both abemaciclib and palbociclib. These findings were validated across multiple ER+ breast cancer cell lines (T47D, ZR-75-1, CAMA1, EFM19). Cells expressing interface-disrupting CDK6 mutants (R31C, V16D) were sensitized to CDK4/6i, whereas kinase-dead CDK6 (K43M, D163N) mutants — which also lack INK4 interaction — similarly restored sensitivity. Overexpression of wild-type CDK6 did not accumulate cells in G1 upon CDK4/6i treatment, whereas CDK6 mutants defective in INK4 binding did.

**Genes / cancer types:** CDK6, CDKN2B (p15INK4B), CDKN2C (p18INK4C), RB1 · HR+ breast cancer (cell lines)

### F4: Bifunctional CDK4/6 PROTAC degraders overcome INK4-mediated resistance in vitro and in vivo

**Finding:** Novel PROTAC degraders (BSJ-05-017 linking palbociclib to VHL E3 ligand; BSJ-03-096 linking palbociclib to CRBN E3 ligand) potently degraded both CDK4 and CDK6 at 10 nM and suppressed proliferation, cell-cycle progression, and senescence equally in CDK4/6i-sensitive and -resistant breast cancer cells.

**Detail:** The selective CDK6 degrader BSJ-03-123 (CRBN-linked) degraded CDK6 dose-dependently but failed to suppress long-term growth due to preserved CDK4 activity. BSJ-05-017 and BSJ-03-096 degraded both kinases, abolishing RB phosphorylation and E2F1–cyclin A2 expression in both sensitive and resistant cells. Molecular dynamics modeling showed that E3 ligase adapters adopt stable conformations despite INK4 occupancy, explaining why degraders bypass the structural barrier that blocks ATP-competitive inhibitors. Diastereomeric reversal of the VHL ligand chiral centers abolished degradation activity, confirming on-target PROTAC mechanism. BSJ-05-017 demonstrated equipotent antiproliferative activity and senescence induction across resistant and sensitive models.

### F5: Multiple genetic pathways converge on CDK6–INK4 complex formation to drive resistance without CDK4/6 kinase domain mutations

**Finding:** Despite thousands of patient tumors sequenced, not a single point mutation in CDK4 or CDK6 kinase domains has been identified as a CDK4/6i resistance mechanism; instead, CDK6 upregulation via multiple converging genetic alterations (FAT1/PTEN/ARID1A loss) combined with INK4 protein binding constitutes a structurally distinct resistance mechanism.

**Detail:** This contrasts sharply with resistance mechanisms for EGFR (T790M) and BCR-ABL (T315I) inhibitors. The CDK6–INK4 complex resists ATP-competitive inhibitors via an allosteric conformational change that reduces the drug-binding pocket without impairing catalytic activity. This structural mechanism predicts that all current-generation CDK4/6i (palbociclib, ribociclib, abemaciclib) will be similarly affected, as p18INK4C binding reduced binding pocket volume for both abemaciclib (−87.65%) and palbociclib (−85.03%). The convergence of FAT1 loss, PTEN loss, and ARID1A loss on CDK6 upregulation suggests these alterations should be considered resistance biomarkers in clinical trials of CDK4/6i combinations.

## Open Questions

- Can CDK6 protein expression by IHC or CDK6 gene copy number by sequencing serve as a prospective biomarker for CDK4/6i resistance in clinical trials?
- Do PROTAC degraders targeting CDK4/6 have an acceptable therapeutic index in patients, given the essential roles of CDK4/6 in normal cell cycle regulation?
- Does resistance mediated by the CDK6–INK4 complex also occur in non-breast cancer types where CDK4/6i are being evaluated (e.g., liposarcoma, NSCLC)?
- Can ribociclib or abemaciclib, which have different INK4-CDK6 binding affinities from palbociclib, partially overcome CDK6–INK4 resistance?
- Does co-targeting the Hippo pathway (YAP1 inhibition) or PI3K/AKT (PTEN loss context) prevent CDK6 upregulation and forestall resistance?

## Limitations

- Clinical resistance data are inferred from genomic alterations in metastatic tumor biopsies; direct CDK4/6i treatment status at biopsy and longitudinal resistance documentation are limited
- The 17% estimate of CDK6-upregulating alterations likely underestimates the full prevalence of CDK6-mediated resistance since other mechanisms (e.g., transcriptional upregulation without genetic driver) are not captured
- PROTAC data are preclinical (cell lines, PDX, and in vivo mouse models); clinical PK/PD and safety data in patients are not available
- Study focused on ER+/HER2− breast cancer; generalizability to other cancers with CDK4/6i use is unknown
- The structural modeling is based on existing crystallographic structures and molecular dynamics simulations; experimental cryo-EM or X-ray structures of the full ternary CDK6–INK4–PROTAC complex are lacking
