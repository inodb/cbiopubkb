## Summary

The A5 consortium performs deep multi-omic profiling (WGS, WTS, small-RNA-seq, DNA methylation, C-circle analysis, snRNA-seq, snATAC-seq) on 94 SDHB-mutant pheochromocytomas and paragangliomas (PCPG) from 79 patients — the most comprehensive genomic characterisation of these rare tumours to date. Key discoveries include: (1) sympathetic and parasympathetic PCPG have cell-of-origin-distinct transcriptional and methylation profiles; (2) TERT and ATRX alterations are found in 61% of metastatic cases and are strongly associated with metastatic progression; (3) two acquired resistance mechanisms to DNA alkylating chemotherapy are identified — MGMT overexpression and mismatch repair deficiency causing hypermutation; (4) a quiet somatic mutation landscape with rare cooperative driver events including EPAS1 (HIF-2α) mutations. Data are available in cBioPortal as `hnsc_a5consortium_2025`.

## Key Findings

### F1: TERT and ATRX alterations are the dominant somatic drivers and are strongly associated with metastatic PCPG

**Finding:** TERT/ATRX alterations are present in 21/34 (61%) metastatic cases (Pearson Chi-squared p=9.3×10⁻⁸) and in only 1 non-metastatic tumour (which had short follow-up). TERT-altered cases have significantly elevated tumour mutation burden and larger primary tumour size. TERT/ATRX status independently predicts poorer overall survival.

**Detail:** TERT alterations include hotspot promoter mutations (chr5:1295113/1295135 G>A) in 14 metastatic cases (17.7%), plus one AFF4-TERT fusion from chromothripsis. ATRX alterations include nonsense, splice-site, frameshift, and large deletion mutations; all lead to low ATRX expression via nonsense-mediated decay. Late acquisition of pTERT mutations is supported by two clonal evolution cases: in E146, the primary was TERT wild-type while the paired metastasis was pTERT mutant; in E143, a subclonal pTERT primary gave rise to fully pTERT-mutant metastases. 1,152 and 1,448 genes are differentially expressed in TERT- and ATRX-altered tumours respectively vs. non-metastatic primaries.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TERT`, `ATRX` · SDHB-mutant pheochromocytoma and paraganglioma

### F2: ATRX loss triggers alternative lengthening of telomeres (ALT) in PCPG — a distinct telomere maintenance mechanism

**Finding:** C-circles (a hallmark of ALT activity) are detected in all ATRX-altered and one TERT-altered primary tumour. ATRX-altered tumours have significantly higher telomere tumour:blood ratios than TERT-altered or TERT/ATRX wild-type cases (p<0.00025). Telomere variant repeat (TVR) patterns are aberrant in C-circle-positive tumours.

**Detail:** ATRX loss impairs histone H3.3 deposition at telomeres, leading to telomeric replication stress and ALT. Unlike TERT-driven telomere maintenance, ALT operates through homologous recombination. Only one intrachromosomal telomeric insertion was detected, and TERRA expression was elevated but not significantly so in ATRX-altered tumours. Importantly, in patient E143, ALT features (C-circles, long telomeres) co-existed with a subclonal pTERT mutation in the primary, but only the pTERT-mutant clone metastasized, demonstrating competitive clonal dynamics between two telomere maintenance mechanisms.

**Genes / cancer types:** `ATRX`, `TERT` · SDHB-mutant PCPG

### F3: Sympathetic and parasympathetic PCPG have distinct cell-of-origin molecular profiles with clinical relevance

**Finding:** Unsupervised UMAP clustering of WTS, small-RNA-seq, and DNA methylation data clearly separates parasympathetic (non-chromaffin, head-and-neck PG [HN-PG]) from sympathetic (chromaffin cell) PCPG. HN-PG are biochemically silent (low TH, DBH), lack chromaffin markers (CARTPT, TFAP2B, GATA3, HOX genes), and show high TPH1 expression (serotonin synthesis). Low SLC6A2 (NET) expression in some sympathetic PCPG has therapeutic implications for MIBG-based theranostics.

**Detail:** 4,615 genes and 36 microRNAs are differentially expressed between HN-PG and sympathetic PCPG. HOX gene clusters (HOXA1-10, HOXB, HOXC) are lowly expressed in HN-PG with concordant DNA hypermethylation. Low SLC6A2/NET expression in sympathetic PCPG corresponds to gene promoter methylation, predicting reduced MIBG (¹²³/¹²⁴/¹³¹I) uptake and limiting theranostic applicability. Two mediastinal dopamine-secreting PG cluster with HN-PG, indicating a non-chromaffin cell-of-origin and reclassifying presumed sympathetic tumours. TERT/ATRX alterations are absent from HN-PG.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TH`, `DBH`, `SLC6A2`, `CARTPT`, `TPH1`, `TFAP2B`, `GATA3` · Parasympathetic vs. sympathetic PCPG

### F4: Two distinct chemotherapy resistance mechanisms identified in post-treatment SDHB-deficient PCPG

**Finding:** Among 10 tumours resected post-cytotoxic treatment (12% of cohort), two mechanisms of acquired resistance to DNA alkylating chemotherapy are identifiable: (1) MGMT promoter overexpression (epigenetic silencing reversal) neutralising alkylating agents, and (2) mismatch repair (MMR) deficiency causing hypermutation (SBS11 signature, DNA alkylation damage). These are mutually exclusive resistance mechanisms.

**Detail:** Chemotherapy-related SBS11 (temozolomide/CCNU-associated DNA alkylation signature) is detected in post-treatment cases. MGMT overexpression — opposing its expected methylation-mediated silencing — allows tumour cells to repair alkylation damage before it can cause lethal cross-links. MMR deficiency creates a hypermutator phenotype that, while potentially immunogenic, causes drug resistance through error-prone replication bypass. Identifying the resistance mechanism in a patient biopsy could guide second-line therapy: MMR-deficient hypermutators may benefit from immune-checkpoint blockade.

**Genes / cancer types:** `MGMT`, `MLH1`, `MSH2`, `MSH6`, `PMS2` · SDHB-mutant metastatic PCPG

**Data availability:** `partial`

### F5: EPAS1 (HIF-2α) somatic mutations represent rare cooperative driver events in SDHB-deficient PCPG

**Finding:** Two somatic EPAS1 mutations near codon 531 (PHD2 prolyl hydroxylation target) are identified in otherwise quiet SDHB-mutant genomes — E230-P1 (p.Tyr532Cys) and E183-P1 (p.Asp539Gly). These represent cooperative second hits amplifying pseudohypoxia signalling already induced by SDHB deficiency. Recurrently altered cancer genes also include chromatin modifiers KMT2C (n=2) and BCOR (n=2), TP53 in three metastatic tumours, and a MYCN hotspot mutation (p.Pro44Leu).

**Detail:** SDHB deficiency leads to succinate accumulation, inhibiting PHD1/2 and causing oxygen-independent HIF-α stabilisation (pseudohypoxia). EPAS1/HIF-2α mutations at the PHD2 recognition site would abrogate prolyl hydroxylation and further amplify HIF-2α activity, creating a compounded pseudohypoxia phenotype. The PHD2/HIF-2α axis is therapeutically tractable: belzutifan (HIF-2α inhibitor) is approved for VHL disease and is under investigation in SDHx-related tumours. Chromosome 7 gains are enriched in metastatic PCPG (GISTIC q<0.05), implicating chr7-encoded drivers in metastatic progression.

**Genes / cancer types:** `EPAS1`, `KMT2C`, `BCOR`, `TP53`, `MYCN` · SDHB-mutant PCPG

## Open Questions

1. Can TERT/ATRX mutation status in primary PCPG replace or complement current imaging-based metastatic risk surveillance to guide intensity of follow-up?
2. Does belzutifan or other HIF-2α inhibition have efficacy in EPAS1-mutant SDHB-deficient PCPG in a prospective trial?
3. Do MMR-deficient hypermutated post-treatment PCPG respond to immune-checkpoint blockade, given their elevated neoantigen load?
4. What drives metastasis in the 39% of metastatic cases without TERT/ATRX alterations, and are there alternative telomere lengthening mechanisms?
5. Is low SLC6A2 expression reliably predictive of MIBG non-uptake, and what is the best alternative theranostic strategy for NET-low sympathetic PCPG?

## Limitations

- The cBioPortal study identifier listed in the raw file (`hnsc_a5consortium_2025`) may not accurately reflect the PCPG tumour type; users should verify the study identifier directly in cBioPortal.
- Small cohort (n=94 tumours) limits statistical power for rare subgroup analyses, especially for post-treatment resistance mechanisms (n=10 post-treatment tumours).
- snRNA-seq and snATAC-seq applied only to a subset, limiting single-cell insights to selected cases.
- Functional validation of EPAS1 mutations as cooperative drivers is inferential; experimental models are lacking.
- Long-term treatment outcome data are limited; the association of MGMT/MMR resistance mechanisms with clinical outcomes requires prospective validation.
