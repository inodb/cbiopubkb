## Summary

This study examines whole exome sequencing (WES) of 114 metastatic biopsies from 81 patients with either castration-resistant prostate adenocarcinoma (CRPC-Adeno, n=51 patients) or castration-resistant neuroendocrine prostate cancer (CRPC-NE, n=30 patients). The mutational landscape of CRPC-NE is broadly similar to CRPC-Adeno, but with key distinctions: *RB1* loss (70% vs 32%), *TP53* mutation/deletion (67% vs 31%), and near absence of *AR* genomic alterations in CRPC-NE. Serial biopsy analysis of individual patients supports divergent clonal evolution as the mechanism—CRPC-NE arises clonally from a CRPC-Adeno precursor rather than from pre-existing neuroendocrine clones or linear/independent evolution. In contrast to the similar DNA landscapes, genome-wide DNA methylation (eRRBS) shows strong epigenetic segregation between CRPC-NE and CRPC-Adeno, with *EZH2* overexpression and widespread epigenetic dysregulation. A 70-gene integrated NEPC molecular classifier is developed and validated across four independent datasets, identifying up to 8% of metastatic tumors as NEPC with >0.99 precision/recall.

## Key Findings

### F1: RB1 and TP53 co-loss defines CRPC-NE and distinguishes it from CRPC-Adeno

**Finding:** *RB1* is deleted in 70% of CRPC-NE vs 32% of CRPC-Adeno (p=0.003). *TP53* is mutated or deleted in 67% of CRPC-NE vs 31% of CRPC-Adeno (p=0.0043). Concurrent *RB1* and *TP53* loss is present in 53% of CRPC-NE vs 14% of CRPC-Adeno (p<0.0004).

**Detail:** Concurrent *RB1* and *TP53* loss is known to promote small cell carcinoma pathogenesis in primary lung and prostate. Beyond RB1/TP53, CRPC-NE and CRPC-Adeno share a broadly similar overall spectrum of somatic alterations — there are no significant differences in non-silent point mutation rate, polyploidy, or copy number genomic burden (>30% of genome aberrant in both). Five of six samples with highest SNV counts (115–663 SNVs) showed MMR gene alterations, consistent with microsatellite instability.

**Genes / cancer types:** `RB1`, `TP53` · Castration-resistant prostate cancer (CRPC-NE vs CRPC-Adeno)

### F2: AR genomic alterations are absent in CRPC-NE — opposite of CRPC-Adeno where AR amplification/mutation is a dominant resistance mechanism

**Finding:** *AR* point mutations are absent in CRPC-NE, and gains when present are low-level and explained by tumor polyploidy. In contrast, 29 CRPC-Adeno cases show *AR* focal amplification or point mutation, and 21 have alterations in *AR* co-activators (*FOXA1*, *NCOR1/2*, *ZBTB16*).

**Detail:** The ARv7 splice variant was observed in both subtypes, but the ARv7:wild-type AR ratio was significantly lower in CRPC-NE (p=0.0025). Overall AR protein and mRNA expression are lower in CRPC-NE, consistent with an AR-indifferent cell state. The absence of AR genomic alterations in CRPC-NE is interpreted as clonal selection of non-AR-amplified subpopulations during AR-targeted therapy rather than reversal of pre-existing amplification.

### F3: Serial biopsy analysis supports divergent clonal evolution — CRPC-NE arises from a CRPC-Adeno precursor through clonal divergence

**Finding:** Phylogenetic analysis of serial tumor biopsies from individual patients supports divergent clonal evolution as the predominant mechanism of CRPC-NE emergence: a subclone within CRPC-Adeno selectively acquires *RB1*/*TP53* loss and epigenetic alterations, diverging from the AR-driven lineage.

**Detail:** In patient WCMC7520, homozygous *BRCA2* deletion and *TP53* mutation are shared across primary and all metastatic sites (common ancestor), while *MYCN* shows site-specific allelic states (divergence). In patient WCMC161, allele-specific analysis of three time-points (CRPC-Adeno lymph node, CRPC-Adeno bone, CRPC-NE liver on abiraterone) ruled out parallel evolution. Divergent clonal evolution (model V) is favored over linear or independent clonal evolution. Additional newly identified feature: *CYLD* is deleted in 51% of CRPC-NE samples (FDR<10% for both DNA and mRNA), associated with AR signaling gene decrease and NF-κB/TGF-β pathway de-repression.

**Genes / cancer types:** `BRCA2`, `TP53`, `RB1`, `MYCN`, `CYLD`, `DEK` · CRPC-NE (metastatic progression)

### F4: Strong epigenetic segregation between CRPC-NE and CRPC-Adeno — EZH2 overexpression drives epigenetic reprogramming

**Finding:** Despite broadly similar DNA mutational landscapes, CRPC-NE and CRPC-Adeno are strongly segregated by genome-wide DNA methylation (eRRBS unsupervised analysis). EZH2 mRNA is 2-fold higher in CRPC-NE (p<10⁻⁶), and EZH2-repressed target genes (*DKK1*, *NKD1*, HOX genes) are downregulated. EZH2 inhibitor GSK343 preferentially reduces viability of the NCI-H660 CRPC-NE cell line.

**Detail:** 22% of the top dysregulated transcripts in CRPC-NE show concordant DNA methylation changes (p<0.0002). The tumor suppressor *SPDEF* is hypermethylated and underexpressed in CRPC-NE (p<10⁻⁹)—SPDEF suppresses EMT and metastasis. Epigenetic clustering identified three CRPC-Adeno cases with clinical features of AR independence that grouped with CRPC-NE on methylation analysis, suggesting a pre-transition or transitioning state. Epigenetically dysregulated pathways include neuronal differentiation, EMT, stem cell programs, and cell-cell adhesion.

**Genes / cancer types:** `EZH2`, `SPDEF`, `DKK1`, `NKD1`, `HOXA11`, `HOXA13`, `NKX3-1`, `DNMT1`, `DNMT3A`, `DNMT3B` · CRPC-NE

### F5: 70-gene integrated NEPC molecular classifier identifies CRPC-NE with >0.99 precision/recall across independent cohorts; applicable to routine biopsies

**Finding:** An integrated 70-gene NEPC classifier using DNA, RNA, and methylation data identifies CRPC-NE with precision and recall >0.99 in the discovery cohort and outperforms conventional NE markers, AR mRNA, and single-gene approaches across four validation datasets. Up to 8% of metastatic prostate tumors score as NEPC-positive.

**Detail:** The classifier incorporates genes prioritized by genomic, transcriptomic, or epigenomic status, including *AURKA* and *MYCN* (previously described). In 683 prostate samples across TCGA, SU2C/PCF 2015, WCMC 2011/14, and this study, elevated NEPC classifier score was found in up to 8% of metastatic tumors and in none of treatment-naïve adenocarcinoma or benign prostate. Among those with elevated scores, >80% had pathologic features of CRPC-NE. Tumors with moderate or rising NEPC score during CRPC-Adeno progression may represent AR-independent transition states. LNCaP cells (adenocarcinoma line) treated long-term with enzalutamide acquired *SPDEF* methylation—a CRPC-NE molecular feature.

**Genes / cancer types:** `AURKA`, `MYCN`, `SPDEF`, `NCAM`, `PEG10`, `SYP`, `CHGA`, `AR` · All prostate cancer stages

**Data availability:** `partial`
> RNA-seq data in `nepc_wcm_2016` supports classifier re-scoring. Full 70-gene list available from Supplementary Table 9. Methylation layer requires dbGaP access.

## Open Questions

- Can the NEPC classifier be applied to circulating tumor DNA for early detection of CRPC-NE transition, enabling pre-emptive platinum chemotherapy before histologic conversion?
- Does EZH2 inhibitor treatment in CRPC-NE patients delay or prevent transition to AR-independent disease, or does it work as a therapeutic agent post-transition?
- What is the minimal genetic/epigenetic event required to initiate the divergent clonal evolution toward CRPC-NE—is *RB1* loss the initiating event, or does *TP53* mutation come first?
- Do the ~20% of NEPC-classified adenocarcinomas (epigenetically NEPC but morphologically adenocarcinoma) respond differently to standard AR-directed therapies?
- Can *CYLD* loss serve as an early biomarker or driver of AR-independence before full NE conversion?

## Limitations

- Cohort from a single tertiary referral center (Weill Cornell); may not be representative of all CRPC patients
- 17 patients with serial biopsies; larger longitudinal cohort needed to fully characterize evolutionary trajectories
- WES does not capture structural variants (fusions) comprehensively; RNA-seq used for fusion discovery
- Differences in prior therapies across patients may confound mutational comparisons
- eRRBS (enhanced Reduced Representation Bisulfite Sequencing) does not cover the full methylome
- Classifier validation cohorts are relatively small for CRPC-NE (rare entity); tumor heterogeneity within biopsies not fully addressed
- In vitro EZH2 inhibitor data from cell lines; clinical benefit in patients remains to be established
