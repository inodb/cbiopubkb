## Summary

TCGA comprehensive multiplatform analysis of 230 resected lung adenocarcinomas (WES, mRNA-seq, miRNA-seq, DNA methylation, CNA arrays, RPPA; low-pass WGS on n=93). Mean mutation rate is 8.9 per Mb (high). Eighteen statistically significantly mutated genes are identified, including novel genes RIT1 (activating) and MGA (loss-of-function, mutually exclusive with MYC amplification). RTK/RAS/RAF pathway driver events are extended to 76% of cases by nominating NF1, RIT1, ERBB2, and MET amplification as additional drivers in oncogene-negative tumors. MET exon 14 skipping (4%) is identified as an actionable RNA event driven by somatic DNA changes. Three mRNA transcriptional subtypes are defined: terminal respiratory unit (TRU/bronchioid), proximal-inflammatory (PI/squamoid), and proximal-proliferative (PP/magnoid). CIMP-H and chromatin/splicing gene mutations (SETD2, ARID1A, SMARCA4, RBM10, U2AF1) are prominent epigenetic themes. Full data in `luad_tcga_pub` on cBioPortal.

## Key Findings

### F1: RTK/RAS/RAF driver events extended to 76% of cases; new drivers in oncogene-negative tumors

**Finding:** Known RTK/RAS/RAF activating events (KRAS 32%, EGFR 11%, BRAF 7%, ALK/ROS1/RET fusions, MET exon 14 skipping, ERBB2 insertions) account for 62% of tumors. In the oncogene-negative 38%, focal ERBB2 and MET amplifications, NF1 loss-of-function mutations (11%), and RIT1 activating mutations (2%) are enriched, raising the RTK/RAS/RAF driver fraction to 76%.

**Detail:** NF1 (RAS-GAP) loss-of-function mutations are significantly enriched in oncogene-negative tumors (P<0.01) and were previously underappreciated as lung adenocarcinoma drivers because prior studies lacked power for all loss-of-function classes. RIT1 mutations (mostly Q79 mutations, homologous to RAS Q61) transform NIH3T3 cells and activate MAPK and PI3K signaling — supporting a driver role. KEAP1 and TP53 mutations are also enriched in oncogene-negative tumors, suggesting alternative mechanisms of pathway activation. ~24% of tumors still lack an identifiable driver within RTK/RAS/RAF.

**Genes / cancer types:** KRAS, EGFR, BRAF, ALK, ROS1, RET, MET, ERBB2, NF1, RIT1, TP53, KEAP1 · Lung adenocarcinoma

### F2: MET exon 14 skipping — a DNA-encoded RNA aberration found in 4% of cases

**Finding:** MET exon 14 skipping, which stabilizes MET protein (by removing the ubiquitin ligase Cbl docking site encoded in exon 14), occurs in 10 tumors via somatic DNA alterations: 5′ or 3′ splice site mutations/deletions (9 cases) or a Y1003* stop codon mutation that disrupts splicing enhancers (1 case). This RNA-level event is detectable only by combined DNA + RNA sequencing.

**Detail:** MET exon 14 skipping was quantified by percentage skipping from RNA-seq; confirmed in samples with relevant DNA alterations. The Y1003* case demonstrates a novel cis-acting splicing mechanism driven by a stop codon disrupting a splicing enhancer. Combined DNA + RNA analysis also enabled confirmation that 75% of WES-detected somatic mutations are present in expressed RNA (≥5× coverage), providing an important quality metric. MET exon 14 skipping is now an FDA-approved companion diagnostic target for capmatinib and tepotinib.

**Genes / cancer types:** MET · Lung adenocarcinoma

### F3: Three mRNA transcriptional subtypes linked to distinct driver genes, smoking, and prognosis

**Finding:** Three transcriptional subtypes are defined and renamed: terminal respiratory unit (TRU, formerly bronchioid) harboring most EGFR mutations and ALK/ROS1/RET fusions, and prognostically favorable; proximal-proliferative (PP, formerly magnoid) enriched for KRAS mutations and STK11 inactivation; proximal-inflammatory (PI, formerly squamoid) characterized by NF1+TP53 co-mutation and solid histopathology.

**Detail:** TRU subtype: EGFR mutations, kinase fusions, low ploidy, low mutation rate, enriched in never-smokers. PP subtype: KRAS mutations, STK11 chromosomal loss/inactivating mutation/low expression, associated with smoking (transversion-high). PI subtype: NF1+TP53 co-mutation, solid histology, enriched for TP53 mutations. iCluster integrative analysis identifies 6 clusters that partially overlap with mRNA subtypes, with higher ploidy and mutation rate in clusters 1–3 (TP53-enriched, proximal subtypes). TRU subtype membership is prognostically favorable. miRNA and RPPA analyses also reveal heterogeneity partially overlapping with mRNA subtypes.

**Genes / cancer types:** EGFR, ALK, ROS1, RET, KRAS, STK11, NF1, TP53 · Lung adenocarcinoma

### F4: MGA loss-of-function mutations as novel MYC pathway mechanism, mutually exclusive with MYC amplification

**Finding:** MGA (Max-interacting protein in the MYC pathway) is recurrently mutated with frameshift and nonsense loss-of-function mutations in 8% of lung adenocarcinomas. MGA mutations are mutually exclusive with focal MYC amplification (Fisher's exact P=0.04), suggesting MGA inactivation as an alternative mechanism of MYC pathway dysregulation.

**Detail:** MGA functions as a MAX-binding transcriptional repressor; its loss would be expected to de-repress MYC target genes through an excess of MYC-MAX heterodimers relative to MGA-MAX complexes. This finding underscores the importance of the MYC transcriptional axis as a therapeutic target in lung adenocarcinoma, even in tumors lacking MYC amplification. MYC amplification (8q24) and CCND3 amplification (novel peak) are among significant CNA findings. Chromothripsis was identified in 6% of WGS cases (6/93).

**Genes / cancer types:** MGA, MYC, MAX · Lung adenocarcinoma

### F5: CIMP phenotype and chromatin/splicing gene mutations define epigenetic heterogeneity

**Finding:** DNA methylation clustering identifies a CIMP-High (CIMP-H) phenotype associated with hypermethylation of CDKN2A, GATA2/4/5, RASSF1, WNT pathway genes (SFRP1, SOX17, WIF1), and MYC overexpression. SETD2 mutation is associated with CDKN2A methylation (rather than CDKN2A deletion/mutation), defining a distinct epigenetic tumor subtype with low ploidy and fewer overall mutations.

**Detail:** Chromatin-modifying genes mutated: SETD2 (9%), ARID1A (7%), SMARCA4 (6%). RNA splicing genes: RBM10 (8%, X-linked, enriched in males P=0.002) and U2AF1 (3%, S34F hotspot) with U2AF1 S34F causing 129 significant alternative splicing events including CTNNB1 (β-catenin) alternative splicing. EGFR mutations are enriched in female patients (P=0.03). Transversion-high tumors (smoking-associated) carry KRAS mutations; transversion-low (non-smoker/adenocarcinoma) tumors carry EGFR mutations, RB1 frameshift indels, and kinase fusions.

**Genes / cancer types:** SETD2, ARID1A, SMARCA4, RBM10, U2AF1, CDKN2A, EGFR, KRAS · Lung adenocarcinoma

**Data availability:** `available`

## Open Questions

- Which of the 24% of lung adenocarcinomas without an RTK/RAS/RAF driver event harbor druggable non-canonical pathway activations?
- Do NF1 loss-of-function mutations predict resistance to KRAS inhibitors (sotorasib, adagrasib) when they co-occur, as suggested in other contexts?
- Can U2AF1 S34F-driven alternative splicing of CTNNB1 be therapeutically targeted (e.g., splicing factor inhibitors)?
- What fraction of SETD2-mutated, CDKN2A-methylated CIMP-H tumors respond to CDK4/6 inhibitors (given CDKN2A inactivation)?
- Does MET exon 14 skipping detected by DNA (splice site mutations) vs. RNA-seq differ in predictive value for MET inhibitor response?

## Limitations

- Only resected (early-stage) tumors included; molecular landscape of advanced/metastatic lung adenocarcinoma may differ (treatment selection, acquired resistance)
- Median follow-up 19 months; formal survival analyses in supplementary only
- Statistical analysis of significantly mutated genes used combined TCGA + previously published cohort (n=412) for power; results specific to n=230 TCGA samples alone may differ
- Low-pass WGS performed on only n=93 samples; structural rearrangement landscape (chromothripsis, novel fusions) incompletely characterized for the full cohort
- RPPA protein analysis on n=181 samples only; pathway activation at protein level (MAPK, mTOR) shown to diverge from DNA-level alterations, suggesting additional undiscovered mechanisms not captured by the molecular platforms used
