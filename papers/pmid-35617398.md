## Summary

This Science paper uses ATAC-seq, RNA-seq, and MSK-IMPACT sequencing of 22 patient-derived organoids, 6 PDX, and 12 cell lines to classify castration-resistant prostate cancer (CRPC) into four chromatin-based molecular subtypes: CRPC-AR, CRPC-NE, CRPC-WNT, and a newly identified CRPC-SCL (stem cell-like). CRPC-SCL, driven by AP-1 transcription factors (FOSL1) cooperating with YAP/TAZ and TEAD, emerges as the second most prevalent subtype in 366 patient samples (28%) and is associated with shorter ARSI treatment duration. AP-1 and YAP/TAZ inhibitors (T-5224, verteporfin) selectively suppress CRPC-SCL growth, suggesting subtype-specific therapeutic targets.

## Key Findings

### F1: Four chromatin-based subtypes of CRPC identified by ATAC-seq

**Finding:** Consensus k-means clustering of ATAC-seq peaks from 35 CRPC models (organoids, PDX, cell lines) identified four reproducible subtypes: CRPC-AR (AR-dependent), CRPC-NE (neuroendocrine), CRPC-WNT (Wnt-driven), and CRPC-SCL (stem cell-like / AP-1-driven), each with subtype-specific enhancer accessibility.

**Detail:** 861,195 reproducible ATAC-seq peaks identified; signature peaks enriched at enhancers (not promoters; Fisher's exact p=4.18e-126). Subtypes were reproduced by hierarchical clustering and UMAP independently. CRPC-WNT contained 4 samples, all with Wnt pathway alterations (CTNNB1 hotspot mutations in 3; APC deletion + RSPO2 gain in 1). CRPC-SCL comprised 11 organoids and cell lines DU145 and PC3.

**Genes / cancer types:** AR, FOXA1, NEUROD1, ASCL1, TCF7L2, FOSL1 · Castration-resistant prostate cancer

### F2: CRPC-SCL is a novel AP-1-driven subtype, the second most common in patients (28%)

**Finding:** CRPC-SCL, not previously described, is enriched in mammary stem cell and cancer stem cell signatures (CD44, TROP2A), TGF-β, EMT, and AP-1 (FOSL1) programs; it is the second most prevalent subtype after CRPC-AR in 366 clinical patients by transcriptomic classification.

**Detail:** Nearest Template Prediction (NTP) and SVM classified 206/266 SU2C and 82/100 WCM CRPC patients; CRPC-SCL accounted for 28% of classified patients. FOSL1 was the top-ranked TF by combined expression, chromatin accessibility at motifs, and regulatory network out-degree. Unassigned samples by NTP showed a tendency toward CRPC-SCL, suggesting it may also represent a transitional state.

**Genes / cancer types:** FOSL1, FOSL2, JUNB, FOS, CD44, TROP2A · Castration-resistant prostate cancer

**Data availability:** `partial`

### F3: CRPC-SCL patients have shorter time on ARSI treatment

**Finding:** Among 56 SU2C CRPC patients with time-on-ARSI (enzalutamide/abiraterone) data, CRPC-SCL patients exhibited significantly shorter treatment duration by Cox log-rank statistics, indicating AR signaling inhibitor resistance in this subtype.

**Detail:** CRPC-SCL samples lack AR amplification/mutation enrichment seen in CRPC-AR. The subtype is AR-negative/low, consistent with inherent insensitivity to ARSIs. RB1 loss was enriched in AR-negative/low samples (14/24) vs. CRPC-AR (2/12; p=0.02), but CRPC-SCL does not uniformly exhibit NEPC features despite RB1/TP53 co-loss in some cases.

**Genes / cancer types:** AR, RB1, TP53 · Castration-resistant prostate cancer

### F4: AP-1 (FOSL1) cooperates with YAP/TAZ and TEAD to maintain CRPC-SCL chromatin state

**Finding:** ChIP-seq in MSKPCa3 and DU145 showed significant co-occupancy of FOSL1, YAP, TAZ, and TEAD1 at CRPC-SCL-specific ATAC-seq peaks (but not CRPC-AR peaks), with a positive feedback loop between YAP/TAZ and FOSL1 maintaining subtype-specific open chromatin.

**Detail:** TEAD motifs were the second most enriched motifs in CRPC-SCL-specific accessible peaks after AP-1. YAP/TAZ target signature was strongly enriched in CRPC-SCL (GSEA FDR <0.001). Double knockdown of YAP/TAZ decreased chromatin accessibility at CRPC-SCL peaks, downregulated FOSL1 at both RNA and protein levels, and reduced CRPC-SCL target gene expression. YAP/TAZ knockdown reduced growth in MSKPCa3 and DU145, but not in CRPC-AR lines MSKPCa2 and 22Rv1.

**Genes / cancer types:** FOSL1, YAP (YAP1), TAZ (WWTR1), TEAD1 · Castration-resistant prostate cancer

### F5: FOSL1 overexpression shifts CRPC-AR toward CRPC-SCL chromatin state (lineage plasticity)

**Finding:** Stable overexpression of FOSL1 alone or with YAP or TAZ in LNCaP (CRPC-AR) cells significantly increased chromatin accessibility at CRPC-SCL-specific peaks and decreased accessibility at CRPC-AR-specific peaks, with concordant upregulation of CRPC-SCL signature genes (FDR <10e-5), demonstrating FOSL1 as a pioneer factor driving lineage plasticity toward the SCL state.

**Detail:** Permutation test p<0.001 for both CRPC-SCL opening and CRPC-AR closing upon FOSL1 OE. Co-expression of FOSL1+YAP or FOSL1+TAZ showed greater chromatin remodeling than FOSL1 alone, consistent with cooperative AP-1/YAP-TAZ co-occupancy.

**Genes / cancer types:** FOSL1, YAP1, TAZ · Castration-resistant prostate cancer

### F6: AP-1 and YAP/TAZ inhibitors selectively suppress CRPC-SCL growth

**Finding:** AP-1 inhibitor T-5224 and YAP/TAZ inhibitor verteporfin preferentially suppressed growth of CRPC-SCL organoid MSKPCa3 and cell line DU145 relative to CRPC-AR line MSKPCa2, supporting the therapeutic vulnerability of this subtype to AP-1/YAP-TAZ pathway inhibition.

**Detail:** YAP/TAZ activity (sum of z-scores) was significantly higher in CRPC-SCL patients compared to other groups (p<0.0001, Wilcoxon rank-sum test) and was negatively correlated with AR expression across 266 SU2C patients (Pearson r=−0.201, p<0.001). CRPC-SCL also showed enrichment of FGFR signaling and FGF ligand/receptor expression, suggesting additional therapeutic targets.

**Genes / cancer types:** FOSL1, YAP1, TAZ, FGFR, CCND1 · Castration-resistant prostate cancer

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Can CRPC-SCL be identified in clinical practice using a minimal gene expression signature deployable on clinical RNA sequencing panels?
- Do CRPC-SCL tumors arise de novo or through AR-to-SCL lineage plasticity induced by ARSI pressure, and at what frequency?
- Would inhibition of AP-1/YAP-TAZ axis reverse ARSI resistance in CRPC-SCL patients?
- What is the genomic correlate of CRPC-SCL—is there a recurrent somatic alteration (beyond RB1/TP53 loss) that predisposes to this state?
- Is CRPC-SCL enriched in specific metastatic sites (bone, visceral) with different clinical behavior?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Organoid biobank is small (22 models), enriched for aggressive disease with rapid ARSI progression; may not represent the full CRPC spectrum.
- CRPC-WNT subtype is underrepresented (4 samples), limiting conclusions about this group.
- ChIP-seq and mechanistic experiments performed in only 2 CRPC-SCL lines; requires validation in additional models.
- Time-on-ARSI clinical data available for only 56 of 266 SU2C patients.
- The transcriptomic classification of patients does not incorporate spatial/temporal heterogeneity within tumors.
