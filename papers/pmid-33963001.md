## Summary
This retrospective single-institution study analyzed 573 patients with liver-limited intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma (IHC) treated with curative-intent resection (n=237), hepatic arterial infusion chemotherapy (HAIC; n=196), or systemic chemotherapy alone (SYS; n=140) between 2000–2018 at MSKCC. Among node-positive (N1) patients, HAIC and resection achieved equivalent survival (18.1 vs. 19.7 months median OS), both superior to SYS (11.2 months). Genomic analysis using MSK-IMPACT (n=219) showed that high-risk alterations (*TP53* mutation, *KRAS* mutation, *CDKN2A/B* deletion) conferred dramatically poor prognosis in N1 patients (median OS 12.1 months), whereas *IDH1/2* mutations and *FGFR2* fusions did not stratify survival in the LNM setting.

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: HAIC Equivalent to Resection for Node-Positive (N1) IHC Patients
- **Detail**: For node-negative (N0) patients, resection provided the longest median OS (59.9 months, 95% CI 47.2–74.3), significantly better than HAIC (24.9 months) and SYS (13.7 months; P<0.001). By contrast, for node-positive (N1) patients, resection (19.7 months, 95% CI 12.1–27.2) and HAIC (18.1 months, 95% CI 14.1–26.6) had statistically equivalent survival (P=0.560), while both were superior to SYS (11.2 months; P=0.024). Multivariable Cox modeling confirmed a significant interaction between treatment and lymph node status (P<0.001), with HAIC inferior to resection only in N0 patients (HR 2.09, 95% CI 1.6–2.8). HAIC patients had greater disease burden (74% multifocal vs. 22% for resection), making equivalence more striking.
- **Genes/Alterations**: Clinical outcome analysis, not gene-specific
- **Data availability**: available — clinical data linked to genomic profiles in ihch_mskcc_2020
- **Tool support**: native — survival analysis by treatment and nodal status native in cBioPortal; clinical attribute filtering

### Finding 2: High-Risk Genomic Alterations (TP53/KRAS/CDKN2A/B) Confer Uniformly Poor Prognosis in N1 Disease
- **Detail**: Among N1 patients with genomic data available, those with at least one high-risk alteration (*TP53* mutation, *KRAS* mutation, or *CDKN2A/B* deletion) had median OS of 12.1 months (95% CI 5.7–21.5) vs. 30.9 months in wild-type patients (P=0.002). This adverse prognosis was independent of treatment modality (resection or HAIC combined). Individual alterations were each prognostic: *CDKN2A* deletion (median OS 9.4 vs. 23.0 months, P=0.022), *CDKN2B* deletion (6.1 vs. 25.8 months, P<0.001), combined *CDKN2A/B* deletion (7.1 vs. 27.2 months, P<0.001). The *INK4b-ARF-INK4α* locus (9p21) encodes p16 and p15 cyclin-dependent kinase inhibitors, and its deletion is associated with aggressive carcinogenesis across multiple tumor types.
- **Genes/Alterations**: TP53 (mutation, adverse), KRAS (mutation, adverse), CDKN2A (deletion, adverse), CDKN2B (deletion, adverse)
- **Data availability**: available — all four genes in ihch_mskcc_2020 (MSK-IMPACT); survival data linked
- **Tool support**: native — mutation/deletion queries, survival analysis by alteration status native; composite high-risk group requires manual OR logic
- **Concepts**: #TP53 #KRAS #driver-genes #survival #biomarker #cholangiocarcinoma #biliary

### Finding 3: CDKN2A/B Deletion Confers Worst Prognosis in N1 IHC—Median OS ~7 Months
- **Detail**: Combined *CDKN2A/B* deletion in N1 patients was associated with median OS of only 7.1 months (95% CI 1.7–17.0), versus 27.2 months in wild-type patients (P<0.001). Deletions in *CDKN2A* alone and *CDKN2B* alone were each independently prognostic (P=0.022 and P<0.001 respectively). CDKN2A/B deletions were identified using MSK-IMPACT copy number analysis and occurred in sufficient frequency (≥5%) to be included in the prognostic model. These data suggest that N1 patients with CDKN2A/B deletion are unlikely to derive meaningful benefit from either resection or HAIC, warranting enrollment in clinical trials investigating novel systemic approaches.
- **Genes/Alterations**: CDKN2A (deletion, HR 3.3, P=0.030), CDKN2B (deletion, HR 6.0, P<0.001), combined CDKN2A/B deletion (HR 5.0, P<0.001)
- **Data availability**: available — CNA data in ihch_mskcc_2020 via MSK-IMPACT
- **Tool support**: native — CNA query, survival analysis by CDKN2A/B deletion status native in cBioPortal
- **Concepts**: #driver-genes #copy-number #survival #biomarker #cholangiocarcinoma #biliary

### Finding 4: IDH1/2 Mutations and FGFR2 Fusions Do Not Stratify Survival in Node-Positive IHC
- **Detail**: Among the most prevalent genomic alterations identified (IDH1 18.7%, ARID1A 15.5%, FGFR2 15.5%, TP53 14.6%, BAP1 13.2%), neither *IDH1/2* mutations nor *FGFR2* fusions had a significant effect on OS in N1 patients. *IDH1/2mut* HR was 0.73 (P=0.429); *FGFR2fus* HR was 0.64 (P=0.545). The median OS for N1 IDH1/2-mutant patients was 27.7 months (ns vs. 18.1 months WT). Of note, 10/49 IDH1/2-mutant patients received IDH inhibitor therapy as part of clinical trials, though this was not sufficient to demonstrate a benefit. This finding contrasts with the generally perceived "favorable biology" of IDH-mutant cholangiocarcinoma.
- **Genes/Alterations**: IDH1 (18.7%, no survival impact in N1), IDH2 (rare), FGFR2 fusions (15.5%, no survival impact in N1), ARID1A (15.5%), BAP1 (13.2%)
- **Data availability**: available — IDH1, FGFR2 mutations/fusions in ihch_mskcc_2020; FGFR2 fusion detection via MSK-IMPACT
- **Tool support**: native — IDH1/2 mutation and FGFR2 fusion queries, survival stratification native; api for subgroup analyses
- **Concepts**: #IDH1 #FGFR #fusion #actionability #targeted-therapy #cholangiocarcinoma #biliary

### Finding 5: Genomic Profile of Intrahepatic Cholangiocarcinoma at MSKCC (N=219)
- **Detail**: Among 219 patients profiled with MSK-IMPACT (covering 341–468 genes), the most prevalent oncogenic drivers were: IDH1 (18.7%), ARID1A (15.5%), FGFR2 (15.5%), TP53 (14.6%), BAP1 (13.2%). Only genes altered in ≥5% of patients were included in prognostic analyses. Sequencing was performed predominantly on primary tumor tissue (91.5%), with a minority from local recurrence (2.8%), extrahepatic recurrence (1.9%), or lymph node metastasis (6.6%). The MSK-IMPACT panel expanded from 341 to 468 genes over the study period, affecting data availability for some genes in earlier cases.
- **Genes/Alterations**: IDH1 (18.7%), ARID1A (15.5%), FGFR2 fusions (15.5%), TP53 (14.6%), BAP1 (13.2%), KRAS, CDKN2A/B
- **Data availability**: available — all alterations in ihch_mskcc_2020; note panel version heterogeneity affects some gene availability
- **Tool support**: native — mutation frequency oncoprint, co-occurrence analysis, actionability via OncoKB all native
- **Concepts**: #cholangiocarcinoma #biliary #IDH1 #FGFR #mutation #driver-genes #actionability

### Finding 6: Selective Lymphadenectomy Supported by Imaging Accuracy
- **Detail**: Combining N0 and NX (no nodes sampled) patients into a single node-negative reference cohort was validated by the absence of survival difference between N0 and NX patients (median OS 39.2 vs. 37.1 months, ns). This supports the use of selective (rather than routine) lymphadenectomy guided by contemporary imaging (CT, MRI, PET) and intraoperative assessment. Among patients who underwent lymphadenectomy, the median number of nodes harvested was 2 (range 1–26), reflecting variable sampling completeness. N1 patients had more multifocal disease (70.2% vs. 38.9%) and more poorly differentiated tumors (51.1% vs. 27.7%) than N0 patients.
- **Genes/Alterations**: Clinical finding; LNM (HR 2.41 univariable), multifocal disease (HR 2.35 univariable), tumor size (HR 1.09 per cm)
- **Data availability**: available — clinical data including nodal status, tumor size, multifocality in ihch_mskcc_2020
- **Tool support**: native — clinical variable queries and survival analysis by nodal status native
- **Concepts**: #cholangiocarcinoma #biliary #survival #biomarker

## Open Questions
- Can cBioPortal's ihch_mskcc_2020 dataset be used to identify which IDH1-mutant patients with N1 disease respond to ivosidenib or other IDH inhibitors (clinical trial data linkage)?
- Do co-occurring alterations (e.g., IDH1 + BAP1 or ARID1A) further stratify prognosis within the N1 patient subgroup?
- Is there a correlation between high-risk genomic alterations (CDKN2A/B, TP53, KRAS) and specific imaging features (tumor size, multifocality) that could allow non-invasive risk stratification prior to treatment?

## Limitations
- Retrospective single-institution study with inherent selection bias; HAIC and SYS cohorts had greater disease burden than resected patients
- Genomic data available for only ~50% of HAIC/resection patients; may not represent the full cohort
- MSK-IMPACT panel expanded (341→468 genes) during the study period, introducing heterogeneity in gene coverage across patients
- SYS cohort staging based on imaging (not pathologic), potentially over-estimating N1 rates
- Adjuvant chemotherapy use was heterogeneous (30.8% of all resected, 76.3% of N1 resected) reflecting temporal trends before major trial results
- No randomization; treatment assignment reflects disease burden and clinical judgment, confounding survival comparisons
