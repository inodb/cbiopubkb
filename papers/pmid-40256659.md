## Summary

Prospective cfDNA analysis of 201 patients with metastatic urothelial carcinoma (mUC) nested within CALGB 90601, a phase 3 trial of gemcitabine/cisplatin ± bevacizumab. Pretreatment plasma was sequenced using MSK-ACCESS (129-gene panel, >15,000× depth). The study found no significant association between DDR gene alterations and cisplatin response, but identified high baseline ctDNA VAF and alterations in *TERT*, *PIK3CA*, and *ERBB2* as independent poor prognostic markers. This is among the largest cfDNA biomarker studies in mUC embedded in a randomized phase 3 trial.

## Key Findings

### F1: High baseline ctDNA VAF is an independent poor prognostic marker

**Finding:** Higher variant allele frequency (VAF) in pretreatment ctDNA is independently associated with shorter overall survival and progression-free survival in mUC patients treated with cisplatin-based chemotherapy.

**Detail:** After multivariable adjustment for visceral metastases and ECOG performance status, the cube-root-transformed 75th-percentile VAF (³√VAF 75pc) was associated with OS (HR 2.51, 95% CI 1.26–5.00; p=0.009) and PFS (HR 2.18, 95% CI 1.02–4.67; p=0.045). Patients with VAF >0.082 had OS HR 1.81 (95% CI 1.25–2.63; p=0.002) vs. VAF ≤0.018 reference group.

**Genes / cancer types:** ctDNA VAF (pan-genomic) · Metastatic urothelial carcinoma

### F2: TERT promoter alterations associate with shorter OS

**Finding:** *TERT* promoter alterations in cfDNA are the most prevalent alteration (highest frequency in the landscape) and independently predict shorter overall survival in mUC.

**Detail:** After adjustment for visceral metastases and ECOG performance status, *TERT* alteration was associated with OS (HR 1.59, 95% CI 1.15–2.19; p=0.005). *TERT* was highly correlated with ctDNA VAF (p<0.001), suggesting it partially captures tumor burden signal. When comparing *TERT* vs. ³√VAF 75pc in models, both gave similar concordance probability estimates (~0.62).

**Genes / cancer types:** `TERT` (promoter) · Metastatic urothelial carcinoma

**Data availability:** `partial`
> TERT promoter mutations visible in blca_msk_2025 for archival tumor data. cfDNA-specific TERT calls from this study are not in a separate public cBioPortal dataset.

**Tool support:** `native`
> TERT promoter mutations can be queried natively in the blca_msk_2025 study; survival association requires using the Survival tab after cohort filtering.

### F3: PIK3CA and ERBB2 cfDNA alterations are independent poor prognostic markers

**Finding:** Alterations in *PIK3CA* (17% of cfDNA) and *ERBB2* (detected in cfDNA) are independently associated with shorter OS after adjusting for VAF, visceral metastases, and ECOG performance status.

**Detail:** *PIK3CA* alterations: HR 1.91 (95% CI 1.20–3.04; p=0.006) for OS. *ERBB2* alterations: HR 1.64 (95% CI 1.08–2.49; p=0.019) for OS. Both models adjusted for ³√VAF 75pc. CPE for PIK3CA and ERBB2 models: 0.63 each. *PIK3CA* detection rate was numerically higher in cfDNA (17%) vs. matched tumor (13%), suggesting cfDNA captures additional clones.

**Genes / cancer types:** `PIK3CA`, `ERBB2` · Metastatic urothelial carcinoma

**Data availability:** `partial`
> PIK3CA and ERBB2 alterations in archival tumor visible in blca_msk_2025. The cfDNA-specific alteration calls driving this finding are not separately loaded in cBioPortal.

**Tool support:** `native`

### F4: DDR gene alterations in cfDNA are not significantly associated with cisplatin response or survival

**Finding:** Oncogenic DDR gene alterations detected in pretreatment cfDNA (13% of patients) show no statistically significant association with chemotherapy response, OS, or PFS, though the analysis was underpowered.

**Detail:** Oncogenic DDR alterations detected in 27/201 patients (13%): ATM n=11 (5.5%), ERCC2 n=9 (4.5%), BRCA1 n=6 (3%), BRCA2 n=2 (1%). Response rate: 50% DDR+ vs. 41% DDR−, p=0.4. OS: HR 0.78 (95% CI 0.50–1.22; p=0.3). PFS: HR 0.77 (95% CI 0.48–1.22; p=0.3). Oncogenic ERCC2 rate in cfDNA (4.5%) was lower than TCGA (11%), limiting power.

**Genes / cancer types:** `ERCC2`, `ATM`, `BRCA1`, `BRCA2` · Metastatic urothelial carcinoma

**Data availability:** `partial`
> DDR gene mutations visible in blca_msk_2025 from archival tumor sequencing. cfDNA-specific DDR calls not separately accessible.

**Tool support:** `native`
> DDR gene query possible in blca_msk_2025 natively. Cross-referencing with response data requires clinical attribute filtering.

### F5: cfDNA detects more oncogenic alterations than matched archival tumor for key genes

**Finding:** In patients with paired cfDNA and archival tumor data (n=107), cfDNA detected numerically higher rates of oncogenic alterations in PIK3CA, RB1, ERCC2, BRAF, and KRAS compared with matched archival tumor.

**Detail:** Comparing cfDNA vs. matched tumor: PIK3CA 17% vs. 13%; RB1 14% vs. 12%; ERCC2 6.5% vs. 4.7%; BRAF 5.6% vs. 4.7%; KRAS 5.6% vs. 3.7%. Of DDR gene alterations, 57.1% detected in both, 21.4% tumor-only, 21.4% cfDNA-only, highlighting intratumoral/intertumoral heterogeneity. FGFR3 alterations detected in 19% of cfDNA, consistent with prior studies.

**Genes / cancer types:** `PIK3CA`, `RB1`, `ERCC2`, `BRAF`, `KRAS`, `FGFR3` · Metastatic urothelial carcinoma

**Data availability:** `partial`

**Tool support:** `api`

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Would DDR gene analysis reach significance with a larger cfDNA cohort or with tumor-informed ctDNA assays that improve sensitivity for ERCC2 detection?
- Does high baseline ctDNA VAF remain prognostic in the era of enfortumab vedotin + pembrolizumab first-line therapy, where the treatment landscape has shifted?
- Can ERBB2 alteration status in cfDNA predict benefit from HER2-directed ADCs (trastuzumab deruxtecan, disitamab vedotin) given the 33–83% response rates reported?
- Does TERT promoter alteration frequency differ between cfDNA and tumor tissue in mUC, and can it be used for clinical trial stratification?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Only 42% of CALGB 90601 patients had sufficient plasma for cfDNA; potential sampling bias (though baseline characteristics were similar)
- Half of patients received gemcitabine/cisplatin + bevacizumab; bevacizumab arm had no clinically meaningful OS difference but could theoretically confound biomarker associations
- MSK-ACCESS panel covers 129 genes; epigenomic and transcriptional features, and DDR genes outside panel coverage, not assessed
- DDR gene analysis was substantially underpowered (n=27 oncogenic DDR alterations, ERCC2 n=9)
- Archival tumor DNA (not always contemporaneous with cfDNA) used for paired comparison; may underestimate clonal evolution
