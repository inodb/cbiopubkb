## Summary

This TCGA study comprehensively profiled 173 pheochromocytomas and paragangliomas (PCC/PGLs) across six molecular platforms (WES, copy number, mRNA-seq, miRNA-seq, DNA methylation, RPPA). A driver mutation, fusion gene, or copy number alteration was identified in 95% of tumors, with pathogenic germline mutations found in 27% of patients across eight susceptibility genes. The study identified *CSDE1* as a novel somatic driver and *MAML3* fusion genes (e.g., *UBTF-MAML3*) as recurrent oncogenic events activating Wnt signaling. Integrated multi-platform analysis classified PCC/PGLs into four molecular subtypes — kinase signaling, pseudohypoxia, Wnt-altered, and cortical admixture — with the Wnt-altered subtype associated with poor clinical outcome and potential therapeutic vulnerabilities.

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: Germline mutations in susceptibility genes are prevalent
- **Finding**: Pathogenic germline mutations were detected in 46 of 173 patients (27%) across eight known PCC/PGL susceptibility genes.
- **Detail**: *SDHB* (9%), *RET* (6%), *VHL* (4%), and *NF1* (3%) were most frequently mutated; *SDHD*, *MAX*, *EGLN1* (PHD2), and *TMEM127* were each ≤2%. Driver alterations showed striking mutual exclusivity across the 21-gene set, indicating tumors typically carry at most one major driver.
- **Genes**: SDHB, RET, VHL, NF1, SDHD, MAX, EGLN1, TMEM127
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: germline susceptibility, mutual exclusivity, hereditary PCC/PGL

### Finding 2: CSDE1 is a novel somatic tumor suppressor driver
- **Finding**: *CSDE1* (formerly *UNR*) is a significantly mutated gene in PCC/PGL not previously described as a cancer driver.
- **Detail**: Four tumors harbored truncating *CSDE1* mutations (two frameshift, two splice-site), all clustering proximally within the gene. Multi-platform integration showed concurrent genomic deletion and underexpression consistent with loss-of-function. *CSDE1* mutant tumors clustered in the Wnt-altered expression subtype and showed expression profiles matching published *Csde1* knockout mouse data.
- **Genes**: CSDE1
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: tumor suppressor, Wnt signaling, somatic driver, loss-of-function

### Finding 3: Recurrent MAML3 fusion genes activate Wnt signaling and associate with poor prognosis
- **Finding**: Ten tumors harbored *MAML3* fusion genes (*UBTF-MAML3* or *TCF4-MAML3*), representing a gain-of-function event activating Wnt and Hedgehog signaling.
- **Detail**: *MAML3* fusions arise from chromosomal translocations involving 4q31.1 and 17q21.31 (*UBTF*) or 18q21.2 (*TCF4*). Fusion-positive tumors showed 2.7-fold *MAML3* overexpression, extensive DNA hypomethylation (4,229 differentially methylated probes), upregulation of Wnt pathway genes (*WNT4*, *WNT11*, *DVL3*, β-catenin), and underexpression of miR-375 (a negative regulator of Wnt member *FZD8*). NOTCH target genes were not recurrently overexpressed despite MAML3's canonical role as a NOTCH co-activator. The *MAML3* fusion was significantly associated with aggressive-disease-free-survival and metastatic-free-survival.
- **Genes**: MAML3, UBTF, TCF4, WNT4, WNT11, DVL3, FZD8, NKD1, GLI2
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: fusion gene, Wnt signaling, DNA methylation, prognosis, metastasis

### Finding 4: Four molecular subtypes classify the full spectrum of PCC/PGL
- **Finding**: Unsupervised mRNA expression clustering identified four reproducible subtypes: kinase signaling, pseudohypoxia, Wnt-altered, and cortical admixture.
- **Detail**: The **kinase signaling** subtype was enriched for *NF1*, *RET*, *TMEM127*, and *HRAS* mutations plus fusion genes (*NF1*, *BRAF*, *NGFR*) and MAPK/RAS pathway activation. The **pseudohypoxia** subtype contained *SDHB*, *SDHD*, *VHL*, and *EPAS1* mutations with genome doubling and hypermethylation driven by succinate/fumarate accumulation. The **Wnt-altered** subtype was defined by *MAML3* fusions and *CSDE1* mutations, with Wnt/Hedgehog overexpression and poor clinical outcome. The **cortical admixture** subtype showed adrenal cortex marker expression (*CYP11B1*, *CYP21A2*, *STAR*), reduced tumor purity, and *MAX* germline mutations. Subtypes were validated in an independent PCC/PGL cohort and were concordant across methylation, copy number, miRNA, and RPPA clustering.
- **Genes**: NF1, RET, HRAS, TMEM127, SDHB, SDHD, VHL, EPAS1, MAML3, CSDE1, MAX, BRAF, NGFR
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: molecular subtypes, expression clustering, multi-platform integration, classification

### Finding 5: Molecular markers of aggressive and metastatic disease
- **Finding**: Nine molecular features were significantly associated with aggressive-disease-free-survival, including several novel markers beyond the established *SDHB* and *ATRX* associations.
- **Detail**: Poor aggressive-disease-free-survival was associated with *MAML3* fusion gene, *SDHB* germline mutation, somatic *ATRX* or *SETD2* mutations, high somatic mutation burden, Wnt-altered or pseudohypoxia expression subtype, and hypermethylated subtype. Favorable outcome was associated with epinephrine/metanephrine positivity and the kinase signaling subtype. Ki-67 expression correlated with metastatic disease in a subset (n=62); the highest Ki-67 case was *MAML3* fusion-positive.
- **Genes**: MAML3, SDHB, ATRX, SETD2
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: api
- **Concepts**: prognosis, metastasis, biomarkers, aggressive disease, survival analysis

### Finding 6: Pathway-level view maps diverse drivers to four signaling axes
- **Finding**: Driver alterations across germline mutations, somatic mutations, and fusion genes converge on four key signaling pathways: kinase/MAPK, pseudohypoxia/HIF, Krebs cycle/epigenetics, and Wnt signaling.
- **Detail**: Kinase pathway: *NF1*, *HRAS*, *RET*, *BRAF*, *FGFR1*, *NGFR*, and PKA subunits (*PRKAR1A*, *PRKACA*). Pseudohypoxia: *VHL*, *ARNT* (HIF1β), *EPAS1* (HIF2α), *EGLN1* (PHD2). Krebs cycle/epigenetics: *SDHB*, *SDHD*, *IDH1* — succinate/fumarate accumulation inhibits 2-OG-dependent demethylases driving epigenetic silencing. Wnt: *MAML3*, *CSDE1*. *ATRX* mutations co-occurred with *SDHB* mutations suggesting synergistic tumorigenesis; FDA-approved therapies exist for *VHL*, *RET*, *BRAF*, *EPAS1*, and *FGFR1* alterations.
- **Genes**: NF1, HRAS, RET, BRAF, FGFR1, VHL, EPAS1, EGLN1, ARNT, SDHB, SDHD, IDH1, ATRX, MAML3, CSDE1
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: pathway analysis, actionability, targeted therapy, epigenetics, Krebs cycle

### Finding 7: Low somatic mutation rate with high driver mutual exclusivity
- **Finding**: PCC/PGLs have one of the lowest somatic mutation rates among cancer types (mean 0.67 mutations/Mb), yet a driver alteration is identified in 95% of tumors due to highly penetrant single-driver events.
- **Detail**: The low mutation rate and strong mutual exclusivity among the 21 key genes indicate that PCC/PGLs are typically driven by a single dominant event — either germline or somatic — making this tumor type a model for single-driver cancer biology and personalized therapy.
- **Genes**: HRAS, NF1, EPAS1, RET, CSDE1, SDHB, VHL, MAML3
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: tumor mutation burden, mutual exclusivity, driver landscape, precision medicine

## Open Questions

1. Can Wnt/β-catenin inhibitors (e.g., PRI-274) or STAT3 antagonists (BB1608) achieve clinical benefit in *MAML3* fusion-positive PCC/PGLs, and do upstream fusion partners (*UBTF* vs. *TCF4*) confer distinct therapeutic vulnerabilities?
2. Does *CSDE1* function as a tumor suppressor in other cancer types beyond PCC/PGL, and what is the molecular mechanism linking *CSDE1* loss to Wnt pathway activation?
3. Why do *MAML3* fusions activate Wnt and Hedgehog signaling rather than NOTCH, despite MAML3's canonical role as a NOTCH co-activator?
4. Do mastermind family fusion genes in other tumor types (e.g., *CRTC1-MAML2* in mucoepidermoid carcinoma, *PAX3-MAML3* in sinonasal sarcoma) share a common Wnt activation mechanism?

## Limitations

1. Head and neck paragangliomas were excluded because pre-surgical embolization causes tumor necrosis insufficient for molecular profiling, limiting generalizability to this anatomical subtype.
2. Distant metastatic events occurred in only 11 patients (6%), limiting statistical power for metastasis-specific analyses and potentially leaving rare markers undetected.
3. RPPA proteomics data were available for only 76 of 173 tumors, restricting the power of protein-level analyses.
4. As a cross-sectional resection cohort, the study cannot definitively resolve the temporal order of driver events (e.g., whether DNA translocation precedes amplification of *MAML3* fusion genes) or establish causal therapeutic relationships.
