## Summary

This paper presents the final, long-term analysis of both cohorts from the IMvigor210 phase II trial, with a median follow-up of 96.4 months in cohort 1 (untreated, cisplatin-ineligible; n=119) and 46.2 months in cohort 2 (platinum-pretreated; n=310). The primary analyses were previously reported ([pmid-27939400](pmid-27939400.md) for cohort 1; separate publication for cohort 2). At the final data cut (June 2023), the 5-year OS rate in cohort 1 was 21.6% overall and 27% in PD-L1-high (IC2/3) patients. In cohort 2, the 4-year OS was 15.3% overall and 30.3% in IC2/3 patients. Median DOR was 59.1 months (cohort 1) and 24.8 months (cohort 2), confirming durable responses in a subset of patients with no new safety signals.

## Key Findings

### F1: 5-year OS of 21.6% in first-line cisplatin-ineligible mUC with sustained ORR of 23.5%

**Finding:** At the final analysis (median follow-up 96.4 months), cohort 1 maintained an ORR of 23.5% (95% CI 16.2–32.2%) with a 5-year OS rate of 21.6% (95% CI 13.7–29.5%), confirming durable long-term benefit for a subset of patients.

**Detail:** CR rate was 10.1%. Median OS was 16.3 months (95% CI 10.4–24.5 months). Median DOR by independent review was 59.1 months (95% CI 37.5–93.8 months), demonstrating very long remission durations in responders. The 4-year OS rate was 29.0% overall. Treatment duration: median 3.4 months, but 21% of patients received atezolizumab for >1 year.

**Genes / cancer types:** PD-L1 (IC scoring), urothelial carcinoma

### F2: PD-L1 IC2/3 status enriches for durable long-term survivors in both cohorts

**Finding:** In cohort 1, the 5-year OS rate for IC2/3 patients was 27.0% (vs 15.8% IC0 and 22.6% IC1). In cohort 2, the 4-year OS for IC2/3 patients was 30.3% (vs 7.3% IC0 and 9.4% IC1), demonstrating markedly better long-term survival in PD-L1-high patients.

**Detail:** Cohort 2 IC2/3 median OS was 11.9 months vs 7.9 months overall, and the 4-year OS gap is substantial (30.3% vs 15.3%). In cohort 1, counterintuitively, IC0 patients had a numerically longer median OS (20.2 months) than IC2/3 (12.3 months), though the 5-year OS favored IC2/3 (27% vs 15.8%), reflecting heterogeneous early vs late outcomes. Median DOR in IC2/3 patients (cohort 1): 93.8 months (95% CI 11.1–NE).

**Genes / cancer types:** PD-L1 (VENTANA SP142), urothelial carcinoma (both cohorts)

### F3: Platinum-pretreated cohort (cohort 2): ORR 16.5%, durable responses in IC2/3 subgroup

**Finding:** Cohort 2 (n=310, platinum-pretreated) had ORR 16.5% per RECIST 1.1 (95% CI 12.5–21.1%) and 19.7% per mRECIST, with IC2/3 achieving 27.0% and 28.0% respectively, confirming clinically meaningful responses in PD-L1-high platinum-refractory disease.

**Detail:** Median OS was 7.9 months overall and 11.9 months in IC2/3. CR rate was 7.1% overall, 14% in IC2/3. Median DOR was 24.8 months (95% CI 13.8–30.4 months) overall and 29.7 months (95% CI 13.8–NE) in IC2/3. The 1-year OS rate was 36.9% overall and 49.9% in IC2/3. The majority (81.6%) of patients had died by final analysis.

**Genes / cancer types:** PD-L1 (IC scoring), urothelial carcinoma (platinum-pretreated)

### F4: No new safety signals after up to 8+ years of follow-up

**Finding:** Grade 3/4 treatment-related AEs occurred in 21.8% (cohort 1) and 18.7% (cohort 2) of patients; the most common AE was fatigue (33.6% and 31.0%). No new safety signals emerged with long-term observation.

**Detail:** Treatment-related AEs of any grade occurred in 74.8% (cohort 1) and 71.3% (cohort 2) of patients. Pruritus (13.4%/13.2%), diarrhea (12.6%/8.7%), and decreased appetite (10.1%/11.0%) were common. One treatment-related death (sepsis) occurred in cohort 1; no fatal AEs in cohort 2. Discontinuation due to AEs was 13.4% (cohort 1) and 6.8% (cohort 2). Long treatment durations (median 83 doses maximum) did not yield unexpected cumulative toxicity.

### F5: Rapid time-to-response (2.1 months median) but durable responses in both early and later lines

**Finding:** Median time to objective response was 2.1 months in both cohorts (cohort 1 IC subgroups: 2.0–2.5 months), while the median DOR in cohort 1 was 59.1 months and in cohort 2 was 24.8 months, representing very long durations relative to chemotherapy benchmarks.

**Detail:** Atezolizumab responses begin early but are highly sustained once achieved. In cohort 1, median DOR ranged from 40.8 months (IC0) to 93.8 months (IC2/3). In cohort 2, median DOR ranged from 18.6 months (IC0) to 29.7 months (IC2/3). This long DOR with short TTOR supports atezolizumab monotherapy value in patients with good early response.

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Given 30% 4-year OS in IC2/3 platinum-pretreated patients, can PD-L1 combined with TMB identify a curative-potential subset?
- How does the long-term OS of IMvigor210 cohort 1 (first-line monotherapy) compare with enfortumab vedotin + pembrolizumab combinations now becoming standard of care?
- Why does IC2/3 in cohort 1 show shorter median OS (12.3 months) but higher 5-year OS (27%) than IC0 (20.2 months median, 15.8% at 5 years)—what clinical/molecular factors drive early death vs. late cure in IC2/3?
- Can the RNA-seq-derived TCGA molecular subtypes (reported in primary analysis) be integrated with PD-L1 IC status to better define long-term survivors?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Single-arm design with no concurrent control; comparisons to chemotherapy benchmarks are historical
- Both cohorts are fully mature (final analysis); no further updates planned
- Biomarker analyses (TMB, TCGA subtype) reported in primary papers but not updated in this final analysis
- Cohort 2 PFS data cut-off is 2016; only OS updated to 2023 for this cohort
- Atezolizumab monotherapy is no longer a regulatory-approved first-line option for all cisplatin-ineligible patients in some jurisdictions following emergence of combination therapies (enfortumab vedotin + pembrolizumab)
- Non-MSK/European academic centers; external generalizability may differ from community practice populations
