## Summary

The Sherlock-Lung study characterized the genomic landscape of 232 treatment-naïve never-smoker lung cancers (LCINS) using high-coverage whole-genome sequencing (mean 85× tumor). Three distinct subtypes based on somatic copy number alterations were identified: *piano* (49.6%, minimal CNAs, KRAS-enriched, stem cell-like), *mezzo-forte* (30.2%, chr arm amplifications, EGFR-enriched), and *forte* (20.2%, whole-genome doubling). No tobacco mutational signatures were detected even with second-hand smoke exposure. RTK-RAS pathway alterations occur in 54.3% of tumors with strong mutual exclusivity; five genomic alterations independently doubled mortality. UBA1 is identified as a likely novel driver in the *piano* subtype. Germline AR variants implicate hormonal mechanisms in LCINS pathogenesis.

## Key Findings

### F1: Three CNA-based subtypes — *piano*, *mezzo-forte*, *forte* — with distinct genomic and biological profiles

**Finding:** Unsupervised clustering of arm-level somatic copy number alterations (SCNAs) identified three subtypes with increasing genomic complexity. *Piano* (49.6%) has minimal SCNAs, very low TMB (0.7 Mut/Mb), long telomeres, high subclonal mutation fraction, and stem cell-like properties suggesting slow clonal evolution. *Mezzo-forte* (30.2%) features specific arm-level amplifications (1q, 5p, 7p, 7q, 8q) and EGFR mutation enrichment. *Forte* (20.2%) is dominated by whole-genome doubling (WGD) and is most similar to LUAD from smokers.

**Detail:** 52.6% of LUAD from smokers (PCAWG cohort) fall into the *forte* cluster. TMB in *forte* (1.4 Mut/Mb) and *mezzo-forte* (1.6 Mut/Mb) is 2× higher than *piano* (p = 2.0×10⁻⁷). Carcinoids cluster almost exclusively in *piano* and have the lowest TMB (0.4 Mut/Mb). HLA-LOH — common in smoker LUAD (~40%) — was found in only 5.2% of LCINS, all LUAD, mostly in *forte* and *mezzo-forte*. TP53 mutations were most common in *forte* (31.9%) versus *piano* (7.0%). RET fusions were exclusively found in *piano* (2.6%).

**Genes / cancer types:** TP53, MDM2, EGFR, KRAS, RET, NKX2-1, SETD2 · LUAD, carcinoid

### F2: RTK-RAS pathway altered in 54.3% of tumors with strong mutual exclusivity across seven genes

**Finding:** Among RTK-RAS pathway genes, EGFR was most frequently altered (30.6%), followed by KRAS (7.3%), ALK (6.0%), MET (4.3%), ERBB2 (3.9%, all indels), ROS1 (2.6%), and RET (1.3%). A strong mutually exclusive distribution was observed across all seven genes. RTK-RAS+ tumors had significantly higher burden of SNVs/indels, SCNAs, SVs, kataegis, WGD, BRCA2 LOH, and lower telomere ratios versus RTK-RAS− tumors.

**Detail:** EGFR mutations dominated in *mezzo-forte* (51.4% vs. 9.0% in smoker LUAD), while KRAS was enriched in *piano* (76.5% of KRAS+ tumors). Oncogenic fusions (ALK, ROS1, RET, and others) were found in 24 (10.3%) tumors and were mutually exclusive with EGFR mutations (P = 1.1×10⁻⁴). The 49 tumors bearing both TP53 deficiency and RTK-RAS activating mutations had significantly higher TMB, kataegis, WGD, and BRCA1/BRCA2 LOH. Known driver fusion rearrangements were found in 10.3% of tumors, exclusively in RTK-RAS+ cases.

### F3: No tobacco mutational signatures detected; APOBEC (SBS2/13) dominates in 58% of samples

**Finding:** SigProfiler-based deconvolution of 232 LCINS identified 14 SBS signatures from COSMIC — none were tobacco-associated (SBS4 absent). APOBEC signatures SBS2 and SBS13 were the dominant contributors in 58% of samples (n=135, ≥100 SNVs attributed). SBS18 (reactive oxygen species damage) was present in 46% of samples, enriched in *forte* and *mezzo-forte* subtypes. SBS8 (nucleotide excision repair / late replication errors) was found in 13% overall but 30.3% of carcinoids.

**Detail:** APOBEC-attributed SNVs were mostly subclonal (median fold-change = 1.2), with substantial inter-tumor heterogeneity. Four hypermutated tumors (TMB > 8 Mut/Mb) had >80% APOBEC-dominant signatures. APOBEC enrichment was significant in TP53-deficient (P = 1.9×10⁻⁸) and RTK-RAS+ (P = 3.5×10⁻⁸) tumors. SBS8 was not found in LUAD from smokers (PCAWG cohort), suggesting a different etiology for never-smoker tumors. Six indel (ID1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 9) and four DBS (DBS2, 4, 9, 11) signatures were also identified.

### F4: UBA1 identified as a novel driver gene in the *piano* subtype

**Finding:** UBA1 — an E1 ubiquitin-conjugating enzyme that orchestrates the cellular DNA damage response — was identified as a likely new driver gene in LCINS, with 6 of 9 mutations occurring in the *piano* subtype. All 25 recurrently mutated genes exhibited signals of positive selection. UBA1 is one of the few genes with recurrent mutations in *piano* LUAD that lacks other known recurrent RTK-RAS or TP53 drivers.

**Detail:** *Piano* LUAD tumors rarely harbor known recurrent drivers, with the exception of NKX2-1 (n=4, exclusively *piano*), SETD2 (n=8/10 in *piano*), and UBA1. Carcinoids in *piano* were enriched with chromatin-remodeling gene mutations (e.g., ARID1A). Germline variants in known cancer susceptibility genes were rare but included BRCA1 (n=3, 2 in *piano*), ATM (n=2), and RAD51D (n=2). Rare predicted deleterious germline variants in CYP21A2 (n=8) and GLUD2 (n=6) were recurrently identified, and AR was the most frequently germline-mutated known cancer susceptibility gene (n=5, 4 in *piano*), implicating sex hormone pathways.

### F5: Five genomic alterations independently doubled mortality; RTK-RAS gene impacts on survival are distinct

**Finding:** Five genomic alterations independently doubled mortality in multivariate survival analysis. Genes within the RTK-RAS pathway had distinct and variable impacts on survival depending on which gene is affected, challenging uniform treatment approaches across RTK-RAS-altered LCINS. TP53-deficient tumors and RTK-RAS+ tumors with both alterations co-occurring had the worst genomic complexity profiles.

**Detail:** TMB was significantly associated with tumor stage, histology, and age at diagnosis but not tumor purity. Median TMB across all LCINS was 1.1 Mut/Mb — more than 7-fold lower than LUAD in smokers (P = 7.0×10⁻⁷³). TP53 mutations were mutually exclusive with MDM2 amplifications (P = 0.03), and both were associated with higher genomic instability. Structural variant hotspot regions included MDM2, TERT, 6p21, MYC, CDKN2A, NKX2-1, and GNAS. Non-clustered SVs were enriched in TP53-deficient and RTK-RAS+ tumors. Mitochondrial DNA copy numbers were higher than in smoker LUAD (P = 0.01).

**Genes / cancer types:** TP53, MDM2, CDKN2A, TERT, MYC, NKX2-1, EGFR, KRAS, ALK · LUAD

## Open Questions

- What drives the stem cell-like, slow-growth *piano* subtype in the absence of RTK-RAS drivers or TP53 loss? Is UBA1 a true oncogenic driver or permissive collaborator?
- Can the three SCNA subtypes guide therapeutic selection (e.g., EGFR inhibitors for *mezzo-forte*, immunotherapy vs. not for *forte*)?
- Do germline AR variants cause a genuine predisposition to *piano* LCINS via androgenic signaling, and what mechanism is involved?
- Why are APOBEC signatures predominantly subclonal in LCINS, and does this subclonality limit immune recognition and immunotherapy response?
- Can the 5 alterations doubling mortality be validated prospectively as actionable prognostic biomarkers in clinical trials for never smokers?

## Limitations

- Cohort is 97.4% European ancestry, limiting generalizability to Asian LCINS populations where EGFR mutations predominate
- No RNA-seq data for the full cohort; transcriptomic characterization is incomplete for non-EGFR, non-RTK-RAS tumors
- WGS data not integrated with protein-level expression (proteomics absent)
- Study restricted to never smokers with unknown exposures; tobacco-adjacent cases (second-hand smoke) not always separable
- The 5 alterations doubling mortality were identified in the same cohort used to discover them — prospective validation is needed
