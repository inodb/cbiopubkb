## Summary

As part of the ICGC PedBrain Tumor Project, this paper presents an integrative deep-sequencing analysis of 125 medulloblastoma tumor-normal pairs (WGS n=39, WES n=21, custom-capture n=65). Key findings include: tetraploidy as a frequent early event in Group 3 and 4 tumors (40–54%); a positive correlation between patient age and somatic mutation rate; identification of 8 significantly mutated genes by MutSig (CTNNB1, DDX3X, PTCH1, SMARCA4, MLL2, TP53, KDM6A, CTDNEP1) with strong subgroup-specificity; discovery of the first medulloblastoma fusion genes (SHH-DNAJB6 fusion, LCLAT1-ERBB4, MLLT6-MRPL45); and widespread chromatin modifier mutations across all subgroups (36% of cases). The study establishes the genomic landscape of medulloblastoma subgroups and identifies potential therapeutic targets, especially for Group 3 and 4 patients with dismal prognosis.

## Key Findings

### F1: Tetraploidy is a frequent early event in Group 3 and Group 4 medulloblastoma and drives genomic instability

**Finding:** Tetraploidy was identified in 40% of Group 4, 54% of Group 3, 29% of SHH, and 14% of WNT tumors. The near-25% somatic mutation allele frequency (instead of expected ~50%) identified tetraploid baseline genomes confirmed by FISH. Tetraploid tumors show significantly more large-scale copy-number alterations (median 10 vs. 4 per tumor, p=0.008).

**Detail:** Tetraploidy-derived allele frequencies (25% for heterozygous events) enabled genome-wide ploidy prediction from sequencing data (novel approach). FISH with centromeric probes for chromosomes 1 and 11 confirmed predicted ploidy in 17/18 tested cases. Some cases showed even higher polyploidy (ICGC_MB45: 4n with 4:0 and 3:1 allele ratios) before settling at ~4n baseline. Age–mutation rate correlation absent in tetraploid cases (r²=0.04, p=0.5) vs. diploid (r²=0.52, p=3×10⁻⁵), suggesting tetraploidy replaces replication-time-accumulation of mutations as the primary driver of genomic instability. Tetraploidy in Group 3/4 tumors motivates trials of mitotic checkpoint kinase or kinesin inhibitors (e.g., Eg5/KIF11 inhibitors).

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53` (tetraploid SHH tumors), copy-number landscape · Medulloblastoma (Group 3, Group 4, SHH)

### F2: Somatic mutation rate positively correlates with patient age and is significantly elevated in TP53-mutant SHH tumors

**Finding:** Genome-wide somatic mutation rate correlates with patient age (r²=0.35, p=7.8×10⁻⁵, Pearson). SHH tumors with TP53 mutations (including LFS germline cases) have ~2.5× higher mutation rates (mean 1.1/Mb vs. 0.43/Mb, p=4.5×10⁻⁶). Average mutation rate 0.52/Mb (WGS cohort) — lower than adult tumors, consistent with pediatric origin.

**Detail:** Mean 10.3 non-synonymous coding SNVs per case in Discovery cohort. WNT subgroup had the second-highest mutation rate after SHH-TP53 despite good prognosis and few copy-number changes. 77% of non-synonymous SNVs were unique to a single case (587/760), demonstrating extreme genetic heterogeneity. 25 singleton mutations and 53 total SNVs matched COSMIC, showing rare contributions from known cancer genes. The age-mutation correlation (stronger in diploid tumors) suggests some MB tumors originate during embryogenesis, requiring fewer genetic hits in tetraploid context.

### F3: Eight significantly mutated genes with strong subgroup specificity; chromatin modifiers altered in 36% of all cases

**Finding:** MutSig analysis identifies 8 significantly mutated genes: CTNNB1 (WNT, 15% overall; 100% of WNT tumors), DDX3X (8%, enriched in WNT), PTCH1 (6%, SHH), SMARCA4 (5%, Group 3), MLL2/KMT2D (5%), TP53 (4%, SHH-LFS), KDM6A/UTX (4%, Group 4), CTDNEP1 (3%, Group 3/4). Overall 36% of cases harbor chromatin modifier mutations.

**Detail:** CTNNB1 mutations co-occur with chromosome 6 loss in WNT tumors (13/15 cases). DDX3X helicase-domain mutations in WNT MB cluster at RNA-binding surface, predicting altered (not simply lost) function. SMARCA4 helicase-domain mutations in Group 3 are analogous to DDX3X. KDM6A (H3K27 demethylase; chromosome X, frequently lost in female Group 3/4) truncating mutations in 4/40 Group 4 cases indicate tumor suppressor role. CTDNEP1 truncating mutations in 4 tumors, 3 accompanied by 17p loss via isodicentric 17q — making it a strong candidate 17p tumor suppressor in MB. Chromatin modifiers span all subgroups: MLL2/KMT2D, KDM6A, BCOR (recurrent InDels), SMARCA4, EZH2-related pathway components.

### F4: Discovery of the first medulloblastoma fusion genes, including a SHH-DNAJB6 fusion and ERBB4-LCLAT1 fusion

**Finding:** RNA-seq and large-insert mate-pair sequencing identified novel in-frame gene fusions in medulloblastoma: a SHH-DNAJB6 fusion (N-terminally truncated SHH protein with extreme overexpression) in a TP53-mutant SHH tumor; LCLAT1-ERBB4 intrachromosomal fusion (ERBB4 previously linked to MB); and MLLT6-MRPL45 from complex chr1/17 rearrangements. RNA-seq confirmed all fusions as expressed transcripts.

**Detail:** ICGC_MB34 (SHH, somatic TP53 mutation): chr7 amplicon includes entire SHH gene and disrupts DNAJB6 such that its first exon is fused to SHH exons 2–3, skipping SHH exon 1. Predicted N-terminally truncated SHH protein. SHH expression was the highest of all 301 medulloblastomas tested. LCLAT1-ERBB4 fusion (ICGC_MB18): intrachromosomal translocation. MLLT6-MRPL45 (ICGC_MB6): complex chr1/17 rearrangement causing strong MRPL45 overexpression. Only 48% of DNA SNVs were observable at the RNA level (expressed); 38% resided in very lowly expressed genes (RPKM<1), suggesting many hits are not required for tumor maintenance after initiation.

### F5: TBR1 and EOMES define inversely correlated subpopulations within Group 4, suggesting distinct cell-of-origin lineages

**Finding:** RNA-seq revealed monoallelic expression of a TBR1 p.G275C mutation (also detected in a prior study) and confirmed TBR1 and EOMES as Group 4-specific markers with inverse expression correlation. Expression of each gene is tightly linked to its DNA methylation status, suggesting epigenetic mechanisms define Group 4 subpopulations.

**Detail:** TBR1 (T-box transcription factor; neuronal lineage commitment marker) and EOMES/TBR2 (earlier neuronal progenitor marker) are inversely expressed across Group 4 medulloblastomas (n=104): tumors are either TBR1-high/EOMES-low or EOMES-high/TBR1-low, with methylation regulating expression. TBR1 p.G275C validated in 1/85 additional sequenced cases (Group 4). These markers reflect different stages of neuronal differentiation, suggesting Group 4 MB may arise from distinct cell-of-origin populations or represent distinct differentiation states. This finding prefigures later molecular subdivision of Group 4 MB.

## Open Questions

- Does tetraploidy in Group 3/4 medulloblastoma confer sensitivity to spindle assembly checkpoint (SAC) inhibitors or kinesin (Eg5/KIF11) inhibitors — and can this be tested in the `mbl_icgc` or subsequent cohorts?
- What is the functional consequence of helicase-domain DDX3X mutations in WNT MB — do they create gain-of-function oncogenic activity or act as dominant-negatives?
- Does CTDNEP1 loss (with 17p loss via isodicentric 17q) functionally cooperate with MYC amplification in Group 3 MB to drive the most aggressive phenotype?
- Can TBR1-high vs. EOMES-high Group 4 MB be further distinguished clinically (age at diagnosis, survival, chemotherapy response) with larger cohorts?
- Do the SHH-DNAJB6 and ERBB4-LCLAT1 fusions create druggable dependencies (e.g., SMO inhibitor for SHH fusion, HER4/ERBB4-directed therapy)?

## Limitations

- Mixed sequencing platform cohort (WGS, WES, custom-capture) complicates direct comparison of mutation rates across cases.
- Small numbers in WGS discovery set (n=39); power to detect low-frequency subgroup-specific events is limited.
- Pediatric cohort (0–17 years) only; adult medulloblastoma may have distinct genomic features.
- No RNA-seq on the full cohort (only n=28); fusion gene discovery is incomplete.
- CTDNEP1 identified as candidate 17p tumor suppressor but functional validation not presented.
- EGA data access restrictions limit clinical integration of raw sequencing data outside this study.
- Subgroup classification based on gene expression or IHC; some replication cases had uncertain subgroup assignment (labeled ND).
