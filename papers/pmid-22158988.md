## Summary

First whole-exome sequencing study of the four major neoplastic cyst types of the pancreas — serous cystadenomas (SCA), intraductal papillary mucinous neoplasms (IPMN), mucinous cystic neoplasms (MCN), and solid pseudopapillary neoplasms (SPN) — using microdissected neoplastic epithelium from 32 surgically resected cysts (8 per type). The unifying discovery is that all four cyst types are driven by alterations in genes encoding components of ubiquitin ligase complexes (SCAs via VHL, IPMNs and MCNs via RNF43) or proteins that resist ubiquitin-mediated degradation (SPNs via CTNNB1). The study also reveals that just five genes (VHL, RNF43, CTNNB1, GNAS, KRAS) can distinguish among cyst types with high accuracy, establishing a molecular framework for noninvasive cyst-fluid-based diagnosis. RNF43, first identified here as recurrently mutated in human cancer, is firmly established as a tumor suppressor in pancreatic mucinous cysts.

## Key Findings

### F1: VHL is the defining mutated gene in serous cystadenomas — four mutations detected, all identical to germline VHL syndrome alleles

**Finding:** Four of 8 SCAs harbored somatic inactivating mutations in VHL, the 3p tumor suppressor gene responsible for von Hippel–Lindau syndrome; an additional four SCAs showed LOH at the VHL locus without detectable point mutations, suggesting alternative inactivation. All four VHL mutations were identical to those found in renal cell carcinoma or germline VHL syndrome patients, confirming their inactivating nature.

**Detail:** Mutations: N78S (SCA 23), W117L (SCA 35), C162W (SCA 38), S80R (SCA 40). Three had concurrent 3p LOH, supporting classic two-hit tumor suppressor inactivation. The four SCAs without mutations still showed 3p loss adjacent to VHL, suggesting epigenetic silencing or structural rearrangements. VHL regulates HIFα ubiquitination; loss leads to stabilization of HIF-1α, driving the distinctive angiogenic architecture of SCAs. Importantly, VHL mutations were detectable in cyst fluid DNA in 50% (9/18) of tested SCA fluids — opening a non-invasive diagnostic avenue.

### F2: RNF43 is a novel cancer tumor suppressor, inactivated by predominantly truncating mutations in 6/8 IPMNs and 3/8 MCNs

**Finding:** RNF43, an E3 ubiquitin ligase gene on chromosome 17q, is identified for the first time in any human cancer as a recurrently mutated tumor suppressor. It harbors predominantly inactivating mutations (5 of 6 mutations in IPMNs were nonsense substitutions), and 4 of 6 IPMN-mutant tumors also showed chromosome 17q LOH at the RNF43 locus — classic biallelic inactivation. Three MCNs also harbored RNF43 mutations (2 nonsense, 1 missense), making RNF43 the only gene mutated in both mucinous cyst types.

**Detail:** The probability of RNF43 being a passenger gene is <10⁻¹², by binomial test. RNF43 encodes a RING-domain E3 ubiquitin ligase that had been shown to interact with p53-regulatory protein complexes. Its tumor suppressor role in pancreatic ductal epithelium is confirmed by the preponderance of loss-of-function alleles. Subsequent studies showed that RNF43 mutations confer sensitivity to porcupine inhibitors (Wnt pathway). In contrast, GNAS (hotspot codon 201, 5/8 IPMNs) and KRAS (codon 12, 5/8 IPMNs and 6/8 MCNs) were the other frequent drivers in mucinous cysts.

### F3: Solid pseudopapillary neoplasms have remarkably few mutations (avg 2.9 per tumor) but universal CTNNB1 mutations — the lowest mutational burden of any sequenced adult cancer at the time

**Finding:** All 8 SPNs harbored CTNNB1 missense mutations — always at codons 32, 33, 34, or 37 — representing the phosphorylation-regulatory hotspot where β-catenin must be phosphorylated to be ubiquitinated and degraded. Only one of the eight SPNs had any LOH whatsoever. The average of 2.9±2.1 nonsynonymous mutations per SPN was the lowest reported for any adult solid tumor at that time, suggesting SPNs may arise from a single driver event.

**Detail:** β-catenin (CTNNB1) normally undergoes phosphorylation at serine/threonine residues in codons 32–37 by GSK3β, allowing VHL/ubiquitin complexes to mark it for degradation. Mutations at these codons prevent phosphorylation and consequent degradation, causing constitutive WNT/β-catenin activation. The mutant allele frequency was 39–59%, confirming high neoplastic cellularity. The paucity of other alterations challenges the multi-hit model of solid tumor formation in adults, unless translocations or epigenetic changes account for additional hits not detectable by exome sequencing.

### F4: Five-gene molecular diagnostic panel (VHL, RNF43, CTNNB1, GNAS, KRAS) can classify all four cyst types with high accuracy from cyst fluid

**Finding:** The study demonstrates that mutations in just five genes — VHL, RNF43, CTNNB1, GNAS, and KRAS — are essentially mutually exclusive across cyst types and together provide a near-perfect molecular classifier: SCAs = VHL or 3p LOH only; IPMNs = RNF43 + GNAS/KRAS; MCNs = KRAS/RNF43, no GNAS or CTNNB1 or VHL; SPNs = CTNNB1 only. This creates a clinically actionable molecular framework since cyst fluid is obtainable by endoscopic fine-needle aspiration without surgery.

**Detail:** All 8 SCAs had VHL mutation or 3p LOH but never RNF43, GNAS, KRAS, or CTNNB1 mutations. All 8 IPMNs had RNF43, GNAS, or KRAS alterations and never VHL or CTNNB1. MCNs always had KRAS or RNF43 but never GNAS, CTNNB1, or VHL. SPNs had only CTNNB1 and none of the others. VHL was detectable in 50% of SCA cyst fluids by targeted capture, validating the liquid approach. The clinical implication is powerful: 1 in 3 pre-operative diagnoses is incorrect, and erroneous surgical resection of asymptomatic SCAs is a significant problem that molecular testing could prevent.

### F5: Mutational burden scales with malignant potential across cyst types: SPNs < SCAs < MCNs < IPMNs < invasive PDAs

**Finding:** The average number of somatic nonsynonymous mutations per cyst type correlates with their known malignant potential: SPNs (2.9 ± 2.1, low-grade malignant but curable), SCAs (10 ± 4.6, never malignant), MCNs (16 ± 7.6, can progress), IPMNs (27 ± 12, can progress to PDA), and previously reported PDAs (~48). This progression spectrum provides biological context for understanding cyst-to-cancer evolution.

**Detail:** The differences are all statistically significant (P < 0.001 for any pairwise comparison). The mutation numbers also help resolve the paradox that SPNs — despite being designated "low-grade malignant" — have fewer mutations than SCAs which are truly benign: this is because SPN malignancy may be driven by the single CTNNB1 event enabling metastasis via non-mutation pathways rather than progressive accumulation. For IPMNs, the ~27 mutations per tumor is roughly half of PDA (48 ± 23), consistent with IPMN being a precursor state on the path to invasive cancer.

## Open Questions

- Can the five-gene cyst fluid panel (VHL, RNF43, CTNNB1, GNAS, KRAS) detect mutations from the usually acellular cyst fluid with sufficient sensitivity in a prospective clinical setting?
- Do additional genetic alterations in GNAS, RNF43, KRAS, or TP53 within IPMNs or MCNs predict progression to invasive PDA?
- What proteins does RNF43 ubiquitinate in vivo, and how does this relate to its tumor suppressor function in pancreatic ductal cells?
- Why does VHL inactivation produce SCAs in the pancreas but renal cell carcinoma in the kidney — what tissue-specific factors determine neoplasm type?
- Can SPNs arise from a single CTNNB1 mutation, or do epigenetic events or structural rearrangements represent the required "second hit"?
- What is the clinical significance of RNF43 mutation type (truncating vs. missense) for progression risk in IPMNs and MCNs?

## Limitations

- Small cohort (n=8 per cyst type); low power for rare mutation discovery and precise frequency estimates
- High-grade IPMNs only; findings may not generalize to low-grade IPMNs with distinct biological behavior
- Exome sequencing does not detect promoter mutations, large structural rearrangements, or epigenetic silencing — four SCAs without VHL mutations likely have non-coding or structural VHL inactivation not captured
- Cyst fluid VHL detection was performed in only 18 SCA fluid samples — diagnostic validation in larger prospective cohorts needed
- Study predates RNA-seq; gene fusions and splice-site mutations beyond those detectable by exome sequencing were not assessed
- SPN paucity of mutations may reflect microdissection bias or tumor cell heterogeneity; single-cell approaches could test whether SPNs have truly simple genomes
- Mixed or high-grade lesions (e.g., IPMN with associated carcinoma) may differ genomically from the resected specimens studied
