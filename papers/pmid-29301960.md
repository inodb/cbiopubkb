## Summary

This landmark study performed whole exome sequencing (WES) on pre-treatment tumors from 35 patients with metastatic clear cell renal cell carcinoma (ccRCC) treated with anti-PD-1 monotherapy (nivolumab) as part of a prospective clinical trial (discovery cohort), validated in an independent cohort of 63 ccRCC patients treated with anti-PD-(L)1 ± anti-CTLA-4 therapies. The study identifies loss-of-function (LOF) mutations in *PBRM1* — encoding a subunit of the PBAF SWI/SNF chromatin remodeling complex — as the first genomic predictor of ICI benefit in ccRCC (discovery p=0.012; validation p=0.0071). Unlike in TMB-driven cancers, ccRCC has low TMB (~1.1 mutations/Mb) and its ICI responsiveness appears mediated by altered transcriptional output (JAK/STAT, hypoxia, and immune signaling pathways) following PBRM1 loss. Data are available in cBioPortal as `ccrcc_dfci_2019`.

## Key Findings

### F1: PBRM1 loss-of-function mutations are the dominant genomic correlate of ICI benefit in ccRCC

**Finding:** Truncating (LOF) mutations in *PBRM1* are significantly enriched in tumors from ccRCC patients with clinical benefit from anti-PD-1 therapy in both discovery (9/11 CB vs. 3/13 NCB; p=0.012, OR=12.93) and validation cohorts (17/27 CB vs. 4/19 NCB; p=0.0071, OR=6.10).

**Detail:** All truncating *PBRM1* alterations in the discovery cohort co-occurred with 3p deletion (nearly ubiquitous in ccRCC), resulting in complete biallelic LOF. Most mutations were predicted to be clonal. *PBRM1* LOF occurs in up to 41% of all ccRCC tumors. Notably, one NCB patient with *PBRM1* LOF also had a *B2M* alteration (disrupting antigen presentation), providing a mechanistic explanation for ICI resistance despite PBRM1 loss.

**Genes / cancer types:** `PBRM1`, `B2M` · Clear cell renal cell carcinoma (ccRCC)

### F2: PBRM1 loss is associated with improved OS and PFS on anti-PD-1 therapy

**Finding:** Patients with biallelic *PBRM1* loss had significantly prolonged overall survival (OS) (log-rank p=0.0074) and progression-free survival (PFS) (p=0.029) compared to PBRM1-intact patients, and showed sustained tumor burden reductions.

**Detail:** Spider plots of tumor burden change demonstrate consistent and durable tumor shrinkage in PBRM1-LOF patients. The association was not explained by differences in baseline clinical features (age, gender, baseline PD-L1 IHC, nivolumab dose). Three NCB patients with truncating PBRM1 mutations had long and/or censored OS, suggesting that factors beyond PBRM1 status (e.g., B2M loss, other resistance mechanisms) can override ICI sensitization.

### F3: Tumor mutational burden does not predict ICI response in ccRCC

**Finding:** Unlike in melanoma or NSCLC, ccRCC has low TMB (median ~82 nonsynonymous mutations per exome, ~1.1 mutations/Mb) and TMB does not distinguish CB from NCB patients. Intratumoral heterogeneity (subclonal fraction) is also similar between response groups.

**Detail:** This finding establishes ccRCC as an immunotherapy-responsive tumor type despite low TMB — a paradigm distinct from TMB-driven ICI sensitivity in melanoma/NSCLC. The mechanism appears to involve PBRM1-mediated alteration of the tumor cell transcriptome (immune pathway expression) rather than high neoantigen load. Antigen presentation machinery alterations (HLA, B2M) are uncommon and present in both CB and NCB tumors.

**Genes / cancer types:** Pan-gene · ccRCC

**Data availability:** `available`

### F4: PBRM1 loss alters JAK/STAT, hypoxia, and immune signaling transcriptional programs in ccRCC

**Finding:** Gene expression analysis of PBRM1-deficient ccRCC cell lines and PBRM1-deficient primary tumors reveals altered transcriptional output in JAK/STAT, hypoxia-response, and immune signaling pathways — providing a mechanistic basis for ICI sensitization independent of TMB.

**Detail:** PBAF (PBRM1-containing SWI/SNF complex) regulates chromatin accessibility at promoters of immune and hypoxia response genes. Loss of PBRM1 may alter the tumor cell's baseline immune signaling milieu, making tumors intrinsically more recognizable by the immune system. This mechanism distinguishes ccRCC from mutation-burden-driven ICI sensitivity and suggests epigenetic reprogramming as a mediator of immune response.

**Genes / cancer types:** `PBRM1`, `VHL`, `SETD2`, `BAP1` · ccRCC

**Data availability:** `not-available`
> Gene expression data from PBRM1-deficient cell lines not deposited in cBioPortal. TCGA ccRCC expression data (kirc_tcga) can be cross-referenced for PBRM1 expression correlates but was not part of this study's clinical cohort.

### F5: Recurrently mutated ccRCC genes (VHL, PBRM1, BAP1, SETD2, MTOR) define the broader genomic landscape; only PBRM1 predicts ICI benefit

**Finding:** MutSig2CV analysis identifies seven recurrently mutated genes in the discovery cohort (including VHL, PBRM1, BAP1, SETD2, MTOR, KDM5C). Only PBRM1 shows enrichment of LOF mutations in ICI responders; other driver genes (VHL, BAP1, SETD2) do not predict clinical benefit.

**Detail:** VHL is mutated in ~90% of ccRCC and thus provides no discriminatory power for ICI response. BAP1 mutations were more frequent in NCB patients (trend only, not significant). The specificity of PBRM1 as a predictor — despite being one of many SWI/SNF subunit genes altered in ccRCC — suggests a PBAF-specific (not pan-SWI/SNF) mechanism, as BAP1 is part of a different complex (PR-DUB). SETD2 and MTOR pathway mutations showed no significant associations.

**Genes / cancer types:** `VHL`, `PBRM1`, `BAP1`, `SETD2`, `MTOR`, `KDM5C` · ccRCC

**Data availability:** `available`

## Open Questions

- Do PBRM1 LOF mutations predict ICI benefit in ccRCC patients treated with combination ICI + VEGF-targeted therapies (e.g., pembrolizumab + axitinib)?
- What is the mechanistic basis for the PBRM1-specific (vs. BAP1 or SETD2) ICI sensitization — which target genes or chromatin loci are PBAF-specifically regulated?
- Can PBRM1 LOF be detected by liquid biopsy (cfDNA) to guide therapy selection in ccRCC without fresh tissue biopsy?
- Do ccRCC patients with concurrent PBRM1 LOF and BAP1 mutations have differential ICI outcomes?
- Is PBRM1 ICI predictive biomarker value validated in prospective randomized ccRCC ICI trials (CheckMate 214, KEYNOTE-426)?

## Limitations

- Discovery cohort is small (N=35); while validated in 63 patients, combined N=98 limits power for secondary gene associations
- Validation cohort includes a mix of anti-PD-1 and anti-PD-L1 ± anti-CTLA-4 treatments, introducing therapeutic heterogeneity
- Gene expression analyses performed in cell lines and external TCGA data, not in the ICI clinical cohort itself
- PBRM1 LOF does not guarantee ICI benefit (3 of 11 CB patients lacked PBRM1 LOF; 4 of 19 NCB had PBRM1 LOF)
- B2M and antigen presentation alterations not systematically assessed in validation cohort
- Transcriptomic and proteomic profiling of the immune microenvironment not available for the clinical cohort
