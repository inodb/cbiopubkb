## Summary

Single-nucleus RNA sequencing (snRNA-seq) of six MYOD1^L122R^-mutant spindle cell rhabdomyosarcoma (SRMS) tumors — the largest single-cell dataset for this ultrarare subtype — combined with regulatory network analysis (metaVIPER/ARACNe), bulk RNA-seq (n=24), and targeted DNA sequencing (n=20, MSK-IMPACT). Three coexisting tumor cell states are defined: a progenitor-like state enriched for MYOD1 activity, a proliferative transition state, and a partially differentiated state. A paracrine IGF2→IGF1R→PI3K/AKT/mTOR axis from progenitor to transition/differentiated states is identified as a conserved oncogenic dependency. IGF1R and PI3K/mTOR inhibitors show selective sensitivity in patient-derived xenograft (PDX) cultures and organoids, and PI3K/mTOR inhibition produces meaningful disease control in vivo. Recurrent IGF2/PI3K/AKT genomic alterations (including PIK3CA hotspot mutations in 30–40% of cases) reinforce the therapeutic hypothesis. This paper establishes a regulatory network–based framework for uncovering actionable targets in transcriptionally complex rare cancers.

## Key Findings

### F1: Three coexisting tumor cell states reflect MYOD1-driven disruption of myogenic differentiation

**Finding:** snRNA-seq with metaVIPER protein activity inference resolves MYOD1^L122R^-mutant SRMS into three cell states present in virtually every tumor: (i) a progenitor-like state enriched for MYOD1 activity, (ii) a proliferative transition state, and (iii) a partially differentiated state with reduced MYOD1 activity. The same architecture is conserved in patient-derived xenografts, confirming its tumor-intrinsic origin rather than sampling variation.

**Detail:** InferCNV confirmed tumor vs. non-tumor identity in 177,051 malignant nuclei (92% of total dataset). State assignments were driven by protein activity profiles (metaVIPER on ARACNe-derived single-cell regulatory networks), not solely gene expression, enabling detection of regulators with low transcript abundance. MYOD1 activity — not just transcript — is highest in the progenitor state and declines toward the differentiated state, consistent with the known role of MYOD1^L122R^ in arresting myogenic maturation. Bulk RNA-seq in 24 additional samples validated MR activity patterns identified from single-cell data.

**Genes / cancer types:** `MYOD1`, `MYOG` · Spindle cell/sclerosing rhabdomyosarcoma (SRMS)

### F2: Paracrine IGF2→IGF1R→PI3K/AKT/mTOR signaling axis sustains transition and differentiated tumor cell states

**Finding:** Ligand-receptor analysis identifies a paracrine axis in which IGF2 secreted by progenitor-state cells signals through IGF1R on transition and differentiated-state cells, activating PI3K/AKT/mTOR. This axis is conserved across patient tumors and PDX models, and is pharmacologically actionable.

**Detail:** IGF2 is among the most differentially expressed ligands in the progenitor state; IGF1R is highly expressed in transition and differentiated states. Inhibition of this axis using IGF1R inhibitors and PI3K/AKT/mTOR inhibitors showed selective sensitivity in ex vivo drug screens of PDX cell cultures and organoids. In vivo testing of a PI3K/mTOR dual inhibitor achieved meaningful disease control; combination with chemotherapy produced objective tumor regression in a PDX model. Genomic data (MSK-IMPACT, n=20) revealed recurrent IGF2/PI3K/AKT pathway alterations including PIK3CA hotspot mutations in ~30–40% of cases, reinforcing transcriptional dependencies with genomic evidence.

### F3: MYOD1^L122R^ VAF >60% reflects homozygosity or aneuploidy, with heterogeneous co-mutations

**Finding:** Among four MSK-IMPACT–profiled cases, MYOD1^L122R^ variant allele frequency (VAF) exceeds 60%, consistent with either homozygosity of the mutant allele or increased mutant allele burden due to chromosome 11p aneuploidy. Co-occurring genomic alterations are heterogeneous across patients, highlighting a shared transcriptional dependency not captured by genetics alone.

**Detail:** MSK-IMPACT (341–505 gene panel) was applied to four tumors with matched normals. The high MYOD1 VAF was confirmed in all four and contextualized by copy-number analysis. PIK3CA hotspot mutations represent the most recurrent co-mutation (~30–40% of MYOD1-mutant SRMS in prior reports). The absence of actionable mutations in many tumors and variable co-mutation landscape underscored why transcriptomics-first (not genomics-first) target discovery is preferable in this disease.

### F4: Cell state–specific master regulators nominate additional combination therapy targets

**Finding:** Beyond the IGF2-PI3K axis, each cell state harbors distinct master regulator (MR) proteins — identified by metaVIPER as top differentially activated transcriptional regulators — that represent non-oncogene dependencies for rational combination therapy.

**Detail:** MR proteins are enriched in tumor-essential genes and synthetic lethality candidates, per prior literature validating VIPER-based target discovery. MRs associated with myogenic differentiation and cytoskeletal organization were among the most activated. Cell state–specific MR activation patterns were recapitulated in the 24-sample bulk RNA-seq cohort, validating the single-cell findings at scale. Specific MR targets are nominated as candidates for pharmacological disruption in combination with PI3K/mTOR inhibitors.

**Genes / cancer types:** `MYOD1`, `MYOG` and cell state–specific MR targets · Spindle cell/sclerosing rhabdomyosarcoma (SRMS)

### F5: Chemotherapy resistance is near-universal; pathologic response correlates with cytodifferentiation and survival

**Finding:** Four of six patients showed minimal/no histologic response to neoadjuvant VAC chemotherapy and subsequently died of disease. Two patients with partial responses — including therapy-induced cytodifferentiation and MYOD1 expression loss — remain alive with no evidence of disease. This establishes chemotherapy-resistant SRMS biology and supports the urgency of IGF1R/PI3K-targeted strategies.

**Detail:** All six patients received neoadjuvant vincristine, actinomycin-D, cyclophosphamide (VAC) +/− radiation. Post-treatment specimens from four non-responders contained viable hyperchromatic spindle cells with minimal treatment effect. Immunohistochemistry showed diffuse/strong MYOD1 in all samples, while myogenin was rare/negative — consistent with MYOD1^L122R^-driven maturation arrest. The two responding patients had smaller tumors (2.9 cm and 9.2 cm) vs. non-responders (8.5–24 cm).

## Open Questions

- Do specific co-mutations (e.g., PIK3CA hotspot) predict differential response to IGF1R vs. PI3K/mTOR inhibitors among MYOD1-mutant SRMS patients?
- Can the three tumor cell states be distinguished by non-invasive biomarkers (e.g., plasma proteomics, circulating tumor cells) to guide real-time therapy selection?
- Does the progenitor cell state drive chemotherapy resistance specifically, and would targeting progenitor-state MRs enhance VAC sensitivity?
- What is the developmental origin of MYOD1^L122R^-mutant SRMS — does the progenitor state reflect a blocked normal myogenic progenitor or a de-differentiated mature cell?
- Are the same IGF2-PI3K/AKT/mTOR dependencies present in MYOD1-mutant SRMS arising in adults vs. children (wide age range: 7–76 years)?
- Can the MR-based regulatory network framework (ARACNe/VIPER) be standardized for clinical use in rare sarcomas beyond SRMS?

## Limitations

- Very small cohort (n=6 snRNA-seq, n=24 bulk RNA-seq, n=20 DNA); rare disease limits sample size
- All snRNA-seq tumors were obtained post-neoadjuvant chemotherapy; pre-treatment single-cell transcriptomics unavailable for comparison
- PDX models derived from a subset of cases; in vivo drug testing done in a single PDX model
- The VIPER/metaVIPER approach infers protein activity from RNA-based regulons; direct proteomics not performed
- Clinical follow-up is limited; long-term survival data from IGF1R/PI3K inhibitor therapy not yet available
- MSK-IMPACT targeted panel (n=20) does not capture the full genomic landscape; WGS/WES not performed on the snRNA-seq cohort
- Bulk RNA-seq validation cohort (n=24) was not independently prospectively collected
