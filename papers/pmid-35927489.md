## Summary

This study assembles the most comprehensive molecular atlas of chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL) to date, integrating genomic, transcriptomic, and epigenomic data from 1,148 patients across six institutional cohorts (WES/WGS n=1,074; RNA-seq n=712; DNA methylation n=999). The analysis identifies 202 candidate genetic drivers (109 novel), doubles the known CLL driver gene catalog, and refines the biology of the two IGHV-defined subtypes (M-CLL: mutated; U-CLL: unmutated). Novel gene expression clusters (ECs) further subcategorize CLL, are independent prognostic factors, and add value beyond IGHV status — discordant M-CLL cases (M-CLL in EC-u clusters) have median FFS of 5.3 years vs. 10.7 years for concordant M-CLL. An integrated multivariable prognostic model incorporating IGHV subtype, genetic alterations, epigenetic subtype (epitype), proliferative history (epiCMIT), and expression clusters achieves the most refined outcome stratification yet described for CLL.

## Key Findings

### F1: 202 candidate CLL driver genes identified — 109 novel; 24.2% of patients carry a novel putative driver

**Finding:** Analysis of 984 WES samples using MutSig2CV identified 82 putative CLL driver genes from recurrent sSNV/indels (37 novel), plus 6 additional via CLUMPS (3D protein structure clustering): MAP2K2, DIS3, and DICER1. Including 120 recurrent sCNA drivers (5 novel gains, 30 novel losses) yields 202 total candidate drivers — doubling the previously identified CLL driver catalog. 24.2% of patients have at least one mutation in a newly discovered driver; 4% carry a novel driver as their only sSNV/indel driver alteration.

**Detail:** Novel drivers include MAP2K2 (kinase domain mutations activating ERK, similar to MAP2K1), DIS3 (RNA exosome catalytic subunit, mutated at cancer hotspots D479/D488), DICER1, INO80 (chromatin remodeling, catalytic subunit), and RFX7 (tumor suppressor implicated in Burkitt lymphoma and DLBCL). 7/37 novel drivers (18.9%) have mutations clustered in functional domains, and 7 (18.9%) have roles in other mature B cell malignancies, supporting their driver status. Novel pathways highlighted include protein synthesis/stability and cytoskeletal/extracellular matrix regulation — not previously implicated by known CLL drivers. A novel 5q32 deletion (11.9%) encompasses RPS14 and TCOF1 (ribosome/genomic integrity), overlapping the common deleted region in MDS 5q- syndrome.

### F2: M-CLL and U-CLL are molecularly distinct entities with different leukemogenic trajectories

**Finding:** Despite similar coding mutation burdens (1.14 vs. 1.11 mutations/Mb, p=0.98), U-CLL has 2.16× more significant putative driver genes than M-CLL (54 vs. 25, p=0.0015) and more sCNA drivers. Timing analysis shows divergent clonal trajectories: BRAF mutation is early in M-CLL but late in U-CLL (q<0.1); MYD88 is early in M-CLL; chr20p loss and FUBP1 alterations are early in U-CLL. BCL2 translocations (via aberrant V(D)J recombination) characterize M-CLL, while chr14 deletions (via CSR, deleting DICER1, TRAF3, ZFP36L1) characterize U-CLL.

**Detail:** 90 of 141 (79.7%) WGS patients have ≥1 SV; ~46% of SVs are clonal, supporting early driver roles. Trisomy 12 is a shared early event; TP53 and NOTCH1 mutations are intermediate in both subtypes. Mutational signature analysis identifies AID-related signatures (SBS84/85) differentially enriched by subtype: SBS85 is higher in M-CLL (p=1.6×10⁻⁹) and SBS84 clusters are enriched in U-CLL. Genome-wide mean clonal mutation count is higher in M-CLL (12.6 vs. 9.6, p=6×10⁻¹⁴), suggesting prolonged AID activity during germinal center experience in M-CLL. Driverless cases are almost exclusively M-CLL, suggesting alternative non-genetic mechanisms (epigenetic?) of leukemogenesis.

### F3: Novel gene expression clusters (ECs) independently predict CLL outcome and refine IGHV classification

**Finding:** Unsupervised clustering of RNA-seq from 712 patients defines gene expression clusters (ECs) that are independent prognostic factors for FFS (p<0.001) and OS (p=0.007) after adjusting for IGHV status in multivariable analysis. Critically, 47 cases with discordant IGHV status and EC show EC-driven outcome: discordant M-CLLs (M-CLL in EC-u clusters) have median FFS of 5.3 vs. 10.7 years for concordant M-CLLs (p=0.031); discordant U-CLLs (U-CLL in EC-m clusters) have longer FFS than concordant U-CLLs (p=0.0024).

**Detail:** ECs are stable over years in most patients (p<10⁻⁶ by permutation in 19 longitudinally sampled patients), with occasional EC shifts potentially reflecting clonal evolution. An EC classifier built on 26 genes achieves ~80% overall accuracy (near-perfect PPV for EC-m3 and EC-i at ~85% recall). ECs were validated in an external CLL cohort (n=136) with comparable EC distributions. EC-i (intermediate) was associated with adverse FFS in the multivariable model; EC-m3 and EC-m4 were protective. CHD2 alterations were identified as potentially contributing to EC discordance in M-CLL — linking chromatin remodeling to transcriptional phenotype switching.

### F4: Epigenetic subtypes (epitypes) and epiCMIT reflect CLL cell of origin and proliferative history

**Finding:** DNA methylation analysis defines three CLL epitypes reflecting B-cell differentiation state: naive-like (n-CLL), intermediate (i-CLL), and memory-like (m-CLL). The epiCMIT score quantifies cumulative mitotic history from methylation data. In the integrative multivariable model, the n-CLL epitype emerges as a strong predictor of both FFS and OS, emphasizing cell-of-origin as a key determinant of clinical behavior. epiCMIT influences OS more than FFS.

**Detail:** Epitypes are derived from 999 methylation samples and capture a CLL-specific epigenomic differentiation spectrum that partially, but not completely, mirrors IGHV status (U-CLL is enriched in n-CLL; M-CLL enriched in m-CLL). IGHV status and epiCMIT have complementary prognostic information — neither alone captures the full spectrum of outcomes. The integrated model reveals that epigenetic data (epitype + epiCMIT) contributes prognostic information beyond genetics alone. GitHub classifiers for epitypes and epiCMIT are publicly available (github.com/Duran-FerrerM/CLLmap-epigenetics), enabling clinical application.

### F5: Integrated multivariable model identifies critical prognostic features across genetic, epigenetic, and transcriptomic layers

**Finding:** A multivariable elastic-net Cox regression model integrating IGHV status, genetic alterations, epitypes, epiCMIT, and ECs identifies the most refined prognostic model to date for CLL. Key components for FFS: ZNF292, SF3B1, ASXL1, 17p deletion (genetic); EC-i adversely, EC-m3/m4 protectively (transcriptomic). Key for OS: n-CLL epitype, epiCMIT, IGHV, 5q32 loss (novel), TP53/17p, 8q gain (MYC). NOTCH1 and ATM were significant only by univariate analysis, suggesting co-occurrence with other drivers accounts for their model exclusion.

**Detail:** 41 genetic features are associated with FFS or OS in U-CLL vs. 18 in M-CLL (p=0.004), consistent with U-CLL's greater genetic complexity. Novel prognostic events include: ZC3H18 mutation and 5q32/15q25.2 loss in M-CLL for FFS; RFX7 and NFKB1 mutations in U-CLL for poor FFS/OS; 5q32 loss associated with adverse OS (novel). In the treatment-naive subset analysis, TP53 mutation without 17p deletion was not independently prognostic in U-CLL — aligning with clinical data showing that TP53 mutation alone does not worsen outcomes with contemporary ibrutinib/venetoclax therapy.

## Open Questions

- What drives leukemogenesis in the "driverless" M-CLL cases — are they epigenetically dysregulated without coding drivers, or do they harbor noncoding alterations not detected by WES?
- How do the novel expression clusters map to treatment response to BTK inhibitors (ibrutinib/acalabrutinib) and BCL2 inhibitors (venetoclax), the current standards of care?
- Can the 26-gene EC classifier be deployed clinically from RNA extracted from peripheral blood CLL cells, and at what cost/accuracy threshold?
- Do CHD2 alterations mechanistically drive EC discordance (M-CLL shifting to EC-u phenotype), and could targeting CHD2-driven epigenetic reprogramming reverse poor-prognosis phenotypes?
- What is the clinical significance of the novel 5q32 deletion (RPS14/TCOF1) in CLL? Could it confer sensitivity to ribosome biogenesis inhibitors as seen in del(5q) MDS?

## Limitations

- Multi-institutional cohort with variable sample collection protocols, treatment histories, and follow-up durations; harmonization may not fully eliminate batch effects.
- RNA-seq available for only 712/1,148 patients; EC analysis and transcriptomic findings do not generalize to the full WES cohort.
- 177 WGS samples available for SV analysis — a minority of the cohort, limiting SV discovery power.
- Prognostic analyses mix patients from active surveillance (n=680) with clinical trial patients (n=416), creating heterogeneity in treatment exposure that could confound survival analyses.
- TP53/17p collinearity required removing one from prognostic models; their individual contributions cannot be fully disentangled.
- Study predates widespread use of BTK and BCL2 inhibitors; prognostic associations may shift in contemporary treatment settings.
