## Summary

Comprehensive TCGA molecular analysis of 164 oesophageal carcinomas (90 ESCC, 72 EAC) combined with 359 gastric adenocarcinomas using WES, SNP CNA arrays, DNA methylation, mRNA, miRNA, and RPPA. Key findings: ESCC and EAC are molecularly distinct diseases that should not be combined in clinical trials; ESCC molecularly resembles squamous cancers of other organs (head/neck, lung); EAC is molecularly indistinguishable from chromosomally unstable (CIN) gastric cancer and may represent a single disease entity. Three ESCC molecular subtypes are defined (ESCC1-3). Actionable amplifications in ERBB2, VEGFA, and CCNE1 predominate in EAC; CCND1 and SOX2/TP63 amplifications characterize ESCC. Data available in `stes_tcga_pub` on cBioPortal.

## Key Findings

### F1: ESCC and EAC are molecularly distinct diseases — combining them in trials is inappropriate

**Finding:** Integrated clustering across all platforms (SCNA, methylation, mRNA, miRNA) cleanly separates ESCC from EAC, mirroring their histological distinction. The two histotypes differ in significantly mutated genes, copy-number alteration patterns, methylation profiles, and protein expression. No overlap in driver gene amplification patterns was observed. Combining EAC and ESCC in clinical trials risks diluting signals from histotype-specific targetable alterations.

**Detail:** In ESCC, significantly mutated genes include TP53, NFE2L2, MLL2/KMT2D, ZNF750, NOTCH1, and TGFBR2. In EAC, significant mutations include TP53, CDKN2A, ARID1A, SMAD4, and ERBB2. ESCC has high rates of CCND1 amplification (57%) and CDKN2A inactivation (76%); EAC more commonly shows CCNE1 amplification, ERBB2 alteration (32%), and SMAD4 deletion. DNA hypermethylation (including CDKN2A epigenetic silencing) is enriched in EAC. ESCC shows upregulation of Wnt and p63 pathways; EAC shows upregulation of E-cadherin (CDH1) signaling and ARF6/FOXA pathways.

**Genes / cancer types:** TP53, CDKN2A, CCND1, CCNE1, NFE2L2, ZNF750, NOTCH1, ERBB2, SMAD4, ARID1A · Esophageal squamous cell carcinoma, esophageal adenocarcinoma

### F2: EAC is molecularly equivalent to CIN gastric cancer — a single disease entity may be warranted

**Finding:** EAC clusters with chromosomally unstable (CIN) gastric cancer on all molecular platforms (mRNA, methylation, SCNA). 71 of 72 EACs are classified as CIN. Integrated clustering of 288 CIN gastroesophageal adenocarcinomas finds no consistent separation of EAC from gastric CIN tumors, arguing against treating them as distinct diseases. The gastroesophageal junction shows a gradient from ESCC-like proximally to EBV/MSI/GS gastric subtypes distally.

**Detail:** No EACs are positive for MSI or EBV infection—features that define other gastric cancer subtypes. GEJ adenocarcinomas of indeterminate origin contain MSI-positive and EBV-positive tumors, supporting the concept of a molecular gradient from oesophagus to stomach. DNA methylation clustering reveals progressive hypermethylation from distal to proximal GEA-CIN tumors (70% of EAC in highest-methylation clusters vs. 30% of gastric CIN). CDKN2A epigenetic silencing is more frequent in EAC; MLH1 methylation (CIMP) is absent in all CIN tumors. VEGFA and MYC amplifications are more common in EAC than gastric CIN. These findings support the gastric origin hypothesis of Barrett's oesophagus.

**Genes / cancer types:** CDKN2A, VEGFA, MYC, FHIT, WWOX, APC, RUNX1 · Esophageal adenocarcinoma, gastric carcinoma

### F3: Three ESCC molecular subtypes defined by NRF2, NOTCH1, and SMARCA4/PI3K alterations

**Finding:** Integrative clustering of ESCC identifies three subtypes: ESCC1 (characterized by NRF2 pathway alterations — NFE2L2/KEAP1/CUL3/ATG7 — and SOX2/TP63 amplification); ESCC2 (NOTCH1/ZNF750 mutations, KDM6A/KDM2D inactivation, CDK6 amplification, higher APOBEC signature and leukocyte infiltration, elevated Caspase-7); and ESCC3 (rare: SMARCA4 mutations, PI3K pathway activation, KMT2D/MLL2 alterations, no evidence of cell cycle dysregulation, all from Western patients). ESCC1 shows geographic enrichment in Vietnamese patients and NFE2L2 mutations.

**Detail:** NFE2L2 mutations are present in 24% of Vietnamese patients vs. 6% of others (P=0.017), suggesting a common oxidative stressor or genetic predisposition. ESCC2 shows elevated BST2 (immunomodulatory) expression with lower methylation, potentially actionable. ESCC3 has TP53 mutations in only 1 of 4 samples and shows PI3K pathway alterations in all 4—distinct from ESCC1/2. APOBEC signature is enriched in ESCC2 and in Eastern European/Russian patients. ESCC1 resembles the classical subtype of lung SCC and HNSCC. HPV transcripts in ESCC resemble HPV-negative HNSCC, arguing against an aetiologic role for HPV in ESCC.

**Genes / cancer types:** NFE2L2, KEAP1, SOX2, TP63, NOTCH1, ZNF750, KDM6A, SMARCA4, CDK6 · Esophageal squamous cell carcinoma

### F4: ERBB2 alterations in 32% of EAC — beyond amplification to mutations and fusions

**Finding:** ERBB2 is the most frequent oncogenic target in EAC, altered in 32% of cases (vs. 3% in ESCC). Beyond amplification, ERBB2 point mutations are found in 4 tumors lacking amplification, and 6 cases with ERBB2 amplification express a fusion transcript (ERBB2 exon 12 fused to JUP 3'UTR), omitting the transmembrane and kinase domains. These diverse alteration types suggest more patients may benefit from ERBB2-directed therapy than amplification status alone would predict.

**Detail:** Clinical approval of trastuzumab was limited to gastric and GEJ adenocarcinomas; ERBB2-positive EACs are treated off-label. Additional EAC oncogenic amplifications include KRAS, EGFR, IGF1R, VEGFA, GATA4, and GATA6. VEGFA amplification (6p21.1) may predict response to anti-angiogenic therapies. GATA6 amplification has been experimentally validated in EAC and gastric cancer. In ESCC, EGFR is altered in 19% (amplification + mutation), and PI3K pathway components (PIK3CA, PTEN, PIK3R1) in 24%.

**Genes / cancer types:** ERBB2, VEGFA, GATA4, GATA6, KRAS, EGFR, IGF1R · Esophageal adenocarcinoma

### F5: Cell cycle dysregulation is near-universal and histotype-specific; CDK inhibitor rationale established

**Finding:** CDKN2A inactivation affects 76% of both ESCC and EAC (through distinct mechanisms: deletion predominates in ESCC; deletion + mutation + epigenetic silencing in EAC). Downstream cell cycle kinase amplifications differ: CCND1 amplification in 57% of ESCC vs. 15% of EAC; CCNE1 amplification is more common in EAC; CDK6 amplification in ESCC2. RB1 loss in 13% of ESCC. This near-universal cell cycle dysregulation, with histotype-specific mechanisms, establishes a rationale for CDK4/6 (ESCC) or CDK2 (EAC/CCNE1) inhibitor trials.

**Detail:** CDKN2A epigenetic silencing (methylation) is more frequent in EAC; MGMT and CHFR methylation are also found in the most hypermethylated EAC cluster, suggesting potential sensitivity to alkylating agents and microtubule inhibitors, respectively. The pattern of cell cycle dysregulation in ESCC (CCND1 amplification, RB1 deletion) parallels lung SCC and HNSCC, reinforcing the cross-organ squamous cancer biology.

## Open Questions

- Does the molecular equivalence of EAC and CIN gastric cancer justify combining them in clinical trials for systemic therapies such as ERBB2-directed agents?
- Can the three ESCC molecular subtypes (ESCC1/2/3) predict differential response to chemoradiotherapy, particularly given the NRF2 pathway link to chemoradiotherapy resistance in ESCC1?
- Does ERBB2 mutation (without amplification) in EAC predict trastuzumab or pertuzumab response, and what is the functional impact of the ERBB2-JUP fusion transcript?
- What geographic/environmental exposures drive the NFE2L2 mutation enrichment in Vietnamese ESCC patients beyond tobacco chewing?
- Can the APOBEC mutational signature enrichment in ESCC2 be exploited therapeutically (e.g., APOBEC-induced vulnerabilities to DNA damage repair inhibitors)?

## Limitations

- Western-biased cohort for EAC (predominantly from USA/Europe); Eastern populations represented mainly in ESCC (Vietnamese patients); limits generalizability of EAC findings to Asian populations
- ESCC3 has only 4 cases—too small for robust subtype characterization; conclusions about PI3K/SMARCA4 biology are preliminary
- No MSI-positive or EBV-positive cases among EACs; GEJ indeterminate tumors may complicate cross-cancer comparisons
- Low-pass WGS limited to 51 oesophageal tumors; structural variant landscape incompletely characterized
- RPPA performed on only 113 tumors; proteome coverage limited and antibody-based
- Treatment-naive requirement for tissue collection limits representation of the true clinical population encountered at advanced disease stages
