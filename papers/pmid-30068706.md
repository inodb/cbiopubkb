## Summary

Prospective MSK-IMPACT sequencing and IHC profiling of 197 tumors from 189 patients with advanced/recurrent endometrial cancer at MSK. The cohort is predominantly high-grade (75%), heavily treated, and enriched for metastatic specimens. Key findings include: genomic landscape similar to TCGA primary disease when matched by histology/grade; identification of MSI in tumors where IHC failed; discovery of germline *BRCA2* and *MLH1* mutations; a novel copy-number cluster (Cluster C) with heterozygous losses and significantly worse PFS; 67% of patients had potentially actionable alterations; and 27% of actionable patients enrolled on matched trials with 47% clinical benefit rate.

## Key Findings

### F1: Genomic MSI detection identifies patients missed by standard IHC

**Finding:** Integrating MSIsensor genomic scoring with mutational signature decomposition identifies MSI-H tumors missed by standard MMR protein IHC, and can distinguish POLE-driven ultramutation from MSI.

**Detail:** 24/189 tumors (13%) MMR-deficient by IHC (vs. 39% in TCGA, p=5×10⁻¹¹). Six additional MMR-P/not-tested tumors had high somatic TMB (≥20 mut/Mb), including one confirmed POLE hotspot-mutant and cases with possible occult MMR deficiency. Combined MSIsensor + mutational signature decomposition correctly classified 97% (29/30) of known/suspected MMR-D cases, including one MMR-competent by IHC with MSIsensor score indicating MSI-H. No additional MSS patients with high TMB were identified outside MSI/POLE mechanisms. MSI accounts for the vast majority of TMB-high endometrial cancer.

**Genes / cancer types:** `MLH1`, `MSH2`, `MSH6`, `PMS2`, `POLE` · Advanced endometrial cancer

### F2: A novel CNA cluster (Cluster C) with heterozygous losses marks poor prognosis

**Finding:** Hierarchical clustering of genome-wide somatic copy number alterations reveals three clusters; Cluster C, characterized by heterozygous losses across the genome, has significantly worse median PFS₁ than the other clusters.

**Detail:** Cluster A: TP53-mutant, high-grade, whole-genome doubling (WGD); median PFS₁ 17.4 months. Cluster B: PTEN-mutant endometrioid, gains of chr 1q/8/10; median PFS₁ 17.0 months. Cluster C: high-grade endometrioid and non-endometrioid, no 1q gains, large-scale heterozygous losses without WGD; median PFS₁ 9.6 months — significantly worse than A and B (p=0.006). Cluster C-like tumors also identified retrospectively in TCGA Cluster 4 (≈50% of SCNA-high cluster) but heavy censoring in TCGA prevented robust outcome comparison. This prognostic signal persists even vs. TP53-mutant patients with WGD.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53`, `PTEN` · Advanced endometrial cancer (serous, high-grade endometrioid, carcinosarcoma)

**Data availability:** `available`
> Copy number data and clinical outcome data available in ucec_msk_2018 via cBioPortal.

**Tool support:** `api`

### F3: 67% of patients harbor potentially actionable alterations; 47% clinical benefit rate on matched trials

**Finding:** 67% of patients (127/189) harbor at least one potentially actionable alteration; 27% of these were matched to a clinical trial, and 47% of matched patients achieved clinical benefit.

**Detail:** Most common actionable alterations: *PIK3CA* mutation 35% (n=66), *PTEN* mutation 29% (n=54), MSI-H 16% (n=30), *ERBB2* amplification 16% (n=16). Trial matches: PI3K (n=12), dual PI3K/mTOR (n=6), ERBB2 (n=6), AKT (n=4), mTOR (n=2), FGFR inhibitors (n=2). Of 34 matched patients, 31 received targeted therapy alone. Clinical benefit rate 47% (16/34), exceeding historic expectations. One serous *ERBB2*-amplified patient achieved 14-month complete response to trastuzumab emtansine (T-DM1). 8.5% (16/189) of all sequenced patients achieved benefit from matched therapy.

**Genes / cancer types:** `PIK3CA`, `PTEN`, `ERBB2`, `FGFR1`, `FGFR2`, `FGFR3`, `AKT1`, `MTOR` · Advanced endometrial cancer

### F4: TP53 and PTEN frequencies differ markedly from TCGA, reflecting histologic enrichment in the advanced cohort

**Finding:** Despite substantially different clinical features between the MSK advanced cohort and TCGA primary-disease cohort, the mutational profiles are similar when matched by histology/grade — but *TP53* mutations (63% vs. 36%, p=4×10⁻⁵) and *PTEN* alterations (22% vs. 56%, p=8×10⁻⁹) differ significantly due to histologic composition differences.

**Detail:** MSK cohort: 75% high-grade tumors (vs. 48% in TCGA); 24% serous, 19% carcinosarcoma, 16% endometrioid G3 vs. TCGA: predominantly low-grade endometrioid. *TP53* mutation rate higher in MSK (63%) due to higher-grade/serous proportion. *PTEN* lower in MSK (22%) due to fewer low-grade endometrioid cases. High-grade endometrioid had significantly higher *TP53* than low-grade (29% vs. 3%, p=0.013). *PIK3CA* enriched in mixed histology and overall in high-grade tumors (p=0.035). *FGFR1* amplification enriched in carcinosarcomas; *ERBB2* amplification enriched in serous tumors (p=0.007 and p=0.018).

### F5: Germline pathogenic variants identified as clinical byproduct: BRCA2 and MLH1

**Finding:** Paired tumor-normal sequencing identified three patients with truncating germline *BRCA2* mutations (serous and carcinosarcoma histologies) and one patient with a pathogenic germline *MLH1* splice-site mutation associated with Lynch syndrome.

**Detail:** Three *BRCA2* germline truncations: c.5946delT (6174delT Ashkenazi founder, n=2 — 1 serous, 1 carcinosarcoma) and c.5799_5802delCCAA (1 carcinosarcoma). One carcinosarcoma had LOH; serous retained wild-type allele; third non-evaluable. Germline *MLH1* splice site mutation (c.1731+1G>T) in grade 2 endometrioid with biallelic somatic CN-LOH; MSIsensor score 19.19. ESR1 ligand-binding domain hotspot mutations (5 endometrioid patients); 3 appeared de novo without prior endocrine therapy exposure. Germline analysis conducted after irreversible anonymization; further clinical description limited.

**Genes / cancer types:** `BRCA2`, `MLH1`, `ESR1` · Advanced endometrial cancer

**Data availability:** `partial`

**Tool support:** `custom`

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Does the Cluster C (heterozygous-loss) CNA pattern predict response to specific therapies (e.g., PARP inhibitors, CDK4/6 inhibitors) beyond its prognostic significance?
- With pembrolizumab now approved for MSI-H tumors, what proportion of the ~16% MSI-H advanced endometrial cancer patients in community practice are missed by IHC and would benefit from genomic MSI testing?
- Can the combination of MSI + POLE ultramutation status guide immunotherapy sequencing (anti-PD-1 vs. observation) in the adjuvant setting for endometrial cancer?
- Do de novo *ESR1* mutations (without prior endocrine therapy) in endometrial cancer respond to ER-directed therapies (fulvestrant)?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Single-center study (MSK); rate of clinical trial matching dependent on MSK's specific trial portfolio, not generalizable
- Retrospective clinical annotation; germline findings anonymized, limiting clinical characterization of germline-positive patients
- Overall sample size (n=189) limits power for histology-specific subgroup analyses
- Only 18% of all sequenced patients received matched-targeted therapy; 8.5% achieved clinical benefit — absolute numbers are small
- Genomic copy number clustering (Cluster C) requires validation in an independent advanced endometrial cancer cohort
- OncoKB annotations applied as of December 2016; actionability landscape has since expanded substantially (e.g., pembrolizumab for MSI-H)
