## Summary

Shoushtari et al. (2021) prospectively sequenced 696 cutaneous and unknown primary melanomas using MSK-IMPACT (341–468 genes) at MSKCC and Lehigh Valley, identifying oncogenic RTK-RAS-MAPK pathway alterations in 96% of tumors across 9 mutually exclusive driver groups. Time to treatment failure (TTF) on PD-1 monotherapy varied significantly by MAPK driver class—BRAF V600 and NRAS Q61 tumors fared worse than NF1 or other driver tumors—independent of TMB and clinical features. Among BRAF V600 wild-type patients who progressed on checkpoint blockade, 8 of 27 (30%) treated with genomically matched therapy derived ≥6 months of benefit, including 4 complete responses to kinase inhibitors targeting fusions or non-V600 BRAF/MEK alterations. The study establishes multigene sequencing as clinically actionable for all cutaneous and unknown primary melanomas.

## Key Findings

### F1: MAPK pathway drivers detected in 96% of melanomas via multigene sequencing

**Finding:** Targeted multigene sequencing identified at least one oncogenic RTK-RAS-MAPK pathway alteration in 670 of 696 (96%) cutaneous and unknown primary melanomas, far exceeding what is detected by single-gene BRAF/NRAS testing alone.

**Detail:** BRAF V600E/K/R mutations were present in 31% (216/696); NRAS alterations in 29%; NF1 loss-of-function in 23%; non-V600 BRAF in 97 tumors (of which 18% Class 2, 10% Class 3); MAP2K1/MAP2K2 in 7%; KIT in 4%; HRAS in 2%; KRAS in 1.3%. One-third of tumors (33%) harbored co-alterations in ≥2 MAPK pathway genes. Clonality analysis (N=428) showed 92% had only clonal driver alterations.

### F2: Nine mutually exclusive MAPK driver groups differ in TMB and clinical features

**Finding:** Tumors were classified into 9 mutually exclusive MAPK driver groups whose median TMB differed dramatically (p=9.5e-56), ranging from 7–8 mut/Mb in BRAF V600E to 43 mut/Mb in NF1 and 37 mut/Mb in KIT mutant tumors.

**Detail:** BRAF V600E and unknown driver tumors had the lowest TMB (7–8 mut/Mb); BRAF V600K/R, non-V600 BRAF, NRAS Q61, and other known drivers had intermediate TMB (14–18 mut/Mb); other RAS mutants had 22 mut/Mb; KIT 37 mut/Mb; and NF1 43 mut/Mb. NF1 was enriched in head/neck primaries and older males with UV-associated mutations. BRAF V600E was enriched in younger patients (median diagnosis age 50 years), while KIT was enriched in older patients (median 72 years). TMB rose steadily by decade of age at diagnosis (4.7 mut/Mb <20 years to 25.6 mut/Mb >80 years).

### F3: PD-1 monotherapy TTF varies significantly by MAPK driver class, independent of TMB

**Finding:** Among 181 patients treated with frontline PD-1 monotherapy, time to treatment failure (TTF) differed markedly by driver group (p<0.0001), with NRAS Q61 and BRAF V600 tumors showing worse outcomes than NF1 or other driver tumors, even after adjusting for TMB, ECOG PS, and AJCC stage.

**Detail:** Median TTF was 4.2 months (NRAS Q61), 7.5 months (BRAF V600), 22 months (NF1), and not reached (Other/Unknown). In multivariate analysis, "Other/Unknown" driver class was independently associated with better TTF vs. BRAF V600 (HR 0.35, 95% CI 0.18–0.67, p=0.002). Log10(TMB) was also independently significant (HR 0.41 per 10-fold increase, 95% CI 0.25–0.67, p<0.001). Driver class remained significant after controlling for ECOG performance status (HR 4.98 for ECOG 2–3 vs. 0, p<0.001) and AJCC stage.

### F4: Nivolumab plus ipilimumab TTF does not vary by MAPK driver or primary site

**Finding:** For 141 patients receiving frontline nivolumab plus ipilimumab (nivo+ipi), TTF did not differ by MAPK driver group or primary site of melanoma, in contrast to PD-1 monotherapy.

**Detail:** While PD-1 monotherapy TTF varied by driver (p<0.0001) and by primary site (face > other cutaneous > unknown primary, p=0.019), neither association was seen in the nivo+ipi cohort. This likely reflects both a higher-risk patient population selected for combination therapy in this non-randomized cohort and distinct immunologic mechanisms of combined PD-1+CTLA-4 blockade. Increasing TMB was associated with longer TTF for both nivo+ipi (HR 0.51 per 10-fold increase, p=0.008) and OS (HR 0.47, p=0.021).

### F5: Non-canonical MAPK alterations (BRAF Class 2/3, fusions) show distinct co-alteration patterns

**Finding:** BRAF Class 3 mutations (e.g., D594N) were co-altered with other MAPK drivers in 97% of cases, versus only 38% for Class 2 mutations; BRAF V600E co-altered in only 12% vs. 37% for V600K/R, reflecting fundamental mechanistic differences in ERK activation.

**Detail:** Class 3 BRAF alterations are RAS-dependent and typically co-occur with NF1 truncations, RTK alterations, or CBL mutations that activate RAS, explaining their near-universal co-alteration rate. Class 1 BRAF V600E, NRAS Q61, and MAP2K1 indels were more often "sole drivers." MAP2K1 missense mutations (unlike indels) commonly co-occurred with other MAPK drivers. NF1 was most frequently co-altered with RTK genes, BRAF Class 3, or CBL. Among BRAF V600 alleles, V600E was significantly less likely than V600K/R to harbor MAPK co-alterations (11.6% vs 37%, p=0.0003).

### F6: 30% clinical benefit rate with genomically matched therapy in checkpoint-refractory BRAF V600-WT melanoma

**Finding:** Among 172 BRAF V600 wild-type patients who progressed on checkpoint blockade, 27 (16%) received genomically matched targeted therapy, and 8 (30%) achieved clinical benefit lasting ≥6 months, including 4 durable complete responses.

**Detail:** Matched therapies included MEK/ERK inhibitors for RAS alterations (N=13) or BRAF Class 2 alterations (N=6), TRK inhibitors for NTRK fusions (N=3), and others. Median TTF of matched therapy was 3.2 months (range 0.4–43.7 months). Complete responses were achieved with trametinib (BRAF K601E + MAP2K1 E203K), crizotinib (ROS1 fusion), larotrectinib (NTRK1 fusion), and PLX8394 (BRAF-AGK fusion, Class 2). The number needed to sequence for one patient with ≥6 months benefit in BRAF V600-WT patients was 21.

### F7: Unknown primary melanomas have similar genomic profiles to cutaneous melanomas

**Finding:** Melanomas of unknown primary (20% of cohort, N=140) had a similar rate of RTK-RAS-MAPK pathway alterations to cutaneous melanomas, but showed inferior TTF to PD-1 monotherapy compared to face/scalp primaries.

**Detail:** The rate of MAPK pathway drivers was similar between cutaneous and unknown primary melanomas (Supp Figure 2A). However, TTF of PD-1 monotherapy varied by primary site (p=0.019): highest for face/scalp primaries, intermediate for other cutaneous sites, and lowest for unknown primary. This pattern was not observed with nivo+ipi. The high rate of tertiary referral patients in this cohort may have biased toward over-representation of unknown primary cases.

## Open Questions

- Why does MAPK driver class influence PD-1 monotherapy TTF independently of TMB—does ERK activation strength modulate T-cell inflammation or tumor immunoediting?
- Can MEK/ERK inhibitors be rationally combined with checkpoint blockade to improve outcomes in NRAS Q61 or BRAF V600 mutant tumors with low TMB?
- Are BRAF Class 2/3 non-V600 alterations and MAP2K1/MAP2K2 alterations effectively targetable with approved MEK inhibitors (e.g., trametinib) in prospective trials?
- What explains the inferior PD-1 monotherapy outcomes in unknown primary melanomas versus face/scalp primaries, given similar MAPK profiles?
- As RAS-mutant and NF1-mutant melanomas represent the largest unmet need, which combination strategies can overcome intrinsic resistance in these subgroups?
- Do the MAPK driver–TMB–outcome correlations generalize to adjuvant checkpoint inhibitor settings or other tumor types?

## Limitations

- BRAF V600E mutant tumors were likely underrepresented because patients with known BRAF V600E were less likely to be offered MSK-IMPACT sequencing (prior hotspot testing was common).
- High proportion of unknown primary melanomas may reflect referral bias to a tertiary cancer center.
- PD-L1 status was unknown for most samples, preventing assessment of its interaction with driver groups and outcomes.
- Retrospective, non-randomized treatment allocation; PD-1 monotherapy vs. nivo+ipi groups are not directly comparable due to selection bias.
- TTF in BRAF V600 tumors may be confounded by availability of effective second-line BRAF+MEK inhibitor therapy.
- Mutation calling may vary by bioinformatic pipeline, limiting reproducibility across NGS platforms.
- Genomically matched therapy cohort (N=27) is small and retrospective; response rates should be interpreted cautiously.
