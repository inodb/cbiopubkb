## Summary

Exome sequencing of 160 periampullary cancers (98 ampullary adenocarcinomas [AMPAC], 44 distal bile duct [CAC], 18 duodenal adenocarcinomas [DUOAC]) establishes a shared genomic landscape dominated by WNT pathway mutations (~46% overall), with *ELF3* as a novel, predominantly inactivating tumor suppressor mutated at 10.6%—three times higher than in any other cancer type. MSI is common (~8–15% depending on site) and associated with improved survival; two-thirds of MSI cases have Lynch syndrome germline mutations. The data support a molecular classification of periampullary tumors that transcends anatomical origin, with WNT pathway status as a potential therapeutic stratifier.

## Key Findings

### F1: ELF3 is a novel, high-frequency tumor suppressor in periampullary cancers

**Finding:** *ELF3*, a transcriptional regulator of *TGFBR2*, is mutated in 10.6% of periampullary tumors with predominantly inactivating frameshift and nonsense mutations distributed across the gene—a pattern characteristic of tumor suppressor loss.

**Detail:** ELF3 mutation frequency in this cohort is 3× higher than in any other cancer type (q < 1.6×10⁻¹¹, MutSigCV). *ELF3* mutations co-occur with *WNT* pathway mutations in 71% of cases (Chi-square p = 0.02). A concurrent study found *ELF3* mutations in 9.5% of extrahepatic CAC. *ELF3* was also found mutated in a duodenal adenoma with intraepithelial neoplasia, suggesting early-stage involvement. 75% of *ELF3*-mutant tumors were lower grade (stage I or II). ELF3 regulates *TGFBR2*, *EGR1* and suppresses *AR* and *MMP9* transcription.

**Genes / cancer types:** `ELF3` · Ampullary cancer · Duodenal adenocarcinoma · Distal bile duct adenocarcinoma

### F2: WNT pathway mutations in ~half of all periampullary cancers

**Finding:** The *WNT* signaling pathway is mutated in 46% of periampullary patients overall, and is differentially mutated by anatomical site: 72% DUOAC, 49% AMPAC, 30% CAC (Chi-square p < 0.05).

**Detail:** *APC* and *SOX9* mutations drive the higher WNT frequency in DUOAC. By histological subtype, intestinal subtype tumors had 67% WNT pathway alterations vs. 30% in pancreatobiliary subtype—mirroring the anatomical gradient. WNT mutations were even found in ~25% of CAC (a "pancreatobiliary" tumor) and in pancreatobiliary-subtype tumors, supporting a pan-periampullary WNT dysregulation. RNA expression analysis confirmed that WNT-mutated tumors have significantly higher WNT target gene expression (p < 0.001). Several WNT-pathway small molecule inhibitors were in clinical trials at the time of publication.

### F3: MSI is common and associated with Lynch syndrome germline mutations and better survival

**Finding:** MSI was identified in 12 patients across all three tumor types; 2/3 of MSI cases had germline Lynch syndrome mutations (predominantly *PMS2*). MSI was associated with significantly better survival in AMPAC (log-rank p = 0.04) and all periampullary tumors combined (p = 0.0028).

**Detail:** In AMPAC, MSI frequency was ~3% among MSS patients overall; all 6 AMPAC MSI patients were alive at 2–8 years post-diagnosis (no negative survival events). MSI mutation rates were dramatically higher than MSS: 68, 127, and 108 mutations/Mb in AMPAC, CAC, and DUOAC respectively, vs. ~3.8–4.7/Mb in MSS. *PMS2* mutations accounted for half of Lynch syndrome cases in this cohort—notable because *PMS2* accounts for <5% of Lynch syndrome cases in the general population. MSI was more common in DUOAC than CAC.

**Genes / cancer types:** `PMS2` · `MLH1` · `MSH2` · Ampullary cancer · Duodenal adenocarcinoma

### F4: RTK/RAS/PI3K pathway is altered in 84–94% of all periampullary patients

**Finding:** The RTK/RAS/PI3K pathway is activated at a statistically similar high rate across all three periampullary tumor types (84–94%), with mutant *KRAS* as the major oncogene and *TGF-β*/*PI3K* pathway mutations associated with better outcomes.

**Detail:** *SMAD4*, the most commonly mutated TGF-β pathway gene in AMPAC and CAC, mirrors PDAC's genetic landscape. TGF-β pathway mutations were independently associated with better overall survival (multivariate Cox p = 0.0059, HR = 0.42). PI3K pathway mutations were also associated with better overall survival (multivariate Cox p = 0.036, HR = 0.43). Mutation signature #1 (AC/AT>AN, enriched in non-transcribed regions) at >20% in 9.6% of all tumors was associated with poor outcomes (multivariate Cox p = 0.02).

**Genes / cancer types:** `KRAS` · `SMAD4` · `TP53` · Ampullary cancer · Distal bile duct adenocarcinoma · Duodenal adenocarcinoma

### F5: Histological and IHC classification systems are poorly concordant with molecular profiles

**Finding:** Anatomical and IHC-based subtype classification of periampullary tumors agreed only 62% of the time for AMPAC, 77% for CAC, and 53% for DUOAC—suggesting these classification systems are inadequate for treatment stratification.

**Detail:** Subtype classification by IHC (MUC1/CDX2) vs. histological morphology yielded discordant results in 38–47% of AMPAC and DUOAC cases. Tumors originating in each organ site could be classified as any of the three subtypes (intestinal, pancreatobiliary, mixed). No unique molecular characteristics were specifically associated with one tumor subtype or one tumor type. A focal deletion in the *KDM4C* promoter was uniquely significant in AMPAC and associated with decreased *KDM4C* and *UHRF2* expression.

**Genes / cancer types:** `KDM4C` · Ampullary cancer

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Would WNT pathway inhibitors (β-catenin inhibitors in clinical trials) benefit the 46% of periampullary patients with WNT pathway mutations?
- Can *ELF3* mutation combined with WNT status (71% co-occurrence) define a therapeutic subgroup within ampullary cancers?
- Should all periampullary cancer patients undergo MSI/MMR testing to identify Lynch syndrome and immunotherapy candidates?
- Does the abundance of *PMS2* Lynch syndrome mutations in this cohort reflect site-specific mutagenic pressure, or ascertainment bias?
- Can RNA-based WNT expression signature (WNT antagonist + agonist + target genes) serve as a surrogate for genomic WNT pathway activation?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Classification of ampullary tumors as AMPAC, CAC, or DUOAC based on AJCC 7th edition anatomical criteria is subjective and varies across pathologists.
- IHC subtype concordance with morphology was only 53–77%, limiting subgroup analyses.
- RNA-seq performed on only 30 of 160 patients (subset), limiting transcriptomic findings to a subgroup.
- All patients underwent surgical resection with curative intent; findings may not generalize to unresectable or metastatic disease.
- Survival analysis limited by variable follow-up and relatively small patient numbers within subtypes.
