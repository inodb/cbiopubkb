## Summary

Pereira et al. performed targeted sequencing of 173 genes in 2,433 primary breast tumours from the METABRIC cohort, integrating mutation data with pre-existing copy-number and gene expression profiles and long-term clinical follow-up (median 115 months, 2,319 patients). Using a ratiometric driver-identification method, they identified 40 mutation-driver (Mut-driver) genes stratified by ER status. Key contributions include mapping subtype-specific mutation frequencies across the 10 IntClust molecular subtypes, characterising intra-tumour heterogeneity via MATH scores, and demonstrating that the prognostic impact of *PIK3CA* mutations in ER+ disease is context-dependent on IntClust background.

## Key Findings

### F1: 40 Mutation-Driver Genes Identified, Stratified by ER Status

**Finding:** A ratiometric method (Vogelstein ONC/TSG scoring) applied to 2,433 tumours identified 40 Mut-driver genes — 22 ER+ only, 3 ER− only, and 15 shared — encompassing both established and novel breast cancer drivers.

**Detail:** Top coding mutation frequencies: *PIK3CA* 40.1%, *TP53* 35.4%, *MUC16* 16.8%, *AHNAK2* 16.2%, *SYNE1* 12.0%, *KMT2C* 11.4%, *GATA3* 11.1%. Novel Mut-driver genes supported by this cohort include *SF3B1* (recurrent K700E; 3.5% ER+), *TBX3* (TSG 58% in ER+; 6.0%), *RUNX1* (TSG 59% in ER+; 3.7%), *CBFB* (TSG 66% in ER+; 6.0%), *FOXO3*, and *AGTR2*. Overall 22.6% of tumours had a coding mutation in at least one of seven chromatin-regulator Mut-driver genes (*KMT2C*, *ARID1A*, *NCOR1*, *CTCF*, *KDM6A*, *PBRM1*, *TBL1XR1*). 45.2% of tumours harboured a functional mutation in at least one Akt-pathway gene (*PIK3CA*, *AKT1*, *PIK3R1*, *PTEN*, *FOXO3*).

**Genes / cancer types:** `PIK3CA`, `TP53`, `GATA3`, `CDH1`, `MAP3K1`, `SF3B1`, `TBX3`, `RUNX1`, `CBFB`, `KMT2C`, `ARID1A` · Breast (ER+, ER−)

### F2: Subtype-Specific Mutation Frequencies Across 10 IntClust Subtypes

**Finding:** Mut-driver gene mutation frequencies vary dramatically across the 10 IntClust molecular subtypes, revealing distinct genomic programmes underlying each subtype's clinical behaviour.

**Detail:** *TP53* functional mutations ranged from 4.4% in IntClust8 and 10.0% in IntClust3, to 64.2% in IntClust5 (HER2-enriched) and 84.6% in IntClust10 (basal-like). *GATA3* mutations were enriched in IntClust1 (20.0%) and IntClust8 (19.5%) vs. 8.9% overall. *CBFB* was enriched in IntClust3 (7.8%) and IntClust8 (9.7%) vs. 3.3% overall. *PIK3CA* mutations in IntClust10 and IntClust9 were nearly universally clonal (median CCF=1), while a subset of IntClust3 *PIK3CA* mutations were subclonal (median CCF=0.96). Formal interaction analysis identified nine genes differentially mutated across IntClusts at FDR=0.01.

### F3: CDH1 Mutations Define Lobular Carcinoma; ER-Status-Specific Mutation Patterns

**Finding:** Inactivating *CDH1* mutations were a hallmark of invasive lobular carcinoma (52.6%) and were almost absent in ER− tumours, whereas *TP53* was strongly associated with ER− and HER2+/ER− disease.

**Detail:** *CDH1* inactivating mutations occurred in 52.6% of lobular carcinomas vs. 3.4% of ductal/NST tumours; 96.0% of *CDH1*-mutant tumours also had LOH at 16q22. *CDH1* mutations were mutually exclusive with *TP53* (OR=0.23). *TP53* functional mutations appeared in 67.5% of HER2+/ER− vs. 42.6% of HER2+/ER+ tumours. *PIK3CA* helical-domain mutations (codons 542/545) were enriched in ER+ (P=0.042) while kinase-domain mutations (codon 1047) were enriched in ER− tumours (P=0.012). *CDH1* co-mutated with *PIK3CA* (OR=2.1), *TBX3* (OR=3.2), *RUNX1* (OR=3.3), and *ERBB2* (OR=5.7).

**Genes / cancer types:** `CDH1`, `TP53`, `PIK3CA`, `ERBB2`, `TBX3`, `RUNX1` · Breast (luminal/lobular, HER2+, ER−)

### F4: Prognostic Impact of Driver Mutations — MAP3K1, GATA3, TP53, PIK3CA

**Finding:** Multivariable Cox models revealed gene-specific and ER-status-specific survival associations, with *MAP3K1* and *GATA3* mutations conferring better outcomes in ER+ disease, while *TP53* worsened ER+ prognosis and *PIK3CA* worsened ER− prognosis.

**Detail:** In ER+ tumours: *MAP3K1* mutations (HR=0.56, 95% CI=0.38–0.82) and *GATA3* mutations (HR=0.58, CI=0.40–0.82) were associated with longer breast cancer-specific survival (BCSS). *SMAD4* inactivating mutations (HR=3.4, CI=1.4–8.3) and *USP9X* inactivating mutations (HR=3.0, CI=1.2–7.2) were associated with worse BCSS in ER+. *TP53* mutations were adverse in ER+ (HR=1.6, CI=1.3–2.0, P=0.0001) but not ER−. *PIK3CA* mutations were prognostically adverse in ER− (HR=1.4, CI=1.1–1.9) but not in ER+ overall. *NF1* inactivating mutations were adverse in ER− (HR=2.7, CI=1.3–5.5).

### F5: PIK3CA Prognostic Effect Is IntClust-Dependent in ER+ Tumours

**Finding:** *PIK3CA* mutations in ER+ tumours had no overall prognostic effect, but significant adverse interactions were detected in specific IntClusts (1+, 2+, 9+), explaining prior contradictory reports.

**Detail:** Univariable Cox models with an IntClust-by-mutation interaction term identified significant adverse associations between *PIK3CA* mutations and BCSS in IntClusts 1+ (17q23 amplification), 2+ (11q13–14 amplification), and 9+ (8q24/MYC amplification). Patients in IntClusts 3+, 4+, 7+, and 8+ with *PIK3CA* mutations had outcomes similar to wild-type patients in their respective groups. The prognostic effect was independent of mutation domain (helical vs. kinase) and cancer cell fraction, suggesting that the CNA background — not the mutation itself — determines clinical context. These findings have direct implications for patient selection in PI3K inhibitor trials.

### F6: Intra-Tumour Heterogeneity (MATH Score) Associates with Outcome

**Finding:** Higher mutant-allele tumour heterogeneity (MATH) scores, reflecting greater intra-tumour heterogeneity, were associated with worse outcome in ER+ tumours, although IntClust2 (poor prognosis) paradoxically had low MATH scores.

**Detail:** ER+ tumours had lower MATH scores overall (median=0.29, IQR=0.18–0.44) than ER− tumours (median=0.41, IQR=0.25–0.56). Higher MATH scores were associated with worse BCSS in ER+ cancers. IntClust10 had the highest MATH scores (median=0.47) consistent with its genomic instability. IntClust2 (11q13–14 amplification of *CCND1*/*PAK1*; poor prognosis) had unexpectedly low MATH scores (median=0.25) and low chromosomal instability (CIN), suggesting that early clonal fixation of a dominant driver CNA — rather than mutational diversity — drives poor outcomes in this subtype.

**Genes / cancer types:** `CCND1`, `PAK1` · Breast (ER+, ER−, IntClust2/10)

**Tool support:** `api`

### F7: Actionable and Cross-Cancer Mut-Driver Genes Identified in Breast Cancer

**Finding:** The Mut-driver gene list includes genes previously established as drivers in other cancer types — such as *KRAS*, *ARID1A*, *PBRM1*, *CDKN2A*, *MEN1* — suggesting therapies targeting these alterations in other settings may be applicable to breast cancer.

**Detail:** *KRAS* codon 12 mutations were present in 11 samples with high ONC scores in both ER+ (89%) and ER− (60%) cases. SWI/SNF complex members *ARID1A* (ER+: TSG=45%, 5.2%) and *PBRM1* (ER−: TSG=25%, 2.5%) — common drivers in bladder and renal cancer — were Mut-drivers in breast cancer; ARID1B synthetic lethality may be exploitable. *AGTR2* had 6 recurrent P271L mutations (ER+: ONC=56%, 0.51%) and is a potential therapeutic target. Pathogenic germline mutations were identified in *BRCA1* (1.36%), *BRCA2* (1.64%), *CHEK2* (2.22%), and *TP53* (0.82%).

## Open Questions

- Does IntClust stratification generalise as a predictor of PI3K inhibitor response in prospective clinical trials?
- What are the functional consequences of recurrent *SF3B1* K700E mutations on splicing and therapeutic vulnerability in breast cancer specifically?
- Can MATH score or CCF-based clonal architecture metrics be used prospectively to stratify patients for treatment intensity or sequencing?
- Do the actionable cross-cancer Mut-drivers (*KRAS*, *ARID1A*, *PBRM1*) confer similar therapeutic dependencies in breast cancer as in their primary cancer types?
- How do the subclonal *CDH1* mutations in IntClust4+ tumours contribute to disease progression or lobular-like phenotype switching?
- What drives the paradoxically low intra-tumour heterogeneity in IntClust2 despite its poor prognosis, and how should these patients be managed?

## Limitations

- Targeted panel of 173 genes covers only a fraction of the genome; structural variants, non-coding mutations, and copy-number-level changes outside covered genes are not captured by sequencing.
- Tumour samples are from FFPE blocks; lower DNA quality may increase false-positive/negative calls compared to fresh-frozen whole-exome sequencing.
- Lacks matched RNA sequencing; expression data comes from a separate microarray platform not generated alongside these sequencing data.
- Germline variant filtering relied on a panel-of-normals approach and population databases; rare pathogenic germline variants may be misclassified as somatic or missed.
- Long-term follow-up data are not available for all 2,433 patients (clinical data for 2,319); treatment heterogeneity across the multi-cohort dataset may confound survival associations.
- MATH score and CCF estimates are approximations dependent on sequencing depth and purity estimates; results require external validation in independent cohorts.
