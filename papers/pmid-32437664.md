## Summary

This investigator-initiated, single-arm phase 2 trial (NCT02954536) evaluated first-line pembrolizumab combined with trastuzumab and chemotherapy (fluoropyrimidine + platinum) in 37 patients with HER2-positive metastatic esophagogastric (mEG) cancer at MSKCC. The study met its primary endpoint: 70% (26/37) of patients were progression-free at 6 months (vs. 55% historical control). The ORR was 91% among 35 patients with measurable disease, including 17% complete responses. Median PFS was 13.0 months and median OS was 27.3 months — substantially exceeding historical benchmarks for trastuzumab + chemotherapy alone (OS 13.8 months). ERBB2 amplification in both tumor and ctDNA predicted longer PFS. All clinical and genomic data are available in cBioPortal as `egc_trap_msk_2020`.

## Key Findings

### F1: First-line pembrolizumab + trastuzumab + chemotherapy achieves 91% ORR and 27-month median OS in HER2-positive mEG cancer

**Finding:** The combination of pembrolizumab, trastuzumab, and chemotherapy yielded an ORR of 91% (32/35 patients with measurable disease; 95% CI 78–97%), including 17% complete responses and 74% partial responses. Median OS was 27.3 months (95% CI 18.8–NA) — nearly double the 13.8-month historical control for trastuzumab + chemotherapy alone.

**Detail:** The study met the primary endpoint: 26/37 (70%, 95% CI 54–83%) patients were progression-free at 6 months. Median PFS was 13.0 months (95% CI 8.6–NA), with a 6-month PFS rate of 75% (95% CI 63–91%) and 12-month OS of 80% (95% CI 68–95%). All 35 patients with measurable disease demonstrated tumor regression (ranging from −20% to −100%). Non-target lesion escape was the primary pattern of progression in 54% (12/22) of patients who discontinued therapy. Results supported the subsequent KEYNOTE-811 phase 3 trial.

### F2: ERBB2 amplification in both tumor and ctDNA predicts significantly longer PFS (16.4 vs. 6.2 months)

**Finding:** Patients with ERBB2 amplification confirmed in both tumor tissue NGS and ctDNA had significantly longer PFS (median 16.4 months, n=14) compared to those without concordant ERBB2 amplification (median 6.2 months, n=12; p=0.013), suggesting dual confirmation as a predictive biomarker.

**Detail:** ERBB2 amplification or focal gain was detected in 66% (21/32) of patients by tumor NGS and 54% (18/33) by ctDNA (Guardant360). Tissue-ctDNA concordance for ERBB2 amplification was 93% (27/29 patients). Tumor NGS ERBB2 amplification alone trended toward longer PFS (14.6 vs. 7.8 months, p=0.12, non-significant). All 20 patients with ERBB2 amplification by tumor NGS had HER2 IHC 3+; 6 patients with equivocal IHC 2+/FISH-positive lacked NGS-confirmed amplification, likely due to tumor heterogeneity. ctDNA-matched tumor alterations declined after the first dose of pembrolizumab + trastuzumab in 13/16 evaluable patients, demonstrating early molecular response.

### F3: Activity of pembrolizumab + trastuzumab induction (without chemotherapy) — 48% had early tumor regression after cycle 1

**Finding:** Among 25 patients who received an initial induction cycle of pembrolizumab + trastuzumab only (no chemotherapy), 48% (12/25) demonstrated CT-confirmed reduction in target lesions at week 3, and 2 achieved partial responses before chemotherapy initiation.

**Detail:** The study allowed an optional induction cycle of biologic therapy alone (pembrolizumab 200 mg + trastuzumab 8 mg/kg) before adding chemotherapy in cycle 2. The 25 patients who received the induction cycle had similar median PFS (13 months, 95% CI 6.45–NR) and 12-month OS (91%, 95% CI 75–100%) to the 12 patients who started chemotherapy immediately (PFS 14.6 months; 12-month OS 75%, 95% CI 60–95%). This suggests that anti-HER2 + anti-PD-1 combination has intrinsic anti-tumor activity beyond chemosensitization and supports mechanistic rationale for the combination based on trastuzumab-induced PD-L1 upregulation and T cell recruitment.

### F4: Efficacy is independent of pre-treatment PD-L1 status — challenging PD-L1 as a predictive biomarker in this setting

**Finding:** Pembrolizumab + trastuzumab + chemotherapy showed high activity regardless of PD-L1 CPS status, with the regimen showing benefit even in PD-L1-negative (CPS <1) patients, supporting clinical use without mandatory PD-L1 pre-screening in HER2-positive mEG cancer.

**Detail:** Of 37 enrolled patients: 13 (35%) had CPS <1 (PD-L1 negative), 14 (38%) had CPS ≥1 (PD-L1 positive), and 10 (27%) had no PD-L1 data. The high ORR (91%) across the cohort, including PD-L1-negative patients, contrasts with evidence that pembrolizumab monotherapy requires PD-L1 positivity for activity in gastric cancer. This finding — consistent with mechanistic evidence that trastuzumab augments HER2-specific T-cell responses and promotes immune cell trafficking — was influential in the design of the phase 3 KEYNOTE-811 trial, which similarly did not restrict enrollment by PD-L1 status.

### F5: Mutational landscape via MSK-IMPACT reveals heterogeneous genomic co-alterations in HER2-positive mEG cancer

**Finding:** MSK-IMPACT profiling of 32 pre-treatment tumors identified ERBB2 amplification in 66% and a heterogeneous co-alteration landscape including mutations in TP53, ARID1A, CCNE1, KRAS, and MSI-high status; WES additionally enabled mutational signature analysis and neoantigen prediction.

**Detail:** Not all IHC/FISH-positive HER2 cases harbored ERBB2 amplification by NGS: 6 patients with IHC 2+/FISH-positive lacked NGS amplification, likely reflecting low-level or heterogeneous amplification. MSI status was assessed by MSIsensor; mutational signatures were decomposed using WES. WES was performed on 31/32 patients using the TEMPO pipeline (BWA-mem, Strelka2, Mutect2, FACETS for CNAs). The co-alteration landscape demonstrates that HER2-positive EG cancer is genomically heterogeneous beyond the ERBB2 amplification, potentially explaining the variability in depth and duration of response despite uniformly high ORR.

## Open Questions

- Does the superior OS (27.3 months) reflect synergistic anti-HER2/anti-PD-1 activity, improved chemosensitization, or patient selection bias in this single-arm study?
- Can ctDNA decline after cycle 1 (pembrolizumab + trastuzumab induction) serve as an early pharmacodynamic biomarker to guide continuation or modification of therapy?
- What genomic co-alterations beyond ERBB2 amplification level (e.g., CCNE1 amplification, KRAS mutation, MSI-high) predict resistance or response to the triplet regimen?
- Does PD-L1 CPS status interact with ERBB2 amplification level to further stratify prognosis and benefit from immunotherapy in HER2-positive EG cancer?
- Can the non-target escape pattern of progression (54% of progressors) be attributed to specific molecular mechanisms, such as spatial tumor heterogeneity in ERBB2 amplification?

## Limitations

- Single-arm, non-randomized design without a concurrent control — comparisons to historical benchmarks (ToGA trial) may be confounded by patient selection and treatment era
- Small cohort (n=37); powered to detect 6-month PFS improvement from 55% to 75% but underpowered for subgroup biomarker analyses
- Central HER2 confirmation not required pre-enrollment: 6/15 patients enrolled based on outside institution testing could not have HER2 positivity centrally confirmed, introducing potential misclassification
- Short median follow-up (13 months) limits mature OS and long-term durability data
- ctDNA analysis (Guardant360) not uniformly integrated with tissue NGS across all patients due to assay availability and timing
- PD-L1 CPS not available in 27% of patients, limiting definitive conclusions about PD-L1 as a predictive biomarker
