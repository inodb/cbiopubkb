## Summary

Analyzing 24,146 cancer patients from the MSK-IMPACT prospective sequencing cohort, this study shows that cancer therapy—particularly platinum agents, topoisomerase II inhibitors, and radiation—selectively expands clonal hematopoiesis (CH) clones carrying DNA damage response (DDR) gene mutations (TP53, PPM1D, CHEK2), while ASXL1 mutations are preferentially selected by smoking. Longitudinal serial sampling in 525 patients provides direct evidence that DDR clones outcompete others specifically under cytotoxic/radiation pressure in a dose-dependent manner. Pre-existing CH mutations were present at therapy-related myeloid neoplasm (tMN) diagnosis in 59% of cases, with TP53 CH conferring a 6.9-fold elevated tMN hazard.

## Key Findings

### F1: CH prevalence is 30% in cancer patients; DDR gene mutations enriched by prior cancer therapy

**Finding:** Clonal hematopoiesis is common in cancer patients (30%), with DDR gene mutations (TP53, PPM1D, CHEK2) specifically enriched following cancer treatment.

**Detail:** 11,076 unique CH mutations in 7,216 individuals (30% prevalence); median VAF 5.0% (range 2–78%). 52% classified as putative driver mutations (CH-PD). CH more frequent in previously treated vs. treatment-naive patients (OR=1.3, P=1×10⁻⁶). DDR genes: OR_TP53=2.8 (q=2×10⁻⁴); OR_PPM1D=4.3 (q<10⁻⁶); OR_CHEK2=4.5 (q=6×10⁻⁶). Spliceosome genes (SRSF2, SF3B1) most strongly linked to aging (OR up to 5.0). DNMT3A/TET2 not significantly affected by therapy exposure.

**Genes / cancer types:** `DNMT3A`, `TET2`, `ASXL1`, `TP53`, `PPM1D`, `CHEK2`, `SRSF2`, `SF3B1` · Pan-cancer

### F2: Therapy class specificity — platinum and topoisomerase II inhibitors most strongly drive DDR-CH

**Finding:** Among cancer therapies, external beam radiation (OR=1.4), platinum agents (OR=1.2), and topoisomerase II inhibitors (OR=1.3) are most strongly associated with CH-PD; targeted and immunotherapies are not.

**Detail:** Carboplatin specifically associated with CH (OR=1.4, P=0.001), while cisplatin (P=0.10) and oxaliplatin (P=0.88) were not. PPM1D mutations most strongly linked to platinum (OR=3.2, q<10⁻⁶), radionuclide therapy (OR=6.2, q=7×10⁻⁶), topoisomerase II inhibitors (OR=2.0, q=0.002), taxanes (OR=1.8, q=0.003), and radiation (OR=1.8, q=0.04). TP53 CH associated with platinum (OR=2.1), radiation (OR=1.8), and taxanes (OR=1.9). CHEK2 with platinum (OR=2.4) and topoisomerase II (OR=2.2). Dose-response confirmed for carboplatin (p-trend=3×10⁻⁵) and cisplatin (p-trend=0.04).

### F3: Longitudinal sampling confirms direct causal selection of DDR clones by cytotoxic therapy

**Finding:** Serial blood sampling in 525 patients provides direct evidence that DDR gene (TP53, PPM1D, CHEK2) clones grow specifically under cytotoxic/radiation therapy but not in untreated patients.

**Detail:** 74% of 525 patients had CH at first sampling; 95% of CH mutations present at both timepoints (median interval 23 months). Among treated patients: DDR clones showed faster growth vs. non-DDR clones (P<0.05 for TP53, CHEK2, PPM1D). In patients with one DDR + one non-DDR mutation: DDR clone dominated under therapy; non-DDR clone (e.g., DNMT3A) dominant without therapy. 28% of stable mutations showed growth, 10% decreased. Newly detected mutations more frequent in cytotoxic/radiation-treated patients (4%) vs. untreated (1%; P=0.06).

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53`, `PPM1D`, `CHEK2`, `DNMT3A` · Pan-cancer (solid tumors)

### F4: Pre-existing CH mutations present in 59% of tMN cases at time of primary cancer diagnosis

**Finding:** In 35 paired primary cancer / tMN cases, the CH mutation driving leukemic transformation was detectable at time of primary cancer sequencing in 59% of cases, with TP53 dominant.

**Detail:** 40% (14/35) of tMN patients had TP53 mutations; 10/14 TP53 CH mutations detectable at primary cancer workup. In 92% of TP53 tMN cases, complex karyotype or isolated chromosomal aneuploidy co-occurred at transformation. 91% of transformations involved acquisition of additional mutations (FLT3, KRAS, NRAS) beyond the initiating CH clone. In all 19 cases where CH predated tMN, the CH mutation was present at tMN diagnosis.

### F5: CH presence (VAF >2%) confers HR=6.9 for tMN; TP53 and spliceosome mutations show strongest risk

**Finding:** CH is a strong predictor of subsequent therapy-related myeloid neoplasm (tMN) in cancer patients, with hazard ratio 6.9; TP53 and spliceosome gene mutations carry the highest individual risk.

**Detail:** Analysis of 9,437 therapy-exposed cancer patients (75 tMN events). HR for CH VAF >2%: 6.9 (P<10⁻⁶). Risk increases with clone size and number of mutations. TP53 mutation strongest single-gene predictor; spliceosome genes (SRSF2, U2AF1, SF3B1) also strongly associated. Synthetic model for breast cancer: 96% of patients have <1% 10-year AML/MDS risk; top 1% risk patients face ~9% absolute increased risk from adjuvant chemotherapy.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53`, `SRSF2`, `U2AF1`, `SF3B1` · Pan-cancer; breast cancer model

**Data availability:** `available`

### F6: ASXL1 CH is driven by smoking, not therapy; distinct environmental determinism by gene

**Finding:** Different environmental exposures preferentially select distinct CH gene mutations: ASXL1 by smoking, DDR genes by cytotoxic therapy, spliceosome genes by aging.

**Detail:** ASXL1 CH OR=2.5 for smokers (q=1×10⁻⁴); current smokers OR=3.1 vs. former smokers OR=2.4 (difference non-significant P=0.4). SRSF2 and SF3B1 most strongly age-associated (OR up to 5.0). DNMT3A/TET2 frequency not significantly modulated by therapy. CH was less common in patients of Asian ancestry (OR=0.7, P=1×10⁻³).

**Genes / cancer types:** `ASXL1`, `SRSF2`, `SF3B1`, `DNMT3A`, `TET2` · Pan-cancer

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Can pre-treatment CH profiling be used clinically to inform adjuvant chemotherapy decisions, particularly for patients at highest tMN risk?
- Do platinum-specific CH effects differ meaningfully between carboplatin and cisplatin in prospective studies with larger cohorts?
- Can error-corrected sequencing methods identify clinically significant CH at VAF <2%, expanding the risk prediction window?
- What is the optimal monitoring interval for CH clones in cancer patients receiving cytotoxic therapy?
- Does immunotherapy or targeted therapy genuinely not select for CH, or are current panel sizes insufficient to detect subtle effects?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Retrospective design for cross-sectional analysis; therapy exposure information complete only for patients treated entirely at MSK (59% of total cohort).
- MSK-IMPACT panel covers cancer driver genes only; non-driver CH mutations not captured.
- Minimum VAF threshold of 2% limits detection of small clones.
- Longitudinal cohort included no patients who developed tMN during follow-up, limiting direct causal inference.
- Synthetic risk model is exploratory and not validated prospectively; not intended for clinical implementation.
- Single-cell sequencing not available to confirm intra-patient clone competition dynamics.
