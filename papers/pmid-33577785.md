## Summary

Comprehensive CPTAC proteogenomic characterization of 99 treatment-naïve adult GBMs integrating 10 data platforms: WGS, WES, RNA-seq, miRNA-seq, single-nucleus RNA-seq (18 samples), DNA methylation, TMT-based proteome, phosphoproteome, acetylome, lipidome, and metabolome. Compared to prior TCGA expression-based subtyping, multi-omics NMF clustering reclassified 29% of tumors to different subtypes. Key discoveries include: phosphorylated PTPN11 and PLCG1 as shared downstream signaling hubs for EGFR and PDGFRA; four novel immune subtypes validated by snRNA-seq and IHC; H2B histone acetylation driven by BRD1/3/4, CREBBP, and EP300 in classical-like GBMs; and subtype-specific lipid and metabolic profiles. IDH-mutant GBMs show distinct metabolic reprogramming including elevated 2-HG and altered glycolytic intermediates.

## Key Findings

### F1: Multi-omics NMF clustering identifies three molecular subtypes with 29% reclassification versus TCGA expression-based subtyping

**Finding:** NMF clustering of CNV, RNA, protein, and phosphoprotein abundances across 99 GBMs identifies three subtypes — nmf1 (proneural-like; n=29), nmf2 (mesenchymal-like; n=37), and nmf3 (classical-like; n=26) — that largely recapitulate TCGA expression subtypes but reclassify 29% of tumors. Tumors with "mixed" subtype membership (n=12, low membership scores in ≥2 subtypes) exhibit significantly worse prognosis (log-rank P=1.7e-4).

**Detail:** Each subtype shows distinct pathway enrichment across all omics layers. nmf1/proneural-like: synaptic vesicle cycle, neurotransmission (RNA/protein), TCA cycle and amino acid metabolism acetylation. nmf2/mesenchymal-like: innate immune response, neutrophil degranulation, ECM organization, glycolysis acetylation. nmf3/classical-like: mRNA splicing and RNA metabolism (RNA/protein), chromatin modifier and DNA repair protein acetylation. Three proteins independently associated with poor overall survival: low HIST3H2BB (P=0.0034), high MT-CYB (P=0.03), high PRODH (P=0.096). DNA methylation identifies six subtypes; MGMT promoter hypermethylation in 42% of tumors correlates with decreased MGMT RNA and protein (P=4.9e-11 and 2.6e-6, respectively).

**Genes / cancer types:** `EGFR`, `PDGFRA`, `MET`, `NF1`, `PTEN`, `IDH1`, `MGMT`, `ATRX`, `TERT` · Glioblastoma

### F2: PTPN11 and PLCG1 phosphorylation serve as shared RTK signaling hubs downstream of both EGFR and PDGFRA

**Finding:** Kinase-substrate analysis across 99 GBMs reveals that PTPN11 (SHP2) phosphorylation at Y62 and PLCG1 (PLCγ1) phosphorylation at Y783 are consistently elevated in EGFR-altered tumors despite unchanged total protein levels, identifying phosphorylation-dependent activation as the regulatory mechanism. PDGFRA also drives PTPN11 phosphorylation at Y546/Y584, making PTPN11 a convergent signaling node for both EGFR and PDGFRA RTK pathways.

**Detail:** EGFR SVs (structural variants) were present in 45 tumors, all with concordant copy number amplification, elevated RNA, protein, and EGFR-Y1172 phosphorylation — confirming EGFR pathway activation. In EGFR-altered samples, trans-effects included elevated CTNNB1 (β-catenin) protein (despite decreased mRNA), increased PHLDA1 and PHLDA3 protein (without RNA change), SOX9 upregulation (validated by IHC in 5/6 tumors), and RB1-S795/Y805 phosphorylation. The dual regulation of PTPN11 by both EGFR and PDGFRA — forming the PTPN11/GAB1/GRB2 complex that activates RAS — suggests PTPN11 as a therapeutic bottleneck for RTK-driven GBM. PLCG1-Y783 phosphorylation (activating site) was significantly elevated in EGFR-altered tumors (Wilcoxon FDR < 0.01) with no change in total PLCG1 protein (FDR = 0.11), providing a mechanism for EGFR activation of PLCG1-driven proliferation and invasiveness.

### F3: Four immune subtypes with distinct immune cell compositions, checkpoint marker profiles, and clinical associations

**Finding:** Consensus clustering of xCell immune enrichment scores from bulk omics identifies four immune subtypes: im1 (high macrophage+lymphocyte, immune-high), im2 (macrophage-predominant, low lymphocyte), im3 (lymphocyte/NK-enriched, IDH-mutant-enriched), and im4 (immune-low/desert, collagen and cell cycle pathways). These subtypes are validated by snRNA-seq across 18 tumors, IHC of CD3/CD68/CD163/PD-1/PD-L1, and deep learning H&E morphology analysis.

**Detail:** Mesenchymal subtype (nmf2) is strongly enriched in im1 and im2 (Fisher P=1.65e-15); IDH-mutant tumors overrepresented in im3 (P=1.02e-5). im4 has substantially fewer T cells (1.3% avg) and TAMs (6% avg) vs. im1–3 (7% and 19% avg, respectively). Immune targets elevated in im1 include PD-1, TIM-3, CCL2, CSF-1R — rationale for combining anti-CSF-1R macrophage depletion with checkpoint blockade. im3 is particularly notable: relative depletion of immunosuppressive TAM infiltration with enrichment of T cells and NK cells, and contains most IDH-mutant tumors — raising the possibility that IDH-mutant GBMs may be more immunotherapy-responsive. Deep learning H&E classifier identifies giant cells in im4 tiles vs. inflammatory infiltrates in im1–3, linking morphology to molecular subtypes.

### F4: H2B histone acetylation driven by BRD1/3/4, CREBBP, EP300 in classical-like GBM; negatively correlated with immune infiltration

**Finding:** Unsupervised clustering of >30 acetylation sites on histones H1, H2A, H2B, H3.3, and H4 identifies a subset of GBMs with elevated H2B N-terminal acetylation enriched in classical-like (nmf3) tumors. Lasso regression identifies CREBBP, EP300, BRD1, BRD3, and BRD4 as key regulators of H2B acetylation. H2B acetylation shows significant negative correlation with TME immune enrichment scores and positive correlation with SUMOylation pathway components SUMO1 and UBE2I.

**Detail:** CREBBP protein/acetylation levels associate with H2B-K12, K13, K16, K17, K21 sites; EP300 with H2B-K21, K24. BRD1/3/4, which bind acetylated histones to mediate transcription, show concordant protein and acetylation correlations. H2B acetylation pathways: positively correlated — spliceosome, nuclear receptors, SUMOylation; negatively correlated — ferroptosis, mast cells, reactive oxygen species (suggesting H2B hyperacetylation may suppress ferroptosis vulnerability). UBE2I (SUMO E2 conjugating enzyme) correlates moderately with CDK6 (Pearson r=0.413), a UBE2I target stabilized by SUMOylation. The H2B acetylation–immune infiltration anticorrelation suggests that classical-like, immune-low GBMs with high BRD activity may be candidates for BET inhibitor therapy to remodel the epigenetic state.

### F5: Subtype-specific lipid profiles and IDH-mutant metabolomic reprogramming — elevated 2-HG, glycolytic intermediates, GLUD1

**Finding:** Lipidomic profiling of 75 GBMs identifies >500 differentially abundant lipids across subtypes. Mesenchymal-like GBMs show elevated triacylglycerols (TGs) and depleted phosphatidylcholines (PCs); proneural-like GBMs are enriched for very long chain fatty acids and polyunsaturated fatty acid-containing glycerophospholipids. IDH-mutant GBMs show accumulation of 2-HG (median log2 FC=3.62 vs. IDH-WT), elevated glycolytic intermediates, and reduced glutamate, with GLUD1 protein upregulated as a compensatory mechanism.

**Detail:** ACSL6 (acyl-CoA synthetase, required for DHA incorporation into phospholipids) is drastically reduced in tumors vs. normal brain, particularly in mesenchymal-like GBMs. Proneural-like tumors retain higher ACSL6 and DHA-phospholipid content, resembling normal brain lipid profiles. Mesenchymal-like GBMs uniquely upregulate ACSL4 and ALOX5 (arachidonate 5-lipoxygenase), enzymes that incorporate arachidonic/adrenic acid into PE lipids and catalyze PUFA oxidation — potentially increasing oxidized PE content and ferroptosis vulnerability. DG levels significantly correlate with AKT1, PLCD3, and PLCG1 protein expression, linking lipid signaling to RTK pathway activation. In IDH mutants, elevated GLUD1 expression (Pearson r=−0.29 with glutamate levels) supports accelerated glutamate-to-α-KG conversion feeding 2-HG production.

## Open Questions

- Can the im3 immune subtype (T cell/NK-enriched, IDH-mutant-enriched) predict response to immune checkpoint inhibitors in GBM, which have uniformly failed Phase III trials to date?
- Does the PTPN11/PLCG1 phosphorylation hub represent a druggable therapeutic target that could overcome adaptive resistance to EGFR inhibitors?
- What is the functional significance of the BRD-driven H2B hyperacetylation in classical-like GBMs — does BET inhibition (e.g., JQ1) suppress growth specifically in nmf3/classical-like tumors?
- Can the ferroptosis vulnerability suggested by ACSL4/ALOX5 upregulation in mesenchymal-like GBMs be therapeutically exploited?
- Does GLUD1 upregulation in IDH-mutant GBMs create a metabolic vulnerability exploitable by GLUD1 inhibitors (e.g., EGCG)?
- Why do 29% of tumors change classification moving from TCGA expression-based to multi-omics subtyping, and do reclassified tumors have different treatment outcomes?

## Limitations

- No matched normal brain tissue from the same patients (GTEx frontal cortex used as normal reference)
- Proteomics data and lipidomics/metabolomics data are not in cBioPortal — limits accessibility for cBioPortal-based re-analysis
- Small IDH-mutant cohort (n=7, including 6 IDH1 R132H) limits statistical power for IDH-mutant-specific analyses
- snRNA-seq available for only 18/99 GBMs — immune subtype validation may not fully represent the entire cohort
- Standard of care in GBM has not changed substantially in two decades; clinical outcome data may be confounded by differential surgical resection completeness
- Lipidomics performed on only 75/99 tumors; metabolomics sample size also smaller than the full cohort
- Phosphoproteomic data (PTPN11-Y62, PLCG1-Y783) not accessible via cBioPortal — requires CPTAC Data Portal
- Deep learning H&E analysis uses tiled slides with t-SNE dimensionality reduction; histologic interpretations of giant cells in im4 require prospective validation
