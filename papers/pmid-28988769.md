## Summary

This TCGA study provides a comprehensive multi-platform molecular characterization of 412 chemotherapy-naive muscle-invasive bladder cancers (MIBCs). APOBEC cytidine deaminase mutagenesis dominates the mutational landscape, accounting for 67% of SNVs, and high APOBEC-signature mutation burden is paradoxically associated with improved 5-year survival (75% in MSig1 cluster). mRNA expression clustering identifies five subtypes—luminal-papillary, luminal-infiltrated, luminal, basal-squamous, and neuronal—each with distinct genomic drivers, histologic features, and survival outcomes. A newly described neuronal subtype (5% of cases) carries the worst prognosis despite rarely displaying neuroendocrine histology. Integration across mRNA, lncRNA, miRNA, DNA methylation, copy number, and RPPA platforms converges on subtype-stratified therapeutic hypotheses, including FGFR inhibitors for luminal-papillary, checkpoint immunotherapy for luminal-infiltrated and basal-squamous, and platinum-etoposide for the neuronal subtype.

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: APOBEC Mutagenesis Dominates the Mutational Landscape and Stratifies Survival
- **Finding**: APOBEC-a and APOBEC-b mutation signatures account for 67% of all SNVs. Four mutational signature clusters (MSig1–4) are associated with survival: MSig1 (high APOBEC, high mutation burden) shows 75% 5-year survival; MSig2 (low mutation rate) shows only 22%.
- **Detail**: 58 significantly mutated genes (SMGs) were identified by MutSig2CV, 34 of which are newly identified in this expanded cohort. The overall median non-synonymous mutation rate is 5.8/Mb. Neoantigen load, strongly correlated with APOBEC-signature burden, is an independent predictor of survival. 64% of APOBEC-signature mutations are clonal, suggesting early mutagenic activity. ERCC2 mutations cluster in MSig4 and are enriched in smokers.
- **Genes**: APOBEC3A, APOBEC3B, ERCC2, POLE, TP53, KMT2C, ATM, FAT1, CREBBP, ERBB2, SPTAN1, KMT2A
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: mutational signatures, APOBEC, neoantigen load, tumor mutational burden, clonality, smoking

### Finding 2: Five mRNA Expression Subtypes with Distinct Biology and Prognosis
- **Finding**: NMF consensus clustering of RNA-seq data identifies five MIBC subtypes: luminal-papillary (35%), luminal-infiltrated (19%), luminal (6%), basal-squamous (35%), and neuronal (5%). Subtypes are associated with overall survival (p=4×10⁻⁴).
- **Detail**: Luminal subtypes express uroplakins (UPK2, UPK1A) and urothelial differentiation markers (FOXA1, GATA3, PPARG). Basal-squamous tumors express CD44, KRT5, KRT14, and squamous markers; enriched in TP53 mutations and more frequent in women. Luminal-infiltrated tumors have the lowest purity (median 0.46), high EMT scores, and express PD-L1/PD-1 immune markers. The neuronal subtype carries the worst prognosis, is driven by TP53/RB1 loss, and rarely shows NE histology.
- **Genes**: FOXA1, GATA3, PPARG, UPK1A, UPK2, CD44, KRT5, KRT14, KRT20, SNX31, CD274, PDCD1, TP53, RB1, E2F3
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: molecular subtypes, EMT, luminal, basal, neuronal, urothelial differentiation, immune infiltration, PD-L1

### Finding 3: FGFR3 Alterations Define the Luminal-Papillary Subtype and Are Therapeutic Targets
- **Finding**: FGFR3 mutations, amplification, overexpression, and FGFR3-TACC3 fusions are enriched in the luminal-papillary subtype (44% of cases have a dominant FGFR3 role). FGFR3 mutations are more frequent in lower-stage tumors and associated with better survival.
- **Detail**: 42 of 57 FGFR3-mutated tumors are luminal-papillary; 8 of 10 FGFR3-TACC3 fusions occur in this subtype. PPARG is involved in TSEN2-PPARG and MKRN2-PPARG fusions (n=6 total), with retained DNA-binding and ligand-binding domains suggesting functional activity. Pan-FGFR inhibitors are proposed as potential treatment for luminal-papillary MIBC. Luminal-papillary tumors have low carcinoma-in-situ scores and appear to arise from non-invasive papillary precursors.
- **Genes**: FGFR3, TACC3, PPARG, TSEN2, MKRN2
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: gene fusions, FGFR3, targeted therapy, luminal-papillary, pan-FGFR inhibitor, PPARG fusions

### Finding 4: Chromatin Modifier Genes Are Frequently and Predominantly Inactivated
- **Finding**: 10 of the 39 SMGs with mutation frequency >5% are chromatin-modifying or chromatin-regulatory genes; mutations in these genes are 50% frameshift or nonsense vs. 26% for other SMGs, indicating strong functional relevance.
- **Detail**: Chromatin modifiers include the histone demethylase KDM6A, methyltransferases KMT2A/C/D, acetylases CREBBP/EP300/KANSL1, SWI/SNF member ARID1A, and Polycomb genes ASXL1/ASXL2. ARID1A, CREBBP, and KDM6A also sustain genomic deletions. Recent preclinical studies implicate BRD4-EZH2 as a growth pathway in bladder cancer, especially with KDM6A loss, creating opportunities for BET inhibitors or EZH2 inhibitors.
- **Genes**: KDM6A, KMT2A, KMT2C, KMT2D, CREBBP, EP300, KANSL1, ARID1A, ASXL1, ASXL2
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: chromatin remodeling, epigenetic regulation, inactivating mutations, BET inhibitor, EZH2, therapeutic targets

### Finding 5: Neuronal Subtype Has Worst Survival Despite Lacking Neuroendocrine Histology
- **Finding**: The neuronal mRNA subtype (5% of cases, n=20) has the worst 5-year survival, expresses neuronal and neuroendocrine markers, and is defined by TP53/RB1 pathway loss—but 17 of 20 tumors lack histopathologic NE features.
- **Detail**: 50% of neuronal-subtype tumors have concurrent TP53 and RB1 mutations (or TP53 + E2F3 amplification); 85% have p53/cell-cycle pathway alterations overall. High cell-cycle gene signature reflects a proliferative state. Etoposide-cisplatin regimens are proposed by analogy with small-cell NE tumors at other sites. The subtype was independently validated in a separate cohort.
- **Genes**: TP53, RB1, E2F3, MDM2
- **Data availability**: available
- **Tool support**: native
- **Concepts**: neuronal subtype, neuroendocrine, small cell, p53/Rb pathway, poor prognosis, etoposide-cisplatin

### Finding 6: lncRNA and miRNA Subtypes Independently Predict Survival Beyond mRNA Subtypes
- **Finding**: Unsupervised clustering of 171 lncRNAs identifies 4 clusters and of 142 miRNAs identifies 4 clusters, both associated with 5-year survival, EMT score, and CIS gene-set expression. Multivariate Cox regression retains lncRNA and miRNA subtype as independent survival predictors.
- **Detail**: lncRNA cluster 3, a better-survival subset of luminal-papillary, is enriched for FGFR3 mutations/fusions, high tumor purity, and papillary histology. MiRNA cluster 3 shows the best survival with high miR-200 expression, low EMT, and low PD-L1. miRNA cluster 4, a subset of basal-squamous, shows elevated TP63 and EGFR regulon activity and poor survival. Several cancer-associated lncRNAs (DANCR, MALAT1, NEAT1, UCA1, GAS5, NORAD) are differentially abundant across subtypes.
- **Genes**: FGFR3, TP63, EGFR, CD274 (PD-L1), PDCD1 (PD-1)
- **Data availability**: partial
- **Tool support**: api
- **Concepts**: lncRNA, miRNA, non-coding RNA, molecular subtypes, EMT, survival, regulon

### Finding 7: DNA Methylation Identifies Hypomethylated Luminal-Papillary Subset and 158 Epigenetically Silenced Genes
- **Finding**: Unsupervised methylation clustering identifies 5 major clusters. A hypomethylation cluster (cluster 4) strongly overlaps with luminal-papillary mRNA subtype, FGFR3 mutations, and papillary histology; it shows better survival. Integrative analysis identifies 158 epigenetically silenced genes.
- **Detail**: Hypomethylation cluster 4 tumors are high purity, FGFR3-mutated, CDKN2A-deleted, node-negative, younger patients (median 61 vs. 69 years). Of 158 epigenetically silenced genes, CDKN2A, FAT1, and CASP8 show mutual exclusivity between mutation and methylation-based silencing. No promoter hypermethylation was found for TP53, PTEN, TSC1, TSC2, NF1, NF2, or RB1.
- **Genes**: CDKN2A, FAT1, CASP8, FGFR3, LXN, PARP6, NAPRT, SPATC1L
- **Data availability**: partial
- **Tool support**: api
- **Concepts**: DNA methylation, epigenetic silencing, hypomethylation, luminal-papillary, FGFR3, CDKN2A

## Open Questions

1. Does MSig1 (high APOBEC, high neoantigen load) prospectively identify bladder cancer patients who benefit most from immune checkpoint therapy, and can APOBEC burden serve as a clinical biomarker?
2. Can the neuronal subtype be reliably identified by a reduced immunohistochemical panel (e.g., neuroendocrine/neuronal markers) rather than full RNA-seq, enabling clinical trial enrollment?
3. Is the luminal-infiltrated subtype's apparent resistance to cisplatin-based NAC and sensitivity to anti-PD-L1 therapy (atezolizumab) causal or confounded by the EMT/stromal microenvironment composition?
4. Which of the 158 epigenetically silenced genes represent druggable vulnerabilities, and can hypomethylating agents or HDAC inhibitors selectively exploit these silencing events?

## Limitations

1. Median follow-up for surviving patients was only 20.9 months, limiting the statistical power of long-term survival analyses and potentially underestimating the prognostic impact of some subtypes.
2. Neoadjuvant chemotherapy use after tumor acquisition (n=12) was not included in survival models; subsequent therapies were not tracked, making it difficult to attribute survival differences to subtype-specific biology vs. treatment.
3. The RPPA proteomic platform covered only 208 antibodies and 343 of the 412 tumors, providing an incomplete view of the proteome and limiting the generalizability of proteomic subtype findings.
4. Subtype classification was developed on TCGA data and may not directly transfer to clinical settings without validated reduced gene-set classifiers and prospective cohort testing.
