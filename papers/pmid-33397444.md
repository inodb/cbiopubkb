## Summary

Integrative multi-omic profiling (WES, RNA-seq, DNA methylation EPIC arrays) of 40 upper tract urothelial carcinomas (UTUC; renal pelvis and ureter), including 20 non-muscle-invasive (NMI) and 20 muscle-invasive (MI) cases. Key contributions: (1) Identification of ZFP36L1 as a novel significantly mutated tumor suppressor (20%); (2) ZFP36 family mutations collectively in 26.7%; (3) Two DNA methylation epi-clusters (EpiC-high/EpiC-low) that stratify UTUC by muscle-invasive status, immune infiltration, SWI/SNF gene mutations, and patient survival; (4) FGFR3 mutations associate with hypomethylation, immune-desert phenotype, and favorable prognosis; (5) Mutational signatures dominated by APOBEC (signature 2/13), endogenous deamination (signature 1), and an unknown signature 16. This is a relatively small, French-institution cohort (n=40), supplemented by MSKCC UTUC validation and TCGA BLCA comparisons.

## Key Findings

### F1: ZFP36L1 is a novel significantly mutated tumor suppressor in UTUC (20%), with ZFP36 family mutations at 26.7%

**Finding:** ZFP36L1 (zinc-finger RNA-binding protein) is a significantly mutated gene (FDR=2.62×10⁻³) in 20% of UTUC, predominantly by frameshift insertions/deletions (6/8 mutations). Combined mutations in ZFP36, ZFP36L1, and ZFP36L2 family members reach 26.7% — the highest rate across any cancer type profiled by TCGA.

**Detail:** ZFP36L1 knockdown in bladder cancer cells induces EMT (loss of E-cadherin, spindle morphology) and increases cell migration without affecting proliferation, supporting a tumor-suppressor role through regulation of ARE-containing mRNAs. ZFP36L1 mutations are enriched in UTUC compared to bladder carcinoma; ZFP36L2 E249X is a hotspot mutation also seen in TCGA BLCA. ZFP36 family mutations are associated with higher mutational load in both UTUC and TCGA BLCA. In bladder carcinoma, ZFP36 family mutations correlate with improved overall survival (pooled TCGA/DFCI/BGI cohort, n=562, p=0.01). ZFP36L1 mutations co-occur with KDM6A and STAG2 mutations.

**Genes / cancer types:** `ZFP36L1`, `ZFP36L2`, `ZFP36`, `KDM6A`, `STAG2` · UTUC, bladder urothelial carcinoma

### F2: FGFR3 mutations (50%) drive NMI, immune-desert tumors and associate with favorable prognosis

**Finding:** FGFR3 is the most significantly mutated gene in UTUC (50%, FDR=3.15×10⁻³). FGFR3 mutations are enriched in NMI vs. MI tumors (73% vs. 27%, p=0.027), associated with significantly lower immune infiltration (MeTIL score), lower stromal scores, and improved OS (p=0.005) and PFS (p=0.007).

**Detail:** FGFR3 mutations co-occur with KMT2C mutations (log₂ OR=3.1, p=0.04), validated in the MSKCC UTUC cohort. A single FGFR3-TACC3 fusion was identified in one case (RNA-seq, n=20). FGFR3-mutated tumors display genome-wide hypomethylation (97.8% of 84,717 differentially methylated probes are hypomethylated in FGFR3-mutant vs. wild-type), and cluster within the EpiC-low subtype. The FGFR3-mutated immune-desert phenotype is independent of muscle invasiveness status, potentially explaining why FGFR3-targeted therapies (erdafitinib) may be combined with immunotherapy. This finding was validated in TCGA BLCA where FGFR3 mutations correlate with lower immune infiltration.

**Genes / cancer types:** `FGFR3`, `KMT2C`, `TACC3` · UTUC, bladder urothelial carcinoma

### F3: DNA methylation epi-clusters (EpiC-high/EpiC-low) stratify UTUC by prognosis, invasiveness, and immune infiltration

**Finding:** Unsupervised methylation clustering identifies two robust epi-clusters: EpiC-high (hypermethylated, MI-enriched, immune-infiltrated, SWI/SNF-mutated, poor outcome) and EpiC-low (hypomethylated, NMI-enriched, immune-desert, FGFR3-mutated, favorable outcome). The epi-cluster is a stronger prognostic factor than standard clinicopathological variables.

**Detail:** EpiC-high (n=23; 65.7%): 50% harbor SWI/SNF gene mutations (vs. 0% in EpiC-low, p=0.01), enriched for MI tumors (69.6%), higher MeTIL score, shorter OS (HR=6.57; 95% CI 1.87–23.04; p=0.038) and distant metastasis-free survival (HR=6.55; p=0.036). EpiC-low (n=12; 34.3%): 90% FGFR3-mutated (vs. 28% in EpiC-high, p=0.004), NMI-enriched (91.7%), immune-desert. DMRs in EpiC-high are enriched for PRC2 targets, MLL targets, SMARCA2 targets, and ZEB1 targets. The UTUC methylation classification was validated in TCGA BLCA (BLCA-C1 = hypermethylated, immune-high, poor prognosis; BLCA-C2 = FGFR3-enriched, immune-low, favorable prognosis; p=0.035).

**Genes / cancer types:** `ARID1A`, `SMARCA4`, `PBRM1`, `FGFR3`, `SWI/SNF complex` · UTUC, bladder urothelial

### F4: APOBEC mutational signatures dominate UTUC; FGFR3 mutations inversely associate with immune infiltration independent of stage

**Finding:** APOBEC signatures (2 and 13) are predominant in 50% of cases (NMF cluster C3, n=15). Three NMF clusters identified: signature 16 (C1, n=7), signature 1/endogenous deamination (C2, n=8), and APOBEC/13 (C3, n=15). FGFR3-mutated tumors consistently show lower immune infiltration regardless of NMF cluster or muscle invasiveness.

**Detail:** The APOBEC etiology is consistent with bladder/urothelial biology. Contrary to expectations, APOBEC signatures were not associated with higher TIL scores (MeTIL) in this UTUC cohort. The FGFR3-immune-desert link suggests that FGFR3 signaling may suppress immune cell recruitment — a mechanism potentially reversible by FGFR inhibition combined with immune checkpoint blockade. Average mutational burden: 2.9 ± 3.5 mutations/Mb; median 1.5/Mb (range 0.3–15.7).

**Genes / cancer types:** `FGFR3`, `ARID1A`, `ZFP36L1`, `MLL2` · UTUC

### F5: UTUC shares genomic features with bladder carcinoma but with distinct gene frequencies — FGFR3, ZFP36L1, KMT2C enriched; PIK3CA depleted

**Finding:** Compared to NMI bladder carcinoma, NMI-UTUC shows higher rates of KMT2C mutation and lower PIK3CA mutation. Compared to MI bladder carcinoma (TCGA, n=412), MI-UTUC shows significantly more GANAB, CRIPAK, and ZFP36L1 mutations. Focal CNAs include CDKN2A/B loss (9p21.3), YWHAZ gain (8q22.3), MDM2 gain (12q15), and CCNE1 gain (19q12).

**Detail:** The 8q22.3 gain (YWHAZ) is enriched in MI tumors and mutually exclusive with FGFR3 mutations (p=0.03); co-occurring with TP53 mutations (p=0.02). Integrative iClusterBayes analysis on 28 cases with both WES and methylation identified FGFR3 (posterior probability 0.99), SWI/SNF (0.63), KMT2D (0.63), and TP53 (0.56) as key contributors. This comparative analysis positions UTUC as a distinct urothelial entity warranting tumor-type-specific genomic characterization rather than extrapolation from bladder cancer.

## Open Questions

- What is the mechanism by which FGFR3 mutations induce genome-wide hypomethylation in urothelial carcinoma?
- Can epi-cluster classification (EpiC-high/EpiC-low) guide immunotherapy selection (EpiC-high immune-infiltrated vs. EpiC-low immune-desert)?
- What is the functional role of ZFP36L1 as a tumor suppressor — which ARE-containing mRNAs does it regulate in urothelium?
- Are patients with EpiC-low/FGFR3-mutated UTUC candidates for erdafitinib or other FGFR3 inhibitors, and does this correlate with methylation class?
- How do UTUC epi-clusters relate to the TCGA consensus bladder cancer molecular subtypes (luminal papillary, luminal unstable, basal/squamous, etc.)?

## Limitations

- Small primary cohort (n=40) limits statistical power; WES on 30/40 cases.
- All samples microsatellite stable; MSI UTUC (e.g., Lynch syndrome-associated) not represented.
- RNA-seq available for only 20 cases; transcriptomic findings are underpowered.
- Retrospective design; no treatment-stratified outcome analysis.
- Validation primarily in TCGA BLCA (bladder), not an independent UTUC cohort, given limited public UTUC data availability.
