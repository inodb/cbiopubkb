## Summary

A multi-omic single-cell study of 297 clonally expanded melanocytes from 31 donors reveals two coexisting subpopulations in human sun-exposed skin: low-mutation-burden (LowMut) and high-mutation-burden (HighMut) melanocytes. LowMut cells carry clock-like mutational signatures (SBS1/SBS5), express neural-crest/stem-cell genes, are morphologically smaller and less dendritic, and reside predominantly in hair follicles — a UV-shielded niche. HighMut cells accumulate UV-induced signature SBS7, express differentiation/pigmentation genes, and populate the interfollicular epidermis. Spatial transcriptomics (10X Xenium) validates the positional model. The authors propose that hair follicle melanocytes periodically migrate to replenish the UV-damaged epidermis. The study demonstrates the unique power of combining somatic mutational histories with gene expression and spatial context in a cell atlas approach.

## Key Findings

### F1: Two coexisting melanocyte subpopulations — LowMut and HighMut — persist in sun-damaged skin

**Finding:** In skin biopsies with high overall mutation burden (>3 mut/Mb), a consistent subpopulation of melanocytes with remarkably low mutation burden (bottom quartile) coexists with heavily UV-damaged melanocytes (top quartile). These subpopulations are defined relative to the tissue median, not an absolute threshold.

**Detail:** Mutation burdens of individual melanocytes were plotted against the tissue median from the same biopsy. Even in heavily sun-exposed tissue, the lower quartile of melanocytes maintained substantially fewer mutations than the tissue average, implying protected-niche residence rather than differential UV exposure. Upper/lower quartile trendlines diverge progressively with increasing tissue damage. Only biopsies with tissue mutation burden ≥3 mut/Mb were used to ensure reliable subpopulation separation.

**Genes / cancer types:** `MITF`, `MLANA`, `PMEL`, `TYRP1` · Melanocytes (normal skin); relevance to melanoma biology

### F2: LowMut melanocytes carry clock-like signatures; HighMut melanocytes are dominated by UV signature SBS7

**Finding:** Mutational signature decomposition reveals that HighMut melanocytes have a greater fraction of cytosine-to-thymine transitions at dipyrimidine contexts (UV damage) and significantly higher SBS7 fractions, while LowMut melanocytes are enriched for clock-like SBS1 and SBS5 — implying protection from UV radiation throughout their history.

**Detail:** Signature analysis used SigProfilerAssignment (v0.1.8) against COSMIC v3.4 signatures. In HighMut cells, median SBS7 (UV) fraction is significantly higher (Wilcoxon p < 0.0001). LowMut cells have higher SBS1 (deamination) and SBS5 (clock-like replication) fractions. This pattern is internally consistent with the hypothesis that LowMut cells have spent most of their lives shielded from UV radiation. Only cells with ≥10 mutations were included in formal signature analysis.

**Genes / cancer types:** `BRAF`, `NRAS`, `MC1R`, `HERC2` · Melanocytes (normal); melanoma precursor biology

**Data availability:** `not-available`

### F3: Distinct gene expression programs: LowMut cells are stem-like; HighMut cells are differentiated

**Finding:** Differential gene expression (DESeq2, 10% FDR) shows LowMut melanocytes overexpress neural crest, smooth muscle, and connective tissue genes (stem-like program), while HighMut cells overexpress pigmentation, antigen presentation, and immune response genes (differentiated program). The LowMut gene set overlaps significantly with published melanocyte stem cell (MSC) signatures and the HighMut gene set overlaps with adult/differentiated melanocyte signatures.

**Detail:** LowMut upregulated genes include neural-crest markers: `VCAN`, `FBN1`, `PALLD`, `ITM2A` (connective tissue); `TAGLN`, `MYL9`, `MYLK` (smooth muscle); `SEMA3C`, `TCF4`, `DAAM2` (neuronal). HighMut upregulated genes include `HMOX1`, `ABCC2`, `MC1R`, `HERC2`, `LIPA`, `HLA-DPA1`. Gene set enrichment against MSigDB confirmed these ontologies. WIMMS analysis places LowMut cells in "AXL/Neuronal/Invasive" melanocyte state and HighMut cells in "Differentiated" melanocyte state — two axes relevant to melanoma phenotype switching.

**Genes / cancer types:** `VCAN`, `FBN1`, `TAGLN`, `SEMA3C`, `TCF4`, `HMOX1`, `ABCC2`, `MC1R`, `HERC2`, `HLA-DPA1` · Melanocytes / Melanoma

### F4: Morphological differences — LowMut cells are smaller and less dendritic than HighMut cells

**Finding:** Quantitative morphometry of clonal colonies shows HighMut melanocytes have significantly higher perimeter-to-surface-area ratios (complexity), more dendritic extensions, and larger cell areas compared to LowMut melanocytes (Student's t-test p < 0.005 for all metrics).

**Detail:** Colony images captured by Zeiss Axiovert or Invitrogen EVOS; cells segmented with FIJI; perimeter, surface area, and dendrite count computed per cell. Morphological complexity (perimeter/area ratio) is a proxy for dendritic arborization. High complexity in HighMut cells mirrors known morphology of mature epidermal melanocytes in the interfollicular epidermis (dendritic, large), while LowMut cells resemble the ovoid, compact melanocytes found in the hair bulge stem-cell niche.

**Genes / cancer types:** `MITF`, `MLANA` · Melanocytes

### F5: Spatial transcriptomics reveals hair follicle as UV-protected reservoir; LowMut cells migrate to epidermis

**Finding:** 10X Xenium spatial transcriptomics of normal skin shows HighMut-signature melanocytes are almost exclusively in the interfollicular epidermis, while LowMut-signature melanocytes predominate in hair follicles and, when in the epidermis, cluster near follicular openings — consistent with a migration model where follicle-resident stem cells periodically replenish the UV-damaged epidermis.

**Detail:** A 360-gene Xenium panel (generic skin panel + all LowMut/HighMut marker genes) was applied to one FFPE skin section (~96 million transcripts, ~300K cells) from a 63-year-old male with solar elastosis. Melanocytes were scored by relative expression of LowMut vs. HighMut gene signatures. Hair follicle compartment: almost exclusively LowMut (Exact Poisson test p < 0.001). Interfollicular epidermis: predominantly HighMut; LowMut cells near follicular openings. LowMut cells in epidermis are smaller by Xenium cell area (Tukey HSD p < 0.001). The study proposes that UV-induced epidermal damage triggers waves of melanocyte emigration from the follicular bulge — paralleling vitiligo repigmentation by follicular melanocytes under UV therapy.

**Genes / cancer types:** `MITF`, `MLANA`, `MC1R`, `VCAN`, `TCF4` · Melanocytes / Melanoma

**Data availability:** `not-available`

## Open Questions

- Do LowMut (follicle-resident) melanocytes represent the true cells of origin for melanoma, and do they retain fewer UV mutations because they are the proliferating stem-cell pool?
- How does the frequency of LowMut melanocytes change with age, cumulative UV exposure, and in individuals with high melanoma risk (e.g., MC1R variants, HERC2 polymorphisms)?
- Are melanoma subtypes (superficial spreading vs. nodular vs. desmoplastic) traceable to LowMut vs. HighMut cells of origin?
- Can WIMMS or similar computational tools applied to cBioPortal melanoma datasets identify tumors resembling "LowMut" (AXL/invasive) vs. "HighMut" (differentiated) states and correlate them with prognosis?
- Does therapeutic UV (vitiligo treatment) that recruits follicular melanocytes into the epidermis transiently lower the average UV mutation burden of epidermal melanocytes?
- Could this replenishment model be harnessed therapeutically to replace heavily mutated epidermal cells in skin cancer prevention?

## Limitations

- Single-cell approach requires ex vivo clonal expansion, which may alter gene expression or (to a lesser extent) mutational profiles
- Only 297 cells from 31 donors — far fewer cells than typical single-cell RNA-seq atlases, limiting statistical power for rare subpopulation discovery
- Spatial transcriptomics performed on a single skin section from one donor; spatial findings need broader validation
- Separation of epidermal cells by dispase may preferentially harvest interfollicular epidermis melanocytes, potentially undersampling true hair-follicle-resident LowMut cells
- Study limited to normal melanocytes; direct comparison to melanoma mutation landscapes would require integration with tumor datasets
- Preprint (bioRxiv); not yet peer reviewed at time of indexing
- The 5-gene Xenium scoring approach is an inference of mutation burden from expression — not a direct measurement of somatic mutations in spatial sections
