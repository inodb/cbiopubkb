## Summary

Landmark WES study of 538 CLL samples — the largest at the time — including 278 pre-treatment samples from the phase III CLL8 trial (FCR vs. FC). Using MutSig2CV, the study identifies 44 putative CLL driver genes (26 novel), including previously unrecognized cancer genes RPS15 and IKZF3. Novel biological themes include MYC activity (MGA, PTPN11, FUBP1), MAPK-ERK signaling (NRAS, KRAS, BRAF with non-canonical mutations, MAP2K1), and RNA processing/export. Clonality analysis reveals a temporal map of CLL evolution with del(13q) and tri(12) as earliest events. Subclonal driver presence at baseline predicts shorter PFS (HR 1.6) in CLL8. Matched relapse WES from 59 patients demonstrates clonal evolution is nearly universal (57/59 cases), with TP53/del(17p) selectively expanding under therapy. Data deposited at dbGaP (phs000922.v1.p1) and available in `cll_broad_2015` on cBioPortal.

## Key Findings

### F1: 44 CLL driver genes identified; 26 are novel, collectively found in 33.5% of CLLs

**Finding:** WES of 538 CLLs with MutSig2CV identified 44 putative driver genes — 18 previously known and 26 newly identified. Combined with 11 recurrent somatic copy number variants (sCNVs), 91.1% of CLLs have at least one driver event and 65.4% have at least two. Novel putative drivers include genes in RNA processing (FUBP1, XPO4, NXF1), chromatin modification (ASXL1, HIST1H1B, BAZ2B, IKZF3), DNA damage (CHEK2, BRCC3, ELF4, DYRK1A), and B cell activity (TRAF2, TRAF3, CARD11).

**Detail:** Statistical power to detect genes mutated in 5% of patients was saturated at n=538 (94% power for 3%, 61% for 2%). Targeted orthogonal validation with Fluidigm deep sequencing confirmed 91.6% (65/71) of tested mutations. RNAseq orthogonal validation detected 78.1% of putative driver mutations at sites with >90% power. Most drivers showed higher prevalence in IGHV-unmutated CLL (more aggressive subtype), except del(13q), MYD88, and CHD2 which were enriched in IGHV-mutated CLL.

**Genes / cancer types:** SF3B1, ATM, TP53, NOTCH1, XPO1, MYD88, BIRC3, RPS15, IKZF3, MGA, BRAF, MAP2K1, NRAS, KRAS · Chronic lymphocytic leukemia

### F2: Novel drivers RPS15 and IKZF3 — previously unrecognized cancer genes

**Finding:** RPS15 (ribosomal protein S15) was recurrently mutated in 4.3% (n=23) of CLLs, with mutations localized to the C-terminal region at highly conserved sites (median conservation 94/100). IKZF3 (Aiolos, a B cell transcription factor) had recurrent L162R substitutions in 2.0% (n=11) of CLLs at a conserved amino acid (93/100). RPS15 mutations are associated with adverse gene expression profiles; IKZF3 upregulation is a known negative prognostic factor, and IKZF3 CCF increased at relapse in 3/4 cases with longitudinal data.

**Detail:** RPS15 is a component of the S40 ribosomal subunit. Gene set enrichment analysis revealed upregulation of gene sets related to adverse CLL outcome and immune response in RPS15-mutated tumors. IKZF3 is a key B cell development transcription factor; its upregulation (not mutation) has been associated with adverse clinical outcome in CLL. RPS15 mutations are associated with shorter PFS in CLL8 (Bonferroni P=0.024). IKZF3 L162R mutations cluster at a functional domain, distinct from the broad expression-based associations previously reported.

**Genes / cancer types:** RPS15, IKZF3 · Chronic lymphocytic leukemia

### F3: MAPK-ERK pathway altered in 8.7% of CLLs; BRAF mutations are non-canonical

**Finding:** 8.7% of CLLs have at least one MAPK-ERK pathway driver: NRAS (n=9, 1.7%), KRAS (n=14, 2.6%), BRAF (n=21, 3.9%), or novel driver MAP2K1 (n=12, 2.2%). BRAF mutations in CLL do not involve the canonical V600E hotspot seen in other cancers; instead, they cluster in the activation segment of the kinase domain — a different mechanism with clinical implications, as BRAF inhibitors may be less effective for non-canonical BRAF mutations.

**Detail:** Prior therapy was associated with enrichment of DDX3X and MAP2K1 mutations, suggesting therapeutic selection. The enrichment of MAPK-ERK pathway alterations is novel and suggests therapeutic exploration of MEK/ERK inhibitors in CLL. BRAF mutations in hairy cell leukemia involve V600E; the CLL-specific non-canonical clustering suggests a CLL-unique biology. KRAS and NRAS are detected in 4.1% combined.

**Genes / cancer types:** BRAF, NRAS, KRAS, MAP2K1 · Chronic lymphocytic leukemia

### F4: Temporal map of CLL evolution — del(13q) and tri(12) are earliest events

**Finding:** Cancer cell fraction (CCF) analysis across 538 cases, combined with clonal vs. subclonal classification, enabled construction of a temporal map of CLL driver evolution. SCNVs are earliest: del(13q) and tri(12) are the initial divergent points. Del(11q) is an intermediate event with strong convergence. Late drivers (SF3B1, ATM sSNVs) show convergent evolution — multiple distinct alleles in the same tumor. Biallelic inactivation of ATM follows del(11q) (monoallelic loss first), while TP53 biallelic inactivation requires concurrent del(17p) and TP53 mutation.

**Detail:** For ATM: del(11q22.3) is clonal while ATM sSNVs are subclonal and shift in CCF over time (convergent evolution in 9 cases). For TP53: del(17p) and TP53 mutations rise concordantly (co-occurrence odds ratio 97.22 vs. 10.99 for ATM/del(11q)). This supports that TP53 requires true biallelic inactivation while ATM may be haploinsufficient. Analysis of 23 driver pairs (≥5 edges) established a directed temporal network.

**Genes / cancer types:** del(13q), tri(12), del(11q), ATM, TP53, del(17p), SF3B1, IKZF3 · Chronic lymphocytic leukemia

### F5: Subclonal drivers at baseline predict shorter PFS; TP53/SF3B1/RPS15 are prognostic in CLL8

**Finding:** The presence of any detectable pre-treatment subclonal driver was associated with significantly shorter PFS in CLL8 (HR 1.6, 95% CI 1.2–2.2, P=0.004), significant in both FC and FCR arms. Among specific drivers, only TP53 mutation, SF3B1 mutation, and RPS15 mutation (Bonferroni P=0.024) were significantly associated with shorter PFS in the 278 CLL8 pre-treatment samples. IGHV unmutated status, most drivers were more prevalent in this high-risk subtype.

**Detail:** The CLL8 cohort provides the first prospective, pre-treatment-blinded analysis of CLL genomics on a uniform therapy regimen. The genomic analysis team was blinded to clinical outcome data. Seven CLL genes had previously suggested prognostic impact; this study validated only TP53 and SF3B1 while adding RPS15 as a new prognostic marker. Harrell's C-index improved when genomics added to clinical models.

**Genes / cancer types:** TP53, SF3B1, RPS15 · Chronic lymphocytic leukemia

### F6: Clonal evolution at relapse is near-universal; TP53/del(17p) selectively expand

**Finding:** In 59 matched pre-treatment/relapse WES pairs, large clonal shifts were detected in 57/59 cases (97%), demonstrating that CLL evolution under therapy is the rule, not the exception. The relapse clone was already detectable at pre-treatment WES in 18/59 (30%) cases. By targeted deep sequencing, relapse drivers were detected in pre-treatment in 11/41 additional cases. Three evolution patterns: stable (del(13q), tri(12), del(11q)); selectively expanding under therapy (TP53/del(17p), IKZF3); or variable (SF3B1, ATM).

**Detail:** TP53 mutation and del(17p) showed concordant increases in CCF at relapse in all 12 cases harboring both events — no case showed a single TP53 allele shift. IKZF3 CCF increased in 3/4 relapse cases. Convergent evolution was seen for ATM (9 instances of multiple distinct ATM mutations in a single CLL) and SF3B1. This convergent evolution reflects high fitness advantage of biallelic inactivation of these TSGs under treatment pressure.

## Open Questions

- Does the identification of non-canonical BRAF mutations in CLL suggest that novel BRAF/MEK inhibitor combinations (distinct from V600E-targeting vemurafenib) should be clinically investigated?
- Can pre-treatment subclonal TP53 mutation detection (beyond detectable by standard clinical testing) guide prophylactic TP53-directed treatment adjustments in the era of ibrutinib/venetoclax?
- What is the functional significance of RPS15 C-terminal mutations — do they affect ribosomal stress sensing (p53 pathway) or mRNA translation fidelity?
- As novel targeted therapies (BTK/BCL2 inhibitors) transform CLL treatment, do the same driver gene associations with relapse hold, or do different selective pressures emerge?
- Can the temporal map of CLL driver acquisition inform early intervention windows — e.g., treating after del(13q) before convergent evolution toward TP53 loss?

## Limitations

- WES provides insufficient sensitivity (~10% VAF limit) for rare subclonal mutations; low-frequency subclones may be missed in pre-treatment samples
- CLL8 cohort uses FCR as standard-of-care; findings may not generalize to BTK or BCL2 inhibitor therapy contexts
- Temporal map derived from cross-sectional CCF analysis is inferential, not directly observed; longitudinal multi-sampling would confirm ordering
- RNAseq available for only 156/538 cases (29%), limiting pathway-level expression validation
- Cohort is predominantly European; population-level differences in CLL driver frequencies are not captured
