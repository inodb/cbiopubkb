## Summary

This study demonstrates that super-enhancer translocations — not just the canonical MYB-NFIB fusion — drive MYB overexpression in adenoid cystic carcinoma (ACC). Whole-genome sequencing of 18 primary ACCs and primagrafts, combined with chromatin immunoprecipitation and chromosome conformation capture, revealed diverse chromosomal rearrangements that juxtapose super-enhancers from NFIB, TGFBR3, or RAD51B loci to the MYB promoter. MYB protein binds these translocated enhancers, establishing a self-reinforcing feedback loop. Downstream, MYB drives alternate cell-fate programs — cooperating with TP63 in myoepithelial cells and Notch signaling in luminal epithelial cells. BET bromodomain inhibitors slow tumor growth in ACC primagraft models in vivo.

## Key Findings

### F1: Super-enhancer translocations are the unifying mechanism of MYB overexpression in ACC
**Finding:** MYB rearrangements were detected in 15 of 20 (75%) ACCs. The unifying feature across all rearrangement types is the juxtaposition of super-enhancer regions — from NFIB, TGFBR3, or RAD51B loci — to the MYB locus, driving its overexpression regardless of whether a canonical fusion protein is formed or the 3′ UTR is lost.
**Detail:** 6/20 tumors had canonical NFIB-MYB fusions with 3′ UTR loss (30%); 6/20 had NFIB-MYB rearrangements retaining the 3′ UTR (30%); 2/20 had novel TGFBR3-MYB rearrangements; 1/20 had a RAD51B-MYB rearrangement. Several rearrangements occurred at the 5′ end of MYB, inconsistent with fusion protein production. All rearrangements associated with high MYB mRNA expression by qRT-PCR.
**Genes / cancer types:** MYB, NFIB, TGFBR3, RAD51B; adenoid cystic carcinoma.

**Tool support:** native > Mutations and structural variants viewable in cBioPortal OncoPrint and Mutations tabs.

### F2: MYB binds translocated super-enhancers, creating a positive feedback loop
**Finding:** MYB protein directly binds the translocated super-enhancer elements that were brought into proximity with the MYB promoter by chromosomal rearrangement. Chromosome conformation capture (Hi-C/3C) confirmed physical looping interactions between translocated enhancers and the MYB promoter, establishing a self-reinforcing transcriptional feedback loop.
**Detail:** ChIP-seq (H3K27ac) identified active super-enhancers in the translocated NFIB and TGFBR3 regions. MYB ChIP-seq confirmed MYB binding at these enhancers. This feedback loop sustains high-level MYB expression independent of the initial translocation trigger.
**Genes / cancer types:** MYB; adenoid cystic carcinoma.

### F3: MYB drives alternate cell-fate programs in ACC myoepithelial and luminal lineages
**Finding:** MYB binds enhancers genome-wide that support distinct transcriptional programs in the two ACC cell lineages: cooperating with TP63 in the myoepithelial compartment and activating a Notch signaling program in luminal epithelial cells.
**Detail:** Integrative analysis of MYB ChIP-seq with lineage-specific chromatin accessibility identified TP63 co-binding at myoepithelial enhancers and Notch target gene activation in luminal cells. This explains the characteristic biphasic histology of ACC (myoepithelial + luminal cells forming tubules/cribriform structures).
**Genes / cancer types:** MYB, TP63, NOTCH pathway genes; adenoid cystic carcinoma.
**Data availability:** not-available > Lineage-specific ChIP-seq and enhancer data not in cBioPortal.
**Tool support:** custom > Integrative ChIP-seq analysis; external lineage marker data.

### F4: BET bromodomain inhibitors slow ACC growth in primagraft models in vivo
**Finding:** BET bromodomain inhibitors (e.g., JQ1), which disrupt super-enhancer-driven transcription, slow tumor growth in ACC primagraft models in vivo, providing proof-of-concept for targeting the MYB super-enhancer feedback loop therapeutically.
**Detail:** ACC primagrafts (patient-derived xenograft-like models) treated with BET inhibitors showed reduced proliferation rates compared to vehicle. However, BET inhibitors were noted to be insufficient for complete tumor eradication, and resistance mechanisms were noted.
**Genes / cancer types:** MYB; adenoid cystic carcinoma.
**Data availability:** not-available > In vivo primagraft drug data not in cBioPortal.
**Tool support:** custom > Primagraft growth curve analysis; external preclinical data.

### F5: MYB rearrangements define ACC independently of fusion transcript detection
**Finding:** Because super-enhancer hijacking — not fusion protein production — is the key oncogenic mechanism, standard detection methods focused on fusion transcripts will miss ~30% of ACC cases with NFIB-MYB rearrangements that retain the 3′ UTR and ~20% with non-NFIB partners.
**Detail:** qRT-PCR confirmed high MYB expression in all 15 rearrangement-positive cases regardless of partner or UTR status. Diagnostic panels that rely solely on MYB-NFIB fusion transcript detection will fail to identify a substantial fraction of MYB-rearranged ACCs.
**Genes / cancer types:** MYB, NFIB, TGFBR3, RAD51B; adenoid cystic carcinoma.

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- What fraction of MYB-rearrangement-negative ACCs (5/20 in this cohort) drive MYB overexpression through non-structural mechanisms (e.g., promoter methylation, transcription factor rewiring)?
- Can BET inhibitor sensitivity be predicted from super-enhancer strength or MYB binding intensity?
- Do MYBL1 (MYB-like) alterations in other cancers use a similar super-enhancer hijacking mechanism?
- Can TP63/Notch lineage markers be used to subtype ACC for therapy selection?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Small cohort (n=20 ACCs) limits statistical power and generalizability.
- Primagraft models may not fully recapitulate tumor heterogeneity or the tumor microenvironment.
- BET inhibitors show partial efficacy in vivo; complete tumor suppression was not achieved.
- Mechanistic studies (ChIP-seq, Hi-C) were primarily performed in primagraft cell lines, not patient-matched primary tissue.
- Non-NFIB MYB translocation partners (TGFBR3, RAD51B) are rare and their functional characterization is limited.
