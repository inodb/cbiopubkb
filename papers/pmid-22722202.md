## Summary

This landmark Nature study reports whole-exome sequencing (WES) of 103 primary breast carcinoma/normal pairs from patients in Mexico and Vietnam, plus whole-genome sequencing (WGS) of 22 additional pairs, covering all major breast cancer expression subtypes. Beyond confirming recurrent drivers (*PIK3CA*, *TP53*, *AKT1*, *GATA3*, *MAP3K1*), the study discovers two major novel findings: (1) *CBFB* is identified for the first time as a significantly mutated gene in breast cancer — or any epithelial cancer — with frameshift and nonsense mutations in ER+ tumors accompanied by deletions of its partner *RUNX1*; and (2) a recurrent *MAGI3-AKT3* fusion gene is identified in triple-negative breast cancer (TNBC), producing constitutively active Akt kinase that is sensitive to ATP-competitive (but not allosteric) Akt inhibitors. The study establishes that breast cancer genomics shows molecular subtype-specific mutation patterns, and demonstrates that actionable kinase fusions exist in TNBC — a subtype with limited targeted therapy options.

## Key Findings

### F1: CBFB identified as a novel significantly mutated gene in breast cancer — first report in any epithelial cancer

**Finding:** *CBFB* (core-binding factor beta subunit) is mutated in 4 ER+ breast cancer samples (1 nonsense, 3 truncating frameshifts) and is identified by MutSig analysis as a statistically significant cancer gene (q<0.1 after FDR correction). CBFB mutations are accompanied by hemizygous deletion of the other *CBFB* allele, and two additional cases show homozygous deletion of its DNA-binding partner *RUNX1*, suggesting convergent inactivation of the CBFB/RUNX1 complex.

**Detail:** CBFB encodes the non-DNA-binding subunit of a heterodimeric transcription factor complex with RUNX1/2/3. While CBFB-MYH11 translocations are well-established drivers of acute myeloid leukemia (AML), inactivation of this complex in epithelial cancers had not previously been described. All 4 CBFB mutations are loss-of-function (frameshift or nonsense). The RUNX1 deletions in 2 additional cases are homozygous, providing further evidence for the tumor suppressor function of this complex in breast epithelium. The sample size is insufficient to determine subtype specificity, but all CBFB-mutant cases were ER+. This finding suggests a role for luminal differentiation transcription factors in ER+ breast carcinogenesis.

**Genes / cancer types:** `CBFB`, `RUNX1` · Breast cancer (ER+)

### F2: Recurrent MAGI3-AKT3 fusion in triple-negative breast cancer constitutively activates Akt and is sensitive to ATP-competitive Akt inhibitors

**Finding:** Whole-genome sequencing identifies a balanced translocation between *MAGI3* (1p) and *AKT3* (1q) in a triple-negative, basal-like breast cancer, producing an in-frame *MAGI3-AKT3* fusion. Screening 235 additional breast cancer samples by RT-PCR identifies the fusion in 8 samples total, including 5 of 72 (6.9%) TNBC cases. The Magi3-Akt3 fusion protein is constitutively phosphorylated at Ser473 (kinase domain) and activates downstream targets (GSK3β); it is sensitive to ATP-competitive Akt inhibitor GSK-690693 but not to allosteric MK-2206.

**Detail:** The fusion joins *MAGI3* intron 9 to *AKT3* intron 1, producing a chimeric protein that retains the intact Akt3 kinase domain but lacks the pleckstrin homology (PH) domain (responsible for membrane recruitment and allosteric inhibitor binding). Deletion of the MAGI3 second PDZ domain removes the interaction with PTEN, effectively combining loss of PTEN-mediated PI3K suppression with constitutive Akt kinase activation in a single rearrangement. AKT3 is the dominant AKT family member in hormone receptor-negative breast cancers. The fusion drives focus formation in Rat-1 fibroblasts (transformation assay). The MK-2206 insensitivity (allosteric inhibitor targeting PH domain, which is deleted in the fusion) vs. GSK-690693 sensitivity (ATP-competitive, targeting the intact kinase domain) has immediate clinical trial implications for fusion-positive TNBC patients.

**Genes / cancer types:** `MAGI3`, `AKT3`, `AKT1`, `PTEN` · Triple-negative breast cancer

### F3: Six significantly mutated genes identified; AKT1 and PIK3CA are mutually exclusive and converge on PI3K pathway

**Finding:** MutSig analysis identifies 6 significantly mutated genes (q<0.1): *TP53* (27%), *PIK3CA* (27%), *AKT1* (6%), *GATA3*, *MAP3K1*, and the novel *CBFB*. *AKT1* E17K mutations (PH-domain activating) and *PIK3CA* mutations (helical domain 40%, kinase domain 47%) are mutually exclusive in this dataset, consistent with convergent PI3K/Akt pathway activation. ABSOLUTE analysis shows 21/31 TP53 mutations are homozygous.

**Detail:** AKT1 E17K is present in 6 samples and activates PI3K signaling by altering PH domain membrane recruitment. PIK3CA mutations cluster canonically at H1047R (kinase domain) and E542K/E545K (helical domain). The mutual exclusivity of AKT1 E17K and PIK3CA mutations suggests they are functionally equivalent at the pathway level, and that combined PI3K inhibition is unlikely to provide additional benefit in single-driver tumors. MAP3K1 mutations are restricted to ER+ disease (5 mutations in 3 ER+ patients) with a pattern consistent with biallelic inactivation (2 compound heterozygous cases). GATA3 mutations are frameshift events near the 3′ coding sequence, disrupting zinc-finger domains required for DNA binding, predominantly in luminal tumors.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53`, `PIK3CA`, `AKT1`, `GATA3`, `MAP3K1`, `CBFB` · Breast cancer (all subtypes)

### F4: Genomic rearrangement burden is subtype-specific; basal-like and HER2-enriched subtypes show highest rearrangement rates

**Finding:** Rearrangement analysis of 22 WGS cases reveals subtype-specific structural complexity: basal-like (median 237 rearrangements) and HER2-enriched (median 246) subtypes have dramatically higher rearrangement rates than Luminal A (median 30). No rearrangement was seen in more than one sample, suggesting that most structural events are private/patient-specific rather than recurrently selected.

**Detail:** dRanger algorithm applied to 22 WGS cases identifies 165 candidate rearrangements, with 89/165 (54%) confirmed by PCR. The high rearrangement rate in basal-like and HER2-enriched tumors is consistent with defective homologous recombination DNA repair (e.g., BRCA1/2 deficiency in basal-like) and focal amplifications (HER2). Importantly, no previously reported recurrent fusions (e.g., MAST-Notch family from cDNA-seq) were identified in this WGS cohort, highlighting platform- and detection method-specific differences. The single MAGI3-AKT3 fusion, found in this WGS cohort, is the only recurrent fusion confirmed at the DNA level.

**Genes / cancer types:** `BRCA1`, `BRCA2`, `ERBB2`, `MAGI3`, `AKT3` · Breast cancer (basal-like, HER2-enriched)

**Data availability:** `partial`
> Structural variant data from the WGS subset may be partially available in brca_broad. Full rearrangement catalog from dRanger is in supplementary tables but not uniformly loaded into cBioPortal.

**Tool support:** `api`

### F5: ERBB2 somatic mutations (including S310F) activate signaling independently of amplification in HER2-enriched and Luminal B subtypes

**Finding:** *ERBB2* somatic mutations are identified in 3 samples (S310F in 2 cases; 1 additional mutation). Two cases carry the identical activating S310F extracellular domain mutation and belong to HER2-enriched and Luminal B subtypes without *ERBB2* amplification, providing evidence that somatic point mutation can be the primary ERBB2 activation mechanism in non-amplified tumors. S310F is confirmed to activate ERBB2 in vitro.

**Detail:** ERBB2 S310F, located in the extracellular domain, was known to have transforming activity from in vitro studies (personal communication at time of publication). Importantly, the two S310F cases are confirmed to be distinct patients (different germline and somatic genotype profiles), validating recurrent selection for this specific mutation. This finding predates the broader recognition of ERBB2 mutation (vs. amplification) as a distinct oncogenic mechanism, now targetable by neratinib and other pan-ERBB inhibitors. In contrast to the typical amplification-driven HER2+ subtype, ERBB2 mutation-positive tumors may have distinct biology and drug sensitivity profiles.

## Open Questions

- What is the full prevalence and functional significance of CBFB mutations and RUNX1 deletions in larger breast cancer cohorts (TCGA BRCA: ~1,000 samples)?
- Can the MAGI3-AKT3 fusion be used as a patient selection biomarker for ATP-competitive Akt inhibitor trials in TNBC?
- Do CBFB/RUNX1-inactivated breast cancers have specific vulnerabilities (e.g., to CDK4/6 inhibitors) that can be exploited therapeutically?
- Are ERBB2 somatic mutation-positive tumors (non-amplified) responsive to HER2-targeted antibodies (trastuzumab, pertuzumab) or only to small-molecule kinase inhibitors?
- What additional structural rearrangements are present in this cohort that were not detectable by the dRanger algorithm or confirmation PCR?
- Do the Mexico and Vietnam breast cancer cohorts differ in underlying germline variants (BRCA1/2, PALB2) that contribute to tumor genomics, and how does this interact with somatic driver selection?

## Limitations

- Relatively small WGS cohort (n=22) limits power for rearrangement discovery; most structural events are private to individual patients
- Patient populations from Mexico and Vietnam may not be representative of North American or European breast cancer populations (different germline backgrounds and environmental exposures)
- MAGI3-AKT3 fusion screening performed by RT-PCR in 235 additional samples — non-systematic ascertainment could miss variant breakpoints
- WES validation rate for indels is only 46% (vs. 94% for point mutations), suggesting substantial false-positive rate in indel calls
- PAM50 subtyping from exon arrays rather than RNA-seq; subtype assignments may differ from later RNA-seq-based classifications
- 2012 study predates current breast cancer driver gene knowledge (e.g., CDH1 mutations in lobular, ESR1 mutations); the significantly mutated gene list is likely incomplete
- Cohort size (n=103 WES) insufficient for robust subtype-stratified SMG analysis; CBFB ER+ specificity cannot be confirmed statistically
- No matched germline panel; BRCA1/2 and other hereditary breast cancer gene germline variants not systematically analyzed
