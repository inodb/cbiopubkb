## Summary

The GLASS (Glioma Longitudinal Analysis) Consortium study integrating longitudinal RNA-seq and DNA-seq from 304 adult diffuse glioma patients treated at 37 hospitals. Paired initial/recurrent tumor samples from 168 patients with RNA-seq and 256 with DNA-seq data were analyzed. Key findings: (1) IDH-wild-type (IDHwt) and IDH-mutant (IDHmut) gliomas recur through distinct transcriptional and cellular mechanisms; (2) hypermutation and acquired *CDKN2A* deletions drive proliferating neoplastic cell expansion at recurrence; (3) IDHwt tumors become more neuronal and invasive at recurrence, reflecting possible neuron-glioma interaction; (4) mesenchymal transition at recurrence is causally linked to a specific immunosuppressive myeloid cell state via ligand-receptor interactions. The study used CIBERSORTx deconvolution against 55,284 single-cell transcriptomes and multiplex immunofluorescence spatial imaging to reconstruct tumor architecture and identify recurrence-associated phenotypes as potential therapeutic targets.

## Key Findings

### F1: IDHwt and IDHmut gliomas undergo distinct subtype transitions and cell-state changes at recurrence

**Finding:** In IDHwt glioma, the dominant bulk transcriptional subtype switched at recurrence in 49% of patients, with classical-to-mesenchymal being the most common transition. In contrast, 78% of IDHmut tumors remained proneural at both time points. Concurrently, IDHwt recurrent tumors showed significantly higher oligodendrocyte admixture and lower differentiated-like neoplastic cell proportions, while IDHmut recurrent tumors showed increased proliferating stem-like neoplastic cells and decreased differentiated-like fractions.

**Detail:** CIBERSORTx deconvolution against 55,284 single-cell transcriptomes identified 12 cell states including three pan-glioma neoplastic states (differentiated-like, stem-like, proliferating stem-like). Validation via synthetic mixing of scRNA-seq profiles, matched scRNA-seq ground truth, and multiplex immunofluorescence confirmed the accuracy of the deconvolution approach. Among IDHmut-noncodel tumors specifically, the increase in proliferating stem-like cells at recurrence was significant, while IDHmut-codel tumors showed significant increases in non-proliferating stem-like cells. The subtype switch in IDHwt tumors is paralleled by changes in histological feature composition (IVY GAP reference anatomy), with mesenchymal tumors enriched for differentiated-like neoplastic and stromal/immune cells.

**Genes / cancer types:** IDH1, IDH2 · Diffuse glioma (IDHwt GBM, IDHmut astrocytoma, IDHmut-codel oligodendroglioma)

### F2: Hypermutation and acquired *CDKN2A* deletions associate with increased proliferating neoplastic cells at recurrence

**Finding:** Hypermutation (elevated somatic mutation burden resulting from alkylating chemotherapy-induced mutagenesis via MMR/MSH6 pathways) and acquired *CDKN2A* homozygous deletions at recurrence were each independently associated with an increase in the proportion of proliferating stem-like neoplastic cells across both IDHwt and IDHmut gliomas, indicating active tumor growth as a shared recurrence mechanism.

**Detail:** Hypermutation in glioma typically arises post-temozolomide via acquired MSH6 or MSH2 mutations; this study confirms the transcriptional correlate — increased proliferating neoplastic cells — linking genetic evolution to functional phenotype. *CDKN2A* deletion at recurrence is a strong independent predictor of cell cycle deregulation and proliferative expansion. Both events appear in both IDHwt and IDHmut contexts, representing pan-glioma recurrence vulnerabilities. The mechanistic link between *CDKN2A* loss and proliferating stem-like expansion is consistent with CDK4/6-cyclin D pathway derepression.

**Genes / cancer types:** CDKN2A, MSH6, MSH2 · Diffuse glioma (all subtypes)

### F3: Recurrent IDHwt gliomas upregulate neuronal signaling programs, implicating neuron-glioma interactions in invasion

**Finding:** At recurrence, IDHwt glioma neoplastic cells exhibited significantly increased expression of neuronal signaling gene programs, with leading-edge tumor features enriched in oligodendrocyte-like and stem-like neoplastic cells. This transcriptional shift toward a neural identity at the invasive front suggests that interactions between neoplastic cells and the surrounding neuronal microenvironment may promote tumor invasion and progression.

**Detail:** Deconvolution of GLASS bulk RNA-seq against Ivy GAP spatial anatomy (five histological features: leading edge, infiltrating tumor, cellular tumor, pseudopalisading cells around necrosis, microvascular proliferation) revealed that leading-edge regions contain high stem-like and oligodendrocyte proportions, while pseudopalisading areas near necrosis harbor differentiated-like neoplastic cells. The increase in oligodendrocyte admixture observed at recurrence in IDHwt tumors corresponds to increased representation of leading-edge histological features, supporting greater invasiveness. The upregulation of neuronal programs by neoplastic cells at the invasive front is concordant with published single-cell data showing neuron-glioma synaptic interactions as drivers of invasion.

**Genes / cancer types:** SOX2, OLIG2, EGFR, CD44 · IDHwt glioblastoma

### F4: Mesenchymal transition at recurrence is driven by a specific myeloid cell state via ligand-receptor interactions

**Finding:** Proneural-to-mesenchymal subtype switching at recurrence in IDHwt glioma was causally associated with a specific immunosuppressive myeloid cell state (M2-like macrophages/microglia) that interacts with neoplastic cells through defined ligand-receptor pairs. This mesenchymal transition is not attributable to somatic mutation alone but to microenvironmental reprogramming.

**Detail:** Mesenchymal tumors are enriched in stromal and immune cells, and the specific myeloid cell state identified at recurrence signals to neoplastic cells via ligand-receptor interactions. Proneural-to-mesenchymal transition (observed in a subset of IDHwt tumors) was associated with increased representation of this myeloid state at recurrence, and the transition correlated with loss of stem-like neoplastic cells and gain of differentiated-like mesenchymal-like (CD44+) neoplastic cells. Multiplex immunofluorescence confirmed spatial co-localization of this myeloid state with mesenchymal neoplastic cells in tumor core regions. These results implicate therapeutic targeting of myeloid-neoplastic cell interactions as a strategy to prevent or reverse mesenchymal transition.

**Genes / cancer types:** CD44, TGFB1, SPP1, LGALS9 (representative ligand-receptor genes) · IDHwt glioblastoma

### F5: GLASS longitudinal dataset enables tracking of clonal dynamics in treatment-resistant glioma

**Finding:** Integration of paired initial/recurrent DNA-seq across 256 patients revealed both linear and branching evolutionary patterns in glioma. The expanded GLASS cohort (from 222 to 304 patients vs. prior publication) with overlapping RNA and DNA allows joint genomic/transcriptomic analysis of treatment-associated cell state changes — a unique longitudinal resource for defining mechanisms of therapy resistance.

**Detail:** The availability of paired tumor samples enables clonal phylogeny reconstruction and identification of recurrence-specific alterations beyond what cross-sectional studies allow. Previously published GLASS DNA-seq analyses established hypermutation as a recurrence-specific event driven by temozolomide; this RNA integration confirms the functional cellular consequences. The expanded cohort with 37 hospital sites provides the largest longitudinal glioma multi-omics dataset to date, allowing robust IDH-stratified analyses. Data are publicly available at Synapse and a subset (DNA-level data) is in cBioPortal.

**Genes / cancer types:** IDH1, IDH2, CDKN2A, MSH6, TERT, EGFR, PTEN, RB1 · Diffuse glioma

## Open Questions

- Can therapeutic targeting of the myeloid-neoplastic ligand-receptor axis (e.g., anti-SPP1, anti-TGFB) prevent or reverse mesenchymal transition and improve outcomes in IDHwt glioma?
- Do neuronal signaling programs in invasive IDHwt glioma represent a targetable dependency, given evidence for synaptic interactions (AMPA receptor signaling) promoting glioma progression?
- Is the proliferating stem-like neoplastic cell expansion driven by acquired *CDKN2A* deletion at recurrence reversible with CDK4/6 inhibitors?
- Can serial liquid biopsy (ctDNA) from CSF or blood reliably detect hypermutation emergence or *CDKN2A* deletion before radiographic recurrence in glioma?
- Do IDHmut-codel (oligodendroglioma) recurrences follow the same microenvironmental dynamics as IDHmut-noncodel, given the small number (n=9) in this cohort?

## Limitations

- Limited IDHmut-codel cases (n=9 RNA-seq pairs) due to their inherently better prognosis; IDHmut analyses were pooled to maintain statistical power
- Bulk RNA-seq deconvolution (CIBERSORTx) is an approximation; spatial and cellular resolution are inferred, not directly measured — complementary multiplex immunofluorescence validates but does not fully replace single-cell resolution
- Ligand-receptor interaction analysis is correlative; causal roles of specific pairs require experimental validation
- Heterogeneous treatment regimens across 37 hospitals limit controlled analysis of therapy-specific effects on recurrence phenotype
- Single-cell RNA-seq reference panels from 11 patients may not capture the full diversity of glioma cell states across all subtypes and treatment contexts
