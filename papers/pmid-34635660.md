## Summary

MSK proof-of-concept study establishing *RAD51B* as a novel breast and ovarian cancer susceptibility gene. Among 3,422 consecutively accrued breast and ovarian cancer patients who underwent tumor/germline sequencing via MSK-IMPACT, germline loss-of-function (LOF) *RAD51B* variants were identified in 0.26% — significantly enriched over the gnomAD population frequency of 0.09% (OR 2.69; 95% CI 1.4–5.3; p=0.004). Tumors with biallelic *RAD51B* inactivation (germline LOF + somatic LOH) displayed dominant mutational signature 3, elevated large-scale state transition (LST) scores, and co-occurring biallelic *TP53* mutations — all hallmarks of homologous recombination deficiency (HRD). Functional assays confirmed that *RAD51B* silencing impairs RAD51 nuclear foci formation and sensitizes cells to olaparib and mitomycin-C. The HRD phenotype is intermediate relative to *BRCA1/2*, consistent with *RAD51B*'s role as an accessory factor. Findings support adding *RAD51B* to clinical germline testing panels for breast and ovarian cancer.

## Key Findings

### F1: Germline *RAD51B* LOF variants enriched 2.7-fold in breast and ovarian cancer patients

**Finding:** Among 3,422 consecutively accrued breast and ovarian cancer patients who consented to paired tumor/germline sequencing, 9 female carriers of germline *RAD51B* LOF variants were identified (0.26%), a carrier frequency significantly higher than in gnomAD controls (0.09%; OR 2.69; 95% CI 1.4–5.3; p=0.004). No enrichment was observed across the full pan-cancer cohort of 18,087 patients (OR 0.73; p=0.28), indicating tumor type specificity.

**Detail:** Among the 9 female carriers: 5 had breast cancer (4/5 ER+PR+HER2−, 1 ER−PR−HER2+) and 4 had high-grade serous ovarian cancer. Average age at breast cancer diagnosis was 49 years (range 30–61) and ovarian cancer 67.3 years (range 58–79). Eight of 13 total carriers had a first- or second-degree relative with breast or ovarian cancer. The most recurrent allele was c.139 C>T (p.Arg47*), carried by 7/13 patients across multiple ethnicities, predominantly Hispanic/Latino and Caucasian. In gnomAD, the MAF of this allele was 0.01%, indicating it is rare but not absent in the general population.

**Genes / cancer types:** RAD51B · Breast carcinoma, high-grade serous ovarian carcinoma

### F2: Biallelic *RAD51B* inactivation (germline LOF + LOH) produces an HRD tumor phenotype

**Finding:** Tumors with biallelic *RAD51B* inactivation (n=6 with germline LOF plus somatic LOH of the wild-type allele) exhibited dominant mutational signature 3 in all 5 cases with WES data and elevated LST scores, both established markers of defective homologous recombination. LST scores were significantly higher in biallelic *RAD51B* loss versus WT *RAD51B* cases with LOH (p=0.02), confirming that loss of the *RAD51B* allele, not merely LOH, drives the HRD phenotype.

**Detail:** Biallelic *RAD51B* tumors showed an intermediate HRD phenotype: LST scores and signature 3 exposure were significantly lower than *BRCA1/2*-deficient tumors (from TCGA) but significantly above WT controls. This is concordant with *RAD51B*'s role as an accessory RAD51 paralog in the BCDX2 complex (RAD51B–RAD51C–RAD51D–XRCC2) — essential for, but not the core of, HR. LOH of the WT allele was observed at a significantly higher rate in tumors with germline *RAD51B* LOF variants (6/18) versus VUS carriers (6/59; p=0.02), indicating selection for biallelic loss. Five of six biallelic cases also harbored biallelic *TP53* mutations, a pattern enriched in BRCA-associated HRD cancers.

**Genes / cancer types:** RAD51B, TP53 · Breast carcinoma, high-grade serous ovarian carcinoma

### F3: *RAD51B* deficiency impairs RAD51 foci formation and sensitizes cells to PARP inhibition and interstrand crosslinkers

**Finding:** shRNA-mediated silencing of *RAD51B* in two normal breast epithelial cell lines (MCF-10A and MCF-12A) significantly impaired RAD51 nuclear foci formation following ionizing radiation — the gold-standard functional readout of HR competence — compared to shRenilla controls. RAD51B-deficient cells also showed markedly increased sensitivity to olaparib (PARP inhibitor) and mitomycin-C (interstrand crosslinker), albeit less pronounced than BRCA1-deficient cells.

**Detail:** Two independent shRNAs against *RAD51B* yielded consistent results in both cell lines, confirming on-target effects. The DR-GFP HR reporter assay in U2OS cells further demonstrated that the recurrent c.139 C>T (p.Arg47*) variant — which truncates the Walker A and B motifs required for RAD51C interaction — cannot complement HR deficiency beyond empty vector, in contrast to WT *RAD51B* which increased recombination repair ~2-fold. The intermediate sensitivity to olaparib and MMC relative to BRCA1 deficiency is consistent with genomic data showing intermediate LST/signature 3 scores, collectively suggesting a partially attenuated HRD phenotype sufficient for therapeutic targeting.

**Genes / cancer types:** RAD51B, BRCA1 · Breast epithelial cells (MCF-10A, MCF-12A), U2OS

### F4: *RAD51B* LOF variants are nearly as prevalent as combined *RAD51C/D* in breast/ovarian cancer

**Finding:** In the MSK cohort, *RAD51B* LOF germline variants were identified in 9/3,422 breast/ovarian cancer patients (0.26%), compared to a combined 16/3,422 cases (0.47%) for *RAD51C* and *RAD51D* together. Unlike *RAD51C/D*-associated cancers that tend to be triple-negative breast cancers, the predominant breast cancers in *RAD51B* carriers were HR-positive (4/5), suggesting distinct tissue/cell-of-origin biology.

**Detail:** The relative prevalence of *RAD51B* variants compared to *RAD51C/D* in this cohort differs from prior ovarian cancer studies that identified fewer *RAD51B* cases. This may reflect differences in tumor type composition (this cohort includes both breast and ovarian) or sequencing methodology. The hormone receptor-positive phenotype of *RAD51B*-associated breast cancers parallels *BRCA2* (rather than *BRCA1*), and may relate to differential roles of RAD51 paralogs in mammary progenitor cell maturation. The rarity of carriers limits definitive subtype association.

## Open Questions

- Is *RAD51B* penetrance sufficient to justify inclusion on NCCN-recommended germline testing panels, and if so, what risk-reduction interventions are appropriate?
- Do *RAD51B*-deficient tumors respond to PARP inhibitors in clinical settings, analogous to *BRCA1/2*-deficient cancers?
- Why do *RAD51B*-associated breast cancers skew toward hormone receptor-positive subtypes, unlike the triple-negative phenotype seen with *RAD51C/D*?
- Are there additional non-truncating *RAD51B* variants (e.g., missense in the Walker A/B domain) that are pathogenic but classified as VUS?
- What is the clinical significance of monoallelic *RAD51B* LOF without LOH — do these tumors have a milder HRD phenotype that still confers therapeutic sensitivity?

## Limitations

- Small carrier cohort (n=9 female breast/ovarian carriers) limits statistical power for subgroup analyses (e.g., by hormone receptor status or histologic subtype)
- Segregation data unavailable for most families, precluding formal co-segregation analysis
- Not all novel *RAD51B* truncating alleles were functionally validated; pathogenicity of non-Walker-motif truncations remains inferred
- TCGA validation cohort (n=6 biallelic cases) is small and may have ascertainment biases
- Prospective clinical outcomes data (PARP inhibitor response, overall survival) not available in this study
