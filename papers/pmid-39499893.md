## Summary

This prospective-retrospective biomarker analysis of the SWOG S1314 randomised trial (GC vs. ddMVAC neoadjuvant chemotherapy followed by cystectomy for muscle-invasive bladder cancer) applied MSK-IMPACT sequencing to pre-treatment TURBT specimens from 184 patients. Deleterious DDR gene alterations (defined as truncating mutations or any ERCC2 alteration) in a 9-gene panel (ERCC2, ERCC5, ATM, ATR, BRCA1, BRCA2, FANCC, RECQL4, RAD51C) were found in 23% of patients and significantly associated with pathologic complete response (pT0, OR 3.24; P = 0.003) and a trend toward improved progression-free survival (HR 0.54; P = 0.053). ERCC2 mutations (15%) specifically predict pT0 (OR 3.33; P = 0.009). These findings support ongoing prospective trials of DDR-guided bladder preservation strategies.

## Key Findings

### F1: Deleterious DDR alterations in 23% of MIBC predict pathologic complete response (pT0) with OR 3.24

**Finding:** Deleterious DDR gene alterations (truncating mutations in ERCC2/ERCC5/ATM/ATR/BRCA1/BRCA2/FANCC/RECQL4/RAD51C, or any ERCC2 alteration) are found in 23% (41/179) of patients and are associated with pT0 (OR 3.24; 95% CI 1.51–6.94; P = 0.003), adjusted for clinical stage and performance status.

**Detail:** 51% pT0 rate and 56% <pT2 rate in DDR-altered patients versus a 34% overall pT0 rate in the population. This is the largest prospective neoadjuvant chemotherapy trial with correlated DDR sequencing to date. Importantly, variants of unknown significance (VUS) when included do not show this association — only loss-of-function (deleterious) alterations predict response, underlining the need for functional classification of DDR variants. Median TMB in DDR-altered tumours (26 mut/Mb) is more than double that of unaltered tumours (12.4 mut/Mb overall).

### F2: ERCC2 mutation (15% prevalence) is the strongest predictor of pathologic complete response

**Finding:** ERCC2 is the most frequently altered DDR gene (15%, 28 alterations in 27 tumours) and ERCC2 alteration is associated with pT0 (OR 3.33; 95% CI 1.35–8.22; P = 0.009), concordant with prior retrospective evidence.

**Detail:** ERCC2 encodes a nucleotide excision repair (NER) DNA helicase; most ERCC2 mutations impair NER, reducing the tumour's ability to repair platinum-induced DNA damage. 22/28 ERCC2 alterations in S1314 have been functionally characterised as NER-impairing by a proteoprobe assay. ERCC2 was found in twice as many responders (22%) as non-responders (10%). Median TMB in ERCC2-altered tumours is 23.1 mut/Mb vs. 10.7 mut/Mb in ERCC2-wildtype. Nine ERCC2 alterations were found in non-responders, highlighting that not all ERCC2 mutations are functionally equivalent — some fall outside conserved helicase domains and may not impair NER.

### F3: Genomic landscape of S1314 MIBC closely resembles TCGA MIBC, with elevated RB1 alteration frequency

**Finding:** The most frequently altered genes in S1314 (TERT promoter, TP53, KMT2D, KDM6A, ARID1A, ATM, ERCC2) closely mirror the TCGA MIBC cohort, validating the S1314 genomic cohort as representative; RB1 alterations are more prevalent in S1314 (26% vs. 18% in TCGA).

**Detail:** TERT promoter mutations are the most frequent overall alteration. Epigenetic regulators (KMT2D, KDM6A, ARID1A) are highly altered, consistent with known MIBC biology. The elevated RB1 rate in S1314 may reflect enrichment for muscle-invasive (cT2+) disease or trial selection factors. Median TMB of 12.4 mut/Mb reflects the high mutational burden characteristic of MIBC.

### F4: Deleterious DDR alterations trend toward improved progression-free survival (HR 0.54)

**Finding:** Patients with deleterious DDR gene alterations have a PFS hazard ratio estimate of 0.54 (95% CI 0.29–1.01; P = 0.053) compared to DDR-wildtype patients — a trend consistent with, but not conclusively demonstrating, improved survival in this underpowered subgroup.

**Detail:** 77 PFS events and 54 OS events in the 179-patient survival cohort. The trial was not powered for biomarker-stratified survival analysis. No significant association between ERCC2 alteration status and survival (HR 0.64, P = 0.23 for PFS; HR 0.57, P = 0.23 for OS), possibly reflecting the low number of ERCC2-altered patients (n=24). The observed trend supports the ongoing Alliance A031701 trial, which tests DDR-guided bladder preservation after clinical complete response to chemotherapy.

### F5: Functional validation is needed — not all ERCC2 mutations predict sensitivity; VUS excluded from association

**Finding:** A subset of ERCC2 mutations in non-responders (9 mutations in 8 patients) and the absence of predictive power when VUS are included highlight that functional classification of DDR alterations — not merely their presence — is critical for clinical biomarker use.

**Detail:** ERCC2 mutations outside conserved helicase domains (e.g., near C-terminus) do not impair NER in vitro and do not predict cisplatin sensitivity. VUS in DDR genes when included with deleterious alterations eliminate the pathologic response association. A proteoprobe assay that measures NER efficiency in vitro has been used to classify ERCC2 variants; ongoing functional characterisation of all DDR gene alterations is underway. This has implications for test design: binary gene-level alteration calls are insufficient; variant-level functional classification is needed.

## Open Questions

- Can a refined functional DDR biomarker (proteoprobe-validated ERCC2 + LOF in other DDR genes) support bladder-preservation without cystectomy in prospective randomised trials?
- Will the Alliance A031701 trial (DDR-guided bladder preservation, NCT03609216) confirm clinical utility of this 9-gene DDR panel?
- What is the predictive value of germline DDR mutations (vs. somatic) for cisplatin sensitivity in MIBC?
- Do RNA-based molecular subtypes (luminal/basal) interact with DDR alteration status to modify chemotherapy response?
- Can urine-based liquid biopsy (urinary tumour DNA) complement tissue DDR testing to reduce biopsy inadequacy and capture tumour heterogeneity?

## Limitations

- Retrospective biomarker analysis of a prospectively conducted trial; potential selection bias (only patients with adequate tissue included)
- 9-gene DDR panel based on prior evidence; broader DDR pathways not interrogated
- Underpowered for DDR-stratified survival analysis (n=41 deleterious DDR vs. n=138 wildtype)
- ~15% of pT0 responses at cystectomy may be due to complete transurethral resection rather than chemotherapy effect, confounding response attribution
- No RNA expression data; molecular subtypes (luminal/basal) not assessed
- VUS functional impact unknown for many DDR alterations; prospective functional characterisation ongoing
- MSK-IMPACT panel captures exons of 505 genes; intronic and non-coding DDR alterations potentially missed
