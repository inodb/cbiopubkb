## Summary

Comprehensive genomic and transcriptomic profiling of 887 pediatric AML (pAML) cases revealed 23 mutually exclusive molecular categories that classify 91.4% of the cohort—far exceeding the 68.5% captured by the WHO 5th edition classification. Twelve of these categories are newly defined (including *UBTF*, *BCL11B*, *GLIS* fusions, and *FET/ETS* fusions) and carry distinct expression profiles, HOX gene signatures, cooperating mutations, and clinical outcomes, forming the basis of a new prognostic framework for pAML.

## Key Findings

### F1: 23 molecular categories classify 91.4% of pAML, vs 68.5% by WHO 5th

**Finding:** Integration of RNA-seq (fusions, ITD/PTD, expression) and DNA sequencing data defines 23 mutually exclusive molecular categories that comprehensively classify pAML.

**Detail:** 887 pAMLs assigned to 23 categories; 91.4% classified vs 68.5% by WHO 5th edition. 12 new categories not in WHO 5th include: UBTF tandem duplications, BCL11B SVs, GLIS2–3 fusions, FET/ETS fusions (e.g., FUS::ERG), PICALM::MLLT10, KAT6A rearrangements, MNX1 SVs, RUNX1::RUNX1T1-like, and CBFB-GDXY insertions. Pathogenic fusions/SVs in 70.7%; KMT2A rearrangements 20.3%, RUNX1::RUNX1T1 12.4%.

**Genes / cancer types:** `KMT2A` · `RUNX1` · `UBTF` · `BCL11B` · `NUP98` · `NPM1` · `CEBPA` · Pediatric AML

### F2: RAS pathway mutations in 37.5% of pAML with category-specific dependency

**Finding:** RAS pathway alterations are the most frequent cooperating mutations, but KRAS vs NRAS usage is strongly category-dependent, suggesting distinct signaling dependencies.

**Detail:** RAS pathway mutations in 37.5% (333/887) of pAML; 21.3% of those have ≥2 RAS pathway mutations. KRAS enriched in HOXA group (20.7%) and KMT2A r; rare in HOXB group (3.9%). NRAS prevalent in both HOXA (20.7%) and HOXB (17.4%). FLT3-ITD enriched in NUP98r, NPM1, UBTF, KMT2A-PTD, BCL11B (66.2% of FLT3-ITD+ cases). WT1 mutations co-occur with FLT3-ITD and enriched in NUP98r, UBTF, BCL11B.

### F3: HOXA and HOXB expression superfamilies define cross-category biological groupings

**Finding:** Two expression-based superfamilies (HOXA group: 18.5%; HOXB group: 23.3%) transcend individual molecular categories and carry distinct mutational dependencies and stem cell characteristics.

**Detail:** HOXA group includes mostly KMT2A r and KAT6A r; associated with monocytic differentiation and high KRAS/FLT3-TKD. HOXB group includes NPM1, NUP98r, UBTF, KMT2A-PTD, DEK::NUP214; associated with high stemness scores, FLT3-ITD (67.5% of FLT3-ITD+ patients), and WT1 mutations (57.6%). HOXB group generally poor prognosis (except NPM1). XAGE1 testis-specific proteins highly expressed in HOXA subcluster.

### F4: Molecular categories strongly predict clinical outcome and validate a new prognostic framework

**Finding:** Molecular categories are strongly associated with clinical outcomes in two independent cohorts, enabling a new prognostic framework combining molecular category assignment with minimal residual disease (MRD).

**Detail:** Good-prognosis categories (RUNX1::RUNX1T1, CBFB::MYH11, CEBPA) show high GMP scores (median >0.20). KMT2A r (poor prognosis) has low stemness-related scores. New entities like UBTF and BCL11B show distinct prognostic profiles. LSC17 and iScore (stemness/inflammation scores) correlated with molecular categories. MRD + molecular category framework forms basis for future treatment stratification.

**Genes / cancer types:** `RUNX1` · `CBFB` · `CEBPA` · `KMT2A` · `UBTF` · Pediatric AML

### F5: Cytogenetically cryptic alterations underscore need for sequencing-based diagnosis

**Finding:** Many category-defining alterations (NUP98::NSD1, GLIS fusions, UBTF tandem duplications, CEBPA/GATA1 mutations) are cryptic by cytogenetics alone, making RNA-seq or targeted sequencing essential for correct pAML classification.

**Detail:** Of 23 molecular categories, several are invisible to conventional karyotype: NUP98::NSD1 (cryptic fusion), GLIS2–3 fusions, UBTF tandem duplications, BCL11B SVs. In addition, AML-MR-defining mutations (e.g., EZH2) frequently co-occur with other defining alterations rather than forming an independent group in pAML (range 0.1–2.1%), supporting their exclusion as a defining pAML category.

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Can the HOXA/HOXB superfamily classification predict response to targeted FLT3 or RAS inhibitors in pAML?
- Do the newly defined categories (UBTF, BCL11B) have adult AML equivalents or are they pediatric-specific?
- What molecular mechanisms explain KRAS vs NRAS preferential usage across HOXA vs HOXB groups?
- Can MRD combined with molecular category replace current risk stratification for pAML clinical trials?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Cohort spans multiple datasets and collection periods; potential heterogeneity in sequencing protocols
- 76 "Unclassified" cases (8.6%) remain without a molecular category; many are rare and require larger cohorts
- Prognostic validation performed in two cohorts but not all molecular categories had sufficient sample size for robust survival analysis
- Functional characterization of newly defined categories (e.g., CBFB-GDXY) is limited
