## Summary

CellMiner is a web-based bioinformatics platform providing integrated access to genomic (22,217 genes across 5 microarray platforms, 360 microRNAs) and pharmacologic (18,549 compounds including 91 FDA-approved drugs) data for the NCI-60 cancer cell line panel. The platform introduces z-score normalization for cross-platform gene expression integration, a pattern-comparison tool for identifying correlations between any combination of gene expression, microRNA, and drug activity profiles, and demonstrates applications including multidrug resistance analysis (ABCB1/P-gp), colon-specific gene/drug discovery, and EGFR inhibitor family recognition. The tool makes the NCI-60 database accessible to non-informaticist researchers.

## Key Findings

### F1: Cross-platform z-score integration of 5 microarray platforms provides high-reliability composite gene expression for NCI-60

**Finding:** Averaging z-scores across up to five independent microarray platforms yields more reliable and reproducible gene expression estimates for the NCI-60 than any single platform alone.

**Detail:** 22,217 genes profiled; quality control drops probes with <1.2 log₂ intensity range or probe-to-probe Pearson correlation <0.30 across 60 cell lines. Final integration retains probes with average r ≥0.60. Example: ABCB1 represented by 46 highly correlated probes; high-expression outliers (NCI-ADR-RES, HCT-15) concordant with known P-glycoprotein overexpression and doxorubicin resistance. Genes with only one qualifying probe included but flagged as lower reliability. Z-score transform centers and normalizes data for cross-gene comparison.

**Genes / cancer types:** `ABCB1` · NCI-60 (pan-cell-line)

**Data availability:** `available`
> Gene expression z-scores for all 22,217 genes available in `cellline_nci60` on cBioPortal.

**Tool support:** `native`
> Expression levels per cell line accessible via the Plots tab in cBioPortal (expression vs. cell line). CellMiner web tool at discover.nci.nih.gov/cellminer provides direct z-score query.

### F2: Drug activity z-score integration across repeated GI50 experiments enables robust pharmacologic profiling of 18,549 compounds

**Finding:** Applying the same z-score and quality-control framework to drug GI50 data (across multiple repeat experiments) produces reliable, comparable drug activity profiles for nearly 18,549 compounds including 91 FDA-approved drugs.

**Detail:** GI50 determined at 48h using sulforhodamine B assay; experiments with range <1.2 log₁₀ or <35 cell lines tested are excluded. Repeat experiments must have inter-experiment Pearson r ≥0.60 at p<0.05 (for 35+ cell lines). 47,540 compounds tested; 18,549 pass QC. Example: doxorubicin represented by 122 highly correlated experiments. NCI-ADR-RES most resistant to doxorubicin (consistent with ABCB1 overexpression); pattern-comparison of ABCB1 expression with doxorubicin activity reveals strong positive correlation. Romidepsin (FK228) and bouvardin identified as ABCB1-related compounds by this approach.

**Genes / cancer types:** `ABCB1` · NCI-60 (pan-cell-line)

**Data availability:** `available`
> Drug activity data (GI50 as z-scores) available in `cellline_nci60` on cBioPortal for FDA-approved drugs; full compound set via CellMiner.

**Tool support:** `native`
> Plots tab in cBioPortal can display drug activity by cell line. CellMiner provides NSC-number-based drug query.

**Concepts:** #drug-response #NCI60 #actionability

### F3: Pattern-comparison tool identifies known and novel gene-drug-microRNA relationships across NCI-60

**Finding:** The CellMiner pattern-comparison tool systematically identifies statistically significant correlations (Pearson, p<0.05) between any user-defined pattern and gene expression, microRNA expression, or drug activity profiles across the NCI-60.

**Detail:** Example 1 (MDR): ABCB1 z-score pattern matched against all drugs identifies romidepsin, chromomycin, and bouvardin as correlated (previously unknown for chromomycin). Example 2 (EGFR family): erlotinib drug activity pattern identifies gefitinib, afatinib, and lapatinib as top correlated drugs (all EGFR family inhibitors), rediscovering known mechanistic groupings. Example 3 (E-cadherin regulation): CDH1 pattern comparison retrieves ZEB1, SNAI2, ZEB2, TWIST1 as negatively correlated transcriptional repressors; miR-200 family as positively correlated (r=0.41–0.73), consistent with published regulatory biology. Example 4 (colon specificity): colon-cell-line pattern retrieves TRIM15 (r=0.901), RNF43, VIL1 (colon markers); miR-215 and miR-194 (colorectal prognostic microRNAs); selumetinib (AZD6244) and sunitinib (both in CRC trials).

**Genes / cancer types:** `ABCB1`, `CDH1`, `ZEB1`, `ZEB2`, `SNAI2`, `TWIST1`, `TRIM15`, `RNF43`, `VIL1`, `EGFR`, `ERBB2` · NCI-60 (pan-cell-line, colon highlighted)

**Data availability:** `available`
> Expression and drug data in `cellline_nci60`; pattern comparison requires CellMiner web tool or data download.

**Tool support:** `api`
> Pattern comparison not natively available in cBioPortal; requires CellMiner web interface (discover.nci.nih.gov/cellminer) or downloading data and computing Pearson correlations in R/Python.

**Concepts:** #drug-response #NCI60 #actionability #BRAF #KRAS

### F4: MicroRNA expression in NCI-60 reveals miR-17-92 cluster co-expression with MYC and leukemia/colon specificity

**Finding:** The miR-17-92 oncogenic cluster shows enriched expression in leukemia and colon cell lines, and pattern comparison robustly recovers all 6 cluster members and MYC as correlated.

**Detail:** miR-18a (from miR-17-92 cluster) shows high specificity for leukemia and colon NCI-60 lines. Pattern comparison retrieves miR-17, miR-19a, miR-19b, miR-20a, and miR-92a with Pearson r=0.77–0.96 at top positions. MYC ranked 37th (r=0.61), consistent with known MYC-driven transcription of miR-17-92. 360 microRNAs profiled using Agilent 15k Human miRNA Microarray (V2); data presented as mean-centered log₂ intensities (not z-scores, as single platform). Available at GEO accession GSE22821.

**Genes / cancer types:** `MYC` · NCI-60 (leukemia, colon)

**Data availability:** `partial`
> microRNA expression data from `cellline_nci60` on cBioPortal may be limited; full dataset via GEO (GSE22821) and CellMiner.

**Tool support:** `api`
> MicroRNA data available via CellMiner; pattern comparison for miRNA-gene-drug correlations requires CellMiner web interface or data download.

### F5: Colon-specific gene and drug discovery: TRIM15, RNF43, VIL1, selumetinib identified by tissue-pattern matching

**Finding:** A colon-specific input pattern (1s for non-colon, 5s for colon lines) retrieves known and novel colon cancer biomarkers and drugs with high correlation, demonstrating the utility of CellMiner for tissue-specific target discovery.

**Detail:** Top gene: TRIM15 (r=0.901; minimally studied tripartite motif gene; candidate colon-specific marker). RNF43 (ubiquitin ligase, known CRC upregulation; r~0.85). VIL1 (actin-binding, established CRC diagnostic marker). Top microRNAs: miR-215 (r=0.745) and miR-194 (r=0.739), both known CRC prognostic indicators. Top clinical drugs: selumetinib/AZD6244 (MEK inhibitor, CRC trials), sunitinib (CRC trials), pyrazoloacridine (modest CRC activity). Novel compound NSC732298 showed highest colon specificity (r=0.653) but had no clinical trial history.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TRIM15`, `RNF43`, `VIL1`, `KRAS` · NCI-60 (colon cell lines)

**Data availability:** `available`
> Gene expression data for TRIM15, RNF43, VIL1 in `cellline_nci60`. Drug activity for selumetinib available via CellMiner.

**Tool support:** `api`
> Tissue-pattern matching not available natively in cBioPortal; requires CellMiner pattern comparison with a custom 60-element array upload, or downloading data and scripting in R/Python.

**Concepts:** #NCI60 #drug-response #KRAS #actionability

### F6: CellMiner enables systems pharmacology: cross-resistance grouping of EGFR inhibitors by drug activity pattern

**Finding:** Using erlotinib's drug activity pattern as input, CellMiner identifies gefitinib, afatinib, and lapatinib as the top correlated drugs, demonstrating that pharmacologic cross-resistance patterns can recover mechanistic drug classes without prior knowledge.

**Detail:** Erlotinib (NSC718781) activity pattern queried against all 18,549 compounds; gefitinib, afatinib, and lapatinib (all EGFR/ERBB family inhibitors) ranked at top of significant drug correlations. This confirms the pattern-comparison approach can de novo cluster drugs by mechanism of action. Similar approach used for ABCB1 (P-gp) cross-resistance grouping. Applications include identifying repurposing candidates and predicting resistance mechanisms.

**Genes / cancer types:** `EGFR`, `ERBB2` · NCI-60 (pan-cell-line)

**Data availability:** `available`
> Drug activity for erlotinib, gefitinib, afatinib, lapatinib in `cellline_nci60` via CellMiner.

**Tool support:** `api`
> Drug-drug activity cross-correlation requires CellMiner pattern comparison (Drug NSC# input) or scripting on downloaded GI50 matrices.

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Can drug activity patterns in the NCI-60 predict clinical response in genomically annotated patient cohorts (e.g., MSK-IMPACT, TCGA) by matching cell line molecular features to patient tumor profiles?
- Do the colon-specific candidate compounds (e.g., NSC732298) identified by CellMiner show clinical activity in molecularly stratified CRC trials?
- Can CellMiner microRNA-drug correlations be validated in patient-derived organoids or PDX models, where the tumor microenvironment is preserved?
- How well do NCI-60-derived gene-drug correlations (2012 data) translate to modern drug sensitivity datasets (e.g., PRISM, GDSC, DepMap)?

## Limitations (from paper)

- NCI-60 consists of 60 established cancer cell lines; does not capture tumor microenvironment, stromal interactions, or clonal heterogeneity.
- Pharmacologic data based on GI50 at 48h using sulforhodamine B assay; may not capture cytostatic vs. cytotoxic distinctions or long-term drug effects.
- Pattern comparison p-values not corrected for multiple comparisons; false discovery rate inflation in large-scale screens.
- microRNA data from a single Agilent platform (not multi-platform z-score averaged); less reliable than gene expression integration.
- Drug GI50 available only for compounds tested in ≥35 cell lines; compounds tested in fewer lines are excluded.
- Cell line identity and transcriptomic profiles may have drifted since original characterization; genetic instability in long-term culture may affect reproducibility.
