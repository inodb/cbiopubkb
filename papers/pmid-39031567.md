## Summary

This retrospective cohort study characterizes the clinicopathologic and molecular features of pathogenic *ERBB2* mutations in 2,638 endometrial carcinomas (ECs) sequenced with MSK-IMPACT at Memorial Sloan Kettering. *ERBB2* mutations occur in 2.6% (69/2,638) of ECs, predominantly as the kinase domain hotspot V842I (38%) and the juxtamembrane domain R678Q (25%). In sharp contrast to *ERBB2* amplification — which marks high-grade, copy number-high/TP53-abnormal, serous carcinomas — *ERBB2*-mutated/non-amplified ECs are predominantly low-grade endometrioid carcinomas, highly enriched for the MSI-H (59%) and POLE (11%) molecular subtypes, with high TMB and low chromosomal instability. Most have low HER2 protein expression (IHC 0/1+: 66%). *ERBB2* mutations are clonal in 87% of cases, indicating they are true pathogenic drivers rather than passenger events. Survival outcomes are similar to wildtype ECs; preliminary trastuzumab data suggest mutation-specific response patterns (R678Q and S310Y respond; V842I is resistant). HER2-directed antibody-drug conjugates (ADCs) — especially trastuzumab deruxtecan — may be the most promising therapeutic strategy given low IHC expression.

## Key Findings

### F1: ERBB2 mutations (2.6%) define a clinicopathologically distinct EC subgroup enriched for MSI-H and POLE molecular subtypes

**Finding:** *ERBB2* pathogenic mutations occur in 2.6% of ECs and, unlike *ERBB2* amplification, are predominantly found in low-grade endometrioid carcinomas of the MSI-H and POLE molecular subtypes — not in the copy number-high/TP53-abnormal serous subtype that harbors most amplifications.

**Detail:** Of 58 *ERBB2*-mut/non-amp ECs with molecular subtype data: 59% MSI-H, 11% POLE, 13% CN-L/NSMP, 17% CN-H/TP53abn. In the comparison cohort (ERBB2-wt/non-amp), molecular subtype distribution was: 24% MSI-H, 5.6% POLE, 33% CN-L/NSMP, 37% CN-H/TP53abn (p < 0.001). Histologically, 66% of mut/non-amp were endometrioid (79% of these were Grade 1 or 2). *ERBB2*-amplified ECs (wt/amp) are predominantly (92%) CN-H/TP53abn serous/high-grade — an entirely different pathologic entity. TCGA validation (n=529): 15 *ERBB2*-mutated cases (2.8%), 73% endometrioid, 73% MSI-H — consistent with MSK findings.

### F2: ERBB2-mutated/non-amplified ECs have markedly elevated TMB and low chromosomal instability

**Finding:** *ERBB2*-mutated/non-amplified ECs have significantly higher tumor mutational burden (median 43.2 mut/Mb) than both wildtype (median 6.1 mut/Mb) and amplified (median 4.4 mut/Mb) ECs, with very low chromosomal instability (median FGA 0.5%).

**Detail:** TMB differences were highly significant (p < 0.001 for both comparisons). Elevated TMB persists even when stratified by MSI status: within MSI-H ECs, *ERBB2*-mut/non-amp had higher TMB than MSI-H *ERBB2*-wt/non-amp (median 50.3 vs 29.8 mut/Mb, p < 0.001); within MSS ECs, *ERBB2*-mut/non-amp also had higher TMB (median 10.1 vs 5.3 mut/Mb, p < 0.001). Fraction of genome altered (FGA): *ERBB2*-mut/non-amp = 0.5% (range 0–33.1%), *ERBB2*-wt/non-amp = 5.1%, *ERBB2*-wt/amp = 23.4% (p < 0.001 for both comparisons). This pattern is consistent with *ERBB2* mutation arising within a "mutator phenotype" (MSI/POLE) background, followed by clonal expansion.

### F3: ERBB2 mutations are clonal in 87% of cases, co-occurring with ARID1A, PTEN, PIK3CA as likely drivers

**Finding:** Pathogenic *ERBB2* mutations are clonal in 87% of evaluable cases, with the most common co-mutations being *ARID1A* (65%), *PTEN* (57%), *PIK3CA* (54%) — consistent with a low-grade endometrioid driver landscape — plus *TP53* (46%), *FBXW7* (35%), *PPP2R1A* (17%) in higher-grade cases.

**Detail:** Cancer cell fraction analysis (ABSOLUTE v1.0.6) classified *ERBB2* mutations as clonal in 53/61 evaluable cases (87%). This indicates that *ERBB2* mutations occurred early in carcinogenesis and underwent positive selection, rather than being incidental passenger mutations in a hypermutated background. MSI-H cases with *ERBB2* mutations were enriched for germline or somatic MMR gene mutations (43% Lynch syndrome germline, 57% somatic MMR mutations among those without MLH1 promoter hypermethylation), in contrast to the general population of MSI-H ECs which are predominantly MLH1-hypermethylated (~70%).

### F4: HER2 IHC expression is low in most ERBB2-mutated ECs — most are IHC 0/1+

**Finding:** The vast majority of *ERBB2*-mutated/non-amplified ECs have low or absent HER2 protein expression by IHC (0/1+ in 66%, 2+ in 27%), with IHC 3+ occurring only in cases that also have *ERBB2* amplification.

**Detail:** HER2 IHC performed on 41 *ERBB2*-mutated ECs: IHC 0 = 27%, 1+ = 39%, 2+ = 27%, 3+ = 7.3%. All three IHC 3+ cases had both *ERBB2* mutation and amplification. Higher IHC scores correlated with TP53 mutation (79% of IHC 2+/3+ had TP53 vs 33% of IHC 0/1+, p=0.009) and CN-H/TP53abn molecular subtype (71% of IHC 2+/3+ vs 3.7% of IHC 0/1+, p < 0.001). No specific mutation (V842I, R678Q, S310F/Y) was associated with HER2 expression level. Implication: standard HER2-testing criteria (IHC 3+ or 2+ with FISH amplification) will miss most *ERBB2*-mutated ECs, and mutation-specific testing by NGS is required.

### F5: ERBB2 amplification (not mutation) drives poor prognosis in EC; mutation-specific trastuzumab responses

**Finding:** *ERBB2* amplification without mutation is associated with significantly worse PFS and OS on univariate analysis (HR 4.32 for PFS, 4.35 for OS vs wildtype; p < 0.001), while *ERBB2* mutation alone shows similar survival to wildtype EC. On multivariate analysis (adjusting for age, stage, molecular subtype), *ERBB2* status is no longer significant, reflecting the confounding by molecular subtype. Among 6 trastuzumab-treated *ERBB2*-mutated patients, responses tracked with mutation position: R678Q → complete response; S310Y → stable disease; V842I → disease progression.

**Detail:** Median PFS and OS were not reached for either wildtype or *ERBB2*-mut/non-amp ECs (median follow-up 21.8 months). *ERBB2*-wt/amp: median PFS 12.8 months, median OS 31.9 months. Multivariate Cox model (age, stage, molecular subtype, ERBB2 status): ERBB2 status p=0.52 for PFS, p=0.69 for OS. All 6 trastuzumab-treated patients also had ERBB2 amplification detected by IHC or FISH; the single patient with only mutation (EC22) had low-level amplification by FISH (ratio 2.1). Consistent with pre-clinical data: V842I confers trastuzumab resistance; R678Q is sensitive; S310Y is sensitive to trastuzumab/lapatinib/neratinib in models.

## Open Questions

1. Will HER2-directed ADCs (trastuzumab deruxtecan or T-DM1) show clinical efficacy in *ERBB2*-mutated/non-amplified ECs despite low HER2 IHC expression, as seen in ERBB2-mutant NSCLC?
2. Does combination of anti-HER2 therapy with checkpoint immunotherapy (given frequent MSI-H) produce synergistic benefit in this population?
3. Why is *ERBB2* mutation specifically enriched in MSI-H ECs arising from germline/somatic MMR gene mutations vs. MLH1-hypermethylated tumors — is this related to the higher TMB in the former subgroup?
4. What fraction of *ERBB2*-mutated ECs with low-level IHC expression retain sufficient HER2 receptor on the cell surface for ADC internalization?
5. Can specific *ERBB2* mutation types (kinase domain vs. juxtamembrane vs. extracellular) predict differential response to neratinib, lapatinib, and ADCs?

## Limitations

- Retrospective single-institution cohort; limited survival analysis N (n=34 *ERBB2*-mut/non-amp for survival).
- Only 6 patients received trastuzumab and all had concurrent amplification/IHC positivity — no patients treated with targeted therapy for mutation alone.
- Long-term follow-up (median 21.8 months) may be insufficient to detect survival differences given favorable MSI-H molecular subtype in this group.
- HER2 IHC performed using EC-specific criteria (Fader trial guidelines), which differ from breast/gastric IHC criteria — limits cross-cancer comparisons.
- TCGA validation cohort (n=15 *ERBB2*-mutated) is underpowered for subgroup analyses.
