## Summary

Reanalysis of MSK-IMPACT targeted sequencing data from 364 primary invasive lobular carcinomas (ILC) identifies 25 cases (6.9%) lacking *CDH1* genetic alterations. Among these, CDH1 promoter methylation (assessed by MSP and ddPCR) accounts for 63% (10/16 with available tissue). Three cases harbor loss-of-function alterations in *AXIN2* (deleterious fusion, inversion, truncating mutation). WGS of three cases without CDH1 genetic/epigenetic inactivation reveals a novel *CTNND1* (p120) deleterious fusion. Functional AXIN2 knockout in MCF7 cells recapitulates lobular-like features: anoikis resistance, increased migration, and reduced E-cadherin surface expression without *CDH1* transcriptional silencing. Findings establish that ILC is a convergent phenotype driven by disruption of the E-cadherin cell adhesion complex through multiple molecular mechanisms. Data in `ilc_msk_2023` on cBioPortal.

## Key Findings

### F1: CDH1 promoter methylation accounts for 63% of genetically CDH1-WT ILC

**Finding:** Of 25 primary ILCs lacking *CDH1* inactivating genetic alterations, 10/16 with available tissue (62.5%) display *CDH1* promoter methylation by MSP and/or ddPCR. Nine of ten methylated cases also show concomitant 16q loss and complete loss of E-cadherin by IHC, consistent with bi-allelic epigenetic+genomic inactivation. One case shows methylation without 16q loss and has reduced (not absent) E-cadherin expression, suggesting mono-allelic epigenetic silencing.

**Detail:** These findings revise the prior estimate that CDH1 promoter methylation is infrequent in ILC: the key insight is that among CDH1-mutation-negative ILCs (a selected subgroup), methylation is the predominant mechanism. The overall rate in the full ILC cohort (86% CDH1 bi-allelic by genetics + some proportion of the remaining 14% by methylation) suggests that true CDH1 wild-type ILCs are exceedingly rare. Classic ILC histology (6/10 methylated cases) is associated with CDH1 methylation + 16q loss, while ILC variants (pleomorphic, trabecular) are more frequent among non-methylated cases. Mutational signatures in CDH1 wild-type ILCs are similar to CDH1-altered ILCs: predominantly APOBEC (53%) or clock-like/aging (27%).

**Genes / cancer types:** CDH1 (16q22.1) · Breast invasive lobular carcinoma

### F2: AXIN2 loss-of-function alterations (fusion, inversion, truncating mutation) drive lobular phenotype in 3 ILCs

**Finding:** Reanalysis of MSK-IMPACT data identifies three ILCs with inactivating *AXIN2* alterations: (1) CDW21: trabecular ILC with *AXIN2* W78* truncating mutation (clonal by WGS); (2) CDW29: classic ILC with *PLXDC1-AXIN2* fusion deleting exons 3–13 and key functional domains (DIX, β-catenin-binding, GSK3β-interacting, RGS partial); (3) CDW30: pleomorphic ILC with focal 17q inversion truncating AXIN2 at exon 5. No LOH of the wild-type AXIN2 allele is observed in any case, consistent with AXIN2 haploinsufficiency as the mechanism.

**Detail:** AXIN2 is a multifunctional scaffold protein in Wnt, TGFβ, and Hippo pathways that regulates cell adhesion and differentiation. Loss of AXIN2 functional domains (DIX domain for Wnt signaling, β-catenin binding, GSK3β interaction) would impair E-cadherin complex stability through multiple pathways. CDW21 also harbors a somatic *BRCA2* Q2506Sfs*21 with LOH, dominant HRD mutational signatures (SBS, ID6), and HRDetect score 0.99 — identifying this as an HR-deficient ILC. The co-occurrence of AXIN2 LOF and BRCA2 bi-allelic inactivation in CDW21 may reflect selection for both cell adhesion disruption and DNA repair deficiency during lobular carcinogenesis.

### F3: CTNND1 (p120) deleterious fusion identified by WGS in ILC lacking CDH1 genetic/epigenetic inactivation

**Finding:** WGS of CDW20, a grade 2 classic ILC with complete E-cadherin loss and cytoplasmic p120 expression but no CDH1 genetic/epigenetic alteration and no AXIN2 alteration, reveals a t(3;11) translocation creating a *CTNND1-DENND6A* fusion. The fusion is predicted to cause loss of exons 12–20 of CTNND1, which encode armadillo repeat domains essential for p120's function in stabilizing the E-cadherin adhesion complex.

**Detail:** p120 (CTNND1) is a catenin that directly interacts with E-cadherin; loss of p120's armadillo repeats disrupts the cadherin-catenin adhesion complex, recapitulating the functional effect of CDH1 loss. CTNND1 is known to be haploinsufficient, and no LOH of the wild-type allele is observed — consistent with haploinsufficiency as the driver mechanism. CDW20 displays a clock-like/aging SBS signature and ID14 indel signature. This finding extends the spectrum of ILC-driving cell adhesion gene alterations to include CTNND1 structural rearrangements, undetectable by standard targeted panel sequencing.

**Genes / cancer types:** CTNND1 (p120), DENND6A · Breast invasive lobular carcinoma

### F4: AXIN2 knockout in MCF7 cells induces lobular-like features — anoikis resistance, migration, E-cadherin reduction

**Finding:** CRISPR-Cas9 AXIN2 knockout (94.5% frameshifting alleles, ~70–80% mRNA reduction) in MCF7 cells (ER+/HER2-negative, E-cadherin-expressing) recapitulates hallmark ILC features: increased cell viability and growth in low-attachment (non-adherent) conditions, increased cell viability after reseeding following 72h suspension culture (anoikis resistance), significantly increased cell migration in wound healing assay, and reduced E-cadherin surface expression by flow cytometry and immunofluorescence. CDH1 mRNA levels are unchanged, indicating post-transcriptional/post-translational E-cadherin regulation by AXIN2.

**Detail:** These phenotypes parallel those observed upon CDH1 siRNA knockdown in the same cell line (performed as a positive control), validating the lobular phenotype readout. CTNND1 (p120) mRNA and protein levels are unchanged in AXIN2-KO vs. control MCF7 cells, confirming that AXIN2 LOF does not converge on p120 expression as an intermediate. The reduction of E-cadherin cell surface expression without transcriptional change suggests AXIN2 modulates E-cadherin complex stability or trafficking at the post-translational level. 3D Matrigel cultures of AXIN2-KO MCF7 cells show decreased E-cadherin in acinar structures, consistent with disrupted cell-cell adhesion morphology.

**Genes / cancer types:** AXIN2, CDH1, CTNND1 · Breast cancer cell line model (MCF7)

### F5: ILC lacking CDH1 alterations shares genomic landscape with CDH1-altered ILC — PI3K as dominant driver

**Finding:** The most frequently altered gene in 25 ILCs lacking CDH1 genetic alterations is *PIK3CA* (40%; hotspot mutations H1047R n=4, E542K n=3, E545K n=1, others), followed by *TP53* (20%), *ERBB2* (16%), *AXIN2*, *CCND1*, *PAK1*, *PTEN*, *RUNX1*, *KMT2C* (12% each). No statistically significant differences in somatic alteration frequencies are found between CDH1 wild-type and CDH1 bi-allelic ILCs. TMB (p=0.07) and chromosomal instability (FGA, p=0.12) are comparable between groups.

**Detail:** The preservation of PIK3CA as the dominant driver across all ILC subgroups — regardless of CDH1 status or epigenetic mechanism — highlights the centrality of PI3K/AKT pathway activation in ILC biology. Mutational signature analysis in CDH1 wild-type ILCs shows APOBEC dominance (53%) and clock-like/aging (27%), identical to CDH1-altered ILCs (as previously reported), confirming shared mutagenic processes. The comparable FGA values indicate that chromosomal instability is similarly distributed regardless of CDH1 mechanism, refuting the hypothesis that CDH1 wild-type ILCs constitute a genomically distinct high-instability subgroup.

**Genes / cancer types:** PIK3CA, TP53, ERBB2, AXIN2, CCND1, PAK1, PTEN, RUNX1, KMT2C · Breast invasive lobular carcinoma

## Open Questions

- Can prospective *CDH1* promoter methylation testing at diagnosis identify patients with epigenetically driven ILC who may require different treatment monitoring or have distinct prognoses?
- Does AXIN2 haploinsufficiency in ILC sensitize tumors to Wnt pathway inhibitors (Porcupine inhibitors, β-catenin inhibitors) that could be tested in basket trials?
- What post-translational mechanism links AXIN2 loss to reduced E-cadherin surface expression without transcriptional *CDH1* changes — does AXIN2 regulate E-cadherin trafficking, ubiquitination, or cleavage?
- Are additional structural variants in cell adhesion genes (beyond CTNND1, AXIN2) present in the remaining ~37.5% of CDH1 wild-type ILCs lacking promoter methylation — requiring comprehensive WGS of the full cohort?
- Does the HR-deficient AXIN2-mutant ILC (CDW21, HRDetect 0.99) have distinct sensitivity to PARP inhibitors, and can HRD status be used to stratify CDH1 wild-type ILC for treatment?

## Limitations

- Only 16 of 25 CDH1 wild-type ILCs had sufficient FFPE material for methylation analysis; cases without material may bias methylation frequency estimate
- WGS performed on only 3 cases; the molecular basis of ILC lacking both CDH1 genetic/epigenetic inactivation and AXIN2/CTNND1 alterations remains unknown
- Epigenetic alterations in other cell adhesion genes besides CDH1 were not assessed; comprehensive epigenome analysis of ILC is warranted
- AXIN2 protein expression could not be assessed in cases with AXIN2 genetic alterations due to unavailability of material
- Functional AXIN2 KO studies used a single luminal A ER+ cell line (MCF7); the lobular phenotype induction was partial (not full loss of E-cadherin), and different ILC subtypes (pleomorphic, HER2+) were not modeled
