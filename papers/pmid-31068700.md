## Summary

Expanded multi-omic characterization of 1,072 CCLE cancer cell lines profiled by WES (n=326), WGS (n=329), RNA-seq (n=1,019), RRBS DNA methylation (n=843), miRNA (n=954), global histone modification profiling (n=897), and reverse-phase protein array (RPPA; n=899). Integrating these layers with functional screens (Project Achilles shRNA, CRISPR-Cas9 DRIVE) reveals novel drug biomarkers and synthetic lethal dependencies. Key findings include: MDM4 exon-6 splicing as a predictor of MDM2-inhibitor sensitivity beyond TP53 status; RPL22L1–RPL22 paralogue synthetic lethality linked to MSI; LDHB promoter methylation predicting LDHA dependency; SOX10 methylation–expression–dependency axes in melanoma; and pSHP2 as a cross-lineage biomarker for ponatinib sensitivity. Full data available in `ccle_broad_2019` on cBioPortal.

## Key Findings

### F1: MDM4 exon-6 splicing determines MDM2-inhibitor sensitivity independently of TP53 status

**Finding:** MDM4 produces two major isoforms — full-length (MDM4-FL, includes exon 6) and short (MDM4-S, skips exon 6). MDM4-FL expression is the primary determinant of MDM2-inhibitor (nutlin-3a) sensitivity and MDM4 gene dependency among TP53 wild-type cells, outperforming total MDM4 mRNA as a predictive biomarker.

**Detail:** RNA-seq across 1,003 CCLE lines reveals that MDM4-FL (exon-6 inclusion ratio >0.35) strongly correlates with nutlin-3a AUC (P<2.2×10⁻¹⁶) and DEMETER dependency scores. RPL22L1 expression is the top correlate of MDM4 exon-6 inclusion. RPL22 loss-of-function (frameshift hotspot in MSI tumors; ~68% of 99 inferred-MSI CCLE lines) elevates RPL22L1 expression and MDM4 exon-6 inclusion, linking microsatellite instability to MDM2-inhibitor sensitivity through the MDM4 splicing axis.

**Genes / cancer types:** MDM4, MDM2, RPL22, RPL22L1, TP53 · Pan-cancer (cell lines)

> RNA splicing (exon inclusion ratios), mutation calls, and drug sensitivity data in `ccle_broad_2019`. MDM4 exon-6 inclusion ratios derived from CCLE RNA-seq.

**Tool support:** `api`

### F2: Promoter methylation–synthetic lethality connections: RPP25/RPP25L and LDHB/LDHA paralogue pairs

**Finding:** Promoter hypermethylation of RPP25 predicts dependency on its paralogue RPP25L; methylation of LDHB predicts LDHA dependency and vice versa. These represent novel cancer-specific synthetic lethal interactions driven by epigenetic silencing of one gene family member.

**Detail:** RRBS methylation data correlated with Achilles/DRIVE dependency scores across 843 cell lines. RPP25 hypermethylation occurs in bladder, ovary, endometrium, and glioma lineages, leading to dependence on RPP25L (a RNase P/MRP complex component). LDHB–LDHA reciprocal methylation is found in primary tumors (TCGA) as well. VHL promoter methylation in three renal clear cell lines is identified as an alternative biallelic inactivation mechanism alongside copy number deletion.

**Genes / cancer types:** RPP25, RPP25L, LDHA, LDHB, VHL · Bladder, ovarian, endometrial, glioma, renal clear cell (cell lines)

**Data availability:** `available`
> RRBS methylation data available in `ccle_broad_2019`. Dependency scores downloadable from DepMap (depmap.org).

**Tool support:** `api`
> Methylation–expression correlation requires custom analysis; data downloadable via cBioPortal data hub or DepMap API.

### F3: pSHP2 (phospho-PTPN11-Y542) predicts ponatinib sensitivity across AML and RTK-altered cell lines

**Finding:** RPPA-measured phospho-SHP2 (pSHP2, pY542) is the strongest predictor of ponatinib sensitivity in AML cell lines and primagraft models, outperforming PTPN11 mRNA. High pSHP2 was validated in vivo: ponatinib extended survival in two high-pSHP2 AML primagrafts but not a low-pSHP2 control.

**Detail:** Ponatinib (BCR-ABL inhibitor with broad RTK activity) sensitivity correlated with pSHP2 in AML, CML, rhabdoid sarcoma, and thyroid lineages. Among 9 ponatinib-sensitive AML lines, 7 had FLT3, PDGFRA, FGFR1, or KIT alterations — established RTK activators. RNA-seq of two sensitive primagrafts confirmed FLT3-ITD (NVAM-61786) and BCR-ABL fusions (CBAM-87679). pSHP2 may serve as a pan-RTK activation surrogate for patient stratification.

**Genes / cancer types:** PTPN11, FLT3, PDGFRA, FGFR1, KIT, BCR-ABL · AML, CML (cell lines and primagrafts)

**Data availability:** `partial`
> RPPA protein expression data for 213 antibodies across 899 CCLE lines available in `ccle_broad_2019`. Phosphoprotein data (pSHP2) available via DepMap/CCLE portal. In vivo primagraft data not in cBioPortal.

**Tool support:** `api`
> RPPA data downloadable from depmap.org and CCLE portal. Custom correlation analysis needed; not directly supported by cBioPortal native UI.

### F4: Histone modification clusters identify EP300/CREBBP truncations as gain-of-function events

**Finding:** Global histone profiling (42 H3 marks, 897 cell lines) identified a new cluster with elevated H3K18ac and H3K27ac enriched for EP300 and CREBBP truncating mutations in the CH3 domain distal to the HAT domain — representing the first cancer-associated gain-of-function mutations for p300 and CBP.

**Detail:** Unsupervised clustering reproduced known EZH2-mutant and NSD2-mutant clusters. The new cluster contains EP300 and CREBBP truncations predicted to leave the HAT domain intact while removing C-terminal regulatory sequences, resulting in increased substrate acetylation and broader chromatin accessibility. This could explain oncogenic activity distinct from the loss-of-function model assumed for CREBBP in lymphoma.

**Genes / cancer types:** EP300, CREBBP, EZH2, NSD2 · Pan-cancer (cell lines)

**Data availability:** `available`
> Histone H3 modification profiling data available via CCLE portal (depmap.org). Mutation data in `ccle_broad_2019`.

**Tool support:** `api`
> Histone data not in cBioPortal UI; downloadable from DepMap. Mutation calls for EP300/CREBBP available via cBioPortal API on `ccle_broad_2019`.

### F5: TERT promoter mutation is the most common non-coding somatic mutation in cancer cell lines; lineage-specific methylation drives gene dependency

**Finding:** TERT promoter mutations occur in 16.7% (84/503) of CCLE cell lines across multiple lineages, making it the most frequent non-coding mutation. Separately, SOX10 promoter hypomethylation restricts SOX10 expression to melanoma and predicts strong SOX10 knockdown sensitivity — a prototype of lineage-specific methylation-driven dependency.

**Detail:** TERT promoter mutation frequency mirrors patterns in primary tumors (TCGA). SOX10 methylation is lineage-specific (melanoma only), and mRNA expression–dependency correlation is r=0.79 (P<2.2×10⁻¹⁶). More broadly, promoter hypomethylation of lineage transcription factors (PAX8, HNF1B, HNF4A) predicts lineage-specific dependency — a generalizable principle for identifying cancer vulnerabilities.

**Genes / cancer types:** TERT, SOX10, PAX8, HNF1B, HNF4A · Melanoma, thyroid, ovarian, endometrial (cell lines)

**Data availability:** `available`
> TERT promoter mutation status and promoter methylation data available via CCLE portal. Mutation calls in `ccle_broad_2019`.

**Tool support:** `native`

## Open Questions

- Can MDM4 exon-6 inclusion and RPL22 status be used as prospective patient stratification biomarkers in MDM2-inhibitor clinical trials?
- Do EP300/CREBBP CH3-domain truncations identified in cell lines correspond to gain-of-function mutations in primary tumors, and are they druggable?
- What is the full repertoire of paralogue synthetic lethalities driven by promoter methylation across cancer types?
- Does pSHP2 reliably identify RTK-activated patient tumors for RTK-inhibitor trials in histologies beyond AML?
- How well do CCLE multi-omic profiles predict primary tumor biology, given the known discordances in mutational drift between passage-matched cell lines?

## Limitations

- Genetic drift identified in 3–10% of cell lines may affect reproducibility of somatic variant calls, especially for passenger mutations
- RPPA panel covers only 213 antibodies, limiting phosphoproteomic depth; does not capture most kinase substrates
- RRBS methylation covers ~17,182 gene promoters but does not provide full CpG coverage or distal regulatory elements
- Drug sensitivity data (CTRP, GDSC) and functional screens (Achilles, DRIVE) were generated on different cell line subsets and passage numbers, limiting cohort overlap
- miRNA and histone profiling results were not directly integrated into a unified predictive model
