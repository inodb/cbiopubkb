## Summary

Prospective MSK-IMPACT sequencing of 1,134 colorectal adenocarcinomas (1,011 from 979 mCRC patients, 123 from early-stage CRC) defines the genomic landscape of metastatic CRC. Key discoveries include: novel intronic splice-site mutations in *APC*, large in-frame *CTNNB1* exon 3 deletions (WNT pathway alterations raised to 96%), and a genomic basis for the right- vs. left-sided survival difference in MSS mCRC. Right-sided MSS primaries had enriched KRAS/BRAF/PIK3CA/PI3K/TGFβ pathway alterations; left-sided primaries relied on RTK ligand overexpression rather than mutations. Five mitogenic pathway subgroups with distinct survival outcomes are defined. Full dataset publicly available at `crc_msk_2017`.

## Key Findings

### F1: Novel non-coding and structural WNT pathway alterations raise frequency to 96% of CRCs

**Finding:** Previously undetected intronic splice-site mutations in *APC* (exon 9) and large in-frame *CTNNB1* exon 3 deletions expand the WNT pathway alteration frequency to 96% of MSI-H and 85% of MSS CRCs.

**Detail:** A recurrent intronic SNV 8 bp upstream of *APC* exon 9 (chr5:112151184 A>G) found in 35 MSS cases; confirmed by ddPCR to introduce a splice acceptor site causing frameshift. Absent from all other 9,939 non-CRC tumors in MSK-IMPACT database. *CTNNB1* large exon 3 in-frame deletions (spanning entire exon 3, amino acids 22–33) in 29 cases, exclusive to MSS tumors, mutually exclusive with APC alterations (p<0.001), confirmed by β-catenin nuclear IHC. These deletion events are too large for standard indel callers and too small for CNA callers, explaining why they were missed previously.

**Genes / cancer types:** `APC`, `CTNNB1`, `RNF43` · Colorectal cancer (MSS and MSI-H)

**Data availability:** `available`
> crc_msk_2017 contains the full mutation, CNA, and fusion dataset including these APC intronic and CTNNB1 exon 3 deletion variants.

**Tool support:** `api`
> Standard APC alteration frequency visible natively. Intronic APC splice variants require API-level filtering by genomic position (chr5:112151184). CTNNB1 exon 3 in-frame deletions require custom size filtering via API; not natively distinguished from short indels in the Mutations tab.

### F2: Right-sided MSS mCRC has worse survival driven by genomic pathway differences

**Finding:** Right-sided MSS mCRC has significantly shorter 5-year OS compared to left-sided (45% vs. 67%; p<0.001), and this survival difference is explained by differences in somatic mutation landscape rather than primary tumor site per se.

**Detail:** In multivariate Cox model including primary site and gene alterations, oncogenic *APC* (HR=0.57, p<0.01, protective), *BRAF* (HR=2.02, p<0.001), *KRAS* (HR=1.40, p<0.01), and *NRAS* (HR=2.59, p<0.01) were significant predictors, but primary tumor site was not (HR=1.29, p=0.14). Right-sided tumors: higher median mutation burden (7.15 vs. 5.92/Mb, p<0.001), enrichment for *KRAS*, *BRAF*, *PIK3CA*, *AKT1*, *RNF43*, *SMAD2*, *SMAD4*; peritoneal metastases more common. Left-sided tumors: enrichment for *APC*, *TP53*; liver/lung metastases predominate (surgically resectable).

**Genes / cancer types:** `KRAS`, `BRAF`, `PIK3CA`, `NRAS`, `APC`, `TP53`, `SMAD4`, `RNF43` · MSS metastatic colorectal cancer

### F3: Five mitogenic pathway subgroups define genomic heterogeneity in MSS mCRC with distinct survival

**Finding:** MSS mCRC can be classified into five mitogenic signaling subgroups with significantly different outcomes: RTK-only, RAS-MAPK, PI3K-only, concurrent RAS-MAPK/PI3K, and no alteration — each with distinct survival and metastatic tropism.

**Detail:** >75% of right-sided tumors had RAS-MAPK/PI3K activation; ~50% of left-sided tumors had no mitogenic pathway alteration or RTK-only activation. Survival was longest for no-alteration or RTK-only groups; shortest for RAS pathway–altered groups. RAS pathway alterations significantly associated with multiple sites of first metastasis (p<0.05). Metastatic tropism patterns (liver/lung for left; peritoneum for right) partially explained by genomic subgroup, not just primary site.

**Genes / cancer types:** `KRAS`, `NRAS`, `BRAF`, `PIK3CA`, `PTEN`, `ERBB2` · MSS metastatic colorectal cancer

### F4: BRAF mutations in CRC span three mechanistic classes with distinct RAS co-mutation patterns

**Finding:** MSS mCRC harbors all three classes of BRAF mutants (V600/class 1; constitutively active dimers/class 2; hypoactive/class 3), each with distinct co-mutation patterns with RAS pathway genes, predicting sensitivity vs. resistance to EGFR inhibition.

**Detail:** Class 1 (V600E, 5% of MSS) and class 2 BRAF mutants signal independently of RAS; rarely co-occur with RAS mutations; predicted insensitive to EGFR inhibitors. Class 3 (hypoactive/kinase-dead) mutants frequently co-occur with *RAS* mutations; those without concurrent RAS mutations amplify EGFR/RTK signaling and are predicted EGFR-inhibitor–sensitive. BRAF V600E in MSI-H CRC: 22% of MSI-H vs. 5% of MSS; MSI-H BRAF V600E patients older at diagnosis (median 72 vs. 55 years, p<0.01). CRC has higher proportion of class 3 BRAF mutants than melanoma or lung cancer.

**Genes / cancer types:** `BRAF`, `KRAS`, `NRAS`, `EGFR` · MSS and MSI-H colorectal cancer

### F5: 37% of MSS mCRC harbors potentially actionable alterations; MSI-H tumors (4% of mCRC) are all actionable

**Finding:** Using OncoKB classification, 46% of right-sided and 30% of left-sided MSS mCRC harbors potentially targetable oncogenic alterations. Among MSI-H mCRC (4% of cases), 86% are actionable vs. 37% of MSS (p<0.001).

**Detail:** MSI-H enrichments include: *BRAF* V600E (22%), *BRCA1/2* (22%), *NTRK* fusions (8%), *PIK3CA*/PTEN (41%/35%). MSS actionable alterations primarily *BRAF* V600E (5%) and *PIK3CA*. RTK alterations rare in MSS: *ERBB2* amplification in 4% of MSS mCRC, predominantly left-sided primaries (7% vs. 2% right). Four tumors with activating *EGFR* hotspot mutations (L861Q, L858R, R776H) potentially sensitize to EGFR TKIs. One *EGFR* S492R post-cetuximab resistance alteration identified.

**Genes / cancer types:** `BRAF`, `PIK3CA`, `PTEN`, `ERBB2`, `NTRK1/3`, `BRCA1/2`, `EGFR` · MSS and MSI-H mCRC

### F6: TP53 alteration is the only driver enriched in metastatic vs. early-stage CRC; high genomic concordance between primary and metastatic sites

**Finding:** Comparing early-stage CRC to metastatic CRC primaries and metastases, only *TP53* alterations are significantly enriched in metastatic disease; *FBXW7* alterations are enriched in early-stage tumors (potentially protective). Primary and metastatic tumor specimens show high genomic concordance.

**Detail:** 47% of MSK-IMPACT CRC specimens are metastases (vs. 0% in TCGA). TP53 enrichment in mCRC vs. early-stage confirmed across TCGA and MSK data. Among 18 patients with >1 sample sequenced, few genomic differences between specimens. Median mutation burden in MSS mCRC: 7 per MB (range 1–361). MSK cohort demographically younger (median 54 vs. 70 years TCGA) and more stage IV (60% vs. 14%) — reflecting clinical referral patterns for sequencing.

**Genes / cancer types:** `TP53`, `FBXW7`, `APC`, `KRAS` · Metastatic vs. early-stage colorectal cancer

## Open Questions / Hypotheses

- Do the five mitogenic pathway subgroups from MSS mCRC predict differential response to EGFR inhibitors, MEK inhibitors, or emerging KRAS G12C inhibitors in subsequent clinical datasets?
- Can CTNNB1 exon 3 in-frame deletions be reliably called in real-time clinical sequencing and do they predict response to WNT pathway–directed therapies?
- Does the higher RTK ligand expression in left-sided CRC (amphiregulin, epiregulin, neuregulin) correlate with differential benefit from cetuximab/panitumumab?
- Are ERBB2-amplified right-sided MSS mCRC tumors (low prevalence, 2%) biologically and clinically distinct from ERBB2-amplified left-sided tumors?

## Limitations (from paper)

- Single-center dataset from a major cancer referral center; enriched for mCRC patients seeking additional genotyping to guide anti-EGFR use (selection bias)
- Mix of 341/410/468-gene panel versions during study period; frequencies adjusted but gene-level comparisons may be impacted
- Survival data from time of metastatic diagnosis available, but treatment data not systematically reported
- Left/right-sided survival difference analysis restricted to MSS cases only
- Ligand expression data from TCGA (primary tumors) rather than from the MSK mCRC cohort directly
- No RNA-seq in MSK cohort; expression-based analyses are TCGA-derived and cross-cohort
