## Summary

This single-institution retrospective study of 61 patients with extremity rhabdomyosarcoma (RMS) treated at MSKCC over two decades integrates clinicopathologic staging and MSK-IMPACT genomic profiling (available for 40% of cases) to identify prognostic biomarkers. The cohort is dominated by FOXO1 fusion–positive alveolar RMS (ARMS; 85%), of which 70% carry PAX3::FOXO1 and 26% PAX7::FOXO1 transcripts. Metastatic disease (5-year OS 29%), high-risk group, and age ≥10 years are the strongest adverse survival factors. PAX3::FOXO1 confers significantly worse OS than PAX7::FOXO1 (36% vs. 86% at 5 years). Among sequenced ARMS, CDK4 amplifications and CDKN2A deletions (each 8–17%; mutually exclusive) occur in 28% and associate with acral location, high-risk disease, and significantly worse OS (p=0.029). Data are available in cBioPortal as `rms_msk_2023`.

## Key Findings

### F1: Metastatic disease, high-risk group, and age ≥10 years are the dominant adverse prognostic factors in extremity RMS

**Finding:** Metastatic disease at diagnosis was the strongest predictor of poor outcome (5-year EFS 19%, 5-year OS 29%) compared to localized (5-year EFS 58%, OS 84%) and nodal involvement only (5-year EFS 43%, OS 66%). High-risk group (HR=2.78, p=0.010) and age ≥10 years (HR=2.26, p=0.034) independently affected OS.

**Detail:** The overall cohort 5-year EFS was 36% and 5-year OS was 59%. Of 31 patients with metastatic disease, 55% had on-treatment progression and 23% had off-treatment relapse. Metastatic sites included bone marrow (55%), bone (52%), lung (10%), and other visceral sites. High-risk disease was confirmed as an independent OS predictor by multivariate analysis (HR=2.7, p=0.02). Nodal involvement alone had a comparatively lower impact than distant metastasis — 5-year OS of 66% vs. 29% — suggesting aggressive locoregional treatment (radiotherapy + lymphadenectomy) may provide benefit in N1M0 patients.

### F2: PAX3::FOXO1-positive ARMS carries significantly worse prognosis than PAX7::FOXO1 — 5-year OS 36% vs. 86%

**Finding:** PAX3::FOXO1 fusion type is an independent adverse prognostic factor for OS (HR=3.45, p=0.016 univariate; HR=2.86, p=0.004 multivariate), associated with older age at diagnosis and a striking difference in post-relapse survival compared to PAX7::FOXO1.

**Detail:** PAX3::FOXO1 (n=36) patients had a median age of 11 years vs. 3.5 years for PAX7::FOXO1 (n=16). Five-year OS for PAX3::FOXO1 was 36% vs. 86% for PAX7::FOXO1. Five-year EFS did not differ significantly (26% vs. 40%), indicating that the OS difference is driven primarily by post-relapse survival: 50% of relapsed PAX7 patients survived long-term vs. only 6% of relapsed PAX3 patients. Among PAX3-positive ARMS, 67% were classified as high risk compared to 25% of PAX7-positive cases.

### F3: CDK4 amplification and CDKN2A deletion — mutually exclusive, present in 28% of ARMS — associate with poor OS

**Finding:** CDK4 amplifications and CDKN2A deletions are mutually exclusive, together occurring in 28% of ARMS cases with genomic data, enriched in acral sites and high-risk patients. Their presence significantly correlates with worse OS (p=0.029) in the sequenced ARMS cohort.

**Detail:** In 18 sequenced ARMS patients: CDK4 amplification in 8–17% of cases, CDKN2A deletion in 8–17%, and the combined CDK4-or-CDKN2A group in 5 of 18 (28%). All but one of these five patients had high-risk disease. Median OS dropped from 5 years in the whole ARMS group to 3.9 years in the CDK4/CDKN2A-altered subset. Median EFS dropped from 1.7 years to 1.0 year. CDK4 amplification on the 12q13-15 locus co-occurred with MDM2 and GLI1 amplifications in some cases. These alterations are putatively mutually exclusive with each other in the cell cycle pathway (CDK4 amplification activates CDK4/6 while CDKN2A deletion eliminates its inhibitor p16INK4A).

### F4: MED12 alterations are the most frequent recurrent SNVs in ARMS (8–17%) with unique hotspot mutations

**Finding:** MED12 alterations (missense mutations and deletion) were identified in three ARMS patients (8–17%), including two unique hotspot missense mutations (H1508L and G1807V), representing the only recurrent SNVs in the ARMS sequenced cohort.

**Detail:** MED12 encodes a mediator complex subunit involved in transcriptional regulation and chromatin remodeling. Two missense mutations (c.4523A>T p.H1508L; c.5420G>T p.G1807V) and one deletion were observed in three sequenced ARMS patients. MED12 mutations have been described in other sarcoma subtypes and represent a potential therapeutic vulnerability via CDK8/19 inhibition. ERMS cases showed a higher incidence of SNVs overall (including HRAS Q61L, TP53/RB/FGFR4 deletions, NF1 frameshift), while SRMS cases showed the canonical MYOD1 L122R hotspot (co-occurring with PIK3CA in one case).

### F5: Sentinel node mapping and prophylactic nodal irradiation improve locoregional control — 18F-FDG-PET/CT misses occult nodal disease

**Finding:** Sentinel node mapping identified occult nodal disease missed by 18F-FDG-PET/CT in two patients. Four patients who received prophylactic nodal irradiation (PNI) had no nodal relapse, suggesting potential benefit of systematic locoregional treatment in extremity RMS.

**Detail:** Lymphoscintigraphy-guided sentinel node mapping was performed in 72% of patients (44/61). Two patients with negative baseline 18F-FDG-PET/CT had nodal involvement confirmed only by sentinel node sampling (one with a 1.2 mm focus, one with scattered immunostaining-positive cells). Four patients who received PNI did not experience relapse, though this is based on very small numbers. The high rate of nodal involvement (56% total: 18% N1M0, additional nodal-positive M1 cases) underscores the importance of systematic staging in extremity RMS, including combined PET/CT and sentinel node sampling.

## Open Questions

- Does CDK4/6 inhibitor therapy (palbociclib, ribociclib) have activity in CDK4-amplified or CDKN2A-deleted RMS, and can these alterations serve as prospective biomarkers for CDK4/6 inhibitor trials?
- What is the mechanistic basis for the dramatically better post-relapse survival of PAX7::FOXO1 vs. PAX3::FOXO1 ARMS — is this driven by immunologic, metabolic, or intrinsic biology differences?
- Can routine prophylactic nodal irradiation improve survival in extremity ARMS, and at what threshold of locoregional disease burden?
- Does MED12 mutation status in ARMS correlate with response to specific systemic therapies or predict transcriptional program dependencies?
- Can liquid biopsy (ctDNA) detect early relapse or residual disease in extremity RMS with better sensitivity than imaging alone?

## Limitations

- Only 40% of the cohort (24/61 patients) had MSK-IMPACT sequencing, limiting statistical power for genomic-survival correlations
- Single-institution retrospective design spanning 20 years with evolving treatment protocols limits comparability across eras
- Small numbers of ERMS (n=7) and SRMS (n=2) preclude subtype-specific genomic-survival analyses
- Survival analysis of CDK4/CDKN2A alterations performed only in 18 sequenced ARMS patients — requires validation in larger cohorts
- Prophylactic nodal irradiation benefit (n=4) is based on too few patients to be statistically conclusive
- No RNA-seq data available; fusion calling based on FISH/RT-PCR limits discovery of atypical or novel fusions
